# C. Northcote Parkinson

C. Northcote Parkinson appears in [[the-laws-of-the-office-revisited]] as the writer behind [[ParkinsonsLaw]]. The episode traces the law to his humorous 1955 Economist essay about bureaucracies growing regardless of the actual amount of work.

In the source, Parkinson's joke becomes a practical workplace mechanism: deadline length can change the amount of effort, polish, coordination, and delay people add to a task. That makes him part of the episode's larger [[WorkplaceIncentiveDesign]] branch rather than only an office-satire footnote.
