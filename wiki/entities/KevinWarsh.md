# Kevin Warsh

Kevin Warsh appears in [[jerome-powell-and-the-test-of-fed-independence]] as the incoming [[DonaldTrump]]-nominated [[FederalReserve]] chair and a former Fed board member. The episode uses him less as a biography subject than as the next test of whether [[CentralBankIndependence]] survives leadership change.

## Source Position
- The source says Warsh previously served on the Fed board, which [[LaelBrainard]] treats as a reason to give him the benefit of the doubt.
- Brainard's argument is that board service exposes officials to the Fed's dual mandate and the need to avoid political judgments.
- Warsh is connected to the episode's broader question: independence is not settled by Powell's behavior if future chairs face similar pressure.

## Connections
- [[FederalReserve]] - institution Warsh is described as incoming chair of.
- [[DonaldTrump]] - nominating president in the source.
- [[LaelBrainard]] - former Fed official commenting on his likely institutional understanding.
- [[CentralBankIndependence]] and [[JeromePowell]] - principle and predecessor comparison.
