# Kathleen Claussen

Kathleen Claussen appears in [[the-supreme-court-struck-down-a-bunch-of-trumps-tariffs-now-what]] as the Georgetown law professor explaining the trade-law stakes of the Supreme Court ruling. She says trade lawyers did not generally treat the International Emergency Economic Powers Act as a tariff statute, which makes her the source's main guide to [[IEPATariffAuthorityLimit]].

Claussen also explains the fallback route through [[Section122TariffAuthority]]. In the episode, Section 122 permits tariffs up to 15% for a limited balance-of-payments period, but it is not presented as a clean replacement for IEPA because it expires after 150 days unless [[UnitedStatesCongress]] extends it.
