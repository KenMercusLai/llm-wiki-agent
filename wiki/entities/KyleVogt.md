# Kyle Vogt

Kyle Vogt appears in [[socialradarspod-emmettshear-v2]] as the technical cofounder who helped [[JustinTV|Justin.tv]] build its own live-video infrastructure. [[EmmettShear]] says the team found Kyle by emailing an MIT hacker list while looking for hardware help, then came to see him as a true cofounder because his contribution was central.

The source's strongest Kyle Vogt point is infrastructure learning. Justin.tv initially used a CDN, but cost and feature limits pushed the team to build more of the video system internally. Kyle's early system included a proxy that saved a copy of the stream as it passed through, making him part of the technical path from a lifecasting stunt toward [[Twitch]].

[[tsr-s3-kylevogt-v3final-tsr-s3-kylevogt-v3final]] turns Vogt from a supporting Justin.tv figure into the main founder case. He describes joining Justin.tv from an MIT jobs mailing list because the backpack live-streaming system required hardware, software, networking, and improvisation. The experience taught him a "we'll figure it out" attitude that later made [[Cruise]] feel possible despite investor skepticism and the size of the self-driving problem.

The Cruise section makes Vogt a hard-tech founder case. He used [[HardProblemMVPScoping]] to reduce self-driving to a first highway retrofit, endured [[HardTechFundraising]] through roughly 120 pitches, brought in [[DanielKan]], pivoted from retrofits to [[RobotaxiEconomics]], and later sold Cruise to [[GeneralMotors]] because autonomy needed capital, manufacturing, and vehicle-scaling support. His safety framing connects Cruise to [[AutonomousVehicleSafetyBenchmark]] and [[EnvelopeExpansionDeployment]] rather than only a "move fast" startup story.
