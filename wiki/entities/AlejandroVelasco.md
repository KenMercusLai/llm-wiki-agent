# Alejandro Velasco

Alejandro Velasco is the Venezuela expert used in [[venezuelas-recent-economic-history-update]] to explain how an emergency exchange-rate measure became an economic trap. The episode attributes to him the view that post-strike currency controls initially made sense, but became dangerous once [[Venezuela]] kept them after the immediate crisis.

Velasco also anchors the update's stabilization account. The source says he treats the U.S. dollar as the main factor that restored some planning ability after hyperinflation, while emphasizing that [[DollarizedStabilization]] remained unequal because many Venezuelans did not have access to dollars.
