# Tuan mac Cairill / 图安

Tuan mac Cairill / 图安 is the reincarnating witness at the center of one major story in [[142-kaierte-shenhua-gushi-diyidan-deluyi-hui-mengjian-bianxing-yang-ma-865479403]]. The episode begins his tale with the abbot Finnian hearing of a powerful old-faith man who refuses Christian festivals and worship. Tuan first shuts Finnian out, then opens the door because hospitality requires him not to let a guest freeze or starve.

Tuan's story turns [[CelticAnimalTransformation]] into historical memory. He survives an early plague and then moves through forms as stag, boar, eagle, salmon, and human, witnessing successive peoples arrive in Ireland: Partholon, Nemed, the [[TuathaDeDanann|Tuatha De Danann / 达努神族]], and the Sons of Mil. When he is reborn after the queen eats the salmon, his testimony can enter a Christian narrative without erasing the old-faith memory he carries.

## Key Claims
- Tuan is a memory archive in bodily form.
- His transformations preserve identity across different animal lifespans and powers.
- The story lets Christian conversion and pre-Christian knowledge meet through hospitality rather than simple replacement.
- Tuan's testimony supports [[MythAsHistoricalEvidence]] as cultural memory, but not as a literal settlement chronology.
