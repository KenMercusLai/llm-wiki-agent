# Charles Goodhart

Charles Goodhart appears in [[the-laws-of-the-office-revisited]] as the economist behind [[GoodhartsLaw]]. The episode identifies him as a former adviser to the Bank of England and professor emeritus at the London School of Economics, then turns his monetary-policy observation into a general rule about measurement and control.

His role in the source is not only historical attribution. The episode uses his idea to connect a teenage cashier's scanning metric, hospital emergency targets, and broader [[WorkplaceMetricGaming]] into the same institutional pattern: once a number becomes a control target, people may optimize the number rather than the real outcome.
