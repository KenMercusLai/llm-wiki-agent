# Cruise

Cruise appears in [[134-shuju-de-zongshu-he-xiechen-liao-xinshidai-de-shiyou-lishi-bantu-shuju-jinzita-dingjia-yu-recipe]] as the autonomous-driving company where [[XieChen]] says he first validated that simulation and synthetic data could support algorithm training rather than remain toy demos.

[[tsr-s3-kylevogt-v3final-tsr-s3-kylevogt-v3final]] adds Cruise's own founder-origin account through [[KyleVogt]]. Vogt says he started Cruise after deciding that self-driving cars could be attacked with a startup-shaped first wedge rather than only a research-lab approach. The first product was a retrofitted Audi highway lane-keeping demo, making Cruise a case in [[HardProblemMVPScoping]], [[JankyMVP]], and [[HardTechFundraising]].

The same source explains the pivot from consumer retrofits into [[RobotaxiEconomics]]. Retrofit support created legal, liability, and vehicle-compatibility complexity, while ride-hailing suggested a larger business if autonomy could remove the driver. Cruise then treated deployment as safety and operations work: it studied human-driver benchmarks, expanded its operating envelope gradually, and eventually sold to [[GeneralMotors]] to access capital, manufacturing, and scale.
