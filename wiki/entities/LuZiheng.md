# Lu Ziheng

Lu Ziheng is the founder of [[Kaiwuji]] and the guest in [[ni-you-yi-ba-nenggou-wa-chu-jinzi-de-chanzi-kending-buhui-xian-gei-bieren-yong-duitan-kaiwuji-lu-ziheng-yong-ai-faming-xin-cailiao-lvhl1-hy1gwtainujjgf8xbs4fyh]]. The episode describes him as a Nanjing University of Science and Technology undergraduate, Hong Kong University of Science and Technology PhD, former Microsoft Research AI-for-science researcher, and participant in building the materials direction at Beijing's Zhongguancun Institute.

Lu's core argument is that [[AIMaterialsDiscovery]] will matter only if models can generate or identify material candidates that survive expert judgment, synthesis, validation, scale-up, and customer use. He repeatedly pushes against a pure-tool framing: if a model can discover high-value material IP, the company should use it to build materials first.

## Key Claims
- Lu separates materials work into original compound/IP discovery and downstream process scale-up plus commercialization.
- He thinks AI's largest near-term leverage may be discovering "blockbuster" material IP rather than only optimizing known production processes.
- He treats scalable models, diffusion generation, simulation, and broad property prediction as a route toward useful generalization in materials.
- He argues that hard domain expertise still matters: model teams, simulation specialists, and senior experimental scientists need close daily collaboration.
- His talent view emphasizes hard specialization in one domain, broad vision, agency, taste, belief, and willingness to work on major technical problems.
