# Jacques-Louis David / 雅克-路易·大卫

Jacques-Louis David / 雅克-路易·大卫 appears in [[125-mala-zhisi-beibi-de-ansha-huo-zhengyi-de-chujue-783972516]] as the revolutionary painter whose [[DeathOfMaratPainting|The Death of Marat]] turns [[JeanPaulMarat|Marat]] into a martyr. The episode treats the painting as a political image, not only an art-historical masterpiece.

David's visual choices matter: Marat's body is cleaned, softened, and made saint-like, while [[CharlotteCorday|Corday]] is absent. The source reads that omission as part of [[RevolutionaryMartyrdomImageMaking]], where art can decide which body carries pity, virtue, sacrifice, and public memory.

The episode also mentions David in relation to [[AntoineLavoisier|Lavoisier]], whose domestic and scientific life had earlier been painted by David. That connection lets one artist sit between revolutionary sainthood and the scientific elite later threatened by revolutionary suspicion.
