# 《麦田里的守望者》 / The Catcher in the Rye

《麦田里的守望者》 / The Catcher in the Rye is the [[JDSalinger|J. D. Salinger / 塞林格]] novel discussed in [[01-maitian-shouwangzhe-20shiji-de-meiguo-jiabaoyu-523554422]]. The episode begins from the common reaction that the book feels like a loose record of a expelled teenager wandering through New York, then argues that the looseness is how [[HoldenCaulfield|Holden Caulfield / 霍尔顿]]'s sensitivity, anger, tenderness, disgust, and loneliness are made perceptible.

The source's strongest interpretive frame is [[AmericanJiaBaoyuComparison]]. It places Holden beside [[JiaBaoyu|贾宝玉]] from [[HongLouMeng|《红楼梦》]]: both are privileged, bookish, school-resistant, affectionate toward sisters or girls nearby, disgusted by vulgarity, and unable to accept a world that converts feeling into status, exams, money, or social performance.

The title image gives the episode its central ethical reading. Holden tells [[PhoebeCaulfield|Phoebe / 菲比]] that he wants to catch children before they fall from a cliff at the edge of a field. The source treats this as a protective fantasy and a confession of danger at once: Holden wants to save other children from adult corruption while he is already close to collapse himself.

The reception branch is deliberately double. The novel is described as both a banned/required American school book and a youth-culture signifier; it is also linked to violent cases where readers or attackers invoked it. The episode rejects a causal reading that makes the book a murder manual, using [[AntiheroMisreading]] to separate textual tenderness from unstable or symbolic appropriation.
