# Mori Ogai / 森鸥外

Mori Ogai / 森鸥外 appears in [[shaqingqing-x-hanzhao-shichuan-zhuomu-wo-baochi-de-sixiang-doushi-yinwei-queqian-894931314]] as an established Meiji literary elder whose circle helps measure [[IshikawaTakuboku|石川啄木]]'s poetic talent. The episode notes that Takuboku could enter high-status literary occasions, including poetry gatherings connected to Mori, because contemporaries recognized his ability in verse.

Mori's role in this source is therefore two-sided. He represents the elite, educated literary order Takuboku did not fully belong to, but he also marks the fact that Takuboku's talent crossed some class and credential boundaries.

## Key Claims
- Mori helps locate Takuboku inside a Meiji literary hierarchy rather than outside literary society altogether.
- Recognition from higher-status writers makes Takuboku's poetic gift harder to dismiss as later legend.
- The contrast strengthens [[MeijiIntellectualPrecarity]] because talent could open doors without solving poverty, credentials, or work instability.
