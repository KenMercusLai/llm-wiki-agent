# Carmichael's Bookstore

Carmichael's Bookstore appears in [[bookstore-economics]] as the Louisville, Kentucky independent bookstore where [[FisherNash]] decides whether and how many copies of new titles to buy. The episode presents the store as a concrete setting for [[BookstoreBuying]] because its limited square footage, two-store operation, display table, bestseller shelf, and customer base all shape the buying decision.

The [[PlanetMoneyBook]] case makes Carmichael's a visible example of how local bookstore choices can amplify or suppress demand. Fisher's order moved from display quantity to 20 copies per store after sales-rep advice and the reporting visit changed the store's expectation of local interest.

## Key Claims
- The store's physical constraints make inventory discipline central; it cannot carry every title publishers offer.
- Display-table eligibility gives copy count a merchandising role, not just an inventory role.
- The store benefits from [[BookReturnability]], but unsold books still tie up space, attention, and freight cost.
- The source shows independent stores using local reader knowledge alongside data from catalogs, comps, and publisher reps.
