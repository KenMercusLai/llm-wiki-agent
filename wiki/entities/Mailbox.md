# Mailbox

Mailbox appears in [[tsr-s4-drewhouston-v5-tsr-s4-drewhouston-v5]] as the email product [[Dropbox]] acquired while trying to diversify beyond file storage. [[DrewHouston]] later describes killing Mailbox, [[Carousel]], and other non-productivity bets after [[GooglePhotos]] clarified how aggressively incumbents could clone, bundle, and give away adjacent products.

The source makes Mailbox part of Dropbox's strategic focus story. It is not presented as a standalone product postmortem; instead it marks a period where Dropbox had to decide whether broad usefulness was strength or strategic exposure.
