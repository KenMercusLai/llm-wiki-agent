# Yan Junjie

Yan Junjie, also referred to as I.O. in the source, is the founder and CEO of [[MiniMax]]. In [[duihua-minimax-yan-junjie-m3-10x-jihua-10t-moxing-he-zhineng-de-zhongju-lqtilt8flvmv99v0gshhyfyraibe]], he explains MiniMax's model progression, argues that AI's core value is productivity, and frames model progress, agents, domain experts, scaling, and interpretability as connected parts of the same intelligence problem.

## Key Views
- AI coding should expand production capacity and make more people productive, not be reduced to a simple headcount story.
- M1, M2/M2.7, and [[MiniMaxM3]] show how model capability, token usage, coding feedback, and product expectations interact.
- [[ModelHarnessCoEvolution]] matters because agents and harnesses are not merely downstream from models; real workflows also shape model requirements.
- [[FrontierModelScaling]] is not blocked by one magic obstacle, but by time, compute, architecture, training efficiency, and data limits.
- [[DomainExpertAlignment]] is needed when AI moves into safety, finance, law, and other areas where domain judgment cannot come only from researchers or engineers.
- [[AIInterpretabilityByAI]] is a long-term safety question: stronger AI may be needed to help humans understand AI itself.
