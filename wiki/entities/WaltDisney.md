# Walt Disney

Walt Disney is the founder and creative force at the center of [[the-walt-disney-company-1]]. The episode follows him from early commercial art and failed Kansas City animation ventures to [[TheWaltDisneyCompany]], [[MickeyMouse]], [[SnowWhiteAndTheSevenDwarfs]], [[Disneyland]], and the unrealized Florida/Epcot vision.

The source portrays Walt as a founder whose strength was not simply drawing or storytelling, but the ability to connect art, technology, distribution, and audience emotion into new formats. His repeated willingness to risk the company created [[ArtCommerceIntegration]] and the [[EntertainmentIPFlywheel]], but it also made [[RoyDisney]]'s financial discipline essential.

[[107-xianliao-weida-daoyanmen-de-bagua-di-yi-dan-744150818]] adds a moral-biographical counterpoint to the business-history branch. The [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode contrasts Disney's friendly "Uncle Walt" persona with labor conflict, animator fear, personal-brand absorption of collaborators' work, rumors and urban legends, and political controversy. In the wiki, this connects Walt to [[DirectorMythDeflation]], [[FilmSetPowerAndAbuse]], and [[ArtisticAchievementMoralAccountability]] without replacing the existing [[EntertainmentIPFlywheel]] account.
