# Adidas

Adidas is a global sportswear company discussed in [[e241-paoxie-jishu-diedaishi-malasong-paojin-2-xiaoshi-kao-ren-haishi-kao-xie]] through [[AdidasUltraBoost]], Adizero Evo SL, and [[AdidasAdiosProEvo3]]. The source frames Adidas as the brand that turned both comfort/lifestyle running shoes and elite marathon racing shoes into commercial narratives.

[[vol-264-ba-shijiebei-zuowei-fangfa-999416048]] adds Adidas as a [[FIFAWorldCup]] marketing case in China. The episode says Adidas moved beyond product launches and store dressing into more than 40 offline activities, including a large Shanghai football event, while also showing local marketing autonomy through a meme-responsive T-shirt and Tencent-linked variety content.

## Key Claims
- [[AdidasUltraBoost]] is treated as an era-defining commercial product because it brought TPU supercritical-style foam into mainstream running and lifestyle use.
- [[AdidasAdiosProEvo3]] is presented as a lightweight, sellable, elite-racing product tied to the episode's reported sub-2 marathon result.
- The episode says Adidas' Pro Evo line may use scarcity and high price not only because of yield and cost, but also to pull attention toward lower-tier mass-market versions.
- The World Cup source adds a brand-activation layer: Adidas uses event space, local memes, and football lifestyle to turn sponsorship into [[OfflineBrandActivation]].
- Adizero Evo SL is used as an example of racing aesthetics moving into broader fashion and lifestyle consumption.
