# Clark Ashton Smith / C.A.史密斯

Clark Ashton Smith is the central writer introduced in [[73-xujing-qitan-kongbu-youmo-zuihaode-kesulu-675865061]], where [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] presents him as a major early figure in [[CthulhuMythos|Cthulhu Mythos / 克苏鲁神话]] and [[WeirdFiction|weird fiction]]. The episode repeatedly contrasts him with [[HPLovecraft|H. P. Lovecraft / 洛夫克拉夫特]]: Lovecraft is framed as the origin point of the mythos, while Smith is framed as the more poetic, legendary, sensuous, and blackly comic stylist.

The episode's Smith is not only a horror writer. He is treated as a poet and visual artist whose prose depends on rare words, invented antiquity, dreamlike images, and ornate translation difficulty. [[XujingQitan|《虚境奇谭》]] becomes the episode's route into his style, especially through [[TheVaultsOfYohVombis|《深谷住民》]], [[SevenGeases|《七咒赋》]], [[TheDoorToSaturn|《通往土星之门》]], and [[TheJourneyToSfanomoe|《前往斯法诺莫埃的旅途》]].

## Key Claims
- Smith's "Cthulhu feeling" comes from language, antiquity, scale, and [[AntiAnthropocentricSatire]] as much as from monsters.
- The episode treats him as part of an early mythos triad with [[HPLovecraft]] and [[AugustDerleth]].
- His stories often place greedy, arrogant, rationalist, or doctrinally certain humans inside worlds where older powers have their own priorities.
- His comic scenes do not cancel horror; they make human self-importance look smaller.
- His major story worlds are tracked by [[SmithStoryWorlds]], especially Hyperborea, Averoigne, Zothique, and Poseidonis.
