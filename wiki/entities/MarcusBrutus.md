# Marcus Brutus / 马库斯·布鲁图斯

Marcus Brutus / 马库斯·布鲁图斯 is the conspirator whose name gives [[CaesarAssassination|Caesar's assassination]] its strongest republican symbolism in [[124-kaisa-zhisi-lishi-de-yunjiao-shifou-yi-shixiao-781894361]]. The episode notes the claimed descent from the older Brutus who expelled Rome's last king, making him valuable to conspirators who needed the killing to look like defense of the [[RomanRepublic|Roman Republic]] rather than private murder.

The source keeps Brutus morally complicated. [[JuliusCaesar|Caesar]] had pardoned him after civil war and promoted him, so his participation could look like betrayal. At the same time, Brutus's refusal to kill [[MarkAntony]] and other Caesar allies shows his desire to frame the act as lawful tyrannicide rather than purge or coup.

That moral boundary became a strategic weakness. By preserving the conspirators' justice narrative, Brutus also left Caesar's friends, soldiers, will, and heir with enough room to defeat the republican cause.

[[125-mala-zhisi-beibi-de-ansha-huo-zhengyi-de-chujue-783972516]] uses Brutus as the classical comparison for [[CharlotteCorday|Charlotte Corday]]. The point is not that the two acts are identical, but that both show how assassination can dramatize republican refusal while failing to restore the political order the assassin hopes to defend.
