# 连阔如 / Lian Kuoru

连阔如 is the storyteller and author whose [[JiangHuCongTan|《江湖丛谈》]] grounds [[102-jianghu-congtan-pianshu-heihua-he-minjian-daoyi-736516488]]. The episode presents him as someone whose authority came from crossing several marginal and performance worlds: a fallen banner-family background, early apprenticeships, small trade, fortune-telling, pingshu training, theater networks, and wartime broadcasting.

The source emphasizes that Lian's writing mattered because he disclosed insider knowledge. By publishing [[ChunDianArgot|春点]] terms, trade rules, scam routines, and lodging customs, he made [[StreetJianghuSociety]] more visible to outsiders. The episode treats that act as risky and socially useful: it could anger people who depended on secrecy, but it also helped ordinary readers recognize predatory routines.

## Key Claims
- Lian's life experience makes him a bridge between quyi performance, street livelihood, and documentary writing.
- His anti-Japanese wartime broadcasting story gives the episode a moral frame: he is presented as a performer who could resist propaganda through historical storytelling.
- His value to the wiki is not only biographical; he is a source node for [[ChunDianArgot]], [[JianghuScamCraft]], and [[InformalJianghuOrder]].
