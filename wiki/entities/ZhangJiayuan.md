# Zhang Jiayuan

Zhang Jiayuan is the founder of [[MultiCard]] and a roundtable guest in [[duihua-minimax-yan-junjie-m3-10x-jihua-10t-moxing-he-zhineng-de-zhongju-lqtilt8flvmv99v0gshhyfyraibe]]. He discusses model orchestration, AI coding cost, the commoditization of code generation, and the importance of product and engineering taste when many people can now build quickly.

## Key Views
- Not every workflow needs the strongest model; models can be assigned different coding, review, and mentoring roles.
- [[MiniMaxM3]] can be useful as a coding model, but its output still needs review and judgment.
- AI coding is becoming commodity-like, so deciding what not to do becomes more important.
- Product managers have long practiced a version of vibe coding through programmers, but AI changes the speed and accessibility of that loop.
- Core maintainers must define roadmap, taste, context, and boundaries for external contributors.
- He accepts that AI may eventually exceed humans in many capabilities, but warns against outsourcing the most important thinking to AI.
