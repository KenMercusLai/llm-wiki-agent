# Flood v. Kuhn

Flood v. Kuhn appears in [[how-we-got-free-agents-in-baseball]] as the [[SupremeCourt|Supreme Court]] case where [[CurtFlood|Curt Flood]] challenged [[MajorLeagueBaseball|Major League Baseball]]'s [[BaseballReserveClause|reserve clause]]. The source says Flood's lawyer framed the reserve clause as a group boycott, blacklist, and restraint of trade.

The Court ruled against Flood, saying that any inconsistency in baseball's antitrust status should be fixed by [[UnitedStatesCongress|Congress]] rather than the Court. In the episode's interpretation, the case still mattered because it made [[PublicOpinionLitigationStrategy|public opinion]] and player leverage stronger even though it preserved the [[BaseballAntitrustExemption|baseball antitrust exemption]].
