# Odysseus

Odysseus is the central hero of [[TheOdyssey]] and the focal character in [[in-it-to-bin-it-nigel-farage-v-count-binface-6a58abcdc152a357db17c20e]]'s critique of [[ChristopherNolan]]'s adaptation. The source says Nolan's film turns him from a morally difficult ancient hero into a more straightforward modern good person.

The episode uses that change to define the problem of [[HomericAdaptationModernization]]. Modern viewers may want psychological explanation, trauma framing, and moral simplicity, but the ancient figure's strangeness is part of what makes the epic hard to adapt without flattening it.

[[ep269-cong-manwei-yuzhou-dao-aodesai-weihe-zongyao-fanpai-yingxiong-shishi-ltklotheqpup6oamyflfzpxtipsw]] adds a homecoming and recognition layer. [[LiuChang|刘畅]] stresses that Odysseus is not simply pursuing indefinite adventure; his goal is fixed, and the return matters because Ithaca is the social world where his identity, household order, and [[AncientGreekHonorCommunity|honor]] can be recognized.
