# Kaiden Booth

Kaiden Booth appears in [[do-prediction-market-bettors-make-anything-better]] as a [[Kalshi]] trader who flew to San Francisco to listen outside a stadium rehearsal and time Charlie Puth's national anthem performance before the Super Bowl.

His example is one of the episode's clearest cases of [[PredictionMarketTraderAlpha]] as costly, physical, niche information gathering. It shows how prediction markets can reward unusual effort while also raising the question of whether this kind of edge improves public knowledge or mainly redirects attention toward tradable settlement details.

## Connections
- [[Kalshi]] - platform where the episode places the trade.
- [[PredictionMarketTraderAlpha]] - main concept his example supports.
- [[PredictionMarketPublicGoodClaim]] and [[PredictionMarketEthics]] - public-value and incentive questions raised by the example.
