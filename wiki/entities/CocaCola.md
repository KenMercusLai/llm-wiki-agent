# Coca-Cola

Coca-Cola appears in [[ep80-yu-chali-mangge-de-kuashikong-duihua-dang-yanjing-shiming-shi-women-kanjian-shenme-lh4q7vpcfqoynssw23dbwcerkn7i]] as the long-duration consumer-habit example in the [[CharlieMunger]] and [[WarrenBuffett]] case cluster. The episode frames Coca-Cola as a business where human desire, distribution, sugar, caffeine, memory, and brand familiarity make future demand easier to reason about than many complex trend forecasts.

## Source Position
- The episode says Coca-Cola taught the investors to see stability across time rather than chase every market movement.
- The core asset is not only the beverage formula, but the repeated consumer habit and global familiarity around it.
- The case extends the See's Candies and American Express lessons from gift trust and payment trust into everyday consumption.
