# 林垚 / Lin Yao

[[sp-05-liuqing-linyao-qiangsha-yibao-jutou-an-beihou-de-sikao-xia-fenlie-de-meiguo-hui-you-xianzheng-weiji-ma-790241978]] extends Lin Yao's contribution from health-insurance legal analysis into constitutional and movement politics. He argues that [[DonaldTrump]]'s 2024 win should not be mistaken for an overwhelming [[MAGA]] mandate, uses H-1B conflict and [[ElonMusk]] to show [[MAGACoalitionFracture]], and explains why [[USConstitutionalReformConstraint]], [[PresidentialSystem|presidentialism]], and weakened state experimentation make U.S. reform difficult.

林垚 / Lin Yao appears in [[sp-04-liuqing-linyao-qiangsha-yibao-jutou-an-beihou-de-sikao-shang-guanyu-mincui-sixing-he-sixing-790230209]] as a Shanghai New York University assistant professor of politics. The source uses his long U.S. living experience and his 2018 family car-accident billing story to make [[USHealthInsuranceDenialPolitics|U.S. health-insurance dysfunction]] concrete.

Lin's main contribution is institutional and legal. He explains how hospitals, physicians' groups, ambulance providers, insurers, employer coverage, network status, billing errors, and debt collection can fragment a single accident into years of dispute. He also links health-care reform difficulty to [[USFederalismVetoPoints|federalism and veto points]], and later analyzes why the [[LuigiCaseSuspect|Luigi]] case may differ under New York and federal criminal law.
