# Falcon 9

Falcon 9 is the reusable launch vehicle at the center of [[145-koushu-spacex-kaifashi-he-qiangaoguan-honglide-liao-masike-yongrenguan-zuida-ipo-taikong-yu-ai-renlei-wenming-kuozhang-qianzou]]. [[LouisHong]] treats its 2015 landing and later repeated missions as the real industry turning point because it changed the cost and reliability assumptions behind commercial space.

The episode presents Falcon 9 as aerospace's Model T: not the final vehicle, but the first system to make mass production, repeated operations, and lower-cost access to orbit look achievable. Its accidents in 2015 and 2016 also serve as a source case for [[HighResponsibilityDensity]], because Louis's departments were connected to the failures and the organization had to reason through unknown edge conditions under intense pressure.

[[ruhe-douzhu-yike-huojian-s10e21-b66fdf0f-d428-4f0c-8412-b0c7581132d0]] uses Falcon 9 as the mature landing-leg route in [[RocketRecoveryRouteChoice]]. The source emphasizes that this route has strong validation across land and sea platforms, but it carries rocket-side landing hardware and still demands precise terminal control.

[[tsr-ycoffsite-andylapsa-v1-audio-tsr-ycoffsite-andylapsa-v1-audio]] uses Falcon 9 as the partial-reuse proof point behind [[ReusableRocketEconomics]]. [[AndyLapsa]] says first-stage reuse let [[SpaceX]] raise flight rate and redirect manufacturing capacity, but the still-expendable upper stage remains a [[SecondStageReuseConstraint]].
