# 《魔鬼出没的世界》 / The Demon-Haunted World

《魔鬼出没的世界》 / The Demon-Haunted World is the [[CarlSagan|Carl Sagan]] book discussed in [[177-xiaoshihou-de-shu-zenme-jiu-name-haokan-971996036]] as the adult answer to an environment where scientific curiosity and pseudoscience were often mixed together. The episode names it through [[Beimin|北明]]'s translation/publication connection and uses it to frame skepticism as part of preserving wonder.

In this source, the book's wiki role is not a full chapter summary. It is a durable anchor for the claim that public science education has to fight both ignorance and credulous enchantment: UFO claims, qigong, crystal skulls, apocalyptic alignments, and "unsolved mystery" culture can feed curiosity while also weakening evidence discipline.

[[185-mogui-chumo-de-shijie-guanyu-yinmoulun-ufo-waixingren-bangjia-an-maitianquan-x-dangan-jiemi-ri-996181368]] turns the book into the episode's main structure. The discussion moves from [[UFOConspiracyCulture]], [[RoswellIncident]], [[Area51]], [[Majestic12Documents]], [[AlienAbductionNarrative]], and [[CropCircleDebunking]] into Sagan's tools for evidence discipline: [[ScientificSkepticism]], [[AppealToIgnorance]], [[GarageDragonTest]], [[EvidenceOverTestimony]], and [[ScientificSelfCorrection]].

## Key Claims
- The book is presented as a tool for distinguishing scientific wonder from unsupported claims.
- Its relevance emerges from childhood memory: the same generation that absorbed science optimism also absorbed mass-market pseudoscience.
- The episode connects the book to [[ScienceOptimismAndPseudoscience]] rather than treating skepticism as a purely abstract epistemology.
- Episode 185 adds the book's social warning: skepticism is harder than mystery in media environments, but becomes more urgent under [[AIInformationPollution]].
