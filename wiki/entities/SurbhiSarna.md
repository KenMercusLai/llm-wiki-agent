# Surbhi Sarna

Surbhi Sarna is the [[YCombinator]] partner and former founder and CEO of [[NVisionMedical|nVision Medical]] interviewed in [[tsr-s4-surbhisarna-v3-tsr-s4-surbhisarna-v3]]. The episode traces her path from a teenage ovarian-cyst scare into [[WomensHealthDiagnosticGap]], [[OvarianCancerDiagnostics]], and a medical-device company built around reaching the fallopian tube without surgery.

The source makes Sarna a strong [[FounderProductFit]] case, but not because personal pain alone was enough. She combined the medical scare with science reading, prior medtech work, physician conversations, regulatory advice from [[CindyDAmicus|Cindy D'Amicus]], and experienced technical hiring before [[NVisionMedical|nVision Medical]] moved through [[MedicalDeviceClinicalValidation]] and FDA clearance.

The fundraising thread makes Sarna a [[SoloFounderFundraisingBias]] and [[FounderResilience]] case. She says it took roughly a year and a half to raise the first $250,000, that investors did not yet see women's health as a major category, and that she had to make personal burn reductions credible enough for investors to underwrite the first prototype path.

After selling to [[BostonScientific]], Sarna stayed involved with commercialization and later connected the experience to her work helping underrated founders at [[YCombinator]]. [[WithoutADoubt]] is the memoir branch of the same story: in the episode, Sarna says she was not without doubt about every decision, only about patient need and her desire to work on it.
