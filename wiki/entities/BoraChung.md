# 郑宝拉 / Bora Chung

郑宝拉 / Bora Chung is the Korean writer centered in [[117-zuzhoutu-guaitan-wenxue-ye-yao-shangzhuo-chifan-766574341]]. The source presents her as a 1976-born author whose collection [[CursedBunny|《诅咒兔》]] entered the 2022 Booker International discussion, while also noting her work as a translator and researcher of Russian and Polish literature.

The episode's reading of Chung stresses force over obscurity. Her stories are not valued because they hide meaning behind difficult allegory; they are valued because a head in a toilet, a cute cursed lamp, a marrow-sucking creature, or a gold-bleeding fox can make pressure and harm legible with very little insulation.

In this wiki branch, Chung links [[GuaitanLiterature|怪谈文学]] to [[GenderedDomesticHorror]], [[PostRevengeEmptiness]], and [[GenreLiteraryLegitimacy]]. The source keeps the Booker-style frame of patriarchy and capitalism, but argues that the deeper affect is often loneliness after revenge rather than clean social critique.
