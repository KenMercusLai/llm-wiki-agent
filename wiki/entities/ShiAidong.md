# 施爱东 / Shi Aidong

施爱东 / Shi Aidong is the folklore scholar recommended at the end of [[127-xianliao-shier-shengxiao-zhi-long-shenqi-dongwu-zai-nali-788239655]]. The episode identifies him as the author of [[TheInventionOfChineseDragon|《中国龙的发明：近现代中国形象的域外变迁》]], the book that anchors the source's argument about the modern transformation of dragon imagery.

For the wiki, Shi's role is methodological. He lets the episode treat [[ChineseDragon|龙]] as a historically changing image rather than a timeless ethnic essence. His book is used to connect missionaries, European China-fashion and later anti-China caricature, the [[QingDragonFlag]], totemist theory, nationalist pressure, and popular music into one [[DragonNationalSymbolInvention]] sequence.

## Key Claims
- Shi Aidong is used as the source's main scholarly reference for modern Chinese dragon-image history.
- The episode presents his work as especially useful because it crosses folklore, image circulation, translation, and nationalism.
- His book keeps the dragon-symbol question historical: a symbol can feel ancient while its national meaning is partly modern.
