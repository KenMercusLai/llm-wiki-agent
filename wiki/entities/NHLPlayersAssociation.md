# NHL Players' Association / NHLPA

The NHL Players' Association appears in [[106-bie-zai-haipa-chongtu-kuai-lai-xuexi-tanpan-shu-742539645]] as the player-side union in the 2004 [[NationalHockeyLeague|National Hockey League / NHL]] labor-dispute case. The episode presents the union's resistance to a salary cap as an example of how a loss frame can harden negotiation.

The source does not use the NHLPA to deny player interests. Instead, it treats the dispute as a case in [[SportsCollectiveBargaining]] where [[ZeroSumBias]], [[FramingEffect]], and [[EscalationOfCommitment]] made both sides lose a season before a lower cap was accepted.
