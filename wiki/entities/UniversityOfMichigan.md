# University of Michigan

The University of Michigan appears in [[indicators-of-2025-and-what-to-watch-in-2026]] through the consumer sentiment index cited by [[KennyMalone]]. The source says the index was around 100 before the pandemic, in the 70s the previous year, and in the 50s in 2025.

The institution's role in this wiki branch is methodological: the index gives [[ConsumerSentimentIndicator]] a concrete measurement hook while still leaving room for [[AggregateIndicatorsLivedExperienceGap]], because a sentiment reading captures mood, expectations, and affordability anxiety differently from GDP or labor-market statistics.
