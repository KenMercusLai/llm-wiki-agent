# 《聊斋志异》

《聊斋志异》 is the ghost-story collection used in [[136-chunri-mingmei-liaoliao-guishen-827911486]] to show how underworld offices become literary situations. The episode mentions stories where a judge swaps a woman's head and where Song Tao is examined for a city-god appointment but delays taking office so he can care for his mother.

In the source, these stories matter less as supernatural facts than as moral and social tests. They make [[UnderworldBureaucracy]] legible by turning divine offices into exams, appointments, favors, kinship obligations, and uncomfortable human choices. They also connect folk religion to literary satire: a ghost court can expose worldly assumptions about officialdom, filial piety, gender, and personal relationships.

[[148-wangshi-zhisi-duanjing-tuiyuan-shang-de-lishi-zhuangjing-892588934]] adds a second role for the book through [[JonathanSpence|史景迁]]'s [[TheDeathOfWomanWang|《王氏之死》]]. The episode treats [[PuSongling|蒲松龄]]'s stories as [[FictionAsHistoricalEvidence]] for the world around [[WangShiTancheng|王氏]]: hunger, wife sale, bandit households, 《促织》-style extraction, women managers, widows, fox or ghost women, and revenge fantasies all reveal fears and desires that official records leave thin.
