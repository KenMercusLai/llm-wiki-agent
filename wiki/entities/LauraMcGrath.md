# Laura McGrath

Laura McGrath appears in [[how-to-make-a-book-into-a-bestseller]] as a [[TempleUniversity]] professor who teaches the history of the bestseller and explains why the [[NewYorkTimesBestsellerList|New York Times bestseller list]] carries special weight for publishers, readers, and writers.

Her role is to turn the list from a prestige label into an economic institution. She explains [[BestsellerListOpacity]], historical attempts to game the list, the role of pre-orders, and why [[BulkBookBuying]] and [[BookLaundering]] matter especially in nonfiction.

## Key Claims
- The New York Times list is treated as more important than other bestseller lists by many publishers, readers, and writers.
- Bestseller placement can generate new demand through store placement, online visibility, advertising language, and status.
- People have tried to influence the list for as long as the list has existed.
- Secrecy about reporting stores and weighting makes gaming harder but also creates a strategy problem for authors and publishers.
- Nonfiction authors can have strong incentives to seek bestseller status because it can raise speaking and consulting value.
