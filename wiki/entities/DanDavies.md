# Dan Davies

Dan Davies is cited in [[eric-ries-incorruptible-by-design-wrgromn5peq]] through the idea of [[AccountabilitySinks]]. The host uses Davies's frame to describe complex systems that produce outcomes nobody wants while leaving no obvious person or institution accountable for changing them.

## Key Claims
- Davies's accountability-sink idea helps explain why institutional harm can persist without needing a single villain.
- In Ries's response, the concept becomes a prompt for designing better [[StartupGovernance]], ownership, standards, and accountability mechanisms.
- The frame complements [[FinancialGravity]] because both move attention from isolated bad actors toward repeatable system pressures.
