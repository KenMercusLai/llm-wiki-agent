# Winthrop Kellogg / 凯洛格

Winthrop Kellogg appears in [[68-fengkuang-shiyan-shi-ai-zhe-gaisi-de-qiuzhiyu-664384549]] through the chimpanzee-child co-rearing experiment. The source says Kellogg raised the chimpanzee Gua and his son Donald under similar conditions to test learning and adaptation, then stopped after Donald's language development lagged and he imitated Gua's vocalizations.

The episode treats the case as scientifically memorable but ethically troubling. It belongs to both [[AnimalExperimentEthics]] and [[ChildExperimentEthics]]: Gua was removed from and returned to chimpanzee social life, while Donald became a developmental subject before he could consent. The source avoids claiming a simple causal explanation for Donald's later suicide.
