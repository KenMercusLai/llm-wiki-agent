# Sha Qingqing / 沙青青

Sha Qingqing / 沙青青 appears in [[shaqingqing-x-hanzhao-shichuan-zhuomu-wo-baochi-de-sixiang-doushi-yinwei-queqian-894931314]] as the translator of [[TakubokuBiography|唐纳德·基恩《石川啄木》]]. He explicitly presents himself as approaching [[IshikawaTakuboku|石川啄木]] through biography, historical context, diaries, and literary networks rather than as a specialist in poetry close reading.

His translation work matters because [[DonaldKeene|Donald Keene / 唐纳德·基恩]] wrote in English while relying heavily on Japanese names, places, diaries, and late-Meiji literary history. The episode says Sha had to check Japanese materials repeatedly, which turns the biography into a bridge between [[ModernJapaneseInteriorDiscovery]] and [[MeijiIntellectualPrecarity]].

## Key Claims
- Sha's main contribution is contextual: he restores Takuboku to family decline, credentials, poverty, friendship, marriage, and political pressure.
- He treats Takuboku's diary-based self-exposure as evidence for literary modernity rather than only as embarrassing gossip.
- He helps separate source-grounded biography from loose moral caricature, making him useful to [[LiteraryGossipAsContext]].
