# 苏轼 / Su Shi / Su Dongpo

Su Shi, also known as Su Dongpo, is the central figure of [[105-su-dongpo-yiqiang-haomai-zhiqi-yi-dupi-buhe-shiyi-741072645]]. The episode treats him as a cultural icon whose poetry, prose, food stories, and personality are familiar, then deliberately restores the less polished person underneath: talented, funny, impulsive, stubborn, frightened in danger, and still unwilling to betray his conscience.

The source reads his life as a sequence of pressures. [[ChengFuren|程夫人]] and [[SuXun|苏洵]] give him family and moral formation; [[OuyangXiu|欧阳修]] recognizes his literary talent; [[WangAnshi|王安石]], the [[WangAnshiReforms|王安石变法]], and [[SongFactionalPolitics|宋代党争]] make public speech costly; the [[WutaiPoetryCase|乌台诗案]] turns writing into criminal evidence; and exile in [[Huangzhou|黄州]], [[Huizhou|惠州]], and [[Danzhou|儋州]] tests whether his cheerfulness can survive real loss.

The episode's strongest claim is that Su Shi's greatness is cumulative. [[DingFengBoSuShi|《定风波》]], [[RedCliffFu|《赤壁赋》]], [[NianNuJiaoChibiHuaiGu|《念奴娇·赤壁怀古》]], [[DongpoRou|东坡肉]], [[SuCauseway|苏堤]], and his public works in [[Hangzhou|杭州]] and [[Xuzhou|徐州]] belong to one figure: a writer-official whose [[EarnedOptimism]] is inseparable from [[ScholarOfficialPublicDuty]] and [[ConscienceOverFaction]].

[[114-youdao-chi-ao-shangju-shi-liaoliao-pangxie-de-quwen-shihua-758424117]] adds a smaller food-literary cameo. The episode uses Su Shi's crab-related poetic and anecdotal afterlife, including "一蟹不如一蟹", to show how Song literati taste, joking, seasonal eating, and proverb-making help turn [[Crab|螃蟹]] into [[CrabCulturalSymbolism]].
