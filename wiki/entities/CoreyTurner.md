# Corey Turner

Corey Turner is the [[NPR]] education correspondent reporting in [[can-the-trump-administration-make-college-cheaper]]. The [[PlanetMoney]] episode uses his reporting to explain the Trump administration's graduate-loan caps, the [[BennettHypothesis]], and the uncertain evidence on whether federal aid raises tuition.

His role in the source is explanatory rather than biographical. He turns [[FederalStudentLoanCaps]] into a policy mechanism: fewer federal dollars may pressure schools to cut prices, but the same mechanism can also reduce enrollment, push borrowers toward private loans, or make students bear the first cost of the price signal.
