# Facebook

Facebook appears in [[tech-20260716-0716-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260716-0716-mp-tech-pod-128]] as one of the [[Meta]] platforms named in state attorneys general litigation over alleged youth addiction and hidden harms. The source pairs Facebook with [[Instagram]] as the product surface where state officials allege addictive features kept young users online longer.

The page's current role is narrow: Facebook is not treated as a separate company history here, but as part of [[SocialMediaProductLiability]] and [[StateAGPlatformLitigation]]. The episode links its legal exposure to design choices, children's privacy claims under [[COPPA]], internal-knowledge allegations, and the broader [[BigTobaccoPlatformAnalogy]].

[[e233-guigu-youyi-de-quanli-wang-ruhe-xingcheng-liaoliao-bide-tier-de-sixiang-qimeng-pintu-2a529abf-3c14-4ae7-a0a5-fe1340a66c7d]] adds an earlier business-strategy reading through [[PeterThiel]] and [[ReneGirard]]. The [[SiliconValley101]] episode uses Facebook as a [[MimeticCompetitionAvoidance]] and network-effect example: people join partly because other people are already there, making imitation a growth mechanism rather than only a rivalry problem.

[[tsr-s3-replit-v2-tsr-s3-replit-v2]] adds a developer-tools career chapter through [[AmjadMasad]]. After [[Codecademy]], Amjad joined Facebook for a deeper technical challenge and worked on the early [[ReactNative]] team. The source also says he considered turning [[Replit]] into a Facebook project, then rejected that path because he worried about losing ownership and company control.

[[can-transforming-neighborhoods-help-kids-escape-poverty]] adds a research-measurement role. The [[PlanetMoney]] episode says Facebook connection data helped show that children in successful [[HOPEVIProgram|HOPE VI]] sites were more connected to better-off children, supporting [[CrossClassSocialCapital]] as the mechanism behind [[NeighborhoodOpportunityRevitalization]] rather than architecture alone.
