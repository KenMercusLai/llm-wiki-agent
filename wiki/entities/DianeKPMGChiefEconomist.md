# Diane (KPMG Chief Economist)

The supplied source identifies this speaker only as Diane, [[KPMG]]'s chief economist. In [[would-you-trust-an-economist-with-your-economy]], she is the economist most directly associated with [[EconomicForecastingLimits]] and with the painful professional memory of missing the housing bubble and the 2008 financial crisis.

Her comments make the episode's trust problem practical rather than abstract. She says economists are often asked short-term questions their tools cannot answer cleanly, rejects conspiracy claims about [[BureauOfLaborStatistics]] data, and says distrust has pushed her to look beyond aggregate GDP and unemployment toward sector details, household costs, and conversations with people.
