# Helion

Helion appears in [[tsr-s4-samaltman-v4-tsr-s4-samaltman-v4]] as an example of [[SamAltman]]'s more active recruiting strategy when he became [[YCombinator]] president. Altman says he visited private fusion companies and thought Helion was by far the best, making it a concrete example of YC moving more deliberately toward ambitious hard-tech companies.

[[tsr-s5-davidkirtley-v2-audio-tsr-s5-davidkirtley-v2-audio]] makes Helion a full company-building case through founder and CEO [[DavidKirtley]]. Kirtley describes Helion's product as fusion generators for low-cost, safe, clean baseload electricity, using deuterium and helium-3 and a technical strategy built around [[FusionEnergyRecovery]]. The source adds [[HelionPolaris|Polaris]] as the seventh-generation machine, [[HelionOrion|Orion]] as the planned Microsoft-linked power plant, and the [[Nucor]] project as a behind-the-meter industrial-power path.

The episode also shifts Helion from a pure technical moonshot into a startup-operating case. [[YCombinator]] pushed the company toward [[DeepTechProductFocus]], investors such as [[MithrilCapital]] and [[CapricornInvestmentGroup]] had to underwrite long timelines and uncertainty, and Helion's work with regulators and precision manufacturing extends [[HardTechRegulatoryPathfinding]] and [[PrecisionManufacturingAsStrategy]]. Kirtley says success requires broad deployment, not only a historic fusion demonstration or one power plant.
