# Cardinal Tetra

The Cardinal Tetra is the small, bright aquarium fish at the center of [[the-little-pet-fish-that-saved-a-town-in-the-amazon]]. In the episode, the species is both a global pet-fish commodity and a civic emblem for [[Barcelos]], where wild collection once supported the local [[PiaberoEconomy]].

The fish also makes the source's conservation argument concrete. Researchers initially worried that millions of wild Cardinal Tetras were being overharvested, but the episode says later evidence showed the species was abundant and that many fish die naturally each dry season. That finding allowed [[ProjectPiaba]] to argue that [[SustainableWildHarvesting]] could protect flooded forest by keeping local income tied to intact habitat.

## Key Claims
- The episode describes the Cardinal Tetra as one of the world's most popular aquarium fish.
- It is adapted to the acidic dark-water environment of the [[RioNegro]].
- It became the symbol of [[Barcelos]] through banners, shirts, tanks, decorations, and festival performance.
- Farmed Cardinal Tetras from Florida and later Asia sharply weakened demand for wild Amazon fish.
- Its market history shows how biological specificity can become less defensible once technical reproduction is solved.
