# News Corp

News Corp enters the wiki through [[tech-20260306-0306-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260306-0306-mp-tech-pod-128]] as the media company signing a multiyear [[AIContentLicensing]] deal with [[Meta]]. The episode says the deal gives Meta access to News Corp's U.S. and U.K. content, including real-time information and archives that can support AI products and model training.

The source treats News Corp less as a media-brand case than as a publisher bargaining case. As AI answer systems reduce referral traffic to news sites, publishers face a choice between suing AI companies over content use or licensing content and taking revenue while they search for subscriber, attribution, promotion, and brand-visibility models.

[[tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3-tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3]] adds a different News Corp context through [[RupertMurdoch|Rupert Murdoch]] and the [[WallStreetJournal|Wall Street Journal]]'s [[Theranos]] investigation. The source says Murdoch was both the Journal's owner and a Theranos investor, but that [[JohnCarreyrou|John Carreyrou]]'s reporting continued. That makes News Corp part of a [[MediaOwnershipIndependenceRisk]] branch as well as an AI-content-licensing branch.
