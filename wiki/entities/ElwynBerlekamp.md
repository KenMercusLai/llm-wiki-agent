# Elwyn Berlekamp

Elwyn Berlekamp appears in [[vol-103-wenyi-fuxing-keji-ximengsi-de-fengshen-zhilu-shi-lianghua-zhiwang-gengshi-dongcha-renxing-de-dashi-lulzvnaxdb4klqag-p2yyqlmiikl]] as the figure who pushed [[RenaissanceTechnologies]] and the [[MedallionFund]] toward shorter-horizon, repeated trading. The episode emphasizes that this was not today's millisecond high-frequency trading; it meant trades held for hours or days, executed often enough for small edges to compound.

His contribution links [[KellyCriterion]] to implementation. Betting size, certainty, transaction costs, margin, latency, and market impact all had to be modeled together, which made [[ShortTermStatisticalArbitrage]] a full operating system rather than a list of signals.
