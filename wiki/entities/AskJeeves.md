# Ask Jeeves

Ask Jeeves appears in [[tsr-s4-ronconway-v4-tsr-s4-ronconway-v4]] as one of [[RonConway]] and [[BenRosen]]'s early internet-software investments. Conway says Rosen found the company, framed it as search with a good user interface, and that they quickly visited the Berkeley team to help with action items and hiring needs.

In this source, Ask Jeeves is a [[ConversationalSearchInterface]] case. It made search feel like asking a question and became a major early win for Conway and Rosen, helping seed early SV Angel funds before the later [[Google]] investment.

[[tsr-s4-ronconway-part3-v2-tsr-s4-ronconway-part3-v2]] later uses Ask Jeeves as one of the search companies competing before [[Google]] became dominant. Conway had prior context with Ask Jeeves and says the broader search market was crowded, strategically important, and still weak on relevance.

The source uses Ask Jeeves as part of the contrast that made [[PageRankSearchRelevance]] legible. Existing players had traffic, brands, and public-market attention, but Conway believed Google had a better answer to the ranking problem.
