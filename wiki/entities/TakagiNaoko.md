# 高木直子 / Takagi Naoko

高木直子 / Takagi Naoko is the Japanese illustrator and comic essayist discussed in [[52-hao-ai-gaomu-zhizi-xiangei-zhengzai-dapin-de-ni-634050956]]. The [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode presents her as a creator whose appeal comes from ordinary life rather than spectacle: leaving a design-company job, moving toward Tokyo illustration work, burning through savings, calling publishers with fear, doing part-time jobs, and drawing the small humiliations and comforts of city life.

Her work matters in the wiki because it gives a concrete case for [[EverydayAutobiographicalComics]]. The episode treats her diary-like drawings as a way to make rented rooms, half-price food, commuting mistakes, job-search anxiety, loneliness, eating, marriage, motherhood, travel, and local [[Japan]] festivals available as shared experience. Her career also touches [[OrdinaryHardshipNarrative]] and [[CareerSelfRescue]]: the recovery is not framed as triumph over adversity, but as repeated attention to life until the life becomes drawable and publishable.
