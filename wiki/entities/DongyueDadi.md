# 东岳大帝

东岳大帝 is the Taishan-linked ruler of life and death discussed in [[136-chunri-mingmei-liaoliao-guishen-827911486]]. The episode presents him as older and more mountain-cosmological than the Fengdu or Yanluo frames, drawing on Taishan's sacred geography, imperial ritual status, and stories about the dead returning to or being governed through the eastern peak.

The figure helps the source show why Chinese afterlife belief resists neat hierarchy. [[FengduDadi|丰都大帝]], 东岳大帝, and [[YanluoWang|阎罗王]] can all appear as death authorities because they come from different textual, ritual, and local streams. The overlap itself becomes a case of [[ChineseFolkReligionLayering]] rather than a contradiction the episode tries to harmonize.
