# 吴敬梓

吴敬梓 is the author of [[RulinWaishi|《儒林外史》]], discussed in [[58-rulin-waishi-jiashe-kaogong-chengle-weiyi-chulu-645207859]] as a writer whose satire depends on observation, patience, and social placement rather than only anger. The episode notes that many figures in the novel can be connected to real-world prototypes, and it treats the work's long formation as part of why some characters shift or reappear in complicated ways.

The source reads Wu's ideal figure partly through [[DuShaoqing|杜少卿]]: generous, unwilling to flatter officials, emotionally sincere, and skeptical that fame, examination rank, or ritual correctness can measure a person. At the same time, Wu's method is broader than self-portrait. Through failed scholars like [[ZhouJin|周进]] and [[FanJin|范进]], predatory gentry, women such as Shen Qiongzhi, and ordinary artists, he turns personal experience into [[WanErDuoFeng|婉而多讽]] and [[SatiricalGroupPortrait]].
