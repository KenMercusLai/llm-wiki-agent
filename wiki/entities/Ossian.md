# Oisin / Ossian / 鹅相

Oisin / Ossian / 鹅相 appears in [[142-kaierte-shenhua-gushi-diyidan-deluyi-hui-mengjian-bianxing-yang-ma-865479403]] in two related ways. First, the episode uses him as the son of [[FionnMacCumhaill|Fionn mac Cumhaill / 芬恩]] and Sadhbh, a woman held in deer form by hostile druidic magic. Second, it invokes the later Ossian controversy around James Macpherson as part of the Celtic revival and Romantic-era rediscovery of national myth.

In the source's Fenian story, Oisin's birth makes [[CelticAnimalTransformation]] a kinship problem: the child is not simply magical; he is the trace left after Fionn's wife is re-enchanted and disappears. In the literary-history layer, "Ossian" becomes a signal of how modern Europe desired ancient national epics while arguing over authenticity, collection, translation, and invention.

## Key Claims
- Oisin links Fenian hero story, animal transformation, lost mother, and later literary revival.
- The source treats the Macpherson controversy as a caution around authenticity without reducing the whole Ossianic field to pure fabrication.
- Oisin/Ossian is useful for [[MythicSourceLayering]] because mythic figure, oral material, manuscript memory, and modern literary nationalism all gather under one name.
