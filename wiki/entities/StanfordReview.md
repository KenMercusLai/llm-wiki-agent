# Stanford Review

Stanford Review is the conservative student publication that [[e233-guigu-youyi-de-quanli-wang-ruhe-xingcheng-liaoliao-bide-tier-de-sixiang-qimeng-pintu-2a529abf-3c14-4ae7-a0a5-fe1340a66c7d]] presents as the starting point for [[PeterThiel]]'s political network. The episode says Thiel co-founded it as a [[StanfordUniversity]] undergraduate in 1987 with Norman Burke and used it to challenge campus progressivism and the Stanford Daily.

In the source, the publication matters less as a student newspaper than as a training ground. It combines anti-progressive argument, donor support, provocation, elite-school network formation, and later career ties to figures such as [[DavidSacks]], [[KeithRabois]], [[JoeLonsdale]], and [[StephenCohen]].

## Source Position
- The episode places Stanford Review inside [[CampusConservativeMediaNetwork]] rather than treating it as an isolated campus controversy.
- It is linked to [[OlinFoundation]]-style support for conservative student media.
- It becomes an early filter for people willing to oppose campus-progressive norms and join Thiel's wider network.
