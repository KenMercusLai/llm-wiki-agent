# White Snake Legend / 白蛇传

[[WhiteSnakeLegend|白蛇传]] is the main Chinese snake-woman case in [[128-xianliao-shier-shengxiao-zhi-she-cong-yemengjiade-dao-zhaolinger-797116305]]. The episode treats the legend as a forked tradition rather than one stable moral: one line remembers White Lady as an heroic, healing, emotionally serious figure, while another written and religious line makes the snake wife a seductive or dangerous being subdued by clerical authority.

The page matters to [[SnakeFemalePowerMotif]] because the source reads White Lady as more than a generic monster. She can carry love, courage, medicine, flood power, fertility, beauty, and defiance. At the same time, the story's monk, demon-detection, and containment motifs show how female snake power can be reclassified as danger when it exceeds social control.

## Key Claims
- White Snake material should be read as layered folklore, not one doctrinally fixed tale.
- The same snake-woman figure can be goddess-like, romantic, monstrous, eroticized, or punished depending on the version and social frame.
- Parallels with Baijie Furen, Russian snake-wife stories, and pop-cultural figures such as [[ZhaoLinger|赵灵儿]] are useful comparison prompts, but this source does not prove direct descent.
