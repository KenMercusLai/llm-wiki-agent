# Petaluma

Petaluma is the direct-to-consumer plant-based dog food company discussed in [[advice-line-with-jeffrey-hollender-of-seventh-generation-f8ce2410-1176-47d9-be44-03f9fa945714]]. [[CarolineBuck]] explains that the company makes oven-baked formulas from whole-food ingredients such as chickpeas, pumpkin, and peanut butter, with most sales online and a subscriber base as the core business.

## Key Points
- Petaluma's category is polarizing because some consumers react emotionally against plant-based dog food.
- The advice is to lead with dog health, function, and evidence rather than guilt or defensive equivalence.
- Guy suggests acknowledging skepticism directly, while Jeffrey pushes the company to claim superiority if the evidence supports it.
- Free samples, mix-in usage, and treats are treated as lower-friction entry paths.
