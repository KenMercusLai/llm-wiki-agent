# Peter Wason / 彼得·沃森

Peter Wason appears in [[132-zhenglun-yu-shuofu-women-weishenme-chaojia-zenme-cai-suan-ying-807583318]] through the number-rule experiment: people see a few confirming examples, infer a rule, and often stop searching for disconfirming evidence. The episode uses this as evidence that people tend to map small samples onto large realities too quickly.

The source's Wason thread supports [[PublicArgumentForBystanders|public argument]] and [[ConstructiveDissent|constructive dissent]]. Argument can enlarge the sample by adding facts, rival cases, and alternative hypotheses, but only if participants do not immediately assimilate every new example into the old belief.

## Connections
- [[CognitiveInertia]] - stopping the search early reduces mental cost.
- [[ObservationBeforeInference]] - methodological corrective to small-sample certainty.
- [[ConstructiveDissent]] - argument can help expose what the first sample missed.
- [[RationalHumility]] - confidence should fall when a rule has not been actively tested.
