# 《天龙八部》

《天龙八部》 is treated in [[112-xianliao-jinyong-diyidan-mingjiao-jingran-jintian-hai-cunzai-754593095]] as the effective beginning of the [[JinYong|金庸]] wuxia universe after the exceptional antiquity of [[YueNvJian|《越女剑》]]. The source uses it to explain why Song settings are especially fertile for Jianghu narrative.

The episode places the novel inside [[HistoricalJianghuSpace]]. Northern Song commercial life, population movement, urban associations, beggar organization, tattoo culture, and culinary markets all help make characters, sects, and unofficial power plausible. Qiao Feng's body, identity, and public fate therefore belong to a world where social mobility and ethnic boundary conflict coexist.

The hosts also argue that Qiao Feng's tragedy is not a strict historical inevitability. They note that Liao-Song relations could include diplomacy and peace, so the novel's suicide ending is read through [[JinYongPoliticalReading]] as a literary statement about binary identity tearing a person apart.
