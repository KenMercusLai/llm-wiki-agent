# Longma / 龙马

[[Longma|龙马]] is the dragon-horse figure used in [[171-xianliao-shier-shengxiao-zhi-ma-guanyin-dashi-de-xingqu-aihao-ji-laotouhuan-juese-de-linggan-959614962]] to connect [[Horse|马]] with Chinese origin symbolism. The episode retells the motif of a dragon-horse emerging from the Yellow River with the Hetu pattern on its back, inspiring Fuxi's trigrams and linking horse imagery to cosmology, order, and civilizational beginning.

The page records the episode's use of Longma as a hybrid. Its power comes from crossing animal categories: horse speed and dragon numinosity combine into a sign of legitimacy, pattern, and extraordinary vitality.

## Key Claims
- Longma turns horse imagery into an omen of cosmic and social order.
- The dragon-horse hybrid supports the episode's broader claim that cultures attach supernatural explanation to horse speed and force.
- The source treats Longma as part of [[HorseReligiousMythology]] and [[HorseCulturalSymbolism]], not as ordinary animal history.
