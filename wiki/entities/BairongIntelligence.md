# Bairong Intelligence

Bairong Intelligence is the enterprise AI company discussed in [[weishenme-gongsi-yong-buhao-ai-cong-jiaolv-dao-xingdong-de-3-ge-guanjian-dongzuo-duitan-bairong-zhineng-zhang-shaofeng-lgarngnaqran2c9p4jssurvt6ces]] and [[e225-saasye-shuqianyi-shizhi-zhengfa-ai-ruhe-biange-zuzhi-jiagou]]. Chair and CEO [[ZhangShaofeng]] presents the company as both a user and provider of [[DigitalEmployees]], with work spanning customer service and marketing, approval, risk control, HR, reimbursement, legal review, recruiting, and professional-service workflows.

## Position In The Episode
- The source says Bairong had about 3 billion RMB revenue in 2024, profit around 400-500 million RMB, and a team of roughly 1600-1700 people, with close to 1200 R&D engineers.
- Bairong uses a "standard person" benchmark to compare digital-employee output with human output rather than only counting agent instances.
- Its internal rollout emphasizes existing workflow simulation, compliance boundaries, employee incentives, and API access to business systems.
- The company sells AI work through [[OutcomeBasedAIPricing]] patterns such as standard-employee output, work volume, hours, or transaction-linked fees.
- Bairong's [[Baijian]] platform extends its agent approach into legal, consulting, tax, finance, and globalization-related professional services.
- The E225 [[SiliconValley101]] source says Bairong manages more than 200,000 silicon-based employees across roughly 200 AI roles, using [[SiliconCarbonGovernance]] as its internal organization frame.
- Bairong describes three commercial modes in E225: [[AIStaffing]], AI BPO through [[AIBPORollUp]], and an [[EnterpriseAgentStore]] platform for partner-built agents.
- Its customer-service case shows 50 human service staff replaced in the workflow by 18 AI employees plus 5 human partners, with the remaining employees retrained to build and deliver agents for other clients.
