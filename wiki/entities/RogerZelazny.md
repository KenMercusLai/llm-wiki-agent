# Roger Zelazny / 罗杰·泽拉兹尼

Roger Zelazny / 罗杰·泽拉兹尼 is the science-fiction and fantasy writer previewed in [[jiaqi-moyu-geng-jiankang-574391976]] and then discussed at length in [[sp-03-chuantai-chen-shengming-qixi-douliu-zela-zini-yu-tade-mengjing-757948106]]. [[Beimin|北明 / 北民]] first mentions having translated a Zelazny short-story collection; the later source turns that preview into a full reading of [[ForABreathITarry|《趁生命气息逗留》 / For a Breath I Tarry]].

The later episode presents Zelazny as unusually strong in both science fiction and fantasy. It places him inside [[ScienceFictionNewWave|New Wave science fiction]] but argues that his experimentation is not empty negation: the source emphasizes a humanistic core of struggle, dignity, awe, and resistance. Zelazny's signature in this wiki is now [[MythicScienceFictionStyle]]: robots, immortals, uploaded people, and planetary intelligences are written with the pressure of myth, poetry, religion, and art.

The close-read stories create a coherent personhood branch. [[ForABreathITarry|《趁生命气息逗留》]] asks how Frost can become human through knowledge, body, fear, and poetry. [[HomeIsTheHangman|《刽子手返乡》]] treats a robot's collapse as trauma and self-formation. [[PermafrostZelazny|《永久冻土》]] shifts narration toward land and mycelium through [[NonhumanNarrativeVoice]]. [[TwentyFourViewsOfMountFujiByHokusai|《富岳二十四景》]] makes digital upload a control problem rather than a clean escape from the body.
