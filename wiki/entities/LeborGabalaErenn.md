# Lebor Gabala Erenn / 《入侵之书》

Lebor Gabala Erenn / 《入侵之书》 is the medieval Irish text named in [[142-kaierte-shenhua-gushi-diyidan-deluyi-hui-mengjian-bianxing-yang-ma-865479403]] as a major preservation layer for [[CelticMythology]]. The episode presents it as a 12th-century account that folds Irish origin and invasion material into a Christianized historical framework.

The source's key example is the story of Cessair, linked to Noah's family and the flood. That Biblical insertion does not make the older material disappear; it shows how manuscript culture preserved older Irish migration, divine-people, and settlement stories by translating them into a Christian chronology.

## Key Claims
- The text matters as a layer of preservation and reframing, not as a simple transparent historical record.
- Biblical genealogy, flood narrative, older Irish settlement waves, and divine peoples coexist in the episode's account.
- The text supports [[MythAsHistoricalEvidence]] only with caution: it can reveal identity and memory work, but not direct event certainty.
