# Xiaohongshu Hackathon Peak Competition

Xiaohongshu Hackathon Peak Competition, nicknamed "黑颠" in the source, is the 48-hour hackathon discussed in [[ba-7-wei-heikesong-xuanshou-qing-jin-boke-guanjun-guai-cai-he-48-xiaoshi-bumian-de-yexinjia-lhozhsuqbw8csa5tj5tqc7saqrex]]. [[Shanbin]] says the event gathered more than 200 people and was designed to find people who were genuinely creating, not only teams following a trend.

The event began at midnight on April 8 and ended at midnight on April 10, using gong and drum rituals, memes, encouragement messages, and playful pressure to create a less formal and more expressive site atmosphere. The source treats it as an [[AIHackathons]] case where [[VibeCoding]], design taste, hardware making, and public storytelling combine.

## Key Claims
- The event valued public creativity, peer energy, and mutual help alongside prize outcomes.
- Contestants included students, designers, investors, serial entrepreneurs, non-coders, hardware makers, and an assistive-technology builder.
- The event made [[BuildingPublic]] visible because many participants posted their journey on [[Xiaohongshu]] before and during the contest.
- The source frames the hackathon as a relationship network and confidence-building site, not only as a product screen.
