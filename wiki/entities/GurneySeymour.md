# Gurney Seymour / 高尔尼·西摩尔

Gurney Seymour is the man [[113-henglieta-yu-nachang-jiang-renleixue-songshang-shenpanxi-de-moushaan-756562827]] presents as convicted for the murder of [[HenriettaSchmerler|Henrietta Schmerler]]. The episode says investigators linked him to the case through witness testimony, a reported confession, and later trial proceedings.

The source's treatment of Seymour matters less as a full biography than as a legal and interpretive pressure point. In court, the episode says he recanted and claimed Schmerler had initiated or provoked the encounter; the defense then used character attacks and a supposed "horse-riding taboo" to reframe violence as misunderstanding or consent. That makes the case central to [[VictimBlamingAsInstitutionalDefense]] and [[CulturalRelativismVictimProtectionBoundary]].
