# Giovanni Aldini / 乔瓦尼·阿尔迪尼

[[141-zhizao-fulankensitan-kehuan-zhimu-he-tade-shenqi-shidai-857923931]] places Aldini inside the wider [[AnimalElectricityDebate]] after [[LuigiGalvani|Luigi Galvani]] and [[AlessandroVolta|Alessandro Volta]]. His corpse-electrification demonstrations become one of the clearest bridges between observed muscular movement, public shock, and the [[GalvanicReanimationImaginary]] around [[Frankenstein|《弗兰肯斯坦》]].

Giovanni Aldini appears in [[68-fengkuang-shiyan-shi-ai-zhe-gaisi-de-qiuzhiyu-664384549]] as the episode's most vivid [[ScientificPublicSpectacle]] case. The source describes him moving beyond electrified frogs and animal parts to public demonstrations on freshly executed bodies, where electrical current caused facial and muscular movements that terrified spectators.

In the episode, Aldini matters because his work sits at a boundary: it is connected to early electrical physiology and to the cultural world that made [[Frankenstein]] plausible, but it also shows how experiment can become spectacle before modern [[ExperimentalScienceEthics]] has stable guardrails.
