# John Bogle

John Bogle is referenced in [[e44-li-xiaobo-duihua-mengyan-zheci-jiu-zheyang-ba-lpcrvfgnseaed-eambk9ofnvuq2]] through [[Vanguard]] and the question of whether a founder knows where "enough" is. [[MengYan]] treats Bogle less as a generic index-fund hero and more as an example of founder character, governance, and restraint in a financial industry where extracting more from customers is often technically possible.

The source connects Bogle to [[KnowingEnough]], [[PassiveInvesting]], and customer-aligned financial institutions. His role in this ingest is to anchor the episode's claim that investor-friendly products require both economic structure and a founder culture able to resist higher take rate.

[[vanguard-1]] turns Bogle from a reference point into a full business-history case. It follows him from Depression-era thrift and Princeton research through [[WellingtonManagement]], his firing, the fund-board loophole, the creation of [[Vanguard]], the first retail index fund, and the later ETF and succession conflict. The source's distinctive claim is that Bogle's durable contribution was not only popularizing indexing, but pairing indexing with [[FundholderMutualOwnership]] so that the [[CostMattersHypothesis]] could become an operating system.

The episode also complicates Bogle's legacy. His hostility to ETFs preserved his anti-speculation principle but also caused Vanguard to miss the first ETF wave that [[StateStreet]] and later [[BlackRock]] helped define. His survival after a heart transplant created a real [[FounderSuccession]] problem because the company had already moved into a successor-led era.
