# John Polidori / 约翰·波利多里

John Polidori appears in [[82-xianliao-weida-zuojia-de-bagua-di-er-dan-695228589]] as [[LordByron|Lord Byron / 拜伦]]'s private doctor and as the figure whose vampire story is linked to Byron's aristocratic persona. The source presents The Vampyre as an early vampire-literature work and as a bridge toward the later gentlemanly Dracula image.

Polidori's role is mainly a literary-afterlife role. He shows how one famous person's style and scandal can become raw material for another writer's gothic type, which is why the episode folds him into [[LiteraryGossipAsContext]] rather than treating him only as a footnote to Byron.

[[141-zhizao-fulankensitan-kehuan-zhimu-he-tade-shenqi-shidai-857923931]] places him back inside the [[VillaDiodati|Villa Diodati / 迪奥达蒂别墅]] gathering with [[MaryShelley|Mary Shelley / 玛丽·雪莱]], [[PercyShelley|Percy Shelley / 珀西·雪莱]], [[ClaireClairmont|Claire Clairmont / 克莱尔·克莱尔蒙特]], and Byron. The source uses the same storm-bound ghost-story game to show that Frankenstein and the modern vampire branch share a social and climatic origin scene.
