# Emperor Ping of Han / 汉平帝

Emperor Ping of Han / 汉平帝 is the child emperor elevated by [[WangMang|王莽]] in [[126-wangmang-zhisi-shensheng-diguo-de-huixuanbiao-he-huixuanbiao-de-huixuanbiao-786255780]]. The episode says Wang Mang married his favored daughter to Ping and likely still imagined himself, at least initially, as a Huo Guang-style regent rather than necessarily as founder of the [[XinDynasty|Xin dynasty]].

Ping's illness and death become a key boundary crossing. Wang Mang staged a Zhougong-like prayer to die in the emperor's place, hid the text in a golden casket, and then moved toward "acting emperor" status after Ping died. The source notes that the poisoning claim lacks firm evidence and that Ping's survival would not obviously have blocked Wang Mang's regency.

## Key Claims
- Ping's minority made regency and court management decisive.
- His marriage alliance tied imperial succession more tightly to Wang Mang's family.
- The source treats his death as politically useful to Wang Mang while still cautioning against unsupported certainty about poisoning.
