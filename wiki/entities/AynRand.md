# Ayn Rand / 安·兰德

Ayn Rand / 安·兰德 enters the wiki through [[135-xianliao-weida-zuojiamen-de-bagua-di-san-dan-823746670]] as the episode's closing and most doctrinally charged writer. The source describes her as a Soviet-era emigre from Belarus who reached the United States, worked around film, wrote scripts and novels, and built an intellectual following around radical individualism, rational self-interest, capitalism, entrepreneur heroism, and [[Objectivism|Objectivism / 客观主义]].

The episode's stance is ambivalent. It says Rand's work can energize young readers and that the host once benefited from it, but it also calls the books toxic when followers extract the hardest, most aggressive piece as a total worldview. This makes Rand a key case for [[IdeologicalTotalizationRisk]] and [[PureRationalityTrap]]: a doctrine that promises clarity can become a way to reduce complex people to abstract categories.

Rand also extends [[FreeLovePracticeGap]]. The source says she advocated romantic freedom and denied ownership claims over lovers, yet became furious when [[NathanielBranden|Nathaniel Branden / 纳撒尼尔·布兰登]] left her for a younger disciple, publicly breaking with him and trying to expel him from the movement. The episode uses this to show how a freedom doctrine can become controlling when tested by loss and jealousy.

The source also complicates her later political symbolism. Rand is often used by U.S. conservatives, but the episode says she opposed dictatorship and religion, voted in ways that do not map neatly onto later Republican iconography, and rejected Ronald Reagan as a conservative representative.
