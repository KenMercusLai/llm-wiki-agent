# 《经济学的思维方式》 / The Economic Way of Thinking

《经济学的思维方式》 / The Economic Way of Thinking is the economics book discussed in [[61-zicong-yongyou-jingjixue-de-siwei-fangshi-rensheng-dou-bian-jiandan-le-650559353]]. The episode stresses that this is the [[PaulHeyne|Paul Heyne / 保罗·海恩]] book, not the different Chinese-titled book by Thomas Sowell.

In the source, the book is valuable because it teaches economics as a way to think before it teaches economics as a professional apparatus. It begins from social order, markets, cooperation, costs, and value rather than from mathematical prerequisites. That makes it useful for ordinary readers who want to understand work, study, consumption, policy, and institutions without first becoming economics majors.

## Key Claims
- The book works as general education because it starts with problems ordinary people already meet: traffic, work time, buying decisions, cooperation, and why modern production is possible.
- The episode contrasts the book with more standard economics-principles textbooks: professional students may still need a fuller formal sequence, but general readers need a usable conceptual map first.
- The book's central contribution to the wiki is [[EconomicWayOfThinking]], not a single policy doctrine. The episode says it is market-friendly and influenced by liberal and Austrian frames, but it also discusses conditions such as property rights, information asymmetry, monopoly, and institutions.
- Its daily examples connect [[CostBenefitThinking]], [[OpportunityCost]], [[MarginalAnalysis]], [[MarketCoordination]], [[ComparativeAdvantage]], and [[SubjectiveValue]].
