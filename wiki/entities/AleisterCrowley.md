# Aleister Crowley / 阿莱斯特·克劳利

Aleister Crowley appears in [[60-xianliao-weida-zuojiamen-de-bagua-di-yi-dan-648693204]] as the occult figure whose rivalry with [[WBYeats|W. B. Yeats / 叶芝]] makes the [[GoldenDawn|Golden Dawn / 金色黎明]] segment especially comic. The source says the two disliked each other immediately: Yeats considered Crowley immoral, while Crowley framed Yeats as jealous of his magical ability.

Crowley matters here as a source-local connector, not as a full occult biography. The episode uses him to show that literary circles and occult circles overlapped in a way that could generate real creative influence, ideological seriousness, personal rivalry, and absurd posturing.
