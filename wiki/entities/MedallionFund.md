# Medallion Fund

The Medallion Fund is the [[RenaissanceTechnologies]] fund used in [[ep88-chuanyue-lianghua-zhifu-ximengsi-ai-hui-rang-putongren-geng-rongyi-zhuanqian-haishi-geng-nan-lhvigzza2ugmayezkbrxufkmp4l1]] to illustrate the power and limits of [[QuantitativeInvesting]]. The episode presents its returns as the result of many weak, repeated, low-correlation signals rather than a few high-conviction company calls.

[[vol-101-ji-anquan-shouyi-you-gao-liudongxing-hai-hao-de-touzi-daodi-cunzai-ma-lr7xt-2cgoru2gfdbatvbiv78ixg]] uses the fund as a space- or capacity-scarcity example inside [[AssetScarcityPremium]]. In that source, the fund's exclusivity illustrates that an attractive strategy may stop being available to outside investors because too much capital would weaken the opportunity.

[[vol-103-wenyi-fuxing-keji-ximengsi-de-fengshen-zhilu-shi-lianghua-zhiwang-gengshi-dongcha-renxing-de-dashi-lulzvnaxdb4klqag-p2yyqlmiikl]] adds more mechanics around the fund's legend: short holding periods, many small long-short positions, high fees, leverage, risk cutbacks, and eventually removing outside investors. It separates the Medallion myth from Renaissance's later external funds, whose strategies and performance should not be treated as equivalent to Medallion itself.

## Key Claims
- The fund's edge is compared to casino logic: small probabilistic advantages become powerful when repeated many times with disciplined sizing.
- Its example makes ordinary retail copying unrealistic because the fund depends on proprietary data, compute, talent, execution, and organizational design.
- The fund also illustrates why capacity matters: an edge can disappear if too much capital chases it or if the trader's own activity changes the market.
- Vol.101 adds that capacity scarcity can make a high-quality strategy inaccessible rather than broadly investable.
- Vol.103 adds that fees, leverage, short-horizon turnover, and closed access are part of the fund's structure, not incidental branding details.
