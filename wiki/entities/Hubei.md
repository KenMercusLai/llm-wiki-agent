# Hubei / 湖北

Hubei is the provincial context for [[LanShili]] and [[EastStarAirlines]] in [[no-203-businiao-lanshili-979295393]]. The episode describes Lan as an entrepreneur associated with [[Wuhan]] and with the "Hubei richest person" label at his peak, while East Star was framed as a Hubei and central-China private-airline breakthrough.

The province matters because East Star's airline approval required support beyond the company itself. The episode says provincial and local officials backed the application to the [[CivilAviationAdministrationOfChina]], which made the later failure politically sensitive as well as commercially damaging.

[[65-longwang-zhinu-1931-nian-de-changjiang-hongshui-658100922]] adds Hubei as a middle [[YangtzeRiver|Yangtze]] floodplain and wetland-history setting. The episode links [[Yunmengze]], rice agriculture, dikes, the [[HanRiver|Han River]], [[Wuhan]], [[Hankou]], and disaster relief to show how provincial geography became part of [[NaturalHazardSocialDisaster]] in 1931.

## Source Position
- Hubei is a regional-development and political-support setting for the source's private-airline story.
- The episode treats East Star's approval as partly a local-government achievement, not only a founder achievement.
- The later conflict over whether East Star could move toward Zhengzhou shows how regional ownership and local asset retention shaped the restructuring fight.
- In episode 65, Hubei is also an environmental-history setting where [[WetlandAdaptation]] gave way to denser settlement and flood-control dependency.
- [[FloodControlRiskTransfer]] matters because dikes and openings could protect one area while shifting floodwater toward another.
