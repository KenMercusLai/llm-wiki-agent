# Mary Ann Nichols

Mary Ann Nichols is one of the Whitechapel victims discussed in [[50-kaitangshou-jieke-jiean-baogao-nvfayi-de-zhengyi-zhizuo-630666604]]. The episode uses her biography to move the [[JackTheRipper]] story away from spectacle and toward [[VictorianWomenPrecarity]].

The hosts emphasize that Nichols was not simply born into a fixed label of "fallen woman." She had education, marriage, children, and a working-class household before poverty, her husband's affair, loss of support, unstable housing, alcohol, and the workhouse system narrowed her options. Her story becomes the episode's clearest example of how [[VictimStigmaInTrueCrime]] can erase the social processes that put victims in danger.
