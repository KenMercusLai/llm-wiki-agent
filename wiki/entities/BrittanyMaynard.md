# Brittany Maynard / 布列塔尼

Brittany Maynard is the U.S. dignity-death case discussed in [[119-anlesi-xianchang-zhengyin-shengming-ruci-zhengui-770771087]]. The episode identifies her as a 29-year-old American woman with a serious brain tumor who moved from California to Oregon because California did not yet allow the choice she sought while Oregon had an assisted-dying framework.

The source presents her public videos and interviews as both ethically powerful and ethically risky. They gave language to [[EndOfLifeAutonomyAndDignity]], but the episode also notes concerns about media exposure, suicide-reporting effects, and whether public storytelling can unintentionally become a model for vulnerable listeners.

## Key Claims
- The episode frames Brittany's choice as a refusal to call her death ordinary suicide: cancer was ending her life, and she sought control over the timing and manner.
- Her case links personal suffering to lawmaking because [[DanDiaz]] later continued advocacy for California's dignity-death bill.
- The case sits beside the Janet Hall counterexample, keeping [[AssistedDyingSafeguards]] and treatment uncertainty visible.
