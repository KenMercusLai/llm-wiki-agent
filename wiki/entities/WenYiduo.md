# 闻一多 / Wen Yiduo

闻一多 / Wen Yiduo appears in [[127-xianliao-shier-shengxiao-zhi-long-shenqi-dongwu-zai-nali-788239655]] through his dragon-totem argument in 《伏羲考》. The episode presents him as a major reason the dragon became intellectually persuasive as a collective origin symbol in modern China.

The source treats Wen's argument as politically powerful but methodologically risky. By describing the dragon as a composite creature that absorbed many other totems, he gave [[ChineseDragon|龙]] a strong symbolic explanation for national unity. But the episode frames this as part of [[TotemismNationalism]]: scholarship under crisis can choose the version of antiquity that seems most useful for building a modern community.

## Key Claims
- Wen Yiduo's dragon-totem theory helped make the dragon more attractive as a national-origin symbol.
- The episode does not treat 《伏羲考》 as neutral antiquarian research; it reads it inside modern nationalist pressure.
- His argument gives [[DragonNationalSymbolInvention]] an intellectual bridge between ancient myth and modern "descendants of the dragon" identity.
