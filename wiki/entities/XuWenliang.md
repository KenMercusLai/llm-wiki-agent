# 徐文亮 / Tom

Xu Wenliang, also called Tom in the source, is a guest in [[266-cong-hongguo-dao-ai-duanju-shui-zai-ge-shui-de-ming-lgzf6bu7bfalr5qvnhlfzkufahob]] with experience across web novels, audiobooks, short dramas, and comic dramas. He brings the cross-format comparison: web fiction, listening platforms, live-action short drama, AI comic drama, and AI photorealistic drama all compete through different cost, audience, and monetization structures.

His main claim is that [[AIShortDrama]] can eat into live-action short-drama share when live-action topics become too homogeneous and expensive. He also emphasizes that platform policies, filing/备案 tiers, copyright risk, and creator over-copying shape what survives after the first wave of novelty.

## Key Points
- He compares some low-cost comic-drama formats to a visual version of audiobook or commentary consumption.
- He argues that AI photorealistic drama has a larger potential audience than ACG-only formats because it can address broader mainstream users.
- He links AI short-drama opportunity to under-served genres such as military, fantasy, feng-shui, and Journey-to-the-West-like categories that are harder to film cheaply.
- He cautions against dozens of teams copying the same hot topic instead of expanding original stories.
