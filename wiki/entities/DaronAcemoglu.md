# Daron Acemoglu

Daron Acemoglu appears in [[chef-vs-robot]] as the [[MIT]] economist explaining automation's labor-market effects. The episode uses him to separate [[AutomationDisplacementEffect]] from [[AutomationReinstatementEffect]] rather than treating robots as simply good or bad for jobs.

His cited research gives the restaurant example a broader labor-economics backdrop. The source says one new robot per thousand workers in automation-heavy industries from 1990 to 2007 reduced employment by about three workers and lowered wages by about 0.4 percent, while also noting that automation can create new complementary work.
