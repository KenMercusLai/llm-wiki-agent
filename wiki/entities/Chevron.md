# Chevron

Chevron appears in [[chevron-venezuela-and-the-paradox-of-plenty]] as the American oil major whose long presence in [[Venezuela]] gives the episode its throughline. The source says Chevron's predecessor participated in the early oil rush after the [[LakeMaracaibo]] gusher helped turn Venezuela into a [[Petrostate]].

The company matters because it stayed while others left. After [[OilNationalization]] and later [[HugoChavez]]'s tightening of control over [[PDVSA]], [[ExxonMobil]] and [[ConocoPhillips]] exited, but Chevron negotiated terms and remained. Under [[NicolasMaduro]] and U.S. sanctions, Chevron continued negotiating with both Caracas and the [[UnitedStates]] to keep operating and exporting oil.

The episode treats Chevron's staying power as both commercial patience and geopolitical argument. Chevron told U.S. officials that if it left, [[China|Chinese]] companies could step in; the source says Chevron employed about 3,000 Venezuelans and produced roughly a quarter of Venezuela's oil. That makes the company a concrete case in the episode's [[PoliticalResourceCurse]] frame: corporate continuity can preserve capacity, but it also depends on unstable governance, licenses, sanctions, and state-company bargaining.
