# 《事已至此，走保险》

《事已至此，走保险》 is the insurance nonfiction book discussed in [[159-yao-jingming-yao-shanliang-yao-jiejue-wenti-931818201]]. The episode presents it as a compact, case-heavy account by [[AJianInsuranceWriter|阿健]], a former claims worker, using real claims stories to explain how policy clauses, field investigation, evidence, complaints, discretionary payment, and human judgment meet after accidents and illness.

The book's wiki role is to shift insurance from an abstract sales or planning topic into [[InsuranceClaimsHandling]]. Instead of asking only whether insurance is worth buying, the episode uses the book to ask how a rider's route, a child's hospital transfer, a worker's disability appraisal, a drowned borrower's beneficiary structure, or a flooded factory's evidence record changes what actually gets paid.

## Key Claims
- The book treats insurance as a social-risk interface: contracts and actuarial logic matter, but claims are lived through injuries, deaths, family conflict, business interruption, and children's accidents.
- Its practical value comes from showing where ordinary people lack leverage: policy language, evidence collection, medical necessity arguments, complaint channels, and anti-fraud investigation.
- The book does not present claims adjusters as saints or villains; it uses [[AJianInsuranceWriter|阿健]] and colleagues such as 欧阳 and Michael to show a profession balancing rule enforcement with workable settlement.
- The title's "走保险" is not passive resignation. In the episode it means returning a chaotic event to a structured process: identify coverage, preserve evidence, communicate, and solve the actual problem.
