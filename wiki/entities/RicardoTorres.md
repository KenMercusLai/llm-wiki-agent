# Ricardo Torres

Ricardo Torres is the Cuban economist interviewed in [[dark-times-for-cubas-economic-experiment]]. The [[PlanetMoney]] episode identifies him as an economist at American University in Washington, D.C. who left [[Cuba]] almost five years before the episode and uses him to explain why the current crisis is an intensification of long-building structural problems.

Torres supplies the episode's analytical bridge between [[YaserGonzalezCabrera]]'s daily stress and Cuba's larger model. He frames Cuba's history as a movement between [[ExternalPatronDependence]] and selective [[ConstrainedMarketReform]], with the [[SovietUnion]], [[Venezuela]], tourism, and the [[UnitedStates]] all shaping what the Cuban state could afford to promise.

## Source Position
- Torres says Soviet support let poverty coexist with coverage for shelter, food, education, and health care.
- He presents the Special Period as both economic collapse and ideological betrayal for many Cubans who still believed socialism could work.
- He argues that private-sector expansion remained constrained because the Cuban state wanted markets to complement, not overtake, the state sector.
- In the conclusion, he says Cuba's way out requires negotiation with the [[UnitedStates]] because domestic dysfunction and U.S. pressure are both binding constraints.
