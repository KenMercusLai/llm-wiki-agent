# Robert Kelchen

Robert Kelchen appears in [[can-the-trump-administration-make-college-cheaper]] as a higher-education researcher whose findings complicate the [[BennettHypothesis]]. The source says he studied business, medical, and law schools across the country and did not find evidence of a simple loan-driven tuition effect.

Kelchen's role is to keep [[FederalStudentLoanCaps]] from becoming an automatic answer. He argues that some graduate programs can be profitable, but others are expensive to provide, so caps may not create much room for tuition cuts and may instead expose students to [[LoanCapAccessRisk]].
