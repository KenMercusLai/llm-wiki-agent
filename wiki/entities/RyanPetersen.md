# Ryan Petersen

Ryan Petersen is the founder and CEO of [[Flexport]] in [[tsr-s3-ryanpetersen-v6-tsr-s3-ryanpetersen-v6]]. The episode presents him as a founder whose advantage came from combining software instincts with direct experience in importing, customs brokerage, freight forwarding, and physical supply-chain bottlenecks.

[[how-to-get-through-the-strait-of-hormuz]] adds Petersen as the logistics expert explaining the [[StraitOfHormuz]] crisis. He introduces [[GeneralAverage]] as a reason cargo owners can share costs from ship damage, and argues that a toll or permission regime matters less as a surcharge than as a challenge to [[FreedomOfNavigationTradeOrder|freedom of navigation]].

Petersen traces Flexport's origin to motorcycle importing with his brother, where domestic trucking software was easier to build than international freight and customs coordination. That pain became [[OperationalPainFounderInsight]]: opaque freight quotes, surprise arrival fees, paper title documents, and fragmented email workflows showed where software could help.

The source also makes Petersen a [[FounderOperationalReset]] case. After hiring [[DaveClark]] from [[Amazon]] as CEO and briefly joining [[FoundersFund]], Petersen returned when the board asked him to lead again. He says the return period forced Flexport to recover budget discipline, improve earnings, and reduce headcount costs after a capital-heavy phase.
