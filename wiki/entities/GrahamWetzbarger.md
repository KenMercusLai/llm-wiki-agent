# Graham Wetzbarger

Graham Wetzbarger is presented in [[seven-allegedly-fake-chanel-bags-vs-the-realreal]] as [[TheRealReal]]'s first chief authenticator. He joined in 2013 and helped build the company's process for checking luxury goods at scale.

The episode uses Wetzbarger to show that [[LuxuryResaleAuthentication]] depends on craft knowledge as well as databases. His four-part frame examines materials, construction, hardware components, and brand identifiers, turning specialist pattern recognition into a marketplace quality-control system.

## Key Claims
- Wetzbarger's work made authentication a repeatable operating process rather than only an individual hunch.
- His method highlights why a serial number alone is not enough; font, placement, physical details, and construction patterns can carry more signal.
- The source uses his experience to contrast independent authentication with [[BrandControlledAuthentication]].
