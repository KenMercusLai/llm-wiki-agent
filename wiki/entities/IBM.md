# IBM

[[133-ibm-yu-nacui-weishenme-putongren-bu-ying-zanmei-jizeizhuyi-815684110]] adds a much darker historical branch for IBM. The [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode argues that IBM and [[Dehomag|德霍梅格]] were not merely distant sellers of machines to [[NaziGermany|Nazi Germany]]: punched-card systems required formats, customization, service, maintenance, and business control that connected the company to [[DataEnabledPersecution|data-enabled persecution]] during [[TheHolocaust|the Holocaust]].

The source centers [[ThomasJWatson|Thomas J. Watson]] as the executive who controlled the German subsidiary structure, used trade-and-peace language, managed public risk around Nazi ties, and recovered European assets after the war. The episode therefore turns IBM into a case for [[CorporateComplicityWithAuthoritarianism|corporate complicity with authoritarianism]], while keeping the claim source-scoped to this historical account.

IBM appears in [[vol-265-kuayue-50-nian-de-meiguo-banben-zhizi-1001004591]] as the company whose research helped define the relational-database opportunity that [[LarryEllison]] later commercialized through [[Oracle]]. The episode says IBM had the technical idea, but its hardware-centered business model weakened the incentive to turn that idea into a software product that might undercut mainframe economics.

The source uses IBM as the incumbent contrast. Its role is to show how a large company's existing revenue model can leave space for a smaller company to package, sell, and scale a technology concept into a market.
