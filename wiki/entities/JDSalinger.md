# J. D. Salinger / 塞林格

J. D. Salinger / 塞林格 is the author of [[TheCatcherInTheRye|《麦田里的守望者》]], discussed in [[01-maitian-shouwangzhe-20shiji-de-meiguo-jiabaoyu-523554422]] as a writer whose famous adolescent narrator should not be reduced to adolescent self-pity. The episode emphasizes that Salinger was an adult author and a World War II veteran, so Holden's anger is presented as a crafted retrospective response to youth, social falseness, and postwar spiritual damage.

The source's biography branch connects Salinger to war and literary gossip. It says he participated in the Normandy campaign, met [[ErnestHemingway|Ernest Hemingway / 海明威]] in Paris, continued writing during wartime, and may have carried war trauma into later reclusion. It also recounts his attachment to [[OonaONeill|Oona O'Neill]] and her later marriage to [[CharlieChaplin|Charlie Chaplin / 卓别林]], using that story to contextualize Salinger's and [[HoldenCaulfield|Holden]]'s hostility toward Hollywood while keeping the explanation speculative.

Later Salinger is framed through withdrawal, publishing control, and religious interest. The episode says he avoided interviews, resisted public interpretation, became interested in Zen or Buddhist practice, and placed strict demands on how [[TheCatcherInTheRye|《麦田里的守望者》]] should appear: no foreword, afterword, author photo, or flashy cover.
