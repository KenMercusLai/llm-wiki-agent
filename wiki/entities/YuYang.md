# Yu Yang

Yu Yang is a technology leader at a listed financial company and a roundtable guest in [[duihua-minimax-yan-junjie-m3-10x-jihua-10t-moxing-he-zhineng-de-zhongju-lqtilt8flvmv99v0gshhyfyraibe]]. He discusses financial AI from the standpoint of user intent, compliance, information filtering, real-time decision context, and emotional companionship around investing.

## Key Views
- Financial users may not know exactly what they want when opening an app, but their underlying goal is often to make money.
- AI's first value in finance is filtering information and lowering the barrier to understanding terminology, indicators, and market effects.
- Compliance constraints prevent the system from directly making stock picks or offering investment advice.
- Trading is not only a text reasoning problem; it depends on user profile, live events, and what to do after conditions change.
- In financial products, companionship matters because users experience anxiety, regret, and hope around gains and losses.
