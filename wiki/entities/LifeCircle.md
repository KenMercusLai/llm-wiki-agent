# Life Circle

Life Circle is the Swiss assisted-suicide organization discussed in [[119-anlesi-xianchang-zhengyin-shengming-ruci-zhengui-770771087]] through [[AnlesiXianchang|《安乐死现场》]]. The episode says its founder, identified in the source as 普莱西科, first encountered assisted suicide through her father's cancer death with help from [[Dignitas]], then later created Life Circle.

The source emphasizes that Life Circle does not actively recommend death. It waits for members to request help and requires conditions such as medical diagnosis, incurability, and unbearable suffering, placing it inside [[AssistedDyingSafeguards]] rather than a simple consumer service.

## Key Claims
- Life Circle exemplifies the Swiss boundary where the final act must be performed by the patient rather than by the doctor.
- The organization's cases make [[AssistedDeathConceptBoundary]] practically important: assisted suicide is not the same institutional act as physician-administered euthanasia.
- Police and forensic review after death keep the Swiss model tied to legal scrutiny as well as personal choice.
