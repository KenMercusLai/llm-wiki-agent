# 《石川啄木》 / Takuboku Biography

《石川啄木》 is the [[DonaldKeene|Donald Keene / 唐纳德·基恩]] biography of [[IshikawaTakuboku|石川啄木]] discussed in [[shaqingqing-x-hanzhao-shichuan-zhuomu-wo-baochi-de-sixiang-doushi-yinwei-queqian-894931314]], with [[ShaQingqing|沙青青]] as the Chinese translator. The episode places it beside [[DuanGeShiWoBeishangDeWanju|《短歌是我悲伤的玩具》]] as the biographical companion to the new short-song translation.

The biography's source value comes from its use of Takuboku's diaries and late-Meiji literary context. It gives readers enough background to connect a compact poem to work failure, debt, family pressure, desire, illness, friendship support, and the [[GreatTreasonIncident|大逆事件]].

## Key Claims
- The biography is not a clean "young genius" memorial; it keeps Takuboku's selfishness, dependence, and self-destructive patterns visible.
- Reading the biography alongside the short songs makes [[ShortFormEmotionalSnapshot]] easier to interpret without overexplaining each poem.
- The book supports the episode's account of [[ModernJapaneseInteriorDiscovery]] because Takuboku's inner fractures become biographical and literary evidence at the same time.
