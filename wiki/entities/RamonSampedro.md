# Ramon Sampedro / 桑佩德罗

Ramon Sampedro is the Spanish assisted-suicide case discussed in [[119-anlesi-xianchang-zhengyin-shengming-ruci-zhengui-770771087]]. The episode says he became paralyzed after a diving accident, spent roughly twenty years in bed, published 《地狱来信》, appeared on television, and was later helped by Ramona in an act his family regarded as a crime and betrayal.

The source uses Sampedro to test [[EndOfLifeAutonomyAndDignity]] against family love and religious-cultural context. His brother's line that he himself could die but his family could not makes the episode's [[FamilyEthicsAtEndOfLife]] branch emotionally explicit.

## Key Claims
- Sampedro's case is not presented as family neglect: the episode says his mother, brother, and sister-in-law cared for him and did not treat him as disposable.
- The conflict turns on whether the patient's own judgment can settle the matter when family members experience the death as loss, anger, and moral injury.
- The case moves the episode away from rights language alone and toward relationship ethics.
