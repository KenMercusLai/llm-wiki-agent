# 《争论与说服》 / How Minds Change

《争论与说服》 is the Chinese translation of [[DavidMcRaney|David McRaney]]'s How Minds Change: The Surprising Science of Belief, Opinion, and Persuasion. In [[132-zhenglun-yu-shuofu-women-weishenme-chaojia-zenme-cai-suan-ying-807583318]], [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] uses the book to move from online quarrels into perception, belief change, group identity, public argument, and practical persuasion.

The episode treats the book less as a debate manual than as a map of how minds become changeable. Its path runs from [[SubjectiveRealityConstruction|constructed reality]] and [[CognitiveInertia|cognitive inertia]] through [[TribalTruth|tribal truth]], [[GroupPolarization|group polarization]], [[ElaborationLikelihoodModel|ELM]], [[DeepCanvassing|deep canvassing]], and [[NamingPowerSocialChange|naming as social change]].

## Key Claims
- The book helps the episode reject the idea that people fail to change only because they lack facts.
- Belief is tied to perception, confidence, motivation, social reward, and group belonging.
- Argument and persuasion have different scales: public argument can work through bystanders and cumulative evidence, while personal persuasion depends on relationship and self-reflection.
- The episode's final use of the book is ethical: the speaker has to know why persuasion matters before trying to change another person.
