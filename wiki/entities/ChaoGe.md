# 超哥

超哥 is a host voice in [[xianliao-xiang-song-zhao-lusi-yi-ben-duan-ge-ji-bianji-tuishu-wu-suo-bu-yong-qi-ji-905512770]], appearing in the [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] and [[YouWenTiYa|有问题呀]] crossover with [[QinZong|秦总]] and [[LiWuya|李乌鸦]]. The source presents him as someone less immersed in young-actor fandom, which lets him reconstruct the [[ZhaoLusi|赵露思]] discourse from search results and video/comment exposure.

His most important contribution is reader response. He is especially struck by an [[IshikawaTakuboku|石川啄木]] short song about arriving at today like a stone rolling downhill, giving the episode one of its clearest bridges between poetry and ordinary exhaustion.

[[sp-06-chuantai-he-liwuya-chao-ge-highliao-zuo-dushu-bozhu-de-shoumu-xinde-819825708]] adds ChaoGe's reading-podcast and creator-method role. He describes moving through journalism, television interviews, [[LuojiSiwei|罗辑思维]], and [[CultureLimited|《文化有限》]], where books became a durable program engine and [[OutputForcedInput]] practice. His strongest claims in this source are that creator identity matters more than formula, literary evaluation is not a scientific standard, and reading can become [[ReadingAsCognitiveRestoration]].

## Key Claims
- 超哥 functions as the partially outside observer who brings a less fandom-native view into the Zhao Lusi discussion.
- His reaction to Takuboku helps the episode show how short songs can work even for listeners who do not normally approach poetry as specialists.
- His on-air stance supports the source's broader move from celebrity news toward [[TankaEverydayExpression]] and [[LiveHumanFeeling]].
- In the sp.06 crossover, he reframes book commentary as sincere ordinary-reader work rather than a performance of literary-critical authority.
- His account of [[CultureLimited|《文化有限》]] makes reading programs a case in [[OutputForcedInput]] and [[BookCreatorWork]].
- His later self-report connects years of reading work to shorter anger and broader understanding of other people.
