# 《趁生命气息逗留》 / For a Breath I Tarry

《趁生命气息逗留》 / For a Breath I Tarry is the [[RogerZelazny|Roger Zelazny / 罗杰·泽拉兹尼]] short-story collection discussed in [[sp-03-chuantai-chen-shengming-qixi-douliu-zela-zini-yu-tade-mengjing-757948106]]. The episode treats the Chinese collection as a good first Zelazny book because its selected stories span 1966 to 1992 and show his changing interests, styles, and techniques.

The source also gives special attention to the title story. In that story, humans are extinct, intelligent machines repair the damaged Earth, and Frost studies human knowledge while betting with Mordel over whether he can become human. The episode reads Frost's path through learning, poetry, body, feeling, and fear as a central case of [[EmbodiedPersonhood]] and [[RobotHumanBoundary]].

## Key Claims
- The collection is not only late Zelazny; it lets readers see continuity and change across his career.
- The title story uses religious-legend form and creation imagery to make a robot's personhood feel mythic rather than merely technical.
- Frost's humanity depends on body and affect, not only information accumulation.
- The source treats the final emergence of fear and poetry as a concentrated humanizing event.
