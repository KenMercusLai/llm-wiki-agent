# Jack 饶街五

Jack 饶街五 is one of the three guests in [[e236-99-de-zuoye-doushi-ai-xie-de-dangdai-mingxiao-sheng-yanli-daxue-hai-shengxia-shenme-b066c769-00d2-4341-bf61-a82bca4181e4]]. The source presents him as a [[NYU]] data-science graduate and [[ColumbiaUniversity|Columbia University]] management-science-and-engineering master's student who has interned at AI companies and is also building his own projects.

Jack supplies the episode's clearest student-workflow evidence. He says about 99% of his assignments or papers involved AI in some substantial way, especially coding-heavy tasks, websites, research projects, and open-ended coursework. He emphasizes that this does not mean AI wrote everything unaudited; students still need enough course and technical understanding to judge whether model output meets the assignment and is correct.

His pre-AI coding experience makes him an important contrast with classmates who began coding only after [[ChatGPT]]. Jack says he could still write code without AI, though he would be slower and would return to old search and documentation habits. That contrast supports the source's broader point that AI can raise productivity while moving the skill boundary toward [[AICodingVerification]], problem setup, and output responsibility.
