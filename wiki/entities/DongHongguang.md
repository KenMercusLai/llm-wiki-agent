# Dong Hongguang / 董宏光

Dong Hongguang is introduced in [[wwdc-26-bu-shang-le-ai-dan-li-zhenzheng-de-ai-zhushou-hai-cha-shenme-s10e15-9ab1512e-a4a8-4ea6-81b5-0ac7ec677d2d]] as the founder and CEO of [[GuangfanTechnology]], a company building AI agent wearable hardware and AI assistants. The episode uses his operating-system, ecosystem, and hardware-product perspective to evaluate [[AppleIntelligence]], [[Siri]], and the broader personal-assistant race after WWDC 26.

His main product thesis is that useful personal AI assistants require more than a strong model. They need context from online behavior and the physical world, cross-device scheduling, edge-cloud compute, [[AgenticWorkflow]], [[AISkills]], [[ModelContextProtocol]], and a permission model that lets assistants act without receiving unsafe blanket authority.

## Source Position
- Dong reads Apple's WWDC AI work as a serious catch-up move, but not a finished assistant platform.
- He argues that phones remain important but are not always the best assistant carrier because they are not continuously available for perception, voice, or no-hand interaction.
- His preferred path is a [[WearableAIAssistant]] built through socially accepted devices such as earbuds and watches rather than a new form factor users must learn from scratch.
- He expects personal AI assistants to become a consensus direction within several years, while different devices keep different roles.
