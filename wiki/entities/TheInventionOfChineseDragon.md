# 《中国龙的发明：近现代中国形象的域外变迁》

《中国龙的发明：近现代中国形象的域外变迁》 is the [[ShiAidong|施爱东]] book recommended in [[127-xianliao-shier-shengxiao-zhi-long-shenqi-dongwu-zai-nali-788239655]]. The episode uses it as the main reference for the claim that the dragon's modern Chinese-national meaning was made through a long image-history process rather than inherited unchanged from antiquity.

The book matters to the wiki because it gives [[DragonNationalSymbolInvention]] a source anchor. The episode's path through missionary translation, European chinoiserie, nineteenth-century caricature, the [[QingDragonFlag]], merchant display, totemist theory, [[WenYiduo]], and [[DescendantsOfTheDragon|《龙的传人》]] is presented as part of this broader argument about modern Chinese image formation.

## Key Claims
- The book is recommended as the best extension source for the episode's dragon-symbol argument.
- Its title captures the episode's key move: "invention" does not mean fabrication from nothing, but historical transformation of inherited images.
- The source treats the dragon's modern national meaning as a product of both foreign image circulation and domestic political need.
