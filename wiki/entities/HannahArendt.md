# Hannah Arendt / 汉娜·阿伦特

Hannah Arendt / 汉娜·阿伦特 is the central figure in [[103-ta-shi-cichuan-yewu-de-guang-ta-shi-hanna-alunte-737939789]], where [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] reads her through biography, political action, exile, and controversy. The episode emphasizes that she had rigorous philosophical training under [[MartinHeidegger|Martin Heidegger]] and [[KarlJaspers|Karl Jaspers]], but resisted being reduced to a philosopher because her own work stayed tied to political reality, judgment, and action.

The source presents Arendt as someone whose ideas came under pressure from life: anti-Semitism in Germany and France, arrest, flight, statelessness, the Gurs internment camp, [[WalterBenjamin|Walter Benjamin]]'s death, and new life in the [[UnitedStates]]. Her later account of [[AdolfEichmann|Adolf Eichmann]] and the [[JewishCouncils|Jewish Councils]] in [[EichmannInJerusalem|《艾希曼在耶路撒冷》]] becomes the episode's test case for truth-telling under public anger.

Arendt's central contribution here is not a slogan but a discipline. [[BanalityOfEvil]] names how bureaucratic normality can carry murderous systems; [[ResponsibilityVsGuilt]] preserves judgment without flattening victims into perpetrators; [[PoliticalTruthTelling]] and [[PowerViolenceDistinction]] keep politics connected to truth, reflection, and collective power rather than force.
