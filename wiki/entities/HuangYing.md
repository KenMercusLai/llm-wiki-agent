# 黄英 / Huang Ying

黄英 / Huang Ying is the veteran film dubbing actor interviewed in [[e234-weilai-shipai-dianying-hai-cunzai-ma-yu-daoyan-luchuan-liaoliao-ai-gei-yingshiren-de-kongju-yu-ziyou-b2be7093-3366-4ee2-8a7a-625f06206ae5]]. The source says she has more than 20 years of dubbing experience and has voiced roles including Hermione in Harry Potter, Mal in Inception, and characters in Mission: Impossible.

Her main contribution is to make [[AIDubbing]] specific. She argues that dubbing is not only clear pronunciation or lip-sync; it depends on translation judgment, personality-specific language, pauses, stress, hesitation, and the ability to leave emotional space for another actor. In the source, these details explain why patterned or stiff performance is easier for AI to replace than scene-aware voice acting.

Huang Ying also makes [[AIVoiceCloningRights]] concrete. She treats voice as a right similar to likeness, says contracts should specify whether AI learning and reuse are allowed, and points to the evidentiary problem of proving infringement when generated voices are blended or denied.

## Key Points
- She distinguishes technical voice reproduction from context-sensitive performance.
- She treats imperfect sounds, breath, breakage, and mouth noise as possible sources of live presence rather than defects to remove automatically.
- She worries less about AI as a tool than about audiences being trained to prefer machine-smoothed expression.
- She frames AI voice use as a consent, contract, evidence, and legal-standard problem.
