# Miguel Tinker Salas

Miguel Tinker Salas appears in [[chevron-venezuela-and-the-paradox-of-plenty]] as the historian whose family and childhood make [[Venezuela]]'s oil economy socially concrete. The episode says he was born in Caripito, an oil camp built by [[CreolePetroleum]], and that his father worked physically demanding oil-industry jobs.

His account turns oil wealth into an [[OilCompanyEnclave]] story. The camp had U.S.-style infrastructure and a strict hierarchy among senior staff, junior staff, and workers, showing how a [[Petrostate]] can import modern comforts while preserving sharp social divisions.

Miguel also provides the source's interpretation of [[Chevron]]'s staying power. He argues that Chevron remained in Venezuela because it treated the country as a long-term investment rather than a short-term opportunity, a view that fits the episode's broader [[PoliticalResourceCurse]] question about corporate and political choices around oil wealth.
