# Wuhan / 武汉

Wuhan is the central city setting for [[LanShili]]'s career in [[no-203-businiao-lanshili-979295393]]. The episode places his early computer shop near Wuhan University, then follows [[EastStarGroup]] through Wuhan restaurants, real estate, tourism, ticketing, and [[EastStarAirlines]] as a local private-airline project.

The city also matters institutionally. Wuhan officials helped coordinate support for East Star's airline application and later participated in the attempted restructuring with [[AirChina]] and [[ChinaNationalAviationHolding]]. The episode's 2009 crisis turns Wuhan from market base into a [[LocalGovernmentEnterpriseRescue]] setting where headquarters, routes, jobs, aircraft, creditors, and local prestige all mattered.

[[65-longwang-zhinu-1931-nian-de-changjiang-hongshui-658100922]] adds Wuhan as the central case for the 1931 Yangtze flood. The episode places the city at the meeting of the [[YangtzeRiver|Yangtze]] and [[HanRiver|Han River]], then treats [[Hankou]]'s concessions, factories, railways, electric systems, warehouses, refugee shelters, and markets as a case of [[ModernUrbanDisasterRisk]].

## Source Position
- Wuhan is not only Lan's hometown base; it is the local demand, government, and airport ecosystem that made East Star possible.
- The proposed move of East Star's headquarters to Zhengzhou became a flashpoint because the airline was treated as a local asset.
- Later projects such as [[WuhanErchangSoda]] show Lan continuing to use Wuhan identity and local brand memory after the airline era.
- In episode 65, Wuhan is a floodplain city where modernization created new forms of flood vulnerability rather than simply removing older risk.
- The 1954 comparison turns Wuhan into a strategic protected city under a different state-capacity regime, while still raising risk-distribution questions.
