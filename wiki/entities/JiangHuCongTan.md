# 《江湖丛谈》 / Jianghu Congtan

《江湖丛谈》 is [[LianKuoru|连阔如]]'s account of old Jianghu trades, argot, scams, rules, performers, and street livelihoods, as discussed in [[102-jianghu-congtan-pianshu-heihua-he-minjian-daoyi-736516488]]. The episode treats the book as a rare guide to Qing/Minguo-era [[StreetJianghuSociety]], especially because it records [[ChunDianArgot|春点]] and the practical mechanics behind fortune-telling, fake medicine, point-mole routines, staged fraud, guards, thieves, beggars, and quyi circles.

The book's importance in the source is methodological. It makes the "江湖" of everyday social operation visible beneath later romantic uses of the term. That visibility is uncomfortable because the same material shows technical skill, mutual help, shame, fraud, dignity, and exploitation in one system.

## Key Claims
- The book is used as a street-social-history source rather than as a wuxia source.
- Its main value is concrete detail: speech codes, trade categories, lodging rules, performance customs, scam scripts, and informal authority roles.
- The episode treats some stories as legendary or oral in texture, so their strongest value is pattern recognition rather than clean documentary proof.
