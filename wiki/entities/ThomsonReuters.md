# Thomson Reuters

Thomson Reuters appears in [[tsr-ycoffsite-jakeheller-audioonly-v1final-tsr-ycoffsite-jakeheller-audioonly-v1final]] as the acquirer of [[Casetext]]. [[JakeHeller]] says he nearly skipped a meeting with Thomson Reuters Ventures because Casetext already had term sheets after the [[CoCounsel|Co-Counsel]] launch window, but the meeting turned strategic when senior leaders said they wanted to acquire the company rather than invest.

In this source, Thomson Reuters is less a full company-history subject than an acquirer-distribution case. Heller argues that selling for $650 million made sense because employee outcomes were large, Thomson Reuters had distribution across legal, accounting, finance, and risk products, and future frontier-model releases could have changed the independent company's position quickly.
