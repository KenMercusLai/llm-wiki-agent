# Curt Flood

Curt Flood appears in [[how-we-got-free-agents-in-baseball]] as the [[StLouisCardinals|St. Louis Cardinals]] center fielder who challenged [[MajorLeagueBaseball|Major League Baseball]] after being traded to the [[PhiladelphiaPhillies|Philadelphia Phillies]] in 1969 against his wishes. The source uses Flood to make the [[BaseballReserveClause|reserve clause]] concrete: even a highly paid star could not freely choose among teams that might want to hire him.

Flood's lawsuit became [[FloodVKuhn|Flood v. Kuhn]]. He lost at the [[SupremeCourt|Supreme Court]], but the episode argues that his willingness to risk his career helped shift public opinion and strengthened the [[MajorLeagueBaseballPlayersAssociation|players union]] before arbitration and collective bargaining opened the door to [[BaseballFreeAgency|baseball free agency]].

## Key Claims
- Flood treated the reserve clause as a labor-rights and dignity problem, not only a contract dispute.
- Race shaped the episode's interpretation because white owners controlled a labor market with many Black players.
- Flood's court loss still became part of the causal path toward player leverage and revenue redistribution.
