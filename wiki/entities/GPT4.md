# GPT-4

GPT-4 appears in [[tsr-ycoffsite-jakeheller-audioonly-v1final-tsr-ycoffsite-jakeheller-audioonly-v1final]] as the model capability jump that changed [[JakeHeller]]'s view of legal AI. The source says [[OpenAI]] gave Heller and a co-founder early NDA-bound access in mid-2022 to an unreleased model that later became an early version of GPT-4.

For the wiki, this page is grounded narrowly in the Casetext source rather than a general model history. Heller contrasts earlier GPT-3 and GPT-3.5 behavior, which could produce legal-sounding nonsense, with the GPT-4-era model's much stronger performance on legal tasks and bar-exam-style evaluation. That contrast made [[Casetext]] willing to redirect the company toward [[CoCounsel|Co-Counsel]] and becomes a concrete [[FrontierModelInflectionPivot]] case.
