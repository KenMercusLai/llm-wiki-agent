# 《幽灵公主》 / Princess Mononoke

[[PrincessMononoke|《幽灵公主》 / Princess Mononoke]] is treated in [[94-jibuli-de-tiancaimen-wo-de-rensheng-jiaogei-reai-he-youyi-722350232]] as [[StudioGhibli|Ghibli]]'s major publicity and distribution battle. The episode emphasizes [[ToshioSuzuki|铃木敏夫]]'s choice of "活下去" as the promotional line and his insistence that the trailers reveal the film's violence and complexity.

For the source, the film shows that promotion can prepare audiences for philosophical density rather than only soften a difficult work. It also becomes a case where [[HayaoMiyazaki|宫崎骏]] is read not as simply summarizing his career, but as pushing himself into a harsher, more unsettled form.

## Key Claims
- Suzuki's campaign judged that audiences were ready for animation with philosophical and violent complexity.
- The chosen slogan, "活下去", made survival the audience's entry point into the film.
- The trailers did not hide the film's darker content, which the source treats as an honest promotional choice.
- The film's box-office success is read as a market response to difficulty, not only spectacle.
- The source treats the film as self-challenge rather than a tidy career culmination.
