# Project Piaba

Project Piaba is the nonprofit conservation and trade-support organization discussed in [[the-little-pet-fish-that-saved-a-town-in-the-amazon]]. The episode presents it as a volunteer network in the United States and [[Brazil]] trying to keep [[Barcelos]]'s wild [[CardinalTetra]] industry alive after farmed competitors reduced demand.

Its strategy is not simply to ask consumers to prefer wild fish. The source says the project works on acclimating wild-caught fish to aquarium life, adjusting pH, improving food, reducing export delays, and building traceability so buyers can understand where and by whom fish were caught. That makes Project Piaba a practical case of [[OriginTraceabilityMoat]] and [[SustainableWildHarvesting]].

## Key Claims
- The project argues that a viable wild ornamental-fish trade can support Amazon habitat protection.
- It competes against large farms in Florida, Singapore, Vietnam, and Malaysia.
- Its improvements target fish survival, export reliability, and buyer confidence.
- The project tries to convert provenance, ethics, and story into market value for wild-caught fish.
