# Major League Baseball

Major League Baseball appears in [[how-we-got-free-agents-in-baseball]] as the league whose [[BaseballReserveClause|reserve clause]] let clubs retain control over player rights after contracts expired. The episode uses [[CurtFlood|Curt Flood]]'s challenge to show how MLB's governance could be defended as [[SportsCompetitiveBalance|competitive balance]] while also operating as [[LaborMonopsony|labor monopsony]].

MLB won [[FloodVKuhn|Flood v. Kuhn]] when the [[SupremeCourt|Supreme Court]] declined to undo baseball's antitrust exception. The source argues that later arbitration and [[SportsCollectiveBargaining|collective bargaining]] still changed the institution by creating [[BaseballFreeAgency|free agency]] and moving more revenue toward players.

## Key Claims
- MLB's reserve system limited player exit options and wage competition.
- The league defended restrictions partly by arguing that unrestricted player markets could let rich teams buy too much talent.
- The episode says MLB owners later sought a salary cap while players resisted, keeping the labor-control debate alive after free agency.
