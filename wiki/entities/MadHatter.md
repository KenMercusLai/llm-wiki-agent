# Mad Hatter / з–ҜеёҪеҢ 

The Mad Hatter is one of the tea-party figures discussed in [[180-ailisi-mengyou-xianjing-shijie-duo-huangdan-wo-yeshi-ziji-de-zhuzai-xia-975539611]]. The episode links him to the [[AliceInWonderland|гҖҠзҲұдёҪдёқжўҰжёёд»ҷеўғгҖӢ]] scene where time is stuck at six o'clock, the table cannot be reset, and conversation moves through riddles, misdirection, and failed etiquette.

The source also explains the social-history layer behind the name: hatters could be associated with madness because mercury exposure in hatmaking was thought to damage workers. In the episode, the Hatter's "murdering time" accusation turns him into an anchor for [[TimeStasisSystem]], while his raven-and-writing-desk riddle anchors [[WonderlandLanguageGames]].

## Key Claims
- The Hatter is not merely silly; he gives the tea party its stopped-time premise and its ruleless conversational pressure.
- The unanswered riddle matters because it demonstrates nonsense as language play rather than puzzle failure.
- The character connects literary absurdity to idiom, labor history, and Victorian social context.
