# 张阔 / Zhang Kuo

Zhang Kuo is the [[Alibaba]] international-business executive interviewed in [[e231-cong-b2b-dao-a2a-agent-xin-jijian-ruhe-rang-yiren-qiye-zuo-quanqiu-shengyi-0f4a2ab9-d3a0-41ad-8db1-6c03c851bd70]]. The episode presents him as the operator behind [[Axio]]'s agentic sourcing and small-business workflow thesis, not as a general AI commentator.

## Source Position
- Zhang argues that B2B trade can move toward [[B2BToA2A]], where buyer agents, seller agents, and a user's own master/subagents coordinate business work.
- He frames [[AgenticB2BSourcing]] as a vertical agent problem requiring Alibaba's trade data, supplier signals, price truth, logistics information, and transaction feedback.
- He says [[OpenClaw]] and [[CloudCowork]] made the agent-workbench paradigm more convincing, while still emphasizing out-of-box usefulness for nontechnical small businesses.
- His AI-native organization diagnostic is whether new models or agent frameworks make a product team excited, anxious, or unaffected; "unaffected" is the danger sign.
- For AI-era talent, he emphasizes technical judgment, business understanding, and designing products for model capabilities three to six months ahead.
