# Carolyn Levy

Carolyn Levy is one of the [[TheSocialRadars]] hosts in [[socialradarsseason1-wrapup-final-socialradarsseason1-wrapup-final]], [[socialradars-edithelliot-v3]], [[socialradarsseason1-pg-v4-updated-finallll-socialradarsseason1-pg-v4-updated-finallll]], [[socialradarspod-brianchesky-final]], [[socialradarspod-stevehuffman-final]], [[socialradarspod-garrytan-final]], [[socialradarspod-davidlieb-final]], [[tsr-s2-trevorblackwell-v2]], [[socialradarsseason1-tracyyoung-final-1]], [[socialradarspod-tonyxu-final]], and [[socialradarspod-paulb-final]], interviewing [[EdithElliott]], [[PaulGraham]], [[BrianChesky]], [[SteveHuffman]], [[GarryTan]], [[DavidLieb]], [[TrevorBlackwell]], [[TracyYoung]], [[TonyXu]], and [[PaulBuchheit]] with [[JessicaLivingston]]. The Season 1 wrap-up adds her role in explaining the show's production rhythm: seven planned episodes became nine, listener feedback helped motivate a second season, and the hosts planned a short recording break before publishing new interviews. In the Elliott source she helps frame [[NooraHealth]] as a nonprofit startup and donor-trust case; in the Graham source she helps frame [[Viaweb]], early [[YCombinator]], [[HackerNews]], and Graham's return to writing; in the Chesky source she helps frame [[Airbnb]]'s hosting origin, YC survival story, and loneliness turn; in the Huffman source she helps frame [[Reddit]]'s origin, sale, early-team strain, and 2015 return; in the Tan source she helps frame the full-circle return to Y Combinator, [[Posterous]], [[InitializedCapital]], and founder self-awareness; in the Lieb source she helps frame Bump's adoption and Google Photos' post-acquisition story; in the Blackwell source she helps frame Blackwell's role in early YC culture; in the Young source she helps draw out the second-time founder, remote-work, values, and family-life dimensions of [[TigerEye]]; in the Xu source she is part of the interview format around [[DoorDash]]'s customer-discovery and marketplace-validation path; and in the Buchheit source she helps surface Gmail, FriendFeed, and YC judgment lessons.

Her role in the wiki is mainly source-contextual: she is part of the interview format that lets startup participants clarify details from [[NooraHealth]], [[Viaweb]], [[YCombinator]], [[HackerNews]], [[Airbnb]], [[Reddit]], [[Posterous]], [[InitializedCapital]], [[Bookface]], [[Bump]], [[Flock]], [[GooglePhotos]], [[Anybots]], [[PlanGrid]], [[DoorDash]], [[Gmail]], [[FriendFeed]], YC origin stories, nonprofit execution, and later company-building lessons.
