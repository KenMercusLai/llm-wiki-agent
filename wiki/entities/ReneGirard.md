# Rene Girard

Rene Girard appears in [[e233-guigu-youyi-de-quanli-wang-ruhe-xingcheng-liaoliao-bide-tier-de-sixiang-qimeng-pintu-2a529abf-3c14-4ae7-a0a5-fe1340a66c7d]] as one of the main intellectual influences on [[PeterThiel]]. The episode emphasizes Girard's mimetic theory: people imitate one another's desires, which can produce rivalry, conflict, and violence.

The source uses Girard to explain [[MimeticCompetitionAvoidance]]. Thiel's preference for monopoly and aversion to ordinary competition are read as a business translation of mimetic rivalry, especially in the episode's discussion of [[PayPal]], [[ElonMusk]], and [[Facebook]].

## Source Position
- The episode notes Girard's Stanford period as important context for Thiel's exposure to his ideas.
- It treats Thiel's use of Girard as selective: business and society are often explained through mimetic patterns, but the source does not claim Girard directly dictates every later choice.
