# Principles of Philosophy / 《哲学原理》

[[PrinciplesOfPhilosophy|《哲学原理》]] is a [[ReneDescartes|Descartes]] work named in [[174-du-dikaer-shi-jian-dashi-966856702]] as one of the harder texts beyond [[DiscourseOnMethod|《谈谈方法》]]. The episode highlights that the work was dedicated to [[PrincessElisabethOfBohemia|Princess Elisabeth of Bohemia]], which makes it part of the source's larger point that Elisabeth was an intellectually serious interlocutor rather than a decorative biographical anecdote.

## Source Position
- The source uses the dedication to show Descartes's respect for Elisabeth's intelligence and philosophical seriousness.
- The text sits near the episode's discussion of [[SubstanceAndExtension]], God, and Descartes's attempt to systematize knowledge.
- It is not treated as the simplest entry point, but as part of the corpus that serious readers may consult after the accessible method text.
