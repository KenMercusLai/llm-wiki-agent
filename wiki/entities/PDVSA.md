# PDVSA

PDVSA appears in [[chevron-venezuela-and-the-paradox-of-plenty]] as the Venezuelan state oil company created when [[Venezuela]] fully nationalized its oil industry in 1976. The source presents [[OilNationalization]] as a way to move value and control from foreign oil companies into the state, while still allowing companies such as [[Chevron]] and [[ExxonMobil]] to operate under stricter contractor rules.

The company's later history turns it into a governance test. Under [[HugoChavez]], the episode says state control tightened further, including over employees and equipment; [[ExxonMobil]] and [[ConocoPhillips]] left, while Chevron negotiated and stayed. Under [[NicolasMaduro]], sanctions, degraded equipment, reduced field productivity, and lost technical labor made rebuilding the industry costly.

In the wiki, PDVSA is the institutional bridge between [[OilRevenueDependence]] and [[PoliticalResourceCurse]]. A national oil company can let a country capture more oil value, but the source argues that weak governance, rent distribution, underinvestment, and political control can turn that control into fragility.
