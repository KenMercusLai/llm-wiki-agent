# Zhangzidao

Zhangzidao appears in [[ep86-mianzi-dizi-rizi-caibao-zhi-jiang-zhe-san-jian-shi-lukmnlkkuxvrvv12frosd9teg7bj]] as the episode's most vivid inventory and audit-risk case. The source describes the company's scallop-related losses, repeated claims about undersea inventory problems, and later regulatory scrutiny using vessel-track evidence, making it a warning that some inventory is hard for ordinary investors to verify.

## Source Position
- Zhangzidao is used to show that inventory can be both economically fragile and difficult to audit.
- The episode links the case to [[InventoryWriteDownRisk]] because biological or undersea assets can disappear, die, or be misreported.
- It also links the case to [[AuditOpinionRisk]] because a standard audit opinion does not eliminate the need to question business reality and asset verifiability.
