# Sell Me a Sasquatch

Sell Me a Sasquatch is the U.S. title and theme chosen in [[board-games-3-whats-in-a-name]] for the [[PlanetMoney]] and [[ExplodingKittens]] card game inspired by [[MarketForLemons|The Market for Lemons]]. In the source, the game turns [[AsymmetricInformation]] into partial-information trading: players can hide parts of deals, bluff, distrust one another, and create party-game disorder from a serious economics paper.

The title matters because it makes the product more than a ruleset. "Sell Me a Sasquatch" gives the game a memorable sales phrase, a collectible creature market, and a cover-art direction that [[JamieWolanski]] reads as promising for [[RetailShelfAppeal]]. The source also treats the name as a constraint because Sasquatch may not be equally recognizable in international markets.

## Key Claims
- The game's economic core is adverse selection and hidden information, but the retail product needs humor, theme, and a quick promise.
- The Sasquatch direction beat generic object-swapping and car-pun alternatives because it felt more coherent and fun to inhabit.
- The title connects [[ProductNamingAsPositioning]] to [[CryptidThemeLegibility]]: the product sounds like a playful market before the buyer understands the mechanics.
- The source presents the U.S. title as decided while leaving European naming or localization as an unresolved [[CrossMarketNamingFriction]] problem.
