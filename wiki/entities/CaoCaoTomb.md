# 曹操墓

曹操墓 is the Chinese archaeology case used in [[69-xianliao-tuili-wenxue-lishixuezhe-ke-bu-jiu-shi-zhentan-ma-666244465]] to parallel [[RichardIII|Richard III / 理查三世]]. The episode says archaeological location, tomb scale, textual comparison, and inscribed materials point toward the identification, yet public doubt often persists because Cao Cao's literary and popular image is already powerful.

The case is useful for [[EvidenceBoundHistoricalRevision]] because it shows the reverse of a courtroom-like mystery: evidence may be available, but public memory shaped by novels, legends, and suspicion can still reject it. The episode uses the "seventy-two suspicious tombs" motif as an example of later narrative pressure that can overwhelm more mundane archaeological reasoning.
