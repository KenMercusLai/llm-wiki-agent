# Josh Reeves

Josh Reeves is a [[Gusto]] co-founder and its long-running CEO in [[tsr-s4-gusto-v3-tsr-s4-gusto-v3]]. He knew [[EddieKim]] from [[StanfordUniversity]], later met [[TomerLondon]] through a Stanford alumni mentorship program, and entered [[YCombinator]] with the team before the idea shifted into payroll.

In the source, Josh's role is the company-building and strategic frame. Early on he focused on go-to-market, sales, marketing, business operations, hiring, onboarding, all-hands, and offices while Eddie and Tomer focused more on engineering, product, and design. He also frames Gusto's long-term direction as [[EntrepreneurshipInfrastructure]]: helping more people start companies by abstracting payroll, benefits, tax, and government complexity.

Josh is also central to the trust and crisis branch. During the [[SiliconValleyBank]] crisis, the source says Gusto put substantial company capital at risk so employees of affected customer companies could still be paid, strengthening the episode's [[PayrollInfrastructureTrust]] and [[CrisisStakeholderLeadership]] themes.
