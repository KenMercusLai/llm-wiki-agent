# Abel Brodeur

Abel Brodeur is the [[UniversityOfOttawa]] economist at the center of [[dont-hate-the-replicator-hate-the-game]]. The episode follows his path from a graduate-student smoking-ban paper, where repeated analysis choices could have produced a publishable significant result, into a broader effort to study and change social-science research incentives.

Brodeur founded the [[InstituteForReplication]] in 2022 and organizes [[ReplicationGames]] so independent teams can rerun code, inspect data, and test whether published results survive [[RobustnessChecks]]. In the source, his role is less moral prosecutor than incentive designer: he wants a credible chance of inspection to make cleaner research behavior normal.
