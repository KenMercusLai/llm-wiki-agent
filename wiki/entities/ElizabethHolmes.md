# Elizabeth Holmes

[[ElizabethHolmes|Elizabeth Holmes]] is the [[Theranos]] founder and public figure in [[tsr-s4-tylershultzpart1-final-tsr-s4-tylershultzpart1-final]]. [[TylerShultz|Tyler Shultz]] describes meeting her through [[GeorgeShultz|George Shultz]] while he was at [[StanfordUniversity|Stanford]] and being impressed by her mission, black turtleneck, deep voice, eye contact, and ability to make listeners feel connected to a transformative healthcare project.

The same source presents Holmes as the architect of an alternate operating reality. Tyler says she used credible people around her as proof points, deflected his request for a meeting into an email, forwarded that email to [[SunnyBalwani|Sunny Balwani]], and reached George first after Tyler resigned by framing Tyler as having a vendetta. The episode also says she explained rollout delays to Tyler's family as extra FDA engagement even as Tyler believed the technology was failing.

Holmes is the episode's central case for [[FounderIdentityNarrativeRisk]]. Tyler and the hosts argue that media, investors, and supporters wanted a female Steve Jobs story, and that Holmes's public narrative reduced scrutiny while later making the visible Theranos fraud damaging for other female founders.

[[tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3-tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3]] adds the post-resignation pressure chapter. Tyler says Holmes attended an intimate Shultz family Thanksgiving while the conflict was already live, inferred that Tyler was a [[WallStreetJournal|Wall Street Journal]] source after [[JohnCarreyrou|John Carreyrou]] contacted Theranos, and continued to hold George's trust even after regulatory findings challenged the company's story. In this source, Holmes's mission narrative keeps power not only by attracting belief, but by making dissent feel personally disloyal.
