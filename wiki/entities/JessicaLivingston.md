# Jessica Livingston

Jessica Livingston is one of the [[TheSocialRadars]] hosts interviewing [[SteveHuffman]] in [[socialradarspod-stevehuffman-final]], [[GarryTan]] in [[socialradarspod-garrytan-final]], [[DavidLieb]] in [[socialradarspod-davidlieb-final]], [[TrevorBlackwell]] in [[tsr-s2-trevorblackwell-v2]], [[TracyYoung]] in [[socialradarsseason1-tracyyoung-final-1]], [[TonyXu]] in [[socialradarspod-tonyxu-final]], and [[PaulBuchheit]] in [[socialradarspod-paulb-final]]. In the Blackwell, Huffman, and Tan episodes she is also a participant in the history being recalled: she helped run early [[YCombinator]], pushed for a Silicon Valley program with [[PaulGraham]], remembers the Mountain View dinners and office constraints, watched YC choose Huffman and [[AlexisOhanian]] over their first idea, and later watched Tan move from [[StartupSchool]] attendee to YC founder, designer, partner, and CEO.

The sources use Livingston's memory to turn YC and founder stories into operating detail. Her recollections of interviews, printed applications, wet paint, bathroom constraints, and visitors such as Demi Moore show [[StartupInfrastructureImprovisation]] at the physical-institution level, while her PlanGrid reflections emphasize [[VerticalSaaSDomainExpertise]], paid customer pain, and the value of explicit [[TigerEye]] commitments. The Tony Xu episode adds [[DoorDash]] as a marketplace version, the Paul Buchheit episode adds [[Gmail]] and [[FriendFeed]] as product-engineering and acquisition cases, the David Lieb episode adds [[Bump]] and [[GooglePhotos]] as cases in frequency, user discovery, and post-acquisition product persistence, the Tan episode adds [[FounderHonesty]], [[Bookface]], [[Posterous]], and YC's outsider-builder mission, and the Huffman episode adds [[Reddit]], [[FounderIdeaPivot]], and founder responsibility over long-lived decisions.
