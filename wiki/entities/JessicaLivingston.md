# Jessica Livingston

Jessica Livingston is one of the [[TheSocialRadars]] hosts interviewing [[PaulGraham]] in [[socialradarsseason1-pg-v4-updated-finallll-socialradarsseason1-pg-v4-updated-finallll]], [[BrianChesky]] in [[socialradarspod-brianchesky-final]], [[SteveHuffman]] in [[socialradarspod-stevehuffman-final]], [[GarryTan]] in [[socialradarspod-garrytan-final]], [[DavidLieb]] in [[socialradarspod-davidlieb-final]], [[TrevorBlackwell]] in [[tsr-s2-trevorblackwell-v2]], [[TracyYoung]] in [[socialradarsseason1-tracyyoung-final-1]], [[TonyXu]] in [[socialradarspod-tonyxu-final]], and [[PaulBuchheit]] in [[socialradarspod-paulb-final]]. In the Graham, Blackwell, Huffman, Chesky, and Tan episodes she is also a participant in the history being recalled: she helped start and run early [[YCombinator]], pushed for a Silicon Valley program with Graham, remembers the Mountain View dinners and office constraints, watched YC choose Huffman and [[AlexisOhanian]] over their first idea, saw Airbnb's cereal boxes as evidence that Chesky and [[JoeGebbia]] would keep surviving, and later watched Tan move from [[StartupSchool]] attendee to YC founder, designer, partner, and CEO.

The sources use Livingston's memory to turn YC and founder stories into operating detail. Her recollections of interviews, printed applications, wet paint, bathroom constraints, and visitors such as Demi Moore show [[StartupInfrastructureImprovisation]] at the physical-institution level, while the Graham episode adds her role in the [[SummerFoundersProgram]], the California move, YC dinners, and the early realization that the first batch contained real companies. Her PlanGrid reflections emphasize [[VerticalSaaSDomainExpertise]], paid customer pain, and the value of explicit [[TigerEye]] commitments. The Tony Xu episode adds [[DoorDash]] as a marketplace version, the Paul Buchheit episode adds [[Gmail]] and [[FriendFeed]] as product-engineering and acquisition cases, the David Lieb episode adds [[Bump]] and [[GooglePhotos]] as cases in frequency, user discovery, and post-acquisition product persistence, the Tan episode adds [[FounderHonesty]], [[Bookface]], [[Posterous]], and YC's outsider-builder mission, the Huffman episode adds [[Reddit]], [[FounderIdeaPivot]], and founder responsibility over long-lived decisions, and the Chesky episode adds [[Airbnb]], [[PeerToPeerMarketplaceTrust]], and survival under the [[FounderCashFlowConstraint]].
