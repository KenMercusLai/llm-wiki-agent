# Pinocchio / 匹诺曹

Pinocchio / 匹诺曹 is the animated puppet at the center of [[AdventuresOfPinocchio|《木偶奇遇记》]], discussed in [[122-pinuocao-chengzhang-de-cantong-jiqi-suo-chuangzuo-de-777464172]]. The episode stresses that he is not only a naughty child who must be corrected: the piece of wood already speaks, feels pain, mocks adults, and resists passivity before [[Geppetto]] carves it.

His growth is repetitive rather than linear. Pinocchio wants to be good, is pulled away by hunger, vanity, fear, play, and scammers, and then returns through shame or need. The source reads this loop as emotionally realistic: becoming human includes wanting to improve, failing again, suffering consequences, and slowly learning care.

## Key Claims
- Pinocchio is a strong [[NonhumanBecomingHumanMotif]] case because the question is not just how wood becomes flesh, but how a self becomes responsible.
- His courage appears before his discipline: he protects the coins for [[Geppetto]], offers himself in the puppet theater, helps catch the chicken thieves, rescues a dog, and later saves his father.
- The shark-belly rescue turns him from protected child into protector, making [[GrowthAsProtectorRole]] more decisive than school ranking.
- His final transformation leaves the wooden body behind, which the episode reads as both completion and loss.
