# Unitree Robotics

Unitree Robotics appears in [[134-shuju-de-zongshu-he-xiechen-liao-xinshidai-de-shiyou-lishi-bantu-shuju-jinzita-dingjia-yu-recipe]] as [[XieChen]]'s example of a robot-body company with a relatively clear boundary: make stable, mass-producible hardware rather than trying to own every layer of the robot brain and data stack.

[[jushen-jibao-26q2-shijie-moxing-dafeng-buting-he-buxiang-bei-tie-biaoqian-de-ren-1-170-1]] adds Unitree as both a humanoid-marathon contender and a research-platform ecosystem. [[ChenZhePeter]] says Unitree was considered one of the favorites before the Beijing humanoid marathon, and he also links mature Unitree hardware plus open-source parameter optimization to easier transfer of whole-body motion-capture data into robots.

[[e244-duan-dao-duan-vs-shangxia-fenceng-jiqiren-lujing-zhizheng-zhengzai-zhuanxiang-fc9a3737-81a9-49cf-a7d6-530c77df836e]] adds [[HanZheng]]'s assessment that Unitree has given the market a strong answer on hardware mass production, while object manipulation may be much earlier. The source reinforces the distinction between body-hardware competence and [[OpenWorldRobotManipulation]] capability.
