# Friedrich Hayek / 哈耶克

Friedrich Hayek / 哈耶克 appears in [[61-zicong-yongyou-jingjixue-de-siwei-fangshi-rensheng-dou-bian-jiandan-le-650559353]] as the source's main reference point for why centralized planning struggles with local and changing information. The episode connects Hayek to [[TheEconomicWayOfThinking|《经济学的思维方式》]] by emphasizing that no outside expert can fully know each person's preferences, timing, constraints, and private circumstances.

The source does not turn Hayek into a demand that readers join one economic school. It says the book appreciates Hayek and Austrian-school insights while still introducing other tools. For the wiki, his role is to ground [[DispersedInformationProblem]] and the claim that [[MarketCoordination]] can coordinate knowledge no single planner possesses.

## Connections
- [[DispersedInformationProblem]] - central knowledge problem associated with Hayek in the episode.
- [[MarketCoordination]] - market mechanism that can use dispersed signals.
- [[EconomicWayOfThinking]] - general reasoning frame extended by Hayek's local-knowledge point.
- [[ComparativeAdvantage]] and [[SubjectiveValue]] - adjacent concepts showing why local preference and ability matter to cooperation.
