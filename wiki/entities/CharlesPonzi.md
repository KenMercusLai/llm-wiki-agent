# Charles Ponzi

Charles Ponzi appears in [[ep28-bainian-jinrong-zhapian-shi-jieji-kuayue-yu-liangdang-ruyu-de-juli-ltpkaw9wxzpxlxo3mhh-0rkimgcj]] as the historical anchor for [[PonziScheme]] mechanics. The episode presents him as an Italian immigrant whose failed jobs, bank exposure, check forgery, prison time, and later international-reply-coupon arbitrage story fed into a promise of extremely high short-term returns.

## Key Claims
- The source treats Ponzi less as a one-off criminal genius than as a case where social pressure, prior failure, financial knowledge, and a plausible arbitrage story converged.
- His original story used international reply coupons and postwar exchange-rate differences to make a return promise feel concrete.
- The scheme became fraudulent when coupon supply, redemption, and scale could not support promised returns, leaving later investor money to pay earlier investors.
- Ponzi's case gives the wiki a named historical origin for the broader pattern already captured by [[InvestmentFraudRedFlags]].
