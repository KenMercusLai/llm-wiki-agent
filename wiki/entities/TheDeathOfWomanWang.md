# 《王氏之死》 / The Death of Woman Wang

《王氏之死》 / The Death of Woman Wang is [[JonathanSpence|史景迁]]'s microhistory of [[Tancheng|郯城]] and the murder of [[WangShiTancheng|王氏]]. In [[148-wangshi-zhisi-duanjing-tuiyuan-shang-de-lishi-zhuangjing-892588934]], [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] presents the book as a compact but structurally ambitious work: the actual murder case appears late because the county's disasters, tax burdens, lawsuits, ghosts, and literary women prepare the social world in which Wang had almost no room to act.

The book's source method is the episode's central issue. It places [[TanchengCountyGazetteer|《郯城县志》]], [[HuangLiuhong|黄六鸿]]'s [[FuhuiQuanshu|《福惠全书》]], and [[PuSongling|蒲松龄]]'s [[LiaozhaiZhiyi|《聊斋志异》]] beside each other. That makes the book a case for [[MicrohistoryFromHostileArchives]] and [[FictionAsHistoricalEvidence]]: administrative documents can show judgment and procedure, while fiction can reveal fears and fantasies around hunger, sex, female agency, and ghostly revenge.
