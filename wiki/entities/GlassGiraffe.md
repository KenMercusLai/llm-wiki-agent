# 《玻璃长颈鹿》

《玻璃长颈鹿》 is [[KanaTomoko|加纳朋子]]'s work as discussed in [[131-richang-zhimi-tuili-shi-wo-wei-neng-shuo-chukou-de-zhenxin-805617229]]. The episode presents its opening as a bitter [[DailyLifeMystery|日常推理]] case: after 17-year-old Ando Maiko is killed, another girl begins repeating details that look like supernatural possession.

The solution shifts the case away from haunting. The girl had been threatened by the same man the previous day, escaped without telling anyone, and then became crushed by the thought that speaking earlier might have prevented Maiko's death. The school doctor's own unresolved counterfactual grief lets her recognize the mechanism: the mystery is created by fear, silence, and guilt rather than a ghost.

## Key Claims
- The source treats the "possession" clue as a psychological mystery, not a supernatural answer.
- The hidden event is an earlier threat that the girl survived but could not disclose.
- Counterfactual guilt is central: the unbearable question is whether speaking sooner could have changed the next day's murder.
- The story makes [[UnspokenFeelingAsMystery|说不出口的心事]] darker than embarrassment or shyness; what remains unsaid can become traumatic self-blame.
