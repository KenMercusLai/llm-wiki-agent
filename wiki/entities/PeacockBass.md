# Peacock Bass

Peacock Bass is the larger trophy fish around which [[Barcelos]] is building a sport-fishing tourism economy in [[the-little-pet-fish-that-saved-a-town-in-the-amazon]]. The source contrasts it with the tiny [[CardinalTetra]]: one fish supported an export-oriented aquarium trade, while the other attracts visitors who pay for guides, hotels, transport, and services.

In the wiki, Peacock Bass is less important as a biological profile than as a tourism-demand object. It marks the town's [[HeritageTourismPivot]] from a locally controlled wild fish supply chain toward visitor-facing work in a [[TourismDependentSmallEconomy]].

## Key Claims
- The episode says many tourists visit [[Barcelos]] hoping to catch Peacock Bass.
- Former piaberos now work as guides, chauffeurs, hotel workers, cleaners, or related tourism labor.
- The species helps rebrand Barcelos as a sport-fishing destination after the [[CardinalTetra]] trade declined.
- This tourism path brings new income but may not preserve the independence of the old [[PiaberoEconomy]].
