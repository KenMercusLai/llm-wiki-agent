# Uniqlo

Uniqlo appears in [[betty-boop-excel-olympics-penny-isms-our-2026-valentines]] as the retail example of self-checkout that customers can actually like. [[AlexiHorowitzGhazi]] records a Valentine to the system at a New York store, while [[KennyMalone]] clarifies that the praise is about a better checkout experience, not a general argument for replacing workers.

The source explains Uniqlo's advantage through [[RFIDCheckout]]. Customers place items in a bin and the system reads RFID tags automatically, avoiding the barcode-scanning friction common in self-checkout. The episode also keeps the economics constrained: RFID tags have become cheaper, but even a few cents per item is easier to absorb in apparel than in low-margin groceries.
