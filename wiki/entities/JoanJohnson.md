# Joan Johnson

Joan Johnson is the co-building operator in [[how-black-hair-care-grew-black-power]]. The episode says she left a good government job to help [[GeorgeEJohnson]] with bookkeeping, labels, jars, trucks, collections, and later leadership at [[JohnsonProducts]].

Joan matters because the source does not reduce Johnson Products to a single-founder story. She appears as an operating partner who helped convert hair-care formulas into a functioning company, enforced collections, shared in the wealth created by the public listing, and later took charge and turned the company around before its 1993 sale.

## Key Claims
- Joan's work connected back-office discipline, customer collections, logistics, and founder-level judgment.
- Her later turnaround role complicates a simple decline story: the company struggled, but the source says Joan took charge before selling it.
- [[OliviaJoanGolly]]'s TikTok archive of Joan's clothing becomes the episode's opening evidence of Black entrepreneurial wealth and visibility.
