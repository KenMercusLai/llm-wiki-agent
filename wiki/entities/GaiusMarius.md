# Gaius Marius / 马略

Gaius Marius / 马略 appears in [[124-kaisa-zhisi-lishi-de-yunjiao-shifou-yi-shixiao-781894361]] as one of the earlier figures whose reforms help explain why the [[RomanRepublic|Roman Republic]] was already unstable before [[JuliusCaesar|Caesar]]. The episode treats the Marius-Sulla conflict as a major step in the republic's internal tearing.

Marius matters especially for [[MilitaryPersonalization]]. His military reforms are presented as shifting loyalty away from the Senate and Roman people toward commanders who could reward soldiers and veterans. That change made later political conflict more dangerous because public office, personal command, and armed loyalty increasingly overlapped.
