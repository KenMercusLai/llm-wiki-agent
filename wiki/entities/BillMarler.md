# Bill Marler

Bill Marler appears in [[the-sneaky-way-companies-get-new-chemicals-into-our-food]] as the food-safety attorney who investigated the [[DailyHarvest]] tara-flour case. The source says he represented hundreds of affected consumers and had previously worked on major food-safety litigation, including a Jack in the Box E. coli case.

Marler's role is important because he shows how private litigation can become an information-gathering mechanism when regulators do not already know what happened. Through [[FoodSafetyLitigationDiscovery]], subpoenas, documents, and testimony, he connected [[TaraFlour]] to both Daily Harvest's product and another product case, then followed the chain to [[Smirks]] and [[MolinosPeru|Molinos]].

The source treats his position as pragmatic rather than anti-market: he says companies generally do not want to poison people, but he argues that weak public consequences reduce caution when [[FoodAdditiveRegulation]] relies heavily on self-certification.
