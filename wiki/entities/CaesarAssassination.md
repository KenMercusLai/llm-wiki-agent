# Assassination of Julius Caesar / وپ؛و’’éپ‡هˆ؛

The assassination of Julius Caesar / وپ؛و’’éپ‡هˆ؛ is the central event of [[124-kaisa-zhisi-lishi-de-yunjiao-shifou-yi-shixiao-781894361]]. The episode places the killing on the Ides of March in 44 BCE at Pompey's Curia, not in the popular image of the Capitoline temple, and treats the location as politically meaningful because it joined Senate setting, [[Pompey]]'s memory, and older Roman anti-king symbolism.

The plot is presented as both principled and compromised. [[MarcusBrutus]] gives the conspiracy republican symbolism; [[GaiusCassiusLonginus]] organizes opposition to dictatorship; [[DecimusJuniusBrutus]] supplies practical access and persuades Caesar to attend. But the source also stresses fear, resentment, jealousy, miscalculation, and the failure to build a post-assassination political order.

The killing did not restore the [[RomanRepublic|Roman Republic]]. Caesar's will, funeral, public anger, [[MarkAntony]]'s reaction, and [[OctavianAugustus|Octavian]]'s inheritance turned the murder into a new phase of civil conflict that ended in the [[RomanEmpire|Roman Empire]].
