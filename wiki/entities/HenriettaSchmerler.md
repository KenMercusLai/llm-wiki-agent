# Henrietta Schmerler / 亨丽埃塔·施梅勒

Henrietta Schmerler is the young anthropology graduate student at the center of [[113-henglieta-yu-nachang-jiang-renleixue-songshang-shenpanxi-de-moushaan-756562827]]. In the episode, she is not treated only as a murder victim: letters, family materials, early writing, outdoor courage, Jewish family background, and academic ambition are used to restore her as a person and developing scholar.

Her fieldwork at the [[WhiteMountainApache|White Mountain Apache]] reservation becomes the episode's central case for [[AnthropologyFieldworkSafety]]. She tried to collect stories, ritual material, and social observation while navigating isolation, limited institutional support, gendered suspicion, stolen notes, and the pressure to prove herself in a discipline that treated independent fieldwork as a rite of passage.

After her death, the source says her public image was repeatedly rewritten through rumor, press sexualization, courtroom character attacks, and disciplinary cautionary tales. That makes her a key node for [[FieldworkSexualViolence]], [[VictimBlamingAsInstitutionalDefense]], and [[VictimStigmaInTrueCrime]].
