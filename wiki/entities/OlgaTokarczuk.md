# Olga Tokarczuk / 托卡尔丘克

Olga Tokarczuk / 托卡尔丘克 is the Polish Nobel Prize-winning writer discussed in [[130-tuokaerqiuke-guaidan-gushiji-xunzhao-he-gaobie-803543569]]. The episode centers on her short-story collection [[BizarreStories|《怪诞故事集》]] and says her better-known Chinese translations also include 《太古和其他的时间》, 《白天的房子，夜晚的房子》, 《云游》, and 《雅各布之书》.

The source presents Tokarczuk as a writer who takes short fiction seriously and uses ordinary locations as openings into the nonordinary. A plane, a household, a sock drawer, a basement of jars, and an automated car can each become a way to examine [[EverydayWeirdness]], selfhood, age, dependence, artificial intelligence, and chosen transformation.

## Key Claims
- Tokarczuk's short fiction is read as demanding because it has to make a complete shift in perception with compressed means.
- Her "weird" mode is not detached from real feeling; it makes fear, grief, loneliness, care, and farewell concrete.
- The episode links her narrative practice to [[FourthPersonNarrative]] because the stories often let a self look at itself from an oblique position.
