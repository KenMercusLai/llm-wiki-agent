# Jürgen Habermas / 哈贝马斯

Jürgen Habermas appears in [[41-shijianqiao-fuchouan-chaoyue-habeimasi-de-qing-yu-fa-614550038]] as the public-sphere theorist invoked by the episode title and closing question. The episode uses him as a foil: it asks whether public political life should be understood only through rational-critical debate, or whether sensational news, melodrama, popular fiction, emotion, and gossip can also create political publicness.

The page is source-scoped. This ingest does not summarize Habermas's broader theory; it records how the episode uses his public-sphere frame to think about Republican-era Chinese media and the [[ShiJianqiaoRevengeCase]].

## Key Claims
- The episode's "beyond Habermas" framing challenges a narrow rationalist model of public discussion.
- The Shi Jianqiao case suggests that emotional, theatrical, and commercial media can shape collective judgment.
- The concept is most directly developed in [[SensationalMediaPublicSphere]].
