# Pew Research Center

[[sp-05-liuqing-linyao-qiangsha-yibao-jutou-an-beihou-de-sikao-xia-fenlie-de-meiguo-hui-you-xianzheng-weiji-ma-790241978]] adds Pew as the polling source [[LiuQing|刘擎]] cites for [[BlackLivesMatter]] support rising around 2020 and later declining by 2023. [[LinYao|林垚]] treats those numbers as relevant but insufficient for judging a movement's long-term effect, connecting Pew to [[IdentityPoliticsAsPoliticalSorting]] rather than only survey methodology.

Pew Research Center appears in [[tech-20260220-0220-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260220-0220-mp-tech-pod-128]] through a study cited by [[StephanieHughes]] on users rarely clicking source links after receiving AI-generated summaries. It also appears in [[tech-20260209-0209-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260209-0209-mp-tech-pod-128]] through smartphone-adoption estimates cited by [[CarlyUrban]]; that episode uses those estimates to show that smartphone ownership rose from about 35% in 2011 to about 85% in 2021.

The Pew context matters in two different measurement settings. For [[GoogleAIOverviews|Google AI Overviews]], click behavior helps explain why more prominent links may not restore publisher traffic; for the [[SmartphoneSurveyPenalty]], smartphone adoption makes [[SurveyModeEffects]] more likely to affect long-running measures such as the [[NationalFinancialCapabilityStudy]].
