# The Social Radars

The Social Radars is the podcast/show context for [[tsr-s2-wrapup-v1]], [[socialradarsseason1-wrapup-final-socialradarsseason1-wrapup-final]], [[socialradars-edithelliot-v3]], [[socialradarsseason1-pg-v4-updated-finallll-socialradarsseason1-pg-v4-updated-finallll]], [[socialradarspod-brianchesky-final]], [[socialradarspod-brianchesky2-final]], [[socialradarspod-stevehuffman-final]], [[socialradarspod-garrytan-final]], [[socialradarspod-davidlieb-final]], [[tsr-s2-trevorblackwell-v2]], [[socialradarsseason1-tracyyoung-final-1]], [[socialradarspod-tonyxu-final]], and [[socialradarspod-paulb-final]]. In the Season 1 wrap-up, [[JessicaLivingston]] and [[CarolynLevy]] say Season 1 grew from seven planned episodes to nine, announce a roughly month-long recording break before Season 2, and describe the show's value as real conversations where guests shared open stories, backgrounds, and challenges. In [[tsr-s2-wrapup-v1]], they close Season 2, name [[EmmettShear]], [[ParkerConrad]], and [[BrianChesky]] moments as especially memorable, confirm Season 3 is planned, and say they expect to publish more clips on [[YouTube]]. In the interview episodes, they interview founders about how companies and institutions were actually built, including [[EdithElliott]] on [[NooraHealth]], [[FamilyCaregiverTraining]], and nonprofit execution, [[PaulGraham]] on [[Viaweb]], [[YCombinator]], [[HackerNews]], and writing, [[BrianChesky]] on [[Airbnb]], hosting, trust, YC, COVID, and founder-led operating discipline, [[SteveHuffman]] on [[Reddit]], [[Hipmunk]], and YC, [[GarryTan]] on [[Posterous]], [[InitializedCapital]], and YC, [[DavidLieb]] on [[Bump]], [[Flock]], and [[GooglePhotos]], [[TrevorBlackwell]] on Viaweb, [[Anybots]], and early YC, [[TracyYoung]] on [[PlanGrid]], [[Autodesk]], and [[TigerEye]], [[TonyXu]] on [[DoorDash]], and [[PaulBuchheit]] on [[Gmail]], [[Google]], and [[FriendFeed]].

[[socialradarspod-brianarmstrong-final]] adds [[BrianArmstrong]] on [[Coinbase]], extending the show from YC, consumer products, and nonprofit execution into crypto-fintech company building. The episode preserves a founder account of the wallet-to-buy-button pivot, [[RegulatedCryptoTrustStrategy]], [[EarlyFintechFraudControls]], the [[SEC]] dispute, [[FTX]], [[MissionFocusedCompany]] policy, and [[FounderMotivationEvolution]].

[[socialradarsseason2-dimitri-final]] adds [[DimitriDadiomov]] on [[ModernTreasury]], extending the show further into fintech infrastructure. The episode turns hidden money movement into company-building material: [[LendingHome]] payment pain, bank partnerships, [[TrustHeavyInfrastructureSales]], [[SiliconValleyBank]] crisis response, [[FinancialOperationsResilience]], [[AcceleratedBankRuns]], and [[FedNow]] all become startup-history evidence rather than back-office detail.

[[socialradarss2-billclerico-final]] adds [[BillClerico]] on [[WePay]], extending the fintech branch backward into the pre-Stripe online-payments era and forward into climate resilience. The episode preserves the rough operating path from YC Summer 2009, bank credibility improvisation, university-club customer discovery, [[PaymentsInfrastructurePivot]], [[EarlyFintechFraudControls]], and the [[JPMorganChase]] acquisition to [[ConvectiveCapital]] and [[FireTechClimateResilience]].

The episode preserves the Collisons' teenage programming path, early [[YCombinator]]-orbit relationships, payments frustration, [[AppStore]] monetization contrast, `/dev/payments`, [[DeveloperFirstPaymentInfrastructure]], [[APIProductDesign]], and the later challenge of [[VisionToRealityExecution]] at company scale.

The episode makes [[FounderMode]] more exploratory than prescriptive: Graham says the phenomenon is real but still being mapped, and uses micromanagement, collaboration, [[SteveJobs]] and [[JonyIve]], [[VibeCoding]], [[Replit]], [[Stoke]], and [[PaulGross]] to probe its boundaries.

[[tsr-ycoffsite-jakeheller-audioonly-v1final-tsr-ycoffsite-jakeheller-audioonly-v1final]] adds the YC offsite conversation with [[JakeHeller]] on [[Casetext]]. [[socialradarspod-emmettshear-v2]] adds [[EmmettShear]] on [[Kiko]], [[JustinTV|Justin.tv]], and [[Twitch]], grounding the show more deeply in [[YCombinator]]'s first batch. The episode preserves the weak-calendar-idea lesson, Kiko's sale to [[Tucows]], the cash cushion that helped Justin.tv survive, the 2008 [[StartupRunwayDiscipline]] reset, [[InvestorRiskNarrative]] around Twitch's hard fundraising, and [[Amazon]] as a founder-friendly acquirer.

The show matters to the wiki as a startup-history source. Rather than only summarizing company outcomes, the episodes preserve operational memories: who had the half-awake browser-software idea, who wrote small checks, who turned around on a train, who wrote scripts, who printed applications, how offices were wired, what robot demos visitors saw, how construction drawings moved through job sites, how founders grieved while building, how second-time founders redesign company operating systems, how a food-delivery marketplace began with PDF menus and founders doing deliveries, how an air-bed hosting experiment became a trust marketplace, how Airbnb later used YC-style unscalable work and crisis focus at public-company scale, how Gmail's launch mixed internal feedback with capacity constraints, how Bump's huge adoption still failed to solve frequency, monetization, and post-acquisition product-politics problems, how a nonprofit used YC-style discipline to train family caregivers, and how YC's own community tools, forums, essays, and advice culture were built as it scaled.
