# The Social Radars

The Social Radars is the podcast/show context for [[socialradarspod-stevehuffman-final]], [[socialradarspod-garrytan-final]], [[socialradarspod-davidlieb-final]], [[tsr-s2-trevorblackwell-v2]], [[socialradarsseason1-tracyyoung-final-1]], [[socialradarspod-tonyxu-final]], and [[socialradarspod-paulb-final]]. In these episodes, [[JessicaLivingston]] and [[CarolynLevy]] interview founders about how companies and institutions were actually built, including [[SteveHuffman]] on [[Reddit]], [[Hipmunk]], and [[YCombinator]], [[GarryTan]] on [[Posterous]], [[InitializedCapital]], and YC, [[DavidLieb]] on [[Bump]], [[Flock]], and [[GooglePhotos]], [[TrevorBlackwell]] on [[Viaweb]], [[Anybots]], and early YC, [[TracyYoung]] on [[PlanGrid]], [[Autodesk]], and [[TigerEye]], [[TonyXu]] on [[DoorDash]], and [[PaulBuchheit]] on [[Gmail]], [[Google]], and [[FriendFeed]].

The show matters to the wiki as a startup-history source. Rather than only summarizing company outcomes, the episodes preserve operational memories: who turned around on a train, who wrote scripts, who printed applications, how offices were wired, what robot demos visitors saw, how construction drawings moved through job sites, how founders grieved while building, how second-time founders redesign company operating systems, how a food-delivery marketplace began with PDF menus and founders doing deliveries, how Gmail's launch mixed internal feedback with capacity constraints, how Bump's huge adoption still failed to solve frequency, monetization, and post-acquisition product-politics problems, and how YC's own community tools and advice culture were built as it scaled.
