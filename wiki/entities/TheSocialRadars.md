# The Social Radars

The Social Radars is the podcast/show context for [[socialradarsseason1-pg-v4-updated-finallll-socialradarsseason1-pg-v4-updated-finallll]], [[socialradarspod-brianchesky-final]], [[socialradarspod-stevehuffman-final]], [[socialradarspod-garrytan-final]], [[socialradarspod-davidlieb-final]], [[tsr-s2-trevorblackwell-v2]], [[socialradarsseason1-tracyyoung-final-1]], [[socialradarspod-tonyxu-final]], and [[socialradarspod-paulb-final]]. In these episodes, [[JessicaLivingston]] and [[CarolynLevy]] interview founders about how companies and institutions were actually built, including [[PaulGraham]] on [[Viaweb]], [[YCombinator]], [[HackerNews]], and writing, [[BrianChesky]] on [[Airbnb]], hosting, trust, and YC, [[SteveHuffman]] on [[Reddit]], [[Hipmunk]], and YC, [[GarryTan]] on [[Posterous]], [[InitializedCapital]], and YC, [[DavidLieb]] on [[Bump]], [[Flock]], and [[GooglePhotos]], [[TrevorBlackwell]] on Viaweb, [[Anybots]], and early YC, [[TracyYoung]] on [[PlanGrid]], [[Autodesk]], and [[TigerEye]], [[TonyXu]] on [[DoorDash]], and [[PaulBuchheit]] on [[Gmail]], [[Google]], and [[FriendFeed]].

The show matters to the wiki as a startup-history source. Rather than only summarizing company outcomes, the episodes preserve operational memories: who had the half-awake browser-software idea, who wrote small checks, who turned around on a train, who wrote scripts, who printed applications, how offices were wired, what robot demos visitors saw, how construction drawings moved through job sites, how founders grieved while building, how second-time founders redesign company operating systems, how a food-delivery marketplace began with PDF menus and founders doing deliveries, how an air-bed hosting experiment became a trust marketplace, how Gmail's launch mixed internal feedback with capacity constraints, how Bump's huge adoption still failed to solve frequency, monetization, and post-acquisition product-politics problems, and how YC's own community tools, forums, essays, and advice culture were built as it scaled.
