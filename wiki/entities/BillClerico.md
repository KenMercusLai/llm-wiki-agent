# Bill Clerico

Bill Clerico is the [[WePay]] co-founder and former CEO interviewed in [[socialradarss2-billclerico-final]]. The episode traces his path from investment banking at Jefferies Broadview, through the [[YCombinator]] Summer 2009 batch with [[RichAberman]], to WePay's 2017 acquisition by [[JPMorganChase]].

Clerico's founder story is useful because it shows regulated startup learning under pressure. WePay had to improvise bank credibility, find early users through manual work, survive weak consumer-payment frequency, absorb major fraud losses, and complete a difficult [[PaymentsInfrastructurePivot]] from group payments into API infrastructure.

[[tsr-s5-tomblomfield-v2-audio-tsr-s5-tomblomfield-v2-audio]] adds Clerico as an external warning to [[GoCardless]]. [[TomBlomfield]] says Clerico told the team their bill-splitting idea was bad, but the founders initially discounted the advice because they suspected a potential competitor was trying to discourage them. The later GoCardless pivot made Clerico's warning part of a second [[PaymentsInfrastructurePivot]] case.

After WePay, Clerico spent time on a Mendocino County ranch, joined a volunteer fire department, and built [[ConvectiveCapital]] around [[FireTechClimateResilience]]. The episode presents this as another founder-product-fit pattern: direct operational exposure to wildfire and firefighting shaped the market thesis.
