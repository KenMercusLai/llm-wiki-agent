# Edward Thorp

Edward Thorp appears in [[e153-gushen-de-paiju-fuli-gongshi-kaili-gongshi-lmyvi9mnlaqib-baspjiehz3epqc]] as the bridge from mathematical gambling systems to Wall Street. The episode describes his blackjack card-counting work, relationship with [[ClaudeShannon]], casino tests, and later move into options, convertible-bond arbitrage, and early [[QuantitativeInvesting]].

## Key Claims
- Thorp is used to show that [[InvestmentEdge]] comes from rule understanding, probability, payoff, and bet size together.
- His casino stories warn that formal rules are not the whole game; counterparty behavior and execution risk can change the outcome.
- His later finance work makes [[KellyCriterion]] and [[PositionSizing]] part of a broader investing discipline rather than only a gambling story.

## Connections
- [[ClaudeShannon]] — collaborator and information-theory reference in the episode.
- [[KellyCriterion]] — repeated-bet sizing idea associated with the Thorp thread.
- [[QuantitativeInvesting]] — finance style the episode connects to Thorp's probability and arbitrage work.
- [[InvestmentRiskManagement]] — practical reason the episode studies Thorp rather than only the blackjack anecdote.
