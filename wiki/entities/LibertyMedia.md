# Liberty Media

Liberty Media is the company that acquired [[FormulaOne]] in the episode's modern business arc. In [[formula-1-1]], Liberty buys the sport at an $8 billion enterprise value in 2016 and then shifts the operating model away from [[BernieEcclestone]]'s centralized but adversarial style.

The source credits Liberty with professionalizing stakeholder relations, improving digital and social access, investing in U.S. market development, supporting [[DriveToSurvive]], and helping introduce [[CostCapEconomics]]. Its role is to turn F1 from a valuable but under-modernized asset into a better aligned [[SportsEntertainmentFlywheel]].

## Source Position
- Liberty viewed F1 as underdeveloped in U.S. audience growth, storytelling, digital access, fan data, and promoter relations.
- Replacing Bernie with Chase Carey signaled a move from founder-dealmaker control toward modern league management.
- Liberty's upside depends on media monetization, fan expansion, race-calendar choices, and preserving team/promoter cooperation.
