# Nestle

Nestle appears in [[141-kafei-zhanzheng-2026-jigouhua-yu-bentuhua-feng-tou-quan-1-141-1]] as the prior owner of [[BlueBottleCoffee]]. The episode uses [[Nestle]] to distinguish consumer-goods ownership from store-chain operation: packaged coffee and global CPG strength do not automatically solve store rollout, local menu cadence, and China retail competition.

The source says Nestle sold Blue Bottle to [[CenturiumCapital]] at a lower valuation than the original acquisition level. In the episode's interpretation, that sale illustrates [[CoffeeChainLocalization]] and ownership fit: an offline high-end coffee brand may need an owner closer to retail operations and local strategic use than a global CPG company can provide.

## Source Position
- Nestle's Blue Bottle ownership is treated as a mismatch between CPG capability and store-led coffee expansion.
- The sale creates the source's link between [[BlueBottleCoffee]], [[CenturiumCapital]], and [[LuckinCoffee]].
- The case qualifies [[CPGDistribution]] because offline branded retail can demand different capabilities from packaged consumer-product distribution.

## Connections
- [[BlueBottleCoffee]] - brand sold by Nestle in the source's account.
- [[CenturiumCapital]] and [[LuckinCoffee]] - buyer and portfolio context.
- [[CoffeeChainLocalization]], [[ExperientialRetail]], and [[CPGDistribution]] - concepts clarified by the sale.
