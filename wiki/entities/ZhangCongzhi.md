# 张从志 / Zhang Congzhi

Zhang Congzhi appears in [[e229-cong-shougong-zuofang-dao-quanqiu-diyi-zhongguo-dongli-dianchi-nixi-shi]] as a journalist covering the power-battery industry. He treats the 2009 [[TenCitiesThousandVehicles]] program as an earlier and more foundational inflection point than the later EV export milestones because it created demand before the private market was ready.

He also sharpens the manufacturing discussion by focusing on consistency and safety. In his account, vehicle batteries require many cells to work together, so small deviations in cell voltage, resistance, capacity, material cleanliness, moisture, or process control can become pack-level reliability and thermal-risk problems.

[[ep270-yi-mei-xinpian-de-manchang-zhengtu-women-li-suanli-ziyou-haiyou-duoyuan-lm7lxlmcnjwnawtq-9typc-fnrci]] extends Zhang's manufacturing-reporting role from batteries into semiconductors. In [[TalkSanlian|Talk三联]], he explains chips through daily-life examples, [[GPU]] parallelism, [[SemiconductorSupplyChain|design/manufacturing/packaging]], [[ElectronicDesignAutomation|EDA]], [[PhotolithographyBottleneck|lithography]], [[TapeOutRisk|tape-out risk]], and the gap between domestic chip production and cost-effective large-scale substitution.
