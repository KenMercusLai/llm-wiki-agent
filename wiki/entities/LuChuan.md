# 陆川 / Lu Chuan

陆川 / Lu Chuan is the film director interviewed in [[e234-weilai-shipai-dianying-hai-cunzai-ma-yu-daoyan-luchuan-liaoliao-ai-gei-yingshiren-de-kongju-yu-ziyou-b2be7093-3366-4ee2-8a7a-625f06206ae5]]. He uses practical film and visual-effects experience to explain where [[VideoModels]] and [[AIVideoProductionWorkflow]] already matter: fast keyframes, character and scene concepts, previs, and communication before a production spends months of labor.

His argument is not anti-AI. The source presents him as eager to use AI, but he keeps separating tool efficiency from artistic judgment. AI can make a shot cheap enough to test, yet a film still has to meet film-history, audience, and commercial-delivery standards rather than getting a quality discount because it was generated.

The source also makes Lu Chuan the main voice for [[LiveActionFilmUnderAI]]. He argues that AI will force creators and investors to justify live-action shooting, leaving the strongest case for real crews, natural light, and unrepeatable human performance moments such as the line-discovery example he gives from [[NanjingNanjing]].

## Key Points
- He treats AI as a tool comparable to a pen, camera, or editing table.
- He sees AI as especially useful for compressed previsualization and visual-effects ideation.
- He warns that mass-aesthetic model alignment can create homogeneous characters and weaken authorial style.
- He argues for [[IndustrialGradeFilmModels]] because feature film and long-form drama require more stable quality than casual social video.
- He believes live action survives when it captures human moments AI cannot easily generate or justify replacing.
