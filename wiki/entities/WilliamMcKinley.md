# William McKinley

William McKinley appears in [[vol-113-cong-jiqian-ye-zhiku-wenjian-zhong-goule-telangpu-2-0-zhizheng-jihua-beihou-de-ren-jigou-sixiang-he-mailuo-lihnknc-3om5evexocb6a6zdwueu]] as the historical president the episode uses to interpret [[DonaldTrump]]'s tariff politics. Drawing on an outside article discussed by the host, the episode says Trump resembles McKinley more than many other predecessors because McKinley combined high tariffs, industrial protection, gold-standard commitments, and territorial/political expansion.

The page records the analogy as the source's interpretation, not as a settled historical equation. The useful point is the policy bundle: [[AmericanProtectionistTradition]] can combine import barriers, weaker domestic regulation, attraction of technology and skilled migrants, low income-tax reliance, and the leverage of a large domestic market.
