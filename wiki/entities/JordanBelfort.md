# Jordan Belfort

Jordan Belfort appears in [[ep28-bainian-jinrong-zhapian-shi-jieji-kuayue-yu-liangdang-ruyu-de-juli-ltpkaw9wxzpxlxo3mhh-0rkimgcj]] as the *Wolf of Wall Street* figure used to explain sales-driven securities fraud. The source emphasizes his sales background, entry into high-commission low-priced stock sales after Black Monday, creation of [[StrattonOakmont]], and later post-prison commercialization through books and sales courses.

## Key Claims
- Belfort's case is framed around sales infrastructure: scripts, urgency, confidence, and commission incentives turned weak products into persuasive transactions.
- The episode treats penny-stock selling as dangerous when the seller's fee can reach a large share of the trade and the buyer has little ability to evaluate quality or liquidity.
- His post-prison media and training career is used to show how crime stories can be repackaged as business mythology.
- The case links fraud to [[InvestorEducation]] because the victim must understand incentives, venue, liquidity, and seller accountability before trusting persuasion.
