# Houston Rockets

The Houston Rockets appear in [[planet-money-vs-the-nbas-tanking-problem]] as the historical NBA example that makes [[SportsTanking|tanking]] economically legible. The episode says the 1984 Rockets lost heavily late in the season when Hakeem Olajuwon and Michael Jordan were entering the draft, then selected Olajuwon and later won championships with him.

The Rockets example matters because it shows why bad incentives can persist. Tanking may damage fan experience and league credibility, but for one franchise the upside from a top pick can be enormous.
