# Zhang Yi

Zhang Yi is the founder and CEO of [[WeilaiBuyuan]] and the central guest in [[jushen-zhineng-de-taotian-da-paomo-zhong-ta-yijing-ba-jiqiren-songjin-300-ge-jiating-duihua-zhang-yi-weilaibuyuan-chuangshiren-ceo-lic8b7dkxts3qjrs6af1rgbf4xrf]]. The episode presents him as a repeat entrepreneur who previously founded [[ZhangmenOneOnOne]], then moved into [[HomeServiceRobots]] after the education-sector Double Reduction shock forced a major rethink of risk, meaning, and long-term company building.

Zhang's robotics thesis combines his electronic-information background, years of serving family users, and belief that [[EmbodiedAI]] needs early physical deployment. He argues that [[F2HomeRobot]] should enter real homes first, learn from use, and build a [[HouseholdRobotDataFlywheel]] before chasing broad sales volume or public attention.

## Key Points
- He describes the [[ZhangmenOneOnOne]] contraction as a founder experience that changed his tolerance for short-term strategy changes and investor pressure.
- He treats child care and light housework as the first plausible household service categories for [[F2HomeRobot]].
- He prefers sustainable commercialization, rental feedback, renewal, referral, and user co-creation over demo-driven hype.
- He accepts that the current [[EmbodiedAI]] market contains bubble dynamics, while still believing the long-term direction is correct.
