# Julius Caesar / 尤利乌斯·恺撒

Julius Caesar / 尤利乌斯·恺撒 is the central figure in [[124-kaisa-zhisi-lishi-de-yunjiao-shifou-yi-shixiao-781894361]]. The episode begins from his famous cultural traces - conquest sayings, the Julian calendar, titles derived from Caesar, and [[CleopatraVII|Cleopatra]] stories - then turns to the more difficult question of how his rise fit into the collapse of the [[RomanRepublic|Roman Republic]].

The source's Caesar is deliberately mixed. He is ambitious, image-conscious, and willing to gamble the republic by crossing the Rubicon, yet he is also presented as courageous, politically magnetic, sometimes clement toward enemies, and interested in public works and institutional enlargement. His problem is not only character. He inherits a political world already damaged by [[LateRomanRepublicCrisis]], [[MilitaryPersonalization]], elite rivalry, and civil war.

His death in the [[CaesarAssassination|assassination on the Ides of March]] becomes the episode's test case for [[PersonalRuleLegitimacyCrisis]]: Caesar had accumulated honors and authority beyond ordinary republican form, but he had not found a stable legitimacy or succession structure that republican elites and citizens could accept.
