Thailand enters the wiki through [[e232-canyin-chuhai-you-xin-zhaor-ma-cong-dingtaifeng-yu-gongcha-naxia-meiguo-chudai-xiaoguan-liaoqi-3bb14dd4-0b13-4173-9d5b-0e63bf98ae1d]] as a food-promotion and tourism comparison case. The episode says Thai restaurants are overrepresented among Asian restaurants in the [[UnitedStates]] relative to the Thai-American population share, then links that presence to government-supported cuisine promotion, chef training, English education, restaurant activity, and tourism familiarity.

The source uses Thailand to show a state-backed version of [[CultureLedFoodAdoption]]. Thai food does not travel only because individual restaurants are good; it is helped by a more coordinated consumer scene where tourism, flavor expectations, restaurant presentation, and official support reinforce each other.
