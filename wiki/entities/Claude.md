# Claude

[[live-anthropic-co-founder-on-ai-and-jobs]] adds Claude as a general frontier model moving toward long-task work and cyber capability. [[JackClark]] predicts that by April 2027, systems like Claude may handle tasks that would take a person roughly 150 hours, and the episode says a cyber-capable Claude system had been shared with roughly 40 companies. This extends Claude from a procurement case into [[AIEconomicDiffusion]], [[AIAutomationRedistribution]], and [[AICyberDefenseUtility]].

[[tech-20260306-0306-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260306-0306-mp-tech-pod-128]] updates Claude from a model in procurement dispute to a possible contractor-removal problem. The episode says Anthropic technology would not be allowed near critical military or warfighting systems under the reported restriction, though lower-risk business uses such as payroll or accounting might still be acceptable.

Claude appears in [[tech-20260227-0227-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260227-0227-mp-tech-pod-128]] as [[Anthropic]]'s model already used by the [[USDepartmentOfDefense]], including for classified purposes. The episode says Claude is deeply entrenched enough that off-boarding it would be difficult, which gives Anthropic leverage even as the Pentagon seeks broader access.

The source turns Claude from a general frontier model into a procurement and governance case. Its value to defense users makes [[DefenseAIProcurement]] concrete, while Anthropic's red lines around mass surveillance and autonomous weapons turn Claude access into a [[FrontierModelUsePolicyConflict]] rather than only a technical capability question.
