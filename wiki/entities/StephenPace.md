# Stephen Pace

Stephen Pace appears in [[bookstore-economics]] as [[WWNorton|W. W. Norton]]'s director of trade sales. The episode uses his role to move from local bookstore buying to publisher-level [[BookPrintRunStrategy]], where order estimates, channel demand, warehouse limits, returns, remaindering, and pulping all shape how many copies are printed before launch.

Pace's perspective extends the earlier [[inside-a-book-auction]] branch from acquisition to fulfillment. If [[TomMayer]] represents the editor's acquisition risk, Pace represents the downstream sales risk of making enough books to satisfy demand without creating excess inventory.

## Key Claims
- Pace starts modeling demand when an editor brings in a book idea for serious consideration.
- Sales-rep estimates from independents, chains, online retailers, and other channels inform the first print run.
- He frames the first print run as aspirational but still accountable to sell-through.
- His goal for the [[PlanetMoneyBook]] is a strong first run that can lead to second and third printings without excessive waste.
