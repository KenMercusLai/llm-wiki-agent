# Inuit

The Inuit appear in [[how-to-get-what-greenland-has-with-permission]] through the episode's description of [[Greenland]] as about 90 percent Indigenous Inuit. The source links that demographic reality to the political problem of outsiders discussing Greenland as if it were empty land or a resource package.

The episode also connects Inuit land norms to Greenland's land system. It says people in Greenland may receive permission to build but do not own the land underneath, and it frames that system through the idea that land is not for sale but shared.

In the source, this matters for [[StrategicAccessWithoutAnnexation]]. Greenlandic cooperation, trade, and defense arrangements have to be understood through local agency and permission rather than an outside state's desire to buy, own, or control land.
