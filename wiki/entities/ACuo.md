# 阿错 / Acuo

阿错 is the Yunnan writer interviewed in [[150-chi-jun-he-acuo-liao-yunnan-de-jizong-songrong-jianshouqing-ca-koushui-897563165]]. The episode presents her as coming from northwest [[Yunnan|云南]] and as the author of [[Cangcheng|《苍城》]], with local knowledge of mushroom eating, mountain gathering customs, [[TeaHorseRoad|茶马古道]] memory, and [[Lijiang|丽江]]-area life.

Her role in the wiki is not just "guest who likes mushrooms." She supplies the episode's strongest boundary conditions: unknown mixed mushrooms should not enter the household, [[Jianshouqing|见手青]] should not be treated as a joke, and tourists should not damage mushroom nests or gather unsafe species for photos. She also turns food detail into fiction detail, explaining how mushroom taste, altered perception, horse transport, and women muleteers enter [[Cangcheng|《苍城》]].

## Key Claims
- 阿错's mushroom hierarchy centers [[Jizong|鸡枞]], while still giving source-scoped taste accounts of [[Matsutake|松茸]], [[GanbaMushroom|干巴菌]], and [[Jianshouqing|见手青]].
- Her local knowledge links taste to place: termite nests, mountain routes, household sorting, seasonal expectation, and local names all matter.
- Her account of [[Cangcheng|《苍城》]] supports [[PlaceBasedFiction]] because the novel's details are rooted in lived geography and inherited survival knowledge.
- She frames contact with mountains and trees as a double feeling of smallness and aliveness, contributing to [[NatureContactSelfPerception]].
