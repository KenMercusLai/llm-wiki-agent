# George E. Johnson

George E. Johnson is the founder figure in [[how-black-hair-care-grew-black-power]]. The episode follows him from poor childhood work in Chicago into on-the-job chemistry learning, the partnership with [[OrvilleNelson]] around [[UltraWave]], the building of [[JohnsonProducts]], and the later national expansion of [[AfroSheen]].

The source presents Johnson as a founder whose advantage came from combining product improvement, customer observation, distribution, community commitment, and media instinct. He used [[SoulTrain]] as a national marketing channel, helped take over [[IndependenceBank]], and later described how going public reduced control and made Johnson Products' market easier for larger companies to read.

## Key Claims
- Johnson did not invent Black hair straightening from nothing; he made an existing barbershop practice more stable, usable, and commercially scalable.
- His business judgment depended on observing Black customers and cultural change closely, especially the shift from straightened styles to afros.
- He used media sponsorship and community institutions as part of company-building, not as separate philanthropy.
- His account of public-company board pressure makes him an example for [[PublicListingControlTradeoff]] and [[FounderControl]].
