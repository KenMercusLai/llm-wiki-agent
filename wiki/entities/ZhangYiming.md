# Zhang Yiming

Zhang Yiming appears in [[touteng-dazhan-ba-nian-hou-zai-ba-zijie-he-tengxun-zai-gege-zhanchang-shang-de-jingzheng-zhuyi-chaikai-zijie-tiaodong-di-6-ji-lvglr-jws7o7utjauxnoqqasj91d]] as the [[ByteDance]] founder whose 2018 WeChat Moments exchange with [[PonyMa]] helped turn the [[ToutengWar]] into a public platform conflict. The episode says Zhang celebrated [[TikTok]] reaching the top of an app ranking while mentioning WeChat blocking and Weishi copying/moving content, after which Pony Ma replied that the claim could be read as defamation.

For the wiki, Zhang matters less as a biography than as the founder lens on ByteDance's relationship with [[Tencent]]. The hosts say he had long treated Tencent as a gravitational force ByteDance could not ignore, which made even non-core-business disputes feel existential when [[Douyin]] was under regulatory and content-safety pressure after [[NeihanDuanzi]].

[[quanmian-yazhi-buliu-kongdang-zijie-tiaodong-ruhe-zuo-zengzhang-zijie-tiaodong-di-7-ji-lqszvmur6jv8b9xt8rjgmwfdqtec]] adds Zhang as the founder-level explanation for why ByteDance growth is hard to copy. [[XuHongliang]] says the hardest element is the "一号位": strategic difficulty judgment, willingness to authorize large budgets, and the ability to let [[ByteDanceGrowthSystem]] operate across data, algorithms, product, finance, and engineering.
