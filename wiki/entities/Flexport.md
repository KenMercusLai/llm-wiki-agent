# Flexport

Flexport is [[RyanPetersen]]'s global logistics company in [[tsr-s3-ryanpetersen-v6-tsr-s3-ryanpetersen-v6]]. The episode describes it as a technology and operations platform for moving goods through air freight, ocean freight, trucking, rail, warehouses, customs, insurers, banks, governments, fulfillment, and parcel delivery.

The source frames Flexport's growth as [[CustomerPull]] across a messy market. The company started with customs brokerage because that was the regulated wedge Petersen had prepared for, but real customers wanted customs bundled with freight forwarding. Flexport therefore broadened from a narrow brokerage application into [[GlobalLogisticsCoordination]].

Flexport's operating model is also a case in [[LogisticsWorkflowAutomation]]. Petersen says the company decomposed door-to-door container movement into 108 tasks, assigned those tasks through workflow screens, and used human operators while software gradually absorbed more work. The episode treats this as the practical path through logistics: full automation had to follow the structure of actual freight work, not bypass it.

The crisis sections add [[LogisticsCrisisResponse]]. Flexport responded to pandemic PPE shortages, Red Sea shipping disruption, and Long Beach port congestion by combining data visibility, field investigation, public explanation, and physical capacity such as borrowed passenger planes and leased 747s.
