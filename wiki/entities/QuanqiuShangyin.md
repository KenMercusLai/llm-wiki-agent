# 《全球上瘾》

《全球上瘾》 is the coffee-centered material-history book discussed in [[74-quanqiu-shangyin-a-kafei-wo-heise-de-apoluo-678615763]]. The episode presents the book as a 1930s work that writes [[Coffee|coffee]] like a mythic historical protagonist rather than as a neutral commodity entry or consumption guide.

The book's appeal in the episode comes from narrative force. Origin legends, Arab religious debates, the [[OttomanEmpire|Ottoman Empire]], European taste adaptation, Paris coffeehouses, French royal monopoly, factory and army use, speculation, and [[Brazil]]ian overproduction are organized as a story of a drink reshaping civilization. That makes the book a source case for [[MaterialHistoryNarrative]].

The episode also marks a limitation. Because the book was first published in 1934, its treatment of colonial plantation labor and slavery is described as less critical and developed than a contemporary material history would need to be.
