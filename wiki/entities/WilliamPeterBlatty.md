# William Peter Blatty

William Peter Blatty appears in [[how-to-make-a-book-into-a-bestseller]] as the author who sued the [[NewYorkTimes|New York Times]] after [[LegionNovel|Legion]] did not appear on the [[NewYorkTimesBestsellerList|New York Times bestseller list]]. The episode uses his lawsuit to explain why the list is legally treated as editorial content rather than a public accounting of raw sales.

Blatty's case matters because it makes the money value of bestseller status explicit. The source says he argued that exclusion from the list could affect book profits, paperback rights, and movie-rights value.

## Key Claims
- Blatty believed Legion sold enough to deserve placement on the Times list.
- He argued that absence from the list caused large commercial losses.
- The courts sided with the New York Times and treated the list as protected editorial judgment.
- The case clarifies that high sales alone do not guarantee a Times bestseller listing.
