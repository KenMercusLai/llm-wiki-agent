# Vanda Ilson Siqueira

Vanda Ilson Siqueira is [[DecoBarcelos]]'s son in [[the-little-pet-fish-that-saved-a-town-in-the-amazon]]. He manages the hotel where the reporters stayed in [[Barcelos]], making him the source's next-generation example of the town's shift from wild ornamental fish toward tourism.

His view is practical rather than nostalgic. The episode says he sees tourists bringing money and hotel expansion, while treating piabero life more as local history. That places him on the other side of [[AramaraCastro]]'s worry that [[HeritageTourismPivot]] may replace independent fish work with visitor-service labor.

## Key Claims
- Vanda Ilson works in the hotel sector rather than as a piabero.
- He says tourists are bringing significant money to [[Barcelos]].
- The episode uses him to show how the [[PiaberoEconomy]] becomes memory for a younger generation.
- His role connects local family transition to broader [[TourismDependentSmallEconomy]] risk.

## Connections
- [[DecoBarcelos]], [[Barcelos]], and [[PeacockBass]] - family, place, and tourism context.
- [[HeritageTourismPivot]] - central transition he represents.
- [[AramaraCastro]] - contrasting local view of the shift.
- [[PiaberoEconomy]] - older economy he sees as history.
