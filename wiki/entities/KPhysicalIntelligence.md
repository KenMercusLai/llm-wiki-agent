# K (Physical Intelligence)

K is the [[PhysicalIntelligence]] researcher interviewed in [[146-dui-physical-intelligence-ke-li-yiming-4-xiaoshi-fangtan-pi-de-kaiyuan-moxing-yanjiu-jiqiren-de-jianghu-zupu-yu-zhujiao-ljmazvdvad7o5md-nuiompd6-1nv]]. The source identifies him as a reinforcement-learning-focused researcher and uses his path through programming, psychology, economics, game theory, machine learning, imitation learning, and robotics to explain why current robot intelligence is a system problem rather than a single-algorithm problem.

K's technical position is pragmatic. He values real-machine performance, learned methods, and [[RobotExperienceData]], but also keeps the traditional robotics insistence on physical success and deployment constraints. In the episode, this makes him a bridge between old robotics control/planning culture and the current [[VisionLanguageActionModels]] and [[RobotReinforcementLearning]] wave.

The episode also gives K a personal intellectual profile: he writes science fiction, thinks about games and future society, uses AI agents such as [[ClaudeCode]] to change his work process, and is skeptical of simple answers to AI doom, consciousness, robot race, and life-meaning questions.
