# 《玄怪录》

《玄怪录》 is the strange-tale collection discussed in [[76-xuanguailu-wantang-dangzheng-meidanwu-zaixiang-xie-damahou-de-gushi-683323220]]. The episode presents it as a central example of [[TangChuanqi|唐传奇]]: politically situated, elegantly written, and full of sudden movement between court life, hidden worlds, foxes, dragons, object spirits, Daoist ritual, and moral tests.

The page is source-scoped. Its importance in the wiki is not a complete bibliography, but the way [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] uses the collection to show that strange tales can carry [[LateTangPoliticalPoetics]], [[ChineseFolkReligionLayering]], and [[TangStrangeTaleRepaymentEthics]] at the same time.

## Source Position
- [[NiuSengru|牛僧儒]] is presented as the authorial center, while the episode repeatedly keeps his political career and literary temperament in the same frame.
- The collection is contrasted with later erotic ghost-story expectations: supernatural encounters often lead to payment, rescue, repayment, or obligation rather than romance.
- The episode treats the stories as literary experiences full of rhythm, imagery, riddles, and ethical aftertaste, not only as folklore data.
