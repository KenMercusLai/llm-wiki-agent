# Ben Castleman

Ben Castleman appears in [[would-you-trust-an-economist-with-your-economy]] as the [[NewYorkTimes]] economics reporter discussing why economists are losing influence and trust. He frames the [[AmericanEconomicAssociation]] session as a moment when the profession had to defend its public authority rather than simply present technical work.

Castleman's role in the source is interpretive. He says economics is in some ways more credible because it is more data-driven and nuanced, but he also argues that economists gain trust when they acknowledge what they got wrong and explain how their views changed.
