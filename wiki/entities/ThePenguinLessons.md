# 《企鹅课》 / The Penguin Lessons

《企鹅课》 / The Penguin Lessons is the memoir discussed in [[138-qie-ke-wo-zai-haitan-shang-jiandao-yiwei-q-laoshi-842331726]]. The episode presents it as [[TomMichell|Tom Michell / 汤姆·米切尔]]'s nonfiction account of finding an oil-covered [[MagellanicPenguin|Magellanic penguin / 麦哲伦企鹅]] in [[Uruguay]], naming it [[JuanSalvadorPenguin|Juan Salvador / 胡安·萨尔瓦多]], and bringing it into [[StGeorgesSchool|St. George's School / 圣乔治学校]] in [[Argentina]].

The book matters in the wiki because the episode reads it across several layers at once: a comic rescue story, a school-community memory, a 1970s Argentina setting, and an ecological argument about [[OilPollutionWildlifeHarm]]. The penguin is not only an emblem of cuteness; it becomes a case for [[WildAnimalRescueResponsibility]], [[CompanionAnimalHealth]], and the way vulnerable beings absorb hidden costs of human systems.
