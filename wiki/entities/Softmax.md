# Softmax

Softmax is the AI company [[EmmettShear]] discusses in [[tsr-ycoffsite-emmettshear-v1-audioonly-tsr-ycoffsite-emmettshear-v1-audioonly]]. Shear says he started the company after his brief [[OpenAI]] CEO experience pushed him toward the question of how to make AI robustly aligned with humanity.

In the source, Softmax's core research question is whether agents can recognize collective belonging. Shear frames alignment as the ability to understand oneself, understand other agents, and recognize when one is part of a shared "we." Softmax is therefore building simulations, benchmarks, and reinforcement-learning environments to observe whether agents can act as a group rather than only follow isolated instructions.

The episode also makes Softmax an organization-design case. Shear says the company began with him and [[AdamGoldstein]], spent roughly a year with an unclear version of the current ideas, and became clearer after [[DavidBlumen]] proposed an architecture that matched the direction Shear thought the company needed. Softmax's method emphasizes [[LearningEnvironmentCenteredAITraining]]: the environment, peer experience, and feedback setting matter as much as model architecture.
