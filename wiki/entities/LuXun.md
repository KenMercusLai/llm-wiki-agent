# 鲁迅

鲁迅 appears in [[58-rulin-waishi-jiashe-kaogong-chengle-weiyi-chulu-645207859]] as the critic whose evaluation helps the hosts frame [[RulinWaishi|《儒林外史》]] as a high point of Chinese satirical fiction. The episode repeatedly invokes his praise to explain why the novel's comedy feels painful: it produces laughter, but the laughter exposes structural humiliation and moral deformation.

The source also links Lu Xun backward and forward in the satire tradition. [[RulinWaishi|《儒林外史》]] is treated as a predecessor to later works such as 《孔乙己》 and 《阿Q正传》, especially in their interest in failed scholars, status performance, social cruelty, and characters who reveal a system by moving through it.
