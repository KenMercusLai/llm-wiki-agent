# 鲁迅

鲁迅 becomes a primary author page through [[75-gushi-xinbian-kulou-bei-fuhuo-hou-hui-ganji-zhuangzi-ma-681010087]], where [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] rereads him through [[GushiXinbian|《故事新编》]]. The episode resists both textbook monumentality and simplified "fighter" caricature, emphasizing his humor, myth rewriting, scientific background, and suspicion toward any doctrine reduced to use.

In that source, [[LiShui|《理水》]] lets Lu Xun use the [[YuTheGreat|大禹]] flood-control myth to satirize insulated scholarship, bureaucratic performance, and [[DisasterAestheticization]]. [[QiSi|《起死》]] turns the [[Zhuangzi|庄子]] skull tradition into a critique of [[QiWuLun|《齐物论》]] as status shield when [[YangDa|杨大]] asks for material compensation.

The source's larger claim is [[InstrumentalBeliefCritique]]. Lu Xun is presented as neither anti-science nor simply anti-religion: he values myth as a root of religion, art, and literature, while attacking "吃教" and any use of science, revolution, loyalty, filiality, or celebrity worship as opportunistic tools.

鲁迅 also appears in [[58-rulin-waishi-jiashe-kaogong-chengle-weiyi-chulu-645207859]] as the critic whose evaluation helps the hosts frame [[RulinWaishi|《儒林外史》]] as a high point of Chinese satirical fiction. The episode repeatedly invokes his praise to explain why the novel's comedy feels painful: it produces laughter, but the laughter exposes structural humiliation and moral deformation.

The Rulin Waishi source links Lu Xun backward and forward in the satire tradition. [[RulinWaishi|《儒林外史》]] is treated as a predecessor to later works such as 《孔乙己》 and 《阿Q正传》, especially in their interest in failed scholars, status performance, social cruelty, and characters who reveal a system by moving through it.

[[57-shuojie-hanzi-shiniaopi-weisha-shi-shi-zitou-642970319]] adds a language-history mention. The episode cites Lu Xun's "汉字不灭，中国必亡" line as part of the modern debate over whether phonetic writing looked more advanced than Chinese characters. In this source, Lu Xun is not the target of criticism so much as a marker of the historical pressure that makes [[HanziTransdialectalFunction]] and [[ChineseCharacterEvidenceDiscipline]] worth explaining carefully.
