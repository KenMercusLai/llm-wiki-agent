# Limitless

Limitless is [[DanSiroker]]'s AI memory company in [[tsr-s3-dansiroker-v3-tsr-s3-dansiroker-v3]], described as the rebranded successor to [[RewindAI]]. Dan presents it as personalized AI powered by what a person has seen, said, or heard, with meeting preparation, live notes, post-meeting summaries, and drafting from personal context as key use cases.

The episode contrasts Limitless with Rewind's native Mac app. Limitless is described as cloud-based and includes a lightweight wearable pendant meant to capture in-person conversations. That makes the company a concrete source case for [[PersonalAIMemory]], [[PersistentAgentMemory]], [[OSLevelContext]], [[WearableAIAssistant]], and [[ConsentBasedRecording]].

The privacy claims in the source should remain attributed to Dan. He says Limitless uses consent mode, confidential cloud protections, encryption, anonymization, legal protections, and a commitment not to sell data. The source does not independently verify those technical or legal claims, but it makes privacy architecture part of the product thesis rather than an external policy afterthought.

## Key Claims
- Limitless uses personal context with large language models so drafts and summaries can reflect the user's own history.
- The pendant is meant to expand memory capture beyond screens and online meetings into in-person conversation.
- Dan says consent mode should avoid recording a new voice until that person has opted in.
- Dan says the company designs cloud protections partly with subpoena and decryption-risk concerns in mind.
- [[BrettBaechek]] and [[Stammy]] are described as co-founders promoted from within.
