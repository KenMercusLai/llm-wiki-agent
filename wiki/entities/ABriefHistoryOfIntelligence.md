# A Brief History of Intelligence

*A Brief History of Intelligence* is the book [[WuTao]] strongly recommends in [[biancheng-de-neiranji-shidai-neihe-konghuang-71-1-71-1]]. The episode uses it to connect biological intelligence, nervous systems, reinforcement learning, simulation, mentalizing, and current AI models.

## Source Position
- The book is presented as one of the episode's two strongest recommendations, alongside trying [[ImmersiveTranslate]].
- The hosts use it to frame intelligence as an evolutionary path from simple stimulus-response behavior toward simulation and modeling of other minds.
- [[WuTao]] asks [[ChatGPT]] where it would place itself in that progression; the model's answer is summarized as being closer to simulation than genuine mentalizing.
- The discussion connects the book to [[WorldModels]] and to the episode's caution that fluent model self-description is not the same thing as demonstrated understanding.

## Connections
- [[ChatGPT]] - model used for the self-placement example.
- [[WorldModels]] - adjacent technical concept around simulation and internal environment modeling.
- [[HumanJudgmentUnderAI]] - humans still need to interpret whether a model's explanation means understanding.
- [[SecondRenaissance]] - broader learning and self-cultivation frame around AI-era education.
