# Johnson Products

Johnson Products is the Black-owned hair-care company at the center of [[how-black-hair-care-grew-black-power]]. [[GeorgeEJohnson]] and [[JoanJohnson]] built the company from the [[UltraWave]] straightener business into a nationally visible consumer-products company associated with [[AfroSheen]], Ultra Sheen, Black employment, the [[IndependenceBank]] loan to [[MartinLutherKingJr]]'s organization, and sponsorship of [[SoulTrain]].

The page matters because Johnson Products turns [[BlackHairCareMarketOwnership]] into a full business case. The company recognized Black consumers, used chemistry and distribution to make products reliable, tied marketing to cultural change, and made its South Side Chicago headquarters a visible symbol of prosperity. Its later public listing also makes it a case in [[PublicListingControlTradeoff]] and [[FounderControl]].

## Key Claims
- Johnson Products began from the effort to commercialize a more reliable Black hair straightener, then scaled into a broader Black hair-care company.
- The company hired mostly Black workers, paid them well across divisions, and became part of a wider [[CommunityEmbeddedBusiness]] ecosystem.
- [[AfroSheen]] showed the company's ability to shift from straightening-era demand to natural-hair demand during the civil-rights and Black Power era.
- Going public in 1971 gave the company capital and prestige but exposed it to board pressure and larger competitors such as [[Revlon]].
- The company's legacy is mixed: it helped create a multibillion-dollar Black hair-care market, but the source says much of that market is now not Black-owned.
