# Emperor Ai of Han / 汉哀帝

Emperor Ai of Han / 汉哀帝 is reread in [[126-wangmang-zhisi-shensheng-diguo-de-huixuanbiao-he-huixuanbiao-de-huixuanbiao-786255780]] as more complicated than the damaged later image centered on [[DongXian|董贤]]. The episode, drawing on [[XiangruiBook|《祥瑞》]], says he tried to constrain the Wang consort family, reduce imperial expenses, attend to government, and consider limits on land and enslaved dependents.

The episode uses him to show how historical reputation can be shaped by later power. If [[WangMang|王莽]]'s rise influenced how Emperor Ai was posthumously framed, then the source becomes part of [[HistoricalMemoryContest]] around the end of the [[WesternHanDynasty|Western Han dynasty]].

## Key Claims
- Emperor Ai was not presented as simply incompetent or morally negligible.
- His suspicion of Wang-family power makes Wang Mang's later return more politically contingent.
- His relationship with Dong Xian remains important, but the episode treats it as only one part of a larger succession and legitimacy crisis.
