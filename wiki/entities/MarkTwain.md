# Mark Twain / 马克·吐温

Mark Twain / 马克·吐温 enters the wiki through [[135-xianliao-weida-zuojiamen-de-bagua-di-san-dan-823746670]] as the episode's example of a great writer whose public-school respectability hides vulgar humor, performance skill, risk appetite, and financial chaos. The source explains that the pen name came from Mississippi River navigation language and connects his steamboat youth, his brother Henry's death in a riverboat explosion, brief Civil War service, journalism, and lecture career to the later author persona.

The episode stresses Twain's taboo-breaking comic style rather than treating vulgarity as a small footnote. His reputation around dirty jokes, scatological humor, and public provocation becomes a case for [[OffensiveHumorSpeechSpace]]: the source argues that a culture able to tolerate offense may produce writers and comedians who stretch public speech.

Twain also extends [[AuthorMythDeflation]]. The episode presents him as curious and inventive but a poor investor: he made money from a self-pasting scrapbook, pursued devices and publishing technology, missed the chance to invest in [[AlexanderGrahamBell|Alexander Graham Bell / 亚历山大·格雷厄姆·贝尔]]'s telephone, and went bankrupt in 1895. His friendship with [[NikolaTesla|Nikola Tesla / 尼古拉·特斯拉]] gives the source one of its physical-comedy anecdotes.
