# Dan Siroker

Dan Siroker is the founder interviewed in [[tsr-s3-dansiroker-v3-tsr-s3-dansiroker-v3]] about [[Optimizely]], [[RewindAI]], and [[Limitless]]. The episode traces him from [[Google]] into the 2008 [[BarackObama]] campaign, where A/B testing campaign pages and donation flows became the origin story for [[ABTestingForMarketers]].

Dan and [[PeteKoomen]] entered [[YCombinator]] Winter 2010 after earlier attempts with [[CarrotSticks]] and [[Spreadly]]. [[PaulGraham]] pushed them toward the A/B testing tool that became Optimizely, and Dan later uses the company as a regret case around [[FounderInstinct]] and [[FounderDelegationDiscipline]]: he says the company suffered when he delegated away the product, engineering, and design work that held his best judgment.

The later half of the source connects Dan's hearing loss, [[MindEmulationFoundation]], Rewind, and Limitless into a personal-capability theme. His thesis is that AI can augment memory when it uses a person's own seen, said, and heard context, but the episode also makes privacy central through [[PersonalAIMemory]], [[ConsentBasedRecording]], [[WearableAIAssistant]], and [[AgentPermissionBoundaries]].

## Key Claims
- Dan's Obama campaign work turned website A/B testing from a Google practice into a political-fundraising case.
- Optimizely came after two weak startup ideas and a YC pivot toward a pain Dan already understood.
- Dan says his largest Optimizely regrets came from ignoring his own founder-level concerns and letting executives pull him away from core product work.
- Hearing loss made assistive technology feel like a superpower and shaped his later interest in memory-enhancing AI.
- Dan's later products move from local screen/audio memory in Rewind to cloud-based personal AI and a wearable pendant in Limitless.
- Dan's public Series A process for Rewind is a case in [[PublicFundraisingPitch]].
