# Tom Minelli

Tom Minelli is the [[IDC]] analyst interviewed in [[tech-20260113-0113-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260113-0113-mp-tech-pod-128]] about AI-driven pressure on memory supply. His source role is to connect data-center demand for [[HighBandwidthMemory]] and other memory products to downstream effects on PCs, smartphones, gaming computers, and smaller hardware vendors.

Minelli's main framing is cyclical but not dismissive. He says memory has long moved through boom-and-bust cycles, but AI infrastructure has created a new demand source large enough to keep shortages active through 2026 and possibly into 2027 unless data-center spending slows.

## Key Points
- He treats the shortage as both a supplier-concentration issue and a capital-intensity issue.
- He says [[MicronTechnology]]'s move away from consumer RAM reflects the pull of larger, higher-margin data-center customers.
- He expects larger PC vendors such as [[HPInc|HP]], [[DellTechnologies|Dell]], [[Lenovo]], and [[Apple]] to have better memory access than smaller regional vendors or custom builders.
- He links [[AIPCMemoryDemand]] to the same shortage because AI PCs require [[NeuralProcessingUnits]] and more RAM.
