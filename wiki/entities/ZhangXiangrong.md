# Zhang Xiangrong / 张向荣

Zhang Xiangrong / 张向荣 is the author of [[XiangruiBook|《祥瑞》]], the book discussed in [[126-wangmang-zhisi-shensheng-diguo-de-huixuanbiao-he-huixuanbiao-de-huixuanbiao-786255780]]. The episode uses Zhang's work to move beyond the flat image of [[WangMang|王莽]] as either treacherous usurper or unusually modern reformer.

In this wiki, Zhang's page is source-scoped. It records the role the author plays in the episode's reconstruction of [[WesternHanDynasty|Western Han]] political culture, [[MandateOfHeavenLegitimacy]], [[AuspiciousOmenPolitics]], and [[ConfucianIdealGovernance]].

## Key Claims
- Zhang's book is treated as the episode's main guide to Wang Mang.
- The episode attributes to the book a broader claim about Confucianism's failed attempt to restrain monarchy.
- The book foregrounds the political use of auspicious signs rather than treating them as incidental superstition.
