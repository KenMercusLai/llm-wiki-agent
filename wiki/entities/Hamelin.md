# 哈默尔恩 / Hamelin

哈默尔恩 is the German city anchoring the [[PiedPiperOfHamelin|花衣魔笛手]] legend in [[111-huayi-modishou-kuailai-he-lishixuejia-yiqi-tuili-tonghua-zhenxiang-751190766]]. The episode presents it not as a decorative setting but as a medieval urban system shaped by the Weser River, bridges, mills, church property, customs, markets, city law, and conflict among abbeys, bishops, nobles, and citizens.

The city matters because [[AbeKinya|阿布谨也]]'s reading depends on social context. Hamelin's promise of civic freedom sat beside military obligations, tax burdens, guild hierarchy, poor servants, women, children, itinerant workers, and despised occupations. That makes the city a concrete case for [[MedievalUrbanMarginality]] and for reading legend through [[LegendAsSocialHistory]].
