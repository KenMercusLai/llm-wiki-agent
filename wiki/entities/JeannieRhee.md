# Jeannie Rhee

Jeannie Rhee is presented in [[seven-allegedly-fake-chanel-bags-vs-the-realreal]] as a lawyer representing [[TheRealReal]] in its dispute with [[Chanel]]. She states The RealReal's rejection of Chanel's claim that only Chanel can determine whether a Chanel item is genuine.

Her role in the wiki is to voice the independent-resale side of [[TrademarkResaleBoundary]]. The RealReal's defense depends on a reseller being able to identify goods accurately, authenticate them independently, and rely on [[FirstSaleDoctrine]] when the goods were lawfully purchased.

## Key Claims
- Rhee frames The RealReal's authentication process as rigorous and legitimate rather than parasitic on Chanel's brand.
- The defense separates descriptive use of a trademark from implying an official brand relationship.
- Her argument turns the case into a market-access question: whether a brand can use authenticity control to constrain resale competition.
