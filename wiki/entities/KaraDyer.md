# Kara Dyer

Kara Dyer appears in [[the-supreme-court-struck-down-a-bunch-of-trumps-tariffs-now-what]] as the small-business owner of [[StorytimeToys]]. Her experience gives the episode its concrete [[TariffPolicyPlanningRisk]] case: tariff changes affected orders from [[China]], supplier choice, cash planning, and whether a refund claim would justify legal costs.

The source says Dyer rejected supplier suggestions such as underreporting goods or value, switched suppliers, avoided large orders for about a year, and focused on testing new products. After the Supreme Court ruling, she expected a 12,000-unit Little Red Riding Hood order to avoid about $15,000 in tariff costs, but she still faced uncertainty because a new 10% [[Section122TariffAuthority|Section 122]] tariff had already been announced.
