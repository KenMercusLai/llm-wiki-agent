# BBC

The BBC appears in [[latin-lessons-the-donroe-doctrine-boost-6a4233e26b6690f46aae4e3d]] through the shutdown of its Droitwich longwave transmitters on June 27, 2026. The episode says those transmitters had operated since 1934, carrying wartime coded messages, Radio 4 programming, cricket commentary, and the shipping forecast.

The source frames the BBC's decision as a [[BroadcastInfrastructureSunset]] case. Maintaining [[LongwaveRadio]] looks hard to justify for a small audience, but the shutdown still raises access questions for remote places and people who cannot easily use digital or internet services.

[[186-rang-ni-xiao-dao-tou-diao-reng-bu-wang-sikao-weida-de-xiaoshi-shijie-manyou-zhinan-998655210]] adds the BBC as the institutional origin for [[LastChanceToSee|《消逝世界漫游指南》 / Last Chance to See]]. The episode describes the 1980s endangered-species project that paired [[DouglasAdams]] with [[MarkCarwardine]], giving the broadcaster a conservation and nature-writing role alongside its public-media infrastructure role.
