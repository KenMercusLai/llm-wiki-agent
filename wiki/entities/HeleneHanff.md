# Helene Hanff / 海莲·汉芙

Helene Hanff / 海莲·汉芙 is the American writer and central letter-writer in [[EightyFourCharingCrossRoad|《查令十字街84号》 / 84 Charing Cross Road]], discussed in [[134-chaling-shizi-jie-84-hao-zaici-yuanliang-shijie-de-liyou-821075226]]. The episode presents her as poor, direct, funny, self-educated, and deeply attached to old British books that New York shops were unwilling or unable to find for her.

Her first 1949 letter to [[MarksAndCo|Marks & Co.]] begins as a practical request for out-of-print books under a small budget. Over time, her tone, jokes, payments, food parcels, stockings, and continuing requests make her a source case for [[EpistolaryFriendship]]: a person can become intimate to others through repeated written attention without sharing the same city or meeting in person.

The episode uses Helene to connect [[OldBookMateriality]] with [[DignityThroughSmallGifts]]. She loves previous-reader marks and old editions, but she also notices the material needs of postwar Britain and sends gifts that help [[FrankDoel|Frank]], [[NoraDoel|Nora]], [[CecilyFarr|Cecily]], and the shop staff preserve comfort and dignity.
