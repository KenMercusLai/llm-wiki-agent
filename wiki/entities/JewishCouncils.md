# Jewish Councils / 犹太委员会

Jewish Councils / 犹太委员会 appear in [[103-ta-shi-cichuan-yewu-de-guang-ta-shi-hanna-alunte-737939789]] through [[HannahArendt|Hannah Arendt]]'s most controversial claims in [[EichmannInJerusalem|《艾希曼在耶路撒冷》]]. The episode says some councils under Nazi occupation helped compile lists, record property, distribute badges, organize transport, or otherwise carry out administrative demands.

The source is careful to frame this through [[ResponsibilityVsGuilt]]. Its claim is not that Jewish victims caused [[TheHolocaust|the Holocaust]] or carried the same guilt as [[NaziGermany|Nazi Germany]]. The harder Arendtian question is whether extreme coercion, survival pressure, and legalistic obedience erase all responsibility, or whether judgment must still ask what people did inside constrained roles.
