# Fionn mac Cumhaill / 芬恩

Fionn mac Cumhaill / 芬恩 is the Fenian hero and warrior-band leader introduced in [[142-kaierte-shenhua-gushi-diyidan-deluyi-hui-mengjian-bianxing-yang-ma-865479403]]. The episode presents him as a dog-loving leader whose stories shape later heroic and Arthurian imagination while remaining funnier and stranger than a clean noble-hero summary would suggest.

The source uses Fionn through several story clusters. In the Bran and Sceolan tale, his beloved dogs turn out to be kin through a woman transformed into a hound. In the [[Ossian|Oisin / Ossian / 鹅相]] branch, Fionn protects Sadhbh in deer form and later recognizes the child born from that enchanted union. In the final race story, Fionn and the Fianna enter an altered heroic space where feasting, impossible speed, humiliation, and comic violence decide Ireland's honor.

## Key Claims
- Fionn is a hero of relation as much as combat: dogs, kinship, hospitality, threats, and loyalty all matter.
- His stories make [[CelticAnimalTransformation]] tender and coercive at the same time.
- The source links Fenian material to later Arthurian and fantasy afterlives without reducing it to a direct source map.
- Fionn's heroic world can be absurd, comic, and violent without losing mythic seriousness.
