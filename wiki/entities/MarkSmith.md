# Mark Smith

Mark Smith is the rare-earth executive whose career anchors [[battlefield-rare-earths-how-the-us-lost-to-china]]. The episode says he joined [[Molycorp]] around 1987 or 1988, later became CEO, and tried to revive the [[MountainPassRareEarthMine]] through [[ProjectPhoenix]] after the 2010 [[Japan]] rare-earth shock made Chinese dominance visible.

Smith's role is partly operational and partly cautionary. He remembers [[Molycorp]]'s unsold cerium and lanthanum as early evidence that [[China]] was undercutting U.S. producers, and he later argues that relying entirely on China grows more dangerous as rare earths become more important. The source also preserves his allegation that China released product to crash rare-earth prices after [[ProjectPhoenix]] expanded, while noting there was no "smoking gun."

The later part of the episode places Smith at [[NioCorp]], where he is trying to develop a rare-earth mine in Nebraska. That role connects him to [[StateBackedRareEarthRebuilding]], because he describes meetings with U.S. defense, commerce, interior, and [[WhiteHouse]] officials and says he would welcome direct government equity.
