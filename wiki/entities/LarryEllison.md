# Larry Ellison

Larry Ellison is the [[Oracle]] founder at the center of [[vol-265-kuayue-50-nian-de-meiguo-banben-zhizi-1001004591]]. The episode presents him first as a database entrepreneur who saw that [[IBM]]'s relational-database research could become a commercial software product, then as a founder whose sales intensity, stock ownership, and competitive style became long-term capital and influence.

The source's distinctive point is that Ellison's Oracle stake became a bridge from enterprise software into media and politics. The episode says he still holds a large Oracle position, has pledged hundreds of millions of shares, has provided guarantees around [[WarnerBrosDiscovery]] financing, and helped create the capital structure behind [[DavidEllison]]'s [[Skydance]] and [[Paramount]] expansion.

The episode also makes Ellison a [[PoliticalRegulatoryLeverage]] case. His reported support for [[DonaldTrump]], Oracle's role in [[StargateAIInfrastructure]], Oracle's [[USTikTok|U.S. TikTok]] governance role, and the regulatory path for media mergers are presented as one timeline where technology wealth seeks policy certainty.
