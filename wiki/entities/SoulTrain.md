# Soul Train

Soul Train is the television show that became [[JohnsonProducts]]' national marketing engine in [[how-black-hair-care-grew-black-power]]. The episode says [[GeorgeEJohnson]] saw the show live, believed it should be broadcast in color, financed a color pilot, and later helped make it national.

For the wiki, Soul Train is a media-distribution case as much as a cultural-history reference. Johnson Products' Afro Sheen and Ultra Sheen ads appeared as part of the sponsorship deal, and George Johnson credited the show with a large rise in sales between 1971 and 1975.

## Key Claims
- The show made Black style, music, dance, and hair visible at national scale.
- Johnson Products used Soul Train sponsorship to connect [[AfroSheen]] with the cultural world where natural hair was being celebrated.
- The episode treats the relationship as reciprocal: Soul Train gained financial support, while Johnson Products gained attention, legitimacy, and category authority.
