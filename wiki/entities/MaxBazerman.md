# Max Bazerman / 马克斯·巴兹曼

Max Bazerman is named in [[106-bie-zai-haipa-chongtu-kuai-lai-xuexi-tanpan-shu-742539645]] as one of the authors associated with [[HarvardClassicNegotiation|《哈佛经典谈判术》]]. The episode places him in a management and behavioral-psychology frame, then uses the book to explain why negotiation errors often come from cognitive and motivational bias rather than poor verbal style alone.

In the episode's reading, Bazerman's branch connects negotiation practice to [[ZeroSumBias]], [[EscalationOfCommitment]], [[FramingEffect]], and [[MotivatedBias]]. Those concepts make the source part of the wiki's broader decision-making material as well as the negotiation branch.
