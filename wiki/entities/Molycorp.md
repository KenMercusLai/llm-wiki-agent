# Molycorp

Molycorp is the rare-earth company at the center of [[battlefield-rare-earths-how-the-us-lost-to-china]]. The episode says Molycorp operated the [[MountainPassRareEarthMine]], became a major supplier by the 1960s, and for years held a powerful position in rare-earth markets, including supplying europium for color televisions.

The company matters because it shows that U.S. rare-earth capacity once existed but did not remain structurally protected. [[MarkSmith]] recalls that by the late 1980s, Molycorp was struggling to sell cerium and lanthanum as cheaper Chinese products entered the market. The company eventually shut down Mountain Pass after losing customers, leaving little rare-earth production outside [[China]] in the source's account.

Molycorp later attempted a comeback through [[ProjectPhoenix]] after the 2010 [[Japan]] rare-earth shock caused prices to rise. The episode treats that comeback as a warning: a mine reopening can fail if the project expands before market stability, processing capability, customer demand, and price resilience are durable.
