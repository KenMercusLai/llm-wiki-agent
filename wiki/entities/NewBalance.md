# New Balance

New Balance is a running and lifestyle footwear brand discussed in [[e241-paoxie-jishu-diedaishi-malasong-paojin-2-xiaoshi-kao-ren-haishi-kao-xie]] as both an older running-shoe reference point and one of the "four slow-running shoe" brands known for comfort, lasts, and width options. The episode also treats New Balance as a beneficiary of running-fashion demand and shelf space opened by [[Nike]] channel changes.

[[tsr-s3-eddylu-v2-tsr-s3-eddylu-v2]] adds New Balance through [[EddyLu]]'s [[GOAT]] market commentary. He says New Balance was experiencing more than 100% year-over-year growth on GOAT, making it part of the sneaker-fashion demand expansion that helped the marketplace.

## Key Claims
- The source links New Balance's consumer appeal to fit, comfort, and width choices rather than only top-end racing technology.
- Its growth is framed as part of a broader [[PerformanceFootwearMarket]] where running shoes become lifestyle products.
- The brand must still respond to the racing-shoe arms race because carbon-plate and super-foam products shape consumer attention.
- The GOAT source adds resale-market evidence that New Balance demand was rising quickly among sneaker buyers.
