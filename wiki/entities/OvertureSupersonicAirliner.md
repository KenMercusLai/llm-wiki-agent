# Overture Supersonic Airliner

Overture is [[BoomSupersonic]]'s planned passenger airliner in [[tsr-s5-blakescholl-v3-finalaudio-tsr-s5-blakescholl-v3-finalaudio]]. The source presents it as the commercial target behind [[BlakeScholl]]'s early spreadsheets, airline-customer conversations, [[XB1SupersonicDemonstrator|XB-1]] prototype work, and later engine strategy.

The product idea is tied to the [[AllBusinessClassSupersonicModel]]. Scholl argues that a much shorter premium flight changes cabin requirements: passengers need less room to sleep, so the aircraft can fit more high-fare seats in a way that improves economics relative to Concorde-like assumptions.

Overture also becomes the place where engine strategy affects customer experience. Scholl says the [[RollsRoyce|Rolls-Royce]] break pushed Boom toward a custom engine path that later enabled [[BoomlessCruise]] and recovered range needed for an undisclosed passenger-cabin breakthrough. That claim is preserved as source perspective.
