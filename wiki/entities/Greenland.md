# Greenland

Greenland is the central territory in [[how-to-get-what-greenland-has-with-permission]], where [[PlanetMoney]] uses it to connect [[CriticalMineralsGeopolitics]], Arctic geography, and territorial-control rhetoric. The episode presents Greenland as self-governing within the Kingdom of [[Denmark]], with its own elected government, language, and significant autonomy.

The source says Greenland matters to the [[UnitedStates]] for two different reasons. Its heavy rare earth deposits make it relevant to the [[RareEarthProcessingBottleneck]], while its position between North America, [[Russia]], and [[China]] makes it relevant to [[GreenlandStrategicAccess]], missile early warning, missile defense, and space surveillance. The episode's core point is that neither value automatically justifies ownership.

The source also stresses local agency. Greenland is described as majority [[Inuit]], sparsely populated, mostly ice-covered, and governed by a land system where people receive permission to build but do not own the land underneath. [[SahaUllsvig]] uses that context to reject a passive or colonial framing: Greenland can trade and cooperate, but is not for sale.
