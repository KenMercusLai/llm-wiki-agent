# 焚景源

焚景源 appears in the closing section of [[137-sangeng-banye-juran-yao-chi-xiangjiao-shide-zailai-yigen-835431951]] as a Chinese muscular-dystrophy patient whose life complicates narrow assumptions about disability. The episode says he makes games, translates, studies Japanese, and plays demanding games with assistive equipment.

The source uses him as a bridge from [[ShikanoYasuaki|鹿野靖明]]'s Japanese case back to contemporary Chinese disability visibility. The point is not that every disabled person should be exceptional, but that disabled people's interests, skills, frustration, pleasure, and stated needs should be publicly recognized.

## Key Claims
- 焚景源 is used as a concrete counterexample to treating disabled people only as recipients of care.
- The episode connects his case to [[SubjectLedCare]]: help should begin from what disabled people say they want and need.
- His example extends [[DisabilityIndependentLiving]] from Japanese movement history toward a broader Chinese listener-facing reflection.
