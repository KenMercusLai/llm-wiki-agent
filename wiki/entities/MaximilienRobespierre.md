# Maximilien Robespierre / 罗伯斯庇尔

Maximilien Robespierre / 罗伯斯庇尔 appears in [[125-mala-zhisi-beibi-de-ansha-huo-zhengyi-de-chujue-783972516]] as one of the revolutionary leaders who help the episode explain how virtue language can harden into terror. He is grouped with [[JeanPaulMarat|Marat]] and [[GeorgesDanton|Danton]] as part of the [[JacobinClub|Jacobin]] radical leadership cluster.

The source emphasizes Robespierre's classical self-fashioning, moral vocabulary, and political romanticism. By presenting revolutionary politics as a struggle of virtue against enemies, he helps make [[RevolutionaryVirtuePolitics]] and [[RevolutionaryTerrorPolitics]] mutually reinforcing.

Robespierre also links this episode to [[PostVictoryOppressionRisk]]. The danger is that a successful revolutionary faction may mistake victory, purity, and public passion for a permanent right to punish.
