# 《曹寅与康熙》 / Ts'ao Yin and the K'ang-hsi Emperor

《曹寅与康熙》 is [[JonathanSpence|史景迁]]'s study of [[CaoYin|曹寅]], [[KangxiEmperor|康熙]], and the Qing imperial household networks behind the [[CaoFamilyQing|清代曹家]]. In [[101-caoyin-yu-kangxi-hongloumeng-xing-zhong-you-shi-735044876]], [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] uses the book to make [[HongLouMeng|《红楼梦》]]'s background legible through offices, accounts, production, southern tours, and imperial trust rather than through puzzle-like identity decoding.

The book matters in the source because it gives the family-history side of [[RedChamberFamilyHistoryEcho]]. Cao Yin is not only a literary ancestor of [[CaoXueqin|曹雪芹]], but a working imperial agent whose success depended on [[JiangningWeavingBureau|江宁织造]], salt finance, [[ImperialBondservantGovernance|包衣身份]], and [[SecretMemorialPoliticalIntelligence|密折]] access. That makes the later Cao family decline a structural story about favor, deficit, successor weakness, and court politics.
