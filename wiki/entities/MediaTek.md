# MediaTek

MediaTek appears in [[no-208-zhongguo-shouji-jianghu-shang-motuoluola-nuojiya-he-ailixin-de-zhushen-huanghun-zhongguo-hulianwang-gushi-23-998056376]] as the chipset and design-solution company whose 2004 turnkey approach changed Chinese handset manufacturing. The episode says MediaTek bundled functions such as calling, multimedia, Wi-Fi, and GPS into integrated phone solutions, sharply lowering the threshold for building feature phones.

[[ai-shidai-de-chaoji-rukou-haishi-shouji-ma-s10e17-523a0d42-4c16-4dd6-a2ab-9277fec1a731]] updates MediaTek from the feature-phone turnkey era into AI-era smartphone co-design. [[ChenYiqiang]] describes how MediaTek works with [[Vivo]] earlier in the chip-definition cycle, using [[Dimensity9500]] to discuss AI compute, NPU architecture, software tooling, and system-level scheduling for [[OnDeviceAI]].

## Source Position
- MediaTek is the technical and business catalyst behind the second domestic handset wave and the explosion of [[ShanzhaiPhones]].
- Its [[TurnkeyHandsetSolutions]] reduced the need for deep in-house handset R&D, letting small manufacturers and brands assemble differentiated devices quickly and cheaply.
- The source treats this as double-edged: it accelerated low-cost access and scene-specific experimentation while also increasing quality, IP, after-sales, and regulatory problems.
- In the AI-phone source, MediaTek is no longer only a threshold-lowering supplier; it is a co-design partner translating terminal scenarios into CPU, GPU, NPU, bandwidth, power, and thermal choices.
- Chen emphasizes that advanced chips must be planned two to three years ahead, so AI compute investment is partly a bet on future terminal applications.
