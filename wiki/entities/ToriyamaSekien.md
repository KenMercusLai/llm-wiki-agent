# 鸟山石燕 / Toriyama Sekien

鸟山石燕 is the yokai image-maker whose illustrated tradition drives much of the interpretation in [[115-jingji-xiayan-jinxi-xu-baigui-bu-yanjiu-yaoguai-zenme-haohao-zuoren-760601309]]. The episode presents [[TataraKatsugoro|多多良胜五郎]] as deeply attached to Sekien's yokai picture books, especially because a single image can contain visual jokes, homophones, literary allusions, and moral satire.

In [[AnganKozo|《暗眼小僧》]], Sekien's image of a water-monkey-like figure on a moored boat eating a catfish becomes the interpretive surface around which Tatara, 佐佐卫门, and 富美 reason. The image does not directly solve the death, but it changes how the story understands language, family failure, and the ethics of the case.

## Key Claims
- Sekien's images function in the source as compressed folklore archives rather than simple monster pictures.
- The episode's [[YokaiImageExegesis|妖怪图像阐释]] depends on treating visual details, sound similarity, classical references, and story context as jointly meaningful but not automatically evidential.
- The source uses Sekien to bridge [[SemioticDetectiveFiction]] and [[EvidenceBoundFolkloreInquiry]]: interpretation is necessary, but it still needs confidence discipline.
