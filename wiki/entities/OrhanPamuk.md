# 奥尔罕·帕慕克 / Orhan Pamuk

奥尔罕·帕慕克 is the Turkish novelist whose [[MyNameIsRed|《我的名字叫红》]] anchors the two-part [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] reading in [[153-wode-mingzi-jiao-hong-yong-yiqian-lingyi-ye-de-xintiao-wancheng-zhechang-mousha-shang-917670296]] and [[154-wode-mingzi-jiao-hong-yong-yiqian-lingyi-ye-de-xintiao-wancheng-zhechang-mousha-xia-919262400]]. The sources present him less through biography than through method: he turns a detective story into a structure for asking how people see, remember, desire, narrate, and inherit art traditions.

The upper episode frames Pamuk through [[Istanbul]] and [[BorderlandCulturalIdentity]], stressing the city's position between East and West and the source's 1590/1950 historical mirroring. The lower episode completes the plot-and-theory arc through Master Osman, the cracked-nostril horse, Olive, and Shekure's ending.

In the episodes' reading, Pamuk's achievement is to make the novel's form carry its argument. The shifting first-person chapters, talking images, murderer confession, embedded romances, color symbolism, archive scenes, and Shekure's final warning all make [[MultiPerspectiveMurderNarration]] and [[StoryWithinStoryNarration]] part of the art question rather than decorative devices.
