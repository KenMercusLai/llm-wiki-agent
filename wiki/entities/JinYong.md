# 金庸 / Jin Yong

金庸 / Jin Yong is the central writer in [[112-xianliao-jinyong-diyidan-mingjiao-jingran-jintian-hai-cunzai-754593095]], where [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] rereads his wuxia fiction as culturally dense popular literature rather than only martial-arts entertainment. The episode emphasizes that he wrote from a [[HongKong|Hong Kong]] public sphere where fiction, newspaper serialization, and political commentary could overlap.

The source presents Jin Yong's craft through [[WuxiaIntertextualCraft]]. Martial art names, poisons, character arcs, and sect histories draw on Zhuangzi, Cao Zhi, the I Ching, Daoist body lore, older swordswoman traditions, and historical anecdotes. This lets the novels feel both accessible and allusive: a fight scene can also carry poetry, philosophy, political discipline, or emotional state.

The episode also makes Jin Yong an anchor for [[HistoricalJianghuSpace]]. [[TianLongBaBu|《天龙八部》]] and other Song or Song-Yuan settings are treated as plausible Jianghu worlds because mobility, cities, associations, tattoo culture, and commercial life create room for unofficial actors. Later settings make the Jianghu contract: Ming and Qing state capacity, population control, factional politics, firearms, and court power increasingly limit martial-arts freedom.

[[JinYongPoliticalReading]] is the source's other major contribution. The hosts read [[XiaoAoJiangHu|《笑傲江湖》]] through 1967 [[HongKong|Hong Kong]], ideological labels, factional violence, and the corrupting logic of power, while still warning against reducing Jin Yong to a single nationalist or anti-nationalist code. His novels are treated as stories that can hold cultural love, historical curiosity, and political suspicion at once.
