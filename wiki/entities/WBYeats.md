# W. B. Yeats / 叶芝

W. B. Yeats is one of the densest cases in [[60-xianliao-weida-zuojiamen-de-bagua-di-yi-dan-648693204]]. The episode begins from the familiar romantic poet image around "When You Are Old," then shifts toward occult belief, strange public behavior, urgent marriage astrology, seances, automatic writing, and the [[GoldenDawn|Golden Dawn / 金色黎明]].

Yeats is central to [[OccultPseudoscienceLiteraryModernity]]. The source describes his wife as a medium-like collaborator, "Leo Africanus" as a spirit or alternate self in their practice, and [[AleisterCrowley|Aleister Crowley]] as a hostile occult rival. The point is not to diagnose Yeats medically, but to show that mystical systems could become part of a writer's creative and personal machinery.

The episode also places Yeats inside [[AuthorMythDeflation]] through politics and late-life desire. His anti-democratic leanings, praise for the Irish Blueshirts, fascination with Mussolini, rejuvenation surgery, and favoritism toward a younger lover's poetry all complicate the romantic-poet stereotype.
