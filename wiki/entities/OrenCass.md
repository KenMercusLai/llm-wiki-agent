# Oren Cass

Oren Cass appears in [[why-are-we-so-obsessed-with-manufacturing]] as a manufacturing-policy voice focused on wages, productivity, and location. He argues that employers complaining about hard-to-fill manufacturing jobs should raise pay, while also saying competitiveness depends on productivity relative to wages.

The source uses Cass to connect factory enthusiasm to concrete labor-market discipline. If jobs remain vacant, the issue may not only be a missing worker pipeline; it may be that the job offer is not attractive enough under current [[ManufacturingJobQuality]] conditions.

[[would-you-trust-an-economist-with-your-economy]] adds Cass as the critic inside an [[AmericanEconomicAssociation]] panel on economics and politics. In that source, he argues that economists missed or mishandled free trade with [[China]], the 2008 financial crisis, inflation, and the persuasion problem facing the profession, making him part of the [[EconomistTrustCrisis]] branch rather than only the manufacturing-jobs branch.
