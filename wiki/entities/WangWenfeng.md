# 王文锋 / Wang Wenfeng

Wang Wenfeng is the founder of [[Sheet0]] and the guest in [[openclaw-zhihou-wo-zhi-xiang-weilai-3-6-ge-yue-de-shiqing-duitan-sheet0-chuangshiren-wang-wenfeng-lu-d4y7qifag6-rc79tp-roxjp4z]]. His main contribution to the wiki is an operator's interpretation of the post-[[OpenClaw]] wave: [[CodingAgentAsUniversalActionLayer|coding agents]] are not only developer tools, but may become the general action layer through which agents manipulate software, files, CLIs, and business workflows.

Wang argues that [[AISkills]] can preserve expert procedures, that [[AgentHarness]] design matters more as agents enter real work, and that [[AIManagingAI]] may be a more important product direction than building a smarter standalone agent. He also revises his own startup method in the episode: after previously trying to solve five-to-ten-year reliability problems, he now prioritizes bottlenecks already visible in the next three to six months.

## Key Positions
- AI coding is the model's "dexterous hand," because code lets the model take concrete action across tools and work surfaces.
- Vertical agents and SaaS products should be questioned when a capable coding agent plus skills can reproduce much of the expert workflow.
- Human value shifts toward taste, tacit understanding, product definition, context, and final review as agents absorb more execution.
- Token budget is not just cloud cost; it becomes an organizational productivity variable that management has to compare with output.
- Following confirmed short-cycle trends can be better than forcing a differentiated long-term prediction in a fast-changing AI market.
