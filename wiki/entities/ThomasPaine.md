# Thomas Paine / 托马斯·潘恩

Thomas Paine / 托马斯·潘恩 appears near the end of [[125-mala-zhisi-beibi-de-ansha-huo-zhengyi-de-chujue-783972516]] as a republican who supported France becoming a republic while opposing the execution of Louis XVI. The episode uses him to show that republicanism did not require unlimited revolutionary killing.

Paine's anti-death-penalty stance gives the episode a counterweight to [[JeanPaulMarat|Marat]] and [[RevolutionaryTerrorPolitics]]. He is presented as someone willing to oppose monarchy and later criticize Napoleon while still resisting the idea that justice should automatically mean execution.

His presence clarifies [[PoliticalAssassinationEthics]]: the episode is interested in why unlawful violence can become emotionally legible under injustice, but it does not abandon the premise that taking life is morally grave.
