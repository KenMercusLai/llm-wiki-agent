# Eugene Fama

Eugene Fama appears in [[so-are-we-in-an-ai-bubble-here-are-clues-to-look-for]] as the Nobel Prize-winning economist associated with the view that markets are mostly efficient. The episode uses him as the hard test for bubble claims: if markets work, researchers should not be able to reliably identify when a bubble will turn before it turns.

The source says [[RobinGreenwood]]'s "Bubbles for Fama" research was partly motivated by that challenge. Fama is portrayed as interested in the data while interpreting it differently, leaving a truce rather than a decisive refutation of [[MarketEfficiency]].
