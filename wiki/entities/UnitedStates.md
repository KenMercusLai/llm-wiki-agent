# United States

[[how-to-get-what-greenland-has-with-permission]] adds the United States as the power trying to secure [[Greenland]]'s minerals and Arctic location without needing to own the island. The [[PlanetMoney]] episode says U.S. interests are real but separable: [[RareEarthProcessingBottleneck]] requires processing capacity and partner networks, while [[GreenlandStrategicAccess]] already partly exists through defense arrangements with [[Greenland]] and [[Denmark]]. The source therefore links U.S. strategy to [[StrategicAccessWithoutAnnexation]] rather than purchase or conquest.

[[sp-05-liuqing-linyao-qiangsha-yibao-jutou-an-beihou-de-sikao-xia-fenlie-de-meiguo-hui-you-xianzheng-weiji-ma-790241978]] extends the United States branch from the [[UnitedHealthcare]] killing into constitutional and social crisis. The [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] / [[DongqiangXidiao|东腔西调]] sequel connects [[DonaldTrump]]'s 2024 win, [[MAGA]], H-1B conflict, [[USConstitutionalReformConstraint]], [[USConstitutionalCrisisRisk]], [[IdentityPoliticsAsPoliticalSorting]], [[BlackLivesMatter]], and [[InstitutionalSpeechPunishment]] into a diagnosis of chronic institutional stress rather than imminent civil war.

[[sp-04-liuqing-linyao-qiangsha-yibao-jutou-an-beihou-de-sikao-shang-guanyu-mincui-sixing-he-sixing-790230209]] adds the United States as a health-insurance and criminal-law legitimacy case. The [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] / [[DongqiangXidiao|东腔西调]] crossover connects [[UnitedHealthcare]], employer-based insurance, [[AffordableCareAct|Obamacare]], fragmented medical billing, and denial-rate opacity to [[USHealthInsuranceDenialPolitics]], then links health-care reform difficulty to [[USFederalismVetoPoints]], [[PopulistDemocraticEnergy]], and [[CapitalPunishmentRetributionProblem]].

[[119-anlesi-xianchang-zhengyin-shengming-ruci-zhengui-770771087]] adds the United States as the dignity-death and Oregon-style assisted-dying comparison case through [[BrittanyMaynard]] and [[DanDiaz]]. The episode presents U.S. self-administration rules as narrower than some European models, then uses Brittany's public account and the Janet Hall counterexample to keep [[EndOfLifeAutonomyAndDignity]], [[AssistedDyingSafeguards]], and treatment uncertainty in tension.

[[e232-canyin-chuhai-you-xin-zhaor-ma-cong-dingtaifeng-yu-gongcha-naxia-meiguo-chudai-xiaoguan-liaoqi-3bb14dd4-0b13-4173-9d5b-0e63bf98ae1d]] adds the United States as a foodservice localization market. The [[SiliconValley101]] episode argues that restaurant and tea-drink brands entering the U.S. have to fit local meal habits, leases, labor rules, permits, landlords, franchisees, and consumer scenes; [[DinTaiFung]], [[GongCha]], [[Haidilao]], [[HeyTea]], [[MoliNaiBai]], [[Chagee]], [[WallaceFastFood]], and [[Jollibee]] become cases for [[RestaurantCulturalLegibility]] and [[USRestaurantRealEstateConstraint]].

[[why-the-us-has-no-guaranteed-paid-vacation]] adds the United States as the labor-policy outlier in [[PlanetMoney]]'s paid-vacation comparison. The episode says the U.S. guarantees no paid vacation or paid holidays by federal law, then explains that gap through [[EmployerBargainedBenefits]] rather than only through culture, taxes, or consumerism: vacation, pensions, and health insurance were left to employer and union negotiation instead of made universal [[PaidVacationAsLaborRight|labor rights]].

[[iran-protests-and-sanctions]] adds the United States as a sanctioning state whose financial power shapes [[Iran]]'s economy and diplomacy. The [[PlanetMoney]] episode follows U.S. policy from the 1979 asset freeze through the 2010-era [[DollarFinancialSanctions]] campaign, the [[BarackObama]]-era nuclear deal, and [[DonaldTrump]]'s 2018 withdrawal, making U.S. credibility central to [[IranSanctions]], [[SanctionsOvercompliance]], and [[USIranNuclearDiplomacy]].

[[dark-times-for-cubas-economic-experiment]] adds the United States as the external power shaping [[Cuba]]'s economic options. The [[PlanetMoney]] episode traces the U.S. embargo from the Cold War through [[BarackObama]]'s travel loosening, [[DonaldTrump]]'s partial reversal, and the source's 2026 account of pressure on oil shipments, framing U.S. policy as central to [[EconomicSanctionsAsViolence]], [[TourismDependentSmallEconomy]], and [[OilDependencyBlackoutRisk]].

[[building-things-and-breaking-things-in-china-summer-school-world-tour]] adds the United States as the comparison case for [[DanWang]]'s [[EngineeringState]] analysis. Wang argues that America should learn from [[China]]'s capacity to build homes, infrastructure, and manufacturing while avoiding its overbuilding, debt, corruption, and social-planning failures; the episode condenses that lesson as [[BuildBuildRegulate]].

[[why-are-we-so-obsessed-with-manufacturing]] adds the United States as a manufacturing labor-market case. The [[PlanetMoney]] episode says factories can support [[SupplyChainSovereignty]], non-college wages, and regional growth, but the U.S. policy goal should be tested through [[ManufacturingPayPremium]], [[ManufacturingJobQuality]], [[ManufacturingWorkforcePipeline]], [[TradableSectorLocalGrowth]], and [[GoodJobsForNonCollegeWorkers]] rather than assuming manufacturing is uniquely good.

[[can-transforming-neighborhoods-help-kids-escape-poverty]] adds the United States as a public-housing and mobility case. The [[PlanetMoney]] episode uses [[HOPEVIProgram|HOPE VI]] and [[RichardAllenHomes]] to show that U.S. anti-poverty policy can operate through place, peers, and surrounding opportunity, while [[PublicHousingDisplacement]] exposes the cost of demolition-led reform when deeply affordable units are not fully replaced.

[[tech-20260319-0319-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260319-0319-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds the United States to the low-cost drone warfare branch through the [[LucasDrone]]. [[StaceyPettijohn]] describes Lucas as a U.S. low-cost uncrewed system essentially reverse-engineered from the Iranian [[Shahed136]], showing that the United States is also responding to [[LowCostDroneWarfare]] with cheaper, good-enough platforms rather than only exquisite high-end weapons.

[[tech-20260312-0312-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260312-0312-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds a market-reaction angle to the U.S.-Iran branch. The [[MarketplaceTech]] episode says gold rose after news that the United States attacked [[Iran]], while [[Bitcoin]] did not show the same immediate flight-to-safety response; [[GilLuria]] then connects possible later Bitcoin demand to war-driven inflation, dollar weakness, and [[CrossBorderCryptoCapitalFlight]].

The [[UnitedStates]] is the central subject of [[the-250-year-experiment-americas-birthday-6a477d9aecd123914320b4ad]], where [[TheIntelligence]] treats the country's 250th birthday as a test of democracy, immigration, historical memory, and cultural power. The episode frames America as both resilient and fragile: constitutional institutions, labor-market assimilation, and cultural energy remain strong, while polarization, voting-rights erosion, harsh immigration enforcement, and executive-power expansion put pressure on the republic.

[[trailer-tocqueville-road-trip-6a31522c9b6fd8dcaba7b494]], [[trailer-tocqueville-road-trip-6a315642780d0f7579af216c]], [[trailer-tocqueville-road-trip-6a31598d780d0f7579b04fb7]], [[trailer-tocqueville-road-trip-6a315920101389aa3d42d134]], and [[trailer-tocqueville-road-trip-6a315813780d0f7579afb892]] add a historical lens for the same civic question. [[JohnPrideaux]] uses [[AlexisDeTocqueville]]'s 1831 journey and [[DemocracyInAmerica]] to ask whether the United States still functions as [[AmericaAsIdea]]: a democratic promise with global symbolic force, not only a state under institutional stress.

[[gulf-co-operation-counsel-what-next-for-the-region-6a4387d73fa89e3338f83da6]] extends the America-at-250 arc forward from the 2007-08 financial crisis through [[BarackObama]]'s election, Sandy Hook, [[DonaldTrump]]'s rise, the pandemic, January 6th, Joe Biden's presidency, and Trump's return. Its U.S. segment frames the republic as still under strain after repeated tests of race, gun politics, executive power, and democratic trust.

The page also connects earlier wiki branches that used America more indirectly. [[NATOAllianceCredibility]] and [[EuropeanDefenseAutonomy]] depend on whether U.S. command, intelligence, and military integration remain reliable; [[USIranNuclearDiplomacy]] depends on U.S. bargaining credibility; and [[Route66NostalgiaTourism]] shows how American mobility myths and roadside identity can keep cultural value alive after infrastructure loses practical utility.

[[shangye-xiaoyang-44-shijiebei-kuojun-yu-fifa-de-quanli-douzheng-993354969]] adds the United States as a sports-business host market. The source argues that U.S. stadium infrastructure, sponsor demand, ticketing norms, resale platforms, and hospitality economics amplify the commercial value of the expanded 2026 [[FIFAWorldCup]].

[[can-world-cup-mania-grow-mls-in-the-us]] adds the United States as the domestic sports market where [[MajorLeagueSoccer]] tries to turn World Cup attention into club fandom. The source emphasizes that soccer's global dominance does not automatically translate into U.S. viewing preference, so local watch parties, host-city stadium exposure, and league marketing become [[MajorEventAttentionConversion]] work.

[[e243-te-lang-pu-huanxing-hongpai-zhiwai-meiguo-ziben-ruhe-yingkong-quanqiu-zutan]] extends the sports branch from host market to capital system. The episode treats the United States as a source of football ownership capital, sports-franchise valuation logic, stadium-real-estate playbooks, rights agencies such as [[CAA]] and [[RelevantSports]], and banks involved in [[FootballTransferReceivablesFinance]].

[[fault-lines-venezuelas-paltry-earthquake-response-6a44e38375e7a3e96111931b]] adds a disaster-recovery and intervention-responsibility case. The episode says the United States sent rescue teams, military personnel, and a vessel toward [[Venezuela]] after the earthquakes, while former officials argued that reconstruction would require a much larger commitment. Because Washington had already intervened heavily in Venezuelan politics, the source frames U.S. recovery aid as tied to stabilization and the promised [[DemocraticTransitionElection]].

[[latin-lessons-the-donroe-doctrine-boost-6a4233e26b6690f46aae4e3d]] adds the United States as a near-abroad investment and strategic-resource actor. The source's [[DonroeDoctrine]] frame says U.S. pressure under [[DonaldTrump]] helped redirect attention and capital toward [[LatinAmerica]], especially where [[China]] influence, mining, and infrastructure projects made regional investment strategically valuable.

[[fear-jerker-americas-ai-backlash-6a3cf783d760508ebaecd9fd]] adds the United States as an AI-politics case. The episode says public fear of job loss, child-chatbot relationships, mental-health effects, tech-company power, data centers, and the speed of AI progress can cut across party lines, making [[AIBacklashPolitics]] and [[DataCenterBacklash]] new tests of how American institutions absorb technological change.

[[roaring-trades-oil-majors-secret-success-story-6a4636f160cad2674e6d9674]] adds the United States as a frontier-model control case. The episode says advanced cyber capabilities pushed the government toward [[FrontierModelReleaseGovernance]], creating uncertainty for [[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]], model users, and investors even as the administration publicly rejected formal licensing.

[[tech-20260710-tech-pod-128-tech-20260710-tech-pod-128]] adds a more concrete U.S. model-review case through [[OpenAI]]'s [[GPT56|GPT-5.6]] launch and the [[CenterForAIStandardsAndInnovation]]. The same episode adds the strategic substitution problem: U.S. companies may use cheaper Chinese models, while the government wants a stronger domestic open-model ecosystem to reduce dependence on providers such as [[ZhipuAI|ZAI]].

[[tech-20260713-tech-pod-128-tech-20260713-tech-pod-128]] adds a U.S. carbon-removal funding case. The [[MarketplaceTech]] episode says the Trump administration rescinded funding for several Biden-era [[DirectAirCapture]] projects, but [[UnitedStatesCongress|Congress]] protected hubs in [[Louisiana]] and [[Texas]], showing that climate-tech support can survive where state interests, industrial policy, and corporate buyers align.

[[coming-in-andy-britains-prime-minister-in-waiting-6a3512974a2a3be0f419384b]] adds two U.S. power cases. Internationally, America may shift from blockade and strikes against [[Iran]] to [[IranPostwarEconomicRelief]] as a way to secure results it did not achieve militarily. Domestically, the [[ObamaPresidentialCenter]] extends the wiki's memory thread by asking how presidential museums shape public memory and whether [[PresidentialMemorialCulture]] gives presidents too much quasi-imperial aura.

[[peace-fire-further-us-iran-strikes-6a4f7acffe878dc8e27e6fbf]] adds two more U.S. cases. Internationally, renewed strikes against [[Iran]] test whether military pressure can coexist with [[USIranNuclearDiplomacy]]; culturally, the [[ThreateFillingStation]] segment reframes [[Route66]] through [[BlackTravelInfrastructure]] rather than only road-trip nostalgia.

[[marine-warfare-le-pen-runs-for-president-6a4e1f2781fe1f6460b7117c]] adds a Route 66 commercial-history case. The [[AmericanGiantsMuseum]] segment treats fiberglass roadside figures, service-station reuse, and [[RoadsideAdvertisingSpectacle]] as evidence that American roadside commerce became cultural memory as [[Route66]] shifted from transport utility to nostalgia travel.

[[tech-20260211-0211-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260211-0211-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds the United States as the diplomatic center of the [[ArtemisAccords]], a U.S.-led framework for lunar exploration rules. Through [[NASA]] and [[Artemis2]], the source places the United States inside [[LunarResourceGovernance]] debates over safety zones, commercial [[SpaceResourceExtraction]], and how to operate near [[China]] and [[Russia]] on the [[Moon]].

[[e229-cong-shougong-zuofang-dao-quanqiu-diyi-zhongguo-dongli-dianchi-nixi-shi]] adds the United States as the contrast case in power batteries. The source says American labs and startups still matter in original battery chemistry, silicon anodes, [[DryElectrodeManufacturing]], [[BatteryRecyclingLoop]], and AI-assisted battery R&D, but that the country lacks China's dense [[PowerBatteryIndustryChain]] and factory-level [[BatteryManufacturingKnowHow]].

[[vol-113-cong-jiqian-ye-zhiku-wenjian-zhong-goule-telangpu-2-0-zhizheng-jihua-beihou-de-ren-jigou-sixiang-he-mailuo-lihnknc-3om5evexocb6a6zdwueu]] adds the United States as the institutional terrain for Trump 2.0. The episode links [[Project2025]], [[AdministrativeStateDismantling]], [[EducationDepartmentAbolition]], [[MeritBasedImmigrationFilter]], [[SupplyChainSovereignty]], and [[TradeReciprocityProtectionism]] into a broader picture where constitutional arguments, bureaucracy, borders, education, money, and tariffs become one governing agenda.

[[173-tanhe-ruhe-bamian-yiwei-zongtong-963141809]] adds a direct constitutional-remedy branch. The [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode uses [[CassSunstein]]'s [[ImpeachmentBook|《弹劾》]] to explain why the U.S. [[PresidentialSystem]] needs [[PresidentialImpeachment]], the [[TwentyFifthAmendment]], and [[SeparationOfPowers]] as emergency correction mechanisms rather than ordinary tools of parliamentary confidence politics.
