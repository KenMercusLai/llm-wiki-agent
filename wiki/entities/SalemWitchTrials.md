# Salem Witch Trials / 塞勒姆猎巫

Salem Witch Trials / 塞勒姆猎巫 is the 1692 New England case discussed in [[91-liewu-sailemu-1692-cong-yizheng-kaishi-714732018]] through [[StacySchiff|斯泰西·西夫]]'s [[TheWitchesSalem1692|《猎巫：塞勒姆1692》]]. The episode presents it as a small-community crisis that begins with girls' abnormal symptoms and then expands through rumor, religious fear, legal procedure, coerced confession, and death sentences.

The source's core Salem point is systemic. The trials become legible through [[WitchHuntMechanism]]: a closed Puritan village, external war and disease anxiety, colonial political instability, gendered suspicion, property and family conflicts, and the desire to prove order all help convert private distress into institutional violence.

## Key Claims
- The episode treats Salem as "original" witch hunting because the formal charge really is witchcraft, while also using it to explain broader persecution mechanisms.
- The accusations expand from socially marginal women to respectable villagers, defenders, children, men, and former accusers, showing how denunciation can become self-protective.
- [[SpectralEvidence]] is central to the collapse: claimed visions and courtroom bodily performances are allowed to count against the accused.
- The eventual halt comes through outside authority limiting evidentiary use and arrests, not through the community naturally correcting itself.
- Later apology, reputation restoration, and memorialization are treated as slow and incomplete attempts to repair a public wrong.
