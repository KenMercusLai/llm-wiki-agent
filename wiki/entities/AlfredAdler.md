# Alfred Adler / 阿尔弗雷德·阿德勒

Alfred Adler / 阿尔弗雷德·阿德勒 is the psychologist discussed in [[98-zibei-yu-chaoyue-xiangqian-kan-qianfang-you-xiwang-729396507]] through [[ZibeiYuChaoyue|《自卑与超越》]]. The episode presents him as a thinker who turns self-help away from pure origin stories and toward present purpose, social interest, contribution feeling, and courage.

The source emphasizes Adler's own biography only to explain his sensitivity to inferiority, bodily limitation, death, and healing. It does not use biography as destiny; instead, it makes Adler's life part of the same argument that people are shaped by early conditions but still ask how to live forward.

## Key Claims
- Adler is contrasted with Freud in the episode as more future-oriented: the question is not only "what caused this?" but "what is this behavior trying to achieve?"
- His [[IndividualPsychology|individual psychology]] is described as whole-person psychology rather than a division of the person into separate parts.
- His account of inferiority makes lack and weakness normal human experiences, not immediate proof of sickness or moral failure.
- His social ethics matter as much as his self-change theory: the episode links him to [[CommunityFeeling]], neighborly concern, and resistance to enemy-making.
- The episode treats Adlerian responsibility as help-oriented, not as a blame formula for people injured by family, illness, or social conditions.
