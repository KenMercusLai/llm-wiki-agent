# Afro Sheen

Afro Sheen is the [[JohnsonProducts]] hair-care product used in [[how-black-hair-care-grew-black-power]] to mark the shift from straightening-era demand to natural-hair demand. The episode describes it as a moisturizer for afros that emerged as younger Black consumers rejected white beauty standards and embraced natural hair during the civil-rights era.

The product matters because it connects [[HairAsPoliticalIdentity]] to [[ConsumerBrandMoat]]. Afro Sheen was not only a formula; it was sold through media, especially [[SoulTrain]], at a moment when a hairstyle was becoming a public statement about Black identity and pride.

## Key Claims
- Afro Sheen shows how product timing can depend on cultural and political signals, not only technical features.
- Its success depended on Johnson Products seeing natural hair as a market to serve rather than a trend to resist.
- The product helped make [[BlackHairCareMarketOwnership]] visible because the company, workforce, ads, and customers were tied to Black culture.
