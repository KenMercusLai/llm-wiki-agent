# Holden Caulfield / 霍尔顿

Holden Caulfield / 霍尔顿 is the protagonist of [[TheCatcherInTheRye|《麦田里的守望者》]] and the main figure in [[01-maitian-shouwangzhe-20shiji-de-meiguo-jiabaoyu-523554422]]. The episode presents him as more than a rebellious schoolboy: he is rich, expelled, foul-mouthed, lonely, often irritating, but also unusually gentle toward people he sees as vulnerable.

The source repeatedly compares Holden to [[JiaBaoyu|贾宝玉]]. Both are young men who resist the expected route of study, career, family ambition, and adult usefulness; both dislike vulgarity and hypocrisy; both are drawn to literature and to girls or sisters as emotionally cleaner spaces than the adult order around them. [[AmericanJiaBaoyuComparison]] names this source-specific analogy.

Holden's tenderness is traced through small scenes: his worry about Central Park ducks, his memory of Jane Gallagher, his unwillingness to shame a classmate over luggage, his conversation with nuns, and his protective love for [[PhoebeCaulfield|Phoebe / 菲比]]. The episode argues that this makes him socially capable but morally unable to accept ordinary falseness for long.

The source also marks Holden's limits. His anger stays mostly inward, avoidant, verbal, or fantastical; this is why the episode distinguishes him from [[Rorschach|罗夏]] and from violent misreadings of the novel. Holden is closer to [[AdolescentAlienationRecognition]] than to heroic violence.
