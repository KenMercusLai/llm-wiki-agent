# Donald Keene / 唐纳德·基恩

Donald Keene / 唐纳德·基恩 enters the wiki through [[shaqingqing-x-hanzhao-shichuan-zhuomu-wo-baochi-de-sixiang-doushi-yinwei-queqian-894931314]] as the author of [[TakubokuBiography|《石川啄木》]], translated into Chinese by [[ShaQingqing|沙青青]]. The episode presents Keene as a major Japanese-literature scholar whose biography relies heavily on [[IshikawaTakuboku|石川啄木]]'s diaries.

Keene's importance in this source is the subtitle-level claim that Takuboku can be read as "the first modern Japanese." The episode interprets this through [[ModernJapaneseInteriorDiscovery]]: Takuboku makes his own selfishness, shame, desire, inconsistency, illness, and political agitation available as literary material.

## Key Claims
- Keene does not sanitize Takuboku's failures; the biography keeps poverty, debt, women, friendship rupture, and self-loathing visible.
- His diary-based method makes [[LiteraryGossipAsContext]] more disciplined because the source of many unflattering details is Takuboku himself.
- The biography helps readers connect individual poems to the concrete life episodes behind them.
