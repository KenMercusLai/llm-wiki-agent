# Tyler Shultz

[[TylerShultz|Tyler Shultz]] is the central witness in [[tsr-s4-tylershultzpart1-final-tsr-s4-tylershultzpart1-final]] and [[tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3-tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3]], a two-part [[TheSocialRadars]] interview about his [[Theranos]] whistleblowing story. The sources present him as [[GeorgeShultz|George Shultz]]'s grandson, a former Theranos intern and employee after [[StanfordUniversity|Stanford]], and an early whistleblower who saw that the company's public blood-testing claims did not match lab reality.

Tyler's account moves from attraction to evidence discipline. He initially found [[ElizabethHolmes|Elizabeth Holmes]] charismatic and mission-driven, then noticed locked rooms, siloed teams, broad NDAs, a launch at [[Walgreens]] without validated Theranos-platform tests, quality-control failures, and pressure from [[SunnyBalwani|Sunny Balwani]] to run patient samples. The syphilis validation and audit behavior made the risk concrete: in Tyler's telling, the problem was not only that the device failed, but that patients could receive medical information the company knew was unreliable.

The episode also makes Tyler a case in [[WhistleblowerEvidenceDiscipline]]. He tried to prove himself wrong by checking with coworkers, managers, family, and a regulator; escalated concerns internally; wrote a detailed email to Elizabeth; preserved the email thread; resigned; and tried to persuade George with help from [[EricaChung|Erica Chung]]. [[tsr-s5-catchup2-v1-tsr-s5-catchup2-v1]] had previously mentioned him only as a listener-favorite archive highlight for the show, using the misspelled "Tyler Schultz"; this page corrects the entity target to Tyler Shultz.

[[tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3-tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3]] follows Tyler after resignation into the [[WallStreetJournal|Wall Street Journal]] phase. He became a deep-background source for [[JohnCarreyrou|John Carreyrou]], was identified by Theranos after the company saw Carreyrou's questions, resisted demands to sign affidavits or name sources, faced expensive legal pressure and private-investigator warnings, and eventually went on the record. The source extends Tyler's page from evidence discipline into [[WhistleblowerLegalIntimidation]] and [[ProtectedWhistleblowerChannels]].
