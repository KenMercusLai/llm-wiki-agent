# French Revolution / 法国大革命

The French Revolution / 法国大革命 is the main historical setting of [[125-mala-zhisi-beibi-de-ansha-huo-zhengyi-de-chujue-783972516]]. The episode frames it as a pressure system formed by absolutist centralization, fiscal collapse, bad harvests, war debt, Enlightenment thought, Parisian urban politics, and a monarchy that lost credibility after failing to stabilize reform.

The source's account moves from the Estates-General and the storming of the Bastille toward factional conflict among the [[Girondins]], the [[JacobinClub|Jacobins]], the Mountain, and the Plain. That sequence matters because [[JeanPaulMarat|Marat]]'s death is not an isolated murder story; it belongs to a struggle over whether republican politics would be restrained by law or carried by popular fear, denunciation, and [[RevolutionaryTerrorPolitics|terror]].

The episode also uses the revolution as a memory and symbol system. [[JacquesLouisDavid|David]]'s [[DeathOfMaratPainting|The Death of Marat]], classical Roman references, virtue language, and later sympathy for [[CharlotteCorday|Corday]] all make the revolution a case in [[HistoricalMemoryContest]] as well as institutional breakdown.
