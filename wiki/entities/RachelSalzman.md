# Rachel Salzman

Rachel Salzman appears in [[how-to-make-a-book-into-a-bestseller]] as a senior publicity director at [[WWNorton|W. W. Norton]] who planned the [[PlanetMoneyBook]] launch roughly a year ahead of publication. Her work connects publicity, pre-orders, live events, and bookstore channels into a launch-week push for the [[NewYorkTimesBestsellerList|New York Times bestseller list]].

The source uses Salzman to show that launch strategy is not only media attention. It is timed coordination across [[PreOrderLaunchConcentration]], [[AuthorPlatformRiskReduction]], store-reporting guesses, ticket-bundled book sales, and post-launch promotion.

## Key Claims
- Norton began selling the book months before publication because pre-orders could count toward launch-week performance.
- The launch plan sequenced media appearances, live events, pre-orders, and bookstore-linked purchases around the publication date.
- Planet Money's NPR platform gave Norton an unusually strong channel for reaching likely buyers.
- Live-event ticket bundles could create individual sales while also supporting bookstores and venues.
