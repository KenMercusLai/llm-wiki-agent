# Geppetto / 杰佩托

Geppetto / 杰佩托 is [[Pinocchio]]'s father in [[AdventuresOfPinocchio|《木偶奇遇记》]], discussed in [[122-pinuocao-chengzhang-de-cantong-jiqi-suo-chuangzuo-de-777464172]]. The episode emphasizes his ordinary, material care: he carves the puppet, takes punishment after Pinocchio runs away, feeds him pears, makes him new feet, and sells his coat to buy a schoolbook.

Geppetto matters because the story's final transformation reverses the care relation. He begins as the protective father, but after being swallowed and weakened he becomes the person Pinocchio must rescue and support. The source treats that reversal as the moment where Pinocchio's humanity becomes more than a reward for being well behaved.

## Key Claims
- Geppetto's sacrifice gives Pinocchio a concrete reason to want to become better.
- The father's vulnerability makes [[GrowthAsProtectorRole]] visible: the child grows when he can carry responsibility back toward the parent.
- The story's family bond is not sentimental only; it passes through hunger, fear, lost time, failed promises, and physical rescue.
