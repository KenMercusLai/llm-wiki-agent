# Richard Thaler

Richard Thaler appears in [[155-meimao-neng-dang-fan-chi-ma-xiang-zhuanqian-gai-zuo-dian-sha-jieju-shi-ying-bimian-shenme-xingwei-jingjixue-siwei-you-shenme-yong-920167210]] through the source's examples of asymmetric risk valuation and [[MentalAccounting]]. The episode uses his questionnaire-style framing to show how the same statistical risk can be priced differently when a person feels they are paying for protection versus being compensated to accept danger.

The source uses Thaler as a bridge from abstract behavioral economics to practical self-checking. If people sort money into mental buckets and react differently to gains, losses, wages, discounts, and compensation, then [[CostBenefitThinking]] has to account for framing as well as nominal price.
