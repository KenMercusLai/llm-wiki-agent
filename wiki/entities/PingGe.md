# 品哥

品哥 is the [[Mianji]] guest in [[e163-yaowanle-bu-shi-yaowanle-lun-yang-ai-de-xintai-yu-xiguan-lqezcpnw8p6cwhjr2wcw68x4uphb]]. His role in the episode is less tool instructor than confidence and workflow translator: he pushes the host to clarify what he wants to create, then explains how non-programmers can work with AI through natural language, structured context, reusable skills, and feedback loops.

The source presents 品哥 as an advocate for treating AI like a high-capability new colleague. [[AISkills]] supply the colleague's operating manual, [[ContextEngineering]] supplies the work materials and user background, [[PersistentAgentMemory]] captures repeated preferences and accepted patterns, and [[OutputQualityGates]] define when the result must be rejected.

## Key Claims

- AI adoption starts with intention: the user has to know what they want to do before tools and models matter.
- Skills should preserve SOPs, prior best practices, and known pitfalls so an agent does not relearn the same work from scratch.
- Good AI work depends on clear input, explicit process, and enough relevant context rather than prompt tricks alone.
- People and agents share a context-management discipline: keep only the current task's relevant material in attention and offload durable memory to external systems.
- AI should help people reclaim life and choice, not merely increase throughput or token consumption.
