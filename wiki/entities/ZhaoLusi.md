# Zhao Lusi / 赵露思

Zhao Lusi / 赵露思 is the actor and public-figure case around which [[xianliao-xiang-song-zhao-lusi-yi-ben-duan-ge-ji-bianji-tuishu-wu-suo-bu-yong-qi-ji-905512770]] organizes its discussion of celebrity vulnerability, public illness rumors, contract dispute discourse, live streaming, and online attack. The page is source-scoped: the episode does not present itself as a verified account of her career or disputes, but as a record of how the hosts encountered and interpreted public conversation about her.

In the episode, [[QinZong|秦总]] wants to send Zhao Lusi [[DuanGeShiWoBeishangDeWanju|《短歌是我悲伤的玩具》]] because [[IshikawaTakuboku|石川啄木]]'s short songs seem suited to a person being read by the public through fragility, resentment, self-defense, and the desire to speak directly. [[LiWuya|李乌鸦]] also uses her own experience of being attacked after wishing Zhao Lusi well to show how quickly celebrity discourse can spill onto ordinary commenters.

## Key Claims
- Zhao functions in the source less as biography than as a case for [[LiveHumanFeeling|活人感]] under public scrutiny.
- The hosts treat her online reception as evidence that audiences can demand realness and punish realness at the same time.
- Her visibility does not make online abuse ethically neutral; the source rejects the idea that celebrity wealth or fame makes a person default fair game.
- The episode's Zhao Lusi discussion is deliberately limited by the hosts' admitted information gaps.
