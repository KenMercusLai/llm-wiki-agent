# Patrick Healy

Patrick Healy appears in [[how-to-make-a-book-into-a-bestseller]] as a [[NewYorkTimes|New York Times]] voice explaining the paper's defense of the [[NewYorkTimesBestsellerList|New York Times bestseller list]]. In the episode, he says the methodology is data-based, separated from book-review preferences, and designed to reflect organic reader demand.

His role is to represent the legitimacy claim behind [[BestsellerListOpacity]]. The list depends on secrecy to reduce manipulation, while the Times also has to convince readers and publishers that the secrecy is not arbitrary preference.

## Key Claims
- The Times knows authors and publishers try to influence the bestseller list.
- The episode attributes anti-gaming steps in the list's analysis to the Times.
- He frames the list as data-based rather than driven by reviews or personal taste.
- The Times wants the list to preserve audience trust by reflecting organic demand.
