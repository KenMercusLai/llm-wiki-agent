# 郑渊洁

郑渊洁 is the central childhood-author figure in [[177-xiaoshihou-de-shu-zenme-jiu-name-haokan-971996036]]. [[QinZong|秦总]] and [[Beimin|北明]] present him as important not only because his stories were imaginative, but because they carried anti-school, anti-authoritarian, socially observant, and child-respecting energy.

The episode uses works and figures such as Pipilu, Luxixi, Shuke, Beta, 《飞马牌汽车》, 《罐头小人》, and 《童话大王》 columns to show how children's literature could expose readers to debate, copyright claims, adult social rules, independent thought, and the possibility that a good child might resist authority to protect something vulnerable.

## Key Claims
- Zheng's value in the source is not just fantasy invention; it is the refusal to treat children as readers who need only safety, obedience, and simplified morals.
- The episode treats his stories as a bridge between [[AntiAuthoritarianEducation]] and [[ChildrensLiteratureComplexity]].
- QinZong's memory of Luxixi in 《罐头小人》 makes "乖孩子" compatible with courage, secrecy, care, and justified refusal.
- Zheng's public copyright disputes and self-narration are remembered as an early source of intellectual-property awareness for young readers.
