The March 24, 2026 episode adds a prediction-market integrity layer through [[StephanieHughes]], [[JontayPorter]], [[MatthewHolt]], [[BenSchifrin]] of [[BetterMarkets]], and [[JohnHolden]] of [[IndianaUniversity]]. The March 23, 2026 episode adds an AI education and homework layer through [[StephanieHughes]] and [[HeatherSchwartz]] of [[RAND]]. It connects student AI use, critical-thinking concern, classroom AI-free time, and [[ChatGPT]] homework examples to [[FirstDraftThinking]], [[AIShortcutRisk]], [[AIAsTutor]], [[SelfDirectedLearning]], and [[LearningHowToLearn]] by showing that the key education question is often when AI enters the work, not whether it is present at all.

The March 19, 2026 episode adds a low-cost drone warfare layer through [[StephanieHughes]] and [[StaceyPettijohn]] of the [[CenterForNewAmericanSecurity]]. The March 18, 2026 episode adds a post-mortem AI ethics layer through [[StephanieHughes]] and [[TomasHoloneck]] of the [[UniversityOfCambridge]]. It connects [[AIGriefBots]], [[PostMortemAIConsent]], and [[DigitalMemorialization]] to [[DigitalPreservation]] and [[HumanConnectionUnderAI]] by showing that preserving a person's data as an interactive avatar can support family memory or survivor testimony while also raising consent, privacy, dignity, access, and grief-exploitation risks.
