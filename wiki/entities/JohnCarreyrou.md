# John Carreyrou

[[JohnCarreyrou|John Carreyrou]] appears in [[tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3-tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3]] as the [[WallStreetJournal|Wall Street Journal]] reporter who contacted [[TylerShultz|Tyler Shultz]] after Tyler had resigned from [[Theranos]] and failed to make the company's leadership or board respond to his concerns. Tyler says he researched Carreyrou, saw his prior investigative record, and decided that helping him ask better questions could protect patients and give [[GeorgeShultz|George Shultz]] a chance to respond while alive.

The source presents Carreyrou as an outside accountability channel rather than simply a reporter collecting a famous scandal. Tyler initially spoke on deep background, supplied emails involving [[ElizabethHolmes|Elizabeth Holmes]], [[SunnyBalwani|Sunny Balwani]], and New York regulators, and later realized Carreyrou had sources above his level, including [[AdamRosendorff|Adam Rosendorff]]. Carreyrou's questions to Theranos helped the company infer Tyler's role, but Tyler says Carreyrou had not named him.

Carreyrou's reporting becomes a case in [[PublicServiceJournalism]] because the episode shows how investigative journalism can convert isolated employee evidence into a public record that regulators, patients, investors, and boards cannot ignore as easily. The serialized Journal articles sustained pressure after the first story and helped move the case from private intimidation into public accountability.
