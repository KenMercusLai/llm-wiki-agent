# William Shakespeare / 威廉·莎士比亚

William Shakespeare / 威廉·莎士比亚 appears in [[124-kaisa-zhisi-lishi-de-yunjiao-shifou-yi-shixiao-781894361]] through the episode's discussion of [[JuliusCaesarPlay|Julius Caesar]]. The source uses the play to show how literary memory can become more familiar than uncertain ancient evidence.

The episode especially emphasizes Caesar's last words. Shakespeare's version turns the scene into a concentrated betrayal and ideals conflict, while older reports preserve a less certain Greek phrase with possible family or intimacy meaning. For the source, Shakespeare does not simply decorate the history; he changes what later audiences feel the assassination means.
