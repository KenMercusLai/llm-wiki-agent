# Amplitude

Amplitude is the [[ProductAnalytics|product analytics]] company [[SpenserSkates]], [[CurtisLiu]], and [[JeffreyWang]] built after the [[Sonalight]] pivot in [[tsr-s5-spenserskates-v2audio-tsr-s5-spenserskates-v2audio]]. The episode presents Amplitude as software for understanding user behavior, retention, and product usage more deeply than the founders could with existing analytics tools.

The company's origin is an [[InternalToolProductization]] case. Sonalight's voice-control app had a striking Demo Day demonstration but weak repeat use, so the founders built their own analysis system to ask whether first-use voice recognition success predicted retention. Other [[YCombinator]] companies, including [[PlanGrid]] and [[Gusto]], recognized the same visibility gap, pushing the team to wind down Sonalight and commit to analytics.

Amplitude's early commercial path makes it a [[CustomerEvidenceStrategy]] and [[FounderLedSales]] case. The first real buyer, [[TwelveGigs|12gigs]], treated $1,000 per month as cheap, while customers and investors from the [[Zynga]] network understood why high-resolution behavioral data mattered for product work.
