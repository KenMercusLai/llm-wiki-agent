# Project 2025

Project 2025 is the [[HeritageFoundation]]-linked conservative transition project discussed in [[vol-113-cong-jiqian-ye-zhiku-wenjian-zhong-goule-telangpu-2-0-zhizheng-jihua-beihou-de-ren-jigou-sixiang-he-mailuo-lihnknc-3om5evexocb6a6zdwueu]]. The episode treats it as more than a manifesto: it combines [[MandateForLeadership2025]], a 180-day transition plan, a potential personnel database, and training infrastructure.

The source is careful not to say that [[DonaldTrump]] will adopt the project whole. Its importance is that it shows a disciplined attempt at [[TrumpismInstitutionalization]]: turning the Trump coalition's ideas into staff, procedures, and department-level action before the administration starts.

## Key Claims
- Project 2025's four promises are family restoration, [[AdministrativeStateDismantling]], sovereignty/border/wealth defense, and protection of individual liberty.
- Its institutional value comes from preparation: authors with prior Trump-government experience, operational detail, and staffing tools.
- The episode reads it alongside the [[RepublicanParty]] platform and [[AmericaFirstPolicyInstitute]] agenda to separate ideological maximalism from likely implementation paths.
- The project makes the administrative state, immigration, education, monetary policy, and trade into linked governing targets.
