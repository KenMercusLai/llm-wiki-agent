# Ottoman Empire / 奥斯曼帝国

[[74-quanqiu-shangyin-a-kafei-wo-heise-de-apoluo-678615763]] uses the Ottoman Empire as the main bridge between Arab coffee adoption and European coffee culture. The episode connects Ottoman court taste, religious controversy, military encounters, Vienna legends, and elite diplomacy in Paris to the spread of [[Coffee|coffee]] as both a drink and a political-social medium.

The source treats Ottoman coffee culture as more than culinary transmission. Coffee's ability to gather people at night and keep them alert makes it a governance issue, while Ottoman luxury, diplomacy, and military contact help explain how European elites and merchants learned to desire and adapt the drink. This links the empire to [[CoffeehousePublicSphere]], [[CaffeinatedModernity]], and [[CoffeeCommodityPolitics]].

The wiki also has an Ottoman cultural branch through [[153-wode-mingzi-jiao-hong-yong-yiqian-lingyi-ye-de-xintiao-wancheng-zhechang-mousha-shang-917670296]] and [[154-wode-mingzi-jiao-hong-yong-yiqian-lingyi-ye-de-xintiao-wancheng-zhechang-mousha-xia-919262400]], where [[MyNameIsRed|《我的名字叫红》]] and [[OttomanMiniaturePainting]] connect Ottoman court culture to art, religious legitimacy, style, murder narration, and Frankish portrait pressure. The upper episode adds [[Istanbul]], secret court-book patronage, religious conservative pressure, and the 1590 setting as a mirror for modern Turkish identity conflict.
