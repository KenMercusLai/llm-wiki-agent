# Boom Supersonic

Boom Supersonic is [[BlakeScholl]]'s commercial supersonic-aircraft company in [[tsr-s5-blakescholl-v3-finalaudio-tsr-s5-blakescholl-v3-finalaudio]]. The episode presents Boom as an attempt to revive passenger supersonic flight through a startup de-risking sequence rather than a government prestige program or a conventional aerospace incumbent project.

Boom's first durable idea in the source is the [[AllBusinessClassSupersonicModel]]: target travelers already paying business or first-class fares, make the flight short enough that passengers need less sleeping space, and use the cabin-density gain to support better economics than Concorde. This made the first market transoceanic premium travel, not overland boomless flight.

The company-building arc then moves through [[HardTechCustomerIntentProof]] and [[HardProblemMVPScoping]]. [[VirginGroup|Virgin]] gave Boom a public customer-intent signal before the company had a passenger aircraft, while [[XB1SupersonicDemonstrator|XB-1]] gave the team a prototype platform for learning supersonic aircraft design and proving execution before [[OvertureSupersonicAirliner|Overture]].

The source's most important strategic turn is the [[RollsRoyce|Rolls-Royce]] engine crisis. Scholl says Boom initially pursued a traditional engine supplier for credibility, but the break forced [[CrisisForcedVerticalIntegration]]. In his account, owning the engine path later enabled [[BoomlessCruise]], better economics, and future product options that the supplier route would not have supported.

## Source Position
- The source argues that Concorde's failure should not be generalized to all passenger supersonic travel.
- Boom's first model did not require solving sonic boom because the initial route set could be mostly over water.
- Scholl says the company's own engine path later made boomless cruise practical without changing the aircraft.
- The passenger timeline, regulatory-reversal claim, airline-order status, and undisclosed engine-enabled benefits should remain source-scoped unless independently checked in a later source.
