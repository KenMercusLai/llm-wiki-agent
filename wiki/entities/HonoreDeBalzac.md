# Honore de Balzac / 巴尔扎克

Honore de Balzac is discussed in [[82-xianliao-weida-zuojia-de-bagua-di-er-dan-695228589]] as a writer whose social-novel ambition sits beside an almost grotesque bodily and financial life. The source names The Human Comedy as encyclopedic social fiction, then spends more time on appetite, coffee, debt, bad manners, self-invented nobility, failed business ventures, and literary feuds.

The episode makes Balzac useful for [[AuthorMythDeflation]] because his greatness is not made dignified. He appears as ambitious and observant, but also extravagant, broke, hungry, caffeinated, status-conscious, and often ridiculous.

Balzac's conflict with [[SainteBeuve|Sainte-Beuve / 圣伯夫]] adds a literary-field layer. Reviews, salons, copyright advocacy, accusations of commercialized "industrial literature," and public rebuttal all shape the source's account of how author reputation was made and fought over in 19th-century France.
