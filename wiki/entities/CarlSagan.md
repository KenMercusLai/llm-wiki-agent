# Carl Sagan

Carl Sagan enters the wiki directly through [[177-xiaoshihou-de-shu-zenme-jiu-name-haokan-971996036]], where [[Beimin|北明]] and [[QinZong|秦总]] connect childhood scientific curiosity, 1990s pseudoscience, and the later Chinese publication/translation context around [[TheDemonHauntedWorld|《魔鬼出没的世界》]]. The source uses Sagan less as a biography than as a public-skepticism anchor.

The episode's Sagan thread matters because it preserves two attitudes at once: wonder at science and resistance to unsupported claims. That makes him a bridge from childhood popular science into [[ScienceOptimismAndPseudoscience]], [[ObservationBeforeInference]], and [[RationalHumility]].

[[185-mogui-chumo-de-shijie-guanyu-yinmoulun-ufo-waixingren-bangjia-an-maitianquan-x-dangan-jiemi-ri-996181368]] makes Sagan the central figure rather than a passing childhood-reading reference. The episode presents him as a scientist and public communicator who can take extraterrestrial life seriously as a possibility while still rejecting UFO, abduction, crop-circle, miracle, and pseudoscience claims that lack strong evidence. This turns Sagan into the wiki's clearest anchor for [[ScientificSkepticism]], [[ScientificSelfCorrection]], and [[ScienceCommunicationDisadvantage]].

## Key Claims
- Sagan functions in the source as the adult skeptical counterweight to a childhood media world full of UFOs, crystal skulls, qigong, end-times speculation, and "unsolved mystery" material.
- His role is not to kill wonder, but to attach wonder to evidence, uncertainty, and public scientific literacy.
- QinZong's earlier publishing path around Sagan-related books also helps explain the weak-tie chain that later connects her to [[Beimin|北明]] in [[172-haoyun-shi-shenme-weisha-shuo-zong-bilei-hui-bai-haoyun-961254860]].
- Episode 185 adds Sagan's stricter method: extraordinary claims should remain open questions until they survive testability, independent confirmation, and correction.
