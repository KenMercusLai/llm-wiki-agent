# Putnam Competition

The Putnam Competition is the undergraduate mathematics competition used in [[137-dui-hong-letong-de-4-xiaoshi-fangtan-ai-for-math-ba-shuxue-biancheng-lean-shuxue-tianshu-zhong-de-zhengming-zhijue-bei-chuangzao-yu-bei-faxian-de-lha-faiwxtget0qmbcosts3cb5vb]] as a benchmark for [[AxiomProver]]. The source claims that [[Axiom]] formalized the 2025 exam problems and had the system complete all 12 problems for a full score.

## Source Position
- The episode treats the Putnam result as a visible milestone, not the whole definition of [[AIForMath]].
- Hong uses it to explain the gap between human elegance and machine proofs: a correct AI proof can be much longer and less intuitive than a human solution.
- The result also functions as an organization story because the team had to formalize problems, run the system, and finish under time pressure.
