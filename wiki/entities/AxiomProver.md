# Axiom Prover

Axiom Prover is the theorem-proving system discussed by [[HongLetong]] in [[137-dui-hong-letong-de-4-xiaoshi-fangtan-ai-for-math-ba-shuxue-biancheng-lean-shuxue-tianshu-zhong-de-zhengming-zhijue-bei-chuangzao-yu-bei-faxian-de-lha-faiwxtget0qmbcosts3cb5vb]]. The source presents it as the central system built by [[Axiom]] for [[AIForMath]], [[InteractiveTheoremProving]], and eventually [[FormalVerification]].

## Source Position
- The episode says Axiom Prover solved all 12 problems from the 2025 [[PutnamCompetition]] exam after the team formalized the problems.
- Its proofs may be long and unlike human proofs, which supports Hong's claim that AI mathematical reasoning can be useful before it becomes elegant.
- Hong wants the system to move beyond proving into conjecturing, knowledge use, auto-formalization, and self-verifying reasoning.
- The episode connects Axiom Prover's math capability to code verification, where proving program properties may become more important than generating more code.
