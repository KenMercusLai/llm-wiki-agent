# Lucius Cornelius Sulla / 苏拉

Lucius Cornelius Sulla / 苏拉 appears in [[124-kaisa-zhisi-lishi-de-yunjiao-shifou-yi-shixiao-781894361]] as the violent precedent behind Caesar-era politics. The episode presents Sulla's conflict with [[GaiusMarius]] and his use of public death lists as a key sign that the [[RomanRepublic|Roman Republic]] had already crossed into civil terror before [[JuliusCaesar|Caesar]] took power.

Sulla also matters in Caesar's early biography. Because Caesar was tied to the Marian side, Sulla demanded that he divorce Cornelia; Caesar refused and fled. The episode uses this story to show Caesar's early pride, loyalty, and appetite for risk.

The source later contrasts Caesar's decision not to rule through a Sulla-style guard-and-terror system. Caesar's clemency may have been politically attractive, but it also left him exposed to elite assassination.
