# Scribd

Scribd is the document-sharing and later book-subscription company founded by [[JaredFriedman]], [[TrippScribd|Tripp]], and later [[TConScribd|T-Con]] after the failure of [[Wholist]]. In [[tsr-s5-jared-v3final-tsr-s5-jared-v3final]], Jared frames the original idea as "YouTube for documents," born from a post-[[YouTube]] acquisition brainstorming cycle about which media types could become uploadable, searchable, and web-native.

The episode turns Scribd into a messy success case rather than a clean product-market-fit story. The company launched in March 2007, received attention from [[HackerNews]], [[Slashdot]], [[Digg]], [[Reddit]], major news outlets, and cable news, and then raised a Series A led by [[Redpoint]] after interest from [[CRV]] and [[SequoiaCapital|Sequoia]]. It also faced immediate [[UserGeneratedContentCopyrightRisk]] because documents could include copyrighted material.

Jared says [[PaulBuchheit]]'s advice changed Scribd's growth model. Rather than treating Scribd as entertainment like YouTube, Buchheit framed it as a search destination. That led to [[SearchDrivenContentGrowth]]: uploads created pages that drew [[Google]] search traffic, which encouraged more uploads and more indexable pages. Jared says Scribd later became a top-100 website with more than a billion annual visitors, while still having [[ShallowProductMarketFit]] compared with companies whose users deeply loved the product.
