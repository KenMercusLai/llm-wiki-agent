# The Prose Edda / 散文埃达

The Prose Edda is the 13th-century explanatory Norse text discussed by [[XiaoChuzhou|萧楚舟]] in [[ep269-cong-manwei-yuzhou-dao-aodesai-weihe-zongyao-fanpai-yingxiong-shishi-ltklotheqpup6oamyflfzpxtipsw]]. The source describes it as a later organizing guide that supplies background, story explanation, and poetic craft instruction for material related to [[PoeticEdda|《诗体埃达》]].

Its wiki value is as a layering signal. Together, the two Eddas show that [[NorseMythology]] reaches the modern reader through oral performance, manuscript preservation, medieval explanation, and later fantasy or superhero reuse rather than through one unmediated ancient book.
