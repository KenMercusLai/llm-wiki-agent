# 《海尔兄弟》

《海尔兄弟》 is the Chinese science-animation series discussed in [[177-xiaoshihou-de-shu-zenme-jiu-name-haokan-971996036]]. [[QinZong|秦总]] and [[Beimin|北明]] treat it as a childhood media case where commercial sponsorship, long production, world travel, science explanation, teamwork, and global geography combined into a surprisingly ambitious popular-science project.

The episode compares its two-brother exploration structure to [[HalAndRogerAdventures|《哈尔罗杰历险记》]], while also emphasizing its local 1990s significance: Chinese children could encounter the Arctic, the Persian Gulf, the Mediterranean, Bermuda, the Sargasso Sea, and Chinese mountains and rivers through an accessible animated format.

## Key Claims
- The series represents science optimism and global curiosity inside Chinese children's media.
- Its sponsorship does not erase its educational role in the source; the episode treats it as a genuine childhood science-and-world-view artifact.
- The show matters as an example of children's media that assumed children could handle geography, environment, risk, and explanation rather than only moral lessons.
