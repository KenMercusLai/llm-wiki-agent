# Jiang Yang

Jiang Yang (匠杨) is the founder of [[Paperboy]] and one of the guests in [[renlei-he-ai-agent-de-zuijia-peihe-fangshi-hai-mei-bei-faming-duitan-paperboy-ltgxurpseowqggfvgc32aurymt-o]]. He frames Paperboy around the claim that the best way for humans and agents to work together has not yet been invented. His prior projects include [[Millian]] and [[SameDev]], which the source uses to establish his background in developer tools and fast AI-assisted product prototyping.

## Views In The Source
- He argues that prompting is an unnatural communication layer compared with high-bandwidth working relationships where collaborators share context.
- He wants agents to learn user taste, judgment, relationship boundaries, and work heuristics through [[OSLevelContext]] and [[PersistentAgentMemory]].
- He describes Paperboy's shift away from replacing [[Slack]] and toward bringing agents into existing workflows.
- He emphasizes market picking, CEO leverage, management learning, recruiting discipline, and the need for both young high-agency builders and deep infrastructure experts.
