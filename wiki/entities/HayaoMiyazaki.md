# Hayao Miyazaki / 宫崎骏

Hayao Miyazaki appears in [[180-ailisi-mengyou-xianjing-shijie-duo-huangdan-wo-yeshi-ziji-de-zhuzai-xia-975539611]] through the episode's discussion of brave girl protagonists, British children's literature, and otherworld stories. That source treats his work as part of the afterlife of [[AliceInWonderland|《爱丽丝梦游仙境》]] rather than as a direct one-to-one adaptation.

[[94-jibuli-de-tiancaimen-wo-de-rensheng-jiaogei-reai-he-youyi-722350232]] makes Miyazaki a central figure rather than a comparison point. It follows his relationship with [[IsaoTakahata|高畑勋]] and [[ToshioSuzuki|铃木敏夫]] from the pre-Ghibli period through [[NausicaaOfTheValleyOfTheWind|《风之谷》]], [[MyNeighborTotoro|《龙猫》]], [[PrincessMononoke|《幽灵公主》]], [[SpiritedAway|《千与千寻》]], and [[TheBoyAndTheHeron|《你想活出怎样的人生》]]. The episode emphasizes his imagination, detail obsession, female protagonists, aircraft fascination, and late-career refusal to end cleanly, while also showing that his work depends on [[CreativeProducerMediation]] and [[AnimationStudioCreativeEcology]].

Across the two sources, Miyazaki remains a strong case for active young protagonists and strange rule-worlds, but no longer as a solitary genius. The Ghibli source reframes him through friendship, rivalry, budgets, deadlines, rights, marketing, and institutional support.

## Key Claims
- Miyazaki is used as a comparative creator for girl-centered otherworld fantasy.
- The source treats British children's literature as one possible influence on his active young heroines.
- The comparison extends Alice from Victorian nonsense into modern animated fantasy.
- The Ghibli episode treats Miyazaki's genius as real but institutionally mediated by Takahata, Suzuki, and the studio.
- [[NausicaaOfTheValleyOfTheWind|《风之谷》]] and [[MyNeighborTotoro|《龙猫》]] show his ability to turn detailed worlds and child-facing fantasy into risky studio projects.
- [[PrincessMononoke|《幽灵公主》]], [[SpiritedAway|《千与千寻》]], and [[TheBoyAndTheHeron|《你想活出怎样的人生》]] show the source's late-Miyazaki pattern: complexity, symbolic pressure, and refusal to make retirement a tidy ending.
