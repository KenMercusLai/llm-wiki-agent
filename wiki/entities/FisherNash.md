# Fisher Nash

Fisher Nash appears in [[bookstore-economics]] as the book buyer at [[CarmichaelsBookstore|Carmichael's Bookstore]] in Louisville, Kentucky. The episode uses Fisher's workflow to show [[BookstoreBuying]] as a practical mix of metadata, comparable sales, shelf-space constraints, customer knowledge, sales-rep advice, and instinct.

Fisher's decisions make retail gatekeeping concrete. They initially ordered the [[PlanetMoneyBook]] in display quantity, then increased the order after [[AlexiHorowitzGhazi]]'s visit suggested higher local demand.

## Key Claims
- Fisher reviews 12,000 to 15,000 titles per season and usually orders about 20 to 25 percent of what is offered.
- They treat comparable title sales as the most important order signal.
- Their copy-count logic connects directly to [[RetailShelfPlacement]] because one, two, and four copies create different levels of visibility.
- Their Planet Money order illustrates how [[AuthorPlatformRiskReduction]] can affect bookstore buying when a local customer base is likely to know the show.
