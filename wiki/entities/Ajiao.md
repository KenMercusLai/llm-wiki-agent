# 阿娇 / 柱子哥

阿娇, also referred to as 柱子哥, is the guest in [[184-zhenshi-yu-wo-you-wanjun-zhi-zhong-huanying-xianv-ajiao-990545010]]. The episode presents her as a writer, late-stage gastric-cancer patient, former legal/finance/outbound-business professional, and public narrator of illness and death.

The source deliberately resists reducing her to an "anti-cancer blogger." Ajiao wants her work to be judged by professional standards, whether as a lawyer, executive, or writer, and she treats illness writing as one part of a larger life that includes travel, work, humor, stubbornness, rescue action, and public concern.

## Key Claims
- Ajiao helped coordinate the [[HadiyaRescueEvent|哈迪亚营救事件]] by applying local knowledge from Pakistan and by favoring low-profile handling when publicity could increase risk.
- Her overseas work involved investment exploration, M&A/legal work, financial-license applications and acquisitions, and repeated country-by-country restarting.
- She often worked without the protections of a large organization, making [[UnprotectedOverseasWork]] central to her account of freedom, risk, and loneliness.
- Cancer changed her work and identity path, but the episode frames it as an intensifier rather than her whole identity.
- Her writing project is grounded in [[PublicIllnessWriting]], [[WholeProcessCancerDocumentation]], and [[HospiceCare]]: she wants to record illness, death, and care in ways that are not sanitized.
- She insists on [[ProfessionalDignityBeyondPatientIdentity]] by refusing pity-based evaluation.
- Her life philosophy is close to [[MeaningThroughExperience]]: meaning is made by experience, presence, self-narration, and subjective commitment.
