# 《是，大臣》 / Yes Minister

《是，大臣》 / Yes Minister is the British political-satire series discussed in [[118-shi-dachen-xinla-fengci-yongbuguoshi-768665425]]. The episode treats it as a peak example of government satire because it makes the day-to-day relationship between elected ministers and permanent civil servants into comedy without losing institutional precision.

The source's reading centers on [[JamesHacker|James Hacker]] as the minister who wants reform and public credit, [[HumphreyAppleby|Humphrey Appleby]] as the permanent secretary defending departmental continuity, and [[BernardWoolley|Bernard Woolley]] as the private secretary caught between them. The show becomes a practical anatomy of [[CivilServiceContinuity]], [[AdministrativeOpacityAsControl]], and [[BureaucraticWorkloadControl]].

The episode also emphasizes that the book version adds diary, letter, and mock-editorial frames. That form extends the satire from dialogue into archives: even the later act of explaining Hacker's papers becomes a new occasion for institutional irony.
