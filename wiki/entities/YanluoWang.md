# 阎罗王

阎罗王 is the Chinese folk form of the Indian Yama/Yanluo death judge discussed in [[136-chunri-mingmei-liaoliao-guishen-827911486]]. The episode treats him as a clear example of religious localization: a figure entering through Buddhist materials becomes part of Chinese popular hell, official judgment, and moral-storytelling systems.

The source stresses that Chinese folk memory often turns admired officials into Yanluo-like judges. Figures such as Bao Zheng, Kou Zhun, Fan Zhongyan, and Han Qinhu are invoked because people want a court after death that is less corrupt than life. Yanluo Wang therefore connects [[AfterlifeMoralAccounting]] with public desire for incorruptible judgment.
