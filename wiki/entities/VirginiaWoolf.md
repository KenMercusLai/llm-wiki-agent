# Virginia Woolf / 弗吉尼亚·伍尔夫

Virginia Woolf is discussed in [[60-xianliao-weida-zuojiamen-de-bagua-di-yi-dan-648693204]] as a writer whose public image is often dominated by depression, trauma, and suicide. The episode does include childhood sexual abuse allegations and mental illness, while marking some family-relationship details as contested in biographical research.

The source then deliberately complicates the tragic-woman frame through [[AuthorMythDeflation]]. Woolf is also shown as mischievous, rebellious, and socially daring, from the Rodin studio anecdote to the Abyssinian hoax in which she and friends tricked the British Navy while disguised as a foreign delegation.

Woolf's page therefore records a caution about single-image biography. Trauma can be real and important without becoming the only interpretive key to a writer's life or work.
