# Niccolo Machiavelli / 马基雅维利

Niccolo Machiavelli appears in [[51-yanshi-fanrenlei-tonghua-gushi-geliefu-youji-ke-shen-le-qu-le-632315320]] as part of the political background used to read [[GulliversTravels|《格列佛游记》]]. That source places him near the beginning of a modern political tendency to build from assumptions about human badness and fixed human nature.

[[72-junzhulun-du-ta-shi-yichang-weixian-de-maoxian-673440837]] turns Machiavelli from a passing reference into a central figure. It reads [[ThePrince|《君主论》 / The Prince]] through Renaissance Florence, the [[FlorentineRepublic|Florentine Republic / 佛罗伦萨共和国]], the [[HouseOfMedici|House of Medici / 美第奇家族]], [[GirolamoSavonarola|Savonarola]], war, exile, and Machiavelli's later writing of [[DiscoursesOnLivy|《论李维》 / Discourses on Livy]].

The episode's Machiavelli is neither a pure republican hero nor an evil tutor. He is a realist who separates political analysis from moral comfort, asks how rulers survive and found order, and forces readers to confront the possibility that good intentions are not enough. That realism is dangerous because it can become admiration for cruelty, but the source keeps returning to conscience, limits, and responsibility.
