# 安徽小木匠 / Anhui Xiao Mujiang

Anhui Xiao Mujiang is the AI short-drama work discussed in [[267-3000-kuai-chengben-3-5-yi-ci-bofang-ai-duanju-zenme-zai-douyin-zhengqian-lrvmi-ruxz81afnqxdfk-dldixhx]]. [[XiaoGuoGege]] says the work was adapted from [[FanqieNovel]] IP, produced with AI tools, published through [[Douyin]] and [[Hongguo]]-related channels, and eventually reached 350 million plays with roughly 500,000 RMB in creator payout.

The episode uses the work as a full-stack case for [[AIShortDrama]]: topic selection, source IP, [[Doubao]] script adaptation, prompt generation, Seedance-style video generation, reuse of flawed generated material, editing, submission, distributor amplification, and delayed settlement all shaped the outcome. Its rural carpenter topic also matters: the creator chose it because the original story had strong interaction and because woodworking, countryside memory, and practical craft resonated with broad older and rural audiences.

## Key Points
- Reported production cost was under 3,000 RMB, mainly compute/tool cost, but the creator invested intensive time and editing labor.
- The source says first-day and first-month playback growth was rapid, eventually reaching 350 million plays.
- Distributor and ad-buying amplification, rather than pure organic recommendation, likely drove much of the scale.
- The work depended on a [[FanqieNovel]] IP-adaptation route that the episode says later closed, limiting direct replication.
- The work is a positive case for [[AIVideoProductionWorkflow]] but a cautionary case for revenue predictability and platform dependence.
