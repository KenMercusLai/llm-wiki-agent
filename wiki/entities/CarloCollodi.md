# Carlo Collodi / 卡洛·科洛迪

Carlo Collodi / 卡洛·科洛迪 is the Italian writer of [[AdventuresOfPinocchio|《木偶奇遇记》]], introduced in [[122-pinuocao-chengzhang-de-cantong-jiqi-suo-chuangzuo-de-777464172]]. The episode presents him as a writer whose lasting work came not from grand literary status, but from turning a personal, anxious, education-facing fairy tale into a story with wide symbolic force.

The source says Collodi began the Pinocchio story in 1881 as a serial and first ended it with the puppet hanged by villains. After reader reaction, he continued the story into the longer version known today. The episode also links his background to the Italian independence period and later disillusionment with politics, after which children's writing, education, and translation became more central.

## Key Claims
- Collodi is the authorial reason the episode distinguishes "writer-made fairy tale" from older folk tale.
- The source treats his first hanging ending as evidence that [[AdventuresOfPinocchio|Pinocchio]] was never designed as a safe moral primer.
- His personal concerns become general through dreamlike adventure, punishment, hunger, family attachment, and transformation.
