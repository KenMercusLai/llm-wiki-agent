# 《克拉克森的农场》 / Clarkson's Farm

《克拉克森的农场》 / Clarkson's Farm is the television-and-book object discussed in [[62-kelakesen-de-nongchang-xiangbudao-ni-shi-zheyang-de-xiaoyang-xiaoen-652404260]]. In the episode, [[JeremyClarkson]] buys and later directly runs a large farm, turning his failures with a Lamborghini tractor, sheep, pigs, bees, ponds, woodland, crop policy, and public complaints into a comic but practical education in [[AgriculturalSystemsReality]].

The source treats the show and book as complementary. The screen version supplies the visible comedy of incompetence and rural characters, while the written version concentrates Clarkson's jokes, complaints, and reflections on farming, environmental policy, and animals.

## Key Claims
- The show's farm comedy depends on real operating constraints: wrong-sized machines, incompatible attachments, dangerous equipment, weather, animals, and government forms.
- Its value is not only "celebrity becomes farmer"; it shows how much invisible skill and coordination sit behind ordinary food production.
- The source uses the farm to test environmental assumptions, especially when a policy looks correct locally but shifts cost to farmers, imports, other habitats, or future maintenance.
