# Frankenstein / 《弗兰肯斯坦》

[[141-zhizao-fulankensitan-kehuan-zhimu-he-tade-shenqi-shidai-857923931]] turns the novel into a full source branch. The episode corrects the public shorthand that "Frankenstein" is the monster's name, then reads the unnamed creature and Victor Frankenstein through [[ScienceFictionContemporaryScienceIntegration]], [[ScientificAnxietyLiteraryMaterial]], [[ModernPrometheusReading]], and [[CreatorResponsibilityEthics]].

The source's central claim is that the novel is not "unscientific" simply because its science is not modern. It absorbs the live scientific problems of [[MaryShelley|Mary Shelley / 玛丽·雪莱]]'s time: [[AnimalElectricityDebate]], [[VitalismLifePrincipleDebate]], [[SpontaneousGenerationLifeOrigin]], [[AlchemyChemistryContinuity]], [[CadaverTradeAnatomyFear]], [[EarlyTransplantationImaginary]], and [[ElectricTherapySpeculation]].

[[Frankenstein]] appears in [[68-fengkuang-shiyan-shi-ai-zhe-gaisi-de-qiuzhiyu-664384549]] as a bridge between literary imagination and experimental science. The episode argues that the fantasy of body animation was not pure invention: public and private electrical experiments on frogs, animal parts, and bodies made galvanic reanimation imaginable in the cultural environment around [[MaryShelley|Mary Shelley / 玛丽·雪莱]].

The source uses the novel less as a plot object than as an example of how science history enters fiction. [[ScientificPublicSpectacle]] and early electrical demonstrations turn the gothic premise into a memory of actual experimental culture, while [[ExperimentalScienceEthics]] keeps the question of bodily manipulation and curiosity's limits visible.
