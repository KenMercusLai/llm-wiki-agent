# David Clements

David Clements appears in [[tsr-ycoffsite-kasishgupta-v1-audioonly-tsr-ycoffsite-kasishgupta-v1-audioonly]] as the leadership coach [[KashishGupta]] credits with teaching him that a founder may be the only person in a company who can afford to be wrong. Gupta uses that lesson to explain why [[FounderMode]] includes risk-taking under uncertainty rather than only direct operational involvement.

In the source, Clements's idea helps separate founder risk from ordinary employee incentives. Employees can be fired for failed bets, while founders have more context, more authority, and often more room to absorb being wrong; therefore a founder who is never wrong may not be taking enough risk.
