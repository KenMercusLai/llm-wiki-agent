# Rahul Banerjee

Rahul Banerjee appears in [[the-latest-tv-innovations-have-their-critics]] as a viewer who found modern high-end OLED television motion distracting for movies. The source says he bought and returned two top-of-the-line OLED TVs, then bought an old plasma TV on Facebook Marketplace for forty dollars.

His role in the wiki is as a concrete consumer example for [[TVMotionStutter]] and [[PlasmaTVPreference]]. Banerjee's case shows that subjective motion quality can matter more than headline specs such as resolution, brightness, and black levels when the use case is film-style content.
