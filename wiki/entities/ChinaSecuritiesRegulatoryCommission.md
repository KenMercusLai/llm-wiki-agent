# China Securities Regulatory Commission

China Securities Regulatory Commission is the securities regulator referenced in [[ep46-lici-niushi-zhongshengxiang-cuoshoubuji-de-xingfu-neng-chixu-duojiu-li10xwkla-3kk-zgde2i0uxbpot2]] as the market institution that emerged after early exchange trading had already begun. The source connects the regulator to fraud responses, speculation warnings, price-limit rules, market expansion, and the cleanup of off-market financing during the 2014-2015 A-share cycle.

## Source Position
- The episode presents early market regulation as reactive and developmental: supervision matured after trading, fraud, and speculative pressure were already visible.
- The 1996 "twelve gold medals" and later price-limit framework are used as examples of regulatory attempts to cool speculation without destroying market confidence.
- The 2015 off-market financing cleanup shows the regulator's role in removing fragile leverage, even when that cleanup can accelerate forced selling.
