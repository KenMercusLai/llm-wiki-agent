# Francois Truffaut / 特吕弗

Francois Truffaut appears in [[108-zu-yu-zhan-ai-yu-bu-ai-bali-jiu-zai-nali-songjian-745812905]] as the [[FrenchNewWave|French New Wave]] director whose adaptation of [[HenriPierreRoche|Henri-Pierre Roche]]'s [[JulesAndJim|《祖与占》 / Jules and Jim]] turns a late-life autobiographical novel into a formally light, emotionally severe film. The episode stresses his youth at the time of the film, while also noting the work's polished maturity.

The source frames Truffaut through both [[AuteurTheory|auteur theory]] and respectful adaptation. He corresponded with Roche, gained approval for script and casting choices, and then made a film that preserved the novel's quick, telegraphic movement while giving the relationship among [[CatherineJulesAndJim|Catherine]], [[JulesJulesAndJim|Jules]], and [[JimJulesAndJim|Jim]] a cinematic rhythm of running, glances, narration, still images, song, war footage, and abrupt death.
