# Kenneth Bianchi

Kenneth Bianchi is the Hillside Strangler case figure used in [[121-jijian-fayi-jianshi-shouce-yiqie-fanzui-jieyou-henji-775064343]] to discuss forensic psychology, adversarial testimony, and feigned mental illness. The episode says Bianchi tried to simulate multiple personality disorder, but expert questioning exposed inconsistencies in the performance.

The case matters for [[CriminalProfilingAsProbabilisticInference]] because it keeps psychological interpretation from becoming automatic story-matching. Courts need expert evidence, but the source shows that suspect performance, diagnosis, responsibility, and strategy must be separated carefully.
