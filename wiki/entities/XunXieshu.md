# 《讯蟹书》

《讯蟹书》 is the crab-defense text by [[LiQi|李琪]] discussed in [[114-youdao-chi-ao-shangju-shi-liaoliao-pangxie-de-quwen-shihua-758424117]]. The episode describes a mock trial in which people accuse the crab of strange form, sideways motion, aggression, and other faults. The crab answers that its body is given by nature, that "无肠" can be read as lacking schemes rather than lacking virtue, and that humans are often more blameworthy than small animals.

The text matters because it crystallizes [[AnimalMoralProjection]]. A crab first becomes a target for human disgust and moral accusation, then becomes a speaker capable of reversing the accusation. The episode uses this to widen [[CrabCulturalSymbolism]] from eating and folklore into legal parody, ethical satire, and sympathy for a creature normally treated as food.

## Key Claims
- The mock-trial structure turns animal stigma into a courtroom-like test of human judgment.
- "No intestines" is reinterpreted from a defect into a possible sign of guilelessness.
- The text's ethical force comes from reversing scale: a small animal accused by humans exposes larger human cruelty and hypocrisy.
