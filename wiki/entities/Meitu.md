# Meitu / 美图

Meitu is the central company case in [[263-sora-si-le-adobe-die-le-meitu-he-qu-he-cong-lgjmyveooc8wpzr0yviggvzvdyfs]]. The episode presents Meitu as moving from consumer beauty tools and past platform ambition toward AI application work across production tools, globalization, ecommerce advertising, and agent-callable capabilities.

The source's Meitu thesis is that a mature tool company can survive model pressure if it keeps moving: raw model output may absorb generic features, but Meitu can still own [[AIApplicationLayerMoat]] through beauty-specific detail, visual taste, user behavior data, ecommerce material workflows, style consistency, batch generation, ROI feedback, and [[ModelContainerStrategy]]. [[WuXinhong]]'s "AI team" framing makes the company less a single editor and more a workflow layer that coordinates multiple capabilities for a result.

## Key Points
- [[MeituXiuXiu]], [[BeautyCam]], [[Meipai]], [[MeituDesignStudio]], [[Kaipai]], and [[Wink]] anchor the episode's Meitu product history.
- The source says Meitu's production-tool and globalization push aims to raise paid conversion and ARPU after its user base is relatively stable.
- [[MeituDesignStudio]] is framed as a vertical workflow product for ecommerce, advertising, and design material generation.
- [[Kaipai]] began from a teleprompter-like function in BeautyCam and became a separate product after usage data showed demand.
- Meitu's competition with [[Jianying]] is not treated as a winner-take-all video-editing fight; the source emphasizes narrower scenarios where Meitu has stronger beauty or visual-detail know-how.
- Meitu's model posture is [[ModelContainerStrategy]], using external APIs, open-source models, self-trained vertical models, and product-layer judgment depending on the task.
- Meitu packaging capabilities as [[AISkills]] is treated as a possible entry into [[ToAgentDistribution]].
