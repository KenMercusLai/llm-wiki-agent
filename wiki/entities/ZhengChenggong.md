# Zheng Chenggong / 郑成功

Zheng Chenggong is the maritime military leader in [[no-207-minnan-wangshi-zhongshen-renjian-banshichu-zhanghai-shengzhong-wanguo-shang-992579918]] whose story connects Ming-Qing transition, anti-Qing resistance, trade networks, intelligence routes, and Taiwan. The episode describes him as splitting from [[ZhengZhilong]] after his father submitted to the Qing, then using Kinmen and Xiamen as bases before turning to Taiwan after the failed northern campaign.

The source treats the Taiwan campaign as more than a simple nationalist recovery narrative. It is also a strategic search for a stable rear base for a maritime regime under military pressure. That places Zheng Chenggong inside [[MinnanMaritimeCommercialCulture]]: politics, family split, sea power, trade routes, and settlement all become parts of one maritime system.

## Source Position
- Zheng Chenggong's resistance depends on maritime logistics and commercial networks, not only battlefield legitimacy.
- Taiwan appears as both symbolic territory and practical base for a sea power under pressure.
- The later Qing coastal relocation policy is framed as an attempt to cut off support to Zheng power, intensifying migration and coastal suffering.
