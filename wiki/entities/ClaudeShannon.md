# Claude Shannon

Claude Shannon appears in [[e153-gushen-de-paiju-fuli-gongshi-kaili-gongshi-lmyvi9mnlaqib-baspjiehz3epqc]] as the information-theory reference behind the episode's treatment of [[KellyCriterion]]. The source connects Shannon's information-transmission framing with repeated betting, blackjack, and [[EdwardThorp]]'s attempt to turn probability information into disciplined capital growth.

## Key Claims
- Shannon gives the episode a way to relate information advantage to capital growth: better information is useful only when it can be transmitted into action through sizing and repetition.
- The episode uses his friendship and collaboration with [[EdwardThorp]] to connect casino probability, information theory, and financial-market tactics.
- Shannon's investment results are mentioned as part of the broader point that mathematical edge still needs long-term discipline.

## Connections
- [[EdwardThorp]] — main historical counterpart in the source.
- [[KellyCriterion]] — sizing rule whose structure the episode compares with information theory.
- [[InvestmentEdge]] and [[PositionSizing]] — concepts that translate information advantage into investable behavior.
- [[QuantitativeInvesting]] — adjacent market style that turns information and signals into repeated decisions.
