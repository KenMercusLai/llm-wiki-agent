# Twitter / X

Twitter appears in the wiki as a social platform, distribution gatekeeper, and startup-context surface. In [[tsr-ycoffsite-chrisbest-audioonly-v1final-mov-tsr-ycoffsite-chrisbest-audioonly-v1final-mov]], [[ChrisBest]] treats Twitter as a major upstream discovery platform for [[Substack]] writers and as the platform controlled by [[ElonMusk]] during Substack's launch of [[SubstackNotes]].

Best's account makes Twitter a direct [[PlatformDependencyRisk]] case. Substack creators could publish and charge on Substack, but readers often found them through Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn, or other networks. Best says that after Substack launched a Twitter-like product, Twitter restricted visibility for posts containing "Substack," impaired search for the term, and slowed Substack links; he also says the company impact was limited because Twitter traffic dependence had already declined.

Twitter also appears elsewhere as part of the social-web environment. In [[socialradarspod-paulb-final]], [[FriendFeed]] struggled partly because its social aggregation product overlapped with Facebook and Twitter and sometimes depended on Twitter outages for growth. In [[socialradarspod-stevehuffman-final]], [[Reddit]] is discussed against larger social and link-sharing competitors, including Twitter and Facebook as platforms whose mistakes smaller platforms could observe.
