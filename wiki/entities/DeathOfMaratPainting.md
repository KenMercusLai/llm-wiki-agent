# The Death of Marat / 《马拉之死》

The Death of Marat / 《马拉之死》 is [[JacquesLouisDavid|Jacques-Louis David]]'s painting of [[JeanPaulMarat|Jean-Paul Marat]] after his assassination by [[CharlotteCorday|Charlotte Corday]]. In [[125-mala-zhisi-beibi-de-ansha-huo-zhengyi-de-chujue-783972516]], it is the episode's entry point into how revolutionary art can manufacture martyrdom.

The source emphasizes the painting's political omissions and transformations. Marat is made calm, clean, generous, and saint-like; Corday disappears from the visual field; the paper and pose redirect attention from a bloody political killing toward sacrifice and charity.

The painting therefore belongs to [[RevolutionaryMartyrdomImageMaking]] and [[HistoricalMemoryContest]]. Later sympathetic images of Corday reverse part of David's arrangement by making the assassin visible, composed, and morally serious.
