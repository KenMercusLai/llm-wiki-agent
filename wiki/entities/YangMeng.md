# Yang Meng / 杨萌

Yang Meng is the founder and CEO of [[AnkerInnovations]] in [[144-dui-yang-meng-de-4-xiaoshi-fangtan-xiaofei-dianzi-si-yu-sheng-di-san-lei-gongsi-duan-ce-moxing-chanpin-fangfa-youxi-moshi-lnjleqjgjo1txupouvygmdv7oo8b]]. The episode presents him as a product-and-organization thinker shaped by computer science, machine-learning research, [[Google]]'s engineering culture, Amazon-channel entrepreneurship, and a long effort to turn a charging-accessory company into a multi-category consumer-electronics system.

His recurring frame is that companies should create concrete value for specific people in specific scenes. That leads to [[OneThreeFiveSevenConsumerSegmentation]], [[ShallowSeaDeepSeaCategoryStrategy]], [[ThirdTypeCompany]], and [[CreatorCulture]] on the business side, and to [[InMemoryComputingForEdgeAI]], [[OnDeviceModelHierarchy]], [[TrueSmartHome]], and [[EnterprisePrearrangedAgents]] on the AI side.

## Source Position
- Yang says he is more excited by visible, useful value than by remote or abstract technical achievement.
- His early Google experience is used to explain Anker's emphasis on openness, equality, problem decomposition, and technical-lead style communication.
- He describes Anker's first decade as a "five-series" stable-quality company and its second-decade ambition as becoming a "seven-series" extreme-innovation company.
- His product method is to create unique value for a defined user segment in a defined scenario.
- He treats AI as a survival condition for Anker because it changes user value, organizational efficiency, and talent growth at the same time.
- His risk posture is middle-intensity: avoid all-in leverage, but invest enough to reach the next step when the direction is clear.
