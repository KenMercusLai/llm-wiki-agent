# Walt Whitman / 惠特曼

Walt Whitman appears in [[60-xianliao-weida-zuojiamen-de-bagua-di-yi-dan-648693204]] as a poet whose public image is shaped by sexuality, bodily self-mythology, and 19th-century pseudo-scientific confidence. The episode connects [[OscarWilde|Oscar Wilde / 王尔德]]'s reported visit to Whitman with Whitman's own evasions around male intimacy, erotic poetry, and invented or unverified claims about female lovers and children.

The source also uses Whitman for [[OccultPseudoscienceLiteraryModernity]]. His interest in phrenology and reported decision to donate his brain to an anthropometric association become comic evidence that literary modernity included confident but unstable sciences as well as serious art.

Whitman's page should therefore be read source-locally. The episode is not a full account of his poetry; it records how [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] uses Whitman to show that canonical author images are built from poems, rumors, bodies, and period beliefs together.
