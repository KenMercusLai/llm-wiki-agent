# William McChesney Martin

William McChesney Martin appears in [[jerome-powell-and-the-test-of-fed-independence]] as the positive historical precedent for a [[FederalReserve]] chair resisting presidential pressure. The episode recounts [[LyndonBJohnson]]'s anger after Martin raised rates and presents Martin's refusal to yield as a defining example of [[CentralBankIndependence]].

## Source Position
- Martin's confrontation with Johnson is used as the episode's clearest example of a Fed chair asserting that a monetary-policy decision had to be final.
- [[BurtonAbrams]] uses Martin as the comparison point for judging [[JeromePowell]]'s resistance to [[DonaldTrump]]'s pressure for lower rates.
- The source treats the Martin example as part of the post-1951 independence era, when Fed autonomy became an institutional norm rather than a settled constitutional guarantee.

## Connections
- [[FederalReserve]] - institution Martin led.
- [[LyndonBJohnson]] - president whose pressure becomes the historical contrast.
- [[JeromePowell]] and [[BurtonAbrams]] - later comparison and evaluator in the source.
- [[CentralBankIndependence]] and [[InflationBias]] - institutional principle and economic rationale.
