# Magellanic Penguin / 麦哲伦企鹅

Magellanic penguin / 麦哲伦企鹅 is the penguin species discussed in [[138-qie-ke-wo-zai-haitan-shang-jiandao-yiwei-q-laoshi-842331726]]. The episode uses it to correct the simple image that penguins only belong to ice and snow: this is a temperate South American penguin, and the source's main individual is [[JuanSalvadorPenguin|Juan Salvador / 胡安·萨尔瓦多]].

The species matters because the source links its biology to the plot. The penguin's waterproofing, swimming need, migration endurance, group behavior, and same-species companionship all affect whether rescue, captivity, release, or human affection is adequate. Oil exposure becomes a concrete case of [[OilPollutionWildlifeHarm]], because crude oil can destroy insulation, mobility, feeding, and survival before a human rescuer even knows what to do.
