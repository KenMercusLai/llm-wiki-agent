# Gary Marcus

Gary Marcus appears in [[tech-20251215-1215-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20251215-1215-mp-tech-pod-128]] as the cognitive scientist and NYU professor emeritus explaining why [[WorldModels]] matter for robust AI. In the [[MarketplaceTech]] episode, he argues that current large language models are dominated by statistical sequence prediction rather than explicit representations of entities, facts, states, and causes.

The source uses Marcus to name the [[LLMWorldModelGap]]: if a model does not maintain something like a scene graph or fact-grounded representation of the world, it can sound fluent while hallucinating or failing at simple factual and causal reasoning. His claim is not that correlation is useless, but that stronger AI will need world models as first-class components and correlation as a supporting mechanism.

Marcus also connects the argument to [[EmbodiedAI]]. Robots and other physical agents need to reason about surfaces, strengths, spatial connections, objects, people, and possible actions. That makes world models a practical requirement for [[PhysicalAGI]], not only an abstract debate about language models.
