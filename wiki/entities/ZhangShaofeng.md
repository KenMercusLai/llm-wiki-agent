# Zhang Shaofeng

Zhang Shaofeng is the chair and CEO of [[BairongIntelligence]] and the guest in [[weishenme-gongsi-yong-buhao-ai-cong-jiaolv-dao-xingdong-de-3-ge-guanjian-dongzuo-duitan-bairong-zhineng-zhang-shaofeng-lgarngnaqran2c9p4jssurvt6ces]]. He argues that enterprise AI should be understood as a production-side shift that changes how office work is organized, measured, paid for, and managed.

## Views In The Episode
- AI should not be treated as a simple tool upgrade; it can become labor-like work capacity inside companies.
- Traditional enterprises should start with bounded, high-frequency workflows such as customer service, marketing, contract review, and reimbursement before attempting broad process redesign.
- Human employees who teach [[DigitalEmployees]] need incentives, otherwise they rationally fear replacement and resist knowledge transfer.
- Existing software systems need APIs so agents can call CRM, order, and office systems as part of [[AgenticWorkflow]].
- Chinese enterprise software needs to avoid repeating the custom-project and person-day trap; Zhang favors [[OutcomeBasedAIPricing]] and service-fee models tied to work delivered.
