# Carol Naughton

Carol Naughton appears in [[can-transforming-neighborhoods-help-kids-escape-poverty]] as the housing-policy voice whose observation helps redirect the HOPE VI evidence toward surrounding-neighborhood opportunity. In the episode, [[MattSteger]] says Naughton suggested that HOPE VI sites near more affluent areas seemed more successful.

Her role is small but structurally important. The source treats her observation as the clue that lets the research move beyond "newer buildings" toward [[CrossClassSocialCapital]], [[MixedIncomeHousing]], and the question of whether low-income children actually interact with higher-income peers.

## Key Claims
- HOPE VI outcomes cannot be explained well by building replacement alone.
- Surrounding-neighborhood affluence becomes a candidate mechanism after Naughton's observation.
- Her contribution connects practitioner pattern recognition to quantitative research.
