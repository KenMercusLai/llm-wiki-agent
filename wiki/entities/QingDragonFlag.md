# Qing Dragon Flag / 大清龙旗

Qing Dragon Flag / 大清龙旗 is the late-imperial flag case in [[127-xianliao-shier-shengxiao-zhi-long-shenqi-dongwu-zai-nali-788239655]]. The episode treats its origin and early form as confused rather than clean: stories involve court choice, diplomatic need, foreign design and production, triangular and rectangular versions, and later disagreement over whether dragon imagery suited a national flag.

Its wiki role is to show how [[ChineseDragon|龙]] moved from imperial and courtly display into public identity. The flag could mark Qing authority in diplomacy, Chinese goods in overseas commerce, official support for the Boxer movement, merchant identity in treaty-port and coastal settings, patriotic display, anti-Qing rejection, and restorationist nostalgia after the 1911 Revolution.

The episode therefore makes the flag a hinge in [[DragonNationalSymbolInvention]]. It did not instantly make the dragon a consensual national symbol, but it helped put dragon imagery into the spaces where nation, dynasty, market, foreign gaze, and public politics could fight over it.

## Key Claims
- The dragon flag's early history is fragmented, which matters because modern national symbols often look cleaner in retrospect than they were in use.
- Merchants used the flag to mark Chinese goods and identity, not only loyalty to the court.
- Revolutionary and restoration politics made hanging or removing the flag a visible political act.
- The flag helped convert imperial dragon imagery into a public symbol available for later national reinterpretation.
