# The RealReal

The RealReal is the luxury resale marketplace profiled in [[seven-allegedly-fake-chanel-bags-vs-the-realreal]]. The episode presents it as a platform built around [[LuxuryResaleAuthentication]]: buyers want discounted secondhand luxury goods, but the market only works if they believe the items are genuine.

[[JulieWainwright]] launched the company in 2011 after seeing unused luxury goods sitting in closets and storage. [[GrahamWetzbarger]] later helped build the authentication process, which the source describes as expert inspection across materials, construction, hardware, and brand identifiers. The company defended itself against [[Chanel]] by arguing that its process was rigorous and that [[FirstSaleDoctrine]] protects resale of lawfully purchased goods.

## Key Claims
- The RealReal's product promise depends on converting hidden authentication work into buyer confidence.
- The company used Chanel's name to identify listed products, not, according to its defense, to imply a Chanel partnership.
- Chanel's case exposed the risk in claiming total authenticity when even a small set of counterfeit allegations can become legally material.
- The company's antitrust counterargument reframed the dispute as incumbent brand pressure against legitimate resale competition.
