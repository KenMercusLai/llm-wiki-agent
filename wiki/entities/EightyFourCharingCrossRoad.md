# 《查令十字街84号》 / 84 Charing Cross Road

《查令十字街84号》 / 84 Charing Cross Road is the book at the center of [[134-chaling-shizi-jie-84-hao-zaici-yuanliang-shijie-de-liyou-821075226]]. The episode presents it as a real correspondence between [[HeleneHanff|海莲·汉芙]] in New York and [[MarksAndCo|马克斯与科恩书店]] in London, especially [[FrankDoel|弗兰克·多尔]], beginning in 1949 and continuing across two decades.

The source's main interpretive move is to resist shrinking the book into a romance. It can sustain romantic fantasy, but its emotional power comes from [[EpistolaryFriendship]], trust-based bookselling, postwar need, old-book searching, reciprocal gifts, and the late grief of never meeting Frank before his death.

The book also matters for [[OldBookMateriality]]. Helene does not only want text; she wants old editions with paper, binding, marginalia, previous-reader traces, and the feel of historical use. That makes the book a source case for [[NonInstrumentalLiteraryReading]] and [[ReadingAsLifeExperience]] because reading includes objects, addresses, waiting, and people who help a book arrive.
