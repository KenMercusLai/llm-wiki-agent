# Jony Ive

Jony Ive is mentioned in [[tech-20251212-1212-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20251212-1212-mp-tech-pod-128]] as the former [[Apple]] designer working with [[OpenAI]] on a possible AI device. The episode uses that collaboration to ask whether the next major AI interface will be a new hardware category or remain centered on the phone.

[[tech-20260717-0717-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260717-0717-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds more detail to that device thread through [[Bloomberg]]-reported expectations cited by [[MarketplaceTech]]. The reported product is described as a tabletop, battery-operated, screenless smart-speaker-like device with [[ChatGPT]] inside and sensors that may personalize responses. The same episode links the device ambition to [[Apple]]'s lawsuit against [[OpenAI]], making Ive's design branch part of [[AIHardwareTradeSecretDispute]] rather than only a form-factor question.

The December 2025 source does not describe the device in detail. Its useful contribution is strategic: even a designer associated with Apple's hardware history has to confront the strength of the [[SmartphoneAIHub]] and the weak record of standalone AI bracelets, glasses, and necklaces.

[[tsr-ycoffsite-pg-audioonly-final-tsr-ycoffsite-pg-audioonly-final]] adds Ive through [[PaulGraham]]'s [[FounderMode]] discussion. Graham uses [[SteveJobs]] and Ive as an example where intense founder involvement can be collaboration instead of micromanagement, because the founder's involvement raises the work rather than simply overriding the person doing it.
