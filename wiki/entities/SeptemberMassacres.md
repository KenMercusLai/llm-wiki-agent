# September Massacres / 九月屠杀

The September Massacres / 九月屠杀 are the 1792 prison killings that [[125-mala-zhisi-beibi-de-ansha-huo-zhengyi-de-chujue-783972516]] treats as a central reason [[CharlotteCorday|Charlotte Corday]] decided [[JeanPaulMarat|Marat]] had to die. The episode describes the massacre as far bloodier than the storming of the Bastille and links it to Parisian fear that enemy forces would free prisoners and turn them into internal enemies.

The source explicitly names Marat as one of the massacre's major encouragers. That claim is what moves the episode away from the saintly Marat of [[DeathOfMaratPainting|The Death of Marat]] and toward the moral problem of [[PoliticalAssassinationEthics]].

The massacre also belongs to [[RevolutionaryTerrorPolitics]] because it shows how public fear, enemy lists, emergency logic, and revolutionary purity can make killing appear preventive, righteous, and popular at once.
