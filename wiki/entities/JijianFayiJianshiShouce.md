# 《极简法医鉴识手册》

《极简法医鉴识手册》 is the forensic-science popularization book discussed in [[121-jijian-fayi-jianshi-shouce-yiqie-fanzui-jieyou-henji-775064343]]. The episode says the Chinese edition was published by Legal Publishing House and that the original belongs to the "For Dummies" family, so the book is framed as an introductory manual rather than a sensational true-crime collection.

For the wiki, the book matters because it gives [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] a way to separate crime-fiction pleasure from real [[ForensicScience|forensic science]]. The episode uses the book to explain why drowning, bloodstains, decomposition, insect stages, toxicology, fingerprints, ballistics, and psychological testimony all need context and confidence limits.

## Key Claims
- The book is treated as an entry-level map of法证鉴识, not as a collection of lurid cases.
- Its value in the episode is methodological: it shows why single-clue certainty is usually weaker than multi-evidence interpretation.
- It helps move the show's mystery-reading branch from plot devices toward [[ObservationBeforeInference]] and [[ScientificSamplingDiscipline]].
