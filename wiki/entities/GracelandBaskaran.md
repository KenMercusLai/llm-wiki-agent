# Graceland Baskaran

Graceland Baskaran is the mining economist and rare-earth expert interviewed in [[how-to-get-what-greenland-has-with-permission]]. [[PlanetMoney]] uses her to separate the existence of mineral deposits from the harder problem of turning those deposits into usable industrial and defense inputs.

Baskaran's central contribution is the [[RareEarthProcessingBottleneck]] frame. She argues that the [[UnitedStates]] does not need to take control of [[Greenland]] to improve mineral security, especially when heavy rare earth extraction there would require difficult Arctic infrastructure, decades of work, and very large capital outlays.

The source also uses her to broaden the partnership map. [[Brazil]], [[Australia]], [[SaudiArabia|Saudi Arabia]], and [[India]] are presented as more plausible near-term supply or processing partners than Greenland, while [[China]] remains the benchmark because of its processing dominance.
