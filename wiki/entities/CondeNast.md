# Conde Nast

Conde Nast appears in [[socialradarspod-stevehuffman-final]] as the media company that acquired [[Reddit]] in a deal that closed on October 31, 2006. [[SteveHuffman]] says the sale was exciting and relieving because Reddit was not yet a high-functioning company, and the acquisition also gave [[YCombinator]] meaningful press attention.

The source's strongest organizational point is that corporate ownership constrained Reddit's startup mechanics. Huffman says Reddit had trouble hiring inside Conde Nast because it could not offer startup-style stock and had to work through corporate bureaucracy, which later made the spinout and option pool important.

[[140-haikeyi-de-jin-nvshi-suoyi-ren-weishenme-yao-nuli-a-854173655]] adds a separate source-scoped Conde Nast branch through [[Jinzi|金子]]'s entrance into fashion media. In this episode, Conde Nast is less an acquisition owner than a workplace where middle-class language, stylish devices, brand signs, and [[RespectabilityTax|体面税]] make [[WorkplaceHiddenRules]] and [[MiddleClassConsumptionPressure]] visible to a newcomer.
