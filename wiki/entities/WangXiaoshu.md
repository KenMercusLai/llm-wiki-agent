# 王小叔

Wang Xiaoshu is a guest in [[266-cong-hongguo-dao-ai-duanju-shui-zai-ge-shui-de-ming-lgzf6bu7bfalr5qvnhlfzkufahob]] whose team has worked on short dramas since 2021, including domestic and overseas distribution and AI comic-drama exploration. He supplies much of the operator view on [[Hongguo]], [[Douyin]], production cost, ad-funded distribution, and creator entry.

His strongest contribution is the [[ShortDramaEconomics]] frame. He repeatedly explains why single-minute cost determines genre choice, content conservatism, platform risk, and whether AI-generated formats can attempt fantasy, sci-fi, military, or historical material that live-action short dramas cannot produce cheaply.

## Key Points
- He argues that Hongguo grew by expanding demand from users who did not necessarily watch much long video.
- He describes how natural traffic can attract distributors, who then amplify a work through paid traffic and share incremental gains.
- He treats [[AIVideoProductionWorkflow]] as a creator-opening force while still requiring scripts, tool learning, repeated generation, editing, and operational discipline.
- His overseas distribution experience includes posting thousands of short dramas on [[YouTube]] and watching pure distribution become less defensible as AI subtitles, translation, and dubbing improve.
