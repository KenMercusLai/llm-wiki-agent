# Paul Freitag

Paul Freitag appears in [[two-indicators-for-lowering-the-rent]] as a supportive-housing voice who favors allowing more boarding houses and SRO-like options, especially for younger and middle-aged people. He emphasizes that they can be built inexpensively and may work well as temporary or shorter-term housing.

His main contribution is the limit case. Freitag warns that dense shared housing can be hard for aging residents who need medical equipment, walkers, home health aides, or incontinence support, and that communicable diseases can spread more easily in dense environments. That keeps [[SharedHousingRevival]] tied to [[SupportiveHousingManagement]] rather than nostalgia for cheap rooms alone.
