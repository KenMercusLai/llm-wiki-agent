# Lyndon B. Johnson

Lyndon B. Johnson appears in [[ep77-si-shi-wan-nian-xin-fu-ye-zhuan-le-san-shi-si-yi-te-lang-pu-jiao-ni-ruhe-gao-qian-lnrx8vkdjedxbmmcwxlnwow-pcab]] as a historical comparison for political power becoming private wealth. The episode focuses on Lady Bird Johnson's purchase of KTBC and the later regulatory approvals that helped the radio and television business become valuable.

[[jerome-powell-and-the-test-of-fed-independence]] adds Johnson as the president who pressured [[WilliamMcChesneyMartin]] after the [[FederalReserve]] raised rates. The source uses the confrontation as the positive historical contrast for [[JeromePowell]]: presidential anger can be intense, but the Fed chair can still insist that a rate decision is final.

## Source Position
- The hosts say Johnson was not wealthy when he entered Congress but left behind substantial wealth.
- KTBC is used as an example of how broadcast-license approvals, network affiliation, and regulatory access can matter more than the purchase price.
- The Johnson case supports the episode's argument that [[PoliticalInfluenceMonetization]] predates [[DonaldTrump]].
- The Planet Money source uses Johnson as the presidential-pressure side of the Martin precedent for [[CentralBankIndependence]].
