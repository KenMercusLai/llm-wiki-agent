# 岛田庄司 / Shimada Soji

岛田庄司 is the Japanese mystery writer discussed in [[144-bei-zuzhou-de-munaiyi-fuermosi-hui-he-laoshe-lianshou-tanan-ma-878168738]] through [[TheCursedMummy|《被诅咒的木乃伊》]], also known in Chinese by the older title 《夏目漱石伦敦杀人事件》. The episode presents this book as a relatively cold or less central Shimada work, but one that remains useful for understanding how Japanese mystery can borrow [[SherlockHolmes|福尔摩斯]] while adding Japanese literary and comic machinery.

His source-scoped role is not only as a puzzle maker. In this episode, Shimada builds a [[HolmesPasticheTradition|Holmes pastiche]] around [[NatsumeSoseki|夏目漱石]], [[JohnWatson|华生]], a fake mummy curse, and [[DualManuscriptNarration|two competing manuscripts]]. The result keeps a [[HonkakuMystery|本格推理]] skeleton, but the host stresses that the book's charm comes from voice, spoofing, real-person insertion, and cross-cultural misunderstanding as much as from the final mechanism.
