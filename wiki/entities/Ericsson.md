# Ericsson

Ericsson is the communications-equipment-rooted member of the old handset trio in [[no-208-zhongguo-shouji-jianghu-shang-motuoluola-nuojiya-he-ailixin-de-zhushen-huanghun-zhongguo-hulianwang-gushi-23-998056376]]. The episode links Ericsson to early telephony in Shanghai, the 1987 Guangzhou mobile-network buildout, 2G GSM infrastructure contracts, and a large share of Chinese mobile-network traffic in the 1990s and early 2000s.

## Source Position
- Ericsson is stronger in the source as a network and infrastructure company than as a durable consumer handset brand.
- Its handset decline is explained partly through supply-chain response: after the Philips radio-frequency-chip factory disruption, [[Nokia]] quickly secured inventory and redesigned around alternative suppliers, while Ericsson reacted more slowly.
- The creation of Sony Ericsson is treated as a partial recovery path that produced memorable phones, but not enough to keep Ericsson as an independent handset king after [[IPhone]] and [[Android]] changed the market.
