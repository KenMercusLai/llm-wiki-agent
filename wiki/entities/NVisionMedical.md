# nVision Medical

nVision Medical is the women's-health medical-device company founded by [[SurbhiSarna]] in [[tsr-s4-surbhisarna-v3-tsr-s4-surbhisarna-v3]]. The source says the company built a device for accessing the fallopian tube without surgery so clinicians could collect cells relevant to [[OvarianCancerDiagnostics]].

The device path is captured in [[FallopianTubeAccessDiagnostics]]. Sarna describes a self-navigating catheter with a linear everting balloon: it could unfold into the fallopian tube, use surface texture to collect cells, and retract into a sheath to reduce contamination while moving through anatomy only about one millimeter wide.

The company is a [[MedicalDeviceClinicalValidation]] and [[CapitalEfficientMedicalDeviceStartup]] case. The episode says early funding supported bench and animal-tissue testing, a working prototype, two clinical studies, FDA clearances in 2015 and 2016, and later a 50-patient study where the device found cancerous cells in all five cases with cancer present in the fallopian tube.

[[BostonScientific]] acquired the company after strategic interest increased. Sarna frames the sale through [[MissionDrivenAcquisitionDecision]]: she had earlier declined life-changing offers because she wanted patient data, then accepted after weighing commercialization risk, investor outcomes, acquirer trust, and the difficulty of funding a large rollout alone.
