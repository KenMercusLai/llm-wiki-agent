# 《七咒赋》 / The Seven Geases

《七咒赋》 / The Seven Geases is one of the main [[ClarkAshtonSmith|Clark Ashton Smith / C.A.史密斯]] stories summarized in [[73-xujing-qitan-kongbu-youmo-zuihaode-kesulu-675865061]]. The episode treats it as the strongest example of Smith's horror-comedy: a proud official named Ralibar Vooz disrupts a sorcerer's ritual, is cursed, and is repeatedly transferred from one supernatural recipient to another because none of them wants or needs him.

The story's comedy depends on cosmic demotion. [[Tsathoggua|萨托古雅]] is too full to accept the offering, Atlach-Nacha is busy weaving, snake people treat the human as already-studied material, and other older beings reject him for practical reasons. The human victim becomes less important each time he is passed along.

## Key Claims
- The story turns cosmic horror into [[HorrorHumorWeirdFiction]] by making supernatural beings act with deadpan practical constraints.
- Its central joke is also [[AntiAnthropocentricSatire]]: the human is not precious prey but inconvenient surplus.
- The repeated transfer structure is a form of [[AbsurdRationality]] because the impossible premise is handled with procedural logic.
- The episode contrasts the story's lack of conventional victory, romance, or heroic arc with [[WeirdTales|Weird Tales / 诡丽幻谭]]-style editorial expectations.
