# Slack

Slack is discussed in [[renlei-he-ai-agent-de-zuijia-peihe-fangshi-hai-mei-bei-faming-duitan-paperboy-ltgxurpseowqggfvgc32aurymt-o]] as the enterprise collaboration product [[Paperboy]] first considered replacing with an "AI Slack." [[JiangYang]] and [[JieDechen]] argue that a direct replacement is hard because Slack has enterprise data, external connections, integrations, network effects, and team-level switching costs. Paperboy therefore shifts toward a complementary layer that brings agents into places where people already work.

[[agi-lai-le-wo-yong-le-yizhou-toupi-fama-duitan-zhang-haoran-moxt-lianhe-chuangshiren-lkiysdddezlyzh8rt2grbbm4r-gq]] adds [[Moxt]]'s view of Slack as a still-useful but human-first IM layer. [[ZhangHaoran]] says the Moxt team still uses Slack, but that private chats and group channels are awkward when users want to bring in personal assistants such as [[Momo]] or many [[AICoworkers]] without copying and forwarding context.
