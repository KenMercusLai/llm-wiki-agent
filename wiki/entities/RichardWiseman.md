# Richard Wiseman

Richard Wiseman is the psychologist referenced in [[172-haoyun-shi-shenme-weisha-shuo-zong-bilei-hui-bai-haoyun-961254860]] through the episode's discussion of luck research and the newspaper photo-counting experiment. The source uses his work to distinguish luck as an attention and behavior pattern from luck as pure fate.

[[139-weishenme-weida-buneng-bei-jihua-youzhizhe-shijingsheng-ma-849965735]] reuses the newspaper experiment inside a broader critique of goal fixation. The episode's emphasis is that a person focused on executing the assigned task can miss the explicit answer in peripheral text, making Wiseman's example a bridge between [[LuckAsInformationBandwidth]] and [[ObjectiveDeception]].

The episode's version of Wiseman is practical: self-described lucky people are presented as more likely to notice unexpected information, while self-described unlucky people may narrow attention so much that they miss obvious prompts. The wiki should keep that claim source-scoped because the episode uses the research as a life-design frame, not as a full psychology review.
