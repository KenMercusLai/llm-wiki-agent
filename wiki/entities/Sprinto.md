# Sprinto

Sprinto is the compliance and trust SaaS company discussed in [[finding-product-market-fit-after-3-years-of-failed-ideas]]. [[GirishRedikar]] describes it as an autonomous trust platform that helps companies prove they have appropriate security, privacy, compliance, and trust safeguards for customers, partners, and regulators.

## Key Claims
- The episode reports more than 3,000 customers across 70 countries, eight figures in ARR, about 350 remote employees, and roughly $30 million to $32 million raised.
- Sprinto emerged from the compliance burden [[RecruiterBox]] experienced while moving upmarket and being asked for SOC 2 reports, ISO certificates, and security questionnaires.
- The founders validated Sprinto before writing code, but the main early risk was [[ServiceProductization]] rather than whether companies needed compliance help.
- Sprinto repeatedly paid auditors and ran real audits to learn the workflow before turning it into software.
- Early distribution relied on [[DemandHarvesting]] in founder groups, VC networks, perk programs, search, ads, and SEO.
- AI affects Sprinto through product autonomy, customer-side [[AIGovernanceAndCompliance]], and new AI-enabled external security threats.
- Sprinto's AI approach preserves [[DeterministicAuditData]] for audit-critical facts while using AI for adjacent interpretation, commitment extraction, and supervised remediation.
