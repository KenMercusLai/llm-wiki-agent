# Garrett Johnson

Garrett Johnson is the [[BostonUniversity]] marketing professor interviewed in [[tech-20251218-1218-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20251218-1218-mp-tech-pod-128]] about [[AISearchAdvertising]]. He argues that AI search and chatbot platforms cannot simply copy classic internet advertising because they still need to win users, attract advertisers away from mature platforms, and build ad infrastructure with enough scale and conversion feedback.

Johnson's main contribution to the wiki is to connect AI monetization to ad-market design. His frame links user trust, advertiser onboarding, shopping data, personalization flywheels, and [[GenerativeEngineOptimization]] into one commercial problem.

[[tech-20260120-0120-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260120-0120-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds Johnson to the [[SurveillancePricing]] branch. He says price discrimination can benefit some consumers and disadvantage others, and suggests that shoppers trying to compare prices may need to hide identity signals through incognito browsing, another device, or changed network conditions.
