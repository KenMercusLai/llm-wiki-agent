# Long March 10B / 长征十号乙

Long March 10B is the Chinese launch vehicle at the center of [[ruhe-douzhu-yike-huojian-s10e21-b66fdf0f-d428-4f0c-8412-b0c7581132d0]]. The episode says it flew as a two-stage, no-booster rocket, with a first stage related to [[LongMarch10A]] and a second stage using liquid-oxygen/methane technology.

The source's main claim is that Long March 10B demonstrates a different reusable-rocket route from [[SpaceX]]'s [[Falcon9]] and [[Starship]]. Instead of landing legs or tower catch, its first stage uses hooks and [[SeaNetRocketRecovery]] on the [[LinghangzheRecoveryShip]], shifting some structural and damping burden from rocket to recovery platform.

The episode treats the first successful catch as a beginning rather than a finished business model. To matter economically, the recovered first stage still needs [[ReusableRocketTurnaround]]: inspection, corrosion assessment, engine checks, control-system validation, consumable replacement, and enough repeated flight data to prove cadence and cost reduction.
