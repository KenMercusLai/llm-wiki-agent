# Julie Zerbo

Julie Zerbo is the fashion law expert quoted in [[seven-allegedly-fake-chanel-bags-vs-the-realreal]]. She explains [[Chanel]]'s case against [[TheRealReal]] through three main allegations: counterfeit sales, improper trademark use, and false advertising.

Her role in the source is to connect courtroom claims to the economics of a luxury [[ConsumerBrandMoat]]. A luxury company sells product quality, but it also sells the meaning and scarcity attached to the mark, so authentication errors can be framed as damage to the brand asset itself.

## Key Claims
- Zerbo presents Chanel's lawsuit as a broader test of who gets to verify luxury authenticity in resale markets.
- Her explanation makes [[TrademarkResaleBoundary]] legible: The RealReal needs Chanel's name to describe goods, while Chanel says the same use can confuse consumers.
- The episode uses her analysis to show why counterfeits affect more than one mistaken transaction.
