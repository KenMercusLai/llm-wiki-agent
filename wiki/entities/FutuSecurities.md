# Futu Securities

Futu Securities is one of the cross-border brokerage platforms discussed in [[ep89-haiwai-quanshang-da-dizhen-kuajing-touzi-xinshidai-li8ya-r5cpz3sifdjby73vh9-rxs]]. The episode uses it as a central example of how overseas brokers attracted mainland Chinese investors through polished app design, Chinese-language community features, IPO subscription access, and an investing experience that felt more familiar than many traditional overseas broker interfaces.

## Source Position
- Futu is described as a product and community success before being treated as a regulatory case.
- The episode says the platform's overseas status does not settle whether it can solicit or serve mainland investors without Chinese approval.
- The cleanup is framed as affecting future buying and account growth more than existing investor property.
- Futu connects the user-experience success of brokerage apps to [[CrossBorderBrokerageRegulation]], [[CapitalAccountInvestmentRestrictions]], and [[CrossBorderFundTransferRisk]].

## Connections
- [[TigerBrokers]] and [[Longbridge]] — other brokerage platforms discussed in the same cleanup.
- [[ChinaSecuritiesRegulatoryCommission]] and [[StateAdministrationOfForeignExchange]] — regulators tied to the securities and FX sides of the issue.
- [[HongKongStockConnect]] and [[QDIIAllocation]] — compliant alternatives discussed in contrast with gray brokerage routes.
- [[InvestmentRiskManagement]] — platform convenience does not remove market, currency, or route risk.
