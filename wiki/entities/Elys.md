# Elys

Elys is [[NaturalSelection]]'s AI-native social product discussed by [[Tristan]] in [[135-he-ziran-xuanze-chuangshiren-tristan-liao-elys-saibo-fenshen-linghun-context-de-huoqu-yu-liudong-he-ai-shejiao-wangluo-ltwegwvo7grn-v-rft0txlmqmcty]]. The product is described as being in testing, with invite limits and early users concentrated in technology, investment, Web3, and related circles.

The core product idea is that each user builds a [[CyberAvatars]] representation carrying high-dimensional context. Those avatars can pre-interact, comment, filter, and match inside an [[AISocialNetworks]] environment, then hand meaningful connection opportunities back to real people.

## Key Points
- Elys is described as more like a "field" than a single companion, in contrast with [[EVE]] as more like "a person."
- Its early success metrics include DAU, real-person connection rate, and the share of real human behavior inside the product.
- Its [[ContextFlywheel]] depends on users seeing enough connection value to keep feeding the system more thoughts, reactions, tasks, and confirmations.
- The product tries to reduce social friction by letting AI handle repetitive screening and pre-interaction while humans keep the final, meaningful connection step.
- The source is explicit that Elys still had product problems during testing, including homogeneous comments, long onboarding, weak new-user guidance, and insufficient user control over avatars.
