# Mayara Felix

Mayara Felix is the Brazilian economist in [[the-giant-factory-town-that-might-be-a-giant-mistake]] who explains why Brazil's old development hopes have become frustratingly deferred. She says Brazilians often call [[Brazil]] "the country of the future," but that phrase has become a joke because the promised future keeps failing to arrive.

Felix contrasts East Asian export-led development with Brazil's more protected domestic-market factory path. In the source, she argues that Brazil now needs to find more productive activities, not necessarily more manufacturing, and points to [[AdvancedAgricultureInnovation]], intense beauty-industry competition, and other local strengths as possible routes beyond [[PrematureDeindustrialization]].
