# 李翔 / Li Xiang

Li Xiang is the guest in [[vol-262-qu-xibanya-mai-zuqiu-julebu-yichang-huangdan-de-shangye-maoxian-992614191]], where he recounts buying into [[JumillaCF]] with [[TangHui]] around the 2016 Chinese football gold-money cycle. The episode frames him as both a football media figure from "西甲欢乐多" and an entrepreneur whose earlier successful exit made the club acquisition feel more achievable than it was.

His retrospective turns the source from a sports anecdote into a business failure case. Li says the project mixed passion, game-like owner fantasies, and a plausible but fragile [[ChinesePlayerOverseasArbitrage]] thesis, then ran into [[FootballClubControlRisk]], [[FootballContractEnforcementRisk]], weak player-development predictability, and a collapsing Chinese exit market.

## Key Claims
- Li treated the European club as a platform for Chinese player development, not merely as a trophy asset.
- He later judged the experience as a failure that changed how he evaluates business loops, MVPs, stop-loss lines, and resource fit.
- His story extends [[FounderEgo]] because football fandom, ownership desire, and confidence after a prior exit helped widen the commitment before the control and return mechanics were proven.

- [[StartupGovernance]], [[FastProductValidation]], and [[FounderCashFlowConstraint]] - existing startup concepts sharpened by the failure.
