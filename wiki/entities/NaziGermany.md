# Nazi Germany / 纳粹德国

Nazi Germany / 纳粹德国 is the historical regime context for [[103-ta-shi-cichuan-yewu-de-guang-ta-shi-hanna-alunte-737939789]]. The episode follows [[HannahArendt|Hannah Arendt]] as German anti-Semitism moves from public propaganda and institutional pressure into arrest, flight, exile, and eventually the industrial murder of [[TheHolocaust|the Holocaust]].

The source's main use of Nazi Germany is moral and political rather than encyclopedic. It asks why many educated people adapted to the regime, why bureaucratic legality could make murder feel like work, and why treating Nazis only as abnormal monsters can protect ordinary listeners from examining their own capacity for [[InstitutionalOvercompliance]] and [[BanalityOfEvil]].

[[133-ibm-yu-nacui-weishenme-putongren-bu-ying-zanmei-jizeizhuyi-815684110]] adds the technical and corporate-administrative branch. The episode argues that [[IBM]], [[Dehomag|德霍梅格]], and [[PunchedCardAdministration|punched-card administration]] helped Nazi Germany turn racial categories, census data, railway logistics, forced labor, and camp records into [[DataEnabledPersecution|data-enabled persecution]].
