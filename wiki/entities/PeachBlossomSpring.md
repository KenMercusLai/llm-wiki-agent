# 《桃花源记》 / Peach Blossom Spring

[[PeachBlossomSpring|《桃花源记》 / Peach Blossom Spring]] appears in [[180-ailisi-mengyou-xianjing-shijie-duo-huangdan-wo-yeshi-ziji-de-zhuzai-xia-975539611]] as a Chinese comparison for the rabbit hole and otherworld entrance in [[AliceInWonderland|《爱丽丝梦游仙境》]]. The episode groups caves, doors, tunnels, stations, and hidden paths as threshold devices that let a narrative move from ordinary space into another rule-world.

For the wiki, Peach Blossom Spring anchors the Chinese side of [[OtherworldPortalNarrative]]. It shows that Alice's rabbit hole belongs to a broader cross-cultural pattern: the story needs a concrete boundary so that the audience can feel the shift in time, space, and social order.

## Key Claims
- The source uses Peach Blossom Spring as a portal comparison, not as a direct influence claim.
- A cave or narrow passage can mark narrative transition as strongly as a magical object.
- The comparison keeps Alice's otherworld structure from being treated as only a Western device.
