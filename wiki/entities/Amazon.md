# Amazon

[[live-anthropic-co-founder-on-ai-and-jobs]] adds Amazon as [[DarylFairweather]]'s former corporate-economist setting and as a consumer-facing dynamic-pricing example. In the source, Fairweather says she worked on employee engagement for frontline call-center workers, while the hosts use Amazon price changes to illustrate [[DynamicPricingFairness]].

[[tsr-s5-blakescholl-v3-finalaudio-tsr-s5-blakescholl-v3-finalaudio]] adds Amazon as part of [[BlakeScholl]]'s founder formation before [[BoomSupersonic]]. Scholl says he worked at Amazon in 2001 as a software engineer on early scalable customer-acquisition systems, including buying Google ads before an ads API existed. In the source, Amazon is a training context rather than the product insight for Boom.

[[tech-20260320-0320-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260320-0320-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds Amazon's ultra-fast delivery push. The episode says Amazon announced one-hour delivery in hundreds of U.S. cities and three-hour delivery in more than 2,000 places for supercenter-like goods, with non-Prime customers paying about $20 for one-hour delivery. [[AnitaRamaswamy]] frames the move as direct competition with [[Walmart]], a continuation of consumer willingness to pay for convenience through [[UberEats|Uber Eats]] and [[DoorDash]], and a case where AI can help both fulfillment and recommendations.

[[tech-20260313-0313-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260313-0313-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds Amazon as the company case behind [[AICodingGuardrails]]. The episode says the [[FinancialTimes|Financial Times]] reported that Amazon engineers met about recent website outages, including incidents discussed in relation to AI use; Amazon told the show that only one discussed incident was AI-related and that none involved AI-written code. [[JewelBurkeSolomon]] uses the story to argue that AI coding and deployment workflows need review, controls, and uptime-oriented oversight.

[[socialradarspod-emmettshear-v2]] adds Amazon as the acquirer of [[Twitch]]. [[EmmettShear]] describes Amazon as a good acquirer for founders who want to keep running their company, because its decentralized model can let acquired businesses preserve brand, management, and operating independence. In this source, Amazon is therefore a [[PostAcquisitionFounderIdentity]] case as much as a media or cloud company.

Amazon appears in [[ep39-feng-man-lou-xiaji-quanqiu-shuaitui-manman-bijin-yanfang-sishou-bubu-weiying-manliao-xiabannian-meigu-meizhai-huilv-lgz718un4sjvgxqbydfikyxz0hcj]] as one of the large cloud buyers whose AI-infrastructure budget helps shape [[Nvidia]] demand. The episode groups Amazon with [[Microsoft]] and [[Google]] to make a customer-concentration and capex-cycle point rather than a detailed Amazon thesis.

[[ep77-si-shi-wan-nian-xin-fu-ye-zhuan-le-san-shi-si-yi-te-lang-pu-jiao-ni-ruhe-gao-qian-lnrx8vkdjedxbmmcwxlnwow-pcab]] adds Amazon as the buyer-side example in [[MelaniaTrump]]'s documentary deal. The source frames the deal as politically sensitive because Amazon's AWS and space-related businesses are described as having meaningful government-contract exposure, making the media payment part of the episode's [[PoliticalIdentityPremium]] and [[PoliticalInfluenceMonetization]] discussion.

[[vol-162-keji-kuaile-xingqiu-44-xin-moxing-sotamen-qihe-xinchun-1-6628-1]] adds Amazon as a frontier AI infrastructure partner. The hosts discuss Amazon's large [[Anthropic]] stake and Trainium commitment as a case where model companies and hyperscalers trade capital, chips, and future compute demand, extending the [[MaaSInfrastructure]] and [[AIInferenceCostStructure]] threads.

[[chule-shiyou-he-haixia-zhejie-yilang-zhanzheng-kaishi-suanji-nide-fuwuqi-le-keji-luandun]] adds Amazon through AWS-style cloud-region exposure in the Middle East. The episode treats cloud facilities as physical infrastructure that can be pulled into geopolitical conflict, making Amazon relevant to [[DigitalInfrastructureWarRisk]], [[DataCenterPhysicalResilience]], and [[WarAwareDisasterRecovery]]. The source frames specific incident claims as podcast discussion material rather than independently verified reporting.

[[stock-options-how-to-hedge-an-ai-bubble-698efe7e7301331f1f674bd5]] adds Amazon to the [[AIBubbleHedging]] discussion. The episode groups Amazon with [[Alphabet]], [[Meta]], and [[Microsoft]] in a planned $660bn AI-spending figure, making the question of future returns on AI capex central to [[AIEquityValuationRisk]].

[[dhaka-matters-an-election-for-bangladesh-698c5a3afeb59e13a3b8a94d]] adds Amazon as a concrete [[CandidateIdentityFraud]] case. The episode says Amazon blocked almost 2,000 applications from North Koreans applying for remote IT roles, making hiring verification part of the broader [[AIHiringArmsRace]] and enterprise-security perimeter.

[[144-dui-yang-meng-de-4-xiaoshi-fangtan-xiaofei-dianzi-si-yu-sheng-di-san-lei-gongsi-duan-ce-moxing-chanpin-fangfa-youxi-moshi-lnjleqjgjo1txupouvygmdv7oo8b]] adds Amazon as the early marketplace context for [[AnkerInnovations]]. [[YangMeng]] describes learning that e-commerce difficulty was not only selling, but finding and improving good products for overseas buyers, making Amazon the first channel where Anker's product-selection and quality strategy became visible.

[[1-ren-gongsi-kang-5-ge-ren-de-huo-hai-yao-guan-50-ge-agents-s10e18-e3a21dde-0bba-4ec2-bf12-5043500ae5c6]] adds an AWS startup-program angle through [[AmazonWebServices]] and [[FromIdeaToFrontier]]. The episode frames Amazon's cloud arm as a startup-support and infrastructure actor for AI-era [[OnePersonCompany]] founders rather than only as a hyperscaler or marketplace.

[[shaoyou-de-shendu-canyu-guo-zijie-meituan-zuzhi-jianshe-de-ren-duitan-ai-chuangyezhe-wei-xiaokang-lof0mb7u5buzec1qtxqrt9kraiud]] adds Amazon as the operating-plan comparison in [[BusinessModelOrganizationFit]]. [[WeiXiaokang]] uses Amazon to represent long-chain, lower-margin businesses where goals, budget, headcount, role level, and bar-raising processes are planned before hiring rather than relying mainly on talent-first team matching.

[[no-200-dianshang-sanguo-zhi-qunxiong-zhulu-yaogua-gongzhang-chijian-juqian-yiji-108-zhong-sifa-974601500]] adds Amazon's China ecommerce branch through [[JoyoAmazonChina]]. The episode says Amazon acquired Joyo but struggled to localize domestic ecommerce competition in China, making Amazon China a contrast with the company's global ecommerce and cloud strength.

[[tech-20251218-1218-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20251218-1218-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds Amazon as an incumbent advertising competitor for [[AISearchAdvertising]]. [[GarrettJohnson]] groups Amazon with [[Google]] and [[Meta]] as mature platforms that already have advertiser relationships, targeting systems, and conversion expectations, making the AI-search ad market harder for new entrants.

[[tech-20260220-0220-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260220-0220-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds Amazon through [[Ring]], its video doorbell and camera subsidiary. The episode uses [[RingSearchParty|Ring Search Party]] to show how an AI-enabled consumer safety feature can raise [[ConsumerCameraSurveillance]] concerns when connected-camera networks, law-enforcement relationships, and partnerships such as [[FlockSafety]] enter the story.

[[e244-duan-dao-duan-vs-shangxia-fenceng-jiqiren-lujing-zhizheng-zhengzai-zhuanxiang-fc9a3737-81a9-49cf-a7d6-530c77df836e]] adds Amazon as the scene-owner and vertical-application robotics case. [[HanZheng]] notes Amazon's long investment in warehouses, Kiva-style logistics, robotic arms, humanoid pilots, and partnerships such as Agility or Covariant-like attempts; the source frames Amazon's strength as deployment environment and business loop rather than necessarily the most general robot foundation model.

[[would-you-trust-an-economist-with-your-economy]] adds Amazon as a return-to-office example in [[NickBloom]]'s [[WorkFromHomeEvidence]] discussion. The source says Bloom suspects some mandates can function as headcount reduction without severance, while noting that Amazon denied this was its motivation.
