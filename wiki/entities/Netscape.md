# Netscape

Netscape appears in [[tsr-s4-ronconway-v4-tsr-s4-ronconway-v4]] as the company that made the early internet usable for ordinary people. [[RonConway]] says [[JimClark]] recognized that users needed a friendly browser layer, found [[MarkAndreessen]] and other University of Illinois engineers working on a browser, and brought them to Silicon Valley.

Conway learned about Netscape through [[MikeHomer]]. He wanted to invest, but the round had already been filled by Clark and [[JohnDoerr]]. The missed allocation mattered less than access to the [[NetscapePlatformEcosystem]]: Conway spent time in the company's hallways and treated it as a gravitational center for the next generation of internet startups.

The source also makes Netscape a [[BrowserAsInternetUnlock]] and [[OpenSourceStartupCostCollapse]] case. Conway compares Netscape's browser moment to [[ChatGPT]] making AI legible, and says open-source technologies and practices around Netscape sharply lowered the capital needed to start internet companies.
