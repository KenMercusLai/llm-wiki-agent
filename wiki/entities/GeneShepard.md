# Gene Shepard

Gene Shepard appears in [[how-to-make-a-book-into-a-bestseller]] as the 1950s radio personality who mocked bestseller-list herd behavior by asking listeners to request a fake book called [[ILibertine|I, Libertine]]. The transcript uses the name Gene Shepard; this page preserves that source spelling.

The prank matters because it makes [[BestsellerStatusFeedbackLoop]] concrete. A nonexistent book became a real object once bookstores received requests, the perceived demand spread, and a publisher decided to turn the joke into an actual title.

## Key Claims
- Shepard criticized bestseller culture as a form of herd mentality.
- His listeners requested a fake book by a fake author, Frederick R. Ewing.
- Requests reportedly spread beyond New York to other cities and countries.
- The episode treats the prank as evidence that perceived popularity can create real market activity.
