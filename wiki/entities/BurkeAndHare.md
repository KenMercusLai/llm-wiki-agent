# Burke and Hare / 伯克与黑尔

Burke and Hare appear in [[141-zhizao-fulankensitan-kehuan-zhimu-he-tade-shenqi-shidai-857923931]] as the source's most extreme example of anatomy demand turning into murder. The episode uses their case to explain why eighteenth- and nineteenth-century anatomy could feel terrifying to the public: medical schools needed bodies, legal supply was limited, and illicit supply chains grew around that gap.

Their role is not to prove that all anatomy was criminal. It is to make [[CadaverTradeAnatomyFear]] concrete and to show why the assembled body in [[Frankenstein|《弗兰肯斯坦》]] could resonate with existing fear of dissection, grave-robbing, and scientific appetite.
