# Hassan Benchikran

Hassan Benchikran is the pulmonary and critical/intensive care physician in San Diego featured in [[tech-20251222-1222-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20251222-1222-mp-tech-pod-128]]. The [[MarketplaceTech]] episode uses him to explain why patient use of AI health answers should be brought into the doctor-patient conversation rather than treated as something clinicians can block.

His role in the wiki is to ground [[PatientAIUse]] and [[DoctorGuidedAIInterpretation]] in a practicing doctor's workflow. He argues that patients will use AI whether doctors approve or not, so the safer response is to preserve trust, ask patients to bring AI outputs into appointments, and interpret those outputs with clinical context.

Benchikran also describes using AI as a physician through ambient scribe tools and research-model digests. In a personal family medical decision, he used AI to organize messy information, compare options, and structure risk-benefit thinking while keeping the final decision with himself.

## Key Claims
- Patient AI use is already happening and cannot be stopped by professional disapproval.
- Dismissing AI-generated health answers can push patients to hide them.
- The doctor can add patient context, clinical history, and professional responsibility that a general model does not have.
- AI can be useful for workflow support, research summaries, and decision organization without replacing medical judgment.
