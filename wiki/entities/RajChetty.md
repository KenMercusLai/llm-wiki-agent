# Raj Chetty

Raj Chetty is the Harvard economist presented in [[can-transforming-neighborhoods-help-kids-escape-poverty]] as the lead research voice behind the episode's HOPE VI evidence. The source connects his earlier neighborhood-effects work to [[HOPEVIProgram|HOPE VI]] as a way to test whether improving or integrating a place can help low-income children without moving everyone elsewhere.

His role in the episode is to separate [[NeighborhoodOpportunityRevitalization]] from simple building replacement. Chetty says children benefited most when revitalized public housing connected them to higher-opportunity surrounding communities, and he offers jobs, information, and aspirations as possible channels for [[CrossClassSocialCapital]].

## Key Claims
- Neighborhoods, blocks, schools, and local peer environments can shape children's adult outcomes.
- Moving children to better neighborhoods can work, but the episode treats it as hard to scale for everyone.
- HOPE VI becomes important because it tests a place-improvement strategy rather than only a relocation strategy.
- Chetty does not present HOPE VI as a program to revive unchanged because [[PublicHousingDisplacement]] remains a serious flaw.
