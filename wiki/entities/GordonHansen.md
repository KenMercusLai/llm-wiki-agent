# Gordon Hansen

Gordon Hansen appears in [[why-are-we-so-obsessed-with-manufacturing]] as the economist who most clearly separates manufacturing nostalgia from the broader good-jobs problem. He says manufacturing has historically paid workers more, especially workers without a college degree, but warns that politicians can take the love of manufacturing too far.

His key contribution to the wiki is the final reframing: the shortage is not simply a shortage of factories, but a shortage of [[GoodJobsForNonCollegeWorkers]]. That places [[ManufacturingPayPremium]] inside a broader labor-policy question shared with [[EmployerBargainedBenefits]] and [[PaidVacationAsLaborRight]].
