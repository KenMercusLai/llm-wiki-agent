# Gordon Hanson

Gordon Hanson appears in [[why-are-we-so-obsessed-with-manufacturing]] as the economist who most clearly separates manufacturing nostalgia from the broader good-jobs problem. He says manufacturing has historically paid workers more, especially workers without a college degree, but warns that politicians can take the love of manufacturing too far.

His key contribution to the wiki is the final reframing: the shortage is not simply a shortage of factories, but a shortage of [[GoodJobsForNonCollegeWorkers]]. That places [[ManufacturingPayPremium]] inside a broader labor-policy question shared with [[EmployerBargainedBenefits]] and [[PaidVacationAsLaborRight]].

[[why-economists-got-free-trade-with-china-so-wrong]] adds Hanson as a coauthor, with [[DavidAutor]] and [[DavidDorn]], of the regional labor-market research behind the [[ChinaShock]] branch. In that episode, the work shows how import competition can produce persistent [[RegionalLaborMarketScarring]] even when free trade produces aggregate gains.
