# 《豌豆公主》 / The Princess and the Pea

《豌豆公主》 / The Princess and the Pea is the central Andersen tale in [[187-wandou-gongzhu-gaomin-he-hundun-shi-ni-zhenming-999958166]], where [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] reads it as a tiny but unstable story about truth, class, gender, bodily sensitivity, and literary ambiguity. The plot appears simple: a prince seeks a true princess, a storm-soaked girl arrives, the queen hides a pea under many mattresses and quilts, and the girl's painful sleeplessness is treated as proof.

The episode's key move is to refuse a single verdict. The tale can mock aristocratic bloodline logic, because "true princess" becomes an absurd skin-sensitivity test. It can also invite sympathy, because the girl is tested without consent, physically hurt, and then made into a marriage prize. In the modern frame, the same figure becomes a contested name for [[HighSensitivityAsDifference|high sensitivity]] rather than only spoiled fragility.
