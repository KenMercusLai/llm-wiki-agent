# Waymo

Waymo is the autonomous-driving company discussed in [[132-dui-xinghaitu-chuangshiren-gaojiyang-de-3-xiaoshi-fangtan]] through [[GaoJiyang]]'s work experience. The source presents Waymo as both an engineering "paradise" and an example of how a strong technical organization can still suffer from large-company drift when rapid growth lacks enough founder-like force.

[[bie-zai-guonei-juan-le-qu-meiguo-kankan-zhiyao-chanpin-hao-jiu-you-ren-fufei-de-shichang-keji-luandun]] adds a consumer-market observation from [[Win]]'s U.S. trip. He says Waymo in San Francisco already operates without a safety operator, but is more expensive than Uber, while Tesla Robotaxi felt cheaper but remained safety-operator constrained in his experience.

## Key Points
- Gao worked on prediction and perception at Waymo and collaborated with [[ZhaoHang]] on VectorNet-style map and trajectory representation.
- He contrasts Waymo's robotics-style modular stack with [[Tesla]]'s more AI-native, data-driven autonomous-driving route.
- He says the Robotaxi business model itself was not wrong, but that the cycle is long and requires large-scale operational persistence.
- His later move to [[Momenta]] came from wanting to be closer to product, customer value, and company operation rather than continuing inside a mature engineering organization.
- The Win source treats Waymo as evidence that autonomous driving is moving from technical showcase toward city-level transport infrastructure, but still with pricing and operational tradeoffs.
