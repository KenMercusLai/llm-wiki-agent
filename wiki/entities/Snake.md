# Snake / 蛇

[[Snake|蛇]] is the central animal in [[128-xianliao-shier-shengxiao-zhi-she-cong-yemengjiade-dao-zhaolinger-797116305]], where [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] treats it as both a real creature and a cultural pressure point. The episode starts from ordinary fear, venom, pet snakes, snake safety, protected wildlife, and biomimetic motion, then moves into world myths, secret knowledge, treasure, rebirth, and snake-woman stories.

The source's snake differs from the wiki's existing zodiac [[ChineseDragon|龙]] and [[Horse|马]] cases. It is not primarily a national symbol or civilizational infrastructure animal. It is a figure of strangeness: no limbs, sudden movement, venom, shedding, winter return, ground contact, and ambiguous beauty make it easy for stories to imagine the snake as both threat and sacred power.

## Key Claims
- Snakes are culturally powerful because they are biologically close enough to be observed and alien enough to resist ordinary human control.
- Real snake behavior and protection status matter; the episode's admiration for snakes does not erase safety boundaries.
- Snake symbolism is unusually polarized: fear, fertility, wisdom, wealth, medicine, seduction, renewal, and divine force can all attach to the same form.
