# Prevagen

Prevagen appears in [[why-is-there-a-supplement-craze-if-they-dont-even-work]] as the episode's main case study in supplement regulation. The source describes it as a memory supplement associated with a synthetic lab-made version of a jellyfish-linked protein, tied in the story to [[MarkUnderwood]] and his mother's multiple sclerosis question.

The case matters because it combines several layers of the wiki's new supplement branch. The episode says the [[FoodAndDrugAdministration|FDA]] questioned safety, the company used [[NeuroShake]] and [[GRASSelfCertification]] to support use of the ingredient, people reported serious side effects while taking the supplement, and the [[FederalTradeCommission|FTC]] eventually won a false-advertising case after years of sales.
