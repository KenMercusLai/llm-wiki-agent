# Ovid / 奥维德

Ovid / 奥维德 enters the wiki through [[130-tuokaerqiuke-guaidan-gushiji-xunzhao-he-gaobie-803543569]], where 〈变形中心〉 invokes his [[Metamorphoses|《变形记》]] as an argument about transformation. In the episode's summary of 崔教授, metamorphosis is not grounded only in difference between forms; it is grounded in likeness and continuity across life.

The source uses Ovid less as classical background than as a conceptual lever for [[TransformationAsExit]]. If humans remain connected to other beings, then a person choosing to leave human form can be framed not only as disappearance, but as an insistence on another continuity.

## Key Claims
- Ovidian metamorphosis gives 〈变形中心〉 a literary vocabulary for chosen bodily change.
- The source's emphasis is similarity across forms, not only spectacular difference.
- The Ovid reference connects [[BizarreStories|《怪诞故事集》]] to older mythic transformation while keeping the story's modern questions about medicine, autonomy, and family grief active.
