# Leo Mirani

Leo Mirani appears in [[strait-and-narrowing-the-iran-deal-crumbles-6a57608bf821a8210984481f]] as the [[TheIntelligence]] participant explaining why [[India]]'s government websites can be poor even though the country has a strong private technology sector. His segment starts from India's online visa website and expands into [[GovernmentWebsiteUsability]], paper-process imitation, procurement limits, and bureaucratic incentives.

The source uses Mirani to argue that the issue is not only visual design. Government systems often need empowered technical leadership, clear product ownership, and officials who can define and judge the thing being built. His counterexamples, [[Aadhaar]] and [[UPI]], show that public digital systems can work when technical competence and decision authority align.
