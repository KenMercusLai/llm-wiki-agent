# Rio Negro

The Rio Negro is the Amazon river setting for [[the-little-pet-fish-that-saved-a-town-in-the-amazon]]. The episode emphasizes its dark, acidic water as the ecological niche that made wild [[CardinalTetra]] supply possible around [[Barcelos]].

The river matters economically because its water chemistry helped create the original local advantage. Once breeders learned to reproduce the conditions needed to farm Cardinal Tetras elsewhere, that river-linked advantage became less defensible, turning the case into [[LocalProductAdvantageErosion]] rather than only a supply-chain story.

## Key Claims
- The episode says the Rio Negro's dark water comes from tannins released by jungle leaves and bark.
- Fish in the area are adapted to unusual acidic water conditions.
- The river and flooded forest form the production setting for [[PiaberoEconomy|piabero]] work.
- The same ecological specificity that created local advantage also explains why farming Cardinal Tetras was technically difficult at first.
