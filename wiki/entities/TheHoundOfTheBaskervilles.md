# The Hound of the Baskervilles / 《巴斯克维尔的猎犬》

The Hound of the Baskervilles / 《巴斯克维尔的猎犬》 is the [[ArthurConanDoyle|Arthur Conan Doyle / 柯南道尔]] Sherlock Holmes story used in [[69-xianliao-tuili-wenxue-lishixuezhe-ke-bu-jiu-shi-zhentan-ma-666244465]] to explain the boundary between gothic atmosphere and rational detection. The episode notes that the story can use hellhound imagery, family legend, moor fear, and supernatural suggestion, but the solution still returns to human action and evidence.

In the wiki, this entity connects the Doyle/Holmes branch to [[HistoricalDetectiveReasoning]] because the episode's larger contrast is not "no atmosphere" versus "atmosphere"; it is whether explanation remains accountable to traces after atmosphere has done its literary work.
