# Exploding Kittens

Exploding Kittens is the game company working with [[PlanetMoney]] in [[board-games-3-whats-in-a-name]] to turn an economics-inspired prototype into a mass-market card game. The source presents the company as the practical retail partner that moves the project from mechanics toward [[GameMechanicToRetailProduct]], including name, theme, cover art, international naming, and big-box shelf appeal.

The company's process in the episode separates working gameplay from sellable packaging. [[ElanLee]] tells the hosts that a great game can still fail if the box does not catch attention, while [[StephaniePesta]] brings theme options and [[YuriHosta]] later surfaces the international issue around the word Sasquatch.

## Key Claims
- Exploding Kittens treats game mechanics and retail packaging as linked but separate stages of product development.
- The company steers the hosts away from a too-literal used-car theme because it could create false expectations about game category.
- Its creative process relies on internal taste, repeated exposure, and what "sticks" more than formal focus-group data.
- The source makes the company a bridge between [[AsymmetricInformation]] as an economics concept and [[RetailShelfAppeal]] as a mass-market product problem.
