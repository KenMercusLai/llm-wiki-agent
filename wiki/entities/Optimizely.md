# Optimizely

Optimizely is the [[YCombinator]] Winter 2010 company [[DanSiroker]] and [[PeteKoomen]] built after [[CarrotSticks]] and [[Spreadly]]. In [[tsr-s3-dansiroker-v3-tsr-s3-dansiroker-v3]], Dan frames it as the product he wished he had during the 2008 [[BarackObama]] campaign: a tool that made [[ABTestingForMarketers]] accessible without engineers building every experiment.

The source emphasizes focus and early proof. [[PaulGraham]] quickly pushed the founders toward the A/B testing idea, Optimizely had a paying design partner before code existed, and the company tested possible names through AdWords before settling on Optimizely. Dan also describes an early nonprofit use case where testing helped a Haiti relief effort raise more than $1 million in incremental donations.

The later Optimizely story is a leadership caution. Dan says the company won its competitive market but that he began falling out of love with it after 2013, made bad executive hires, and confused delegation with abdication. The episode therefore connects Optimizely not only to [[DataDrivenProductCulture]], but also to [[FounderInstinct]] and [[FounderDelegationDiscipline]].

## Key Claims
- Optimizely productized campaign-style A/B testing for nontechnical marketers.
- The company emerged from a YC pivot after two weaker startup attempts.
- Early customer and nonprofit evidence gave the team stronger proof than a speculative idea alone.
- Dan treats investor selection for Optimizely as another testable process, using mock board meetings to compare investor behavior.
- The company became a regret case for founder attention at scale, especially around executives and product distance.
