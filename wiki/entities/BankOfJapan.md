# Bank of Japan

The Bank of Japan appears in [[ep38-feng-man-lou-quanqiu-ziben-shichang-jufu-dongdang-xingfeng-xueyu-shike-jin-zai-zhichi-lszskb0h-qqxsct4yaebegd-1ph9]] as the policy trigger that tightened yen funding conditions and helped unsettle Japanese equities and global risk assets. The episode emphasizes that even a modest rate move can matter when many trades rely on cheap yen funding and exchange-rate stability.

## Source Position
- The speakers treat the timing of Japan's rate increase as surprising and important because U.S. rate-cut expectations were already strengthening.
- The Bank of Japan's move is linked to yen appreciation, pressure on exporters' earnings expectations, and equity outflows from Japan.
- The episode frames the policy change as one trigger inside a larger [[CarryTradeUnwind]] rather than the sole cause of the global selloff.
