# Cooper Katz-McKim

Cooper Katz-McKim appears in [[indicators-of-2025-and-what-to-watch-in-2026]] as the participant choosing consumer spending as a 2026 indicator. His segment contrasts weak [[ConsumerSentimentIndicator|consumer sentiment]] with resilient hard spending data, then asks whether aggregate spending is being carried by higher-income households.

The source uses [[RBC]]'s claim that the top 10% of consumers account for a near majority of spending. That makes Katz-McKim's role a bridge between [[KShapedConsumerSpending]], stock-market wealth exposure, and household stress below the top income slice.
