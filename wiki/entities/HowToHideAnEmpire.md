# How to Hide an Empire

How to Hide an Empire is the book by [[DanielImmerwahr]] referenced in [[how-to-get-what-greenland-has-with-permission]]. In the episode, the book functions less as a full book discussion than as the historical frame for seeing U.S. power beyond the map of formal states.

The source uses Immerwahr's work to explain why strategic expansion does not always look like annexation. Land purchases, inhabited territories, overseas possessions, and military bases can all shift control, jurisdiction, and security access, making [[MilitaryBaseSovereignty]] a key alternative to formal territorial acquisition.

For the Greenland episode, the book's frame supports [[StrategicAccessWithoutAnnexation]]. The [[UnitedStates]] may want minerals, warning systems, and Arctic access, but the source argues those needs can be pursued through agreements with [[Greenland]] and [[Denmark]] rather than by owning the island.
