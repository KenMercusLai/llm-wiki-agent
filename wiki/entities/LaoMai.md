# 老麦

He provides the more trading-structure-oriented explanation of the selloff, emphasizing Japanese equity volatility, overseas capital flows, index futures and options, short covering, the [[YenCarryTrade]], and the difficulty of interpreting a market shock from outside the local market.

- He argues that [[DerivativeAmplifiedVolatility]] helped turn a policy and currency shock into a sharper index move.
- He frames [[WarrenBuffett]]'s cash and [[Apple]] reduction as meaningful risk context without treating them as a precise market-timing signal.
- He cautions against overreading one month of economic data when evaluating [[FederalReserve]] policy.
