# Terry Karl

Terry Karl appears in [[chevron-venezuela-and-the-paradox-of-plenty]] as the political economist used to explain [[Venezuela]] as a [[Petrostate]]. The episode says Karl is fairly sure she coined the term petrostate, and it uses her analysis to connect the country's oil boom to [[DutchDisease]], corruption, waste, and institutional weakness.

Karl's most important contribution in the source is the [[PoliticalResourceCurse]] framing. She rejects the idea that oil itself has moral agency: the resource becomes a blessing or curse depending on how people in political offices and corporate leadership manage it. That frame links [[PDVSA]], [[OilRevenueDependence]], and [[OilNationalization]] to governance choices rather than to geology alone.

The episode also uses Karl's relationship with [[JuanPabloPerezAlfonso]] as a historical bridge. Perez Alfonso told her to study what oil was doing to Venezuela, and the source uses his warning to turn the oil story from a discovery narrative into a political-accountability problem.
