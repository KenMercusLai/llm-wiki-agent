# Jacqueline Susann

Jacqueline Susann appears in [[how-to-make-a-book-into-a-bestseller]] as the author of [[ValleyOfTheDolls|Valley of the Dolls]] and as a pioneer of the modern book tour. The source presents her as an early example of strategic bestseller-list optimization.

Her campaign matters because it connects author promotion to [[BestsellerListOpacity]]. According to the episode, Susann cultivated bookseller relationships, tried to identify stores that reported to the [[NewYorkTimesBestsellerList|New York Times bestseller list]], and directed purchases through those channels to create a strong first-week showing.

## Key Claims
- Susann treated booksellers as a critical part of modern book promotion.
- The episode says she tried to target stores believed to report to the New York Times.
- Valley of the Dolls reached number one on the Times list and later became a hit movie.
- Her case shows that sales location and timing can matter alongside total sales volume.
