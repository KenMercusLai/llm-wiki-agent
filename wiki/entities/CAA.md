# CAA

CAA appears in [[e243-te-lang-pu-huanxing-hongpai-zhiwai-meiguo-ziben-ruhe-yingkong-quanqiu-zutan]] as an American-background agency connected to [[UEFA]] national-team competition commercial development in the 2012-2013 period. The episode uses CAA and CAA11 to show that U.S. influence in European football reaches beyond club ownership into rights packaging, sponsorship, and distribution.

In the source, CAA is part of the monetization layer that sits between governing bodies, competitions, broadcasters, sponsors, and global audiences. That makes it a bridge from [[SportsMediaRights]] to [[AmericanSportsCapitalInEuropeanFootball]].

## Key Claims
- CAA is used as an example of American commercial expertise entering European football rights.
- The agency layer can shape how competitions are sold without owning a club or governing body.
- The source treats rights intermediation as one of the less visible forms of U.S. control.
