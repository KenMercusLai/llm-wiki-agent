# Jean-Paul Sartre / 萨特

Jean-Paul Sartre / 萨特 enters the wiki through [[135-xianliao-weida-zuojiamen-de-bagua-di-san-dan-823746670]] as an important philosopher whose public authority is sharply qualified by the episode. The source says the host dislikes him personally while acknowledging his stature, then contrasts his French intellectual-hero image with wartime passivity, collaboration-era career progress, left politics, private relationships, drug use, poor health, and late-life reconsideration of despair and atheism.

The episode links Sartre to [[Existentialism|Existentialism / 存在主义]] through [[BeingAndNothingness|Being and Nothingness / 《存在与虚无》]] and [[NoExit|No Exit / 《禁闭》]], both discussed as works that succeeded during occupation-era Paris. This makes Sartre part of [[AuthorMythDeflation]] and [[ArtisticAchievementMoralAccountability]]: philosophical influence and postwar meaning-making do not settle questions about personal conduct or political judgment.

Sartre also extends [[FreeLovePracticeGap]] through his long open relationship with [[SimoneDeBeauvoir|Simone de Beauvoir / 西蒙娜·德·波伏娃]] and the episode's later account of his secret support for Arlette Elkaim. Politically, the source criticizes his alliance with Communism, sympathy for revolutionary figures such as [[FidelCastro|Fidel Castro]], and silence or pressure around testimony about abuses in the [[SovietUnion]].
