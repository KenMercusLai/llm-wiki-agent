# Ryo

Ryo is one of the [[NeiheKonghuang]] hosts in [[biancheng-de-neiranji-shidai-neihe-konghuang-71-1-71-1]] and [[zhongwen-boke-huohuashi-yu-zhen-og-neihe-konghuang-72-1-72-1]]. He pushes the discussion toward practical AI translation, small automation, tool discovery, live radio, display setup, and the possibility that routine programming and UI work become less scarce.

## Source Position
- Ryo introduces [[ImmersiveTranslate]] as a practical example of [[AITranslation]] across webpages, subtitles, image text, and manga.
- His NAS file-deduplication example shows how AI can propose an efficient staged algorithm, but the user still validates behavior against real data.
- His Linux I/O tool-discovery example supports the episode's view that AI is strong at mapping a problem to candidate tools and commands.
- His comments about tax-filing apps and routine front-end work sharpen [[TaskAsAService]] and [[HeadlessSoftware]]: if AI or the system can complete the job directly, users may need fewer explicit app flows.
- In episode 72, his preference for live radio clarifies the tradeoff inside [[PodcastAsAsynchronousMedia]]: podcasts are convenient but less simultaneous.
- His desire for more visible windows rather than more macOS Spaces anchors [[DisplayErgonomics]] and the "screen area as working ground" theme.
