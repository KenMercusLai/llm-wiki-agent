# Frank Doel / 弗兰克·多尔

Frank Doel / 弗兰克·多尔 is the [[MarksAndCo|Marks & Co.]] bookseller who becomes [[HeleneHanff|海莲·汉芙]]'s main correspondent in [[EightyFourCharingCrossRoad|《查令十字街84号》 / 84 Charing Cross Road]]. In [[134-chaling-shizi-jie-84-hao-zaici-yuanliang-shijie-de-liyou-821075226]], he is presented as formal, precise, courteous, and serious about old-book searching.

Frank's role begins with professional competence: he answers requests, sends books and invoices, keeps small account balances, and remembers obscure volumes. The episode's reading turns those habits into moral texture. His commercial reliability lets trust form before sentiment appears, and that makes the later friendship feel earned rather than vague.

The source says Helene learned in a January 8, 1969 letter from the shop that Frank had died after complications from an appendicitis operation. That missed meeting becomes the story's deepest aftertaste: the correspondence had already become real friendship, but the long-promised visit never arrived in time.
