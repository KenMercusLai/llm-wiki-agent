# 康熙 / Kangxi Emperor

康熙 / Kangxi Emperor is the emperor whose relationship with [[CaoYin|曹寅]] anchors [[101-caoyin-yu-kangxi-hongloumeng-xing-zhong-you-shi-735044876]]. The episode argues that their tie was not simply private affection for a wet-nurse family. It was a political, fiscal, cultural, and intelligence relationship between an emperor and a trusted imperial bondservant.

In the source, Kangxi uses [[ImperialHouseholdDepartment|内务府]] and [[ImperialBondservantGovernance|包衣]] agents to replace older eunuch-centered service channels, manage royal supply, observe Jiangnan, and bypass ordinary bureaucracy. [[JiangningWeavingBureau|江宁织造]], salt-inspection work, [[SouthernTourPoliticalTheater|南巡接驾]], and [[SecretMemorialPoliticalIntelligence|密折]] all show how Cao Yin became useful as an imperial proxy.

The episode also keeps the risk visible. Kangxi could tolerate Cao Yin's [[ImperialFiscalElasticity|fiscal elasticity]] while the agent remained useful, but the same account gaps and deficits later exposed the [[CaoFamilyQing|清代曹家]] when successors lacked Cao Yin's competence and Yongzheng-era politics turned harsher.
