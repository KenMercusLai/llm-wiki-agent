# Aramara Castro

Aramara Castro, called Mara in [[the-little-pet-fish-that-saved-a-town-in-the-amazon]], is the [[Barcelos]] fish broker who describes herself as a proud piabera warrior. She and her husband sort fish from local piaberos and send them downriver to exporters, making her a key voice for the business infrastructure of the [[PiaberoEconomy]].

Mara also gives the source its strongest caution about the town's transition. She remembers the 1990s and early 2000s as better years for ornamental fishing, says the number of active workers has collapsed, and prefers the independence of fish work to service labor for tourists. Her view keeps [[HeritageTourismPivot]] from reading as a simple success story.

## Key Claims
- Mara represents the broker layer between wild fishers and global exporters.
- She says the best years for ornamental fishing were the 1990s and early 2000s.
- She reports that active piaberos and piaberas have fallen from hundreds to about 30.
- She wants more investment in piaberos even as [[Barcelos]] pivots toward tourism.
