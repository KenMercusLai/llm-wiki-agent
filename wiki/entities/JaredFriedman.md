# Jared Friedman

Jared Friedman is the [[Scribd]] co-founder and later [[YCombinator]] partner interviewed in [[tsr-s5-jared-v3final-tsr-s5-jared-v3final]]. The episode follows him from a Harvard CS path and early [[PaulGraham]] readership into YC's Summer 2006 batch, the failed [[Wholist]] marketplace, Scribd's launch and growth, and then a second career building software inside YC.

The source presents Jared as a technical founder whose fit is strongest around creating early systems rather than operating a large engineering organization. He says he left Scribd in 2015 after roughly ten years because the scaled CTO or engineering-manager role did not match the work he most liked. At YC, that builder orientation reappears as [[YCInternalSoftware]]: application review tools, red-flag tracking, repeat-applicant history, databases, prompts, agents, and AI additions to internal workflows.

Jared's broader wiki role is to connect early YC history to the AI era. He argues that current AI startups feel like 2005 and 2006 because a new technology is again drawing deeply technical founders into open-ended experimentation.
