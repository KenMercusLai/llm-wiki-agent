# 《嫉妒与鄙视》

《嫉妒与鄙视》 is the [[SusanFiske|Susan Fiske / 苏珊·菲斯克]] book used in [[145-jidu-yu-bishi-wo-gai-ruhe-duobi-zhe-shuangxiang-de-dujian-882378647]] to discuss envy, contempt, status hierarchy, stereotype, and empathy. The episode treats the book as a social-psychology guide to feelings people often hide because the feelings are morally embarrassing.

The book's wiki role is to connect emotional self-knowledge to group relations. In the episode's reading, envy looks upward, contempt looks downward, and both become dangerous when comparison erases the other person's concrete humanity.

## Key Claims
- The book gives the episode its paired structure: envy and contempt share comparison as a root but produce different status postures.
- It helps move envy and contempt from private shame into [[SocialPsychology|social-psychology]] mechanisms such as status threat, stereotype, and group identity.
- It supports the episode's claim that ugly emotions can have adaptive signal value while still requiring ethical and relational control.
- It grounds the source's practical emphasis on concrete information, empathy, reframed comparison, praise, cooperation, and team design.
