# 《木偶奇遇记》 / The Adventures of Pinocchio

《木偶奇遇记》 / The Adventures of Pinocchio is [[CarloCollodi|Carlo Collodi]]'s classic tale discussed in [[122-pinuocao-chengzhang-de-cantong-jiqi-suo-chuangzuo-de-777464172]]. The episode treats it as a strange, dangerous, and dreamlike childhood classic rather than a simple "do not lie" lesson.

In the source's reading, the book's center is [[Pinocchio]]'s painful movement from animated wood to human child. He sells his schoolbook, is deceived by the fox and cat, is hanged, revived, jailed, made to guard a chicken coop, loses [[BlueFairy|the Blue Fairy]], becomes a donkey, enters the shark's belly, finds [[Geppetto]], and finally works to support father and fairy. The transformation is therefore an arc of fear, error, shame, care, and responsibility.

## Key Claims
- The famous long-nose scene is a small part of the work, not its governing structure.
- The original is harsher than its common adaptation memory: hunger, bodily damage, death tableaux, prison, animal transformation, and child punishment are central.
- The book belongs to [[AdultFairyTaleReading]] because its moral force is inseparable from danger, dream logic, and adult rereading.
- Pinocchio's final humanity depends on self-knowledge and responsibility, not only school performance or obedience.
- The work becomes a key case for [[NonhumanBecomingHumanMotif]] because the puppet is both nonhuman matter and already a person before his formal transformation.
