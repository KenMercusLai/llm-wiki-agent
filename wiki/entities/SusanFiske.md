# Susan Fiske / 苏珊·菲斯克

Susan Fiske / 苏珊·菲斯克 is the social psychologist whose [[JiDuYuBiShi|《嫉妒与鄙视》]] provides the main framework for [[145-jidu-yu-bishi-wo-gai-ruhe-duobi-zhe-shuangxiang-de-dujian-882378647]]. The episode uses her work to explain envy and contempt through status comparison, stereotypes, group identity, emotion, and practical empathy rather than through simple moral condemnation.

The source presents Fiske as useful because she links everyday ugly emotions to social structure. Envy, contempt, disgust, status vigilance, and group hostility are not treated as mysterious personal failures; they are legible responses to comparison systems that can still be redirected.

## Key Claims
- Fiske's frame helps separate emotional recognition from emotional permission: understanding envy or contempt does not mean indulging attack.
- The episode uses her work to connect individual feeling with [[SocialPsychology]], group hierarchy, status anxiety, and public prejudice.
- Her warmth/competence dimensions help explain why adding concrete information about another person can weaken a stereotype.
- The repair path in the episode turns from diagnosis toward [[EmpathyAgainstComparison]], cooperation, and shared-unit design.
