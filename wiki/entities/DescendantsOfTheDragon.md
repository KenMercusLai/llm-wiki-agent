# 《龙的传人》 / Descendants of the Dragon

《龙的传人》 / Descendants of the Dragon is the popular-song and phrase node in [[127-xianliao-shier-shengxiao-zhi-long-shenqi-dongwu-zai-nali-788239655]]. The episode presents Hou Dejian's post-1978 song as a major vehicle for spreading the line that an ancient Eastern dragon is China and that Chinese people are its descendants.

For the wiki, the song is not treated as the origin of all dragon symbolism. Its role is later and catalytic: after scholarship around [[WenYiduo]] and dragon totem theory made a national-origin reading available, the song gave that reading a memorable emotional form. It therefore closes the episode's [[DragonNationalSymbolInvention]] sequence by turning a contested historical construction into ordinary self-description.

## Key Claims
- The song popularized dragon-descendant language after earlier totem and national-symbol arguments had been developed.
- It helped fix "dragon = China" as a felt identity formula rather than only a scholarly or political claim.
- The episode uses the song to show how modern mass culture can make a recent symbolic synthesis feel ancient.
