# Alfred 林童雨

Alfred 林童雨 is one of the three student/early-career guests in [[e236-99-de-zuoye-doushi-ai-xie-de-dangdai-mingxiao-sheng-yanli-daxue-hai-shengxia-shenme-b066c769-00d2-4341-bf61-a82bca4181e4]]. The episode presents him as a [[TsinghuaUniversity|清华大学]] law-background student who moved toward AI, self-studied CS and agent engineering, served in a student entrepreneurship association, and worked on agent products at [[Manus]].

His source role is to show why AI adoption can be driven by career risk as much as curiosity. After seeing [[ChatGPT]] handle legal analysis and writing tasks, he worried that junior legal and paralegal work would be pressured, then used AI as a learning route into computer science, coding, and AI projects. He estimates that a large share of his AI/CS learning came from dialogue with AI and project review rather than formal coursework alone.

Alfred also supplies the strongest university-value argument in the source. He says [[TsinghuaUniversity|清华大学]] gave him high-quality peers, teacher office hours, and trust-building opportunities, while AI makes old skill-based evaluation less sufficient. His preferred assessment frame is not whether a student used AI, but whether the student can use AI to solve real problems while preserving judgment, responsibility, and values.
