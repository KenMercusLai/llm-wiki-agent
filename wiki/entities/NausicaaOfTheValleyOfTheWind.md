# 《风之谷》 / Nausicaa of the Valley of the Wind

[[NausicaaOfTheValleyOfTheWind|《风之谷》 / Nausicaa of the Valley of the Wind]] is the prehistory hinge for [[StudioGhibli|Studio Ghibli]] in [[94-jibuli-de-tiancaimen-wo-de-rensheng-jiaogei-reai-he-youyi-722350232]]. The episode says the project was blocked because it lacked an original source, so [[HayaoMiyazaki|宫崎骏]] began drawing the manga; [[ToshioSuzuki|铃木敏夫]] then helped turn that publishing route into a film path.

The production exposes the future Ghibli pattern early: insufficient budget knowledge, contractor risk, [[IsaoTakahata|高畑勋]] reluctantly recruited as producer through friendship, intense labor, and a final success that made both artistic reputation and creator-rights economics matter.

## Key Claims
- The manga workaround shows how publishing could make an original animation project fundable.
- Suzuki's choice of Miyazaki's most detailed drawing style helped define the work's ambition and burden.
- Takahata's production role made the film possible despite his initial refusal.
- The film's success and contract design helped establish expectations that creators should share in downstream value.
- The project is treated as Ghibli's practical origin, even before the studio name is formalized.
