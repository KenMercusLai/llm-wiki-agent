# Barcelos

Barcelos is the remote [[Brazil]]ian town on the [[RioNegro]] at the center of [[the-little-pet-fish-that-saved-a-town-in-the-amazon]]. The episode presents it as a town whose economy and public identity were built around wild ornamental fish, especially the [[CardinalTetra]], before farmed competitors sharply reduced demand for fish caught by local piaberos.

The town is also a transition case. It still celebrates the ornamental-fish industry through a large festival and visible civic branding, but many workers have moved toward [[HeritageTourismPivot|tourism work]] around [[PeacockBass]] sport fishing. That makes Barcelos a useful contrast to [[Manaus]]: both are Amazon cases in [[Brazil]], but Barcelos shows a smaller local economy losing control of a copied biological advantage rather than a subsidized factory hub trying to escape the [[MiddleIncomeTrap]].

## Key Claims
- The episode says Barcelos has no roads leading to it and sits between the Rio Negro and the rainforest.
- The town was known in Brazil as the capital of ornamental fish.
- At one point, about 80% of its economy depended on the aquarium-fish trade.
- The annual ornamental-fish festival keeps the [[CardinalTetra]] visible as heritage, tourism branding, and civic memory.
- The town's current pivot toward sport-fishing tourism may bring money, but it also changes worker independence and local identity.
