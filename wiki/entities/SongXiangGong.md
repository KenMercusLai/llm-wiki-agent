# 宋襄公

宋襄公 appears in [[109-xianliao-zuozhuan-zhi-chunqiu-yundonghui-747508773]] as the episode's shorthand for Spring-Autumn rule-conscious warfare. The source notes that later readers often laugh at him for fighting with excessive ritual fairness, but the host reframes that laughter as evidence that later expectations had shifted toward harsher strategic calculation.

The page's role is source-scoped. Song Xiang Gong is not treated here as a full biography; he is a contrast figure inside [[SpringAutumnWarfareRitual]], where the question is why some battle rules could once feel binding, and why they became unstable once opponents learned to exploit them.
