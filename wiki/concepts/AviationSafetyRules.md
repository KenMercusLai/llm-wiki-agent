# Aviation Safety Rules

Aviation safety rules are the passenger-facing flight requirements that can look fussy in normal conditions but reduce risk during security screening, fire events, turbulence, impact, decompression, and emergency evacuation. In [[ep11-kongshao-jiemi-feiji-shang-de-shenmi-guiding-he-qiwen-qushi-lua5j8erhk1smzf5gwmj2jni9-sh]], the guest and host explain liquid limits, smoking bans, tray-table storage, seatback position, and seat belts as accumulated safety practice rather than arbitrary crew control.

## Key Claims
- Cabin crew distinguish airline service from airport/security enforcement: liquid limits are handled before boarding rather than by crew after passengers pass security.
- Smoking and e-cigarette bans are treated as fire-risk controls, especially in lavatories where detectors and confined smoke make evasion dangerous.
- Tray tables and reclined seatbacks can obstruct evacuation or create injury risk when passengers must move quickly.
- Seat belts primarily keep passengers fixed to the seat during turbulence, collision, sudden movement, or decompression.
- Rules feel most unnecessary when flights are normal, but they are designed for rare moments when seconds and small obstructions matter.
