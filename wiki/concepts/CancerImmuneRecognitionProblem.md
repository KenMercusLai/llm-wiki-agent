# Cancer Immune Recognition Problem

Cancer immune recognition problem is [[LiuCheng|刘诚]]'s treatment-philosophy frame in [[e235-20-nian-nei-car-t-zhiyu-aizheng-yu-liucheng-boshi-liaoliao-aizheng-zhiliao-de-diceng-zhexue-90f96f60-25be-45ac-b832-56776a23d534]]. Cancer cells come from the body's own cells, so the immune system does not automatically treat them like foreign viruses or bacteria. A therapy must therefore distinguish cancer cells from normal cells strongly enough to attack the cancer without creating unacceptable self-damage.

The episode uses this frame to compare treatment types. Chemotherapy exploits fast cell division but harms other fast-dividing cells; targeted drugs and ADCs try to find molecular differences; surgery and radiotherapy remove or damage tumor concentrations locally; [[CARTCellTherapy|CAR-T]] tries to teach immune cells who to attack. This also connects to [[CancerVaccinePlatform]], where a vaccine strategy similarly treats cancer as an immune-visibility problem.

## Key Claims
- Cancer is difficult partly because malignant cells are self cells, not clearly foreign invaders.
- Effective oncology treatment depends on a discrimination problem: find something cancer cells have, express, or do that normal cells do not share at dangerous levels.
- Immune activation is double-edged because too little response permits cancer growth while too much response can damage normal tissue.
- [[CARTCellTherapy]] and [[CancerVaccinePlatform]] are adjacent but different answers to the same recognition problem.
