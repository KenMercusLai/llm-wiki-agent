# Death Option As Psychological Relief

Death option as psychological relief is the paradox in [[119-anlesi-xianchang-zhengyin-shengming-ruci-zhengui-770771087]] where knowing that assisted death is possible can make continued life more bearable. The episode describes a severely depressed woman who says that every time she remembers euthanasia is available, she feels able to keep living; it also mentions late-stage cancer patients who improved after knowing assisted suicide remained possible.

The concept is ethically unstable by design. It supports [[EndOfLifeAutonomyAndDignity]] by showing that control can reduce despair, but it also triggers the strongest objections around psychiatric suffering, imitation effects, and whether treatment or care has been exhausted.

## Key Claims
- The availability of an exit can function psychologically as relief rather than only as a path to immediate death.
- This makes simple "legalization causes death" and "choice solves suffering" arguments both incomplete.
- The psychiatric version is especially dangerous because depression can distort future judgment while still producing real suffering.
- Media discussion of the concept needs [[InternetMoralTrial]] restraint and suicide-contagion caution.
