# Revolutionary Virtue Politics / 革命美德政治

Revolutionary virtue politics / 革命美德政治 is the moral language [[125-mala-zhisi-beibi-de-ansha-huo-zhengyi-de-chujue-783972516]] sees around [[MaximilienRobespierre|Robespierre]], [[JeanPaulMarat|Marat]], and the radical [[JacobinClub|Jacobin]] milieu. The episode links virtue to romantic politics: heroes and enemies become sharply divided, sincerity becomes political proof, and public feeling can become more authoritative than institutional restraint.

The source connects this to [[JeanJacquesRousseau|Jean-Jacques Rousseau / 卢梭]] indirectly through sincerity, the general will, and suspicion of hidden interests. When politics demands transparent virtue, ordinary plural interests can look corrupt, and disagreement can look like betrayal.

The concept feeds [[RevolutionaryTerrorPolitics]]. Virtue language does not automatically create terror, but it can make terror feel morally necessary when enemies are imagined as obstacles to the people's purified will.

## Key Claims
- Virtue can become dangerous when it claims direct access to the people's moral will.
- Romantic politics tends to sharpen heroes, villains, martyrs, and traitors.
- Suspicion of hidden interests can make normal disagreement appear like conspiracy.
- Public sincerity is not the same as accountability; it can become a test used to pressure dissenters.
- Terror becomes easier to justify when punishment is framed as defense of virtue.
