# Policy Announcement Trading Risk

Policy announcement trading risk is the market risk created when public officials' statements, hints, posts, or timing around policy announcements move asset prices. [[ep77-si-shi-wan-nian-xin-fu-ye-zhuan-le-san-shi-si-yi-te-lang-pu-jiao-ni-ruhe-gao-qian-lnrx8vkdjedxbmmcwxlnwow-pcab]] uses the episode's account of [[DonaldTrump]] posting about DJT before a tariff-pause announcement as the main illustration: the hosts argue that a small timing gap between speech and policy can create enormous gains for insiders or aligned assets.

## Key Claims
- Ordinary investors are structurally disadvantaged when policy-relevant actors can speak and act on schedules the public cannot predict.
- Policy announcements can move broad benchmarks such as [[SP500]] as well as linked single names such as [[TrumpMediaAndTechnologyGroup]].
- The risk is not only insider trading in the narrow legal sense; it is also institutional unfairness, signaling advantage, and forced reaction time.
- The correct ordinary-investor response is usually smaller sizing, diversified exposure, and explicit rules rather than chasing political hints.
