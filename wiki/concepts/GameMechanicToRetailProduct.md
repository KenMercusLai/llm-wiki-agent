# Game Mechanic To Retail Product

Game mechanic to retail product is the product-development gap in [[board-games-3-whats-in-a-name]] between a playable ruleset and a game that a shopper understands, wants, and buys. The [[PlanetMoney]] team already has a working mechanic based on [[MarketForLemons|The Market for Lemons]], but [[ExplodingKittens]] treats that as only part of the job.

The retail product needs a name, theme, cover, card world, production plan, and retailer story. In the source, [[SellMeASasquatch]] becomes the answer because it turns [[AsymmetricInformation]] into a creature-selling market that can be explained by a box and title instead of by an economics lecture.

## Key Claims
- Playtesting can validate that a game works without proving that strangers will buy it from a shelf.
- A theme has to express the mechanic without misleading the buyer into the wrong genre.
- Retail games need [[RetailShelfAppeal]] and [[ProductNamingAsPositioning]] because the shopper may only make a quick judgment.
- The best commercial theme may diverge from the most literal source concept when literal accuracy creates weak shelf signal.
