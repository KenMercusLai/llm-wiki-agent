# Trace Evidence Forensics

Trace evidence forensics is the investigation of small materials transferred through contact: hair, fibers, dust, soil, pollen, glass, paint, gunshot residue, fluids, tool marks, and other particles. [[121-jijian-fayi-jianshi-shouce-yiqie-fanzui-jieyou-henji-775064343]] frames trace evidence through [[LocardsExchangePrinciple]], where every person becomes a moving producer and collector of residues.

The page connects the new source to older wiki trace pages. [[ForensicHairEvidence]] shows both the promise and wrongful-conviction risk of hair comparison. [[PalynologyAsEvidence]] shows how pollen can preserve location and seasonal information. Source 121 broadens those examples into a general investigative discipline where trace material must be collected carefully and interpreted against background alternatives.

## Key Claims
- Trace evidence is useful because it can connect people, places, objects, and events when direct observation is unavailable.
- Trace evidence is fragile: contamination, secondary transfer, environmental mixing, and biased suspect narratives can weaken it.
- Comparison requires known samples, context, and an explicit confidence level rather than a simple "match" story.
- Trace evidence is strongest when combined with other evidence streams such as fingerprints, toxicology, bloodstains, time estimates, and testimony checks.
