# Constructive Dissent / 建设性异议

[[132-zhenglun-yu-shuofu-women-weishenme-chaojia-zenme-cai-suan-ying-807583318]] adds a public-argument version through [[PeterWason|Peter Wason]]'s number-rule experiment and the episode's claim that argument can widen the sample. Disagreement improves shared judgment when it contributes new evidence, rival cases, or alternative hypotheses rather than only reinforcing [[TribalTruth|tribal truth]].

Constructive dissent / 建设性异议 is the source's claim that teams and societies need disagreement to see more information, correct bias, and create better decisions. [[116-bufucong-de-yishu-fenxiang-yixie-shizhan-fangfa-haiyou-liliang-764416021]] connects this to [[ProjectAristotle|Project Aristotle]], later psychological-safety research, investment-decision experiments, and slides where even wrong minority answers make later dissent more possible.

The concept is not "argue more" in general. Constructive dissent has to be tolerated as a real input to judgment, which means the group must absorb discomfort, lowered confidence, slower decisions, and challenge to hierarchy without immediately treating the dissenter as a threat.

## Key Claims
- Agreement can reduce information search because the group becomes overconfident too early.
- Minority disagreement can improve decision quality even when it is inconvenient.
- Psychological safety is useful but incomplete unless the team also tolerates disagreement and conflict.
- Wrong dissent can still open expressive space for later people who have better information.
- Organizations should recruit or preserve outsider views because insiders often share the same blind spots.
