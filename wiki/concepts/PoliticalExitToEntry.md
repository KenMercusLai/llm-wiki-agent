# Political Exit to Entry

Political exit to entry is the source's nameable pattern where a technology libertarian first imagines escaping state politics, then later chooses to enter and shape state power. [[e233-guigu-youyi-de-quanli-wang-ruhe-xingcheng-liaoliao-bide-tier-de-sixiang-qimeng-pintu-2a529abf-3c14-4ae7-a0a5-fe1340a66c7d]] uses [[PeterThiel]]'s support for [[SeasteadingInstitute]] as the exit phase and his later support for [[DonaldTrump]] and [[JDVance]] as the entry phase.

The concept is useful because it explains a change in tactic without requiring a full change in worldview. The source suggests that Thiel moved from seeking freedom outside the state toward using the American political system as a tool for power, institutional redesign, and coalition building.

## Key Claims
- Exit politics treats the state as a constraint to route around through technology, geography, or private governance.
- Entry politics treats the state as a lever to occupy, redirect, or use in crisis.
- The source reads Thiel's shift as pragmatic confidence in American political access after Trump's rise, not as a simple abandonment of libertarian instincts.
