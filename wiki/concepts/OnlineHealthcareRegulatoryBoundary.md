# Online Healthcare Regulatory Boundary

Online healthcare regulatory boundary is the policy line that determines what internet healthcare can safely and legally do in [[no-206-jiansuo-songyao-kanbing-hulianwang-yiliao-zhexie-nian-zhongguo-hulianwang-gushi-22-991273500]]. The episode highlights rules around online diagnosis, internet hospitals, remote medical services, prescription drugs, doctor identity, first diagnosis, and AI not replacing doctors.

The boundary is not only a restriction. The source argues that clearer regulation after the chaotic mobile-internet period made some business forms more operable: entity-backed [[InternetHospital]] services, compliant [[PharmaceuticalEcommerce]], electronic prescriptions, follow-up consultation, and [[MedicalAIWorkflowIntegration]] inside approved workflows.

[[3d-printing-was-supposed-to-disrupt-prosthetic-costs-it-hasnt]] adds an adjacent medical-device classification case. [[BrittYoung]] argues that implanted devices should remain tightly tested, but external day-to-day prosthetic attachments may need a different [[AssistiveDeviceClassification]] if the goal is broader access rather than treating every aid as a high-burden medical device.
