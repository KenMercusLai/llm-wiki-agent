# Scurvy Nutrition Insight

Scurvy nutrition insight is the practical recognition that diet and fresh plants could prevent or treat scurvy before the modern vitamin explanation was available. In [[120-lanhudao-weida-shiye-zhong-de-yexin-yu-xianshi-772998218]], [[GeorgWilhelmSteller]] notices that local people around [[Kamchatka]] do not suffer scurvy in the same way and connects that difference to eating green plants.

The episode uses this insight to show the value of field observation under crisis. Steller's herbs help sick crew members on the Saint Peter and later on [[BeringIsland]], while officers' earlier failure to prioritize fresh plant collection lets illness weaken the ship's ability to sail, steer, and survive storms.

## Key Claims
- Medical knowledge in expedition settings can begin as comparative observation of diet, local practice, and symptoms.
- Correct practical insight may still fail if command, hierarchy, and logistical urgency prevent uptake.
- Scurvy is not only a disease story; it changes seamanship, decision-making, labor capacity, and survival odds.
- Field medicine links [[ObservationBeforeInference]] to life-or-death institutional action.
