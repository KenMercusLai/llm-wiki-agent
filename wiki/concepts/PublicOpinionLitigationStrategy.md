# Public Opinion Litigation Strategy

Public opinion litigation strategy is the legal-pressure tactic in [[how-we-got-free-agents-in-baseball]] where [[CurtFlood|Curt Flood]] and [[ArthurGoldberg]] fought the [[BaseballReserveClause|reserve clause]] in public as well as in court. Their first move was Flood's letter to [[BowieKuhn]], and the episode later highlights Flood's television interview and [[JackieRobinson]]'s testimony as legitimacy-shifting moments.

The concept matters because Flood lost [[FloodVKuhn|Flood v. Kuhn]] but still helped change the environment for later arbitration and [[SportsCollectiveBargaining|collective bargaining]]. The source therefore treats public opinion as a form of leverage that can survive formal legal defeat.

## Key Claims
- Litigation can alter bargaining conditions even when the plaintiff loses the case.
- Public sympathy may change more slowly than legal precedent but can make old rules harder to defend.
- Credible witnesses can translate a disputed claim into a wider legitimacy problem.
