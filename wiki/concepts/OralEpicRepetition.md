# Oral Epic Repetition

Oral epic repetition is the broader cross-epic version of [[OralFormulaicEpic]] added by [[ep269-cong-manwei-yuzhou-dao-aodesai-weihe-zongyao-fanpai-yingxiong-shishi-ltklotheqpup6oamyflfzpxtipsw]]. The source explains that repeated phrases, epithets, and narrative formulas can feel strange in silent reading but make sense inside performance, memory, breathing, and song.

The episode's examples reach beyond Homer. [[PoeticEdda|《诗体埃达》]] uses recurring address-like formulas to create wonder and tempo, while [[TheEpicOfGilgamesh|《吉尔伽美什史诗》]] repeats [[Gilgamesh|Gilgamesh]]'s grief over [[Enkidu|Enkidu]] until what first looks static becomes emotional accumulation.

## Key Claims
- Repetition in old epics can be a performance tool, not only textual redundancy.
- A repeated line can function like a chorus, breath mark, memory anchor, or emotional intensifier.
- Modern readers misread epic repetition when they expect prose efficiency from material shaped by oral performance.
- The concept complements [[OralFormulaicEpic]] by extending the same logic beyond Homeric fixed epithets.
