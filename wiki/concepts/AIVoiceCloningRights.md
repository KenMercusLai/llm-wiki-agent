# AI Voice Cloning Rights

AI voice cloning rights are the consent, contract, evidence, and enforcement questions raised when a person's voice can be learned, synthesized, blended, or reused by AI systems. [[e234-weilai-shipai-dianying-hai-cunzai-ma-yu-daoyan-luchuan-liaoliao-ai-gei-yingshiren-de-kongju-yu-ziyou-b2be7093-3366-4ee2-8a7a-625f06206ae5]] adds the concept through [[HuangYing]]'s account of dubbing contracts and recent infringement disputes.

The source treats voice rights as parallel to visual likeness rights. Huang Ying says performers need explicit contract terms about whether their voice may be used for AI learning, training, cloning, or downstream use. Unauthorized use is unacceptable in her account even if the generated voice is only part of a blended synthetic voice.

The hard problem is proof. If a counterparty admits copying, enforcement is easier; if it denies copying or mixes several voices, the injured performer may need technical evidence that can survive a legal process. That makes [[AIContentProvenance]] and forensic detection part of the rights problem rather than a separate technical add-on.

## Key Claims
- Voice consent should be explicit, not inferred from ordinary recording or performance contracts.
- Blended voices create attribution and percentage-boundary problems for law and contracts.
- Technical detection matters because human similarity judgment may not be enough for civil evidence.
- Voice cloning extends [[AIPublicLikenessGeneration]] beyond face and image generation.
- Better AI voice quality increases both accessibility and infringement risk.
