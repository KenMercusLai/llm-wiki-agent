# Old Book Materiality / 旧书物质性

Old book materiality is the value readers find in books as handled, aged, socially traveled objects rather than as detachable text alone. [[134-chaling-shizi-jie-84-hao-zaici-yuanliang-shijie-de-liyou-821075226]] develops the concept through [[HeleneHanff|海莲·汉芙]]'s attachment to old editions in [[EightyFourCharingCrossRoad|《查令十字街84号》]]: paper, binding, marginalia, previous-reader marks, and the sense of another person's use all matter.

The episode also makes materiality a labor problem. [[FrankDoel|Frank Doel]] and [[MarksAndCo|Marks & Co.]] cannot simply retrieve a SKU; they search old-book channels, wait for copies, judge editions, and remember a reader's taste. Scarcity and search labor are part of the pleasure because they make arrival feel like care.

This concept extends [[MaterialHistoryNarrative]] into reading culture. A book is not only a container of information but a physical relay among printers, shops, readers, former owners, correspondents, parcels, shelves, and rooms.

## Key Claims
- Old books carry previous-reader traces that can become part of the reading experience.
- Edition, binding, paper, and use marks can matter without replacing the text.
- Scarcity creates a social process around search, trust, patience, and bookseller knowledge.
- Physical books can make literary relation more durable because they remain visible, touchable, and ownable in daily space.
- Treating books only as content strips away the material chain through which many readers actually encounter them.
