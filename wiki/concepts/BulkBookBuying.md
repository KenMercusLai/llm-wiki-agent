# Bulk Book Buying

Bulk book buying is the practice of purchasing large quantities of a title, often through organizations, events, campaigns, or author-funded efforts. [[how-to-make-a-book-into-a-bestseller]] adds the concept as a modern tactic used to influence nonfiction bestseller rankings.

The episode distinguishes bulk buying from ordinary reader demand because the purchases may be motivated by ranking goals rather than direct consumer discovery. It also notes that the [[NewYorkTimesBestsellerList|New York Times bestseller list]] can mark suspicious bulk-supported titles with a dagger symbol.

## Key Claims
- Bulk purchases are especially relevant in nonfiction, politics, religion, business, and thought-leadership contexts.
- Organizations may buy books for employees, attendees, donors, or clients while also helping launch-week sales.
- A bulk-sale mark can carry reputational cost, but the upside of bestseller status can still make the tactic tempting.
- The tactic becomes more attractive when bestseller status increases speaking, consulting, or future media value.
- Bulk buying overlaps with legitimate event sales but raises different trust questions when purchases do not reflect broad individual demand.
