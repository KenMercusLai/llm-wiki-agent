# Automation Reinstatement Effect

Automation reinstatement effect is the labor-market mechanism in [[chef-vs-robot]] where automation creates, reshapes, or complements work rather than only replacing it. [[DaronAcemoglu]] presents it as the counterpart to [[AutomationDisplacementEffect]].

In the restaurant case, reinstatement is visible but limited. [[RobbyWokbot]] still needs a human to load ingredients, follow prompts, handle service flow, and deal with breakdowns. The job may require less traditional wok skill, but it still creates operator, maintenance, process-control, and possibly higher-end culinary roles around the machine.

## Key Claims
- Reinstatement explains why automation can bring workers back into production through new tasks.
- The effect can be weaker than displacement if the new tasks are fewer, lower-paid, or less skill-building than the old ones.
- Partial restaurant automation may create easier-to-fill operator roles while reducing demand for trained chefs.
- Whether reinstatement is good for workers depends on wages, skill accumulation, bargaining power, and career paths.
