# New Right Policy Network

New right policy network is the source's frame for the organizations, funders, staffers, and cultural institutions that make [[DonaldTrump]]'s second-term agenda more organized than his 2016 outsider campaign. [[vol-113-cong-jiqian-ye-zhiku-wenjian-zhong-goule-telangpu-2-0-zhizheng-jihua-beihou-de-ren-jigou-sixiang-he-mailuo-lihnknc-3om5evexocb6a6zdwueu]] uses [[ConservativePartnershipInstitute]], [[AmericanMoment]], [[HeritageFoundation]], and [[AmericaFirstPolicyInstitute]] to make this network visible.

The concept is less about one think tank than about durable capacity. Former officials need employment and coordination after losing office; young conservatives need training and a career ladder; policy shops need documents and staff lists; and elected officials need a shared language for immigration, trade, education, bureaucracy, and national identity.

[[e233-guigu-youyi-de-quanli-wang-ruhe-xingcheng-liaoliao-bide-tier-de-sixiang-qimeng-pintu-2a529abf-3c14-4ae7-a0a5-fe1340a66c7d]] adds a Silicon Valley route into the same broader new-right capacity. Instead of think tanks and policy blueprints, the [[SiliconValley101]] episode emphasizes [[PeterThiel]], [[StanfordReview]], [[FoundersFund]], [[RightWingMediaInfrastructure]], [[RockbridgeNetwork]], and [[JDVance]] as a donor, media, and candidate-selection layer.

## Key Claims
- The network helps Trump-aligned politics survive between administrations.
- It links elite policy work to activist and staffer pipelines.
- Its agenda blends economic populism, political nationalism, foreign-policy restraint or isolationism, immigration restriction, and skepticism toward progressive cultural institutions.
- [[JDVance]] appears in the source as one political figure articulating these new-right principles.
