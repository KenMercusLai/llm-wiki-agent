# BATNA / Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement

BATNA is the best alternative a negotiator faces if no agreement is reached. In [[106-bie-zai-haipa-chongtu-kuai-lai-xuexi-tanpan-shu-742539645]], [[QinZong|秦总]] treats it as the first step in negotiation preparation: the negotiator should ask what reality awaits after failure, not what feels fair or how much has already been invested.

The episode's real-estate example makes the concept concrete. If a seller has another possible buyer at 42 million dollars, that alternative becomes the basis for the seller's [[ReservationValue]]. The same logic appears in the wiki's labor branch through [[StrikeThreatAsBATNA]], where a credible work stoppage changes bargaining power.

## Key Claims
- BATNA is not a wish, fairness claim, or sunk cost; it is the best available no-deal path.
- A better BATNA can raise leverage, while a weak BATNA requires more caution.
- Estimating the other side's BATNA helps identify their likely [[ReservationValue]].
- Negotiators should reassess BATNA as new information arrives.
