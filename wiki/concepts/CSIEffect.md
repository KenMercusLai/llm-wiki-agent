# CSI Effect

CSI effect is the expectation that real criminal cases should produce abundant, fast, high-tech forensic proof because crime television makes that look normal. [[121-jijian-fayi-jianshi-shouce-yiqie-fanzui-jieyou-henji-775064343]] uses the term after discussing CSI-style dramas and the public's fascination with法医/鉴证 work.

In the source, the effect matters in court and online. Jurors may wonder why there is no DNA evidence, and online audiences may treat partial fragments as enough to judge a person. The episode counters both habits by stressing that labs are expensive, resources vary by jurisdiction, and [[ForensicScience|forensic science]] still has uncertainty even when evidence exists.

## Key Claims
- Entertainment can make forensic evidence look more available, cleaner, and more decisive than it is.
- Lack of spectacular evidence is not automatically proof of poor investigation.
- Physical evidence should be valued over unreliable memory, but [[EvidenceOverTestimony]] is not the same as laboratory fantasy.
- Public judgment needs [[RationalHumility]] because real evidence often arrives incomplete, slowly, and with confidence limits.
