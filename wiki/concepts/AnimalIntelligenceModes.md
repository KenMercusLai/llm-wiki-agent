# Animal Intelligence Modes

Animal intelligence modes is the distinction [[39-zhexuejia-yu-lang-zai-langgeduoke-yongheng-de-xiatian-611233065]] draws among wolf, dog, and human intelligence while discussing [[ThePhilosopherAndTheWolf|《哲学家与狼》 / The Philosopher and the Wolf]]. The episode uses door-opening examples and [[Brenin|布列宁]]'s behavior to distinguish causal, mechanical problem-solving from social intelligence oriented toward using or reading other beings.

The source's central contrast is that a wolf studies the door, while a dog studies the human who can open it. Human intelligence then appears as a more dangerous extension of social intelligence: not only cooperation and reading minds, but also deception, conspiracy, hierarchy, and detecting deception.

## Key Claims
- Intelligence is not one ladder with humans at the top; different animals solve different environmental and social problems.
- Wolf intelligence is framed as more mechanical and causally attentive, though the source treats this as an interpretive contrast rather than a complete ethology.
- Dog intelligence is framed as socially embedded in human life, using people as part of the problem-solving environment.
- Human intelligence is powerful partly because it models other minds, but this also enables lying, political calculation, and exploitation.
- Embodied and embedded cognition matter because thinking happens through body, environment, tools, and other beings, not only inside an isolated brain.
