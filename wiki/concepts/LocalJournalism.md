# Local Journalism

Local journalism is reporting on community institutions, events, needs, and accountability questions. In [[congress-has-voted-to-eliminate-government-funding-for-public-media]], [[KatherineMaher]] uses town councils and state championships as examples of the local coverage that could shrink if [[PublicMediaFunding]] disappears.

The source treats local journalism as part of [[PublicServiceJournalism]] because it provides information people may not get from national or commercial media alone. It also links local reporting to [[PublicMediaEmergencyAccess]] because local stations can become trusted channels during crises.

## Key Claims
- Local reporting gives communities visibility and accountability at the town, school, and state level.
- Public-media cuts are presented as reducing the number of people able to ask difficult local questions.
- Local journalism can carry identity value as well as accountability value because it reflects community stories.
