# Deep-Tech Product Focus

Deep-tech product focus is the discipline of narrowing a broad technical platform into one commercial product despite many plausible research, licensing, services, training, or side-market opportunities. [[tsr-s5-davidkirtley-v2-audio-tsr-s5-davidkirtley-v2-audio]] adds the concept through [[Helion]] and [[YCombinator]].

Before YC, [[DavidKirtley]] says Helion was spread across material science, plasma physics, fusion, electricity, and space propulsion. YC pushed the company to choose one product: generators for low-cost, clean, safe baseload electricity. The same focus appears later when Helion refuses to sell helium-3 into smaller markets because that revenue would not solve [[CommercialFusionPower]].

This concept complements [[HardTechFundraising]] and [[FounderFriendlyInvestorSupport]]. A deep-tech company may need long-term investors not only for money, but to protect focus when easy revenue would turn the company into a smaller business.

## Key Claims
- A deep technical platform can create too many plausible directions; focus requires rejecting some real opportunities.
- Accelerator advice can matter in hard tech when it forces product and business clarity rather than technical shortcuts.
- Side revenue is dangerous when it consumes scarce manufacturing, leadership, or technical attention without moving the core mission.
