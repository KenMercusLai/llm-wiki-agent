# Do Too Much Founder Philosophy

Do too much founder philosophy is [[AlexandrWang]]'s phrase in [[tsr-s4-alexandrwang-v3-tsr-s4-alexandrwang-v3]] for responding aggressively when a large technology shift is underway. He uses it to explain why [[ScaleAI]] moved a large share of the company toward generative AI data after [[ChatGPT]] made the new demand curve obvious.

The concept is not a general argument for unfocused overwork. In this source, "do too much" means that if the founder correctly identifies an unusually large discontinuity, ordinary reaction speed may be too slow and underreaction may be the bigger risk.

## Key Claims
- The philosophy depends on identifying a real platform shift, not simply increasing activity.
- It can justify rapid resource reallocation when customer demand and technology capability change together.
- It is risky because overreaction to the wrong trend can damage focus, hiring, and culture.
- The source pairs the philosophy with writing and explicit culture statements because fast movement needs organizational alignment.
