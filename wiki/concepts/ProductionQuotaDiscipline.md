# Production Quota Discipline

Production quota discipline is the problem of keeping producers inside agreed output limits. In [[the-secret-meeting-that-launched-opec]], [[OPEC]] introduces quotas in 1982, but members still have an incentive to cheat because selling extra oil while prices are high can be individually attractive.

The concept is a practical limit on [[OilProducerSupplyCoordination]]. A quota system can stabilize prices only if members accept short-term sacrifice for collective gain. If enforcement is weak, cheating shifts adjustment pressure to the largest or most disciplined producer, which is why [[SaudiArabia]]'s later refusal to keep absorbing the pain becomes so important in the episode.

The source also shows that quota disputes can become geopolitical. The [[UnitedArabEmirates|UAE]] objected to Saudi Arabia's proposed quota increases in 2021 because they used older production-capacity baselines. That dispute made technical quota math a signal of who gets to monetize future barrels under [[GreenParadox]] pressure.
