# Zone Of Possible Agreement / 协议区间

Zone of possible agreement is the range between the two sides' acceptable bottom lines. [[106-bie-zai-haipa-chongtu-kuai-lai-xuexi-tanpan-shu-742539645]] introduces it after [[BATNA]] and [[ReservationValue]]: once each side's alternatives and limits are estimated, the negotiator can see where a deal might exist.

The source's real-estate example puts the seller's bottom line at 42 million dollars and the estimated buyer maximum at 48 million dollars, making 42 to 48 million the likely agreement zone. The episode then warns that weak information can make the negotiator misread the zone and leave value unclaimed.

## Key Claims
- The agreement zone is estimated, not known with certainty.
- New information should update the perceived zone during negotiation.
- A price inside the zone is not automatically optimal; the negotiator still needs to learn how much value is available.
- [[MultiIssueNegotiation]] can widen the effective zone by adding issues beyond the headline price.

## Connections
- [[BATNA]] and [[ReservationValue]] - inputs for mapping the agreement zone.
- [[AnchoringEffect]] and [[ConcessionSignaling]] - tactics that change how the zone is perceived.
- [[ContingentAgreement]] - method for making a deal possible when parties disagree about future outcomes.
