# B2B to A2A

B2B to A2A is [[ZhangKuo]]'s thesis in [[e231-cong-b2b-dao-a2a-agent-xin-jijian-ruhe-rang-yiren-qiye-zuo-quanqiu-shengyi-0f4a2ab9-d3a0-41ad-8db1-6c03c851bd70]] that business-to-business trade can evolve into agent-to-agent coordination. In this frame, a buyer's agent, a seller's agent, platform agents, and a user's own master/subagents negotiate product definition, supplier fit, price, logistics, support, and follow-on operations.

The concept extends [[AgenticCommerce]] into higher-stakes physical trade. Consumer shopping agents can compare products and place orders; B2B agents must also handle specifications, certificates, factories, landed cost, payment timing, delivery risk, customs, after-sales, repeat procurement, and platform guarantees.

## Key Claims
- A2A is not only a messaging protocol; in this source it is a business-workflow claim about who performs trade tasks.
- Buyer and seller agents need shared, auditable context because wrong prices, requirements, or logistics assumptions can destroy margin.
- Marketplace platforms still matter because agents need trusted supplier data, transaction rails, dispute handling, logistics, and payment services.
- Human users remain responsible for goals, acceptance criteria, and high-impact approvals, making [[AgentPermissionBoundaries]] central.
- The shift can support [[OnePersonCompany]] and small-team trade, but only when the agent system carries enough operational truth to substitute for missing back-office capacity.
