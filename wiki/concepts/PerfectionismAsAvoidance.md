# Perfectionism As Avoidance

Perfectionism as avoidance is the source's frame for perfectionism as a self-protective block rather than a reliable route to excellence. In [[129-ruhe-chengwei-zhenzheng-de-buwanmei-zhuyizhe-hunshen-shi-jiner-799220151]], [[QinZong|秦总]] argues that perfectionism often lets a person avoid embarrassment, criticism, failure, or visible inadequacy while appearing to pursue high standards.

The episode separates this from healthy excellence. A person can care about quality, correctness, or craft without making action conditional on perfect conditions, perfect output, or perfect numerical goals. When standards become a shield against trying, they connect to [[RedPenLogic]], [[AchievementPressureMentalHealth]], and [[ActionAgainstAnxiety]].

## Key Claims
- Perfectionism can look like ambition while functioning as procrastination, retreat, or risk avoidance.
- Situational perfectionism waits for ideal conditions; quality perfectionism waits for flawless output; quantity perfectionism turns arbitrary metrics into entry barriers.
- Failure becomes harder to tolerate when the person treats it as proof of identity rather than feedback from one attempt.
- School, family, social media, and gendered appearance standards can train people to keep evaluating themselves before acting.
- The alternative is not sloppiness; it is [[ImperfectActionPractice]], where real action produces feedback that impossible standards never produce.
