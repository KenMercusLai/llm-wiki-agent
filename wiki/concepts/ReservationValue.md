# Reservation Value / 谈判底线

Reservation value is the bottom line at which a negotiator is willing to accept a deal rather than walk away. In [[106-bie-zai-haipa-chongtu-kuai-lai-xuexi-tanpan-shu-742539645]], it is derived from [[BATNA]] rather than from emotion, sunk cost, or a vague sense of fairness.

The source's real-estate case uses a possible 42 million dollar outside offer as the seller's bottom line. The episode then shows why that is only a minimum: if the buyer can actually pay much more, merely beating one's own [[ReservationValue]] may still leave value undiscovered.

## Key Claims
- A bottom line should be grounded in the best no-deal alternative.
- Estimating the other side's bottom line is necessary before judging whether a deal was good.
- Confusing sunk cost with reservation value can trap the negotiator in a bad deal.
- A clear reservation value protects against emotional concession under pressure.
