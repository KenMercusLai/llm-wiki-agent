# True Princess Ambiguity / 真公主暧昧

True princess ambiguity is the reading problem opened by [[187-wandou-gongzhu-gaomin-he-hundun-shi-ni-zhenming-999958166]] through [[ThePrincessAndThePea|《豌豆公主》]]. The tale keeps repeating truth claims: the prince wants a true princess, the girl's sensitivity proves she is true, and the ending says the story is true. Yet the actual test is so strange that it destabilizes the very category it claims to verify.

The concept holds two interpretations together. On one side, the pea test mocks aristocratic distinction by reducing noble essence to impossible bodily delicacy. On the other side, the princess is not merely a comic fake: she arrives in a storm, is tested without consent, is injured, and has no visible choice in the marriage conclusion. The ambiguity lets class satire and gendered sympathy coexist.

## Key Claims
- A truth test can expose the absurdity of the truth category it claims to defend.
- Aristocratic bloodline logic becomes comic when it needs a pea, mattresses, and bruises as evidence.
- The same scene can be satirical toward class hierarchy and still cruel toward the tested girl.
- The tale's repeated "true" language is a formal signal that truth itself is unstable here.
