# KRAS Oncology Target

KRAS oncology target is the source's framing of KRAS as the first mutation target for [[AdventrisPharmaceuticals]]' [[CancerVaccinePlatform]]. In [[tsr-ycoffsite-jenherbach-v1-audio-tsr-ycoffsite-jenherbach-v1-audio]], [[JenHerbach]] calls KRAS a driver mutation that can help turn a healthy cell into a cancerous cell and lead to uncontrolled growth.

Herbach says KRAS causes more than 30% of cancers and is mainly found in lung, colon, and pancreatic cancer. That cross-cancer footprint is why the source treats KRAS as a platform target rather than a one-disease niche. Adventris starts with localized pancreatic cancer because those patients may later relapse into metastatic incurable disease, while the longer-term ambition is preventive vaccination.

## Key Claims
- KRAS is presented as a driver mutation, not just a correlated marker.
- Cross-cancer prevalence makes KRAS attractive for a vaccine strategy that could expand beyond one initial indication.
- The source's quantitative claim is Herbach's founder account: KRAS is said to be involved in more than 30% of cancers.
- The clinical path begins with treatment evidence before the prevention vision can be tested.
