# Social Graph Moat

Social graph moat is the Tencent-side platform advantage emphasized in [[touteng-dazhan-ba-nian-hou-zai-ba-zijie-he-tengxun-zai-gege-zhanchang-shang-de-jingzheng-zhuyi-chaikai-zijie-tiaodong-di-6-ji-lvglr-jws7o7utjauxnoqqasj91d]]. The hosts argue that [[WeChat]] is not merely a messaging app: it is a contact book, wallet, identity layer, social-history archive, work surface, and public/mini-program ecosystem.

This explains why [[Duoshan]] was unlikely to beat WeChat through a social-video pitch alone. ByteDance could build traffic and recommendation products, but social migration requires users to move relationships, identity, daily infrastructure, and trust.

## Key Claims
- Messaging utility is only one layer of a social platform's moat.
- Identity, payment, history, contacts, groups, work coordination, and mini-program habits make switching far harder than installing a new app.
- The same moat can become strategically important in AI because a high-context assistant may need access to social and service workflows.
- The moat also constrains Tencent: protecting entry points can make it slower to expose [[AgentFacingInterfaces]] or open platform data.
