# 体面税 / Respectability Tax

Respectability tax is the source's phrase for the money, time, and performance needed to appear naturally proper inside a high-status social field. In [[140-haikeyi-de-jin-nvshi-suoyi-ren-weishenme-yao-nuli-a-854173655]], [[Jinzi|金子]] applies it to fashion media after entering [[CondeNast|康泰纳仕]]: one must look elegant, relaxed, not too sincere, not too obviously eager, and supported by the right gym, nail, clothing, and luxury-sign routines.

The tax is not only financial. It also charges attention and sincerity. A person must learn what to praise, when to withhold enthusiasm, how to look effortless, and which objects signal belonging. The episode's humor about broken-looking fashion and luxury cosplay reveals the rule as shared performance rather than natural taste.

## Key Claims
- Respectability often presents itself as effortlessness while requiring repeated spending, bodily work, and social reading.
- Industry taste can make direct sincerity look naive, while controlled distance looks refined.
- The tax is harder for people who enter a field without inherited consumer fluency or a budget for its signals.
- Luxury objects can operate less as tools than as shared proof that participants understand the scene.
- Naming the tax reduces its mystery: people can still participate, but they no longer have to mistake the rules for natural superiority.
