# Jianghu Scam Craft

Jianghu scam craft is the older street-fraud repertoire extracted from [[102-jianghu-congtan-pianshu-heihua-he-minjian-daoyi-736516488]]. The episode describes fortune-telling, physiognomy, fake medicine, mole removal, railway-station setups, insurance fraud, and staged family-recognition plots as technical practices that combine observation, language, props, collaborators, fear, greed, and social pressure.

The concept extends [[SocialEngineeringFraud]] by showing that many modern scam mechanics have face-to-face predecessors. A victim is not only lied to; they are placed inside a role. They may become the person receiving secret knowledge, rescuing a fugitive situation, finding a bargain, preventing disaster, proving filial or household responsibility, or confirming what they already fear.

## Key Claims
- Scam craft often begins with observation: clothing, accent, age, emotion, travel route, gender role, and local occupational patterns become clues.
- A "free" first interaction can be a diagnostic tool, not generosity.
- Collaborators create false independence and make staged information look supernatural or accidental.
- Ambiguous pricing and second-step charges let the scam grow after the victim is already committed.
- Pseudoscientific, religious, medical, or folk-theory packaging can make ordinary inference look like authority.
- The source links these routines to modern phone scams, pyramid schemes, fake high-yield products, and AI-enabled fraud as variations on the same social-engineering logic.
