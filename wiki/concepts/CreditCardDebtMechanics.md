# Credit Card Debt Mechanics

Credit card debt mechanics are the repayment, interest, fee, and behavior rules that make card debt feel smaller than it is. [[ep24-fangdai-chedai-xiaofeidai-daidai-weinu-daidai-hai-lswnaa7x8biku9ouyv-c1dkf439]] treats credit cards as useful only when the borrower understands the difference between full repayment, installment, minimum repayment, cash withdrawal, overdue interest, cash-out, and fake-order schemes.

## Key Claims
- Credit cards can build [[PersonalCreditRecord]] when used and repaid properly.
- Bill installments and specialist calls often frame the cost as daily or monthly affordability, which can hide the annualized cost.
- Minimum repayment can prevent immediate overdue status, but the remaining balance may lose interest-free treatment and keep accruing cost.
- Long-term minimum repayment can leave principal largely unresolved while interest grows.
- Cash withdrawal is an allowed bank product but usually has no interest-free period and can include limits, interest, and fees.
- Credit-card cash-out is prohibited or illegal because it turns credit into disguised cash while bypassing transaction authenticity and purpose controls.
- Fake-order or brush-order cash-out can also be a scam pattern: small early rebates may build trust before a larger loss.
- Card security includes overseas-card settings, quick dispute evidence after suspected fraud, and physically destroying old cards, magnetic strips, numbers, and CVV.
