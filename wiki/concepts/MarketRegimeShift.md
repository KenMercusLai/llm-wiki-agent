# Market Regime Shift

Market regime shift is the episode's term for periods when the rules behind market behavior change enough that historical patterns become unreliable. [[ep88-chuanyue-lianghua-zhifu-ximengsi-ai-hui-rang-putongren-geng-rongyi-zhuanqian-haishi-geng-nan-lhvigzza2ugmayezkbrxufkmp4l1]] uses pandemic shock, liquidity expansion, inflation, rate hikes, and policy-driven markets to explain why even strong quantitative systems can lose money.

## Key Claims
- Historical models work best in normal states where the future resembles the past.
- New macro, policy, or liquidity regimes can create states with little or no training sample.
- Investors should lower leverage, diversify more broadly, and reduce confidence when the regime is changing.
- Market regimes also shape whether [[QuantitativeInvesting]], value investing, or [[PassiveInvesting]] is most likely to work.
