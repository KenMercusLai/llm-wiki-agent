# 努力叙事追问 / Effort Narrative Interrogation

Effort narrative interrogation is the source's act of asking why effort became morally mandatory. In [[140-haikeyi-de-jin-nvshi-suoyi-ren-weishenme-yao-nuli-a-854173655]], [[Jinzi|金子]] remembers being shocked when a Hainan classmate asked why people must work hard, while [[QinZong|秦总]] recalls a French sociology class where her language around work sounded older than she realized.

The source does not replace effort with lying flat as the new correct answer. Its sharper claim is that any posture can become a rule: eat bitterness, hustle, relax, be cool, refuse ambition, or perform slackness. Effort becomes healthier only when it is connected to felt purpose, bodily limits, and a chosen direction rather than to an inherited proof of worth.

## Key Claims
- Effort can become identity: a person may feel anger when effort is questioned because the question threatens the moral order that made their life intelligible.
- "Lying flat" can become another template if it is adopted as proof of superiority rather than as honest pacing or refusal.
- Effort should be examined by direction, cost, ownership, and feedback, not only by intensity.
- The source complements, rather than contradicts, action-oriented pages: action matters more when it is no longer used as compulsory self-justification.
- The question "why effort" is useful because it reopens judgment where culture had installed a default answer.
