# Mathematical Abundance

Mathematical abundance is [[HongLetong]]'s vision in [[137-dui-hong-letong-de-4-xiaoshi-fangtan-ai-for-math-ba-shuxue-biancheng-lean-shuxue-tianshu-zhong-de-zhengming-zhijue-bei-chuangzao-yu-bei-faxian-de-lha-faiwxtget0qmbcosts3cb5vb]] that AI can move society from being math-poor to math-rich. If proof, conjecture, formalization, and verification become much cheaper, mathematical discovery can expand in volume and feed more theory into science, engineering, software, and hardware.

## Key Claims
- The bottleneck can shift from proving individual theorems to choosing which problems deserve attention, compute, and human interpretation.
- Human mathematicians become more like research directors, benchmark designers, and taste-bearing collaborators when proof supply expands.
- The episode compares this to a "fold everything" mindset after AlphaFold: if the marginal cost of solving a class of problems falls, the rational scope of curiosity can expand.
- Abundance does not remove mathematical culture; Hong argues that human community, taste, collaboration, and new questions remain part of mathematics.
