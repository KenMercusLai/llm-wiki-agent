# Carceral Economic Dependency

Carceral economic dependency is the condition where local jobs, public revenue, or service capacity depend on prisons, detention centers, or related confinement infrastructure. In [[the-ice-hiring-boom]], [[FolkstonGeorgia]] and [[CharltonCounty]] depend partly on a large ICE detention facility operated by [[GEOGroup]].

The source frames the dependency as morally uncomfortable rather than abstract. Residents need jobs and benefits, local officials need revenue, and detained people remain visible as human beings behind barbed wire.

## Key Claims
- Carceral facilities can become local economic anchors where other stable employers are scarce.
- The dependency creates a conflict between household security and the harms of confinement.
- Local beneficiaries may not control the federal policy choices that decide whether the facility grows or shrinks.
- A carceral development path can crowd out harder questions about alternative public investment and durable jobs.
