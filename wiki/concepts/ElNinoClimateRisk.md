# El Nino Climate Risk

El Nino climate risk is the episode's frame for what happens when a strong [[ElNino]] redistributes heat and moisture across a world that is already warmer. [[missing-peace-will-israel-imperil-iran-deal-6a3bae9fe1238e54b0e886c3]] says the event can raise global average temperatures and alter rainfall patterns, with the largest risks falling on regions already vulnerable to drought, floods, food insecurity, disease outbreaks, and conflict.

The practical part of the concept is preparation under constraint. The episode names drought-tolerant seeds, fodder storage, and water supplies as possible interventions, while warning that falling aid budgets may limit readiness just as food insecurity rises.

[[shangye-xiaoyang-45-eerninuo-yueda-yu-yuegui-1000164352]] adds a fisheries and commodity-price path. The source shows El Nino risk moving through [[PeruvianAnchoveta|anchoveta]] populations, Peruvian catch bans, [[FishmealSupplyChain|fishmeal]] supply, [[AquacultureFeedDependency]], and eventual seafood prices rather than only through crop failure or heat stress.
