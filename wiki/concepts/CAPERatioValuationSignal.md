# CAPE Ratio Valuation Signal

CAPE ratio valuation signal is the source's use of the cyclically adjusted price-to-earnings ratio as a warning about expensive equities. In [[indicators-of-2025-and-what-to-watch-in-2026]], [[DarianWoods]] says the ratio was higher than at any point except just before the dot-com crash.

The episode connects the signal to the AI boom, data-center construction, bubble fears, and the K-shaped economy. That makes the concept a bridge between broad market valuation and [[AIEquityValuationRisk]]: a high price relative to smoothed earnings may coexist with real AI infrastructure demand, but it still raises the question of how much future earnings investors have already priced in.

## Key Claims
- CAPE is useful because it smooths earnings over time rather than reading only the latest year.
- A high CAPE ratio does not by itself predict the timing of a correction, but it can flag a market where expected future returns may be more fragile.
- The source uses CAPE to connect AI enthusiasm, data-center capital spending, and asset-price inequality without claiming that one ratio explains all household stress.
- The indicator complements [[AIInvestmentMetrics]] by focusing on public-market price discipline rather than operating metrics alone.
