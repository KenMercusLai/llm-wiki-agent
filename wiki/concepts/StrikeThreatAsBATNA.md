# Strike Threat as BATNA

Strike threat as BATNA is the use of a credible work stoppage as the worker side's alternative to accepting a weak agreement. In [[diary-of-a-wnba-negotiator]], the [[WNBAPlayersAssociation]] prepared this option by surveying more than 150 players, securing strike authorization, and telling players to save money for a possible stoppage.

[[106-bie-zai-haipa-chongtu-kuai-lai-xuexi-tanpan-shu-742539645]] supplies the general negotiation frame behind this page. A [[BATNA]] is the best no-deal alternative, and strike threat is one labor-specific form whose value depends on credibility, preparation, and the cost of walking away.

The source frames the threat as a turning point. After days of stalled talks, the players told their lawyer to communicate that they would walk if the league did not return that night; the episode then records the league moving toward shared basketball revenue.

## Key Claims
- A strike threat is stronger when members have already discussed the cost and prepared financially.
- The threat depends on unity: bargaining representatives need confidence they are carrying what the broader membership wants.
- Data can justify demands, but a no-deal option is what keeps data-backed demands from being ignored.
- In the source, the strike threat appears to shift the discussion from fixed salary offers toward [[SportsLaborRevenueSharing]].
- A strike threat is only useful as a BATNA if the side making it has calculated its [[ReservationValue]] and can survive the no-deal period.
