# Predestined Choice

Predestined choice is the ethical problem of choosing inside a future that appears already fixed. In [[104-ni-yisheng-de-gushi-zhongdian-yijing-zhuding-haiyao-chufa-ma-739384680]], the title question comes from [[StoryOfYourLife|《你一生的故事》]]: if [[LouiseBanks|Louise Banks / 路易斯·班克斯]] knows her daughter will die young, what does it mean for her to still answer that she wants a child?

The episode does not treat fixed fate as simple passivity. In [[MerchantAndAlchemistsGate|《商人和炼金术之门》]], characters cannot erase the past, but they can understand, repent, receive consolation, or recognize happiness they missed. Choice remains meaningful as participation in a whole life rather than as unlimited power to edit outcomes.

## Key Claims
- Foreknowledge does not automatically make action meaningless.
- Fixed time can shift agency from control toward acceptance, fidelity, grief, repentance, or affirmation.
- The emotional force of the problem depends on concrete relationships rather than abstract determinism alone.
- [[NonlinearTimePerception]] makes the problem cognitive; [[PredestinedChoice]] makes it ethical.
