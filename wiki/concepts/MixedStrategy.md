# Mixed Strategy

Mixed strategy is the [[GameTheory]] pattern where a player deliberately varies between options so an opponent cannot predict and exploit the next move. [[how-to-win-a-penalty-shootout-with-game-theory]] explains it through penalties: a kicker may need to shoot to the weaker side sometimes, and a goalkeeper may need to dive against their own preference sometimes, because always choosing the comfortable option becomes visible.

The source treats [[MixedStrategy]] as both a theory and a learned competitive behavior. Professional penalty takers and goalkeepers can approach the theory because the stakes are high, the choices repeat, and opponents have strong incentives to study patterns.

## Key Claims
- Randomness is useful when predictability would let the opponent condition their response.
- The optimal mix is not simply 50/50; it depends on each player's success rates, tendencies, and how the opponent adapts.
- Real competition complicates the simplified left-right model with height, corners, run-ups, timing, and psychology.
