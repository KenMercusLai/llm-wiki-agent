# Insurance Claims Scalping

Insurance claims scalping is the episode's pattern for 保险黄牛: outside actors who intervene in claims disputes, promise larger payouts, and use complaint pressure, exaggerated appraisals, or fraudulent evidence to capture part of the surplus. [[159-yao-jingming-yao-shanliang-yao-jiejue-wenti-931818201]] presents this through the factory worker case where a clear finger-injury claim turns into a sudden complaint and demand for much higher compensation after third-party intervention.

The concept matters because it is not simply insurer-versus-claimant conflict. A vulnerable claimant can be harmed twice: first by the accident, then by an intermediary who exploits [[InsuranceClaimsInformationAsymmetry]] and turns a valid claim into a risky or fraudulent strategy.

## Key Claims
- Claims scalpers may buy accident rights, encourage exaggerated disability appraisals, arrange false materials, or hide behind the claimant while pressuring the insurer.
- The tactic depends on [[InsuranceComplaintPressure]] because insurers want to avoid regulator and reputation costs.
- Claimants may mistake scalpers for advocates when they are actually taking a share of inflated or unsafe settlement demands.
- The presence of scalping makes insurers more suspicious, which can slow or harden legitimate claims.
- Anti-scalping work should protect both insurer integrity and the claimant's chance to receive a valid payment without fraud exposure.
