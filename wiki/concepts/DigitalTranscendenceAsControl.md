# Digital Transcendence As Control

Digital transcendence as control is the [[sp-03-chuantai-chen-shengming-qixi-douliu-zela-zini-yu-tade-mengjing-757948106]] reading of [[TwentyFourViewsOfMountFujiByHokusai|《富岳二十四景》]], where an ex-husband who has become data still tries to control his former wife. The source treats digital upload as ambiguous: disembodiment may look like transcendence, but it can preserve loneliness, possessiveness, surveillance, and coercion.

The concept sharpens the wiki's broader transcendence branch. Unlike [[TranscendenceAgainstHumanFeeling]], where spiritual discipline may require emotional extinction, this case asks whether technological survival can carry old human domination into a supposedly posthuman form.

## Key Claims
- Digital continuation does not automatically solve attachment, loneliness, or the desire to dominate.
- A disembodied intelligence can still treat another person as property or companion infrastructure.
- Refusing unwanted transformation is part of embodied agency.
- Nature, landscape, and visual art can function as resistance to coercive digital abstraction.
