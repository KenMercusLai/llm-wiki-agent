# Experimental Failure As Knowledge

Experimental failure as knowledge is the source's pattern for failed, lucky, inconclusive, or wrongly interpreted experiments that still teach something. In [[68-fengkuang-shiyan-shi-ai-zhe-gaisi-de-qiuzhiyu-664384549]], [[StubbinsFfirth|Stubbins Ffirth]] survives yellow-fever exposure without proving his full theory, a mamba-venom doctor misjudges the mechanism, spider urine studies do not yield a stable diagnostic tool, and the train-trumpet test of the [[DopplerEffect|Doppler effect]] is difficult to execute.

The concept depends on [[ScientificSelfCorrection]]. Failure becomes knowledge only when the researcher marks what the result did and did not show. If a negative result is converted into certainty, or a dramatic demonstration into an oversized claim, failure stops being useful and becomes overinterpretation.

## Key Claims
- A failed experiment can rule out a path, expose a control problem, or reveal a hidden variable.
- A lucky survival is not the same as proof.
- Inconclusive results need honest boundaries rather than narrative rescue.
- Scientific progress often comes from correcting the interpretation of earlier experiments.
