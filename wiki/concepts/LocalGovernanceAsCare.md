# Local Governance As Care / 地方治理作为照护

Local governance as care is the episode's frame for [[SuShi|苏轼]]'s practical service outside the court center. In [[105-su-dongpo-yiqiang-haomai-zhiqi-yi-dupi-buhe-shiyi-741072645]], the hosts repeatedly resist a merely leisurely Su Dongpo image by pointing to water works, flood defense, relief, medicine, infant rescue, burials, bridges, and schooling.

The concept makes governance visible at the scale of bodies and households. [[Hangzhou|杭州]] is not only West Lake scenery; it includes grain storage, medical facilities, medicine distribution, and the [[SuCauseway|苏堤]]. [[Xuzhou|徐州]] is not only a post; it is flood protection. [[Huizhou|惠州]] and [[Danzhou|儋州]] show that even exile can contain care work.

## Key Claims
- Good local governance often appears as concrete maintenance rather than ideology.
- Infrastructure, medicine, relief, burial, and childcare all belong to public duty when poverty and disaster threaten bodies.
- Cultural memory can preserve a scenic object while forgetting the administrative care that produced it.
- Exile or demotion does not fully remove public responsibility if an official still has presence, relationships, and practical means.
