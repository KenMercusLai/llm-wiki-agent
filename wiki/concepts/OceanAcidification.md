# Ocean Acidification

Ocean acidification is discussed in [[melody-jue-ocean-memory-wxagwid1oeg]] as both a shell-building threat and a chemosensory threat. [[MelodyJue]] links it to abalone, sea bass smell research, weakened future sensing, and the breakdown of cultural and ecological transmission in [[MandySuzanneWong]]'s abalone story.

The source's distinctive contribution is to connect acidification to memory. If ocean chemistry changes what marine beings can smell, taste, build, or respond to, then acidification can damage [[OceanMemory]] as well as bodies and habitats.

## Key Claims
- Acidification can harm shell-forming organisms and also alter chemical sensing.
- Sensory disruption can become memory disruption when organisms depend on chemical cues to identify habitat, food, risk, and prior exposure.
- The source uses acidification as a bridge between climate danger, cultural loss, and [[MultispeciesArchives]].

## Connections
- [[Chemosensation]] — sensory pathway affected by changing seawater chemistry.
