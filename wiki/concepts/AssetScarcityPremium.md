# Asset Scarcity Premium

Asset scarcity premium is the fourth attribute [[vol-101-ji-anquan-shouyi-you-gao-liudongxing-hai-hao-de-touzi-daodi-cunzai-ma-lr7xt-2cgoru2gfdbatvbiv78ixg]] adds beyond return, safety, and liquidity. The episode argues that scarcity can sometimes make an asset look like it breaks the [[InvestmentImpossibleTriangle]], but only because the opportunity is limited by time, regulation, capacity, supply, or access.

The source separates time scarcity from space or capacity scarcity. Time scarcity includes products that existed during a particular regulatory or interest-rate window, such as pre-transition bank wealth products or internet-platform bank deposits. Space scarcity includes strategies or assets with limited capacity, such as the [[MedallionFund]], and finite-supply assets such as gold or [[Bitcoin]].

## Key Claims
- Scarcity is not the same as safety; it can raise return potential while leaving hidden risks intact.
- Regulatory gray zones can create attractive products temporarily, but the same feature can disappear once rules close the window.
- Capacity-limited strategies may stop accepting outside capital because more money would destroy the edge.
- Finite physical or digital supply can create a scarcity narrative, but substitutes and belief changes can weaken it.
- Ordinary investors should treat genuine scarcity as rare and difficult to identify, not as a reason to chase every exclusive product.
- Scarcity claims need the same cash-flow, counterparty, liquidity, and behavior checks as any other investment.
