# Triangle Intimacy Ethics

Triangle intimacy ethics is the problem of reading a three-person intimate structure without reducing it to a cheating plot, a liberation slogan, or a moral panic. [[108-zu-yu-zhan-ai-yu-bu-ai-bali-jiu-zai-nali-songjian-745812905]] adds the concept through [[JulesAndJim|《祖与占》]], where [[CatherineJulesAndJim|Catherine]], [[JulesJulesAndJim|Jules]], and [[JimJulesAndJim|Jim]] move through friendship, marriage, desire, jealousy, household experiment, child care, revenge, and death.

The source emphasizes that the relationship is not merely triangular romance. Catherine's repeated "children" address, the initial sculpture image, Jules and Jim's rivalry, Jules's willingness to give Catherine to Jim, and Jim's mixture of love and withdrawal all make the structure part erotic, part maternal, part aesthetic, part familial, and part self-destruction. [[MoralSuspensionInArtReading]] is needed because ordinary judgment categories capture only fragments of the pattern.

## Key Claims
- A three-person relation can include friendship, desire, dependency, family performance, rivalry, and self-knowledge at once.
- Calling a story "immoral" or "free" can both miss the concrete costs borne by the people inside it.
- Jealousy can persist even when the social rules that authorize jealousy have already broken down.
- Triangle intimacy can become tragic when no participant can translate private freedom into a durable shared form.
