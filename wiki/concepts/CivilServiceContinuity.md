# Civil Service Continuity / 文官连续性

Civil service continuity is the institutional condition foregrounded by [[118-shi-dachen-xinla-fengci-yongbuguoshi-768665425]] through [[YesMinister|《是，大臣》 / Yes Minister]]. Elected politicians arrive, leave, change slogans, and answer to voters; permanent officials remain, remember prior commitments, control files, and preserve departmental knowledge.

The source's phrase "铁打的文官，流水的政客" captures the asymmetry. [[JamesHacker|Hacker]] has public legitimacy and reform ambition, but [[HumphreyAppleby|Humphrey]] has procedural memory, bureaucratic networks, and control over the practical path from intention to action.

The concept is not simply anti-bureaucratic. Continuity can provide stability and expertise. The satire comes from the way stability can become self-protection when officials treat elected change as a disturbance to be absorbed.

## Key Claims
- Permanent administration can outlast electoral cycles and therefore shape what elected mandates can actually do.
- Expertise and memory give officials power over timing, framing, and feasible options.
- A minister's formal authority may be weak when agenda control, document control, and implementation knowledge sit elsewhere.
- Continuity becomes comic and dangerous when preservation of order quietly becomes preservation of departmental interest.
