# Whistleblower Evidence Discipline

Whistleblower evidence discipline is the practice of testing one's own concerns, gathering specific evidence, preserving records, and escalating through available channels before a conflict becomes public. [[tsr-s4-tylershultzpart1-final-tsr-s4-tylershultzpart1-final]] adds the concept through [[TylerShultz|Tyler Shultz]]'s first chapter inside [[Theranos]].

Tyler says he tried to prove himself wrong before deciding the problem was real. He checked with coworkers, managers, friends, family, and a regulator; read lab regulations; wrote a detailed email to [[ElizabethHolmes|Elizabeth Holmes]]; preserved the email thread after [[SunnyBalwani|Sunny Balwani]] attacked his competence; and brought [[EricaChung|Erica Chung]] to speak with [[GeorgeShultz|George Shultz]].

The concept matters because whistleblowing can be attacked as disloyalty, incompetence, or personal grievance. Evidence discipline does not remove the personal cost, but it gives the dissenter a record that can survive gaslighting, family pressure, legal threats, and narrative reversal. In this episode, Tyler's printed email thread becomes a concrete example of preserving contemporaneous evidence before the [[WallStreetJournal|Wall Street Journal]] phase begins.

[[tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3-tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3]] extends the concept into source protection and legal survival. Tyler spoke to [[JohnCarreyrou|John Carreyrou]] on deep background, gave him specific documents and data points, refused to name other sources, refused to sign an affidavit he believed would be false, and eventually went on the record in a controlled way. The source adds a boundary: evidence discipline should be paired with independent counsel and [[ProtectedWhistleblowerChannels]] once legal pressure begins.

[[116-bufucong-de-yishu-fenxiang-yixie-shizhan-fangfa-haiyou-liliang-764416021]] adds a broader disobedience frame. Its academic-fraud and [[PatrickBurns]] examples show that whistleblowing may produce public value while still costing the dissenter status, work, and community standing; evidence discipline helps, but the source's emphasis is that truth-telling also needs allies and [[PsychologicalFlexibilityForDissent|psychological flexibility]].

## Key Claims
- Testing one's own hypothesis is part of responsible escalation, not hesitation.
- Specific data, regulations, dates, and written exchanges are harder to dismiss than generalized concern.
- Corroboration from another employee can matter, but it may not overcome a strong [[CredibilityCascade]].
- Preserving records before resignation can be decisive when leadership later reframes the event.
- Evidence discipline still needs institutional channels that can act; otherwise the burden remains on vulnerable employees.
- Source protection can be part of evidence discipline when the investigation depends on multiple witnesses.
- Refusing to sign a false or misleading statement can preserve the public record, but it may require legal support.
- The social cost of whistleblowing can persist even after the underlying concern is vindicated.
