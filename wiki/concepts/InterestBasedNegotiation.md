# Interest-Based Negotiation / 利益导向谈判

[[132-zhenglun-yu-shuofu-women-weishenme-chaojia-zenme-cai-suan-ying-807583318]] connects this "ask why" discipline to persuasion. In the HPV vaccine example and [[DeepCanvassing|deep canvassing]] sequence, the practical move is not to accuse the other person of ignorance first, but to find what fear, identity, confidence, or value the stated view is protecting.

Interest-based negotiation is the practice of asking what need sits behind a stated demand. In [[106-bie-zai-haipa-chongtu-kuai-lai-xuexi-tanpan-shu-742539645]], [[QinZong|秦总]] repeatedly returns to asking "why" before, during, and after the negotiation.

The episode's delivery-penalty example shows the method. A buyer asks for a high late-delivery penalty because delay would create large downstream losses; the supplier resists because emergency transport is expensive. Once both sides ask why, transportation becomes a new issue and the parties can search for a solution that lowers cost and increases trust.

## Key Claims
- Positions are often rigid because the underlying interests have not been stated.
- Asking why can reveal new issues that belong in [[MultiIssueNegotiation]].
- Information exchange reduces [[MotivatedBias]] and makes intent less speculative.
- Listening is treated as more important than eloquence because the negotiator cannot create value from interests they never discover.
