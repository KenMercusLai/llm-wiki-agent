# 宋代党争 / Song Factional Politics

Song factional politics is the source's frame for the new-party and old-party conflicts around [[SuShi|苏轼]]. In [[105-su-dongpo-yiqiang-haomai-zhiqi-yi-dupi-buhe-shiyi-741072645]], factional struggle emerges from real policy disagreement, personality conflict, court patronage, censorial action, and retaliatory reversal rather than a simple split between good and bad officials.

The episode centers this concept on the [[WangAnshiReforms|王安石变法]]. [[WangAnshi|王安石]] responds to serious state problems, while Su Shi and [[SuZhe|苏辙]] attack damaging execution and suffering among ordinary people. Later, when [[SimaGuang|司马光]] and the old party return, Su Shi also criticizes excessive rollback and retaliation, making the conflict a test of [[ConscienceOverFaction]].

## Key Claims
- Factional conflict can begin from substantive policy problems and still harden into personal and institutional punishment.
- Reform and anti-reform positions both need judgment about implementation, not only moral labels.
- Literary speech becomes dangerous when factional actors can convert it into evidence of disloyalty.
- A person caught between factions may be punished precisely because they refuse total alignment.
