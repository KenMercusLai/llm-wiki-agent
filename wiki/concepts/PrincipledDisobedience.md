# Principled Disobedience / 原则性不服从

Principled disobedience / 原则性不服从 is the central concept added by [[116-bufucong-de-yishu-fenxiang-yixie-shizhan-fangfa-haiyou-liliang-764416021]]. The episode defines it against mere contrarianism, trolling, self-display, or emotional outburst. A person is not valuable because they refuse; refusal becomes valuable when it is grounded in sincerity, contribution, evidence, courage, and willingness to bear social pressure.

The source's formula frames principled disobedience as deviation from convention multiplied by sincerity plus contribution, divided by social pressure. The exact expression matters less than the moral filter: a harmful or domination-seeking position does not become admirable simply because it violates the mainstream.

## Key Claims
- Disobedience needs a principle and a public or interpersonal contribution, not only a mood.
- Social pressure is part of the test because dissent that costs nothing may be style rather than courage.
- Sincerity is not enough; a sincere person can still be reckless, uninformed, or harmful.
- Contribution is not only macro-level reform: small everyday rule-breaking can also make social life less passive or gender-scripted.
- The source connects principled disobedience to truth, but also to method: persuasion, timing, allies, credibility, and emotional endurance decide whether truth travels.
