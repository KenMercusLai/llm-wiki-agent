# Lookalike Publisher Outreach

Lookalike publisher outreach is [[Responna]]'s tactic in [[he-demoted-his-saas-to-sell-a-service-and-4xd-revenue-in-12-months]] for expanding beyond the exact sources already cited by AI answers. [[FarzadRashidi]] says directly pitching already cited websites often has a very low success rate, so the company looks for publishers with similar authority, topical relevance, and keyword profiles.

The method adapts SEO-style prospecting to [[GenerativeEngineOptimization]]. Instead of only asking "which page ranks?", the team asks which kinds of third-party pages AI systems cite, what structure those pages follow, and which adjacent publishers could host fresher content that gives the client a credible place in the citation pool.

## Key Claims
- Citation research should identify patterns, not only a fixed list of target websites.
- The opportunity set expands when similar publishers can be found by authority, semantic relevance, category coverage, and content format.
- Fresh third-party content can imitate the useful structure of already cited pages while adding a current brand mention.
- The method depends on quality control because low-value content placement can pollute AI search and weaken human trust.
- Repeatable look-alike discovery pairs naturally with a [[PublisherRelationshipMoat]] when one provider can bring publishers recurring volume.
