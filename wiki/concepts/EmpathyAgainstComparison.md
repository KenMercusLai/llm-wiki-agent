# Empathy Against Comparison / 对抗比较的同理心

Empathy against comparison / 对抗比较的同理心 is the repair strategy in [[145-jidu-yu-bishi-wo-gai-ruhe-duobi-zhe-shuangxiang-de-dujian-882378647]]. The episode's closing claim is that envy and contempt are rooted in insecurity and anxiety, and that empathy becomes possible when the other person is made concrete enough to see their limits, effort, suffering, warmth, competence, and contribution.

The concept is practical rather than only moral. The source recommends information, reframing, praise, cooperation, and social design so that comparison becomes less total and less humiliating.

## Key Claims
- Knowing more about a person can reduce both envy and contempt because the target stops being only a status symbol or category.
- For envied people, sharing specific lessons, costs, failures, and usable paths can turn comparison into future-oriented hope.
- For people one is tempted to despise, concrete information can shift the feeling from disgust toward sympathy, respect, or at least restraint.
- Personal practice includes self-reliance, selective ignoring, self-support, and checking whether the envied goal is truly worth wanting.
- Team practice includes reducing unnecessary rank display, explaining differences, recognizing distinct abilities, and building shared-unit identity.
- Praise and congratulation are not cosmetic in this frame; they are a way to turn "you versus me" into "we can be related."
