# Veil Of Ignorance

Veil of ignorance is the Rawlsian thought experiment used in [[100-fa-lv-de-bei-lun-xie-tian-xie-di-xing-hao-wo-men-hai-you-luo-xiang-733477302]]. The episode explains it as asking people to design social and legal rules without knowing their own future class, identity, ability, power, or vulnerability.

In the source, [[JohnRawls|John Rawls / 罗尔斯]] is used against social-priority reasoning that makes scapegoating or sacrificing individuals appear reasonable. If the rule-maker might become the scapegoat, the abused spouse, the accused person, or the offender's child, then fairness cannot be judged only from the safe side of the rule.

## Key Claims
- Justice should be tested from positions people do not expect to occupy.
- The thought experiment makes weak people visible before policy and punishment are justified.
- Social welfare arguments are suspect when they quietly assume the decision-maker will never be sacrificed.
- The frame links legal design to humility about one's future social position.
