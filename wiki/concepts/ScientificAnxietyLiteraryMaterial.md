# Scientific Anxiety As Literary Material / 科学焦虑作为文学材料

Scientific anxiety as literary material names the source's pattern where new or unsettled science becomes story pressure. In [[141-zhizao-fulankensitan-kehuan-zhimu-he-tade-shenqi-shidai-857923931]], electricity, corpse movement, anatomy, spontaneous generation, and life-principle debate are not footnotes to [[Frankenstein|《弗兰肯斯坦》]]; they make the novel's creation scene exciting and frightening at once.

The concept also keeps the episode from becoming anti-science. Anxiety here is not proof that science should stop. It is a signal that discovery has social, bodily, and ethical consequences that literature can stage before institutions know how to govern them.

## Key Claims
- Scientific anxiety arises when apparatus and claims move faster than settled interpretation or social trust.
- Literature can preserve fear without turning fear into simple rejection of science.
- The anxiety becomes useful when it asks who is harmed, who is abandoned, who gets evidence, and who owes care after creation.
