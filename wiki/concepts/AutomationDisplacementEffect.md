# Automation Displacement Effect

Automation displacement effect is the labor-market mechanism in [[chef-vs-robot]] where robots take over tasks previously done by workers. [[DaronAcemoglu]] explains the concept as one side of automation's effect on jobs, contrasted with [[AutomationReinstatementEffect]].

The restaurant case makes displacement concrete. [[RobbyWokbot]] does not remove every human task, but it can shift wok work away from a skilled chef toward a lower-training operator role. That matters for [[GoodJobsForNonCollegeWorkers]] because middle-skill kitchen work can be squeezed even when the restaurant still employs people.

## Key Claims
- Displacement occurs at the task level before it appears as full job loss.
- Partial automation can still reduce the demand for a specific skill bundle.
- The source's cited robot research links higher robot exposure to lower employment and wages in affected industries.
- Displacement should be analyzed alongside [[AutomationReinstatementEffect]] because automation can also create complementary tasks.
