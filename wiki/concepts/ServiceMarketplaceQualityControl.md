# Service Marketplace Quality Control

Service marketplace quality control is the operational problem of making a distributed human service reliable, standardized, and trustworthy enough for repeat demand. In [[tsr-s2-adoracheung-v5]], [[Homejoy]] exposes the problem through cleaning: quality depends on tools, chemicals, sequencing, staffing, timeliness, and the provider's reliability.

The concept also explains why [[Instalab]] chooses a different labor model. [[AdoraCheung]] says she is done with the 1099 contractor model and uses full-time W-2 phlebotomists because the company needs more control over process, training, and the patient experience.

## Key Claims
- Marketplace liquidity is not enough when the purchased product is a high-variance human service.
- Founders may need to do the service themselves before they can judge provider quality.
- Labor classification and employment model can shape quality control, not only cost structure.
- Higher prices can make full-time employment and standardization more feasible.
- Quality-control weakness can turn early acquisition into poor retention.
