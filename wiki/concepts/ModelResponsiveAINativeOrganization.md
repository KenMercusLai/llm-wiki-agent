# Model-Responsive AI Native Organization

Model-responsive AI native organization is [[ZhangKuo]]'s diagnostic in [[e231-cong-b2b-dao-a2a-agent-xin-jijian-ruhe-rang-yiren-qiye-zuo-quanqiu-shengyi-0f4a2ab9-d3a0-41ad-8db1-6c03c851bd70]]: when a new SOTA model or agent framework appears, a genuinely AI-native product team should feel excitement or anxiety because the product can immediately become smarter or be disrupted. The dangerous reaction is indifference, because it suggests the product and organization are not actually built around model progress.

The concept connects [[AINativeProductDesign]] with [[AIOrganizationDesign]]. It is not enough to call a feature AI-powered; the system architecture, team workflow, evaluation, and product roadmap should be able to absorb better multimodal, coding, browser-use, computer-use, and long-context agent capabilities without each team restarting integration from scratch.

## Key Claims
- Excitement means the new model can improve the product's intelligence, precision, or workflow coverage.
- Anxiety can also be healthy because it means the product sits close enough to model capability that disruption matters.
- No reaction is a warning that AI is peripheral rather than structural.
- Product managers need to design toward model capabilities three to six months ahead, not only today's interface constraints.
- Legacy systems may need gradual architectural migration so new models improve many product surfaces rather than isolated demos.
- The diagnostic applies to individuals as well as organizations: people can ask whether they feel a meaningful change in their own work when agent capability shifts.
