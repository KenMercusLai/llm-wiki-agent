# Elaboration Likelihood Model / 精细加工可能性模型

The Elaboration Likelihood Model is the persuasion model used by [[132-zhenglun-yu-shuofu-women-weishenme-chaojia-zenme-cai-suan-ying-807583318]] to explain why facts work only when a person has enough motivation to process them deeply. The episode introduces ELM as a turning point in persuasion research because it asks whether the listener will stop, think, compare reasons, and update rather than merely receive a message.

In the source's practical frame, ELM separates public argument from personal persuasion. Public argument can accumulate available evidence; one-to-one persuasion has to create the conditions under which the other person becomes willing to elaborate.

## Key Claims
- Persuasion depends on motivation and processing depth, not only message quality.
- A strong argument can fail if the listener has no reason to engage it.
- Identity threat can lower usable elaboration by triggering defense before reflection.
- [[DeepCanvassing]] works partly by increasing motivation and lowering defensiveness before reasons are evaluated.

## Connections
- [[CognitiveInertia]] - low motivation keeps processing shallow.
- [[DeepCanvassing]] - method that tries to invite deeper self-examination.
- [[InterestBasedNegotiation]] - adjacent practice of asking why before trying to resolve conflict.
- [[MotivatedBias]] - reason evaluation can be distorted by what the person wants to preserve.
