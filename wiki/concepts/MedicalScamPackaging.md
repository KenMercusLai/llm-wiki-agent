# Medical Scam Packaging

Medical scam packaging is the pattern drawn from [[KaiShiDaJi|《开市大吉》]] in [[48-kaishi-daji-diyiliu-de-youmo-fengci-627375908]]. A hospital can wrap extraction in public-interest slogans, rented status symbols, newspaper visibility, emotional service, and authoritative performance until patients and families mistake appearance for competence.

The source treats the scam as two-sided. The operators design the package, but customers can also want the performance: cars, deference, status, and confident language satisfy anxieties and vanity before medical quality is tested.

## Key Claims
- Healthcare trust can be manufactured through signals that look adjacent to professionalism but do not prove care quality.
- Public-service language can become a sales surface when incentives point toward extraction.
- Status display works because patients and families often face uncertainty, fear, and asymmetric knowledge.
- The concept is a literary predecessor to modern platform, advertising, and search-mediated healthcare trust crises.
