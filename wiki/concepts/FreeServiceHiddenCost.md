# Free Service Hidden Cost / 免费服务隐性成本

Free service hidden cost is the episode's warning that a zero cash price can move cost into time, attention, emotion, quality, privacy, or later purchases. In [[155-meimao-neng-dang-fan-chi-ma-xiang-zhuanqian-gai-zuo-dian-sha-jieju-shi-ying-bimian-shenme-xingwei-jingjixue-siwei-you-shenme-yong-920167210]], the example is a free film with ads, lag, poor resolution, or missing ending: the user pays less cash but may spend more life.

The point is not that paid options are always better. The source asks consumers to compare the whole transaction, including hidden friction and [[OpportunityCost]], before declaring that the cheapest visible price is the most economical choice.

## Key Claims
- Free can be expensive when the missing cash payment is replaced by attention, frustration, wasted time, or degraded experience.
- A true bargain should be judged by total cost and value, not only price tag.
- Hidden costs become easier to see when the buyer uses [[MentalAccounting]] and [[CostBenefitThinking]] before entering the offer.
- The concept helps separate real subsidies from disguised extraction.
