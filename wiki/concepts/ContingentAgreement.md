# Contingent Agreement / 权变协议

Contingent agreement is a negotiation structure that ties later payment, concession, or adjustment to an observable future outcome. In [[106-bie-zai-haipa-chongtu-kuai-lai-xuexi-tanpan-shu-742539645]], it appears in the television-series example: if next year's ratings are low, one side refunds money; if they are high, the other side pays more.

The episode treats contingent agreements as a way to handle uncertainty and disagreement about the future. They can also function as post-settlement agreements, allowing parties who already reached a deal to ask whether another structure would make both sides better off.

## Key Claims
- A contingent clause can convert disagreement about future probability into a conditional exchange.
- The trigger must be observable enough that neither side can easily manipulate it.
- Bad incentive design can backfire if one party benefits from suppressing the measured outcome.
- Contingent terms pair naturally with [[MultiIssueNegotiation]] because they add time and risk as negotiable issues.
