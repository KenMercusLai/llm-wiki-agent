# 明教 / 摩尼教在中国

明教 / 摩尼教在中国 is the religious-history node added by [[112-xianliao-jinyong-diyidan-mingjiao-jingran-jintian-hai-cunzai-754593095]]. The episode begins from [[JinYong|金庸]]'s fictional 明教 and 日月神教, then points toward historical Manichaean survivals in Fujian and the [[Quanzhou|泉州]] region as a reminder that the novels borrow from real religious layers rather than pure invention.

The source treats "明教 still exists" cautiously and locally. The claim is not that Jin Yong's fictional sect survives as a martial organization, but that temples, worship of 光明佛/摩尼, and local reinterpretation can preserve Manichaean material after the original doctrine has been absorbed into Buddhist, Daoist, and folk-religion settings.

This makes the concept a bridge between [[ChineseFolkReligionLayering]] and [[WuxiaIntertextualCraft]]. In religious practice, a foreign or heterodox tradition can be localized and renamed; in fiction, that layered memory can become a sect genealogy, a political label, or a transformed antagonist like [[XiaoAoJiangHu|《笑傲江湖》]]'s 日月神教.

## Key Claims
- 明教 in Jin Yong is fictional, but it draws force from actual Manichaean traces in Chinese religious history.
- Local survival does not require doctrinal continuity; ritual, deity names, temples, images, and reinterpretation can preserve fragments.
- Fujian and [[Quanzhou|泉州]] matter because maritime and local religious layering make Manichaean remains plausible in the source's frame.
- Fictional sect history becomes richer when read beside real religious syncretism and suppression.
