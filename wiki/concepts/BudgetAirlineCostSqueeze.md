# Budget Airline Cost Squeeze

Budget airline cost squeeze is the pressure described in [[spirit-airlines-and-the-future-of-cheap-flights]] when a low-fare airline faces rising inputs and weakening demand from price-sensitive travelers. The episode applies it to [[SpiritAirlines|Spirit Airlines]] after the pandemic, citing higher energy, materials, labor, and pilot costs.

The squeeze is severe because the cheap fare is the product. [[HenryHarteveldt|Henry Harteveldt]] argues that budget airlines have less room to absorb higher costs without undermining their core promise, while some leisure travelers cut back on flying as household pressure rose.

## Key Claims
- Low-cost airlines can be vulnerable when inflation hits inputs they cannot easily avoid.
- Price-sensitive customers may be the first to reduce discretionary travel.
- Higher costs make the [[UltraLowCostCarrierModel]] harder even if planes remain operationally efficient.
- The squeeze can weaken [[AirlineMarketPriceDiscipline]] if budget carriers shrink or disappear.
