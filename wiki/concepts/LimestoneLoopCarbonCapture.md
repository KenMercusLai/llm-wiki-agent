# Limestone Loop Carbon Capture

Limestone loop carbon capture is the [[DirectAirCapture]] process described by [[ChristianToya]] at [[HeirloomCarbonTechnologies|Heirloom Carbon Technologies]] in [[tech-20260713-tech-pod-128-tech-20260713-tech-pod-128]]. The source says Heirloom heats crushed limestone in a kiln, separates it into CO2 and calcium oxide powder, removes and stores the CO2, then lets the powder absorb CO2 from ambient air and turn back into limestone.

The process matters because it makes carbon removal cyclical rather than single-use. The same material is repeatedly calcined and re-carbonated, but the source keeps the economics open: the chemistry may work while the company still faces [[CarbonRemovalCostCurve]], energy-use, storage, and scale challenges.

## Key Claims
- The loop depends on moving between limestone and calcium oxide so the material can repeatedly bind atmospheric CO2.
- The captured CO2 must still be permanently stored or durably used for the climate benefit to count.
- Heating and handling material at scale makes the pathway an industrial operation, not only a lab chemistry concept.
- The process is one direct-air-capture route, not the whole carbon-removal category.
