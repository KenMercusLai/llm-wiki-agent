# Imperfect Action Practice

Imperfect action practice is the episode's practical answer to [[PerfectionismAsAvoidance]]. In [[129-ruhe-chengwei-zhenzheng-de-buwanmei-zhuyizhe-hunshen-shi-jiner-799220151]], it means choosing an action small enough to complete under real conditions: read two lines, write 50 characters, do one push-up, record with wind noise, put books on the shelf before finding the perfect taxonomy, or ask for a raise before the argument feels flawless.

The practice works by changing the unit of success. Instead of asking whether the result is ideal, the person asks whether they have moved from non-action to action. That makes it a cousin of [[LowCostExperimentation]] and [[ActionAgainstAnxiety]], but its target is the perfectionist demand that the first step already look polished.

## Key Claims
- Standards should be low enough that starting is harder to avoid than to do.
- A tiny completed action often grows naturally once the start barrier has been crossed.
- The method converts imagined risk into feedback from the world.
- Imperfect public action can increase trust when the person acknowledges limits honestly.
- The practice is most useful when paired with [[BinaryActionThinking]], [[FailureDesensitization]], and [[ConcreteSelfPraise]].
