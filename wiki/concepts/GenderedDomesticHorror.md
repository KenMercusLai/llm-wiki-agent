# Gendered Domestic Horror / 性别化家庭恐怖

Gendered domestic horror is the pattern [[117-zuzhoutu-guaitan-wenxue-ye-yao-shangzhuo-chifan-766574341]] develops most sharply through [[CursedBunny|《诅咒兔》]]'s story 《头》. The horror does not arrive from a remote monster world; it comes from toilet, hair, excretion, menstruation, pregnancy, marriage, childrearing, aging, and family members who repeatedly say the fear is nothing serious.

The concept matters because the domestic setting makes disbelief part of the horror. The woman in 《头》 is not surrounded only by villains. Her husband and daughter can be ordinary, functional, and even affectionate, yet still fail to meet the reality of what she experiences. That gap turns the monster into a form for ignored female fear.

The source also extends the pattern through 《不受家》, where violence first appears as extraction from a wounded fox and then repeats inside the family through children, blood, money, silence, and confinement. Domestic space becomes a place where outside exploitation is reproduced as inheritance.

## Key Claims
- Gendered horror can appear where bodily processes are treated as private shame rather than shared human reality.
- Family disregard can be frightening without requiring every family member to be drawn as a monster.
- Reproductive and caregiving roles can become uncanny when they erase the person expected to perform them.
- Domestic violence in guaitan form often works by making extraction literal: body, blood, labor, and gold become the same system.
