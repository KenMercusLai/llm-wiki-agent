# Dinosaur As Living Animal / 恐龙作为活着的动物

Dinosaur as living animal is the source's central alternative to treating dinosaurs as monster spectacle. In [[146-bu-ai-zhuluoji-shijie-kuailai-konglong-bihusuo-dang-siyangyuan-886472126]], [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] uses [[DinoSanctuary|《恐龙庇护所》 / Dino Sanctuary]] to ask what dinosaurs would be like as animals with bodies, senses, fear, attachment, illness, aging, and individual temperament.

The frame does not make dinosaurs tame or sentimental. It preserves danger, size, and uncertainty, but asks care questions before spectacle questions: what does this animal smell, fear, remember, need, avoid, learn, or suffer from? That puts extinct-animal imagination near [[AnimalWelfareAsPublicHealth]] and [[CaptiveAnimalCareLabor]] even though the animals in the manga are fictional living reconstructions.

## Key Claims
- A dinosaur story gains texture when each dinosaur has species traits and individual behavior rather than only attack patterns.
- Fear and hesitation can be as important as aggression because they make the animal legible as a living body under stress.
- Injured, old, weak, imprinted, or socially excluded dinosaurs test whether care is for the animal or for the human caretaker's fantasy.
- The frame turns dinosaur wonder from scale alone into a mixture of biology, behavior, ecology, and care.
- [[BirdDinosaurContinuity]] makes the frame easier to grasp because living birds keep part of the dinosaur lineage visible in ordinary life.
