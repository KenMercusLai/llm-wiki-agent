# Fallopian Tube Access Diagnostics

Fallopian tube access diagnostics is the [[NVisionMedical|nVision Medical]] product thesis in [[tsr-s4-surbhisarna-v3-tsr-s4-surbhisarna-v3]]. [[SurbhiSarna]] argues that if many ovarian cancers originate in the fallopian tube, then diagnostic progress may depend on collecting cells from that hard-to-reach location without surgical access.

The technical difficulty is central. Sarna describes the fallopian tube as narrow, twisting, and usually collapsed unless pressurized. The device therefore used a self-navigating catheter with a linear everting balloon that could unfold into the tube, collect cells, and retract into a sheath to reduce contamination.

The concept is a medical-device version of [[HardProblemMVPScoping]]. The first goal was not to solve all women's-health diagnostics at once, but to create a safe access and cell-collection route that physicians could use and that [[MedicalDeviceClinicalValidation]] could support.

## Key Claims
- A diagnostic product may need to solve access before it can solve detection.
- The device has to be designed around real anatomy, hand use, screen use, contamination risk, and physician workflow.
- Site-of-origin reasoning can turn a broad cancer-screening ambition into a narrower technical target.
- Regulatory and clinical validation decide whether the access route becomes a usable medical claim rather than only an elegant mechanism.
