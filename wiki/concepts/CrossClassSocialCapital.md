# Cross-Class Social Capital

Cross-class social capital is the opportunity value created when low-income children or families build real ties with higher-income peers, families, and institutions. [[can-transforming-neighborhoods-help-kids-escape-poverty]] treats it as the likely mechanism behind the HOPE VI gains: children benefited most when revitalized developments were near more affluent neighborhoods and when social interaction crossed class lines.

The episode presents several possible channels through [[RajChetty]]: job or internship access, information about college and careers, and aspirations shaped by seeing different adult paths up close. It also uses [[Facebook]] connection data as evidence that the relevant effect was social integration rather than architecture alone.

## Key Claims
- Opportunity can move through relationships, not only through income transfers or physical infrastructure.
- Higher-income peers and families may supply nonredundant information about school, jobs, and adult possibilities.
- Aspirations may change when children repeatedly see plausible examples outside concentrated poverty.
- Social connection is difficult to infer from building design alone; network evidence matters.
