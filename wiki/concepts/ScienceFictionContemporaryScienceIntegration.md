# Science Fiction As Contemporary Science Integration / 科幻作为当代科学整合

Science fiction as contemporary science integration is the reading frame added by [[141-zhizao-fulankensitan-kehuan-zhimu-he-tade-shenqi-shidai-857923931]]. The episode argues that [[Frankenstein|《弗兰肯斯坦》]] should not be mocked for failing later scientific standards; its science-fiction force comes from absorbing the frontiers, uncertainties, and public anxieties of [[MaryShelley|Mary Shelley / 玛丽·雪莱]]'s own age.

This frame differs from simple prediction. The novel matters because it brings [[AnimalElectricityDebate]], [[VitalismLifePrincipleDebate]], [[SpontaneousGenerationLifeOrigin]], [[AlchemyChemistryContinuity]], anatomy, corpse supply, polar exploration, and [[ElectricTherapySpeculation]] into one literary problem of making life and then being responsible for it.

## Key Claims
- A work can be science-fictionally serious without being scientifically correct by later standards.
- The key test is whether the speculative mechanism is grounded in live knowledge, uncertainty, apparatus, and anxiety from its own historical moment.
- Scientific material becomes literary when it changes responsibility, fear, desire, and social relation inside the story.
