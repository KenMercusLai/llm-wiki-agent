# Greenland Strategic Access

Greenland strategic access is the defense and geography problem in [[how-to-get-what-greenland-has-with-permission]]. The episode says [[Greenland]] matters because it sits near Arctic shipping routes and between the [[UnitedStates]], [[Russia]], and [[China]], with missile-defense and early-warning implications.

The concept differs from mineral access. The source says Greenland's rare earths would be useful but difficult to reach, while [[DonaldTrump]]'s stronger stated argument is strategic location. That makes [[MilitaryBaseSovereignty]] and defense agreements more relevant than mining ownership alone.

The episode also says the U.S. already has a large military presence in Greenland and has had extensive defense access since 1951. Because Greenland is tied to [[Denmark]] and [[NATO]], the source frames the practical question as how to use and expand permission-based access without damaging the alliance structure that already helps make Greenland defensible.
