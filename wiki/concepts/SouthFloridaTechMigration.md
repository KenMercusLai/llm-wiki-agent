# South Florida Tech Migration

South Florida tech migration is the pattern in [[tech-20260220-0220-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260220-0220-mp-tech-pod-128]] where technology and finance companies treat [[Miami]] and nearby [[Florida]] markets as an alternative center of gravity. The episode uses [[Palantir]]'s headquarters move, [[ServiceNow]] office expansion, and [[Citadel]]'s earlier headquarters move as examples.

The source frames the pattern as a mix of taxes, regulation, culture, and political proximity rather than a single cost-saving story. [[TaxFoundation]] data complicates a purely corporate-tax explanation, while [[AlexKarp]]'s earlier Palantir memo and the episode's discussion of South Florida's political ties make the move symbolic as well as administrative.

## Key Claims
- A headquarters move can be strategic signaling even before large numbers of employees relocate.
- Lower-tax and lower-regulation perceptions matter, but they may not fully explain moves when the origin state also has below-average corporate tax rates.
- Cultural distance from Silicon Valley can be part of the message for companies that want to distinguish themselves from mainstream tech politics.
- Political proximity can be an advantage or a signal, especially for companies with government-facing or regulation-sensitive businesses.
- The value of a migration destination can compound if enough firms create a local executive, investor, employee, and service-provider network.
