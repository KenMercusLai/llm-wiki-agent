# Piabero Economy

The piabero economy is the wild ornamental-fish livelihood system described in [[the-little-pet-fish-that-saved-a-town-in-the-amazon]]. Around [[Barcelos]], local fishers such as [[DecoBarcelos]] catch small aquarium fish in the flooded forest, brokers such as [[AramaraCastro]] sort and move them downriver, and exporters connect the fish to global pet markets.

The concept matters because it joins culture, conservation, and supply chain. The [[CardinalTetra]] is a low-margin commodity fish, a civic symbol, and a possible basis for [[SustainableWildHarvesting]] when local income keeps the [[RioNegro]] flooded forest valuable as habitat rather than as cleared land.

## Key Claims
- The economy depends on wild collection, local sorting, downriver export, and global aquarium demand.
- Small per-fish payments can still support households when order volume is high.
- The trade gave [[Barcelos]] public identity through festivals, symbols, and local pride.
- Farmed competition reduced orders and pushed workers toward tourism jobs.
- Its survival now depends on quality control, reliable export, traceability, and consumer willingness to value wild origin.

## Connections
- [[Barcelos]], [[RioNegro]], [[CardinalTetra]], [[DecoBarcelos]], and [[AramaraCastro]] - source system.
