# AI Information Pollution

[[tech-20260105-0105-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260105-0105-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds the election-participation version. [[TimHarper]] warns that AI-generated messages could mislead voters about the time, place, and manner of voting, especially when public voter-file details make false information feel personally credible. He also flags search poisoning, where AI-generated content could push incorrect or outdated voting information into results.

[[tech-20260121-0121-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260121-0121-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds the general media-trust version through [[AvivOvadia]]. The source frames AI information pollution as an [[InformationApocalypse]] risk: synthetic media, scams, propaganda, nonconsensual imagery, and emotionally targeted feedback loops make both fake content and real evidence harder to evaluate, creating an [[AIRealityVerificationTax]] and possible [[RealityApathy]].

[[tech-20260122-0122-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260122-0122-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds the concrete hoax-evidence version through [[CaseyNewton]]. The source shows that pollution can include fabricated badges and institutional-looking documents, not only false posts or generic slop; the added cost is that journalists and readers must authenticate supporting evidence before accepting a story.

AI information pollution is the episode's extension of Sagan-style skepticism into a world where synthetic text, images, audio, and video lower the cost of persuasive false evidence. [[185-mogui-chumo-de-shijie-guanyu-yinmoulun-ufo-waixingren-bangjia-an-maitianquan-x-dangan-jiemi-ri-996181368]] argues that older UFO and pseudoscience mechanisms may become newly powerful when fabrication is cheaper and media attention is shorter.

The concept connects this book episode to the wiki's broader AI literacy branch. The same public that depends on technical systems also needs [[ScientificSkepticism]], source checking, and [[EvidenceOverTestimony]] to avoid treating plausible media as proof.

## Key Claims
- AI does not invent credulity; it scales old weaknesses in evidence evaluation.
- Synthetic evidence makes independent confirmation more important, not less.
- Sagan's warning about technologically complex societies becomes sharper when technical fakery is ordinary.
- AI information pollution can also make real material easier to dismiss, not only fake material easier to believe.
- A high verification burden can shift people from evidence discipline into [[RealityApathy]].
- AI-generated supporting evidence can make false claims look corroborated before anyone has authenticated the source, document, or media provenance.
