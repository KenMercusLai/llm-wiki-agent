# Sports Draft Incentive Design

Sports draft incentive design is the problem of assigning new-player rights without making losing too attractive. [[planet-money-vs-the-nbas-tanking-problem]] uses the [[NationalBasketballAssociation|NBA]] to show why the design is hard: if draft position rewards bad records, teams can rationally convert a weak season into a better chance at transformative talent.

The source compares several designs. A [[DraftLotteryTradeoff|lottery]] adds randomness, the [[DraftWheel]] removes season record from draft order, the [[GoldPlan]] rewards eliminated teams for winning, and [[NoDraftPlayerMarket|abolishing the draft]] shifts young-player allocation into negotiated markets.

## Key Claims
- Drafts try to protect [[SportsCompetitiveBalance]] by giving weak teams access to talent.
- The same rule can create [[SportsTanking]] when losing improves draft position.
- Randomness can reduce guaranteed rewards but cannot eliminate strategic losing when the prize is large.
- Mechanism changes redistribute costs among bad teams, fans, owners, star prospects, and small-market teams.
- A draft system should be evaluated as [[LeagueStakeholderAlignment]], not only as a fairness slogan.
