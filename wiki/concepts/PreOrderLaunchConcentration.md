# Pre-Order Launch Concentration

Pre-order launch concentration is the strategy of accumulating demand before publication so purchases count inside the launch-week window that matters for bestseller lists. [[how-to-make-a-book-into-a-bestseller]] adds the concept through [[RachelSalzman]]'s [[WWNorton|W. W. Norton]] launch plan for the [[PlanetMoneyBook]].

The source shows pre-orders as a legitimate version of timing optimization. Instead of buying books in bulk, publishers and authors use audience reminders, podcast appearances, newsletters, giveaways, and live events to concentrate real individual purchases into the week when rankings are measured.

## Key Claims
- Pre-orders matter because they can count toward first-week sales.
- Launch campaigns often begin months before publication to build a measurable opening week.
- Live-event tickets can include book purchases and route those sales through bookstores.
- Promotions can create operational risk if incentives are not fulfilled clearly, as the Planet Money poster issue shows.
- Pre-order concentration complements [[AuthorPlatformRiskReduction]] because an existing audience is only useful if it converts at the right time.
