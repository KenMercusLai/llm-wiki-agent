# Failure Desensitization

Failure desensitization is the source's practice of reducing fear by safely experiencing embarrassment, rejection, imperfection, or small public awkwardness. In [[129-ruhe-chengwei-zhenzheng-de-buwanmei-zhuyizhe-hunshen-shi-jiner-799220151]], the hosts argue that many feared outcomes are survivable: a rejected raise request, a declined romantic approach, a strange exercise in public, or an imperfect recording usually does not carry the catastrophic meaning imagined in advance.

This concept extends [[ActionAgainstAnxiety]] by making the feared sensation itself part of the learning. The person does not wait to become fearless; they try a bounded action, survive the discomfort, and update the imagined risk with lived evidence.

## Key Claims
- Many perfectionist fears are about symbolic meaning rather than actual cost.
- Repeated small failures can reduce the authority of imagined judgment.
- Desensitization works best when the downside is bounded and the person retains agency.
- The practice helps distinguish "this attempt failed" from "I am a failure."
- It can support [[ImperfectActionPractice]] by making rough first attempts less threatening.
