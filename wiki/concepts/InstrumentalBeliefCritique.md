# Instrumental Belief Critique

Instrumental belief critique is the source's nameable Lu Xun pattern: a doctrine becomes corrupt when it is adopted only because it is useful for eating, status, protection, or political convenience. [[75-gushi-xinbian-kulou-bei-fuhuo-hou-hui-ganji-zhuangzi-ma-681010087]] develops it through [[QiSi|《起死》]], "吃教," and the episode's broader claim that religion, science, revolution, loyalty, filiality, and public reverence can all be used opportunistically.

The concept does not mean that [[LuXun|鲁迅]] rejects all belief, science, myth, or revolution. The episode explicitly argues the opposite: Lu Xun knew science, valued myth and religious imagination as literary and cultural sources, and still attacked the "中学为体，西学为用" habit of treating knowledge systems as removable tools while leaving deeper motives intact.

[[QiSi|《起死》]] gives the literary mechanism. [[Zhuangzi|庄子]] uses [[QiWuLun|《齐物论》]] language until [[YangDa|杨大]] demands concrete compensation; the police and "隐士局长" then show how philosophical prestige can become social shield. [[GuoMoruo|郭沫若]] is used as a literary-politics example of the same "whatever works now" reversal.

## Key Claims
- A belief can become instrumental even when its original content is serious.
- Opportunism differs from pluralism: the problem is using doctrine to avoid responsibility, not learning from several traditions.
- Science can be instrumentalized when it is reduced to technical utility while inherited status values remain untouched.
- Religion can be instrumentalized when faith becomes a route to protection, respectability, or material gain.
- Political language can be instrumentalized when revolution, loyalty, or filiality are switched on and off according to advantage.
