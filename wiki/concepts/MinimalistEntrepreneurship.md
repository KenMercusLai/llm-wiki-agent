# Minimalist Entrepreneurship

Minimalist entrepreneurship is the small-company method associated with [[SahilLavingia]] in [[yi-ren-gongsi-de-lingyizhong-keneng-ai-fuze-jingying-renlei-fuze-reai-yingwen-fangtan-s10e14-33e95bf5-9dd2-45d7-9b5f-6e05a078f2d7]]. In the episode, it means starting from a real audience, customer, or manual valuable process, then automating only after the founder has learned what people will pay for.

The AI-era update is speed, not exemption from judgment. AI can help a founder build a prototype quickly, turn a manual sales process into software, or test more customer/problem pairs every two weeks. But the source keeps [[CustomerPull]], [[ProductLedWillingnessToPay]], [[FounderLedSales]], and [[HumanJudgmentUnderAI]] at the center, because a faster build loop still needs someone to decide what deserves building.

## Key Claims
- Minimalist entrepreneurship is not the same as refusing scale; it is a bias toward small, profitable, understandable operating systems.
- The method starts with a customer, audience, or manual process rather than with company formation or abstract AI capability.
- AI makes earlier building and iteration more plausible, but it does not replace trust, customer choice, pricing, support, or taste.
- [[OnePersonCompany]] is best read as one possible starting mode inside this method, not as the universal final company structure.
