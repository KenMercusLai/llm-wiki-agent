# 东方主义谜题误导 / Orientalist Mystery Misdirection

Orientalist mystery misdirection is the episode-144 pattern where a mystery uses vague "Eastern" atmosphere as a false explanatory frame. In [[TheCursedMummy|《被诅咒的木乃伊》]], the fake Kingsley talks about an Eastern curse, keeps mixed Asian objects, and turns a mummy transformation into a supernatural threat. The solution shows that the curse is a cover for ordinary fraud, corpse substitution, inheritance motive, and staged fire.

[[144-bei-zuzhou-de-munaiyi-fuermosi-hui-he-laoshe-lianshou-tanan-ma-878168738]] stresses that the criminals do not understand the cultures they exploit. Japanese armor, Buddhist-looking objects, Chinese and Japanese associations, and the false "此内61" reading all become props in a British fear fantasy. [[NatsumeSoseki|夏目漱石]] matters because his presence exposes that the so-called Oriental knowledge is confused rather than authoritative.

The concept extends the wiki's detective-fiction branch by making bias part of the clue field. [[SherlockHolmes|福尔摩斯]] must still solve the mechanics, but the reader also has to notice how racialized or exoticized assumptions make the wrong explanation feel plausible.

## Key Claims
- Cultural stereotypes can become misdirection when characters treat unfamiliar objects as proof of supernatural danger.
- Demystification requires separating actual material clues from the atmosphere that prejudice attaches to them.
- A cross-cultural witness can help reveal that an alleged cultural code is incoherent.
- The device is adjacent to gothic-rational Holmes patterns, but its false supernatural layer is specifically built from Orientalized confusion.
