# Animal Moral Projection

Animal moral projection is the habit of reading animal bodies and behavior as evidence for human virtues, vices, social types, or political lessons. [[114-youdao-chi-ao-shangju-shi-liaoliao-pangxie-de-quwen-shihua-758424117]] develops the concept through [[Crab|螃蟹]]: sideways walking, many legs, claws, mud habitat, "无肠", and crab-bucket behavior can all be turned into judgments about distraction, aggression, low character, mutual sabotage, or mutual support.

The episode's strongest counter-move is [[LiQi|李琪]]'s [[XunXieshu|《讯蟹书》]], where the crab answers human accusations and exposes human hypocrisy. That reversal keeps the concept from being only about allegory. It asks whether moral lessons drawn from animals reveal something true, something convenient, or only the storyteller's desire to make nature endorse a human argument.

## Key Claims
- Animal allegories are often unstable because the same animal can be used for opposite lessons.
- Moral projection can stigmatize an animal by turning anatomy into character judgment.
- Personified defense stories reverse the gaze: the animal can become a mirror for human cruelty, hypocrisy, or vanity.
- The concept sits near [[FoodAnimalWelfare]] when moral projection affects how humans justify eating, killing, or caring for animals.
