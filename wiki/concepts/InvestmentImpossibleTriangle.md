# Investment Impossible Triangle

Investment impossible triangle is the [[vol-101-ji-anquan-shouyi-you-gao-liudongxing-hai-hao-de-touzi-daodi-cunzai-ma-lr7xt-2cgoru2gfdbatvbiv78ixg]] frame that high return, high safety, and high liquidity rarely coexist in one ordinary asset. The episode treats this as a practical screening tool: before asking whether an asset is attractive, ask which corner is being sacrificed and whether the sacrifice is visible.

The concept sharpens [[InvestmentRiskManagement]] because it separates attractive yield from asset quality. A product that claims high return, reliable principal, and immediate redemption may be real only in a narrow time window, may hide volatility or default risk, or may be a [[PonziScheme]] or other [[InvestmentFraudRedFlags|fraud signal]].

## Key Claims
- Return, safety, and liquidity should be evaluated together, not as separate slogans.
- Safety includes both mark-to-market volatility and the chance of permanent or total loss.
- A low-volatility product can still be unsafe if the counterparty, collateral, or cash-flow source can fail.
- A liquid product can still be behaviorally dangerous if easy redemption encourages poor timing or frequent trading.
- Sacrificing liquidity can be rational when the investor is paid enough return or gains enough behavioral discipline.
- Sacrificing safety may be tolerable only when position sizing, diversification, and personal risk capacity are explicit.
- The triangle is relative to the interest-rate environment: a nominal yield that looks high in one era may be ordinary or weak in another.
- Apparent triangle-breaking assets should be tested through [[AssetScarcityPremium]] before being treated as permanent opportunities.
