# Clever Opportunism As Moral Risk / 鸡贼主义的道德风险

Clever opportunism as moral risk / 鸡贼主义的道德风险 is [[133-ibm-yu-nacui-weishenme-putongren-bu-ying-zanmei-jizeizhuyi-815684110]]'s closing concept. The episode argues that ordinary people should not admire "smart" accommodation to power, side-switching, loophole use, or strongman worship when those moves weaken the shared rules that protect people without power.

The source distinguishes this from practical intelligence. The problem is not being strategic; it is treating predation as wisdom and morality as naivete. In the episode's frame, [[ThomasJWatson|Thomas J. Watson]] represents the corporate version, [[AppeasementLogic|appeasement logic]] represents the geopolitical version, and [[ReneCarmille|Rene Carmille]] shows the counterexample: cleverness can serve protection and resistance rather than self-excusing collaboration.

## Key Claims
- Ordinary people have material interests in moral norms because those norms restrain arbitrary power.
- Admiring predation trains people to surrender the rules they may later need.
- "Realism" becomes morally corrupt when it only notices the strong and discounts the weak.
- Strategic intelligence is ethically usable when it protects people, preserves truth, or resists coercive systems.
- The source's anti-opportunism point links public history to everyday political language.
