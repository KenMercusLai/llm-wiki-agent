# 春点 / Chun Dian Argot

春点 is the Jianghu argot explained in [[102-jianghu-congtan-pianshu-heihua-he-minjian-daoyi-736516488]]. The episode presents it as a large insider language rather than a small codebook: it identifies people, hides trade information, coordinates action, marks rank, protects secrets, and lets participants in [[StreetJianghuSociety]] decide who is inside or outside.

The episode's strongest point is that argot is ambivalent. [[ChunDianArgot|春点]] can enable [[JianghuScamCraft]] by letting collaborators pass information in front of victims, but it can also help performers ask peers for support, warn others away from conflict, or preserve a fragile livelihood system. Its secrecy is therefore neither simply criminal nor simply romantic.

## Key Claims
- Argot works as social infrastructure: it is identification, coordination, concealment, and boundary-making at once.
- Its value falls when outsiders learn it, which explains the episode's emphasis on secrecy and the taboo against speaking it carelessly.
- Some ordinary Mandarin expressions are presented as possibly carrying Jianghu argot traces, showing how hidden speech can leak into public language.
- The same mechanism reappears in modern [[JargonBoundaryMaking]], though the social position and moral stakes change.
