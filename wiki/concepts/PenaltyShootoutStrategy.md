# Penalty Shootout Strategy

Penalty shootout strategy is the combined tactical, statistical, and psychological preparation for deciding penalty kicks. [[how-to-win-a-penalty-shootout-with-game-theory]] frames it as a [[GameTheory]] problem: takers and goalkeepers must choose under uncertainty while trying to anticipate how the other side has prepared.

The source's central case is the 2008 [[ChelseaFC]] and [[ManchesterUnited]] Champions League final shootout. [[IgnacioPalacioHuerta]]'s report helped Chelsea at first, but the later [[EdwinVanDerSar]] and [[NicolasAnelka]] exchange showed that a team also needs [[StrategicUnpredictability]] after it receives good data.

## Key Claims
- Penalty strategy includes shot direction, height, run-up cues, goalkeeper tendencies, timing, and player pressure.
- Reports can improve preparation but should preserve a mixed plan rather than create a visible script.
- Modern preparation increasingly includes shootout simulations, crowd noise, crib sheets, and deliberate pacing before kicks.
