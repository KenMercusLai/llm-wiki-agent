# Tribal Truth / 部落真相

Tribal truth is the episode's name for beliefs that function as membership, safety, and loyalty inside a group. In [[132-zhenglun-yu-shuofu-women-weishenme-chaojia-zenme-cai-suan-ying-807583318]], conspiracy groups, polarized vaccine discourse, fandom conflicts, party identity, nationalism, and youth subgroups all show how a claim can become harder to revise once it carries belonging.

The concept is not just "people are irrational in groups." The source's sharper point is that changing a belief may mean risking friends, dignity, identity, and protection. That makes ordinary fact correction weak unless the person's social environment or reward structure also changes.

## Key Claims
- A belief can be socially true for the group even when empirically weak.
- Identity-linked claims are harder to update because abandonment can feel like betrayal.
- Outsider contempt can push people deeper into the small group that still accepts them.
- Physical and social mobility can create new evidence and new belonging, making belief change easier.
