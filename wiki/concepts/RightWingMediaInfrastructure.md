# Right-Wing Media Infrastructure

[[sp-05-liuqing-linyao-qiangsha-yibao-jutou-an-beihou-de-sikao-xia-fenlie-de-meiguo-hui-you-xianzheng-weiji-ma-790241978]] extends the concept from elite new-right outlets into local and ethnic-language information ecosystems. [[LinYao|林垚]] argues that [[DemocraticParty]] messaging problems are compounded when right-wing donors and media operators shape local media, Chinese-language circles, Spanish-language circles, and other channels that voters actually use.

Right-wing media infrastructure is the source's frame for outlets, newsletters, events, brands, and investment structures that let Thiel-aligned politics speak outside legacy liberal media. [[e233-guigu-youyi-de-quanli-wang-ruhe-xingcheng-liaoliao-bide-tier-de-sixiang-qimeng-pintu-2a529abf-3c14-4ae7-a0a5-fe1340a66c7d]] uses [[PirateWires]], [[MikeSolana]], [[AmericanGreatness]], [[ChrisBuskirk]], [[RockbridgeNetwork]], and [[1789Capital]] to show the layer between billionaire funding, online narrative, influencer culture, and formal politics.

The concept extends the wiki's politics branch because media is treated as infrastructure, not commentary. In the episode's view, the point is to create channels that can attack opponents, popularize grievances, recruit audiences, and coordinate a new-right ecosystem.

## Key Claims
- Media outlets become political infrastructure when they create repeatable narratives and audiences for movement politics.
- The source treats startup/VC brand work and right-wing media work as increasingly connected through people such as [[MikeSolana]].
- Influencer-facing investments and youth-oriented brands can reach voters that policy shops and traditional newspapers cannot.
- The sp.05 sequel adds that infrastructure can also operate through local and minority-language channels, not only national elite media.
