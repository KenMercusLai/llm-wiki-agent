# Founder Advocacy

Founder advocacy is the investor posture in [[tsr-s4-ronconway-v4-tsr-s4-ronconway-v4]] where [[RonConway]] says [[SVAngel]] acts as an advocate for founders rather than a detached capital provider. Conway uses the phrase to describe public defense, private help, and long-term relationship work.

The source's clearest public example is Conway confronting Kara Swisher after a tense on-stage interview with [[MarkZuckerberg]]. Conway presents the moment as a defense of founder fairness: powerful interviewers, investors, or institutions should not confuse accountability with treating founders ungenerously.

Founder advocacy connects to [[FounderFriendlyInvestorSupport]] but is narrower. It names the stance behind the work; support is the concrete execution through financing, introductions, mediation, distribution help, or crisis response. Later Conway sources extend the same stance into [[FounderCrisisMediation]] and [[EmployeeSeveranceAtShutdown]].

## Key Claims
- Advocacy can include public intervention when a founder is being treated unfairly.
- The stance depends on durable founder relationships rather than one transaction.
- Advocacy is not the same as agreeing with every founder decision; it is a commitment to help founders face problems directly.
- The concept scales into [[FounderFriendlyInvestorSupport]] when it becomes repeated operating help.
