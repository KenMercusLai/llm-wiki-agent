# Spontaneous Generation As Life-Origin Frame / 自然发生论作为生命起源框架

Spontaneous generation as life-origin frame is the source's label for the old belief that some living things could arise without parents from mud, decay, or arranged materials. In [[141-zhizao-fulankensitan-kehuan-zhimu-he-tade-shenqi-shidai-857923931]], examples range from maggots and Nile mud stories to "homunculus" legends and [[AndrewCrosse|Andrew Crosse]]'s later electric-life claim.

The episode uses the frame historically. It does not endorse spontaneous generation; instead, it shows why electricity, chemistry, and biology could be joined in public imagination before microbial contamination and experimental controls were widely legible.
