# Public Housing Displacement

Public housing displacement is the policy harm created when redevelopment, demolition, or replacement reduces the number of public-housing units or pushes residents out of the system. [[can-transforming-neighborhoods-help-kids-escape-poverty]] makes this central to evaluating [[HOPEVIProgram|HOPE VI]]: the program demolished almost 100,000 public-housing units and rebuilt only about 55,000.

The concept keeps successful child-outcome evidence from becoming a simple redevelopment endorsement. A program can create [[NeighborhoodOpportunityRevitalization]] for children who remain in or enter a revitalized site while still imposing severe losses on families who are displaced.

## Key Claims
- Unit counts matter because mobility gains for some residents can coexist with access loss for others.
- Demolition-led reform should be evaluated against replacement depth, right of return, and who is excluded after rebuilding.
- Displacement is not a side issue; it changes the distribution of benefits and harms.
- Housing-quality improvement should be measured alongside [[HousingAffordabilitySupplyMechanics]].
