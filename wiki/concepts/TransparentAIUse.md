# Transparent AI Use

Transparent AI use is the practice of making AI participation visible enough that a teacher, collaborator, or reviewer can judge what the human did and what the tool contributed. In [[tech-20260115-0115-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260115-0115-mp-tech-pod-128]], [[ChristyGerdhary]] describes a writing assignment where students collaborate with AI and color-code which material is theirs and which belongs to the bot.

The concept differs from generic disclosure because it is process-oriented. The student is not only saying "AI was used"; the student is showing the boundary between prior writing, prompt-driven transformation, AI-generated material, human revision, and final meaning. That makes transparency a learning tool as well as an integrity tool.

Transparent AI use is especially important where [[AIDetectorBias]] makes automated suspicion risky. It gives educators a constructive alternative: ask students to make collaboration inspectable and reflective rather than depending only on after-the-fact detection.

## Key Claims
- Transparency should reveal process, authorship, and judgment, not only the presence or absence of a tool.
- Color-coding or annotation can turn AI collaboration into evidence of learning rather than evidence of cheating.
- Transparent use lets students compare generated transformations against their own goals and voice.
- The method works best when paired with assignments that require personal context, embodied work, or explanation of choices.
- Transparency can reduce reliance on AI detectors, but it still depends on clear classroom norms and teacher judgment.
