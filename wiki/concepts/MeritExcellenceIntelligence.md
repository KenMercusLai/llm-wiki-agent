# Merit, Excellence, and Intelligence

Merit, excellence, and intelligence, or MEI, is the hiring and culture stance [[AlexandrWang]] describes for [[ScaleAI]] in [[tsr-s4-alexandrwang-v3-tsr-s4-alexandrwang-v3]]. Wang says Scale wanted to clarify that it would hire the best possible people, reward excellence, and reject the idea that meritocracy conflicts with diversity.

The source presents MEI as Wang's own explanation of a public company-culture post. It also notes that the post attracted criticism, became viral, and was sometimes politicized or misconstrued. For the wiki, MEI should be treated as a participant account of one company's culture position, not as a settled theory of hiring or diversity.

## Key Claims
- MEI is a culture-clarity tool: it tells employees and candidates what the company says it rewards.
- Wang frames the stance as diversity without "wokeness," while still saying no group has a monopoly on excellence.
- The concept acted as both a recruiting filter and a source of public criticism.
- The source connects culture clarity to scaling: [[ScaleAI]] grew quickly and needed more explicit operating principles as headcount increased.
