# Body Farm Forensic Research

Body farm forensic research is the controlled study of human decomposition under different environmental conditions. [[121-jijian-fayi-jianshi-shouce-yiqie-fanzui-jieyou-henji-775064343]] introduces it through [[WilliamBass]], who established the University of Tennessee research facility used to observe bodies in open air, shade, soil, water, vehicles, and other contexts.

The episode treats body farms as grim but necessary evidence infrastructure. They turn decomposition from anecdote into comparative observation, supporting [[ForensicDeathTimeInference]] while also proving why no single decomposition timetable can be applied everywhere.

## Key Claims
- Decomposition knowledge depends on environment: temperature, exposure, shade, water, burial, freezing, insects, and containment can all matter.
- Direct observation can improve forensic reasoning, but it also raises ethical and social discomfort.
- Body-farm data supports time-since-death estimates without making them exact.
- The research is an example of [[ScientificSamplingDiscipline]] applied to a difficult forensic question.
