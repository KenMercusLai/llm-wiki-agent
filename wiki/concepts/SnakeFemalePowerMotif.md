# Snake Female Power Motif

Snake female power motif is the source's frame for how serpent imagery repeatedly attaches to women, goddesses, wives, beauties, witches, monsters, and uncontrolled fertility. In [[128-xianliao-shier-shengxiao-zhi-she-cong-yemengjiade-dao-zhaolinger-797116305]], [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] moves from Fuxi-Nuwa and [[WhiteSnakeLegend|白蛇传]] to Medusa, Eve, snake-woman stories, "snake-scorpion beauty" language, and [[ZhaoLinger|赵灵儿]].

The source treats this association as double-edged. Snake-woman figures can preserve creative power, medical rescue, beauty, sexuality, courage, and self-directed action. They can also be downgraded into witches, temptresses, demon wives, monsters, or objects of clerical and masculine control. The motif therefore extends [[FemaleSelfPossession]] with a folklore-specific caution: symbolic power is not the same as social freedom, but demonization often reveals fear of agency.

## Key Claims
- Snake-woman stories often encode both admiration and anxiety around female generativity and autonomy.
- Later demonization of serpent women can be read as an attempt to make uncontrolled female power morally legible as danger.
- [[WhiteSnakeLegend|白蛇传]] shows the motif's internal split: goddess-like heroine, romantic wife, erotic threat, and subdued demon can coexist across versions.
- Pop-cultural figures such as [[ZhaoLinger|赵灵儿]] show the motif's afterlife without proving direct lineage.
