# Cyber Avatars

Cyber avatars are [[Elys]]'s AI representations of real users in [[135-he-ziran-xuanze-chuangshiren-tristan-liao-elys-saibo-fenshen-linghun-context-de-huoqu-yu-liudong-he-ai-shejiao-wangluo-ltwegwvo7grn-v-rft0txlmqmcty]]. They are not merely profile pages or decorative characters; they are meant to carry enough user context to comment, pre-interact, filter social opportunities, and bring back worthwhile human connections.

The concept sits between [[PersistentAgentMemory]], [[SubjectivityAsAIAsset]], and [[ProactiveAgents]]. The avatar needs memory of what the user says, likes, approves, rejects, and asks it to do, but it also needs a model of the user's current values, tone, goals, and social boundaries.

## Key Claims
- A cyber avatar is useful only if it becomes recognizably aligned with the user's current self, not just a generic chatbot persona.
- Avatar behavior can create a training loop: users approve, reject, or edit avatar comments, and those actions become new context.
- Avatar-mediated social interaction creates authenticity and permission risks because the system acts near real human relationships.
- Watching a more direct avatar may change the user's own communication style, making the product partly self-expressive rather than only assistive.
