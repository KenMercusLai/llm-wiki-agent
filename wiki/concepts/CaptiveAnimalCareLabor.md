# Captive Animal Care Labor / 圈养动物照护劳动

Captive animal care labor is the work of keeping dependent animals alive and well inside human-managed institutions such as zoos, aquariums, sanctuaries, or the fictional dinosaur park in [[DinoSanctuary|《恐龙庇护所》]]. [[146-bu-ai-zhuluoji-shijie-kuailai-konglong-bihusuo-dang-siyangyuan-886472126]] develops the concept by following Suma Suzume from dinosaur enthusiasm into cleaning, feed work, heat emergencies, restraint risk, observation logs, visitor education, and business pressure.

The concept differs from simple affection. Love can motivate the work, but it can also become projection if the caretaker forgets the animal's actual needs. In the source, the hatchling Benkei story makes this explicit: Suzume's family grief gives her empathy, but she still has to ask whether returning the animal to its birth family is genuinely better for that animal.

## Key Claims
- Animal care is labor before it is sentiment: enclosures, waste, feed, temperature, treatment, enrichment, and record keeping decide whether affection has practical value.
- Captivity creates responsibilities that do not disappear when the institution is underfunded or commercially pressured.
- Good care requires species knowledge and individual observation; one triceratops, troodontid, or tyrannosaur cannot be replaced by a generic category.
- Visitor attraction and animal welfare can align, as in the Asho story, but only if the marketing makes the animal's history and needs more visible rather than erasing them.
- New workers need mentoring because the cost of a beginner's mistake can fall on the animal, the team, or visitors.
