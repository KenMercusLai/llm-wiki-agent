# Startup Information Layer

Startup information layer is the pre-accelerator sourcing environment described in [[tsr-s4-ronconway-v4-tsr-s4-ronconway-v4]]. [[RonConway]] says he did not wait for deals to come to him; he stayed close to research analysts, journalists, conference organizers, newsletters, and trade publications to find where internet companies were being created.

The concept extends [[StartupCommunityInfrastructure]] backward. Before [[YCombinator]], [[HackerNews]], and social media made founder discovery more structured, early internet investors used analysts, conferences, publications, and platform-company hallways as an information network. In Conway's case, that network supported the [[InternetSoftwareThesis]] and helped him get close to [[Netscape]], [[AskJeeves]], and later search opportunities.

## Key Claims
- Early markets need information infrastructure before they need polished demo-day infrastructure.
- Analysts, journalists, conferences, and trade media can become deal-flow surfaces when a category is forming.
- Platform companies can act as physical and social hubs for the next wave of startups.
- The information layer rewards active curiosity and relationship maintenance more than passive inbound filtering.
