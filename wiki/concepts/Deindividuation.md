# Deindividuation / 去个体化

Deindividuation / 去个体化 is the source's concept for what happens when a person stops experiencing themselves, or the target, as a concrete individual and instead acts inside anonymity, group identity, arousal, or responsibility diffusion. [[123-renxing-shiyan-dangxin-ni-ziji-779479569]] connects [[GustaveLeBon|Le Bon]], [[LeonFestinger]], [[PhilipZimbardo]], a masked shock experiment, and a Halloween candy experiment to online abuse and collective cruelty.

The episode treats deindividuation as a risk condition rather than a personality type. Darkness, masks, crowding, group language, anonymity, and perceived righteousness can reduce self-awareness and make another person's pain less visible.

[[145-jidu-yu-bishi-wo-gai-ruhe-duobi-zhe-shuangxiang-de-dujian-882378647]] adds a comparison route into the same risk. When envy or contempt is shared inside a group, the target can become a status symbol, enemy category, or joke object rather than a concrete person, making [[InternetMoralTrial|online punishment]] easier to join.

## Key Claims
- Group membership and anonymity can change behavior without changing a person's underlying moral vocabulary.
- Responsibility diffusion makes small violations easier and can escalate when the group normalizes them.
- Online environments can reproduce deindividuation through avatars, handles, swarm language, and fast collective judgment.
- The most dangerous form is when discomfort is suppressed by a feeling of justice.
- Comparison adds another route: envy and contempt can reduce both self-awareness and target-specific empathy.
