# AI-Generated Hoax Evidence

AI-generated hoax evidence is fabricated supporting material, such as badges, documents, screenshots, or polished narratives, that makes a false online claim look professionally corroborated. [[tech-20260122-0122-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260122-0122-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds the concept through [[CaseyNewton]]'s investigation of a viral [[Reddit]] allegation: the suspect source supplied an official-looking badge and an 18-page document that appeared to confirm almost everything in the post.

The concept extends [[AIInformationPollution]] by focusing on fake evidence rather than only fake claims. Its danger is economic: what used to require unusual effort, design skill, or institutional access can become cheap enough that journalists and readers face more [[AIRealityVerificationTax]] before they can decide whether a story is real.

## Key Claims
- Polished supporting material can make a weak online claim feel independently verified.
- A document that confirms every accusation may be a warning sign rather than only corroboration.
- [[AIContentProvenance]] tools such as [[SynthID]] can help, but only when the generated artifact carries detectable signals.
- Hoax evidence can waste newsroom time even when no story is published.
- The reader-side defense is [[OutrageTriggeredSkepticism]], [[EvidenceOverTestimony]], and checking for independent reporting.
