# Carbon Removal Moral Hazard

Carbon removal moral hazard is the risk that technologies such as [[DirectAirCapture]] are used to justify continued fossil-fuel extraction rather than to supplement emissions reduction. [[tech-20260713-tech-pod-128-tech-20260713-tech-pod-128]] adds the concept through criticism of carbon removal, especially when oil companies support projects.

The episode's contrast is [[HeirloomCarbonTechnologies|Heirloom Carbon Technologies]] versus the [[Texas]] hub backed by [[OccidentalPetroleum|Occidental Petroleum]]. Heirloom says it will not take fossil-fuel investment, while critics still worry that the broader category is energy intensive and politically convenient for companies or governments that want climate action without directly reducing drilling.

## Key Claims
- Carbon removal can be useful only if it complements emissions reduction rather than replacing it rhetorically.
- Fossil-fuel involvement can create public skepticism even when a specific project captures real CO2.
- High energy use can weaken climate benefit if the capture process depends on dirty or scarce energy.
- Strong [[CarbonStoragePermanence]] and transparent accounting are necessary but not sufficient; public legitimacy also depends on how removal changes fossil-fuel behavior.
