# Mixed-Income Housing

Mixed-income housing is the urban-policy strategy of placing households with different income levels in the same development or nearby environment. In [[can-transforming-neighborhoods-help-kids-escape-poverty]], [[HOPEVIProgram|HOPE VI]] is presented as trying to transform isolated public-housing projects into communities where low-income children could encounter better-off peers and families.

The source makes the concept conditional. Mixed-income design matters only if it creates real [[CrossClassSocialCapital]] rather than merely replacing buildings or changing the visual form of a site.

## Key Claims
- Mixed-income housing was part of HOPE VI's strategy for reducing concentrated poverty.
- Street-facing homes, yards, open space, and resident income mix are presented as design choices meant to reduce isolation.
- The strongest evidence in the source points to social interaction with higher-income communities, not architecture alone.
- Mixed-income redevelopment can still produce [[PublicHousingDisplacement]] if the number of deeply affordable units falls.
