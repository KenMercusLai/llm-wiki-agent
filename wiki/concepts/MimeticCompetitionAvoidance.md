# Mimetic Competition Avoidance

Mimetic competition avoidance is the source's interpretation of how [[PeterThiel]] adapts [[ReneGirard]]'s mimetic theory into business strategy. [[e233-guigu-youyi-de-quanli-wang-ruhe-xingcheng-liaoliao-bide-tier-de-sixiang-qimeng-pintu-2a529abf-3c14-4ae7-a0a5-fe1340a66c7d]] argues that Thiel's dislike of competition and preference for monopoly can be read as a practical response to mimetic rivalry: if everyone wants the same thing because others want it, ordinary competition becomes destructive imitation.

The episode uses [[PayPal]] and [[Facebook]] as cases. PayPal appears as a conflict-and-merger example involving [[ElonMusk]]'s competing payments company, while Facebook appears as a network-effect case in which users join partly because other people are already there.

## Key Claims
- The source treats competition as a social and psychological pattern, not only a market structure.
- Thiel's "zero to one" logic is framed as a selective use of Girard rather than a neutral law of business.
- Network effects can turn imitation into product growth when a platform makes other people's presence valuable.
