# Reality Apathy

Reality apathy is [[AvivOvadia]]'s term in [[tech-20260121-0121-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260121-0121-mp-tech-pod-128]] for what happens when skepticism stops being evidence discipline and becomes disbelief in everything. In the episode, [[MeganMcCartyCorino|Megan McCarty-Carino]] asks whether generalized skepticism can turn into nihilism; Ovadia argues that institutions and commerce cannot work if people trust nothing.

The concept distinguishes useful doubt from civic paralysis. [[ScientificSkepticism]] and [[HumanJudgmentUnderAI]] ask people to verify claims, compare evidence, and notice uncertainty; reality apathy is the failure mode where the burden of verification becomes so high that people withdraw from shared reality or treat all claims as equally manipulated.

## Key Claims
- Skepticism can become harmful when it destroys all practical confidence rather than improving evidence evaluation.
- AI-generated media increases the risk by making plausible fakery cheap and ubiquitous.
- Reality apathy can create avoidable conflict because communities lose common reference points.
- Responses require [[AIContentProvenance]], verification tools, and stronger institutions, not only individual caution.
