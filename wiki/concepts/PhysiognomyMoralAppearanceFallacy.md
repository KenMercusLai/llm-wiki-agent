# Physiognomy Moral Appearance Fallacy / 以貌定德谬误

Physiognomy moral appearance fallacy names the source's claim that [[Frankenstein|《弗兰肯斯坦》]] challenges older attempts to infer character from face, skull, or outward ugliness. In [[141-zhizao-fulankensitan-kehuan-zhimu-he-tade-shenqi-shidai-857923931]], the creature's frightening appearance does not prove innate evil; his early behavior includes learning, help, longing, and attempted connection.

The concept matters because appearance-based interpretation justifies exclusion before evidence. The episode uses the creature to show how a false moral reading of the body can combine with [[CreatureSocialExclusion]] and [[CreatorResponsibilityEthics]] to produce disaster.
