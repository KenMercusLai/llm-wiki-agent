# Carbon Removal

Carbon removal is the climate-mitigation category of taking already-emitted CO2 out of the atmosphere rather than only reducing future emissions. [[tech-20260713-tech-pod-128-tech-20260713-tech-pod-128]] adds the concept through [[DirectAirCapture]], with [[HeirloomCarbonTechnologies|Heirloom Carbon Technologies]] as the concrete company example.

The episode frames carbon removal as necessary but contested. It says climate mitigation likely needs both emissions cuts and removal of existing atmospheric CO2, yet critics worry that removal can consume large amounts of energy, remain expensive, and become a license for continued fossil-fuel production if used as a substitute for emissions reduction.

## Key Claims
- Carbon removal addresses existing atmospheric CO2, while emissions reduction prevents additional pollution.
- Technical removal does not automatically settle climate politics because cost, energy use, storage permanence, and fossil-fuel involvement all matter.
- Corporate buyers can help create demand, but demand does not remove the need for credible [[CarbonStoragePermanence]] and lower [[CarbonRemovalCostCurve|removal costs]].
- The category is politically unusual because some projects retain bipartisan support even amid broader climate-program cuts.
