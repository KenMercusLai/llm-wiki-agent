# Performative Enforcement Accountability

Performative enforcement accountability is the source's warning that public oversight, lawsuits, or settlements may not change conduct if frontline agents perceive accountability as symbolic rather than real. In [[the-ice-hiring-boom]], [[SethStoughton]] expects lawsuits over immigration-enforcement tactics but doubts that settlement costs alone will motivate a change in tactics.

The concept matters because legal exposure and behavioral correction are different things. If leadership treats damages as a cost of doing business or sends permissive signals, officers may infer that formal rules are less important than political priorities.

## Key Claims
- Lawsuits can expose misconduct without automatically changing field behavior.
- Settlement costs may be a weak deterrent when leadership values aggressive enforcement.
- Official denials can become part of the accountability problem if they replace transparent correction.
- Credible accountability requires consequences that officers and supervisors believe are real.
