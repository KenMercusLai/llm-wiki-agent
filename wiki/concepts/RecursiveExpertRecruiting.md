# Recursive Expert Recruiting

Recursive expert recruiting is [[BlakeScholl]]'s early [[BoomSupersonic]] recruiting pattern in [[tsr-s5-blakescholl-v3-finalaudio-tsr-s5-blakescholl-v3-finalaudio]]. Starting with no first-degree aerospace contacts, Scholl found one second-degree [[SpaceX]] contact and asked each expert whom they would most want in the trenches, then followed that chain to build a talent map.

The method mattered because Boom's category had a severe credibility gap. Aerospace people had a term for unrealistic airplane ideas from internet founders, and Scholl needed experts to decide both whether the idea was serious and whether other serious people would join. The source says he also gathered candidates in a [[SequoiaCapital|Sequoia]] conference room so they could evaluate one another while signaling credibility to investors.

The concept is broader than referral hiring. It is a way for an outsider founder to traverse an expert network while using each conversation to improve technical judgment, identify trusted operators, and build enough mutual confidence for early full-time commitment.

## Key Claims
- In expert-heavy hard tech, the first recruiting job is often mapping trust, not only finding resumes.
- Asking experts for the people they would want beside them can reveal reputation that ordinary professional-network search misses.
- Candidate peer evaluation can create social proof for both hiring and fundraising when the founder lacks category status.
