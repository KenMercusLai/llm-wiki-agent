# Low-Cost Short-Cycle Validation

Low-cost short-cycle validation is [[CaoXudong]]'s operating principle in [[momenta-ipo-hou-zai-fang-cao-xu-dong-jiu-shi-xiang-zuo-mei-you-jin-tou-de-ai-1-172-1]] for keeping a hard-tech company from waiting years to learn whether a route works. Cao says [[Momenta]] tries to design technical and business paths where positive feedback arrives in roughly a week to a month when possible, and in about three months at the long end.

The concept is related to [[FastProductValidation]], but the source applies it to deep technical systems rather than only MVP demand tests. For Momenta, validation can mean a customer delivery milestone, a mainline architecture improvement, a data-driven model result, a VVP diagnosis, or an internal agent workflow that materially reduces delivery cost.

## Key Claims
- Long-horizon strategy still needs short-cycle evidence; otherwise the team cannot distinguish conviction from hope.
- Cheap tests are not casual tests: they must connect to the core route, customer value, and technical architecture.
- In hard-tech AI, validation often requires tooling and architecture work so that field problems can be reproduced, diagnosed, and fixed quickly.
- The principle supports [[AIOrganizationDesign]] because people, priorities, and team structure have to change when short feedback exposes weak assumptions.
- It also supports [[AutonomousDrivingDataFlywheel]] because each loop of deployment, data, training, and delivery must become faster and cheaper for the flywheel to compound.
