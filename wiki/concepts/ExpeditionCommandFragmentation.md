# Expedition Command Fragmentation

Expedition command fragmentation is the failure mode where a difficult mission has no clean authority structure. In [[120-lanhudao-weida-shiye-zhong-de-yexin-yu-xianshi-772998218]], the [[GreatNorthernExpedition]] is too large for simple command: [[VitusBering]], [[AlexeiChirikov]], [[MartinSpangberg]], scientific teams, academies, committees, Senate instructions, local officials, and rival factions all hold pieces of power.

The source's point is not that single-person command would have solved every problem. The Arctic and North Pacific environment remained dangerous. But fragmented command made bad maps harder to challenge, decisions easier to defer, responsibility easier to evade, and personal ambition more damaging inside a project already strained by [[ImperialExplorationLogistics]].

## Key Claims
- Shared authority can protect against arbitrary command, but it can also make accountability unclear.
- Scientific, naval, administrative, and local chains of command can produce incompatible incentives inside one expedition.
- Committee decisions can still be low quality when evidence is weak and no one owns the risk.
- Political protection matters: when Bering's court backers died or weakened, the expedition's internal rivals gained leverage.
