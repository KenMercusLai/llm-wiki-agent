# Galvanic Reanimation Imaginary / 伽伐尼式复活想象

Galvanic reanimation imaginary is the cultural pattern where electrical stimulation of dead or nearly dead tissue seems to promise the return of life. [[141-zhizao-fulankensitan-kehuan-zhimu-he-tade-shenqi-shidai-857923931]] builds it from [[LuigiGalvani|Luigi Galvani]]'s frogs, [[AlessandroVolta|Alessandro Volta]]'s apparatus dispute, and [[GiovanniAldini|Giovanni Aldini]]'s electrified bodies.

The concept explains why [[Frankenstein|《弗兰肯斯坦》]] could feel scientifically alive to its first context. It also needs [[ObservationBeforeInference]]: movement under current can be observed, but treating that movement as restored life, soul, or moral agency is a further interpretation.

## Key Claims
- Electrical movement made the boundary between life and death feel experimentally unstable.
- Reanimation imagery converts apparatus into gothic and science-fiction atmosphere.
- The concept is most useful when it preserves both the genuine observation and the overinterpretation risk.
