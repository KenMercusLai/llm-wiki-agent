# Self-Directed Work

Self-directed work is the work pattern where effort depends less on external assignment and more on whether the person recognizes the goal as their own. In [[ep119-duihua-xiao-sun-qixing-800-gongli-ba-ziji-jiuchu-shenyuan-ningyuan-meitian-gongzuo-22-xiaoshi-wo-ye-buxiang-zai-shangban-le-lmj5rmebef8y8p0ayfptqoqnhiu2]], [[XiaoSun]] says he can work extremely long days when building for himself, but becomes much less effective when doing employment tasks he does not believe in.

The concept is not treated as simple romantic freedom. XiaoSun also says that if the final goal is his own, he still has to do disliked intermediate work such as podcast editing. The practical distinction is therefore not comfort versus discomfort, but whether hard work is tied to a personally accepted direction.

## Key Claims

- Self-directed work can create unusually high effort when ownership, identity, and purpose are aligned.
- It can also create instability if "I do not recognize this" becomes a reason to avoid necessary coordination, maintenance, sales, or team commitments.
- A founder still needs to separate authentic conviction from immature communication, weak follow-through, or lack of commercial discipline.
- The pattern is strongest when paired with [[FounderProductFit]] and weakest when it ignores [[CustomerPull]] or [[FounderCashFlowConstraint]].
