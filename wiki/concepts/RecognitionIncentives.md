# Recognition Incentives

Recognition incentives are non-monetary signals that make effort visible and socially valued. [[the-laws-of-the-office-revisited]] introduces the pattern through [[AliceEvans]]'s Zambia health-worker example and the Planet Money office dishwashing trophy.

The concept matters because the episode does not treat motivation as only pay or punishment. A trophy can work when it marks real effort, makes the desired behavior visible, and gives people a low-stakes reason to keep the norm alive.

## Key Claims
- Recognition can motivate when it is specific, visible, and tied to real effort.
- Non-monetary incentives can avoid some payment distortions, but they still need credibility.
- Recognition works better as appreciation than as fake social pressure.
- In workplaces, recognition can support [[TruthfulSocialProof]] by showing that helpful behavior is actually happening.
