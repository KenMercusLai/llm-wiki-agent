# Constraint-Driven Media Production

Constraint-driven media production is the production discipline shown in [[the-business-of-heated-rivalry]], where budget limits become a design constraint for writing, scheduling, shooting, and crew care. [[JacobTierney]] says [[HeatedRivalry|Heated Rivalry]] shot all six episodes in 36 days, with scripts ready before prep and an effort to avoid punishing overtime.

The source does not romanticize scarcity as automatically good. Its sharper point is that low budgets work only when they are paired with preparation, collaborative problem solving, and a willingness to fix weak scenes instead of burning time through endless takes.

## Key Claims
- Completed scripts can convert budget limits into planning clarity.
- Block shooting can reduce waste when one director and one prepared team manage the season as a whole.
- Shorter days and fewer unnecessary takes can protect both workers and money.
- Constraint can support creativity when it forces decisions, but it becomes exploitation if the production lacks preparation or ownership upside.
