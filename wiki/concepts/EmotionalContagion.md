# Emotional Contagion / 情绪感染

Emotional contagion / 情绪感染 is the source's term for how another person's affect can enter a viewer or listener before reflective judgment catches up. [[123-renxing-shiyan-dangxin-ni-ziji-779479569]] discusses political-speech research where participants imitated emotional expression and where anger and fear were especially contagious.

The episode keeps emotion and belief separate but connected. A person may resist a speaker because of political identity, yet the body can still mirror charisma, fear, or anger before the person reclassifies the feeling as negative.

## Key Claims
- Emotion can travel faster than explicit agreement.
- Anger and fear are especially powerful public-persuasion affects.
- Political identity can reframe an initial bodily response after it happens.
- Media literacy therefore has to notice felt intensity, not only factual content.
