# Degree As Trust Credential

Degree as trust credential is [[KelentoHouTaiyu|Kelento 侯泰宇]]'s argument in [[e236-99-de-zuoye-doushi-ai-xie-de-dangdai-mingxiao-sheng-yanli-daxue-hai-shengxia-shenme-b066c769-00d2-4341-bf61-a82bca4181e4]] that university degrees do not disappear simply because AI weakens the scarcity of classroom knowledge. In his frame, a degree is a social signal: it tells employers, markets, collaborators, and institutions that a person passed a costly filter and can be trusted at some baseline level.

The source separates credential value from learning value. [[AIAsTutor]] can personalize explanations, [[ChatGPT]] and [[Claude]] can help students produce work, and [[AIDefaultLearningEnvironment]] makes tool use normal. But a labor market still needs low-cost screening, and a society still needs ways to assess reliability when generated work is cheap. That makes degrees a trust and evaluation layer, even if their claim to prove knowledge mastery becomes weaker.

## Key Claims
- AI can reduce the informational value of assignments, essays, and routine coding tasks without eliminating the market need for screening.
- A degree can operate as a trust credential even when the actual knowledge learned varies widely across students.
- Credential value depends on the perceived quality of the institution, peer group, admissions filter, and social network, not only on course content.
- [[AIUniversityAssessmentReform]] becomes necessary because credentials lose credibility if schools cannot explain what AI-era student work proves.
- Degree value can coexist with criticism of university: a student may attend pragmatically for the signal while using AI and outside projects for actual learning.
