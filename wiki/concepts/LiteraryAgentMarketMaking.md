# Literary Agent Market Making

Literary agent market making is the work of turning a manuscript or book idea into a competitive publishing opportunity. In [[inside-a-book-auction]], [[LauraNolan]] and [[JaneVonMehren|Jane von Mehren]] help [[PlanetMoney]] move from a vague book possibility to a developed proposal, publisher meetings, and a two-round auction.

The concept differs from [[LiteraryAgentJudgment]]. Judgment is about taste and conviction before demand is visible; market making is about packaging, access, relationship signaling, timing, and competitive process once a project is being sold.

## Key Claims
- Agents can discover or shape a book opportunity before an author has a finished manuscript.
- Publisher access is a filter: the source says editors generally do not read unsolicited proposals that lack trusted-agent sponsorship.
- Market making includes pre-seeding interest through conversations before the formal submission.
- A commission aligns the agent with deal value, but the source also shows agents managing fit, rights, structure, and urgency.
- Auction design is part of the agent's job when multiple publishers are interested.
