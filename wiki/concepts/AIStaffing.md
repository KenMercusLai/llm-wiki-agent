# AI Staffing

AI staffing is the role-based commercial model in [[e225-saasye-shuqianyi-shizhi-zhengfa-ai-ruhe-biange-zuzhi-jiagou]] where a provider designs, trains, and dispatches [[DigitalEmployees]] to fill a customer's work need. [[ZhangShaofeng]] distinguishes it from traditional SaaS because the buyer is not paying for seats or tools, but for labor-like capacity measured by role, workload, time, or completed result. [[e240-openai-lianshou-pe-zaxia-40-yi-meiyuan-liaoliao-guigu-zuihuo-xin-zhiwei-fde]] adds a PE and deployment-company angle: portfolio owners and model companies may need FDE-like teams to staff AI transformation across many workflows.

## Key Claims
- AI staffing sits between software and outsourcing: the provider supplies agent workers, while the customer evaluates outputs and integrates them into the business.
- It naturally pairs with [[ResultAsAService]] and [[OutcomeBasedAIPricing]] because the commercial unit is work performed rather than access granted.
- It still requires workflow diagnosis, training data, permissions, escalation paths, and human accountability before agents can be trusted in production.
- The model can retrain human staff into agent trainers, reviewers, workflow owners, or delivery operators instead of treating automation only as headcount elimination.
- E240 adds that AI staffing requires use-case sequencing and [[AIWorkflowTriage]], because a provider cannot staff a workflow with agents until deterministic, AI-suitable, and human-review steps are separated.
