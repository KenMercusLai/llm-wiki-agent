# Institutional Mechanism Satire

Institutional mechanism satire is the reading frame added by [[118-shi-dachen-xinla-fengci-yongbuguoshi-768665425]] for [[YesMinister|《是，大臣》 / Yes Minister]] and [[YesPrimeMinister|《是，首相》 / Yes, Prime Minister]]. The episode argues that the works remain sharp because their jokes come from repeatable institutional relations rather than only from corrupt individuals or witty lines.

In this source, comedy arises when [[JamesHacker|Hacker]]'s electoral mandate meets [[HumphreyAppleby|Humphrey]]'s permanent official control, [[BernardWoolley|Bernard]]'s split loyalty, [[ArnoldRobinson|Arnold]]'s senior bureaucratic strategy, the unseen prime minister, media pressure, voters, elite schools, and honours systems. Everyone has incentives that make sense locally, which is why the absurdity feels realistic.

This concept extends the wiki's English political-satire branch. [[GulliversTravels|《格列佛游记》]] uses travel fantasy, scale change, and impossible societies; Yes Minister uses filing systems, calendars, speeches, committees, titles, and official language.

## Key Claims
- Satire becomes durable when it exposes a mechanism that keeps working after the immediate political moment passes.
- Characters are funnier when they embody incompatible institutional incentives rather than one-note vice.
- A system can produce absurd outcomes without requiring every actor to be foolish.
- Administrative language, procedure, and etiquette can be comic because they preserve real power while appearing neutral.
