# Parkinson's Law

Parkinson's Law is the deadline pattern in [[the-laws-of-the-office-revisited]] where work expands to fill the time allotted for it. The episode traces the idea to [[CNorthcoteParkinson|C. Northcote Parkinson]] and then tests it by asking [[AlexiHorowitzGhazi]] to report and produce a segment in one day instead of a week.

The source does not treat the law as a universal excuse for speed. [[MengZhu]]'s segment gives it a design boundary: shorter deadlines and fast-completion rewards can reduce unnecessary expansion when the task can genuinely be completed well under constraint.

## Key Claims
- Time available can change perceived task size, coordination overhead, and polishing behavior.
- Short deadlines can force prioritization, but only when quality and safety do not require more time.
- Longer deadlines can invite scope expansion even when the core work is already sufficient.
- Deadline design is part of [[WorkplaceIncentiveDesign]], not only personal productivity advice.
