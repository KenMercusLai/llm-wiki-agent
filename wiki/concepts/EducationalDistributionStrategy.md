# Educational Distribution Strategy

Educational distribution strategy is the publishing plan to move a book through classrooms, courseware, syllabi, or textbook-adjacent channels rather than relying only on ordinary trade promotion. [[inside-a-book-auction]] adds the concept through [[WWNorton|W. W. Norton]]'s winning pitch for the [[PlanetMoney]] book.

The episode says a Big Five publisher offered more upfront money, publicity, airport distribution, and independent-bookstore reach. Norton offered slightly less but proposed full color and a path into educational contexts, which mattered because [[AlexGoldmark]] saw Planet Money's mission as helping people understand the economy.

## Key Claims
- Distribution strategy can be a source of publisher fit, not only a back-office sales plan.
- Educational channels can extend a book's life by making it useful to teachers, students, and courses.
- A lower advance can be rational if the alternative publisher has a stronger mission-aligned channel.
- Format choices such as full color can reinforce the educational use case.
