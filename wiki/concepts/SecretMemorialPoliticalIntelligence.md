# 密折政治情报 / Secret Memorial Political Intelligence

密折政治情报 / secret memorial political intelligence is the source's frame for direct reporting from trusted servants to the emperor. In [[101-caoyin-yu-kangxi-hongloumeng-xing-zhong-you-shi-735044876]], [[CaoYin|曹寅]] begins sending secret memorials to [[KangxiEmperor|康熙]] after the emperor reduces direct southern travel, turning him into an information channel for local rumor, official behavior, possible unrest, and social mood.

The episode's examples make the intelligence function practical rather than cinematic. Cao Yin investigates rumors around figures such as Xiong Cilu, reports rice prices and famine conditions, and handles public rumor after ground collapse by letting people see the site instead of sealing it off. These details connect [[ImperialBondservantGovernance]] to information management: a trusted household servant could be useful because he knew what the emperor wanted to know and how local opinion moved.

## Key Claims
- Secret memorials bypassed ordinary reporting channels and made trust a direct political resource.
- Local information included gossip, price signals, disaster response, official conduct, and possible rebellion, not only formal policy.
- Information work helped replace the emperor's personal travel after repeated southern tours became less frequent.
- Cao Yin's ability to manage rumor and report context was part of why he was valuable beyond textile administration.
