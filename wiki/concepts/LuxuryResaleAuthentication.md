# Luxury Resale Authentication

Luxury resale authentication is the expert and operational process by which a marketplace persuades buyers that a used high-end item is genuine. In [[seven-allegedly-fake-chanel-bags-vs-the-realreal]], [[TheRealReal]] uses authentication to make discounted secondhand Chanel bags buyable online despite [[AsymmetricInformation]] and counterfeit risk.

The source's practical authentication frame comes from [[GrahamWetzbarger]]: materials, construction, hardware components, and brand identifiers all matter. The process is not just a checklist; it combines touch, visual pattern knowledge, serial-number interpretation, and experience with how a brand actually makes objects.

## Key Claims
- Authentication is market infrastructure because buyers will not pay luxury resale prices if they fear fakes.
- Human expertise remains central even when proprietary databases or AI tools help triage items.
- Strong authentication claims can become legal risk when a brand alleges even a small number of counterfeits.
- Luxury resale authentication sits between buyer protection and incumbent brand control, which is why it leads into [[TrademarkResaleBoundary]].
