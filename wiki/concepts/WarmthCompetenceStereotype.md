# Warmth-Competence Stereotype / 热情-能力刻板印象

Warmth-competence stereotype / 热情-能力刻板印象 is the episode's two-axis way of describing how people simplify others. In [[145-jidu-yu-bishi-wo-gai-ruhe-duobi-zhe-shuangxiang-de-dujian-882378647]], the host explains that stereotype reduction can work by restoring the dimension the audience has left out: a high-competence target may need to be seen as warm and concrete, while a low-status target may need to be seen as capable, agentic, and situated.

The concept helps turn empathy into a practical cognition problem. More information is not useful only because it is sentimental; it breaks the flat category that made envy, contempt, pity, or suspicion feel automatic.

## Key Claims
- Stereotypes often compress people into one missing dimension: rich, poor, elite, immigrant, rural, professional, beautiful, or unsuccessful can become too simple to be human.
- Envy is easier when a high-status person is seen as competent but cold, undeserving, or extractive.
- Contempt is easier when a lower-status person is seen as neither capable nor socially warm.
- Concrete stories can restore missing warmth, competence, constraint, effort, and contribution.
- The episode treats this as one route from reflexive feeling toward [[EmpathyAgainstComparison]].
