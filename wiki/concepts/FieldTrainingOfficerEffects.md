# Field Training Officer Effects

Field training officer effects are the durable behavioral changes created when new officers learn from experienced field mentors. In [[the-ice-hiring-boom]], [[MatthewRoss]] says his research using [[DallasPoliceDepartment]] data found that recruits paired with more aggressive field training officers were more likely to use force for at least three years.

The concept shifts attention away from academy hours alone. The episode uses Ross's research to argue that the first real-world trainers, supervisors, and peers may shape conduct more powerfully than formal classroom material.

## Key Claims
- Field mentors transmit norms through example, approval, and correction during real encounters.
- Aggressive field training can have measurable effects beyond the initial training period.
- Formal restraint instruction can weaken if field mentors reward contrary behavior.
- Workforce surges heighten this risk because many new recruits enter the same field-culture pipeline at once.
