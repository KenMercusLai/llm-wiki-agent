# Founder Health Debt

Founder health debt is [[AdoraCheung]]'s analogy in [[tsr-s2-adoracheung-v5]] between technical debt and accumulated personal health problems from startup intensity. After [[Homejoy]], she describes weight gain, high lipids, high cholesterol, hypertension, metabolic problems, convenience eating, and heavy caffeine use.

The concept matters because it reframes founder health as an operating liability, not only a lifestyle preference. Problems can accumulate invisibly while the company is the top priority, then become harder to reverse later. In the source, this experience becomes the founder-product-fit basis for [[Instalab]] and [[AtHomePreventiveHealth]].

[[tsr-s5-tomblomfield-v2-audio-tsr-s5-tomblomfield-v2-audio]] adds [[TomBlomfield]]'s mental-health and recovery version through [[Monzo]]. Tom describes insomnia, anxiety, depression, impaired memory, and decision difficulty during the COVID-era fundraising crisis, then says recovery took three or four years. This extends founder health debt from biomarkers and lifestyle to cognitive and emotional capacity under [[RegulatedFintechCapitalPressure]].

## Key Claims
- Startup intensity can hide health deterioration until biomarkers or symptoms make it visible.
- Health debt compounds because delayed awareness can make later correction harder.
- Founder health products have to fit actual busy schedules, not idealized wellness routines.
- Preventive measurement is useful when it leads to small, realistic behavior changes and repeat feedback.
- Founder health debt can directly impair decision quality, making leadership transition part of the operating response rather than only a personal choice.
