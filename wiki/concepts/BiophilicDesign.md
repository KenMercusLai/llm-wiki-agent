# Biophilic Design

Biophilic design is the episode's design frame for rebuilding homes that stay connected to nature, natural light, and sustainability after wildfire loss. In [[tech-20260112-0112-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260112-0112-mp-tech-pod-128]], [[DavidBrancaccio]] uses the phrase while discussing how a new fire-conscious house can still refer back to the small storybook cottage that was lost.

The concept matters because the source refuses to treat [[FireResilientConstruction]] as only a defensive shell. A resilient house also has to be livable, tasteful, emotionally continuous, and compatible with neighborhood identity, even when its hidden structure changes through [[CrossLaminatedTimber]], [[FireResistantBuildingEnvelope]] layers, or concrete-centered alternatives.

## Key Claims
- Fire resilience does not settle the design problem by itself.
- Natural light, connection to nature, and sustainability can help a rebuilt house feel less bunker-like.
- Neighborhood memory creates a real design constraint when many older homes are lost at once.
- [[SustainableConstructionCarbonTradeoff]] is partly a design question because material choices affect both climate footprint and lived experience.
