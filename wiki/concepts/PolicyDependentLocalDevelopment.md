# Policy-Dependent Local Development

Policy-dependent local development is a local economic strategy that depends on federal or state policy choices that can change after elections. In [[the-ice-hiring-boom]], [[SavannahPollock]] warns that [[FolkstonGeorgia|Folkston]] should not build its future on immigration detention because the policy can change every four years.

The concept is broader than detention. It captures the risk that a town may gain jobs and revenue from a policy-driven facility while having little control over the political cycle that sustains demand for that facility.

## Key Claims
- Policy-driven facilities can create real short-term jobs without creating durable local control.
- A town can become exposed to electoral cycles, agency priorities, court decisions, and budget changes.
- Political success can undermine the local economic model if enforcement goals reduce the need for detention capacity.
- Durable development requires alternatives that do not depend entirely on one contested federal program.
