# Tragedy of the Commons

Tragedy of the commons is the resource-allocation problem where individual users can overuse a shared resource because they do not bear the full cost imposed on others. [[our-mission-find-the-worlds-best-economic-ideas-summer-school-world-tour]] uses water to explain the idea: upstream users can consume water without fully considering downstream users.

In the episode, [[WaterRightsTrading]] is presented as one institutional response. By defining rights and prices, a market can make scarcity visible and push water toward higher-value uses, but the source also shows that market design must account for drought, information, and fairness.

## Key Claims
- Shared resources can be depleted or misallocated when individual users ignore collective costs.
- Property-like rights and prices can reduce overuse, but they introduce distributional and legitimacy questions.
- [[ClimateAdaptation]] makes commons problems harder because historical supply rules may stop matching actual water availability.
