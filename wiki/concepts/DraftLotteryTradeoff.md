# Draft Lottery Tradeoff

Draft lottery tradeoff is the tension between discouraging [[SportsTanking]] and helping genuinely weak teams improve. [[planet-money-vs-the-nbas-tanking-problem]] explains how the [[NationalBasketballAssociation|NBA]] added randomness to the draft so that finishing last would no longer guarantee the best pick.

The tradeoff is that every reduction in certainty weakens both the bad incentive and the recovery mechanism. Flattened odds can make deliberate losing less attractive, but they can also leave a truly bad team without the high draft pick it needs to rebuild.

## Key Claims
- Randomness can reduce obvious tanking without eliminating the expected value of losing.
- Weighted odds preserve some help for weaker teams but keep the incentive link alive.
- Flattened odds protect the product only if teams judge the lower reward as not worth the reputational and competitive cost.
- Lottery reform is therefore a balancing tool, not a complete answer to draft incentives.
