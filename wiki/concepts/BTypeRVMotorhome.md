# B-Type RV Motorhome

B-type RV motorhome is the vehicle category discussed through [[Guigui]]'s secondhand [[MaxusV90]] in [[ep122-yongyou-yiliang-fangche-shi-zhong-shenme-yang-de-tiyan-lhposkcf-otqhppghn1yjvpjgsrd]]. Unlike a C-type RV that replaces the rear body with a larger cabin, a B-type RV keeps the original van shell and builds the living functions inside it.

The source treats B-type RVs as a compromise: they can be easier to drive, safer-feeling, and more city-compatible than larger RVs, but they trade away living space, storage, insulation, and convenience. That makes the category attractive for two people and pets, but less suited to users who expect hotel-like comfort.

## Key Claims

- A-type RVs are closer to bus or heavy-truck homes and may exceed ordinary driver's-license limits.
- B-type RVs preserve the original vehicle shell, improving driving feel and structural confidence while constraining interior layout.
- C-type RVs often offer more space and overhead beds, but their separate cabin construction raises different safety and handling concerns.
- Trailer RVs can detach the living area from the tow vehicle, but parking, reversing, licensing, and disconnected cab/cabin movement can become inconvenient.
- B-type RV height still prevents many underground parking options, so city compatibility is relative rather than automatic.
