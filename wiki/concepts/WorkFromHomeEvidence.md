# Work From Home Evidence

Work from home evidence is the empirical branch of workplace-policy debate introduced by [[would-you-trust-an-economist-with-your-economy]] through [[NickBloom]]. The source says Bloom's findings vary by job: some roles work better in person, many are best hybrid, and some, such as call centers, can perform better remotely.

The concept matters because it tests how expert evidence travels into executive decision-making. Some leaders discount the data because their own careers were built in offices, while return-to-office mandates can have organizational motives beyond productivity claims. Bloom's repair move is to bring data while also listening and avoiding contempt.

## Key Claims
- Remote-work productivity is not one universal answer; job type and workflow matter.
- Hybrid work can be an evidence-backed middle category rather than a compromise slogan.
- Executive intuition can overweight the conditions under which executives personally succeeded.
- Evidence persuasion improves when experts address concerns before presenting results.
- The concept belongs inside [[EconomistTrustCrisis]] because it shows distrust of economics in business settings, not only politics.
