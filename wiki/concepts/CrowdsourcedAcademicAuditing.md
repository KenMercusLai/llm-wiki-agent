# Crowdsourced Academic Auditing

Crowdsourced academic auditing is the source's model for checking published research at scale. In [[dont-hate-the-replicator-hate-the-game]], [[AbelBrodeur]] realizes that individual replications are too slow to shift norms, so the [[ReplicationGames]] organize many researchers into short, focused teams.

The model works because it gives participants training, collaboration, and credit while lowering the cost of inspection. It also changes incentives for authors: even if most papers will never be checked, the perceived chance of being checked can still make researchers keep code, data, and decisions cleaner.

## Key Claims
- Crowdsourcing turns replication from a solitary, conflict-heavy task into a shared event.
- The model can inspect many papers at low direct cost compared with one-off replication projects.
- Junior researchers benefit from institutional cover when serious issues have to be raised.
- The audit can find coding errors, data problems, missing documentation, and weak [[RobustnessChecks]].
- Its deeper purpose is [[ResearchIntegrityIncentives]], not public shaming.
