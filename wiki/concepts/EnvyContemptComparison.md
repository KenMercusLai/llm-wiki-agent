# Envy-Contempt Comparison / 嫉妒-鄙视比较

Envy-contempt comparison / 嫉妒-鄙视比较 is [[145-jidu-yu-bishi-wo-gai-ruhe-duobi-zhe-shuangxiang-de-dujian-882378647]]'s central emotional dyad. The source treats envy and contempt as "fraternal twins": both come from comparison, but envy looks upward toward someone who has a desired status or resource, while contempt looks downward toward someone judged unworthy of care.

This matters because the episode refuses both denial and indulgence. Envy and contempt can signal goals, unfairness, threat, or group norms, but they become corrosive when they turn a person into a rival, inferior, invader, or object outside ordinary sympathy.

## Key Claims
- Envy is not only desire; in the episode it includes the judgment that the other person should not have what they have.
- Contempt is not only dislike; it includes a withdrawal of concern from someone imagined as lower, dirty, stupid, weak, or morally beneath notice.
- The two emotions can convert into one another when status changes, when a high-status target falls, or when a low-status target is made into a threat.
- [[SchadenfreudeAsComparisonEmotion|Schadenfreude]] is a mixed case: the fall of an envied person lets contempt and reward appear together.
- [[DehumanizationByComparison]] is the shared danger because both emotions can replace a concrete person with a status sign.
- Benign envy is possible when the desired thing becomes a cue for one's own future action rather than a wish to strip it from another person.
- The episode's repair path is [[EmpathyAgainstComparison]]: seeing more of the other person's full life, changing the comparison frame, and building cooperation.
