# AI Protein Design

AI protein design is the biological-design branch discussed in [[ai4s-xuyao-kuangren-yu-yexinjia-duihua-yinglingdian-odin-ruguo-shen-cunzai-wo-zenneng-rongren-ziji-bushi-shen-gonglu-boke-lhceyip6dqomrwk38uvqjwoomxyz]] through [[HaotianOdin]]'s time at [[BakerLab]], his discussion of [[AlphaFold]], and [[YinglingdianAI]]'s broader molecular-design ambition. The source treats protein design as one important modality, not as the whole AI-for-biology problem.

The key distinction is structure prediction versus design. [[AlphaFold]] is used as a precedent showing that AI can transform protein-structure work, while diffusion-style methods are described as improving protein-design search. [[HaotianOdin]] then moves beyond protein-only work toward an [[AllModalMolecularWorldModel]] where proteins interact with small molecules, RNA, and DNA.

## Key Claims
- Protein design became more practical as modern AI architectures and diffusion-style approaches reduced brute-force screening needs.
- [[BakerLab]] is presented as a major formation point for this route.
- Protein-only design remains limited if the larger scientific problem depends on cross-modal molecular interactions.
- Model architecture matters, but the source argues that attachment to any one architecture can distract from the real scientific target.
