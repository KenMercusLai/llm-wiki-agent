# Sportsbook Integrity Monitoring

Sportsbook integrity monitoring is the layered oversight system used by licensed sports betting operators to detect suspicious betting patterns, prohibited insiders, and possible event manipulation. [[tech-20260324-0324-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260324-0324-mp-tech-pod-128]] introduces it through the [[JontayPorter]] case, where unusual bets around an [[NationalBasketballAssociation|NBA]] player's early exits became visible through regulated sportsbook data.

The concept matters because the episode uses it as the practical benchmark for [[PredictionMarketIntegrityOversight]]. Licensed sportsbooks geolocate wagers, track every bet, share real-time data with independent integrity monitors, screen out players or other prohibited insiders, and report suspicious activity to leagues and regulators.

## Key Claims
- Monitoring depends on data collection and data sharing, not just post-hoc investigation.
- Insider screening matters when athletes, officials, employees, government workers, or others can influence or know an event outcome.
- Integrity monitors can turn unusual betting patterns into system-wide alerts before a scandal remains isolated.
- Prediction markets may need comparable systems if they continue to host sports, military, or government-adjacent contracts.
