# Sports Event Ticketing

Sports event ticketing is the revenue and access system around selling seats, hospitality, and resale rights for live sports. In [[shangye-xiaoyang-44-shijiebei-kuojun-yu-fifa-de-quanli-douzheng-993354969]], the 2026 [[FIFAWorldCup]] becomes a ticketing case because the tournament combines extra match supply with North American pricing and resale norms.

The source says earlier World Cups were closer to official fixed-price allocation, while the 2026 tournament includes FIFA primary sales, a FIFA-backed official resale platform, and unofficial resale markets. That makes tickets a live revenue surface rather than only a fan-allocation problem, especially when official resale fees are charged to both buyers and sellers.

## Key Claims
- More matches increase seat inventory, but market design determines how much of the willingness to pay reaches the rights holder.
- Official resale lets an organizer participate in secondary-market upside instead of leaving all resale gains outside the tournament economy.
- Ticketing links consumer demand to [[CorporateHospitalityPlatform]] revenue because high-value seats, suites, and business access are part of the same live-event monetization stack.
- Dynamic or market-like pricing can raise revenue while increasing affordability and access concerns.
