# Holo Assets

Holo assets are the E155 shorthand for "heavy assets, low obsolescence": infrastructure whose value comes from physical scarcity, long build cycles, regulation, replacement difficulty, and low risk of being made irrelevant by a near-term software change. The episode uses the term for assets such as power grids, utilities, electricity equipment, hydro or pipeline-like infrastructure, data-center power chains, and other hard assets connected to AI's physical constraints.

[[shangye-xiaoyang-43-ai-shidai-shui-zai-gei-fuwuqi-jiangwen-992085076]] extends the idea from power and data-center capacity into cooling systems. Pumps, liquid loops, heat exchange, water treatment, prefabricated cooling stations, and intelligent control systems become Holo-like assets when AI rack density makes thermal capacity scarce and operationally decisive.

## Key Claims
- AI is not only a software story because every token ultimately depends on electricity, chips, cooling, networks, and data centers.
- Heavy assets can become attractive when investors worry that software moats are easier for agents and skills to bypass.
- Holo assets are not automatically cheap or safe; the thesis depends on entry price, regulation, financing cost, demand durability, and build-cycle constraints.
- The source contrasts Holo-like assets with parts of [[HangSengTechIndex]], which may remain lighter-asset, consumer-cyclical, or lower-growth even when valuations look low.
- The concept connects infrastructure scarcity to [[AIComputeContinuity]]: energy and physical capacity can cap how many tokens the economy can produce.
- Data-center cooling can become part of the same hard-asset thesis because higher compute density creates demand for specialized thermal systems that cannot be replaced by software alone.
