# Street Jianghu Society

Street Jianghu society is the non-wuxia Jianghu foregrounded in [[102-jianghu-congtan-pianshu-heihua-he-minjian-daoyi-736516488]]. Through [[LianKuoru|连阔如]]'s [[JiangHuCongTan|《江湖丛谈》]], the episode treats Jianghu as a field of itinerant trade, street performance, fortune-telling, medicine selling, scams, lodging houses, beggar groups, guards, thieves, and quyi labor.

The concept extends [[HistoricalJianghuSpace]]. That earlier page asks what historical conditions make fictional侠客 plausible; this source asks how actual low-status and mobile workers survived when livelihood, secrecy, skill, fraud, and reputation all overlapped. It keeps the romance of Jianghu under pressure by showing that mobility can mean freedom, precarity, or predation depending on who controls the situation.

## Key Claims
- Jianghu can be a social infrastructure for informal work, not only a genre setting.
- Street livelihood often needs language barriers, local brokers, lodging rules, reputation, and performance technique.
- The same system can support craft, mutual aid, and exploitation at once.
- The source's Jianghu is urban, itinerant, commercial, and marginal rather than courtly or heroic.
- Reading Jianghu this way helps explain why later fiction could romanticize it: real practices already contained secrecy, codes, risk, and reputation.
