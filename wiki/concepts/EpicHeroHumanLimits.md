# Epic Hero Human Limits

Epic hero human limits is the source's frame for reading heroic greatness through finitude rather than only strength. In [[ep269-cong-manwei-yuzhou-dao-aodesai-weihe-zongyao-fanpai-yingxiong-shishi-ltklotheqpup6oamyflfzpxtipsw]], [[SunRuoxi|孙若曦]] makes [[TheEpicOfGilgamesh|《吉尔伽美什史诗》]] the strongest case: [[Gilgamesh|吉尔伽美什]] is nearly divine, but grief, sleep, fear, and the lost rejuvenation plant force him to return to human scale.

The concept also resonates with the Homeric branch. [[Achilles|阿基里斯]] faces insult, glory, grief, and one-time life in [[TheIliad|《伊利亚特》]], while [[Odysseus|奥德修斯]] in [[TheOdyssey|《奥德赛》]] wants not endless adventure but return to the social world that can recognize him.

## Key Claims
- Epic does not only magnify heroes; it can make human boundaries visible by giving heroes more power than ordinary people.
- Friendship and grief make invulnerability impossible.
- Immortality quests can end not in transcendence but in acceptance of finite life and public responsibility.
- Modern adaptations often keep the hero's power image while dropping the acceptance-of-limits arc.
