# Vertical Agent SaaSification

Vertical Agent SaaSification is the failure mode [[Mengqi]] describes in [[yige-ai-chuangshiren-de-xurongxin-zhuang-he-yumei-zhidian-duitan-invoko-ai-chuangshiren-mengqi-lsi79o-z19zplvmqdbpzzneogpk3f]]: a vertical Agent idea starts as an end-to-end automation promise, but professional users pull it back into configurable, auditable, process-heavy SaaS. The product may still use AI, but its operating burden begins to resemble older software or agency work rather than a clean agentic replacement.

The source's Sourcing Agent case makes the pattern concrete. Non-expert founder users wanted final results, while professional marketing users wanted process confirmation, configurable indicators, and auditability. Listening to the expert user made the product more controllable, but also narrowed the automation advantage and pushed the company toward traditional SaaS structure.

## Key Claims
- A vertical Agent can automate a visible slice of work while missing the higher-value parts of the customer's job.
- Expert users may demand controls and review surfaces that make the product more reliable but less end-to-end.
- If customers do not use the product directly, the company may become an agency that earns service revenue while the software remains an internal tool or investor-facing demo.
- Horizontal expansion by similar technical capability can fail when the buyer, budget, and use frequency do not transfer.
- The stronger counterpattern is to build from a workflow where the team has complete context, visible acceptance criteria, and real [[CustomerPull]].
