# Story-Within-Story Narration

Story-within-story narration is the source's frame for how [[MyNameIsRed|《我的名字叫红》]] embeds older tales, images, and performances inside the murder plot. In [[153-wode-mingzi-jiao-hong-yong-yiqian-lingyi-ye-de-xintiao-wancheng-zhechang-mousha-shang-917670296]], the Hüsrev and Shirin romance is not a disposable digression: it teaches the listener how miniature images carry narrative memory and later becomes a code for Black and Shekure's desire.

[[104-ni-yisheng-de-gushi-zhongdian-yijing-zhuding-haiyao-chufa-ma-739384680]] adds a time-philosophy version through [[MerchantAndAlchemistsGate|《商人和炼金术之门》]]. Its nested merchant, alchemist, and traveler stories do not merely decorate the plot; each inset tale tests what fixed time permits: advice, temptation, repentance, consolation, and acceptance without rewriting history.

The technique links plot, art history, and reader attention. Repeated images from [[PersianMiniaturePainting]] and [[OttomanMiniaturePainting]] let old stories reappear inside new circumstances, while [[MultiPerspectiveMurderNarration]] lets people, corpses, images, and even animals become speakers. The result is a mystery whose evidence is also made of stories already told.
