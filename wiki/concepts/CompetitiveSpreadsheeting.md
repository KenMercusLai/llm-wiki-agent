# Competitive Spreadsheeting

Competitive spreadsheeting is the treatment of spreadsheet work as a timed, spectator-legible performance. In [[betty-boop-excel-olympics-penny-isms-our-2026-valentines]], [[JessJang]] praises [[JesseDoherty]]'s [[WashingtonPost|Washington Post]] article about the Microsoft Excel Collegiate Competition, where contestants solve spreadsheet puzzles while their screens are projected for spectators.

The source matters because it reframes [[MicrosoftExcel|Microsoft Excel]] from office infrastructure into a competition medium. That makes spreadsheet fluency look like a combination of logic, modeling, interface skill, speed, and performance under pressure rather than a boring back-office habit.

## Key Claims
- A familiar tool can become spectator entertainment when the task, stakes, display, and narrative framing are clear.
- Spreadsheet fluency behaves like a language in the source: competitors express plans, constraints, and optimization through cells and formulas.
- Sports-style reporting can make invisible technical work legible to non-specialists.
- The concept sits near [[DataDrivenProductCulture]] but focuses on individual skill and performance rather than organization-level metrics.
