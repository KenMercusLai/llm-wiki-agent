# Insurance Claims Information Asymmetry

Insurance claims information asymmetry is the gap between what claimants understand about a loss and what insurers, adjusters, platforms, and intermediaries understand about policy responsibility. In [[159-yao-jingming-yao-shanliang-yao-jiejue-wenti-931818201]], ordinary people often know the injury, illness, or property loss intimately but not the contract triggers, evidence standards, complaint incentives, fraud boundaries, or settlement options that shape payment.

The episode's cases show the asymmetry in different forms. A delivery rider may not realize route details matter; a parent may not know how to argue that private-hospital transfer was medically necessary; a cancer claimant may need old group-policy continuity proved; a factory owner may not know what evidence an insurer needs after a flood; and an injured worker may be pushed by a claims scalper toward exaggerated demands.

## Key Claims
- The information gap is not simply "the insurer knows and the customer does not"; adjusters can also lack facts that claimants, employers, family members, hospitals, or surveillance systems hold.
- The asymmetry becomes most damaging under stress because the claimant is already dealing with injury, illness, death, family conflict, or business interruption.
- Reducing the gap requires contract literacy, evidence preservation, professional communication, and a realistic understanding of insurer incentives.
- [[InsuranceClaimsAssistancePlatforms]] can reduce the gap, but they introduce their own trust and incentive questions under [[InsuranceSalesTrust]].
- [[InsuranceClaimsScalping]] exploits the same asymmetry by selling pressure tactics or inflated promises to vulnerable claimants.
