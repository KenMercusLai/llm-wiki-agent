# Peter Principle

The Peter Principle is the promotion mismatch pattern in [[the-laws-of-the-office-revisited]]: in a hierarchy, people can rise until they reach a role they are not good at. The episode traces the principle to [[LawrenceJPeter]] and uses [[StephanieByrne]]'s self-demotion as its concrete case.

The source's practical point is that promotion often treats strong performance in one role as evidence of fit for a different role. That can be false when the next job requires management, public training, meetings, or authority work rather than the craft skills that made the person successful.

## Key Claims
- Promotion systems can accidentally punish excellence by moving people away from the work they do well.
- Role fit should be evaluated separately from status, pay, and title.
- Self-demotion can be rational when it restores fit, autonomy, and performance.
- The principle belongs inside [[WorkplaceIncentiveDesign]] because advancement rules shape both individual careers and organizational competence.
