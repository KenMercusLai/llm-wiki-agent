# Exception-State Surveillance Technology

Exception-state surveillance technology is the source's reading of how [[CarlSchmitt]]'s crisis politics helps explain [[PeterThiel]]'s support for [[Palantir]]. [[e233-guigu-youyi-de-quanli-wang-ruhe-xingcheng-liaoliao-bide-tier-de-sixiang-qimeng-pintu-2a529abf-3c14-4ae7-a0a5-fe1340a66c7d]] says Thiel treated 9/11 as a moment when liberal-democratic procedures looked insufficient against enemies framed as ideological, religious, or civilizational.

In that interpretation, Palantir becomes more than enterprise analytics or defense procurement. It is also a tool imagined for searching, linking, and acting on data when government claims exceptional conditions. The page preserves that as the episode's interpretive frame, not a complete history of Palantir's products or contracts.

## Key Claims
- Crisis can be used to justify a looser relationship to normal democratic procedure.
- Surveillance and data-analysis tools become politically charged when they are designed for emergency state action.
- The source links the concept to Thiel's reading of liberalism as too reluctant to name enemies.
