# AI Dubbing

AI dubbing is the use of AI voice systems to reproduce or replace parts of voice performance in film and media. [[e234-weilai-shipai-dianying-hai-cunzai-ma-yu-daoyan-luchuan-liaoliao-ai-gei-yingshiren-de-kongju-yu-ziyou-b2be7093-3366-4ee2-8a7a-625f06206ae5]] adds the concept through [[HuangYing]], who distinguishes technical reproduction from scene-aware acting.

The source's main claim is that dubbing depends on context. A pause can mean thinking, lying, resisting, searching for words, or yielding emotional space to another character. Stress, breath, broken sound, mouth noise, and imperfect intensity can make a performance feel present rather than defective.

AI dubbing therefore becomes a boundary case for [[HumanJudgmentUnderAI]]. Voice systems can replace repetitive, formulaic, or stiff delivery more easily than they can replace actors who adjust to character psychology, scene rhythm, and other performers.

## Key Claims
- Lip-sync and clear pronunciation are necessary but not sufficient for strong dubbing.
- Translation and word choice must fit character, rhythm, and mouth shape.
- Human voice acting includes subconscious timing and cooperative emotional judgment.
- Removing all vocal imperfection can make performance feel more machine-like even when the sound is technically clean.
- The practical boundary of AI dubbing is tied to [[AIVoiceCloningRights]], not only to model quality.
