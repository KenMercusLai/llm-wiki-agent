# Book Proposal As Sales Document

Book proposal as sales document is the idea that a nonfiction proposal must sell a book before the book exists. [[inside-a-book-auction]] adds the concept through the year-long process where [[AlexGoldmark]], [[AlexMaiassi]], [[LauraNolan]], and [[JaneVonMehren|Jane von Mehren]] develop sample writing, a chapter outline, and a business case for the [[PlanetMoney]] book.

The proposal is not only an editorial outline. It has to let publishers imagine audience, positioning, format, competition, risk, and upside well enough to price a bid.

## Key Claims
- Proposal work converts a broad media brand into a specific book promise.
- Sample writing lets editors evaluate tone and execution before acquisition.
- A chapter outline makes the book's structure concrete enough to discuss.
- Audience evidence and comparable titles help publishers build internal financial support for a bid.
- The proposal bridges creative work and [[AuthorPlatformRiskReduction|platform-backed risk reduction]].
