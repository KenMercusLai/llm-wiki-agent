# Currency Risk

Currency risk is the episode's reminder that an investor's final return depends on the currency they spend or report in, not only the asset's local-currency yield. [[ep39-feng-man-lou-xiaji-quanqiu-shuaitui-manman-bijin-yanfang-sishou-bubu-weiying-manliao-xiabannian-meigu-meizhai-huilv-lgz718un4sjvgxqbydfikyxz0hcj]] applies this to U.S. Treasuries, dollar deposits, QDII products, RMB/USD moves, and yen/carry-trade volatility.

## Key Claims
- A U.S. bond or deposit yield can be partly erased if the dollar weakens against the investor's home currency.
- Investors should exchange currency for a real use case such as travel, education, long-term allocation, or specific assets, rather than treating FX as a simple short-term trade.
- Professional FX trading often uses high leverage, making it unsuitable as casual retail speculation.
- The episode links currency movements to [[FederalReserve]] cuts, [[BankOfJapan]] policy, [[YenCarryTrade]] changes, Asian currency moves, and [[RMBExchangeRatePolicy]].
