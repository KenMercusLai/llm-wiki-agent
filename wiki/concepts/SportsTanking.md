# Sports Tanking

Sports tanking is the deliberate weakening of competitive effort because losing can produce future advantage. In [[planet-money-vs-the-nbas-tanking-problem]], the [[NationalBasketballAssociation|NBA]] version comes from draft rules that give worse teams better odds at top picks.

The source's key move is to treat tanking as an incentive outcome rather than only a morality failure. Teams, fans, and executives may dislike the spectacle, but when rules make losing valuable, rational teams can still drift toward behavior that damages the product.

## Key Claims
- Tanking turns late-season games into a conflict between current entertainment value and future roster value.
- It can alienate fans who want teams to compete but also want long-run hope.
- The stronger the payoff from a top pick, the harder it is for modest lottery tweaks to remove tanking incentives.
- Anti-tanking reforms must preserve [[SportsCompetitiveBalance]] without making intentional losing the best available strategy.
