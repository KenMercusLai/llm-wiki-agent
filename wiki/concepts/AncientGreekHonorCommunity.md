# Ancient Greek Honor Community

Ancient Greek honor community is the social frame [[LiuChang|刘畅]] uses in [[ep269-cong-manwei-yuzhou-dao-aodesai-weihe-zongyao-fanpai-yingxiong-shishi-ltklotheqpup6oamyflfzpxtipsw]] to explain [[TheIliad|《伊利亚特》]] and [[TheOdyssey|《奥德赛》]]. In this frame, honor is not only private self-esteem; it is public recognition, rank, ritual order, gifts, and the place a hero holds among others.

This is why [[Agamemnon|阿加门农]] taking a war prize from [[Achilles|阿基里斯]] is more than a material dispute. It denies Achilles' position in the Achaean order. The same frame also explains why [[Odysseus|奥德修斯]]' return home matters: he is trying to re-enter the community that can recognize his identity, household order, and cultural rules.

## Key Claims
- Homeric heroes are embedded in status orders; their choices cannot be read only as private psychology.
- War spoils, hospitality, marriage, and household control are recognition systems.
- [[TheOdyssey]]'s travel narrative is also a boundary story between Greek social rules and worlds where those rules may not apply.
- Modern retellings risk flattening Homer if they turn public honor into only personal trauma or generic adventure.
