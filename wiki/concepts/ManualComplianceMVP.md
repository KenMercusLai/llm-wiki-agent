# Manual Compliance MVP

Manual compliance MVP is the validation pattern [[ChristinaCacioppo]] describes in [[tsr-s5-christinac-v2audio-tsr-s5-christinac-v2audio]] when explaining [[Vanta]]'s origin. Before building software, she and her collaborator stopped making unwanted products, talked to founders, and produced a spreadsheet gap assessment for [[Segment]] that mapped [[SOC2Audit]] needs, existing controls, and missing work.

The spreadsheet mattered because it was both customer help and product discovery. Reusing it for [[Front]] showed that startup compliance work had repeatable structure, while the manual format kept the team close to customer language, audit evidence, and implementation gaps before committing to a web app.

[[tsr-ycoffsite-christinacacioppo-v1-audioonly-tsr-ycoffsite-christinacacioppo-v1-audioonly]] reinforces the pattern with the offsite version of the same origin story. Christina says the team spent two weeks in [[Segment]]'s office reading SOC 2 reports, standardizing them, interviewing engineers, and turning the work into a roadmap before adapting the spreadsheet for [[Front]]. The source emphasizes that the manual artifact was useful precisely because many people believed the product could not be built yet.

## Key Claims
- A manual compliance artifact can validate workflow pain before software exists.
- The method works when the artifact is specific enough to solve a real customer problem and general enough to repeat across similar companies.
- Manual work can reveal which parts of compliance are best-practice templates and which parts require company-specific judgment.
- The pattern bridges [[CustomerDiscoveryByDoingWork]], [[PreProductSelling]], and [[ServiceProductization]] because the founder sells or delivers expert work before turning it into product.
- Manual delivery can also test whether an "impossible" product category has enough repeatable structure to become software.
