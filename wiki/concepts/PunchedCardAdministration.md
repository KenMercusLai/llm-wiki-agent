# Punched Card Administration / 打孔卡行政

Punched card administration / 打孔卡行政 is the information-processing layer discussed in [[133-ibm-yu-nacui-weishenme-putongren-bu-ying-zanmei-jizeizhuyi-815684110]]. The episode starts from [[HermanHollerith|Herman Hollerith]]'s tabulating-machine work and follows the technology into [[IBM]], [[Dehomag|德霍梅格]], census taking, railway management, labor allocation, and state classification.

The key point is service and customization. The source stresses that punched-card systems were not simply boxes sold once to a customer; card layouts, machine settings, maintenance, and operators had to be adapted to specific administrative goals. That makes the technology morally different from a passive commodity when it is configured for [[NaziGermany|Nazi Germany]]'s racial state and [[TheHolocaust|the Holocaust]].

## Key Claims
- Information infrastructure can change what a state is practically able to know and do.
- Classification is political when categories decide exclusion, deportation, labor, or death.
- Technical maintenance and customization can make a vendor part of a violent administrative system.
- A tool's history can include both ordinary statistical modernization and catastrophic misuse.
- The same technical knowledge can serve [[DataEnabledPersecution|persecution]] or [[TechnicalResistanceFromWithin|resistance]], depending on institutional context and personal action.
