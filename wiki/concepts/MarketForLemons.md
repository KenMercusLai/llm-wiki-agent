# The Market For Lemons

The Market for Lemons is the economics paper and concept used in [[board-games-3-whats-in-a-name]] as the basis for [[SellMeASasquatch]]. The source treats it as a classic [[AsymmetricInformation]] case: sellers know more about quality than buyers, so distrust can reshape the market.

In this episode, the paper is not presented as a formal model to teach directly. Instead, [[PlanetMoney]] and [[ExplodingKittens]] convert the information problem into partial-information card trading, hidden deal quality, bluffing, and suspicion among players. That makes the concept a bridge between economic explanation and [[GameMechanicToRetailProduct]].

## Key Claims
- Hidden information can change market behavior even when trade would otherwise be valuable.
- The board-game adaptation turns adverse selection into lived experience: players feel uncertainty and distrust before they name the theory.
- A literal used-car theme may be conceptually faithful but commercially weaker if shoppers infer the wrong game category.
- The episode shows that educational source material can survive better as mechanics than as didactic branding.
