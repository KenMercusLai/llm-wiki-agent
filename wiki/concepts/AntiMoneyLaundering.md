# Anti-Money Laundering

Anti-money laundering is the compliance and enforcement frame for detecting, preventing, and investigating attempts to make illegal funds appear legitimate. [[ep25-zhongzi-waizi-najia-qiang-yi-lao-yong-yi-zhao-qianliang-xia-feat-qianliang-hutong-fm-lshfotcmlkqcx6hhtvbpcuqdwuhg]] introduces AML as part of bank onboarding and source-of-funds review, while [[ep44-momo-koudai-limian-de-qian-juran-shi-zang-de-li-4f9d60jmelqituybbioyyflxr]] turns it into a broader personal-risk map across accounts, cross-border transfers, markets, entertainment, recharges, and virtual assets. [[ep89-haiwai-quanshang-da-dizhen-kuajing-touzi-xinshidai-li8ya-r5cpz3sifdjby73vh9-rxs]] adds the overseas brokerage funding version, where informal exchange, false declarations, and unclear counterparties can turn investment access into AML exposure.

[[socialradarspod-brianarmstrong-final]] adds the startup-operator version through [[Coinbase]]. When [[BrianArmstrong]] tried to add bank transfers, [[SiliconValleyBank]] asked about AML policy, forcing him to learn the term and treat compliance as a requirement for the user-facing buy button. This turns AML from a bank-only topic into part of [[RegulatedCryptoTrustStrategy]].

[[tech-20260212-0212-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260212-0212-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds the sanctions and scam-network enforcement version. [[AriRedbord]] argues for targeting the illicit underbelly of a mostly lawful crypto ecosystem, using [[USTreasury|U.S. Treasury]] sanctions, exchange actions, and network disruption against actors such as [[A7A5]], Iran-linked exchanges, and [[PrinceGroup]].

## Key Claims
- AML focuses on the movement and disguise of proceeds from upstream crimes, not on money movement as a neutral technical problem.
- [[BankingKYCCompliance]] is one AML foundation because identity, occupation, income, account behavior, and source of funds need to make sense together.
- Monitoring continues after onboarding: dormant accounts, sudden large transfers, unusual cash deposits, and transactions inconsistent with a user's profile can trigger review.
- AML work depends on lawful information sharing, regulatory cooperation, and evidence; it is constrained by [[BankingComplianceBoundaries]] rather than unlimited bank-to-bank disclosure.
- For ordinary users, the practical lesson is [[ConsumerAMLExposure]]: a person can be pulled into a laundering chain by account lending, cheap services, casual withdrawals, or opaque counterparties even without understanding the full scheme.
- Cross-border brokerage funding can become an AML issue when money moves through underground exchange or unrelated counterparties instead of transparent, purpose-consistent channels.
- For crypto-fintech startups, AML can be a product prerequisite because banking partners may block core payment flows until controls are legible.
- AML can become national-security enforcement when virtual-asset rails are used for sanctions evasion, state-linked cyber activity, or organized scam networks.
