# Bird-Dinosaur Continuity / 鸟类-恐龙连续性

Bird-dinosaur continuity is the evolutionary frame that living birds are part of the dinosaur lineage rather than merely dinosaur-like animals. In [[146-bu-ai-zhuluoji-shijie-kuailai-konglong-bihusuo-dang-siyangyuan-886472126]], the idea appears when Suma Suzume explains to children that a small bird is also a dinosaur relative, and when the hosts discuss feathered dinosaur finds from China.

The concept matters because it changes dinosaur imagination. Dinosaurs are not only gigantic extinct reptiles; some lines connect to small, feathered, warm-bodied, behaviorally complex animals whose descendants are still ordinary presences. That bridge supports [[DinosaurAsLivingAnimal]] and keeps dinosaur popular science connected to [[ObservationBeforeInference]] rather than only spectacle.

## Key Claims
- Bird-dinosaur continuity gives audiences a living reference point for dinosaurs without erasing the diversity of extinct dinosaur forms.
- Feathered dinosaur discoveries changed both scientific and popular reconstructions of dinosaur appearance.
- The continuity frame helps children and general audiences understand that evolution leaves descendants, not just fossils.
- Living birds can inform reconstruction, but they should not be used as a shortcut that makes all extinct dinosaurs behave the same way.
