# User-Generated Content Copyright Risk

User-generated content copyright risk is the legal exposure a platform faces when users upload copyrighted material faster than the company can prevent, detect, license, or remove it. [[tsr-s5-jared-v3final-tsr-s5-jared-v3final]] adds the concept through [[Scribd]], which [[JaredFriedman]] says immediately faced piracy and copyright-infringement problems after launching as a document-sharing site.

The source connects this risk to legal support and product distribution. [[BrianMendoza]] at [[WilsonSonsini|Wilson Sonsini]] helped Scribd avoid being sued out of existence, drawing on [[YouTube]] experience. The same documents that powered [[SearchDrivenContentGrowth]] could also create rights-holder conflict, so growth, moderation, and legal process were not separable.

## Key Claims
- User-uploaded content can create platform value before the platform has mature rights-management systems.
- Search distribution amplifies copyright risk because infringing or disputed material becomes easier to find.
- Early legal design can decide whether a platform survives long enough to build better moderation and compliance.
- The risk differs from ordinary product bugs because outside rights holders can impose litigation, takedown, financing, or acquisition pressure.
