# Stablecoins

Stablecoins are discussed in [[ep88-chuanyue-lianghua-zhifu-ximengsi-ai-hui-rang-putongren-geng-rongyi-zhuanqian-haishi-geng-nan-lhvigzza2ugmayezkbrxufkmp4l1]] as crypto-market infrastructure with real payment demand and macro-financial implications. The episode argues that stablecoins can extend dollar reach and increase demand for U.S. Treasuries, while also raising concerns about private companies taking on quasi-monetary roles.

## Key Claims
- Stablecoin demand is treated as more grounded than purely speculative crypto trading because it is tied to payments, settlement, and dollar access.
- Issuers that hold Treasuries can expand demand for U.S. government debt.
- Private stablecoin issuance creates regulatory and monetary-governance questions.
- The likely path is continued growth alongside stronger licensing, supervision, or digital-dollar-like regulation.
