# Money Illusion / ŤīßŚłĀťĒôŤßČ

Money illusion / ŤīßŚłĀťĒôŤßČ is the tendency to react to nominal amounts rather than real value. [[123-renxing-shiyan-dangxin-ni-ziji-779479569]] uses clock-setting, summer/winter time, poker-chip denominations, and inflation-adjusted wage examples to show how people can be affected by surface numbers even when they know the underlying conversion.

The concept overlaps with economics but the episode reads it psychologically: the representation is part of the action environment. A changed number, chip, or clock can trigger behavior before the person reanchors on the real value.

## Key Claims
- Nominal increases can feel better than smaller nominal increases that are larger in real terms.
- Tokenized or doubled denominations can alter betting behavior even when exchange rates are known.
- Money illusion shows that knowing a conversion rule does not guarantee acting from it.
- Interfaces and accounting labels can shape decisions by changing the felt unit of action.
