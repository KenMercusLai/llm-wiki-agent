# Negotiation As Bargaining / 谈判即商量

Negotiation as bargaining is the core reframing of [[106-bie-zai-haipa-chongtu-kuai-lai-xuexi-tanpan-shu-742539645]]. [[QinZong|秦总]] argues that "谈判" sounds formal and power-heavy in Chinese, while "商量" better captures the episode's method: exchange interests, calculate terms, and create value through communication.

The concept rejects both passive obedience and emotional struggle. A negotiator prepares with [[BATNA]], [[ReservationValue]], and [[ZoneOfPossibleAgreement]], then uses [[InterestBasedNegotiation]] to understand why the other side wants what it says it wants. This makes conflict less like a personality contest and more like a structured decision problem.

[[116-bufucong-de-yishu-fenxiang-yixie-shizhan-fangfa-haiyou-liliang-764416021]] extends the concept into dissent. [[InsiderDissentStrategy|Insider dissent strategy]] applies a similar discipline to disagreement inside groups: state shared goals, avoid unnecessary fear, build credibility before conflict, and treat opponents as persuadable humans rather than obstacles to be defeated.

## Key Claims
- Negotiation is a learnable practice, not a gift restricted to eloquent or high-EQ people.
- "商" points to exchanging interests; "量" points to making terms calculable.
- Treating negotiation as bargaining makes room for [[CostBenefitThinking]] and [[OpportunityCost]] without stripping away relationship concerns.
- The point is not to crush the other side, but to create a result both sides can accept and continue from.
- Dissent can use negotiation discipline when the goal is to make a true or useful objection receivable rather than merely win a clash.
