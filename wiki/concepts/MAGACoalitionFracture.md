# MAGA Coalition Fracture

MAGA coalition fracture is [[sp-05-liuqing-linyao-qiangsha-yibao-jutou-an-beihou-de-sikao-xia-fenlie-de-meiguo-hui-you-xianzheng-weiji-ma-790241978]]'s argument that [[MAGA]] is not a unified national wave. [[LinYao|林垚]] says [[DonaldTrump]] won under favorable institutional conditions but not with an overwhelming popular mandate, and that internal conflict appeared almost immediately around high-skill immigration.

The H-1B dispute is the source's main case. Grassroots or middle-class MAGA supporters can see high-skill foreign workers as threats to American jobs and wages, while [[ElonMusk]] and Silicon Valley employers depend on foreign technical labor. The episode treats this as a coalition built from capital, technology elites, nationalist resentment, and anti-establishment media, not a coherent social program.

## Key Claims
- Trump's 2024 result should be separated from the claim that MAGA represents a settled national consensus.
- H-1B conflict exposes different material interests inside the coalition: nativist labor-market fear versus employer demand for skilled migrants.
- A narrow [[RepublicanParty]] House majority makes Trump-era governing capacity more fragile than election-night narratives suggested.
- The coalition may be stronger at destruction, norm pressure, and enemy-making than at building stable conservative legislation.
- Immigration politics can split between general backlash and selective openness to high-value or high-skill migrants, extending [[ImmigrationBacklashCycle]] and [[MeritBasedImmigrationFilter]].
