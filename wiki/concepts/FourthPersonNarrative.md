# Fourth-Person Narrative

Fourth-person narrative is the episode's shorthand for a narrative position that exceeds a simple first-person or third-person split. In [[130-tuokaerqiuke-guaidan-gushiji-xunzhao-he-gaobie-803543569]], the host uses the idea while reading 〈旅客〉 from [[BizarreStories|《怪诞故事集》]]: an outer "I" hears another person's story, while that story becomes an inward inquiry into who the self is.

The concept matters because it lets the source describe self-recognition without treating the self as one stable speaker. The "I" can be listener, narrator, witness, childhood memory, future body, and stranger at once. That layered position supports [[SelfRecognitionHorror]]: the feared other can turn out to be the self without fully losing its otherness.

## Key Claims
- Fourth-person narrative names a self split across speaker, witness, and object of recognition.
- It helps explain why 〈旅客〉 feels like both a ghost story and a realistic account of internal fear.
- The form makes identity relational: the self is encountered from outside, across time, and through another voice.
