# Private Immigration Detention

Private immigration detention is the use of private operators to run detention facilities for federal immigration enforcement. In [[the-ice-hiring-boom]], [[GEOGroup]] operates the large ICE detention facility in [[FolkstonGeorgia|Folkston, Georgia]] under a federal contract.

The source shows how private detention turns a policy objective into contracts, jobs, bed counts, and municipal revenue. It also makes the moral stakes visible because economic benefits depend on holding more detained people.

## Key Claims
- Private detention converts federal enforcement demand into local jobs and corporate contract revenue.
- Facility expansion can make small towns financially invested in continued detention demand.
- Contract economics can separate who benefits locally from who bears the human cost behind detention walls.
- Private operation does not remove public responsibility because the detention purpose remains federal immigration enforcement.
