# 小镇做题家规训 / Small-Town Exam-Taker Discipline

小镇做题家规训 names the source's account of how school achievement becomes both exit and constraint for a child from a town with limited visible alternatives. In [[140-haikeyi-de-jin-nvshi-suoyi-ren-weishenme-yao-nuli-a-854173655]], [[Jinzi|金子]] describes a North Henan upbringing where study appears as the reliable route out, while other commercial, vocational, or lifestyle possibilities are hard for adults and children to imagine.

The source's useful addition is that "小镇做题家" is not only an internet label. It is a lived training system: obey roles, endure bodily cost, suppress improper beauty, distrust one's own feeling, pursue recognized rankings, and then carry the habit of comparison into new adult worlds. For girls, the discipline is especially contradictory because school may treat beauty and display as danger, while later workplaces require charm, taste, and confidence.

## Key Claims
- The exam route can be genuinely enabling when other exits are invisible, but it can also make non-exam possibilities feel illegible or shameful.
- The discipline works through roles: good student, plain girl, obedient daughter, competitive worker, and later stylish urban professional.
- Local prestige systems may misread specialized choices such as [[ChinaCommunicationUniversity|中国传媒大学]] when they do not fit a simple rank ladder.
- The concept differs from [[AlgorithmicLabeling]]: the label can circulate online, but this page tracks the slower educational and bodily formation behind the label.
- The pattern extends [[ImperialExaminationAsOnlyExit]] into a contemporary setting without equating gaokao society with imperial examination society.
