# Science Under Revolutionary Suspicion / 革命怀疑下的科学

Science under revolutionary suspicion / 革命怀疑下的科学 is the episode's frame for the conflict between [[JeanPaulMarat|Marat]] and [[AntoineLavoisier|Antoine Lavoisier]] in [[125-mala-zhisi-beibi-de-ansha-huo-zhengyi-de-chujue-783972516]]. Marat's frustrated scientific ambitions, his attacks on the Academy, and Lavoisier's elite public roles show how expertise can become politically vulnerable when institutions are read primarily as privilege.

The source does not deny that old-regime offices and elite scientific bodies could be entangled with power. Its concern is the opposite simplification: once scientific authority is treated as merely aristocratic, conspiratorial, or anti-people, evidential disagreement can be swallowed by factional accusation.

Lavoisier's case also extends the wiki's broader science branch. The issue is not bad science alone, but the political environment in which public suspicion can punish expertise without replacing it with better evidence.

## Key Claims
- Revolutionary egalitarianism can expose real privilege while also making technical expertise suspect.
- Failed scientific ambition can become politically dangerous when personal grievance gains a mass platform.
- Institutions of knowledge need criticism, but criticism without evidence can become anti-intellectual punishment.
- The destruction of experts can be justified as popular justice while weakening the public's capacity to know.
