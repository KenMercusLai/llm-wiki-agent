# Folk Character Etymology Risk

Folk character etymology risk is the error pattern where a modern reader explains a Chinese character by a memorable visual story that is not grounded in historical forms. [[57-shuojie-hanzi-shiniaopi-weisha-shi-shi-zitou-642970319]] introduces it through examples such as popular拆字 sayings, foreign tattoos, and the temptation to read "尸" in "屎、尿、屁" as corpse-related.

The concept is a language-specific form of [[InterpretationAndOverinterpretation]]. A modern character is a sign that asks to be interpreted, but the attractive explanation can be a post hoc story. The source's answer is not to remove wonder from characters; it is to route wonder through [[ChineseCharacterEvidenceDiscipline]] and [[CharacterFormEvolution]].

## Key Claims
- A story can be memorable and still be false.
- Present-day visual resemblance is not enough to prove historical meaning.
- Vulgar, sexual, or shocking explanations are especially tempting because they travel easily.
- Classical dictionaries reduce some risk but can still be revised by better evidence.
- Responsible popularization should separate evidence, speculation, and mnemonic play.
