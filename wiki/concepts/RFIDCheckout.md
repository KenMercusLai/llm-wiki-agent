# RFID Checkout

RFID checkout is a retail checkout design where radio-frequency tags let a system identify products without scanning each barcode. In [[betty-boop-excel-olympics-penny-isms-our-2026-valentines]], [[Uniqlo]] is the source case: customers place garments in a bin and the checkout reads the tags automatically.

The concept is useful because the source does not treat technology adoption as pure convenience. [[KennyMalone]] says RFID tags have become much cheaper, but the cost still matters by category. Apparel can absorb a few extra cents per item more easily than low-margin grocery products, so the same checkout system that feels good in a clothing store may not generalize to every retail format.

## Key Claims
- Checkout automation only improves experience if it removes work from the customer rather than pushing barcode-scanning labor onto them.
- Tag cost matters relative to product margin and item price.
- Retail technology should be judged by workflow fit, not by the abstract presence of automation.
- A narrow successful deployment can be more informative than a broad claim that self-checkout is good or bad.
