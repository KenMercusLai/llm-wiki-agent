# Two-Round Presidential Election

A two-round presidential election is an electoral system where a crowded first round can be followed by a head-to-head second round if no candidate wins outright. In [[marine-warfare-le-pen-runs-for-president-6a4e1f2781fe1f6460b7117c]], this structure explains why [[MarineLePen]]'s first-round polling lead in [[France]] does not settle the 2027 outcome.

The source's main lesson is strategic. [[NationalRally]] can benefit from a fragmented field, but the second round tests whether [[FarRightNormalization]] is broad enough to overcome anti-Le Pen consolidation, center-left-center-right transfers, and late campaign shifts.

## Key Claims
- First-round strength can reflect a loyal base without proving majority acceptability.
- Candidate fragmentation matters because it decides who reaches the runoff.
- Second-round polling is more fragile when the election is still months away and candidate identities remain fluid.
- Legal eligibility, party image, and economic pitch all affect whether a candidate can cross from plurality to majority.
