# Adult Independence From Family

Adult independence from family is the episode's practical answer to the original-family dilemma in [[sp-02-wo-you-ziji-de-yuzhou-ruhe-zai-hundun-zhong-zuo-ge-qingxing-de-xingdongpai-748593778]]. [[QianJing|钱静]] accepts that family history shapes a person, but argues for "归因不归罪": use family as an explanation where it is useful, without making daily life into a permanent trial against one's parents.

The source's most concrete recommendation is physical separation. Moving out, even into imperfect shared housing, can become the first forced adulting step because economic freedom and spiritual freedom are hard to separate in a highly entangled parent-child relationship. The episode then shifts growth toward work, labor, city experience, and small personal dreams rather than expecting the family relationship to become fully resolved first.

## Key Claims
- Original family influence is real, but constant blame can consume the energy needed for economic and adult independence.
- Physical distance can reduce rumination and mixed signals even before emotional resolution is available.
- Rent is treated as an autonomy cost, not only a household expense.
- Work and social position can change the family dynamic indirectly because parents may respond differently once the child has a visible adult place.
- Independence is not framed as clean severance; it is a gradual reallocation of attention, money, and self-definition.
