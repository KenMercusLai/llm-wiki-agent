# Product Roast

Product Roast is [[Daqi]]'s public landing-page review practice discussed in [[ep87-duihua-duli-shejishi-daqi-tongguo-sheji-bangzhu-chanpin-zuohao-zengzhang-luymytt48g-ejwsl6bfuko2xsdoc]]. The episode says the activity reviewed about 60 pages and attracted strong attention because it turned design critique into practical product-growth advice.

The wiki uses Product Roast as a concrete inspection pattern for early products. It checks whether a page states the target user, scenario, value proposition, CTA, trust proof, and conversion path, rather than only whether the interface looks modern or uses polished components.

## Key Claims

- Many landing pages fail at story and information architecture before they fail at visual detail.
- AI-first wording is weak when the user does not yet understand what problem is solved or why the product fits their situation.
- Component libraries can make a page look finished while spacing, hierarchy, color consistency, and section grouping remain incoherent.
- Product Roast works because it joins [[BusinessFluentDesign]] with [[LandingPageConversion]]: the critique asks what business result the design is supposed to support.
