# Dehumanization By Comparison / 比较导致的去人性化

Dehumanization by comparison / 比较导致的去人性化 is the episode's shared danger behind envy and contempt. In [[145-jidu-yu-bishi-wo-gai-ruhe-duobi-zhe-shuangxiang-de-dujian-882378647]], both emotions can first strip the target of ordinary personhood: the envied person becomes an illegitimate possessor, while the contemned person becomes an inferior other.

The concept connects individual emotion to group harm. Once the target is no longer concrete, attacks can feel like self-defense, norm enforcement, humor, justice, or class resentment rather than cruelty.

## Key Claims
- Envy dehumanizes by turning another person's success into a hostile sign or stolen resource.
- Contempt dehumanizes by turning another person's weakness, poverty, ignorance, identity, or mistake into evidence that they deserve distance.
- Group labels accelerate the process because nationality, race, gender, school, class, and fandom can replace individual information.
- Disgust is especially dangerous when it attaches to people, because it creates a desire for distance and removal rather than understanding.
- [[InternetMoralTrial]] can make dehumanization scalable by combining partial information, anonymity, and audience reward.
- The source's main antidote is concrete information, direct or imagined contact, and [[EmpathyAgainstComparison]].
