# Public Argument For Bystanders / 给第三方看的争论

Public argument for bystanders is the episode's distinction between trying to make an opponent concede and speaking so third parties can see evidence, values, and a counter-position. In [[132-zhenglun-yu-shuofu-women-weishenme-chaojia-zenme-cai-suan-ying-807583318]], this explains why some online argument can have social value even when the direct target is unreachable.

The concept also sets a boundary. If there is no meaningful audience, no evidence to add, and no relationship worth preserving, repeated private persuasion of a hostile stranger is likely to become emotional consumption rather than useful argument.

## Key Claims
- Argument can be aimed at witnesses, not only opponents.
- Public truth-telling can accumulate material that later helps people cross a belief-change threshold.
- The same practice can become addictive if the speaker chases the feeling of winning rather than the public value of speaking.
- Silence can be the better public act when a person has no knowledge, no stake, or no useful information to add.
