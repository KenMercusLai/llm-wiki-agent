# App Store Ratings And Reviews

App Store ratings and reviews are the trust and feedback layer of [[AppStoreOptimization]]. In [[ep102-duihua-una-quanqiu-toubu-siwei-daotu-app-store-yunying-fuzeren-qinshou-aso-shizhan-jingyan-lscdlnogyhiohre091lwi-ayshug]], [[Una]] says the official ranking weight is not transparent, but ratings and reviews matter because they can affect both user trust and possibly store visibility.

The episode's practical advice is to ask for ratings earlier and more often than many developers expect, because the in-app rating prompt is low-friction and does not force a jump to the store. Comments require more deliberate moments, such as update dialogs or satisfaction prompts, where users can be guided to give feedback after a visible improvement or feature request.

## Key Claims

- Ratings and reviews should not be treated as a passive afterthought once all other ASO work is done.
- Waiting only for heavy milestones may suppress rating volume; earlier moments of initial satisfaction can still produce useful ratings.
- Negative feedback is not always catastrophic if developers fix the issue and reply or guide users back after resolution.
- Review prompts should respect platform boundaries; fake reviews, fake helpful votes, and misleading incentives can create account or ranking risk.
- Ratings and reviews link [[AppStoreProductPageConversion]] with ranking work because they shape what users believe when the product appears in search.
