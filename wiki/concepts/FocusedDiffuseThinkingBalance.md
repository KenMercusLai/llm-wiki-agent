# Focused-Diffuse Thinking Balance / 专注与发散思维均衡

Focused-diffuse thinking balance is [[143-yiwang-de-naoli-jixing-buhao-ke-zha-zheng-a-869667634]]'s practical model for alternating concentrated effort with looser attention. The episode says focused thinking and diffuse thinking inhibit each other, so learning and memory integration need more than uninterrupted concentration.

The concept pushes against the simple slogan that the brain only improves by being used harder. In the source, walks, looking at scenery, showering, sunlight, rhythmic movement, music, drawing, and simple physical tasks are not idle decoration; they help the brain shift mode after intense reading, study, writing, or screen work.

## Key Claims
- Focused attention is useful for study, writing, exams, and deliberate practice, but it cannot do every cognitive job by itself.
- Diffuse attention supports association, memory sorting, consolidation, and recovery.
- Long, high-intensity focus without restorative mode-switching can become fatigue rather than intelligence.
- Phone use is double-edged: external storage can free people to forget unimportant details, but constant scrolling can create restless, fragmented, overactive attention.
- The episode treats "均衡用脑" as the practical target: do not stay permanently in either concentrated work or loose feed-driven wandering.
- The source's brain-health suggestions are lifestyle-level and source-scoped, not medical treatment instructions.
