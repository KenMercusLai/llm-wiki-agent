# Nonhuman Narrative Voice

Nonhuman narrative voice is the technique of shifting prose away from ordinary human sentence, continuity, and viewpoint so that a story can register machine, ecological, planetary, animal, or other nonhuman perception. In [[sp-03-chuantai-chen-shengming-qixi-douliu-zela-zini-yu-tade-mengjing-757948106]], [[Beimin|北明]] highlights this in [[RogerZelazny|Zelazny]]'s [[PermafrostZelazny|《永久冻土》]]: human narration remains comparatively normal, while Glenda's merger with land and mycelium makes the prose fragmentary and strange.

The concept connects literary form to personhood and ecology. A nonhuman voice can make the reader feel that a consciousness is not merely human with new powers; it may have different scale, rhythm, memory, and relation to land.

## Key Claims
- Nonhuman viewpoint often needs syntax, rhythm, and texture changes, not only a declared narrator.
- Fragmented or strange prose can represent transformed agency rather than simply obscure the plot.
- Ecological or machine consciousness can unsettle human-centered assumptions about perception and selfhood.
- The technique fits [[ScienceFictionNewWave|New Wave science fiction]] because form becomes part of the speculative premise.
