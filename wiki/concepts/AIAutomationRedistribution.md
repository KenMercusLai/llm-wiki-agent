# AI Automation Redistribution

AI automation redistribution is the policy frame in [[live-anthropic-co-founder-on-ai-and-jobs]] where [[JackClark]] argues that highly capable AI may force society to move money from a machine economy back into the human economy. The source's concrete mechanism is taxing robots or AI companies if AI-controlled production systems generate returns without a normal human-labor channel.

The concept extends the wiki's existing automation branch. [[AutomationDisplacementEffect]] and [[AutomationReinstatementEffect]] describe task-level labor shifts; AI automation redistribution asks what happens if the displacement side becomes large enough that ordinary labor income no longer distributes productivity gains widely.

## Key Claims
- The policy problem is distribution, not only whether AI raises productivity.
- Robot or AI-company taxation becomes plausible when machine output grows faster than human wage channels.
- Redistribution must be designed before concentrated AI ownership becomes a larger political backlash trigger.
- The source treats this as Clark's forecast and proposal, not as an already-settled economic fact.
