# Major-Event Attention Conversion

Major-event attention conversion is the strategy of turning a temporary surge of attention around a major event into durable customer behavior, local loyalty, or revenue. [[can-world-cup-mania-grow-mls-in-the-us]] adds the concept through [[MajorLeagueSoccer]] and the 2026 [[FIFAWorldCup]]: the event creates attention, but MLS clubs still have to convert that attention into future attendance, ticket sales, merchandise, sponsorship value, and player interest.

The source shows three levels of conversion work. The [[SeattleSounders]] make the tournament communal through a barge watch party, the [[NewEnglandRevolution]] use nearby World Cup matches as a live-sports demonstration, and the [[ChicagoFire]] manufacture a non-host-city event to gather contacts and expose fans to the local club. MLS headquarters adds a broader marketing and player-recruiting layer through [[CamiloDurana]].

## Key Claims
- Event heat is not the same as owned demand; the organizer or adjacent business needs a bridge from the event to a repeatable product.
- Good conversion targets matter because huge awareness can be wasteful if the audience has no reason to become a future customer.
- Physical gathering can make the future product feel socially available before a purchase decision happens.
- The strategy remains uncertain because past major tournaments have produced mixed domestic-league effects.
