# Fire-Resistant Building Envelope

Fire-resistant building envelope is the layered exterior strategy described in [[tech-20260112-0112-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260112-0112-mp-tech-pod-128]]. [[DavidBrancaccio]] says his rebuilt house will use mineral wool insulation that does not burn, with stucco over it, around a [[CrossLaminatedTimber]] structure.

The concept matters because it shifts [[FireResilientConstruction]] away from material slogans. A house may use mass timber, concrete, insulation, stucco, vents, windows, and detailing in combination; the source only discusses some of those layers, but its core point is that fire has to get through the whole assembly before the structure is lost.

## Key Claims
- Fire protection is created through layers rather than one named material.
- Mineral wool and stucco are presented as exterior defenses around the house.
- The envelope strategy complements CLT; it does not make CLT a standalone answer.
- Building-envelope choices connect homeowner design decisions to [[ClimateAdaptation]] and [[FireTechClimateResilience]].
