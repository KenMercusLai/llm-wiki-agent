# Blindness-Memory Art Ideal

Blindness-memory art ideal is the miniature-painting idea that the highest painter does not merely copy what the eyes see, but draws from memory, tradition, and an inward image shaped by masters. [[154-wode-mingzi-jiao-hong-yong-yiqian-lingyi-ye-de-xintiao-wancheng-zhechang-mousha-xia-919262400]] develops the concept through Olive's stories of blind painters and through Master Osman's movement through the treasury.

The ideal has two sides in the source. It can be reverent: the old masters' images are preserved in memory and the painter prepares all life for the loss of ordinary sight. It can also be tragic: Master Osman's self-blinding expresses devotion to [[OttomanMiniaturePainting]] while also revealing despair at its historical decline under [[FrankishPortraiturePressure]].
