# Subjective Reality Construction / 主观现实建构

Subjective reality construction is [[132-zhenglun-yu-shuofu-women-weishenme-chaojia-zenme-cai-suan-ying-807583318]]'s core claim that perceived reality is a brain-built model rather than a direct copy of the outside world. The episode uses the dress-color dispute, kitten visual-development experiments, restored-sight examples, and the crayon-box task to show that perception depends on sensory input, expectation, prior experience, and perspective-taking capacity.

The concept extends [[SocialRealityConstruction]] from social judgment into perception itself. The source does not deny external reality; it argues that people encounter it through models, and those models can make disagreement feel like evidence that the other person is irrational even when the deeper problem is different construction.

## Key Claims
- Felt certainty is not proof that perception is unmediated.
- Experience shapes what information becomes signal rather than noise.
- Disagreement can begin before explicit ideology, at the level of what the person experiences as real.
- [[RationalHumility]] matters because even direct-seeming perception may already include interpretation.
