# Heritage Tourism Pivot

Heritage tourism pivot is the shift from a fading local industry into visitor-facing work that sells the place, memory, and symbols of that industry. [[the-little-pet-fish-that-saved-a-town-in-the-amazon]] adds the concept through [[Barcelos]], where the [[CardinalTetra]] festival keeps the old [[PiaberoEconomy]] visible while the town increasingly earns from [[PeacockBass]] sport-fishing tourism.

The concept is intentionally mixed. Tourism can bring money, hotel expansion, and jobs, as [[VandaIlsonSiqueira]] says in the episode. But [[AramaraCastro]]'s preference for ornamental fishing shows the cost: independent production can become service labor, and living work can become performance, memory, or branding.

## Key Claims
- A town can preserve an industry's symbols after the industry no longer supports most livelihoods.
- Heritage branding can help attract visitors, but it may also signal that the older work has become nostalgia.
- Tourism income should be judged by who owns the hotels, guides, agencies, and services, not only by visitor counts.
- The pivot can preserve place identity while changing labor relations and bargaining power.
- The concept overlaps with tourism dependence when the new visitor economy becomes the main replacement for the old export economy.
