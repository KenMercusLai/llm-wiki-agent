# Tax Shelter Disclosure Regulation

Tax shelter disclosure regulation is the enforcement tool discussed in [[the-leaked-tapes-that-show-how-the-rich-avoid-taxes]], where the [[InternalRevenueService]] proposed a 2023 rule requiring taxpayers and advisers involved in the [[MaltaTaxLoophole]] to identify themselves and disclose the strategy. The episode frames the rule as a way to move from general warning to specific enforcement.

The concept matters because sophisticated shelters can be hard to police if the government does not know who used them. Disclosure changes the information balance: taxpayers and promoters must come forward, pay what is owed, or face penalties if they hide participation.

## Key Claims
- Disclosure rules can convert an opaque tax shelter into a list of taxpayers, advisers, and transactions the government can examine.
- The source says the proposed Malta rule did not become final by the time of the episode.
- Stalled disclosure weakens [[TaxEnforcementCapacity]] because the IRS may know a strategy exists without having complete user and promoter data.
- The source connects disclosure to political timing, agency staffing, and leadership conflicts rather than only technical drafting.
