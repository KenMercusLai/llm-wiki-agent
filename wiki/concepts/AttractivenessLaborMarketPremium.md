# Attractiveness Labor-Market Premium / 外貌劳动力市场溢价

Attractiveness labor-market premium is the source's claim that perceived beauty can affect wages and lifetime labor income. In [[155-meimao-neng-dang-fan-chi-ma-xiang-zhuanqian-gai-zuo-dian-sha-jieju-shi-ying-bimian-shenme-xingwei-jingjixue-siwei-you-shenme-yong-920167210]], the host cites [[DanielHamermesh]] and other research to describe both a wage premium for attractive workers and a penalty for workers judged unattractive.

The concept stays source-scoped because the transcript summarizes research without giving detailed sample conditions. Its wiki role is therefore not to turn the percentages into personal advice, but to record the episode's practical and ethical point: if appearance has economic effects, then ordinary people need clearer cost-benefit thinking around beauty labor, and institutions need to recognize discrimination rather than hide it behind politeness.

## Key Claims
- Appearance can function as a labor-market signal even when that signal is unfair or weakly related to actual competence.
- The episode says male attractiveness effects can be at least as salient as female effects, challenging a simple gender stereotype.
- Cosmetic intervention should be evaluated by expected marginal return rather than by anxiety alone.
- Beauty's labor-market value may come partly through confidence, activities, communication, and perceived team atmosphere, not only the face itself.
