# Boomless Cruise

Boomless cruise is the [[BoomSupersonic]] flight-profile capability described by [[BlakeScholl]] in [[tsr-s5-blakescholl-v3-finalaudio-tsr-s5-blakescholl-v3-finalaudio]]. The source says Boom did not begin by trying to solve sonic boom because its first business case could focus on overwater premium travel. Boom revisited the possibility only after engine and flight-profile work changed what seemed possible.

Scholl explains the mechanism through refraction and Mach cutoff. Weather, altitude, and speed affect whether the sonic boom bends upward and completes a U-turn before reaching the ground. In that account, software computes a safe speed for the conditions, while the engines must support the required profile.

The concept matters because it links aircraft design, engine ownership, operations software, and regulation. Scholl says the [[RollsRoyce|Rolls-Royce]] supplier path would not have enabled boomless cruise, while Boom's own engine path did. That makes boomless cruise part of the episode's [[CrisisForcedVerticalIntegration]] and [[SupersonicRegulatorySpeedLimit]] branch, not just a technical detail.

## Key Claims
- Boomless cruise is presented as an operational and control problem as well as an airframe problem.
- The capability depends on weather, altitude, speed, software calculation, and engine performance.
- In the source's company-history account, it emerged from later constraints rather than from Boom's original founding requirement.
