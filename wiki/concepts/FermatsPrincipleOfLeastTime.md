# Fermat's Principle of Least Time

Fermat's principle of least time is the physics concept used in [[104-ni-yisheng-de-gushi-zhongdian-yijing-zhuding-haiyao-chufa-ma-739384680]] to explain why [[StoryOfYourLife|《你一生的故事》]] links alien language with time. The episode introduces it through light refraction: the path of light can be described as the path taking least time between points.

For the episode, the conceptual shock is that the principle sounds as if light already "knows" the endpoint. That makes it a bridge from physics to [[HeptapodB]] and [[NonlinearTimePerception]], because both challenge the human habit of explaining everything only from prior cause to later effect.

## Key Claims
- The principle gives the episode a physical analogy for whole-path thinking.
- It does not need to be read as literal conscious foresight by light; the point is the contrast with ordinary causal intuition.
- The Chiang story uses the analogy to make language, physics, and narrative form mutually reinforcing.
- The principle helps explain why [[GaryDonnelly|Gary Donnelly / 盖雷·唐纳利]]'s refraction experiment works better for the heptapods than more familiar Newtonian sequences.
