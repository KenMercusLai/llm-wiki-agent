# Procurement Records Journalism

Procurement records journalism is the use of public government purchasing databases to investigate agencies, vendors, and operational priorities. In [[betty-boop-excel-olympics-penny-isms-our-2026-valentines]], [[NickFountain]] praises [[404Media|404 Media]] for showing subscribers how to search federal procurement records while reporting on [[USImmigrationAndCustomsEnforcement|ICE]] and technology.

The method matters because contracts can reveal operational facts before agencies or vendors package them for public relations. The episode says 404 Media's training moves from ordinary contracts such as janitorial services to technology providers, making procurement a practical route into accountability reporting.

## Key Claims
- Public procurement databases can expose what an agency buys, which vendors it relies on, and what capabilities it may be building.
- Reporting method can itself be public-interest knowledge when it teaches readers how to verify or extend the work.
- Vendor descriptions still require care: the episode preserves [[Palantir]]'s dispute over describing an ICE-related tool as a "Google Maps for ICE."
- Procurement records complement, but do not replace, legal analysis of surveillance power, privacy rules, or field use.
