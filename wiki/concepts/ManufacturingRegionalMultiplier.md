# Manufacturing Regional Multiplier

Manufacturing regional multiplier is the local job spillover attached to factory employment. In [[why-are-we-so-obsessed-with-manufacturing]], [[EnricoMoretti]] is cited for an estimate that each manufacturing job creates 1.6 additional local jobs outside manufacturing over 10 years.

The episode uses the multiplier to support but also limit the factory-revival argument. Manufacturing can create supplier demand and service jobs through local spending, yet the same source says an average tech job creates five additional jobs, so manufacturing is not automatically the strongest regional growth lever.

## Key Claims
- Manufacturing jobs can support non-manufacturing local employment through supplier networks and worker spending.
- The cited 1.6 multiplier is positive, but lower than the tech multiplier cited in the source.
- The multiplier depends on whether factories are embedded in a local ecosystem rather than isolated assembly sites.
- Regional policy should compare manufacturing with other [[TradableSectorLocalGrowth|tradable sectors]] instead of assuming factories are uniquely powerful.
