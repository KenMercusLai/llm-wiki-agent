# Forensic Entomology

Forensic entomology is the use of insects and their life stages to infer postmortem interval, movement, exposure, and environmental conditions. [[121-jijian-fayi-jianshi-shouce-yiqie-fanzui-jieyou-henji-775064343]] presents blowflies and maggots as one of the relatively useful tools for [[ForensicDeathTimeInference]], while carefully listing their limits.

The source gives a stage logic: eggs, maggots, pupae, and adult flies imply different time windows. It also stresses the complications: flies may not lay eggs at night, winter activity may be low, cold can pause maggot development, and investigators must preserve live larvae and shed cases correctly.

## Key Claims
- Insect evidence can be more informative than broad decomposition appearance in some cases.
- Insect stages provide time windows, not exact timestamps.
- Weather, season, light, access to the body, and preservation method can all alter the inference.
- Entomology should be combined with decomposition, stomach contents, pollen, toxicology, and scene evidence.
