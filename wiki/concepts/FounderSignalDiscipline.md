# Founder Signal Discipline

Founder signal discipline is the startup-method layer in [[ai4s-xuyao-kuangren-yu-yexinjia-duihua-yinglingdian-odin-ruguo-shen-cunzai-wo-zenneng-rongren-ziji-bushi-shen-gonglu-boke-lhceyip6dqomrwk38uvqjwoomxyz]]. [[HaotianOdin]] describes entrepreneurship as a practice of filtering noise: external investor heuristics, internal ego, public-persona temptation, status labels, and fashionable keywords can all pull a founder away from the problem that made the company worth starting.

The source gives the concept a distinctive vocabulary. Odin talks about entrepreneurship as "修道," says his daily method is waiting, discipline, and thinking, and criticizes fundraising stories that stitch together pipelines, agents, robotics, physical intelligence, anti-aging, and other fashionable labels to satisfy the market. The point is not anti-commercial purity; the same interview says he now takes commercialization seriously from day one. The discipline is to keep business design answerable to the original scientific intention.

## Key Claims
- Founders need to distinguish useful market feedback from narrative drift.
- Financing can alienate a founder by making the company perform for VC taste instead of users, markets, or the founder's own serious problem.
- Status, title, and prestigious institutions can become constraints when they substitute for direct ownership of the work.
- Founder self-belief can itself become noise when it hardens into ego or public-image attachment.
- Commercialization still matters; discipline means aligning commercialization with the real work, not refusing money or market contact.
