# Enterprise Custom Delivery

Enterprise custom delivery is the 2B software implementation pattern used in [[ep128-cong-palantir-dao-openai-fde-hui-chengwei-ai-shidai-zui-zhongyao-de-xin-gangwei-ltozkutz-gvff4xu-feyzflhvz2u]] to de-mystify [[ForwardDeployedEngineer]] work. The episode argues that many of the supposedly unusual [[Palantir]] FDE conditions are familiar in Chinese enterprise software: customer processes are special, historical systems are messy, implicit business knowledge is undocumented, and delivery teams must handle product design, engineering, coordination, training, and acceptance.

The source's sharper point is that the hard part is not naming the role. It is building a team that can enter the customer site, understand local workflows, ship working systems, keep costs under control, and convert one-off delivery into reusable product capability. Without that product-feedback loop, enterprise custom delivery becomes expensive project work rather than compounding software.

## Key Claims
- Traditional custom delivery can involve project managers, product managers, R&D leads, training, resource coordination, and customer acceptance.
- The hardest talent profile combines business understanding, customer communication, engineering ability, product judgment, and internal resource leverage.
- [[ForwardDeployedEngineer]] differs from ordinary delivery when it turns implementation learning into product modules, model feedback, and repeatable deployment patterns.
- AI raises the value of custom delivery because enterprise AI use cases are still uncertain, but it does not remove the cost, scaling, and talent-density constraints of on-site implementation.
