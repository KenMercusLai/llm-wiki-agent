# Credibility Cascade

Credibility cascade is the pattern where respected people or institutions become evidence for one another, letting a weak claim appear validated because everyone assumes someone else has checked it. [[tsr-s4-tylershultzpart1-final-tsr-s4-tylershultzpart1-final]] adds the concept through [[Theranos]] and [[GeorgeShultz|George Shultz]].

[[TylerShultz|Tyler Shultz]] says [[ElizabethHolmes|Elizabeth Holmes]] created an alternate reality by surrounding herself with credible people who pointed to one another as proof. George's belief mattered both externally and inside Tyler's family: if someone as respected as George trusted Elizabeth, others could treat that trust as a substitute for understanding the lab evidence.

The episode shows why credibility cascades are especially dangerous in technical and regulated domains. Board prestige, family trust, investor confidence, and media narratives can all reinforce each other while the actual validation data remains invisible or misunderstood. The antidote is not cynicism toward all credentials; it is independent evidence access and governance that asks who has actually verified the claim.

[[tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3-tsr-s4-tylershultzpart2-v3]] extends the cascade past the first evidence conflict. Tyler says George continued to support Elizabeth after [[WallStreetJournal|Wall Street Journal]] reporting and regulatory scrutiny, and that the Theranos board later issued a statement giving Elizabeth full support. The source shows a stronger form of the pattern: even when outside evidence appears, prestige, family loyalty, money, and belief in a grand vision can keep the cascade intact.

## Key Claims
- Prestige can become circular evidence when each participant assumes another participant performed the real diligence.
- A respected board member can unintentionally raise the social cost of dissent if employees or family members fear contradicting that person's judgment.
- Technical illiteracy makes credibility cascades stronger because non-experts cannot inspect the claim directly.
- Credibility should be paired with traceable verification, especially in patient-facing or safety-critical systems.
- A credibility cascade can persist after contradictory evidence if public loyalty, financial exposure, and mission belief remain tied together.
