# Fisheries Resource Management

Fisheries resource management is the governance problem of limiting catch today so a fishery can recover tomorrow. [[shangye-xiaoyang-45-eerninuo-yueda-yu-yuegui-1000164352]] makes the problem concrete through [[Peru]]'s anchoveta fishery: when [[ElNino]] weakens the food base for [[PeruvianAnchoveta|anchoveta]], catch volumes and juvenile shares become signals for whether continued fishing risks longer-term collapse.

The source presents bans and quotas as a painful but rational tradeoff. A ban worsens immediate [[FishmealSupplyChain|fishmeal]] scarcity and price pressure, but the 1972 collapse is used as a warning that overfishing during a strong climate shock can destroy future supply and turn a fisheries event into a broader economic and political crisis.

## Key Claims
- Juvenile-heavy catches are a stock-health warning, not just a normal catch-quality issue.
- Quotas and bans can be supply shocks in the short run and conservation tools in the long run.
- Climate variability makes management harder because natural population swings and human extraction interact.
- A fishery that supplies global feed inputs can create wider food-price exposure when management restricts catch.
