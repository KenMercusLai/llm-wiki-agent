# Spectral Evidence / 幽灵证据

Spectral evidence / 幽灵证据 is the [[SalemWitchTrials|塞勒姆猎巫]] evidentiary failure foregrounded in [[91-liewu-sailemu-1692-cong-yizheng-kaishi-714732018]]. In the episode's account, courts allowed claims that accusers saw an accused person's specter, or that an accused person invisibly harmed them, to count in the legal process.

The concept matters because it reverses [[EvidenceOverTestimony]]. Instead of requiring independent corroboration for extraordinary claims, the procedure lets performance, vision, fear, and religious interpretation become evidence. That makes it a legal instance of [[ArbitraryAuthorityProcedure]] and a central accelerant inside [[WitchHuntMechanism]].

## Key Claims
- Spectral evidence makes private perception publicly punishable.
- It is structurally hard to rebut because the accused cannot disprove another person's claimed vision or invisible injury.
- Courtroom bodily performances become dangerous when interpreted as proof rather than as symptoms, suggestion, pressure, or staged behavior.
- The episode treats the later rejection of spectral evidence as one reason the trials could stop.
- As a broader warning, spectral evidence names any procedure that lets unfalsifiable invisible harm substitute for testable proof.
