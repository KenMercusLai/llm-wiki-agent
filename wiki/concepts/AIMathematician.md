# AI Mathematician

An AI mathematician is the source's broader target for [[AIForMath]]: a system that can prove, conjecture, formalize, use mathematical knowledge, and eventually help expand research frontiers. In [[137-dui-hong-letong-de-4-xiaoshi-fangtan-ai-for-math-ba-shuxue-biancheng-lean-shuxue-tianshu-zhong-de-zhengming-zhijue-bei-chuangzao-yu-bei-faxian-de-lha-faiwxtget0qmbcosts3cb5vb]], [[HongLetong]] treats [[AxiomProver]] as a step toward this target rather than the whole target.

## Key Claims
- Proof is necessary but incomplete: mathematical creativity also involves asking natural questions, finding useful definitions, and generating conjectures.
- Human mathematicians may shift toward higher abstraction, taste, direction-setting, and benchmark design as AI handles more formal proof labor.
- Machine proof can be correct without being elegant; the system may first become useful through brute-force formal reasoning before matching human intuition.
- A true AI mathematician needs [[AutoFormalization]] so it can read and convert ordinary mathematical literature, not only solve already-formalized statements.
- The source connects the idea to [[MathematicalAbundance]], where math supply expands dramatically and mathematicians allocate attention and compute toward the best problems.
