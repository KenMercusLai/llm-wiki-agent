# Earned Optimism / 走出来的乐天

Earned optimism is the episode's reading of [[SuShi|苏轼]]'s famous "乐天" as a capacity built through experience rather than a temperament untouched by suffering. In [[105-su-dongpo-yiqiang-haomai-zhiqi-yi-dupi-buhe-shiyi-741072645]], optimism is credible because Su Shi has known fear, prison, poverty, demotion, grief, and political danger.

The concept is clearest in the source's reading of [[DingFengBoSuShi|《定风波》]]. The poem's calm is not indifference to wind and rain; it is the ability to hear them and keep walking. [[Huangzhou|黄州]], [[Huizhou|惠州]], and [[Danzhou|儋州]] extend the same pattern across exile stages.

Earned optimism differs from passive consolation. It remains tied to [[ConscienceOverFaction]] and [[ScholarOfficialPublicDuty]]: Su Shi's peace is not withdrawal from the world, because he keeps writing, serving, building, teaching, feeding, and grieving.

## Key Claims
- Optimism is stronger when it has passed through fear and loss rather than denying them.
- A literary line about calm can become escapist if detached from the life pressure around it.
- Resilience can coexist with appetite, humor, anger, political imprudence, and grief.
- "此心安处" in the source means moral and relational steadiness, not merely comfortable surroundings.
