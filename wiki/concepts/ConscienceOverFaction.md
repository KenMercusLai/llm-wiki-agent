# Conscience Over Faction / 良知高于党派

Conscience over faction is the episode's frame for why [[SuShi|苏轼]] repeatedly became politically inconvenient. In [[105-su-dongpo-yiqiang-haomai-zhiqi-yi-dupi-buhe-shiyi-741072645]], Su Shi opposes harmful implementation of the [[WangAnshiReforms|王安石变法]], but later also objects when [[SimaGuang|司马光]] and the old party retaliate against reformers too broadly.

The concept turns "一肚皮不合时宜" from a personality joke into a political diagnosis. Su Shi is not presented as strategically neutral or above all conflict; he has strong views and poor caution. But his loyalty is to his judgment about public suffering and fairness rather than to a stable camp inside [[SongFactionalPolitics|宋代党争]].

## Key Claims
- Refusing factional convenience can look like inconsistency to party actors.
- Moral independence is not the same as political skill; it can increase personal exposure.
- A person can oppose a reform's implementation without endorsing revenge against reformers.
- Conscience becomes politically costly when institutions reward alignment more than judgment.
