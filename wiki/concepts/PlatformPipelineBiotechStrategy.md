# Platform-Pipeline Biotech Strategy

Platform-pipeline biotech strategy is the tradeoff in [[ai4s-xuyao-kuangren-yu-yexinjia-duihua-yinglingdian-odin-ruguo-shen-cunzai-wo-zenneng-rongren-ziji-bushi-shen-gonglu-boke-lhceyip6dqomrwk38uvqjwoomxyz]] between building a neutral [[AIDrugDiscoveryPlatform]] and owning drug pipelines directly. [[HaotianOdin]] says [[YinglingdianAI]] does not plan to build pipelines in the short term because pipeline work can distract from AI development and turn potential pharmaceutical customers into competitors.

The source does not make the platform route absolute. It acknowledges that drug pipelines can sometimes prove platform value, which creates a strategic tension: a platform may need credible biological results, but owning too much downstream asset work can change focus, capital needs, customer trust, and company identity. In the wiki, this sits beside [[MaterialsPipelineCompany]], where [[Kaiwuji]]'s materials source takes the opposite first move by using AI internally to discover and own valuable material IP.

## Key Claims
- Platform focus can preserve model-team attention and customer neutrality.
- Pipeline ownership can prove platform value, but it also introduces clinical, capital, regulatory, and competitive burdens.
- The right choice depends on whether the company is selling capability, owning assets, or using assets as proof.
- In AI-for-science companies, commercialization design is part of the technical strategy because feedback, customers, validation, and cash needs shape what gets built.
