# Lunar Resource Governance

Lunar resource governance is the rule-making problem raised in [[tech-20260211-0211-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260211-0211-mp-tech-pod-128]]: how countries and companies can operate on the [[Moon]], use resources, and stay safe without turning resource access into territorial ownership. [[SadiaPekkanen]] says space law does not allow claims to lunar territory, but the episode says commercial [[SpaceResourceExtraction]] is allowed.

The source treats governance as practical infrastructure. [[ArtemisAccords]] principles, transparency, safety-zone interpretation, technology placement, bases, research stations, and coordination among rivals all shape whether sustained lunar operations can happen without conflict.

## Key Claims
- Resource locations can create first-mover advantages even when territory cannot be claimed.
- Non-binding principles may still shape behavior if enough actors treat them as operating norms.
- [[China]] and [[Russia]] staying outside the [[ArtemisAccords]] makes diplomacy central to lunar operations.
- Governance rules for the Moon are also a starting point for later asteroid, Mars, and deeper-space resource questions.
