# Nonlinear Time Perception

Nonlinear time perception is the episode's frame for experiencing past, present, and future as a completed whole rather than as a sequence of causes followed by effects. In [[104-ni-yisheng-de-gushi-zhongdian-yijing-zhuding-haiyao-chufa-ma-739384680]], this frame emerges through [[StoryOfYourLife|《你一生的故事》]], [[HeptapodB]], and [[FermatsPrincipleOfLeastTime]].

The concept does not merely mean "time travel." It means that a mind may understand an event the way the episode describes light under Fermat's principle: not as local step-by-step reaction, but as a path whose endpoint belongs to the explanation from the start.

## Key Claims
- Nonlinear time challenges ordinary causal narration.
- A narrative can make readers feel the time problem by breaking chronological order.
- Whole-path perception shifts the free-will question from "can I change the future?" to "what does choosing mean if the future is already known?"
- [[MerchantAndAlchemistsGate|《商人和炼金术之门》]] gives a related fixed-time structure without making it identical to Louise's language-induced perception.
