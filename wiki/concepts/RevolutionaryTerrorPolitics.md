# Revolutionary Terror Politics / 革命恐怖政治

Revolutionary terror politics / 革命恐怖政治 is the mechanism [[125-mala-zhisi-beibi-de-ansha-huo-zhengyi-de-chujue-783972516]] sees emerging from the [[FrenchRevolution|French Revolution]]. It is not only killing by decree; it is the interaction of public fear, war, denunciation, factional purity, romantic virtue language, and the claim that enemies of the people must be eliminated.

The episode develops this through [[JeanPaulMarat|Marat]], the [[SeptemberMassacres|September Massacres]], the [[JacobinClub|Jacobin]] attack on the [[Girondins]], and [[MaximilienRobespierre|Robespierre]]'s virtue-centered politics. It also notes how the taboo against admitting partial interests made policy disputes, such as price limits, easier to recode as betrayal of the general will.

The concept extends [[PostVictoryOppressionRisk]] and [[PowerViolenceDistinction]]. Revolutionary victory does not guarantee humane politics; without durable institutions and pluralism, the winning side may treat coercion as proof of virtue.

## Key Claims
- Terror politics grows when fear, emergency, factional identity, and moral certainty reinforce each other.
- Denunciation can become a political technology when opponents are defined as traitors rather than rivals.
- Public transparency and sincerity can backfire when they are joined to suspicion, surveillance, and pressure to prove one's virtue.
- Price, food, and labor conflicts show that material interests continue even when revolutionary language refuses to name them.
- Revolutionary violence can claim to defend the people while making ordinary dissent dangerous.
