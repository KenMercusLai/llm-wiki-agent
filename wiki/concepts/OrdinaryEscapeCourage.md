# Ordinary Escape Courage

Ordinary escape courage is the unresolved action frame [[02-lihun-guodegang-dou-buru-laoshe-youyisi-525913046]] draws from the ending of [[LihunNovel|《离婚》]]. [[LaoLiLihun|老李]] is not a heroic rebel for most of the novel: he hesitates, fantasizes, fails to protect [[LiTaitaiLihun|李太太]], and watches stronger people act. Yet leaving Beiping after promotion, with wife, children, and [[DingErLihun|丁二]], still costs him real status and security.

The concept sits between [[ActionDefinesIdentity]] and [[ActionAgainstAnxiety]]. The source keeps two judgments in tension: [[DingErLihun|丁二]] may be the person who truly acts against evil, while Lao Li's departure may still be the ordinary person's available courage. Escape is not victory, but it can be a real choice when staying has become spiritual surrender.

## Key Claims
- A non-heroic person can still make a costly action that changes the moral meaning of their life.
- Escape should not be romanticized as total freedom; it may be limited, uncertain, and still preferable to surrender.
- Literary endings can preserve ambiguity when they show a move away from a system without proving the new life will work.
