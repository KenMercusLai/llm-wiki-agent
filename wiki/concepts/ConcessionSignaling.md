# Concession Signaling / 让步信号

Concession signaling is the information carried by a negotiator's concessions. In [[106-bie-zai-haipa-chongtu-kuai-lai-xuexi-tanpan-shu-742539645]], [[QinZong|秦总]] says concessions should be labelled because the other side may otherwise discount or forget them to avoid feeling reciprocity pressure.

The episode also treats concession size as a signal. Concessions commonly shrink as a party approaches its [[ReservationValue]], but a negotiator may also shrink concession size strategically to suggest that the bottom line is near. That makes concessions both a relationship tool and an information-management problem.

## Key Claims
- A concession should be made visible if the negotiator wants it to create reciprocal pressure.
- Conditional concessions can clarify expected exchange, but overusing them may reduce trust.
- Fast acceptance can lower the other side's satisfaction by making them think they overpaid.
- Concession patterns should update estimates of the [[ZoneOfPossibleAgreement]], but they can be strategic signals rather than truthful disclosure.
