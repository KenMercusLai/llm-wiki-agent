# Vigilante Justice Universalization Risk

Vigilante justice universalization risk is the source's reason for resisting the easy "替天行道" reading of the [[LuigiCaseSuspect|Luigi]] case. In [[sp-04-liuqing-linyao-qiangsha-yibao-jutou-an-beihou-de-sikao-shang-guanyu-mincui-sixing-he-sixing-790230209]], the guests accept that anger at [[UnitedHealthcare]] and [[USHealthInsuranceDenialPolitics|U.S. health-insurance dysfunction]] can make the act emotionally legible, but they reject turning that sympathy into a general permission for private punishment.

The core issue is universalization. If one person may kill because their target symbolizes injustice, another person may claim the same authority for a racist, authoritarian, sectarian, or conspiratorial definition of injustice. Once law is replaced by private certainty, "justice" depends on whoever is willing to act violently.

## Key Claims
- Moral sympathy for a specific target does not solve the institutional problem of who gets to define justice.
- Private punishment competes with the state's legal monopoly but does not automatically supply due process, evidence standards, proportionality, or accountability.
- A "heroic" target choice can still normalize a method that later actors may use against innocent or vulnerable people.
- The source separates intelligibility from justification: an act can reveal system failure without becoming a legitimate legal or political tool.
- Revolutionary violence and terrorism are treated as a continuum problem, not as categories that always separate cleanly in advance.
