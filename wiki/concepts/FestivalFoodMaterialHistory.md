# Festival Food Material History

Festival food material history is the pattern in [[zhongqiu-kuaile-669967741]] where a seasonal food becomes evidence for time, place, literature, tools, folklore, and old fear. The episode's case is Mid-Autumn crab reading through [[ZhonghuaXieshi|《中华蟹史》]], where eating crabs sits alongside mooncakes, waterways, "沪" as a crab-catching trace, [[HongLouMeng|《红楼梦》]]'s crab banquet, giant-crab myths, and records of people treating crabs as strange objects.

The concept extends [[MaterialHistoryNarrative]] by adding festival timing. An edible object is not only a commodity or ingredient; it can mark a season, organize family tables, invite literary quotation, preserve place-name memory, and reveal where familiarity with an animal was uneven. It also sits near [[ChineseFolkReligionLayering]] and [[QixiFestivalLayering]] because festivals collect foods, rituals, stories, markets, and local practices over time.

## Key Claims
- Seasonal foods can be read as material history when they connect eating practice with tools, waterways, markets, literary scenes, and folklore.
- A festival table can preserve older ecological and regional knowledge even when later eaters experience the food mainly as pleasure.
- Food familiarity is historical; animals now treated as ordinary delicacies could once appear monstrous, uncanny, or dangerous to people outside the eating region.
- Festival food reading should avoid turning one object into a total explanation of a holiday; it works best as a concrete entry point.
