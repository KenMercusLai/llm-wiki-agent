# Romantic Projection Trap

Romantic projection trap is the pattern [[02-lihun-guodegang-dou-buru-laoshe-youyisi-525913046]] uses to read [[LaoLiLihun|老李]]'s relation to [[MaShaonainaiLihun|马少奶奶]] in [[LihunNovel|《离婚》]]. Lao Li wants poetry, moonlight, romantic life, and escape from his arranged marriage, but the woman he imagines as an alternative is partly a projection of the life he has not built.

The trap becomes visible when Ma Shaoye returns and the imagined world of brave revolution and free love collapses into vulgarity. The episode's point is not that Lao Li's desire for a different life is fake; it is that fantasy can borrow another person as evidence before the self has done the harder work of acting.

## Key Claims
- Romantic dissatisfaction can be real while its chosen symbol remains unreliable.
- Imagined escape may preserve the existing trap if it substitutes projection for action.
- Literary satire can puncture fantasy without denying the pain that made fantasy attractive.
