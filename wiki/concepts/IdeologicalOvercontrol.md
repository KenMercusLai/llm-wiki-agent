# Ideological Overcontrol / 意识形态过度控制

Ideological overcontrol / 意识形态过度控制 is the source's pattern for a regime trying to make signs, morals, economy, kinship, ritual, and borders all obey one authoritative scheme. In [[126-wangmang-zhisi-shensheng-diguo-de-huixuanbiao-he-huixuanbiao-de-huixuanbiao-786255780]], [[WangMang|王莽]]'s attempt to "manage everything" links personal discipline, family purges, omen control, reforms, frontier hierarchy, and final violence.

The concept is not a claim that ideas do not matter. It is the opposite: the episode argues that ideas can matter too much when they outrun institutions, feedback, and limits on coercion. That is why [[ConfucianIdealGovernance]] and [[TuoguReformPolitics]] become dangerous when fused to unchecked imperial power.

## Key Claims
- Ideology can coordinate rule but also blind rulers to implementation failure.
- Overcontrol turns moral language into surveillance, purge, and coercion.
- A regime can become more brittle as it tries to regulate more domains.
- The source treats Wang Mang's final brutality as connected to the same pattern that earlier produced moral and omen legitimacy.
