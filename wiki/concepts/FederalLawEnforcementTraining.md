# Federal Law Enforcement Training

Federal law enforcement training is the formal preparation of federal agents and officers before field deployment. In [[the-ice-hiring-boom]], it appears through [[FederalLawEnforcementTrainingCenters]], which train recruits for [[USImmigrationAndCustomsEnforcement|ICE]], [[USBorderPatrol]], and [[USCustomsAndBorderProtection|CBP]].

The episode treats formal training as necessary but insufficient. [[MarkBrown]] describes restraint-oriented crowd-control instruction, while [[TriciaMcLaughlin]] and [[RichardBlumenthal]]'s document dispute leaves the amount of current ICE training unresolved.

## Key Claims
- Training quality includes scenario practice, crowd-control doctrine, language capacity, and legal boundaries, not only total classroom weeks.
- Reported reductions or substitutions matter most when rapid hiring raises the number of new officers entering field supervision.
- Formal training can be overridden when [[FieldTrainingOfficerEffects]], peer expectations, and supervisor orders teach different norms.
- Public disputes over training hours should be kept source-scoped when government statements and oversight documents conflict.
