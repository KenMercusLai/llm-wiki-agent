# Cost Cap Economics

Cost cap economics is the use of spending limits to make sports teams more financially viable while preserving competition. In [[formula-1-1]], [[LibertyMedia]] helps introduce a cap on major [[FormulaOne]] car spending, excluding drivers, top executives, marketing, and power units.

The episode treats cost caps as a turning point for team enterprise value. If team revenue can rise through media, sponsorship, hospitality, and distributions while technical spending is bounded, teams such as [[RedBullRacing]] and [[MercedesF1]] become investable businesses rather than prestige projects with unlimited loss potential.

## Key Claims
- Spending caps can turn participation from a financial arms race into an asset-value strategy.
- Exclusions matter because not all competitive or brand spending is treated equally.
- Cost caps improve [[LeagueStakeholderAlignment]] if teams believe the rules are enforced and distributions are fair.
- Bounded spending can make sponsorship and media growth show up in team profitability.
