# Locard's Exchange Principle

Locard's exchange principle is the forensic premise that contact between people, objects, and places leaves reciprocal traces. [[121-jijian-fayi-jianshi-shouce-yiqie-fanzui-jieyou-henji-775064343]] translates this into the episode's title logic: every act leaves some kind of trace, even if that trace is small, degraded, ambiguous, or never found.

The episode illustrates the principle with ordinary examples such as cat hair, shoe mud, and small environmental residues. The important point is not that every trace solves a case. It is that contact creates evidence opportunities, and [[ForensicScience|forensic science]] depends on noticing, preserving, comparing, and explaining those opportunities with appropriate confidence.

## Key Claims
- Contact usually changes both sides of an encounter.
- Trace evidence can be microscopic, delayed, transferred indirectly, or mixed with innocent background traces.
- The principle supports investigation, but it does not by itself prove identity, intent, timing, or guilt.
- Good use of the principle requires [[ObservationBeforeInference]] and [[ScientificSamplingDiscipline]].
