# Rational Worldview Collapse

Rational worldview collapse is the pattern where a person reaches a conclusion so destabilizing that the very system used to find truth no longer feels habitable. In [[104-ni-yisheng-de-gushi-zhongdian-yijing-zhuding-haiyao-chufa-ma-739384680]], this is clearest in [[DivisionByZero|《除以零》]], where a mathematician confronts a self-consistent route to contradiction.

The source also applies the pattern more broadly to [[TedChiang|Ted Chiang / 江峰南]]'s fiction. [[StoryOfYourLife|《你一生的故事》]] destabilizes linear time, [[TowerOfBabylon|《巴比伦之塔》]] destabilizes cosmological common sense, and [[UnderstandTedChiang|《领悟》]] destabilizes the assumption that more intelligence simply grants more control.

## Key Claims
- Rationality can generate crises from inside its own methods, not only from external irrationality.
- The collapse is emotional and relational as well as intellectual.
- A person who crosses a cognitive boundary may become hard to reach by people still living inside the old frame.
- The pattern complements [[PureRationalityTrap]]: the problem is not only cold reason, but also reason encountering an unbearable truth.
