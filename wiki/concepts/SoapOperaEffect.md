# Soap Opera Effect

The soap opera effect is the disliked visual artifact discussed in [[the-latest-tv-innovations-have-their-critics]] where [[MotionSmoothing]] makes movies look overly sharp, digitized, hyper-real, or like high-speed video. The episode references [[TomCruise]]'s 2018 PSA as evidence that the artifact became a public film-viewing issue.

The concept matters because it turns a setting that appears objectively beneficial - smoother motion - into a taste and intent problem. The same frame insertion that can reduce [[TVMotionStutter]] can also make cinematic content feel unlike cinema.

## Key Claims
- The effect comes from smoothing systems that insert generated frames.
- It can make actors and sets look too present, sharp, or video-like.
- It is controversial because it conflicts with the expected motion texture of film.
- The artifact explains why many viewers reject global motion smoothing even when they dislike stutter.
