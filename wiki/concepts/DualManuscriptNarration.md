# 双手稿叙事 / Dual Manuscript Narration

Dual manuscript narration is the narrative device foregrounded in [[144-bei-zuzhou-de-munaiyi-fuermosi-hui-he-laoshe-lianshou-tanan-ma-878168738]] through [[TheCursedMummy|《被诅咒的木乃伊》]]. The novel frames the same events through a [[NatsumeSoseki|夏目漱石]] manuscript and a [[JohnWatson|华生]] manuscript, making the reader compare not only clues but also narrator temperament, cultural distance, and genre expectation.

The device changes how [[SherlockHolmes|福尔摩斯]] is perceived. In Soseki's telling, Holmes looks unstable, frightening, and absurd; in Watson's telling, Holmes becomes the familiar rational detective. The difference creates a mild Rashomon effect without abandoning detective reconstruction: the case still has an answer, but the emotional and comic meaning of each scene depends on who is narrating it.

This concept sits near [[MultiPerspectiveMurderNarration]], but it is narrower. Multi-perspective narration can disperse a whole novel across many voices; dual manuscript narration asks two authority claims to correct, parody, or destabilize each other.

## Key Claims
- Competing manuscripts can make narration itself part of a mystery.
- A familiar genre voice can normalize behavior that another narrator experiences as bizarre.
- The device works especially well in [[HolmesPasticheTradition|Holmes pastiche]] because Watson already carries reader expectations about how Holmes should sound.
- The same facts can be compatible with different affective readings, so [[InterpretationAndOverinterpretation]] remains relevant even after the case is solved.
