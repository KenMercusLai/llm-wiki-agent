# Community-Embedded Business

Community-embedded business is the pattern where a company does more than sell to a community: it hires inside it, builds institutions around it, finances related organizations, sponsors its cultural channels, and becomes visible evidence of collective possibility. [[how-black-hair-care-grew-black-power]] adds the concept through [[JohnsonProducts]], [[IndependenceBank]], and [[SoulTrain]].

The source's version is grounded in Black hair care. [[GeorgeEJohnson]] and [[JoanJohnson]] built products for Black consumers, hired mostly Black workers, helped create credit access through Independence Bank, supported [[MartinLutherKingJr]]'s organization, and funded national visibility through Soul Train sponsorship.

## Key Claims
- A community-embedded business can convert customer insight into jobs, credit, media, and symbolic visibility, not only profit.
- The same embeddedness can strengthen [[ConsumerBrandMoat]] because customers see the company as part of a shared cultural world.
- Community embeddedness is fragile when outside capital, public-company governance, or larger incumbents change who controls the category.
