# Consider-The-Opposite Bias Correction / 反向设想偏见校正

Consider-the-opposite bias correction is the source's practical method for reducing motivated perception: force yourself to ask whether the same evidence would look equally strong if it supported the opposing side. In [[123-renxing-shiyan-dangxin-ni-ziji-779479569]], the hosts contrast this with the weaker instruction to "be objective," which did not meaningfully reduce bias in the capital-punishment study they discuss.

The method works because it makes counter-argument concrete. It does not demand emotional neutrality; it asks the person to simulate a rival standpoint step by step before deciding how convincing the evidence really is.

## Key Claims
- Abstract objectivity reminders are often too vague to change judgment.
- Bias correction improves when the person must generate reasons the opposing side might find the evidence persuasive.
- The method is especially useful when a study, media story, or political claim confirms an existing position.
- It turns [[RationalHumility]] into a procedure rather than a self-image.
