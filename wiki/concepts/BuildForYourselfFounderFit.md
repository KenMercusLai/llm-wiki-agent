# Build For Yourself Founder Fit

Build for yourself founder fit is the pattern where a founder's strongest product insight starts from a tool they personally want and are impatient to use. [[tsr-s4-ericm-v2-tsr-s4-ericm-v2]] adds the concept through [[EricMigicovsky]], who says both [[Pebble]] and [[Beeper]] began because he wanted the products himself.

The concept is narrower than all [[FounderProductFit]]. Eric's personal need made smartwatch notifications concrete: biking with an iPhone in the Netherlands made glanceable alerts valuable, and years of wearing his own device gave him strong product intuition. Later, fragmented chat networks made Beeper feel like another personal tool. But the source also shows the limit: building for oneself can start a company, while [[FounderUserObsession]], manufacturing capability, capital discipline, and [[ProductVisionDrift]] decide whether it remains durable.

## Key Claims
- Personal use can create unusually specific product requirements before broad market research exists.
- Building for oneself is strongest when the founder's pain is shared by a reachable group of users who will change behavior or pay.
- The pattern can mislead founders if they treat their own need as a complete company vision.
- Repeat founders may discover that their durable motivation is a kind of building, not a single market category.
