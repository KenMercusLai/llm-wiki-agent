# Commercial Fusion Power

Commercial fusion power is the attempt to turn fusion from a physics and prototype challenge into deployable electricity infrastructure. [[tsr-s5-davidkirtley-v2-audio-tsr-s5-davidkirtley-v2-audio]] adds the concept through [[DavidKirtley]] and [[Helion]], whose stated product is low-cost, clean, safe baseload generators.

The concept is not just "fusion works." In Kirtley's source account, commercial fusion requires a chain: fuel choice, extreme plasma conditions, [[FusionEnergyRecovery]], safe high-voltage operation, radiation shielding, permitting, customer contracts, utility interconnection, precision manufacturing, and a business capable of deploying far beyond one historic plant.

Commercial fusion also changes the AI and industrial power branch of the wiki. [[Microsoft]] and [[Nucor]] appear as early customer cases, while Kirtley says future generators could sit near data centers, factories, and manufacturing sites. That links fusion to [[AIEnergyBottleneck]], [[DataCenterOnsitePower]], and [[DataCenterPowerBottleneck]], but as a future clean baseload route rather than a near-term natural-gas workaround.

## Key Claims
- Fusion commercialization should be judged by delivered electricity, cost, safety, permitting, and deployment rate, not only by scientific demonstration.
- Customer agreements can force accountability when a company promises power delivery on a timeline.
- The scale target is industrial: Kirtley says proving fusion once or building one power plant would not count as success if the company cannot deploy at gigawatt scale.
- Commercial fusion depends on adjacent technology curves such as computing, fiber optics, power electronics, programmable logic, and manufacturing precision.
