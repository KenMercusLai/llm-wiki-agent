# Legal Interest Individual Reduction

Legal interest individual reduction is the episode's way of handling the distinction between rights-infringement theory and legal-interest theory. [[100-fa-lv-de-bei-lun-xie-tian-xie-di-xing-hao-wo-men-hai-you-luo-xiang-733477302]] says criminal law can protect legal interests beyond narrow individual claims, but warns that collective interests become dangerous when they cannot be translated back into actual people harmed.

The source uses the postwar German move to reduce supra-individual legal interests back to individual interests as a safeguard. Environmental harm, for example, can count because damaged air, land, water, or health ultimately affects persons rather than only an abstract state or moral image.

## Key Claims
- Rights-infringement theory keeps criminal law close to persons and their claims.
- Legal-interest theory can capture shared harms, but it needs an individual-rights anchor.
- Collective goods become risky criminal-law grounds when they cannot be connected to concrete human loss.
- The reduction test helps keep [[CriminalLawAsPowerLimitation]] from being swallowed by broad state purposes.
