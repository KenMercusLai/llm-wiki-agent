# Social Reality Construction / 社会现实建构

[[132-zhenglun-yu-shuofu-women-weishenme-chaojia-zenme-cai-suan-ying-807583318]] adds a perception-level precursor through [[SubjectiveRealityConstruction]]. Before social judgment becomes argument, the episode says people may already inhabit different felt realities: the dress-color case, visual-development experiments, and restored-sight examples show why perception itself can be model-built rather than directly copied.

Social reality construction is the episode's premise that people do not experience social reality as a neutral recording. In [[123-renxing-shiyan-dangxin-ni-ziji-779479569]], the hosts use examples such as color perception, cultural beauty standards, filial norms, and attribution needs to argue that social judgment is built from perception, biology, culture, identity, and available explanations.

The concept is not pure relativism. The source still values [[ScientificSkepticism]] and experiment, but it warns that felt objectivity can hide how much interpretation has already happened.

## Key Claims
- People seek attribution because explanations provide order, prediction, and control.
- Culture can make the same body, family relation, or public behavior carry different meanings.
- Intuition is useful for fast response but unreliable as a final judge.
- The more natural a judgment feels, the more it may need [[RationalHumility]] and [[ObservationBeforeInference]].
