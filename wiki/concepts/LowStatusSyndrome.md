# Low-Status Syndrome / 低地位综合症

Low-status syndrome / 低地位综合症 is the episode's term for the chronic vigilance, comparison, anxiety, and health cost of feeling socially lower than others. In [[145-jidu-yu-bishi-wo-gai-ruhe-duobi-zhe-shuangxiang-de-dujian-882378647]], it appears when people keep scanning higher-status targets because the gap feels relevant to safety, dignity, and future access.

The concept is not a claim that low status mechanically creates bad character. The episode uses it to explain why long-term inequality and humiliation can make envy, resentment, and attack more tempting, especially when people cannot act directly on the underlying gap.

## Key Claims
- Chronic low-position awareness can trap attention on higher-status people and on signs of being looked down on.
- The harm is bodily as well as emotional: long-term status vigilance is presented as a health burden, not only a mood.
- Envy can become corrosive when it protects self-worth by making the other person's advantage feel illegitimate by default.
- Low-status resentment can be redirected into online attack, conspiracy, or shared group hostility when concrete action feels blocked.
- Reducing the harm requires more than asking people to "stop comparing"; comparison has to become less total, less humiliating, and more connected to possible action.
