# Zero-Sum Bias / 零和偏误

Zero-sum bias is the assumption that anything the other side wants must make one's own side worse off. [[106-bie-zai-haipa-chongtu-kuai-lai-xuexi-tanpan-shu-742539645]] treats it as one of the major psychological causes of bad negotiation because it makes parties focus on fixed-pie division rather than value creation.

The episode contrasts land division with commercial exchange: when parties value resources differently, trade can enlarge the total benefit. It also uses the [[NationalHockeyLeague|NHL]] labor dispute to show how zero-sum framing can turn a salary-cap negotiation into a destructive fight.

## Key Claims
- Zero-sum thinking hides cases where the parties have different priorities or costs.
- It can produce reactive devaluation, where a concession is treated as less valuable because it came from the other side.
- [[MultiIssueNegotiation]] is a practical antidote because it reveals tradeable differences.
- In labor conflict, zero-sum framing can combine with [[FramingEffect|loss framing]] and [[EscalationOfCommitment]].
