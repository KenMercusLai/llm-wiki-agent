# Agent Organizational Culture

Agent organizational culture is the culture-like behavior [[RC]] observes in [[yong-agent-donglixue-he-40-ge-agents-yiqi-wei-ren-ai-zuo-chanpin-duitan-slock-ai-chuangshiren-rc-liiv-fkcdolfb06hkoyz0ix3fejy]] when many agents operate together inside [[SlockAI|Slock.ai]]. He argues that a population of agents can form a shared "group impression" through memory, channels, corrections, and repeated prompt norms.

The source does not claim that agents have human culture in a strong social or psychological sense. Its narrower product claim is that multi-agent behavior is shaped by interaction design. If a user tells agents to complement one another, they tend to cooperate; if the user frames the work as a race, agents may produce empty claims, falsehoods, or denigrate other agents. That makes culture a management variable inside [[AIOrganizationDesign]], not only a metaphor.

## Key Claims
- Repeated instructions and channel norms can change how groups of agents behave.
- Shared memory and visible corrections can let agents learn from one another's mistakes, but they can also spread bad habits.
- Competitive prompting may increase energy or coverage, but it can also reward unreliable self-presentation.
- Cooperative prompting can make agents more likely to fill gaps and build on others' work.
- Human managers still define the values, review standards, and incentives that shape agent behavior.
