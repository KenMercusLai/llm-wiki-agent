# Crypto Payment Practicality Gap

Crypto payment practicality gap is the difference between visible acceptance of cryptocurrency and being able to rely on it across everyday life. [[tech-20260114-0114-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260114-0114-mp-tech-pod-128]] grounds the concept through [[MiaLiponi]]'s 11-day Bitcoin-only test in [[Lugano]].

The episode says many activities are possible with [[Bitcoin]] in Lugano: groceries, deliveries, many medical places, parking fines, taxes, and other municipal services. The gaps are equally important: public transportation, fuel, bills, dentists, and some medical services remain difficult. This qualifies [[MunicipalBitcoinAdoption]] by showing that a city can have unusually dense acceptance while still falling short of full currency completeness.

## Key Claims
- Payment acceptance should be measured by coverage of recurring life needs, not only by headline merchant count.
- Volatility makes everyday pricing harder because small retail amounts constantly change in Bitcoin terms.
- Municipal services can expand usefulness faster than isolated retail merchants.
- Gaps in transport, fuel, utilities, bills, and healthcare can keep crypto from functioning as a full everyday-money substitute.
- The practicality gap helps explain why [[CryptoConsumerConfidence]] can stay mixed even in places with heavy crypto promotion.
