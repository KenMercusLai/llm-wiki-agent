# Cadaver Trade And Anatomy Fear / 尸体贸易与解剖恐惧

Cadaver trade and anatomy fear names the source's historical bridge between medical knowledge and public horror. In [[141-zhizao-fulankensitan-kehuan-zhimu-he-tade-shenqi-shidai-857923931]], anatomical schools need bodies, legal supply is limited, body-snatching grows, and the [[BurkeAndHare|Burke and Hare]] murders make the demand for cadavers look socially dangerous.

The concept explains why the assembled body in [[Frankenstein|《弗兰肯斯坦》]] could feel frightening before any modern laboratory scene appears. Dissection helped medicine learn through bodies, but the social system that supplied bodies raised questions of class, consent, dignity, and fear.

## Key Claims
- Anatomy can produce medical knowledge while still relying on disturbing or unjust body supply.
- Public horror grows when the boundary between dissection, grave-robbing, murder, and scientific curiosity becomes unstable.
- The novel's body assembly belongs to this social history as much as to gothic invention.
