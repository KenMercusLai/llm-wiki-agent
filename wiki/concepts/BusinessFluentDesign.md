# Business Fluent Design

Business fluent design is the design posture [[Daqi]] describes in [[ep87-duihua-duli-shejishi-daqi-tongguo-sheji-bangzhu-chanpin-zuohao-zengzhang-luymytt48g-ejwsl6bfuko2xsdoc]]: designers should speak in terms of goals, users, KPI, product assumptions, tradeoffs, and business problems, not only visual preference. The episode presents this as the difference between being a screen executor and being a product partner.

The idea is especially important in early products because design quality can hide a weak product story. A page can be attractive and still fail if the information architecture, value proposition, trust path, or action path is unclear.

[[stefan-sagmeister-finally-something-good-yqqxftsvema]] adds a public-communication version. [[StefanSagmeister]] treats labels, sources, physical installation constraints, and viewer memory as design requirements, which broadens business fluency into mission fluency: the designer must understand what the work is trying to change in perception.

## Key Claims

- Design critique should ask what problem the page or feature is solving and how the team will know whether it worked.
- Designers gain strategic influence when they can challenge product assumptions using user evidence and business language.
- Visual polish has to sit on top of comprehensible function and information architecture.
- Junior designers can be trained by asking them to state goals, target users, KPI, and constraints before they discuss style.
- AI-era designers may become more valuable for point of view, judgment, and outcome direction than for manual craft alone.
