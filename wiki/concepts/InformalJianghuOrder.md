# Informal Jianghu Order

Informal Jianghu order is the rule system described in [[102-jianghu-congtan-pianshu-heihua-he-minjian-daoyi-736516488]]. Through [[JiangHuCongTan|《江湖丛谈》]], the episode shows 老何, 拜相/拜码头, 常春会, 生意下处, trade-spacing rules, taboo speech, thief-guard negotiation, and mediation through respected insiders as ways that [[StreetJianghuSociety]] maintained working order outside formal law.

The concept is not an endorsement of Jianghu morality. The source repeatedly shows fraud, intimidation, and exploitation. Its point is narrower: when people operate in mobile, low-status, semi-legal, or trust-poor environments, reputation and insider enforcement can become practical constraints even when they are morally mixed and legally fragile.

## Key Claims
- Informal order can exist without formal legality, but it remains vulnerable to coercion, exclusion, and predation.
- The episode's "义气" is often instrumental: people keep rules because everyone needs enough predictability to continue earning.
- Local brokers such as 老何 help strangers interpret a market, avoid conflict, and know when to move elsewhere.
- Lodging rules and trade-spacing customs protect secrets, tools, income, and status boundaries.
- Thief-guard negotiation shows a non-state conflict-management logic, but it also exposes how much protection depends on belonging to the network.
