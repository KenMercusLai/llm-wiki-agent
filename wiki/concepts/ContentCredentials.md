# Content Credentials

Content credentials are the media-provenance mechanism discussed by [[AvivOvadia]] in [[tech-20260121-0121-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260121-0121-mp-tech-pod-128]]. The episode says standards now exist for attaching verification signals to media, including camera-created credentials that can help show an image was captured by a particular device rather than generated later.

The concept sits inside [[AIContentProvenance]]. Its practical value depends on an adoption chain: cameras or phones must create credentials, editing tools must preserve or explain changes, platforms such as [[LinkedIn]] must read or display them, and users must understand what the signal does and does not prove. Ovadia compares the goal to ordinary website authenticity checks: a credential can help, but trust still depends on systems around it.

## Key Claims
- Content credentials address provenance, not the entire truth of a claim.
- Adoption by platforms is as important as the standard itself because most users encounter media through distribution surfaces.
- Credentials can reduce the [[AIRealityVerificationTax]] but cannot replace [[HumanJudgmentUnderAI]].
- Weak adoption leaves [[InformationApocalypse]] dynamics largely intact even when standards exist.
