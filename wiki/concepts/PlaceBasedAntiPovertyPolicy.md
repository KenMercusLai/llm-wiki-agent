# Place-Based Anti-Poverty Policy

Place-based anti-poverty policy is the strategy of improving the environment around low-income residents rather than treating poverty only as an individual cash, relocation, or service problem. [[can-transforming-neighborhoods-help-kids-escape-poverty]] develops this through [[HOPEVIProgram|HOPE VI]] and [[RajChetty]]'s argument that moving every child to a better neighborhood is not scalable.

The source's version is interaction-centered. Housing can matter, but so can schools, sports, and mass transit if they create repeated contact between low-income children and higher-income peers, making [[CrossClassSocialCapital]] a practical design target.

## Key Claims
- Place can be a policy unit when local schools, peers, safety, transit, and institutions shape children's outcomes.
- The strongest HOPE VI lesson is not demolition, but connection to nearby opportunity.
- Non-housing tools can also create interaction across class lines.
- Place policy still needs safeguards against [[PublicHousingDisplacement]].
