# Data-Driven Penalty Preparation

Data-driven penalty preparation is the use of historical penalty records, player tendencies, and behavioral cues to prepare takers and goalkeepers for shootouts. [[how-to-win-a-penalty-shootout-with-game-theory]] adds the concept through [[IgnacioPalacioHuerta]]'s database and his report for [[ChelseaFC]] before the 2008 Champions League final against [[ManchesterUnited]].

The episode's strongest lesson is that data changes incentives rather than replacing strategy. The report helped [[PetrCech]] save [[CristianoRonaldo]]'s penalty and helped Chelsea target [[EdwinVanDerSar]]'s tendency, but repeated use of the same recommendation made Chelsea legible to van der Sar.

## Key Claims
- Historical data can reveal player and goalkeeper tendencies that ordinary memory misses.
- Data is most useful when folded into [[MixedStrategy]] rather than turned into a rigid instruction.
- Once both sides expect reports, the informational advantage narrows and preparation shifts toward simulation, psychology, and adaptation.
