# Convexity Exposure

Convexity exposure is the episode's technical name for a payoff structure that changes nonlinearly as the world moves. In [[e43-zhang-xiaoyu-mengyan-duihua-xuzhe-meiyou-genghaode-shenghuo-lrsfby01kuournly5mlkkzi-ayls]], [[XuZhe]] distinguishes this from simply buying options: the desired structure may involve owning some convexity, selling other options to finance it, and managing multiple dimensions of risk.

The source links convexity to [[Antifragility]] because a convex structure can gain more in extreme states than it loses in ordinary fluctuations. It also links the idea to [[OptionPremiumPricing]]: convexity is never free, and expensive tail options can turn a good idea into a bad trade.

## Key Claims
- Convexity describes nonlinear payoff, not merely market optimism.
- A portfolio can have different convexity exposures across time, strike, asset, volatility, and risk dimension.
- Tail convexity may be costly to hold through quiet markets.
- Selling options can fund convexity only if the seller understands the obligations created.
- The point is portfolio structure, not heroic prediction.
