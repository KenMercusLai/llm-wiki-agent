# Co-Founder Relationship Maintenance

Co-founder relationship maintenance is the deliberate operating work founders do to keep trust, role clarity, and feedback healthy before conflict becomes structural. [[tsr-s4-gusto-v3-tsr-s4-gusto-v3]] adds the concept through [[JoshReeves]], [[EddieKim]], and [[TomerLondon]] at [[Gusto]].

The source gives two mechanisms. First, the founders had an uncomfortable but explicit early conversation about roles: Josh as CEO, Eddie as CTO, and Tomer as chief product officer. Second, they held regular one-on-one walks with a recurring pattern of gratitude, apologies, and feedback. That makes relationship maintenance an operating habit rather than an occasional repair attempt.

The concept is the constructive counterpart to [[CoFounderConflict]]. It also complements [[CoFounderAlignmentLoop]]: alignment is about reaching shared conviction on strategic direction, while maintenance is about preserving the relationship capacity that makes hard alignment possible.

## Key Claims
- Role clarity can reduce hidden status contests before the company is large enough for ambiguity to become expensive.
- Co-founder feedback works better as a repeated ritual than as a one-time crisis conversation.
- Gratitude and apology are operating tools when they keep small injuries from becoming durable resentment.
- Ego control is not only a personality virtue; it affects whether a founding team can preserve trust under pressure.
- Shared values become stronger when founders use them in hiring, feedback, and decision norms rather than leaving them as slogans.
