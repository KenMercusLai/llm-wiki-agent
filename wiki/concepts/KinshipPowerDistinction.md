# Kinship Power Distinction

Kinship power distinction is the episode's methodological guardrail for separating matrilineal descent, matrilocal residence, and matriarchy. In [[178-muquanlun-weida-de-cuowu-or-shenke-de-dongjian-muquan-shehui-cunzai-guo-ma-974657368]], the hosts stress that a society can trace descent through women, keep men in a wife's or mother's household, or honor mother figures without women controlling political power, resource allocation, or public decision-making.

This distinction prevents the strongest overread in [[MatriarchyQuestion]]. The episode uses [[Mosuo]] society as the clearest caution: mother-centered household and descent patterns do not automatically erase male uncle authority or prove female rule.

## Key Claims
- Matrilineal means descent, name, inheritance, or kin identity follows the mother's line.
- Matrilocal means residence after marriage or partnership is organized around the woman's household.
- Matriarchy means women actually control political, economic, ritual, or institutional power.
- Any claim about "mother-right" should specify which of these is being discussed.
- Confusing these terms turns partial evidence into a stronger conclusion than the source can support.
