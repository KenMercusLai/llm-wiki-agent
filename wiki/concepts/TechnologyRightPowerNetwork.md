# Technology Right Power Network

Technology right power network is the source's frame for how [[PeterThiel]] and adjacent Silicon Valley actors turn ideas, capital, media, company formation, and political sponsorship into durable right-wing influence. [[e233-guigu-youyi-de-quanli-wang-ruhe-xingcheng-liaoliao-bide-tier-de-sixiang-qimeng-pintu-2a529abf-3c14-4ae7-a0a5-fe1340a66c7d]] treats the network as more than campaign donations: it begins with [[StanfordReview]], runs through [[PayPal]], [[Palantir]], [[FoundersFund]], and [[ThielFellowship]], and later appears in [[RightWingMediaInfrastructure]] and [[PoliticalTalentPipeline]] work around [[JDVance]].

The concept matters because it connects the wiki's startup and politics branches. The episode argues that venture capital is not only funding companies; in this case it also helps choose founders, publish narratives, support media outlets, and back candidates who can move [[NewRightPolicyNetwork]] ideas into government.

## Key Claims
- Network power is cumulative: campus publications, donor funding, companies, fellowships, media outlets, and political campaigns reinforce each other over decades.
- The source frames Thiel's political role as a "kingmaker" pattern: he often selects, funds, and mentors rather than personally running for office.
- The network blends libertarian exit ideas, anti-progressive campus politics, surveillance-state acceptance after crisis, and national-populist electoral strategy.
- It extends [[TrumpismInstitutionalization]] by adding a Silicon Valley route into movement capacity.
