# No-Draft Player Market

No-draft player market is the allocation model in [[planet-money-vs-the-nbas-tanking-problem]] where incoming players negotiate directly with teams instead of being assigned through a draft. The source uses the [[NationalWomensSoccerLeague|NWSL]], which abolished its draft in 2024, as the comparison case.

The model removes the direct draft reward for losing. It also shifts competition from bad records toward salary, coaching, facilities, culture, development environment, and player preference, while salary caps or other rules can still limit spending.

## Key Claims
- Abolishing the draft can remove a major source of [[SportsTanking]].
- It treats incoming players as market participants rather than assets allocated by league order.
- Small-market teams can still compete if they build attractive environments, but richer or more committed owners may compound advantage.
- The model makes [[SportsPlayerAutonomy]] central to [[SportsDraftIncentiveDesign]].
