# Multimodal Intelligence

Multimodal intelligence is the source's route from language-only systems toward visual, spatial, continuous, and eventually predictive world understanding. In [[133-dui-xie-saining-de-7-xiaoshi-ma-la-song-fangtan-shijie-moxing-taochu-guigu-ami-labs-liangci-jujue-ilya-yang-likun-li-feifei-he-42]], [[XieSaining]] describes stages from pure language models, to show-and-tell image QA, to continuous visual streams and spatial understanding.

## Key Claims
- Language is a powerful interface, but not the whole world and not the only form of thought or decision.
- Visual and spatial intelligence need representations that can process continuous perceptual streams, not just isolated images.
- A final route should move toward predictive [[WorldModels]] that explain the observed world and support planning.
- Over-tokenizing visual streams for LLMs may hide the physical structure that models need to learn.
