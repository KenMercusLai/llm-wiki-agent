# Bestseller Status Feedback Loop

Bestseller status feedback loop is the pattern where a ranking does not merely reflect demand but helps create more of it. [[how-to-make-a-book-into-a-bestseller]] adds the concept through the [[NewYorkTimesBestsellerList|New York Times bestseller list]] and the [[PlanetMoneyBook]] launch.

The episode presents bestseller placement as a sales engine. Once a book appears on the list, publishers can advertise the label, stores can place it on bestseller shelves, online sellers can make it more visible, and future buyers can treat the ranking as social proof.

## Key Claims
- Bestseller status can increase discoverability after the ranking is awarded.
- The value of the label reaches beyond immediate book revenue into rights, author status, and future professional pricing power.
- [[ILibertine|I, Libertine]] shows the extreme version: perceived demand can become real publication activity.
- The Planet Money case shows the ordinary version: list placement lets Norton re-approach accounts and keep promoting the book.
- Feedback loops make ranking systems attractive targets for gaming because the reward is larger than the initial week's sales.
