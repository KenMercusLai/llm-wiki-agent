# Platform Dependency Risk

Platform dependency risk is the vulnerability created when a company builds growth on another platform's traffic, rules, links, ranking, payment path, or creator ecosystem. In [[no-200-dianshang-sanguo-zhi-qunxiong-zhulu-yaogua-gongzhang-chijian-juqian-yiji-108-zhong-sifa-974601500]], [[Mogujie]] and [[Meilishuo]] show the cleanest ecommerce version: their fashion communities generated value partly by sending users to [[Taobao]], but [[Alibaba]] rule changes forced them into harder self-operated platform competition.

## Key Claims
- Platform access can look like product-market fit while the underlying customer relationship remains owned by someone else.
- A rule change can convert a high-margin guide or affiliate business into a costly marketplace-building problem.
- Creator, livestream, and social-platform dependence can repeat the same pattern even after a company escapes the first upstream platform.
- Platform dependency is not always avoidable, but it needs to be priced as strategic risk rather than treated as free distribution.
