# Creature Social Exclusion / 被排斥者如何被制造成怪物

Creature social exclusion is the episode's reading of how the unnamed being in [[Frankenstein|《弗兰肯斯坦》]] becomes violent. In [[141-zhizao-fulankensitan-kehuan-zhimu-he-tade-shenqi-shidai-857923931]], the creature is first capable of learning, sympathy, and help, but Victor's abandonment and repeated human rejection drive him toward revenge.

The concept does not erase the creature's violence. It asks how appearance-based fear, isolation, and refusal of recognition help produce the very danger they claim merely to detect. That links the novel to [[PhysiognomyMoralAppearanceFallacy]] and [[CreatorResponsibilityEthics]].

## Key Claims
- A person or creature can be treated as monstrous before acting monstrously.
- Exclusion can become causal, not only descriptive.
- Moral judgment still has to preserve agency and harm; social production of violence does not make murder harmless.
