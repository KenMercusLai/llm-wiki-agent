# Stepping-Stone Exploration

Stepping-stone exploration is [[139-weishenme-weida-buneng-bei-jihua-youzhizhe-shijingsheng-ma-849965735]]'s practical translation of [[WhyGreatnessCannotBePlanned|《为什么伟大不能被计划》]]. Instead of choosing actions only by whether they visibly approach a final target, a person or institution notices interesting reachable possibilities and uses them to expand the next set of options.

The source applies this to careers, reading, research, creativity, and social policy. A job that is not the final career, a book that does not immediately raise income, a strange image, a failed robot movement, or a basic-science question can all become a stepping stone if it opens future paths. The method is not passivity; it requires attention, taste, bounded experiments, and willingness to follow an unexpected lead.

## Key Claims
- Complex paths are often discovered by moving from one useful intermediate state to another rather than by executing a complete plan.
- Stepping stones can look unrelated to the eventual outcome, so premature target filtering can erase them.
- Reading can be a stepping-stone practice because it enlarges the reader's possible associations before a concrete use is visible.
- Career exploration can work through applications, roles, projects, and weak ties that reveal fit rather than only through long-range prediction.
- Institutions need some room for basic research and strange projects because future breakthroughs may require currently unrecognized intermediates.
