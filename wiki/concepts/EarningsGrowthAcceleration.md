# Earnings Growth Acceleration

Earnings growth acceleration is the market's sensitivity to not only whether earnings are growing, but whether the growth rate is speeding up or slowing down. [[139-paopao-mate-he-pinduoduo-zhide-touzi-me-feng-tou-quan-1-139-1]] uses [[PopMart]] to explain why investors can react negatively to a company that is still growing: a lower second derivative can compress the valuation multiple even when profit rises.

[[ICEInvestor]] illustrates the point with two profit paths that end near the same place. A path that jumps from 45 to 130 to 140 can look like a cyclical surge losing momentum, while 45 to 90 to 140 can look like smoother compounding. The business result may be similar, but market narrative and valuation can differ sharply.

## Key Claims
- The first derivative is whether revenue or profit is increasing; the second derivative is whether the growth rate itself is increasing.
- High-expectation stocks can fall on still-good results when growth acceleration weakens.
- A smoother compounding path can receive a higher-quality narrative than a boom-then-flat path, even with similar end-state earnings.
- Earnings acceleration should be read with valuation, expectations, and time horizon rather than as a standalone signal.
