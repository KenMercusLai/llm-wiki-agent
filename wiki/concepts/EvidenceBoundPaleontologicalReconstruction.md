# Evidence-Bound Paleontological Reconstruction / 证据约束的古生物复原

Evidence-bound paleontological reconstruction is the source's rule for imagining extinct animals: because dinosaurs are gone, reconstructions are partly speculative, but useful speculation still has to answer to fossils, preserved environments, biomechanics, comparison with living animals, and known uncertainty. In [[146-bu-ai-zhuluoji-shijie-kuailai-konglong-bihusuo-dang-siyangyuan-886472126]], this rule explains why [[DinoSanctuary|《恐龙庇护所》]] can be both fantasy and responsible science communication.

The concept sits inside the wiki's broader [[ObservationBeforeInference]] branch. It differs from rigid fact listing because extinct organisms cannot be observed directly in life; it differs from arbitrary monster design because not every cinematic or comic choice is equally grounded.

## Key Claims
- Extinct-animal restoration needs visible uncertainty because the fossil record cannot answer every behavior, color, sound, or social question.
- Plausible reconstruction can use indirect evidence: bone structure, teeth, trackways, nests, feathers, living relatives, ecological context, and injury patterns.
- Fiction can responsibly add expression, personality, and plot when it marks those additions as evidence-shaped imagination rather than settled fact.
- A popular-science work should explain how researchers know, not only what the latest image looks like.
- [[BirdDinosaurContinuity]] strengthens reconstruction by giving living comparative material, but it does not make every dinosaur simply bird-like.
