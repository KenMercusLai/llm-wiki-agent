# Ragnarok / 诸神的黄昏

Ragnarok is the Norse end-time and renewal pattern discussed in [[ep269-cong-manwei-yuzhou-dao-aodesai-weihe-zongyao-fanpai-yingxiong-shishi-ltklotheqpup6oamyflfzpxtipsw]] through [[PoeticEdda|《诗体埃达》]] and [[NorseMythology]]. The source stresses that it is not pure annihilation: the old gods' order ends, but a new earth and new cycle follow.

The episode uses Ragnarok to explain both [[NorthernCourage]] and [[MythicEnvironmentalImagination]]. If the gods already know the final battle is coming, then courage means acting anyway; if the myth is read through northern environments, it also becomes a way to imagine natural force, scarcity, ecological pressure, and human limits.

## Key Claims
- Ragnarok makes fate explicit, but does not cancel action.
- The myth's modern afterlives include superhero apocalyptic battle, fantasy world-building, and ecological reinterpretation.
- Renewal after collapse keeps the pattern from being simple nihilism.
