# Inventory Write-Down Risk

Inventory write-down risk is the danger that goods recorded as assets will later need to be marked down, discounted, impaired, or written off. [[ep86-mianzi-dizi-rizi-caibao-zhi-jiang-zhe-san-jian-shi-lukmnlkkuxvrvv12frosd9teg7bj]] explains the risk through ordinary products such as electronics, clothing, and fresh goods, then uses [[BestBuy]] and [[Zhangzidao]] to show both retail obsolescence and hard-to-verify biological inventory.

[[no-200-dianshang-sanguo-zhi-qunxiong-zhulu-yaogua-gongzhang-chijian-juqian-yiji-108-zhong-sifa-974601500]] adds ecommerce-specific cases. [[Vancl]] shows how fast SKU expansion can turn growth into unsold apparel pressure, while [[FreshGroceryEcommerceEconomics]] makes spoilage, cold chain, shrinkage, and weight disputes part of the same inventory-risk family.

## Key Claims
- Inventory is not automatically valuable; it depends on age, demand, verifiability, storage, and sale price.
- Fast-changing categories such as electronics can lose value when customers shift channels or products become obsolete.
- Perishable or biological inventory can create especially difficult verification problems.
- High inventory can foreshadow future discounting, lower margins, impairment, or cash-flow pressure.
- Ecommerce inventory risk often rises exactly when a platform expands categories to keep GMV growing.
