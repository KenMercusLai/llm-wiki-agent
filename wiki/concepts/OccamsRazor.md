# Occam's Razor

Occam's Razor enters the wiki through [[185-mogui-chumo-de-shijie-guanyu-yinmoulun-ufo-waixingren-bangjia-an-maitianquan-x-dangan-jiemi-ri-996181368]] as one of the ordinary anti-fraud and anti-conspiracy tools recommended by [[QinZong|秦总]] and [[Beimin|北明]]. In the episode, it means preferring the simpler explanation when it accounts for the evidence without multiplying hidden agents, powers, or exceptions.

The source does not treat simplicity as automatic truth. Occam's Razor works with [[ObservationBeforeInference]], independent confirmation, and [[RationalHumility]]; it is a pressure against unnecessary complexity, not a license to ignore hard evidence.

## Key Claims
- A more elaborate explanation is not more scientific because it has more moving parts.
- Simplicity matters only after the relevant observations and alternatives have been checked.
- Occam's Razor is especially useful against [[ConspiracyTheoryPatternSeeking]], where complexity itself can feel like proof.
