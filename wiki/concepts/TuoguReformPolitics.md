# Tuogu Reform Politics / 托古改制政治

Tuogu reform politics / 托古改制政治 is reform justified by appeal to ancient models. In [[126-wangmang-zhisi-shensheng-diguo-de-huixuanbiao-he-huixuanbiao-de-huixuanbiao-786255780]], [[WangMang|王莽]] repeatedly reaches backward to imagined antiquity while trying to reorder land, currency, commerce, ritual, taxation, and state hierarchy.

The concept names why reform can be simultaneously radical and conservative. Wang Mang's program could feel morally elevated because it invoked old order, but the episode argues that this made policy less responsive to actual markets, frontier conditions, administrative limits, and people's capacity to bear disruption.

## Key Claims
- Antiquity can be used to authorize sweeping change.
- A revered model can hide weak implementation design.
- The source treats archaising reform as one driver of the [[XinDynasty|Xin dynasty]]'s crisis.
- Tuogu logic links [[ConfucianIdealGovernance]] to [[WangMangReforms]] and [[IdeologicalOvercontrol]].
