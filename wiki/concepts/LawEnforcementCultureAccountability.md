# Law Enforcement Culture Accountability

Law enforcement culture accountability is the idea that officer behavior depends on organizational norms, supervisor expectations, peer approval, and consequences, not only on written rules or academy training. In [[the-ice-hiring-boom]], [[SethStoughton]] argues he would be shocked if controversial ICE and CBP conduct were only a training failure.

The source pairs Stoughton's view with [[MatthewRoss]]'s field-training research and [[MarkBrown]]'s formal training account. Together they show a possible mismatch: recruits can be taught restraint while field culture and leadership signals teach a different practical standard.

## Key Claims
- Formal training can fail if supervisors and peers reward opposite behavior.
- Veteran involvement in controversial incidents weakens a simple "rushed new recruits" explanation.
- Accountability must be credible to agents before lawsuits or settlements change behavior.
- Culture analysis should include orders, informal peer norms, public denial, and actual discipline.
