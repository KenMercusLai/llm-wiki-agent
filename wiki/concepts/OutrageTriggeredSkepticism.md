# Outrage-Triggered Skepticism

Outrage-triggered skepticism is the media-literacy habit of treating anger, outrage, or instant moral satisfaction as a reason to slow down and verify before sharing or believing a claim. [[tech-20260122-0122-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260122-0122-mp-tech-pod-128]] adds the concept through [[CaseyNewton]]'s advice after investigating a likely AI-generated hoax: people should be especially skeptical when content confirms something they already want to believe.

The concept is not blanket disbelief. It sits between [[ScientificSkepticism]] and [[RealityApathy]]: the goal is to check evidence, source identity, provenance, and whether multiple trusted outlets have reported the story, not to conclude that all media is fake.

## Key Claims
- Strong emotional fit can be a manipulation signal.
- Outrage is risky because it pushes people to post or amplify before checking.
- Independent reporting matters more when the original claim comes from a platform such as [[Reddit]].
- The habit becomes more important when [[AIGeneratedHoaxEvidence]] makes fake corroboration cheaper.
