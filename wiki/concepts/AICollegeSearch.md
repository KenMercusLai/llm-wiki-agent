# AI College Search

AI college search is the student-facing pattern in [[tech-20260225-0225-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260225-0225-mp-tech-pod-128]] where prospective students use AI tools to find schools, compare offers, and evaluate fit. The [[MarketplaceTech]] episode says an [[EAB]] survey found about half of college-bound high schoolers using AI tools in the search, nearly double the prior spring's share.

The source describes several uses: students ask AI for colleges matching bundled preferences, ask [[ChatGPT]] to check [[Reddit]] and [[Instagram]] for campus vibe, and enter acceptances plus scholarships to get pros and cons. The core change is that college discovery moves from short search queries and static ranking lists toward conversational comparison.

AI college search is useful because it can reduce information overload in a costly, complex decision. Its risk is that AI can repeat familiar schools, pull outdated information, or overcompress social and financial tradeoffs. That ties the concept to [[HumanJudgmentUnderAI]], [[CollegeMajorChoice]], [[CollegeCareerPreparation]], [[HigherEducationAIDiscoverability]], and [[AIRankingReinforcement]].

## Key Claims
- AI tools can turn broad student preferences into candidate college lists.
- Conversational prompts can combine program, social, geographic, financial, and campus-culture criteria.
- AI can help compare acceptances and scholarships, but the student still owns the tradeoff.
- Social platforms can become evidence sources when AI summarizes campus vibe.
- AI college search can widen exploration if prompted well, but can narrow it if it repeats rankings and familiar brands.
- Colleges' public information quality matters more when AI tools mediate discovery.
