# Research Integrity Incentives

Research integrity incentives are the rules, expectations, and enforcement odds that shape whether researchers keep their work reproducible before anyone challenges it. [[dont-hate-the-replicator-hate-the-game]] frames [[AbelBrodeur]]'s project as a shift from explaining the [[ReplicationCrisis]] to changing the incentives that produce it.

The episode's core claim is deterrence through credible inspection. Harsh punishment after discovery matters less than the chance that a paper's code, data, and claims might be checked by the [[InstituteForReplication]] or a [[ReplicationGames]] team.

## Key Claims
- Researchers respond to publication rewards, journal requirements, career pressure, and the likelihood of being audited.
- [[PublicationBias]] and [[StatisticalSignificanceThreshold|significance thresholds]] can reward selective or tuned results.
- [[ReplicationPackage|Replication packages]] and [[Preregistration]] create visible commitments, but incentives change more when those commitments are actually inspected.
- Neutral author contact and response windows help keep correction from becoming automatic accusation.
- The strongest system would catch errors before publication, citation, and public spread rather than after.
