# Cross-Laminated Timber

Cross-laminated timber, or CLT, is the pressed-layer wood material [[DavidBrancaccio]] says he is using to rebuild in [[tech-20260112-0112-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260112-0112-mp-tech-pod-128]]. The episode presents CLT as fire resistant, structurally strong, potentially sourced from sustainably managed forests, and suited to off-site fabrication into large panels that can be shipped flat and assembled quickly.

The source's point is not that CLT alone makes a house fireproof. Its value comes inside [[FireResilientConstruction]] when paired with [[FireResistantBuildingEnvelope]] choices such as mineral wool and stucco, and inside [[SustainableConstructionCarbonTradeoff]] when compared with concrete's carbon intensity. The mass-timber examples from [[KLH]] and [[MercerMassTimber]] also connect CLT to institutional projects and schedule savings, not only one homeowner's rebuild.

## Key Claims
- CLT is framed as a fire-conscious update to wood construction rather than a rejection of wood.
- Off-site fabricated panels can reduce on-site framing time and labor.
- CLT can support post-disaster rebuilding speed if cost, permitting, and contractor familiarity work.
- Wood-based construction may store carbon if the building survives for a long time.
- CLT still needs protective exterior layers and good design to become a resilient home.
