# Averaging Down

Averaging down is the losing-position behavior warned against in [[ep76-chuanyue-1940-wo-yu-gupiao-dazuoshou-lifumoer-de-zuihou-duihua-lr0fb5yfiwoxaamdwjjgh4rjeprg]]. The source uses [[JesseLivermore]]'s cotton trade and 1930 bottom-fishing failure to show how adding to a losing active trade can turn a bad thesis into a destructive portfolio event.

## Key Claims
- Averaging down can feel rational because the entry price improves, but the episode argues that the market is often showing the trader that the thesis or timing is wrong.
- Selling winners to fund losers compounds the mistake by keeping weak positions and removing strong ones.
- In leveraged or concentrated trading, loss averaging can turn small errors into catastrophic drawdowns.
- The source distinguishes this from systematic [[PassiveInvesting]] dollar-cost averaging, which is usually diversified, scheduled, and not based on defending one broken trade.
- The preferred alternative in the source is [[StopLossDiscipline]] plus [[Pyramiding]] into positions that have already moved favorably.

## Connections
- [[JesseLivermore]] — core cautionary case.
- [[TrendFollowing]] — framework that rejects adding while the trend is against the position.
- [[Pyramiding]] — opposite position-sizing rule.
