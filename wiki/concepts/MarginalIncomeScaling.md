# Marginal Income Scaling / 边际收入放大

Marginal income scaling is the episode's earning advice: put effort where an additional unit of work can reach more people, produce reusable output, or raise future pricing power. In [[155-meimao-neng-dang-fan-chi-ma-xiang-zhuanqian-gai-zuo-dian-sha-jieju-shi-ying-bimian-shenme-xingwei-jingjixue-siwei-you-shenme-yong-920167210]], one-to-one tutoring becomes the example of a capped model, while online courses, larger classes, cross-region reach, and better narrative or information ability can raise marginal returns.

The concept does not deny labor. It asks whether labor is trapped in "work stops, income stops" repetition, or whether tools, distribution, scarcity, and communication can make the next effort more valuable than the last.

## Key Claims
- Stable marginal cost and stable marginal revenue create capped income unless the worker changes the model.
- Internet distribution is valuable because it can turn local, synchronous labor into reusable or broader-reach output.
- Scaling income still needs scarcity; if supply is unlimited and indistinguishable, broader reach may collapse price instead of raising it.
- Information and narrative ability can change how a person's value is recognized by a market.
