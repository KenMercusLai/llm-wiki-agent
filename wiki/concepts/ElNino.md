# El Nino

El Nino appears in [[missing-peace-will-israel-imperil-iran-deal-6a3bae9fe1238e54b0e886c3]] as a recurring Pacific climate pattern that begins when equatorial winds shift warm surface water and disrupt global climate patterns. The episode explains its strength through Pacific surface-temperature anomalies relative to historical averages.

The source says models suggest the event under discussion could rival or exceed the major 1982-83 and 2015-16 El Nino events. Its importance for the wiki is not just meteorological description, but the downstream [[ElNinoClimateRisk]] created when a strong event arrives on top of global warming and existing food insecurity.

[[shangye-xiaoyang-45-eerninuo-yueda-yu-yuegui-1000164352]] adds a marine-food commodity mechanism. In the episode, El Nino weakens cold-water upwelling near [[Peru]], reducing plankton for [[PeruvianAnchoveta|anchoveta]] and tightening the [[FishmealSupplyChain]] that supports modern aquaculture.
