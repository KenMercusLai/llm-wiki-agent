# Transformation As Exit

Transformation as exit is the frame developed from 〈变形中心〉 in [[130-tuokaerqiuke-guaidan-gushiji-xunzhao-he-gaobie-803543569]]. In that story from [[BizarreStories|《怪诞故事集》]], a woman enters a center that can transform her body, and her family later gathers to witness her emergence as a wolf on the far side of a lake.

The episode treats the choice as ethically difficult because it sits near several boundaries at once: body modification, ecological return, family grief, refusal of human identity, and something the sister calls a form of suicide or euthanasia. [[Ovid]]'s [[Metamorphoses|《变形记》]] gives the story an older language for bodily change, but the modern pressure comes from whether loved ones can accept a chosen departure that they cannot undo or fully understand.

## Key Claims
- Transformation can be narrated as disappearance, death, self-expression, return to nature, or continuity with another form.
- The ethical problem is not only whether the chooser is free; it is also how family members witness a decision that changes the relationship forever.
- The wolf image makes autonomy visible without making it easy: the family sees the transformed person, but only across distance.
- The concept touches [[EndOfLifeAutonomyAndDignity]] and [[SubjectLedCare]] while remaining distinct from literal assisted-death policy.
