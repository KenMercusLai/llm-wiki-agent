# Information Apocalypse

Information apocalypse is [[AvivOvadia]]'s warning in [[tech-20260121-0121-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260121-0121-mp-tech-pod-128]] that people may become unable to reliably trust what they see and hear. The episode treats generative AI as an accelerant rather than the sole cause: misinformation, propaganda, scams, and institutional distrust already existed, but AI lowers production cost while making verification harder.

The concept is broader than fake images or deepfakes. It includes authentic material being dismissed as synthetic, nonconsensual imagery, fraud against vulnerable people, emotionally optimized polarization, and the practical exhaustion of checking every suspicious item. Its downstream danger is [[RealityApathy]], where disbelief becomes so general that shared reality, commerce, journalism, and democratic institutions weaken.

## Key Claims
- Trust collapse includes both false media believed as true and true media rejected as false.
- AI worsens the problem by changing the economics of creation and verification.
- The social cost appears as an [[AIRealityVerificationTax]] carried by users, journalists, platforms, and institutions.
- Technical provenance helps only when paired with adoption, usability, and institutional trust.
