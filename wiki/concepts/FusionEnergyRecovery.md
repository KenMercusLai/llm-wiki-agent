# Fusion Energy Recovery

Fusion energy recovery is [[Helion]]'s source-described strategy of recovering most of the electricity sent into its fusion machine before counting new fusion output. In [[tsr-s5-davidkirtley-v2-audio-tsr-s5-davidkirtley-v2-audio]], [[DavidKirtley]] says Helion proved in 2014 that it could put electricity into fusion electromagnets and recover 96% of it, while [[HelionPolaris|Polaris]] is already recovering more than 90%.

The concept matters because it changes the commercial threshold. If most input electricity is recovered, fusion output does not have to overcome the entire input energy every pulse; it has to cover the unrecovered fraction and produce useful surplus for sale. In the source, that makes high electrical efficiency part of the same business argument as plasma physics.

## Key Claims
- Energy recovery lowers the commercial burden by reducing the amount of net new fusion energy needed per operating cycle.
- Recovery efficiency can be a de-risking metric alongside temperature, pressure, fusion rate, and machine uptime.
- The strategy ties physics to power electronics, pulsed systems, switching, and [[PrecisionManufacturingAsStrategy]].
