# Baseball Antitrust Exemption

The baseball antitrust exemption is the legal backdrop in [[how-we-got-free-agents-in-baseball]] that made [[CurtFlood|Curt Flood]]'s challenge to the [[BaseballReserveClause|reserve clause]] difficult. In [[FloodVKuhn|Flood v. Kuhn]], [[MajorLeagueBaseball|MLB]] argued that prior court precedent had treated baseball differently from ordinary interstate commerce and that change should come through bargaining or [[UnitedStatesCongress|Congress]].

The [[SupremeCourt|Supreme Court]] ruled against Flood and left the exemption in place. The episode's synthesis is that legal defeat did not end the issue because [[PublicOpinionLitigationStrategy|public opinion]], arbitration, and [[SportsCollectiveBargaining|collective bargaining]] later changed the practical labor system.

## Key Claims
- The exemption limited antitrust attack on baseball's reserve system.
- The Court treated the inconsistency as something Congress should fix.
- Antitrust defeat shifted the path of change toward union leverage and contract interpretation.
