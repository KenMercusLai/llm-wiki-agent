# Human Judgment Under AI

Human judgment under AI is the claim that AI can enhance preparation and synthesis but cannot replace fast, situated decision-making in live professional contexts. [[ali-qianwen-lizhi-yuzhen-zai-jiwanren-de-tieqiu-li-ruhe-timian-shengcun-keji-luandun]] gives the example of preparing for a meeting with AI while still needing to answer a boss's real-time challenge without pausing to query a tool. [[duihua-minimax-yan-junjie-m3-10x-jihua-10t-moxing-he-zhineng-de-zhongju-lqtilt8flvmv99v0gshhyfyraibe]] adds engineering and finance versions: developers remain responsible for agent-written code, [[ZhangJiayuan]] keeps the most important thinking for himself, and [[YuYang]] says financial decisions depend on live context and action after events unfold.

## Key Claims
- AI is useful for preparation, framing, and organizing context.
- Live questioning requires internalized understanding, tradeoffs, and expression.
- Good AI use still requires putting the prepared material into the user's own head.
- AI coding output still needs human taste, ownership, review, and responsibility.
- Financial AI can filter and explain information, but regulated and real-time decisions still require human judgment.
