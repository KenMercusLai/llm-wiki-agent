# Wang Mang Reforms / 王莽改制

Wang Mang reforms / 王莽改制 refers to the policy program discussed in [[126-wangmang-zhisi-shensheng-diguo-de-huixuanbiao-he-huixuanbiao-de-huixuanbiao-786255780]] after [[WangMang|王莽]] founded the [[XinDynasty|Xin dynasty]]. The episode lists Wang fields, anti-merchant and agrarian ideals, salt/iron/wine monopolies, mountain-and-marsh taxes, five-equalization credit policy, gold conversion, and repeated currency reforms.

The source's interpretation is neither that the reforms were simply evil nor that they were misunderstood modernity. They are framed as [[TuoguReformPolitics|托古改制]]: idealistic, controlling, and often disconnected from administrative capacity and economic behavior.

## Key Claims
- The reforms tried to force social reality into a morally ordered ancient template.
- Currency and fiscal interventions created uncertainty and stress.
- Monopoly and tax policy expanded state reach without solving state capacity.
- Policy success and policy failure could both increase pressure because the program was too encompassing.
