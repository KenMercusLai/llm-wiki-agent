# Wok Hei

Wok hei is the high-heat wok flavor that [[chef-vs-robot]] treats as the human chef's central advantage over a robot wok. The episode explains it as a special caramelized flavor created by intense heat, open flame, and skilled tossing in dishes such as beef chow fun.

In the source, wok hei is more than a taste note. It is a proxy for embodied restaurant skill: [[PhuongHoangChiang]]'s years of practice, [[JudyHuang]]'s concern for diners' ability to taste the difference, and [[SholaOlunloyo]]'s successful identification of the human-made dish all make wok hei the quality test for [[RestaurantAutomation]].

## Key Claims
- Wok hei depends on heat intensity, timing, ingredient movement, and chef control.
- Diners may detect its absence more clearly in dishes where caramelization and wok aroma are central.
- Robot cooking can compete on consistency and throughput while still struggling to reproduce high-skill heat effects.
- Wok hei makes [[RobotChefCostQualityTradeoff]] dish-specific rather than generic.
