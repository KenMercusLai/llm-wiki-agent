# Aviation Finance Leasing

Aviation finance leasing is the aircraft-access layer highlighted in [[no-203-businiao-lanshili-979295393]]. New airlines such as [[EastStarAirlines]] could not simply buy fleets with internal cash, so lessors and financial institutions such as [[GECAS]] helped convert aircraft ambitions into lease and financing obligations.

The source treats leasing as both enabler and risk multiplier. It allowed [[LanShili]] to move from tourism-ticketing ambition into real airline operation, but it also made [[LeveragedAviationExpansion]] fragile when fuel prices, weather, disasters, and the global financial crisis hit. Lease obligations remain hard even when passenger demand, routes, or credit conditions deteriorate.

## Key Claims
- Aircraft leasing can lower the entry barrier for a new airline but does not make aviation low-capital.
- Leasing turns manufacturer relationships, financing capacity, and creditworthiness into operating constraints.
- Fast fleet commitments can create a public success signal before the airline has proven durable cash flow.
- When shocks arrive, lease obligations can accelerate [[PrivateAirlineFailureModes]].
