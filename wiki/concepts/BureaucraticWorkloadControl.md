# Bureaucratic Workload Control

Bureaucratic workload control is the mechanism in [[118-shi-dachen-xinla-fengci-yongbuguoshi-768665425]] where red boxes, speeches, meetings, parliamentary votes, standard replies, and long official sentences keep [[JamesHacker|James Hacker]] busy enough that he cannot easily control his department. It is one of the main practical techniques inside [[YesMinister|《是，大臣》 / Yes Minister]].

The source treats busyness as a mode of power. If the minister's time is saturated, if documents arrive in locked boxes at night, if replies are standardized, and if language is complex enough to obscure the simple point, then formal authority remains separated from real comprehension and initiative.

This concept complements [[AdministrativeOpacityAsControl]]. Opacity controls what is known; workload control controls whether the person with formal authority has enough attention, energy, and timing to act on what is known.

## Key Claims
- Excess work can be a control system rather than a sign of effective governance.
- Standard language can transform accountability into process without requiring open refusal.
- Long, ambiguous official phrasing can make a simple decision harder to see and therefore harder to challenge.
- Agenda saturation protects whoever controls document flow and scheduling.
