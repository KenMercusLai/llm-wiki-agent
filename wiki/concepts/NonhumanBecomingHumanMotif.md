# Nonhuman Becoming Human Motif

Nonhuman becoming human motif is the pattern where a puppet, creature, machine, spirit, mermaid, or cyborg tries to become human, becomes legible as human, or rejects a narrow imitation of humanity. [[122-pinuocao-chengzhang-de-cantong-jiqi-suo-chuangzuo-de-777464172]] makes [[Pinocchio]] the main case and compares him with [[TheLittleMermaid|《海的女儿》]], robot stories, Chinese fox or object-spirit tales, and [[GhostInTheShellStandaloneComplex|攻壳机动队]].

The motif is not only a body-change plot. In the episode, [[Pinocchio]] becomes human by accepting hunger, guilt, labor, fear, attachment, and responsibility. The comparison with cyborg and robot stories widens the question: becoming human may require embodiment, but it may also require choosing what human imitation is worth keeping or abandoning.

## Key Claims
- "Becoming human" can mean moral, emotional, social, or bodily transformation.
- A nonhuman character can already have a self before the world grants full human status.
- The motif can question humanity rather than simply praise it: adult social life may involve boredom, discipline, loss, and spiritual narrowing.
- The motif connects fairy tales to science fiction because both ask whether personhood is intelligence, body, memory, vulnerability, responsibility, or self-recognition.
