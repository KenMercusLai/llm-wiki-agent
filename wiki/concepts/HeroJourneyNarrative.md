# Hero Journey Narrative

Hero journey narrative is the mythic-growth frame [[122-pinuocao-chengzhang-de-cantong-jiqi-suo-chuangzuo-de-777464172]] uses through [[JosephCampbell|Joseph Campbell / 约瑟夫·坎贝尔]]. In the episode, [[AdventuresOfPinocchio|《木偶奇遇记》]] fits the pattern because [[Pinocchio]] leaves ordinary home life, rejects responsibility, meets mentor figures, enters dangerous rule-worlds, suffers symbolic death, descends into the shark's belly, returns with his father, and changes form.

The source does not use the framework to make the story tidy. Its point is that Pinocchio's apparently loose road-movie structure can still have a deep growth rhythm: tests repeat until the character's desire to be good becomes action, labor, and protection.

## Key Claims
- A hero journey can appear inside a children's fairy tale without turning the tale into simple adventure.
- Refusal and relapse are part of the pattern; the hero may need to fail repeatedly before the call becomes real.
- The "belly" stage matters in Pinocchio because darkness, fear, and parent rescue make the internal change visible.
- Return is not just going home; it means coming back able to care for others.
