# AI Writing Pedagogy

AI writing pedagogy is the source's classroom approach for teaching students to use, inspect, and exceed AI text systems rather than only hide from them or outsource to them. In [[tech-20260115-0115-mp-tech-pod-128-tech-20260115-0115-mp-tech-pod-128]], [[ChristyGerdhary]] argues that students need to be "better than a robot" by understanding what AI can and cannot do.

The episode's strongest assignment pattern is remediation. Students revisit earlier writing and may let AI transform it, collaborate with AI while color-coding which parts came from the student and which came from the tool, or make something AI cannot simply produce. This turns [[TransparentAIUse]] and [[HumanJudgmentUnderAI]] into writing practices: students have to see the difference between generated transformation and meaningful communication.

The concept extends [[FirstDraftThinking]] without duplicating it. First draft thinking protects the student's initial synthesis before AI help; AI writing pedagogy asks what happens after there is human material to compare, remix, disclose, and push beyond generic machine text.

## Key Claims
- Prompt engineering can be understood as writing because it involves purpose, audience, constraint, revision, and judgment.
- AI can be part of writing instruction when students compare outputs and explain authorship rather than quietly submit machine prose.
- Assignments should ask whether a final artifact carries personal memory, embodied making, local language, or material form that AI cannot supply alone.
- Writing instruction may need to move beyond narrow school essay forms toward communication formats students meet outside the classroom.
- AI writing pedagogy works poorly if paired only with surveillance or suspicion; it needs care, transparency, and [[AIDetectorBias]] awareness.
