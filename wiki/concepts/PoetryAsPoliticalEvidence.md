# Poetry As Political Evidence / 诗文作为政治证据

Poetry as political evidence is the episode's frame for how [[SuShi|苏轼]]'s writing becomes dangerous in the [[WutaiPoetryCase|乌台诗案]]. In [[105-su-dongpo-yiqiang-haomai-zhiqi-yi-dupi-buhe-shiyi-741072645]], circulated poems, hand-copied texts, memorial language, jokes, and sympathy for poor prisoners are gathered into accusations against Su Shi.

The concept matters because the same literary power that makes Su Shi influential also makes him vulnerable. A poem about people harmed by new-law debt can be read as compassion, political criticism, or slander against [[SongShenzong|宋神宗]] and the [[WangAnshiReforms|王安石变法]], depending on the factional reader.

## Key Claims
- Literary ambiguity can be exploited by political investigators.
- Circulation changes risk: a private or local poem can become central evidence once enemies collect it.
- Compassion for suffering subjects can be reframed as attack on policy or ruler.
- Speech cases punish not only words but networks, because friends, copyists, and correspondents can be implicated.
