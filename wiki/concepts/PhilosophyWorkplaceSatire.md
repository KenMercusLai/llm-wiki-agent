# Philosophy Workplace Satire

Philosophy workplace satire is the method [[92-bailatu-shangbanji-yong-zhexue-gaoxiao-zhichang-716822350]] finds in [[PlatoGoesToWork|《柏拉图上班记：哲学家的职场百态》]]: move philosophical concepts into company roles, props, and procedures so the idea becomes a joke people can recognize from work.

The source's claim is that these jokes are not arbitrary. [[FriedrichNietzsche|尼采]] fits HR because herd morality and management conflict; [[MichelFoucault|福柯]] fits monitoring because visibility is discipline; [[WalterBenjamin|本雅明]] fits the copier because reproduction becomes literal; [[BaruchSpinoza|斯宾诺莎]] fits corporate theology because the boss-God problem becomes a question of capital control.

## Key Claims
- A workplace frame lowers the entry barrier to philosophy without removing conceptual structure.
- The office is useful because it already contains hierarchy, paperwork, surveillance, status, productivity, and ritual.
- Good satire depends on accurate correspondence between philosophical motif and workplace scene.
- The method turns philosophy into lived situations rather than abstract name recognition.
