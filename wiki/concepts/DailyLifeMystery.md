# 日常推理 / Daily-Life Mystery

日常推理 is the mystery mode foregrounded in [[168-fangxuehou-de-xiaoxiang-zhi-qingchun-zhi-nanian-de-youyi-richang-tuili-qiangshi-huigui-953669535]] through [[ZhongShengli|钟声礼]]'s [[FangxuehouDeXiaoxiang|《放学后的小巷》]]. Instead of beginning from murder, locked rooms, or spectacular tricks, it starts from small everyday anomalies: a tofu pudding order, a borrowed bicycle, an unseen prize, a gear shifter, a class research project, strange post-trauma speech, or a missing dessert.

The episode treats daily-life mystery as adjacent to but distinct from [[PuzzleSnackMystery|推理薯片]]. Both value inference and clue placement, but daily-life mystery can make the answer emotionally heavier because the hidden truth may involve poverty, embarrassment, family pressure, or kindness rather than criminal guilt.

[[131-richang-zhimi-tuili-shi-wo-wei-neng-shuo-chukou-de-zhenxin-805617229]] generalizes the form through [[UnspokenFeelingAsMystery|说不出口的心事]]. [[CactusFlowerStory|《仙人掌之花》]] hides gratitude, [[GlassGiraffe|《玻璃长颈鹿》]] hides terror and guilt, and [[ShoshiminSeries|《小市民》系列]] hides a comic cake theft. That range keeps the concept from becoming only "cozy mystery": the scale is ordinary, but the emotional weight can vary sharply.

## Key Claims
- Small anomalies can be valid clues when they are embedded in shared routines.
- Daily-life mystery depends on knowing what is ordinary: default food toppings, school routes, bicycle habits, and friend behavior.
- The solution can deepen character rather than identify a culprit.
- The form is especially suited to adolescence because students notice small shifts while still acting with direct, awkward energy.
- A good daily-life mystery needs [[ObservationBeforeInference]]: everyday explanations must be tested before the story earns emotional inference.
- The hidden truth often comes from feelings that cannot be directly said: gratitude, shame, fear, guilt, or playful rivalry.
- The mode can be warm, bitter, or comic without leaving the daily-life scale.
