# Underserved Romance Audience

Underserved romance audience is the demand signal in [[the-business-of-heated-rivalry]] that [[BrendanBrady]] and [[JacobTierney]] attach to [[HeatedRivalry|Heated Rivalry]]'s success. The episode argues that romance stories, female desire, and queer joy can be treated as serious audience demand rather than fringe taste or low-status genre consumption.

The concept matters because it turns representation and genre respect into market structure. A show can appear surprising only because buyers have undercounted the audience, misunderstood what the audience wants, or treated desire and joy as less commercially serious than trauma or prestige drama.

## Key Claims
- Romance readership can be a large demand base even when mainstream media treats it as unserious.
- Queer joy can be a positive market promise rather than only a lesson, issue, or trauma narrative.
- Female desire is a business signal when audiences feel the work was made with them in mind.
- Merchandise demand can reveal attachment that ordinary commissioning assumptions missed.
