# Vertical Media Distribution

Vertical media distribution is the strategy of controlling more of the routes through which media reaches audiences and earns money. In [[the-walt-disney-company-1]], Disney moves from dependency on outside customers and distributors toward a system that includes owned IP, merchandising, television promotion, [[Disneyland]], and [[BuenaVistaDistribution]].

The concept is the media version of [[DistributionLedProductBuilding]]. Disney's distribution choices shaped what the company could build: synchronized-sound shorts needed theatrical access, Mickey needed clubs and comics, Snow White benefited from merchandising and rerelease, Disneyland needed [[ABC]], and later film economics benefited from direct distribution.

## Key Claims
- Distribution control reduces dependence on any single customer, buyer, or gatekeeper.
- Vertical distribution can finance product creation when one channel promotes or subsidizes another.
- Media distribution is not only delivery; it can also build demand, trust, and repeat behavior.
- Working capital determines how much distribution a company can internalize.
