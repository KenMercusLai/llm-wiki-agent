# Crosstalk Literary Rhythm

Crosstalk literary rhythm is the source-specific idea that [[LaoShe|老舍]]'s prose in [[LihunNovel|《离婚》]] carries an oral comic timing related to Beijing speech and xiangsheng culture. [[02-lihun-guodegang-dou-buru-laoshe-youyisi-525913046]] uses Lao She's background around Tianqiao performance, his own crosstalk participation, and later help to new crosstalk writing to argue that his humor is a deep command of vernacular rhythm rather than only funny subject matter.

The concept does not reduce the novel to performed comedy. It explains why short sentences, quick turns, concrete food and street details, and social dialogue can make the fiction feel spoken while still serving [[ControlledHumorWithPain]]. The joke lands, but the scene remains literary and claustrophobic.

## Key Claims
- Oral-performance rhythm can be a prose craft resource without making a novel merely transcript-like.
- Lao She's comic timing depends on vernacular control, social observation, and sentence movement at the same time.
- The source treats crosstalk history as context for Lao She's humor, not as proof that the novel should be read only as stand-up comedy.
