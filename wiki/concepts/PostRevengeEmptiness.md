# Post-Revenge Emptiness / 复仇之后的虚无

Post-revenge emptiness is the pattern where revenge succeeds at the plot level but fails to restore the world. [[117-zuzhoutu-guaitan-wenxue-ye-yao-shangzhuo-chifan-766574341]] makes this explicit through [[CursedBunny|《诅咒兔》]]: the cursed rabbit lamp destroys the company and family line that ruined the grandfather's friend, yet the ending is not triumphant. The revenge leaves ghosts, isolation, repetition, and a room where the cursed object still remains.

The concept is adjacent to [[FemaleRevengeAndPoliticalOrder]] but not identical. In the Medea branch, revenge becomes politically and ethically catastrophic because it destroys kinship and city order. In the Cursed Bunny branch, the emphasis is quieter and more desolate: even when the target deserves punishment, the dead do not return and the avenger is not repaired.

It also contrasts with [[PrivateRevengeModernLaw]] and [[FilialRevengePublicSympathy]], where the wiki tracks how vengeance enters law, media, public sympathy, and state pardon. Post-revenge emptiness asks what remains after the spectacle, judgment, or curse has already done its work.

## Key Claims
- Revenge can answer injury without healing loss.
- A satisfying punishment plot may conceal the survivor's continuing loneliness.
- Revenge stories become ethically stronger when they track cost after victory rather than ending at payback.
- In guaitan, a cursed object can make revenge's residue materially visible.
