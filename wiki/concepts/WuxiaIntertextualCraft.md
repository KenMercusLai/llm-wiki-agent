# Wuxia Intertextual Craft

Wuxia intertextual craft is the way martial-arts fiction turns earlier literature, philosophy, religion, medicine, folklore, history, and genre convention into readable action. In [[112-xianliao-jinyong-diyidan-mingjiao-jingran-jintian-hai-cunzai-754593095]], [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] uses [[JinYong|金庸]] to show that a technique name, poison, sect, or duel can carry more than plot utility.

The episode's examples range from 北冥神功, 逍遥游, 庖丁解牛, 凌波微步, 降龙十八掌, 九阴真经, and 空明拳 to 三尸脑神丹 and 生死符. These are not treated as decorative classical references. They turn Zhuangzi, Cao Zhi, the I Ching, Daoist vocabulary, body imagination, and control mechanisms into a system where combat reads as culture.

The concept also covers emotional and relational craft. 杨过's 黯然销魂掌 works because its name, power, and later weakening are tied to grief and reunion; the source's reading of 史婆婆's blade technique likewise makes martial naming a way to express a marriage relation.

## Key Claims
- Martial-art names can carry philosophy, poetry, religion, and character state at the same time.
- Allusion is functional when it helps readers understand personality, sect order, political control, or emotional pressure.
- Wuxia craft can be learned from older literature without being reducible to quotation.
- [[LiangYusheng|梁羽生]] and [[JinYong|金庸]] define one learned branch of this craft, while [[GuLong|古龙]] shows a divergent branch through suspense and mystery.
- Weak later imitation can keep the genre surface while losing the cultural reservoir that made the older work feel dense.
