# Consumer Arbitration Barrier

Consumer arbitration barrier is the accountability problem in [[the-sneaky-way-companies-get-new-chemicals-into-our-food]] where [[DailyHarvest]] customers who accepted terms of service faced arbitration rather than ordinary court litigation. The source says non-signers, including guests, minors, and babies exposed through breast milk, helped the case proceed in court.

The concept matters because court access can affect what facts become public. In this episode, [[FoodSafetyLitigationDiscovery]] helped [[BillMarler]] trace [[TaraFlour]] through the supply chain, but that route was partly enabled by people who had not agreed to the arbitration terms.

The barrier therefore compounds [[PostHarmFoodRegulation]]: if consumers discover harm only after eating a product, contract terms may still limit the public investigation and accountability that follow.
