# Objectivism / 客观主义

Objectivism / 客观主义 enters the wiki through [[135-xianliao-weida-zuojiamen-de-bagua-di-san-dan-823746670]] as [[AynRand|Ayn Rand / 安·兰德]]'s doctrine of rational self-interest, radical individualism, capitalism, and entrepreneur-centered moral language. The episode presents it as both personally energizing for some readers and politically or emotionally dangerous when it becomes a total explanation of human life.

The source's distinctive claim is not only that Objectivism is harsh. It is that a doctrine of freedom can become internally contradictory when its founder's private conduct fails its own declared principles. Rand's break with [[NathanielBranden|Nathaniel Branden / 纳撒尼尔·布兰登]] makes the source's [[FreeLovePracticeGap]] visible inside a movement that claimed to reject possessive love.

## Key Claims
- Rational self-interest can be motivating without becoming a complete ethics.
- Objectivism becomes dangerous in the source when readers extract its most aggressive individualist elements as a universal worldview.
- A movement around anti-collectivist doctrine can still develop discipleship, orthodoxy, and expulsion dynamics.
- The source distinguishes Rand's own complex politics from later conservative use of her as a simplified symbol.
