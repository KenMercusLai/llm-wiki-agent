# Listener-Supported Media

Listener-supported media is a funding model where audiences help pay for journalism and programming through one-time or recurring gifts. In [[congress-has-voted-to-eliminate-government-funding-for-public-media]], [[NPR]] asks listeners to donate what they can, especially through recurring monthly gifts, after the reported elimination of federal public-media funding.

The appeal frames listener support as a way to preserve [[PublicServiceJournalism]], [[LocalJournalism]], and [[PublicMediaEmergencyAccess]] while keeping public media free and accessible. It also contrasts audience support with shareholder or special-interest incentives.

## Key Claims
- Recurring gifts create a more durable funding base than one-time appeals.
- Audience support is presented as aligned with the people public media serves.
- The model becomes more important when public funding is reduced or removed.
