# Platform IP Strategy

Platform IP strategy is the episode's contrast between Tencent's longer IP accumulation and ByteDance's recommendation-led content expansion. In [[touteng-dazhan-ba-nian-hou-zai-ba-zijie-he-tengxun-zai-gege-zhanchang-shang-de-jingzheng-zhuyi-chaikai-zijie-tiaodong-di-6-ji-lvglr-jws7o7utjauxnoqqasj91d]], Tencent is described as stronger in copyright, memberships, literature, video, music, and games, while ByteDance uses products such as Fanqie, [[Hongguo]], Qishui Music, ads, and recommendation to expand supply and consumption.

The point is not that one route is universally better. Tencent's route prizes high-quality works, paid membership, IP reuse, and long-term ownership; ByteDance's route prizes lower-friction consumption, creator supply, recommendation testing, and ad-supported demand expansion.

[[quanmian-yazhi-buliu-kongdang-zijie-tiaodong-ruhe-zuo-zengzhang-zijie-tiaodong-di-7-ji-lqszvmur6jv8b9xt8rjgmwfdqtec]] adds the growth mechanics behind the ByteDance side. [[FanqieNovel]], [[Hongguo]], and [[QishuiMusic]] are framed as products where [[ByteDanceGrowthSystem]], account data, recommendation, material supply, and ad-supported monetization can expand content consumption faster than traditional paid-IP routes.

## Key Claims
- IP platforms can compound through ownership, membership, licensing, sequels, and game/media reuse.
- Recommendation platforms can create new consumption categories by lowering discovery and payment friction.
- Short drama shows how web fiction, ads, free viewing, and platform distribution can turn a promotional format into a standalone content form.
- AI video and AI short drama may intensify the contrast by lowering production cost while raising rights and provenance questions.
- Music and novels show the same logic outside video: [[Douyin]] interest data can feed [[QishuiMusic]], while [[FanqieNovel]] can use free reading, ad buying, and material testing to expand demand.
