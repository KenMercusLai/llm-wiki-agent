# Wild-Animal Rescue Responsibility

Wild-animal rescue responsibility is the ethical problem that begins after a person intervenes in a wild animal's crisis. In [[138-qie-ke-wo-zai-haitan-shang-jiandao-yiwei-q-laoshi-842331726]], [[TomMichell|Tom Michell / 汤姆·米切尔]] rescues [[JuanSalvadorPenguin|Juan Salvador / 胡安·萨尔瓦多]] from [[OilPollutionWildlifeHarm]], but each step after that creates uncertainty: how to clean oil, whether detergents damage waterproofing, whether the penguin can feed, whether release is safe, whether captivity is worse, and whether human companionship can substitute for same-species companionship.

The concept is adjacent to [[ConservationIntervention]] but more intimate. It asks how responsibility changes when intervention is improvised by an ordinary person rather than a planned conservation program. The episode does not treat love as enough by itself; care has to keep asking what the animal needs as an animal.

## Key Claims
- Rescue can be necessary after human-caused harm, but necessity does not make the next steps obvious.
- An animal's resistance, silence, or attachment should be interpreted cautiously because humans may misunderstand distress, trust, fear, or species needs.
- Release is not automatically the ethical endpoint if the animal is injured, isolated, or unable to survive alone.
- Captivity is not automatically justified by affection; water, diet, health, social contact, and species behavior still matter.
- Informal rescue should remain humble about uncertainty and seek better knowledge when possible.
