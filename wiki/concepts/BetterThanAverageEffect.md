# Better-Than-Average Effect / 优于常人效应

Better-than-average effect is the tendency to believe oneself more capable, rational, humorous, logical, or bias-resistant than ordinary people. [[123-renxing-shiyan-dangxin-ni-ziji-779479569]] connects this to the everyday language of false confidence and to studies where people with higher test scores or stronger self-images still failed to avoid perception bias.

The episode's warning is blunt: thinking that one is unusually rational can itself become a bias amplifier. The people most vulnerable to a distortion may be exactly the people who feel least vulnerable to it.

## Key Claims
- Skill and self-knowledge are different capacities.
- Education and test performance do not automatically immunize a person against bias.
- Overconfidence can make corrective feedback feel unnecessary.
- Bias literacy should begin from "this includes me," not "this explains others."
