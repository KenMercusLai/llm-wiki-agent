# 妖怪图像阐释 / Yokai Image Exegesis

妖怪图像阐释 is the source's method of reading a yokai picture as a layered sign system. In [[115-jingji-xiayan-jinxi-xu-baigui-bu-yanjiu-yaoguai-zenme-haohao-zuoren-760601309]], [[TataraKatsugoro|多多良胜五郎]] studies [[ToriyamaSekien|鸟山石燕]]'s image of [[AnganKozo|《暗眼小僧》]] by comparing visual details, sound similarities, classical allusions, local language, tools, and moral jokes.

The concept is adjacent to [[SemioticDetectiveFiction]], but it is more folklore-specific. The image can help explain why a scene feels like a yokai story, yet it should not be treated as direct forensic proof. That tension is why the episode repeatedly places Tatara's wrong deductions next to later, better-bounded interpretations by 富美.

## Key Claims
- A yokai image can preserve puns, place knowledge, object details, religious hints, literary references, and social satire in compressed form.
- Visual resemblance can prompt inquiry, but it does not prove causation or guilt.
- Homophones and folk names matter because they explain how people classify strange events in ordinary speech.
- The strongest reading combines image, text, local context, and ethical consequence instead of only one visual clue.
- Later creative inventions can themselves become legitimate folklore research objects when communities continue interpreting them.
