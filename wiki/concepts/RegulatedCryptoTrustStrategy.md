# Regulated Crypto Trust Strategy

Regulated crypto trust strategy is the choice to make legal legitimacy, banking access, compliance, and institutional credibility part of a crypto product's core design. [[socialradarspod-brianarmstrong-final]] adds the concept through [[Coinbase]]: [[BrianArmstrong]] says the company decided early not to fly under the radar, even when legal opinions and licensing were expensive relative to its funding.

The concept joins product and regulation. Coinbase's buy button depended on bank transfers, bank transfers depended on [[SiliconValleyBank]] and [[AntiMoneyLaundering]] credibility, and later public-company trust depended on licenses, regulator engagement, fraud controls, and clear custody behavior. The source contrasts this posture with the reputational damage caused by [[FTX]].

## Key Claims
- In regulated fintech, the desired user feature may require compliance work before it can launch.
- Trust is not only branding; it is made of banking relationships, legal opinions, licenses, reserve behavior, controls, and transparent communication with counterparties.
- Crypto's open, fast, global promise creates extra trust burdens because users, banks, and regulators need to understand custody, identity, and funds movement.
- Regulatory clarity can become a product and strategy issue when a company cannot confidently list assets or activate licenses.
