# Sensory Hypersensitivity Accommodation / 感官超敏适配

Sensory hypersensitivity accommodation is the practical idea that some people experience ordinary stimuli as unusually intense, painful, or destabilizing, so support should include environmental adjustment rather than only demands for toughness. [[187-wandou-gongzhu-gaomin-he-hundun-shi-ni-zhenming-999958166]] introduces the frame through [[ThePrincessAndThePea|《豌豆公主》]] and the modern language of high sensitivity and sensory hypersensitivity.

The concept extends [[AutismAsHumanDifference]] from [[46-zhe-shijie-weiyi-de-ni-qing-xiangxin-na-duo-manman-zhanfang-de-hua-623717720]], where apparently difficult behavior may reflect sensory overload, anxiety, memory triggers, or predictability needs. Episode 187 is looser and literary rather than clinical, but both sources resist the same failure mode: treating a sensory report as bad attitude before asking what the person is actually experiencing.

## Key Claims
- Sensory discomfort can be real even when others do not perceive the same stimulus as serious.
- Good response may require changing texture, light, sound, heat, schedule, predictability, or escape routes.
- Forced endurance can damage trust when the original problem is overload rather than defiance.
- Accommodation is not indulgence by default; it is an attempt to match conditions to actual bodies and nervous systems.
