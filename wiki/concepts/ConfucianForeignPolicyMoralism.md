# Confucian Foreign Policy Moralism / 儒家外交道德主义

Confucian foreign policy moralism / 儒家外交道德主义 names the source's critique of turning symbolic universal hierarchy into diplomacy. In [[126-wangmang-zhisi-shensheng-diguo-de-huixuanbiao-he-huixuanbiao-de-huixuanbiao-786255780]], [[WangMang|王莽]] treats relations with Xiongnu, Qiang groups, Korean polities, and southwestern border powers as if "civilizing the four directions" could replace practical border management.

The source presents this as a governing failure. Demanding title changes, breaking older arrangements, buying submission, and creating symbolic commanderies made ideological sense inside [[ConfucianIdealGovernance]], but produced frontier instability for the [[XinDynasty|Xin dynasty]].

## Key Claims
- Moral hierarchy is not the same as diplomatic capacity.
- Symbolic submission can create resentment or false confidence.
- Ideological foreign policy can turn stable ambiguity into conflict.
- The concept connects frontier disorder to the broader pattern of [[IdeologicalOvercontrol]].
