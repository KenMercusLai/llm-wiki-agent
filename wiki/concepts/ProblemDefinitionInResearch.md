# Problem Definition In Research

Problem definition in research is the ability to make a question precise, tractable, and consequential enough for a field to move. [[133-dui-xie-saining-de-7-xiaoshi-ma-la-song-fangtan-shijie-moxing-taochu-guigu-ami-labs-liangci-jujue-ilya-yang-likun-li-feifei-he-42]] treats this as one of the core research abilities.

## Source View
[[XieSaining]] uses [[FeiFeiLi]] and [[ImageNet]] as the central example: the contribution was not only collecting data, but defining image classification so that deep learning systems could be compared, improved, and understood. He generalizes the lesson to research itself: losing the ability to define problems means losing much of the ability to innovate.

[[140-dui-yao-shunyu-de-4-xiaoshi-fangtan-qing-yunxu-wo-xiao-feng-yixia-zai-anthropic-he-gemini-xun-moxing-jishu-yuce-yingxiongzhuyi-yi-guoqu-ll7qiciwwgfssorhr4yy-uuqae8h]] adds [[YaoShunyu]]'s frontier-model version. He argues that top labs are less worried about whether AI can improve in general and more worried about whether the next task is well defined enough to train, evaluate, and scale. In this source, a good AI research problem needs a usable environment, data, feedback signal, and evaluation standard.

[[ai4s-xuyao-kuangren-yu-yexinjia-duihua-yinglingdian-odin-ruguo-shen-cunzai-wo-zenneng-rongren-ziji-bushi-shen-gonglu-boke-lhceyip6dqomrwk38uvqjwoomxyz]] adds [[HaotianOdin]]'s biological-startup version. His [[AllModalMolecularWorldModel]] claim is a problem-definition move before it is a proven architecture: if the real biological problem crosses small molecules, proteins, RNA, and DNA, then separate single-modality tools may define the problem too narrowly.
