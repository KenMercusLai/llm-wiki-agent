# AI Advice Moral Outsourcing

AI advice moral outsourcing is the risk in [[a-hawk-who-flew-on-political-winds-lindsey-graham-6a54b56575790d5f01515d55]] that people delegate not only factual tasks but also moral judgment to AI systems. [[SondreSolstad]] says bias may matter less for booking a hotel or writing code, but becomes more serious when people ask AI for counsel, family decisions, or emotionally intimate advice.

The concept extends [[HumanJudgmentUnderAI]] into value formation. If a model's answers reflect particular social, political, language, or censorship defaults, then advice-seeking can quietly import those defaults into personal choices while still feeling neutral because the answer arrives as calm assistance.

## Key Claims
- Advice use is higher stakes than routine task use because it can shape relationships, self-understanding, and moral priorities.
- A model does not need consciousness to influence a user's values; fluent suggestions can still structure the choice space.
- Value surveys matter because they reveal the defaults that may enter advice when users do not notice them.
- Human judgment includes deciding when an AI answer should be treated as a prompt for reflection rather than counsel to follow.
