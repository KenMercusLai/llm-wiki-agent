# Coin Retirement Economics

Coin retirement economics is the decision problem created when a coin costs more to produce than its face value and loses practical payment use. In [[betty-boop-excel-olympics-penny-isms-our-2026-valentines]], [[KennyMalone]] says the [[USTreasury|U.S. Treasury]] and [[UnitedStatesMint|U.S. Mint]] are going to stop minting new pennies, while existing pennies remain legal tender.

The source keeps the economic case and emotional case separate. The penny is expensive to mint and less useful as people pay with cards, phones, and other digital methods, but [[ErikaBeras]] values it as a physical link to the past and as a source of language such as "penny for your thoughts" and "penny wise and pound foolish."

## Key Claims
- Minting can become economically irrational when production cost exceeds face value.
- Payment digitization can reduce the practical role of low-denomination coins even before legal tender status changes.
- Cultural memory can make retirement feel like a loss even when the fiscal case is straightforward.
- The object's afterlife may continue through sayings, collections, drawers, and nostalgia after new production stops.
