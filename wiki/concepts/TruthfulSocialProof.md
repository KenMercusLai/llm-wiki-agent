# Truthful Social Proof

Truthful social proof is the norm-change mechanism in [[the-laws-of-the-office-revisited]] where people become more willing to change when they see that others are changing too. [[AliceEvans]] explains the pattern as a documented but unnamed social-change dynamic.

The truth condition is central. The episode says visible norm messages can work when they show real behavior, such as supported reporting of gender-based violence or accurate college drinking norms. Fake claims that everyone already behaves well can undermine trust and make future signals less credible.

## Key Claims
- People may privately want change but wait for evidence that others will also support or practice it.
- Public examples can lower the perceived social risk of being first.
- Norm campaigns should show true behavior, not aspirational fiction.
- Visible appreciation can complement truthful social proof by making helpful behavior easier to notice.
