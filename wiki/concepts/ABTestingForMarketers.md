# A/B Testing For Marketers

A/B testing for marketers is the [[Optimizely]] product wedge in [[tsr-s3-dansiroker-v3-tsr-s3-dansiroker-v3]]. [[DanSiroker]] had used Google-style experimentation on the 2008 [[BarackObama]] campaign, where web creative tests could materially change signups and donations. [[PaulGraham]] then recognized the startup version during [[YCombinator]]: give marketers a way to run those tests without waiting on engineers for every change.

The concept extends [[DataDrivenProductCulture]] by making experimentation operationally accessible to nontechnical teams. In the source, the value is not "data" in the abstract; it is lowering the friction of testing copy, images, videos, landing pages, donation incentives, and even company names quickly enough that evidence can beat internal taste or political intuition.

## Key Claims
- A/B testing creates leverage when an underdog can take testable risks that a leading incumbent may avoid.
- The marketer-facing wedge matters because growth and campaign teams often own the question but not the engineering resources.
- Testing can reveal non-obvious creative winners, such as a casual family photo beating more polished presidential imagery.
- Tooling changes who can participate in experimentation; it moves A/B testing from a specialized engineering task toward ordinary marketing work.
- The method still needs judgment about what to test, how to interpret results, and when a short-term metric might mislead.
