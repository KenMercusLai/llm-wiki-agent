# Machine Gambling Addiction

Machine gambling addiction is the pattern of compulsive play produced by slot machines, video poker, and similar fast human-machine reward loops. [[44-yunqi-de-youer-dubo-chengyin-yinwei-women-haipa-ziyou-619995308]] adds the concept through [[TheLureOfLuck|《运气的诱饵》]], arguing that modern machine gambling differs from traditional gambling because it is solitary, repetitive, low-friction, and designed to keep the player in a steady loop.

The episode distinguishes this from both casino cheating and simple desire for money. A machine can publish legal odds and still create harm through buttons, credit displays, sound, near misses, cash-out design, ATM access, seating, room layout, and data-triggered interventions. The addictive object is often not the jackpot itself but the continuation of the [[MachineZone|machine zone]].

## Key Claims
- Machine gambling shifts gambling from social confrontation toward repetitive human-machine synchronization.
- The goal for heavy players may become continued play rather than profit.
- Credits and tickets can weaken the player's felt connection to real money.
- [[NearMissDesign]] makes losing feel close to winning, encouraging further play without necessarily changing final odds.
- [[IntermittentReinforcement]] makes reward timing powerful because uncertain reward can drive more compulsive behavior than reliable reward.
- Gambling machines can mimic agency by asking the player to choose, press, hold, or discard while the core outcome remains probabilistic.
- Harm reduction has to examine system design and regulation, not only individual self-control.
