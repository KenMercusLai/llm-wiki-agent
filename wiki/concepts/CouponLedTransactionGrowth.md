# Coupon-Led Transaction Growth

Coupon-led transaction growth is the episode's shift from app growth to business, demand, and scenario growth. In [[faquan-liebian-jisuban-ruhe-yong-hongbao-sheji-zengzhang-zijie-tiaodong-di-8-ji-lijpz1zgy9h2k4qd0c45ixwenu-0]], coupons are not treated as generic discounts; they are mechanisms for moving users into orders, address/payment binding, merchant verification, memberships, cross-selling, and repeat local-service or ecommerce behavior.

The source uses [[Meituan]], [[Taobao]], [[Alibaba]], and [[Douyin]] local life to show why coupons can be more efficient than buying traffic from an external ad platform. If a platform can directly subsidize a milk-tea, food-delivery, hotel, beauty, or travel order and then observe payment, address, merchant, and repeat behavior, it can connect the subsidy to transaction LTV more directly than a pure install campaign.

## Key Claims
- Coupon growth becomes more powerful when the platform can observe the whole transaction: demand, order, payment, fulfillment, and repeat purchase.
- Low-ticket subsidies can create trial, but they may not produce durable high-value users. The source argues for user stratification by order value and scene quality.
- "造节" turns a one-off subsidy into a repeatable consumption cue, as in recurring food or beverage promotions.
- Local-services coupons create a strategic bridge among content, ads, ecommerce, payment, offline merchants, and membership.
- Coupon-led growth is closer to [[AgenticCommerce]] than Lite-app growth because the outcome is an actual service or product transaction, not only time spent.
