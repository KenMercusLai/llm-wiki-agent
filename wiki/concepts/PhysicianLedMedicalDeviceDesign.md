# Physician-Led Medical Device Design

Physician-led medical device design is the source's pattern of treating clinicians as hands-on users whose workflow, ergonomics, and safety judgment shape the product before a prototype is complete. In [[tsr-s4-surbhisarna-v3-tsr-s4-surbhisarna-v3]], [[SurbhiSarna]] starts by pitching her own gynecologist, then uses physician introductions and negative feedback to refine [[NVisionMedical|nVision Medical]].

The episode makes physician feedback practical rather than ceremonial. Doctors had to be able to use the device during an exam, potentially while looking at a screen rather than their hands. Sarna also says the handle had to work across different hand sizes, including female physicians, making overlooked ergonomics part of [[WomensHealthDiagnosticGap]].

This concept extends [[CustomerDiscoveryByDoingWork]] into regulated healthcare. The founder may not perform the procedure herself, but she has to learn enough from clinical users that anatomy, hand use, claim limits, and patient-safety constraints become product requirements.

## Key Claims
- In medical devices, "user discovery" includes procedural safety, hand mechanics, clinical workflow, training burden, and patient risk.
- Negative physician feedback can be as valuable as enthusiasm when it prevents unsafe or awkward design assumptions.
- Device simplicity matters because clinical attention may be split between the instrument, patient, imaging, and screen.
- Product design and regulatory strategy should be connected early, because physician use only matters if the validated claim is clinically acceptable.
