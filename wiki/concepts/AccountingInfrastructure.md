# Accounting Infrastructure

Accounting infrastructure is the episode's implicit claim that audit, bookkeeping, tax, consulting, government accounting, and bank accounting are part of financial order. In [[ep23-minguo-jinrong-wangshi-zhuifengzhe-beihou-de-tiancai-shaonian-yu-yinxing-tixi-lu50lzwnfquabfare58gbhwr42py]], [[GuZhun]], [[PanXulun]], and [[LixinAccounting]] show how technical accounting expertise helped governments, companies, foreign firms, and financial institutions operate in a modernizing Shanghai economy.

The concept extends the wiki's investing and banking pages by shifting attention from products and markets to the boring systems that make claims verifiable: ledgers, audits, tax rules, financial statements, and professional training.

## Key Claims
- Accounting firms can be institutional infrastructure when they standardize how governments, enterprises, banks, and foreign firms record and verify activity.
- A young technical expert can matter because errors, tax systems, and bank accounting all require precise process knowledge.
- Accounting connects private business service to public finance and state capacity, especially in periods of economic transition.
