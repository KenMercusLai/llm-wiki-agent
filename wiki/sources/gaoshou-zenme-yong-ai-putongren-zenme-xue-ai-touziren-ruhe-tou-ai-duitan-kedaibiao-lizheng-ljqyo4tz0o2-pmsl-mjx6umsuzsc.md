## Summary
This [[ShizilukouCrossing]] episode with [[KedaibiaoLizheng]] argues that advanced AI use is less about better prompts and more about rebuilding work around [[AgenticWorkflow]], durable [[ContextEngineering]], and reusable [[AISkills]]. The discussion contrasts chat-style AI use with tool-using products such as [[Codex]], [[ClaudeCode]], and [[Cursor]], then extends the same logic to enterprise deployment through [[ForwardDeployedEngineer]] roles. The second half surveys AI investing themes, including [[AIForScience]], [[EmbodiedAI]], [[WorldModels]], [[EverythingAgent]], [[VideoModels]], [[VoiceInteraction]], and the possibility of [[WeChat]] as a high-context agent platform.

## Key Claims
- Advanced AI users do not merely ask better questions; they build scaffolding around AI, including tools, preferences, documents, process memory, and judgment standards.
- Chat interfaces have limited compounding value because context must be restated, outputs do not naturally accumulate, and tool access is constrained.
- [[AgenticWorkflow]] tools such as [[Codex]], [[ClaudeCode]], and [[Cursor]] matter because they can work across files, tools, and persistent project context.
- [[AISkills]] are valuable when they encode implicit knowledge into explicit boundaries, procedures, standards, and required context; simple style imitation is a weak version of the idea.
- [[ContextEngineering]] may become a durable differentiator as models and base tools converge.
- AI entrepreneurship is getting harder for conventional SaaS patterns because users and competitors can recreate lightweight tools quickly with agents.
- The episode frames [[ForwardDeployedEngineer]] work as a likely enterprise bottleneck: AI needs people who can map processes, culture, databases, and organizational context before deployment.
- The investment discussion suggests two broad escape routes from foundation-model pressure: moonshot domains such as [[AIForScience]] and [[EmbodiedAI]], or deep industry work with hard operational detail.
- The host's AI investment watchlist centers on [[EverythingAgent]], [[EmbodiedAI]], [[WorldModels]], [[VideoModels]], software workflow rebundling, and [[VoiceInteraction]].

## Key Quotes
> "从聊天到 agent" — the episode's recurring framing for the shift from casual AI use to tool-using work systems.

> "脚手架" — the guest's metaphor for the tools, context, preferences, and standards that make AI more useful over time.

> "skill as a service" — the guest's commercial framing for reusable AI workflows that may be easier to sell as outcomes than as raw skill files.

> "旧商业模式的石头不见了" — the guest's description of how familiar startup assumptions are weakening in the agent era.

> "第二次文艺复兴" — the phrase used for a possible creative and generalist resurgence caused by AI reducing routine tool work.
