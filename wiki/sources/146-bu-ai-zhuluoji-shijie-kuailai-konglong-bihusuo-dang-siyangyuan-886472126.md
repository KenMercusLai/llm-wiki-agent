# 146. 不爱侏罗纪世界？快来《恐龙庇护所》当饲养员！

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode begins from disappointment with [[JurassicWorldRebirth|《侏罗纪世界：重生》]] and recommends [[DinoSanctuary|《恐龙庇护所》 / Dino Sanctuary]] as a richer dinosaur story. The hosts argue that the manga works because it treats dinosaurs as living animals with fear, habits, illness, stress, aging, and individual differences rather than as generic monster attractions. The episode links dinosaur popular culture to [[BirdDinosaurContinuity]], [[EvidenceBoundPaleontologicalReconstruction]], [[DinosaurAsLivingAnimal]], and [[CaptiveAnimalCareLabor]].

## Key Claims
- The hosts' dissatisfaction with [[JurassicWorldRebirth|《侏罗纪世界：重生》]] is not simply that the film is insufficiently scientific; they feel the franchise loses force when dinosaurs become interchangeable thriller monsters and character behavior undercuts tension.
- The episode preserves a fair distinction between blockbuster invention and scientific grounding: commercial film can invent for spectacle, but dinosaur fiction is stronger when its inventions still respect what fossils, living relatives, and biomechanics suggest.
- [[BirdDinosaurContinuity]] is used as a child-friendly science bridge: even a small bird can be presented as part of the dinosaur lineage, changing dinosaurs from vanished monsters into a still-visible evolutionary branch.
- [[DinoSanctuary|《恐龙庇护所》]] is framed as science fiction, workplace manga, animal-care story, and protagonist-growth narrative at once.
- The manga's parallel-world setup matters because it asks what society would do if living dinosaurs became zoo animals: breeding, public enthusiasm, accidents, declining attendance, old facilities, and headquarters pressure all become part of the story.
- The episode's central dinosaur frame is [[DinosaurAsLivingAnimal]]: South American theropods, troodontids, triceratops, tyrannosaurs, ankylosaurs, and pachycephalosaurs are discussed through smell, fear, heat stress, injury, attachment, sickness, play, cognition, and old age.
- [[CaptiveAnimalCareLabor]] is the practical lesson behind the manga. Liking dinosaurs does not substitute for cleaning enclosures, moving feed, reading behavior, handling danger, making welfare tradeoffs, and accepting that the animal's good may conflict with the caretaker's projection.
- The story of the troodontid hatchling Benkei makes parental attachment and release ethically complicated: the protagonist has to separate her own family wound from what this individual animal needs.
- The triceratops Asho story connects welfare and business reality. Marketing the broken foot is morally acceptable only because it makes the animal's actual history visible and keeps it housed rather than treating it as a replaceable attraction.
- The episode adds a Chinese dinosaur knowledge coda, especially feathered dinosaurs and early Chinese finds, to show how local discoveries changed global dinosaur imagery.

## Key Quotes
> "不太满足" - the hosts' compact reaction to the new Jurassic film.

> "小麻雀也是恐龙同类" - the manga's child-facing bridge from ordinary birds to dinosaur evolution.

> "喜欢恐龙和有情怀并不等于能胜任这份工作" - the episode's care-labor lesson for the new keeper protagonist.
