## Summary
This [[ShizilukouCrossing]] road-podcast episode interviews [[HaotianOdin|Odin / 浩天]], founder of [[YinglingdianAI|英灵殿]], about building an [[AIForScience]] company whose near-term product is an [[AIDrugDiscoveryPlatform]] and whose long-term ambition is [[ScientificDiscoveryAutomation]]. The discussion centers on an [[AllModalMolecularWorldModel]] that would model and design small molecules, proteins, RNA, and DNA together rather than treating each molecular modality as a separate field. The second half turns the same technical ambition into a founder-method interview: [[HaotianOdin]] explains his move from [[BakerLab]], his preference for platform over drug pipelines, and his attempt to preserve [[FounderSignalDiscipline]] under financing pressure.

## Key Claims
- [[YinglingdianAI]] is presented as a roughly 30-person company with tens of millions of dollars in financing, a short-term goal of becoming a strong [[AIDrugDiscoveryPlatform]], and a longer goal of moving toward general scientific AI.
- [[HaotianOdin]] frames science acceleration as automating data analysis, hypothesis generation, and path selection, making [[ScientificDiscoveryAutomation]] the episode's highest-level technical thesis.
- The company's core technical bet is an [[AllModalMolecularWorldModel]]: one framework that can reason across DNA, RNA, proteins, and small molecules because life processes depend on interactions among all of them.
- [[ODesign|O-Design / Odyssey]] is described as an early open-source exploration where one model could attempt small-molecule, RNA, DNA, and protein design, with simple downstream validation and undergraduate-level participation in multiple targets.
- [[LDesign|L-Design]] is introduced as a coming milestone closer to a molecular world model, with examples such as designing proteins from small molecules, small molecules from small molecules, and DNA aptamers from proteins.
- [[AlphaFold]] and diffusion-style protein design appear as enabling precedents, but [[HaotianOdin]] argues that architecture should serve the scientific problem rather than become an object of attachment.
- [[BakerLab]] matters as both technical formation and exit contrast: the source says [[HaotianOdin]] learned from AI protein design there but left because he wanted to pursue his own technical path rather than become only an engineer inside a famous lab.
- The source treats [[PlatformPipelineBiotechStrategy]] as a live business decision. [[YinglingdianAI]] does not plan to build drug pipelines in the short term because that could distract the AI team and turn pharmaceutical customers into competitors, while still acknowledging that pipelines can prove platform value.
- [[DomainExpertAlignment]] remains necessary. The source emphasizes AI-native researchers, CS/model-training backgrounds, experimental collaboration, commercial interfaces, and willingness to learn over narrow labels.
- [[LifeReprogrammingVision]] is the episode's outer horizon: intervening in interactions among DNA, RNA, proteins, and small molecules is framed as reprogramming life, while the source also treats life creation and consciousness questions as distant and ethically charged.
- [[PersonalizedMolecularMedicine]] is presented as the more reachable ordinary-person milestone: custom drugs based on genotype and protein-expression state rather than one-size-fits-all treatment.
- [[FounderSignalDiscipline]] is the founder-method layer. [[HaotianOdin]] describes entrepreneurship as arising from dissatisfaction with the world, then uses Buddhist practice language, "be yourself," and resistance to VC-friendly buzzword stitching to explain how he tries to keep the company aligned with its original scientific question.
