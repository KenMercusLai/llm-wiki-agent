## Summary
This [[EconomistPodcasts]] trailer introduces [[TocquevilleRoadTrip]], a series from [[JohnPrideaux]] that retraces [[AlexisDeTocqueville]]'s 1831 journey through the [[UnitedStates]] to reassess American democracy. It uses [[DemocracyInAmerica]] to ask whether the United States still works as [[AmericaAsIdea]]: a democratic promise with global symbolic force as well as a country under institutional strain. This file appears to be the `theintelligencepodcast` Acast-feed copy of the already ingested trailer cluster, so it reinforces source coverage rather than adding materially new claims.

## Key Claims
- [[AlexisDeTocqueville]]'s 1831 journey through America is presented as a still-useful historical route for understanding the [[UnitedStates]].
- [[DemocracyInAmerica]] is treated as an enduring interpretive text for American civic life, not only as a political-history classic.
- [[JohnPrideaux]] frames America as both a country and a democratic idea capable of inspiring people beyond its borders.
- The series will test Tocqueville's frame through a modern road trip and interviews with Americans across elite, imprisoned, pro-presidential, and state-harmed social positions.
- The open question is whether Tocqueville's America still exists as the United States approaches its 250th birthday.
