# 139. 为什么伟大不能被计划：有志者事竟成，吗？

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode uses [[WhyGreatnessCannotBePlanned|《为什么伟大不能被计划》 / Why Greatness Cannot Be Planned]] to question goal-first thinking in complex domains. The episode contrasts simple tasks, where goals and metrics can work, with research, careers, creativity, social policy, and reading, where [[ObjectiveDeception]] can make the most direct-looking path a trap. Its strongest synthesis is that people and institutions should protect [[NoveltySearch]] and [[SteppingStoneExploration]]: open exploration, interesting intermediate discoveries, and wider possibility space can matter more than proving constant progress toward a predefined endpoint.

## Key Claims
- The episode begins from the question of why to read if reading does not directly raise income, then reframes reading as [[NoveltySearch]] rather than as a guaranteed income tool.
- The source presents [[KennethStanley]] and [[JoelLehman]] as the AI researchers behind [[WhyGreatnessCannotBePlanned|《为什么伟大不能被计划》]], and uses that framing to import algorithmic and evolutionary examples into life-design questions.
- Goal thinking works better for bounded tasks such as tidying a bookshelf than for open-ended problems such as scientific breakthroughs, career direction, cancer treatment, artificial intelligence, or social governance.
- [[ObjectiveDeception]] appears when an imperfect target function creates a false gradient: steps that look closer to the target may be dead ends, while useful intermediate steps may initially look unrelated.
- The image-breeding experiment shows why complex outcomes are hard to reverse-plan. Images that later resemble castles, birds, or vehicles can descend from intermediate forms that did not resemble the final object.
- The robot maze example is the episode's clearest algorithmic contrast: according to the source, [[NoveltySearch]] found the target in 39 of 40 complex-maze runs, while objective-driven search succeeded only 3 times.
- Biological evolution is used as a non-teleological model: eyes, wings, lungs, and human intelligence are framed as products of constraints and accumulated variation rather than as preselected endpoints.
- [[SteppingStoneExploration]] becomes a personal method: career shifts, job applications, volunteer ties, editing work, podcasting, and reading all become ways to find the next reachable possibility rather than execute a master plan.
- The [[RichardWiseman]] newspaper experiment is used to argue that over-focusing on the assigned task can make a person miss obvious opportunities in peripheral information.
- [[CampbellLaw]] names the policy and organization risk: when quantified indicators become targets, people and systems can optimize the number while corrupting the underlying purpose.
- The episode does not reject all goals or metrics. It preserves goals for small tasks, fairness-constrained tests, and low-impact measurement while warning against overconfidence in major complex systems.
- Basic research and seemingly useless projects are defended as necessary possibility-space expansion, not as automatically valuable outputs.

## Key Quotes
> "错误的指南针可能比没有指南针更危险" - the episode's formulation of bad objective functions in complex domains.

> "不如保持开放" - the closing practical stance toward exploration, reading, and discovery.
