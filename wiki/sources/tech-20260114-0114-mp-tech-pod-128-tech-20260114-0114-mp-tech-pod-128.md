## Summary
This [[MarketplaceTech]] episode reports on [[Lugano]], [[Switzerland]], and its [[LuganoPlanB|Plan B]] initiative to make the city a European [[Bitcoin]] hub. It adds [[MunicipalBitcoinAdoption]] by showing Bitcoin payments in shops, restaurants, and city services, while also adding [[CryptoPaymentPracticalityGap]] because public transportation, fuel, bills, dentists, and some medical services remain hard to cover. The episode's tension is [[CryptoPublicLegitimacy]]: [[MiaLiponi]] presents Bitcoin-only living as possible for a short period, while local skepticism, the vandalized [[SatoshiNakamoto]] statue, and [[MicheleFolletti]]'s defense against crime concerns show that public acceptance is not automatic.

## Key Claims
- [[LuganoPlanB|Plan B]] launched in 2022 as an effort to attract crypto companies and normalize Bitcoin use in [[Lugano]].
- Residents and visitors can still use Swiss francs, but hundreds of shops and restaurants in Lugano also accept [[Bitcoin]].
- A McDonald's coffee purchase shows Bitcoin use as ordinary retail payment, but the exact Bitcoin amount changes with volatility.
- The town council distributed special crypto payment terminals for businesses, lowering the merchant-side adoption barrier.
- [[MiaLiponi]] says she lived only on Bitcoin for 11 days and found it possible, but not complete: public transportation, fuel, bills, dentists, and some medical services remained gaps.
- Groceries, deliveries, many medical places, parking fines, taxes, and other municipal services are described as payable with Bitcoin.
- A metal-slat statue of [[SatoshiNakamoto]] was removed, broken, and thrown into the lake, which the report treats as a sign of local disagreement with crypto promotion.
- A resident named Lucia says she does not use cryptocurrencies and associates them with crime, the dark web, speculation, crashes, and financial loss.
- Mayor [[MicheleFolletti]] argues that cash and fiat money remain larger money-laundering risks than Bitcoin, while acknowledging that both fiat and Bitcoin can be used for good or bad purposes.
- The episode says almost 110 crypto-sector companies have moved to or started in Lugano.
