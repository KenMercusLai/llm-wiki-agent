## Summary
This [[HowIBuiltThis]] episode features [[GuyRaz]] interviewing [[TomRinks]] about the founder and brand-building path behind [[SunBum]]. The story moves from furniture sales, Surf Michigan shirts, [[PsychoChihuahua]], the [[TacoBell]] Chihuahua lawsuit, Christian video work, [[RinksCanetti]], and [[DeLeonTequila]] into Sun Bum's sunscreen launch, where [[VisualBrandSystem]], [[MascotLedBrandRecognition]], [[PlaceBasedBrandPerception]], [[RetailDisplayAsBrandSurface]], and [[ConsumerBrandMoat]] made a legacy category feel newly ownable. Its strongest operating lesson is that emotional positioning and design can create demand, but scale still requires [[CPGDistribution]], [[FounderRoleTransition]], and a partner such as [[SCJohnson]] when manufacturing and regulation outgrow the founding team.

## Key Claims
- [[TomRinks]] learned practical customer psychology while selling furniture on full commission after studying art at Hope College.
- His early Surf Michigan shirt sale to Meijer established a recurring pattern: find an overlooked cultural signal, create a visual identity, and sell before the whole business is mature.
- University of Michigan basketball apparel showed Tom's strength in timing and culture, but weak margin and inventory discipline limited profitability.
- [[PsychoChihuahua]] paired a small cute dog with rebellious copy, giving Tom and [[JoeShields]] an early mascot-led consumer property.
- Tom and Joe say they worked with [[TacoBell]] for about 18 months before the company released a Chihuahua campaign that they believed used their work.
- The lawsuit produced a jury award of $30.1 million plus interest, but Tom says his personal after-fee, after-tax share was far smaller than the headline number.
- The Christian video nonprofit shows Tom applying brand positioning even outside commerce: he wanted a warmer public on-ramp to Christianity after being alienated by harsher religious messaging.
- [[ReneKennedy]] and Tom formed [[RinksCanetti]] in 2007, combining branding fees with equity participation in companies they helped create.
- [[DeLeonTequila]] turned high-end tequila into an emotional identity product through luxury bottle design, skull-and-snake cues, and premium pricing.
- Sun Bum began after Tom was asked to evaluate weak sunscreen concepts and saw that legacy sunscreen brands lacked the kind of youth, family, surf, and travel identity his own kids might want to use.
- The final Sun Bum look combined woodgrain packaging, yellow and brown colors, clean typography, surf references, streetwear, mid-century furniture, and [[SunnySunBumMascot|Sunny]] the ape mascot.
- The name initially worried Tom, but "Sun" kept the brand tied to sunscreen and lines such as "Trust the Bum" helped the casual voice work.
- Early Sunny stickers, trade-show reactions, surf-shop accounts, high-end hotels, and [[RichardBranson]]'s Necker Island account gave the young brand credibility beyond its first-year sales.
- Sun Bum used full retail displays, modified Ikea bookcases, stickers, shirts, footballs, frisbees, and towels to make the brand look larger and more established in stores.
- [[AdamFrancis]] became CEO because Tom knew margins, production, scalability, and financial management were not his strongest skills.
- [[SCJohnson]] bought Sun Bum in 2019, reportedly for $400 million, in part because international sunscreen regulation required larger manufacturing, office, and compliance capacity.

## Key Quotes
> "Surf Michigan, waiting for the big one" - the joke-shirt line that created Tom's first major retail order.

> "yo quiero Taco Bell" - the phrase tied to the campaign Tom and Joe later fought over.

> "Trust the Bum" - a tagline that helped make the Sun Bum name and voice feel coherent.
