# 106.别再害怕冲突，快来学习谈判术！

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode has [[QinZong|秦总]] recommend [[HarvardClassicNegotiation|《哈佛经典谈判术》]] and reframe negotiation as learnable [[NegotiationAsBargaining|商量]] rather than talent, obedience, or emotional fighting. It builds a practical sequence from [[BATNA]], [[ReservationValue]], and [[ZoneOfPossibleAgreement]] to [[AnchoringEffect]], [[ConcessionSignaling]], [[MultiIssueNegotiation]], and [[ContingentAgreement]]. The later sections turn to bias management through [[ZeroSumBias]], [[EscalationOfCommitment]], [[FramingEffect]], [[MotivatedBias]], and [[InterestBasedNegotiation]]. The episode's main contribution is a conflict-handling frame where preparation, information discovery, listening, and asking "why" are more important than eloquence.

## Key Claims
- Negotiation is presented as ordinary "商量": a way to exchange interests, calculate terms, and create value rather than a formal contest of status or rhetoric.
- Good preparation starts by identifying one's [[BATNA]], converting it into a [[ReservationValue]], estimating the other side's alternatives and bottom line, and mapping the [[ZoneOfPossibleAgreement]].
- A deal above one's own bottom line can still be poor negotiation if the negotiator never discovers the other side's real limit or the larger value available.
- Whether to make the first offer depends on information quality: first offers create [[AnchoringEffect|anchors]], but waiting for the other side can reveal information.
- Concessions should be made visible because unlabelled concessions are easy for the other side to discount, and concession size itself becomes a signal.
- A negotiator should preserve relationship value and satisfaction, because extracting every short-term dollar can damage repeat cooperation.
- [[MultiIssueNegotiation]] can create value by letting each side trade low-priority issues for high-priority ones rather than fighting one point at a time.
- [[ContingentAgreement|Contingent agreements]] can handle uncertainty by making later payments or concessions depend on observable future outcomes.
- Negotiation failures often come from bias: [[ZeroSumBias]], [[EscalationOfCommitment]], [[FramingEffect|loss framing]], and [[MotivatedBias]] can make parties reject gains, continue losing fights, or misread the other's intent.
- The episode repeatedly returns to [[InterestBasedNegotiation]]: ask why, listen carefully, exchange information, and search for the real concern behind the stated demand.

## Key Quotes
> "谈判其实是商量" - the episode's central reframing of negotiation.

> "不要把谈判搞僵" - the practical warning against turning disagreement into face-damaging escalation.

> "谈判成功不取决于说得多好，而取决于听得多好" - the closing emphasis on listening and asking why.
