# 中年三账户：现金流、肌肉、睡眠

## Summary
This [[Mianji|面基]] episode turns a 35-year-old host's family-age milestone into [[MidlifeThreeAccounts]]: cash flow, muscle, and sleep as slow-feedback accounts that determine long-run dignity, health, and autonomy. The first account connects household income, recurring obligations, and asset conversion to [[PersonalCashFlowAccount]], [[MiddleClassConsumptionPressure]], and [[SubscriptionFatigue]]. The second and third accounts shift the wiki's midlife frame from identity toward body infrastructure: [[MuscleAsLongevityInfrastructure]] and [[SleepAsDailyHealthAccount]] make aging, recovery, attention, and next-day function concrete.

## Key Claims
- [[MidlifeThreeAccounts]] frames cash flow, muscle, and sleep as measurable, compounding systems whose short-term changes are hard to feel but whose long-term effects can dominate quality of life.
- [[PersonalCashFlowAccount]] treats household finance as a reservoir: labor income, business income, and financial-asset income are inflows, while mortgages, rent, children, parents, retirement, subscriptions, and lifestyle maintenance are outflows.
- The source extends [[MiddleClassConsumptionPressure]] by arguing that midlife pressure comes from a common mismatch: income may plateau or fall while fixed monthly obligations and family responsibilities become harder to cut.
- Recurring digital payments and AI subscriptions extend [[SubscriptionFatigue]] beyond media choice into bank-account fragility, because many small automatic deductions can quietly become a lifestyle floor.
- [[MuscleAsLongevityInfrastructure]] shifts muscle from appearance to reserve capacity: skeletal muscle supports posture, glucose handling, recovery from illness, fall prevention, and older-age independence.
- The source uses [[Outlive|《超越百岁》]] to argue that strength training resembles retirement saving: the earlier deposits matter because later decline and illness can draw down the reserve quickly.
- [[SleepAsDailyHealthAccount]] is presented as the most immediate account, because a poor night can damage attention, mood, decision quality, training recovery, and metabolic function the next morning.
- Sleep is treated as an indirect-control problem. [[EnvironmentOverWillpower]] says the sleeper cannot force sleep itself, but can manage inputs such as wake time, morning light, caffeine timing, bedroom temperature, darkness, screens, alcohol, and weekend schedule drift.
- The source keeps its health practice pragmatic rather than maximalist: it favors walking, cycling, a nearby gym, protein, regular sleep conditions, and measured interventions while criticizing exaggerated supplement and body-hacking culture.
