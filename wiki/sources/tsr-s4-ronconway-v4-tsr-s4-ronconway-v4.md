## Summary
This [[TheSocialRadars]] episode continues [[RonConway]]'s oral history between his [[AltosComputer]] operating career and the later [[Google]] and [[Napster]] chapters. Conway explains [[SVAngel]]'s founder-advocacy posture, his first angel investment in [[NaturalLanguageIncorporated]], [[DonValentine]]'s mentorship, the partnership with [[BenRosen]], and the decision to focus on internet software. The episode's central synthesis is that early internet investing was built from relationships, platform timing, and information networks before accelerators, blogs, and modern seed infrastructure existed.

## Key Claims
- [[RonConway]] frames [[FounderAdvocacy]] as [[SVAngel]]'s mantra: investors should actively defend and help founders, not only fund them.
- Conway's public defense of [[MarkZuckerberg]] after a difficult interview with Kara Swisher shows founder advocacy as social intervention when a founder is treated unfairly.
- Conway says SV Angel invests in founders for life, using [[SeanFanning]] as an example of repeated backing after [[Napster]].
- Conway's first angel investment, [[NaturalLanguageIncorporated]], was an early natural-language AI company in Berkeley that he says was ahead of its time and later found a home through [[Microsoft]].
- After returning to [[AltosComputer]], Conway concluded that he disliked large-company management and HR work, helping push him toward early-stage investing.
- [[DonValentine]] became a practical mentor by inviting Conway to board meetings where he could observe founder advice, making the transition into investing a case in [[FounderInvestorLearning]].
- Conway and [[BenRosen]] built an [[InternetSoftwareThesis]] so they could filter deal flow and focus on the internet before the opportunity was obvious.
- The source treats analysts, journalists, conferences, newsletters, and trade publications as a [[StartupInformationLayer]] before YC-style startup infrastructure existed.
- [[Netscape]] becomes the [[BrowserAsInternetUnlock]] case: the browser made the internet usable for ordinary people and created a platform ecosystem that Conway wanted to be close to even after missing the investment.
- [[MikeHomer]] appears as Conway's route into the [[NetscapePlatformEcosystem]] and as a key marketing and culture builder during the browser war with [[Microsoft]].
- Conway credits [[Netscape]], [[MarkAndreessen]], [[MikeHomer]], and open-source software with creating an [[OpenSourceStartupCostCollapse]] that let internet startups begin with much less capital.
- [[AskJeeves]] becomes Conway and Rosen's early search win: a Q&A-style [[ConversationalSearchInterface]] investment that helped seed early SV Angel funds before [[Google]].
