# 115.京极夏彦今昔续百鬼：不研究妖怪，怎么好好做人？

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode introduces [[KyogokuNatsuhiko|京极夏彦]] through [[KonjakuZokuHyakkiKumo|《今昔续百鬼·云》]], especially the first story [[AnganKozo|《暗眼小僧》]]. The host frames Kyogoku's work as [[YokaiMystery|妖怪推理]] in the demystifying "research group" mode: the plots are dense with yokai, religion, folklore, and strange images, but the final account returns to human behavior, social pressure, war memory, and modernity. The episode uses [[TataraKatsugoro|多多良胜五郎]], [[NumagamiRenji|沼上蓮次]], and [[ToriyamaSekien|鸟山石燕]] to show how folklore research, comic wrong theories, and image interpretation become a way to ask how people should keep living after harm.

## Key Claims
- [[KyogokuNatsuhiko|京极夏彦]] is presented as more than a horror or puzzle-mystery writer: his major appeal is the integration of yokai knowledge, folklore research, character systems, postwar social change, and antiwar feeling.
- [[YokaiMystery|妖怪推理]] differs from the [[SettingBasedMystery|设定系推理]] branch added by episode 99: the source's "走科派" mode does not ask readers to accept supernatural rules as real puzzle physics, but asks why people interpret events through yokai.
- [[HyakkiYagyoSeries|百鬼夜行系列]] and related works form a shared Kyogokudo universe across historical periods, with [[KonjakuZokuHyakkiKumo|《今昔续百鬼·云》]] treated here as an accessible prequel-like entry point.
- [[TataraKatsugoro|多多良胜五郎]] embodies the fieldwork impulse: because documents and local traditions are disappearing after war, modernization, and industrial development, he treats local yokai stories as material that must be collected before it vanishes.
- [[AnganKozo|《暗眼小僧》]] ties a supposed kappa encounter to a property dispute, family inheritance conflict, land-development pressure, stolen documents, an old friend's death, and the revelation that "河童" is also the name of a dog.
- [[ToriyamaSekien|鸟山石燕]]'s image of 暗眼小僧 becomes a case of [[YokaiImageExegesis|妖怪图像阐释]]: homophones, boat and bank details, fish imagery, and moral allusion become interpretable signs without becoming direct proof.
- The story repeatedly lets [[TataraKatsugoro|多多良]] make vivid but wrong deductions, shifting the episode's interest from "who solved it" toward how wrong theories, folklore knowledge, and lived consequences change the people around the case.
- 富美's final reading makes the case ethical rather than only forensic: the dead dog 河童, the "雁木" image, and the family reconciliation make the mystery about mutual loss, responsibility, and how people continue after foolish conflict.
- The episode rejects nationalist origin games around folklore: being ancient or "native" does not automatically make a tradition greater, and using origin claims to prove national superiority is treated as intellectual bad faith.

## Key Quotes
> "走科派妖怪推理" - the host's label for demystifying yokai mystery.

> "不研究妖怪，怎么好好做人？" - the episode's title question and ethical frame.

> "古老并不意味着伟大" - the source's anti-nationalist folklore claim.
