## Summary
This [[ShizilukouCrossing]] episode interviews [[ZhangYi]], founder and CEO of [[WeilaiBuyuan]], about bringing the [[F2HomeRobot]] into roughly 300 Shanghai households through rental and testing. The discussion frames [[HomeServiceRobots]] as a long-cycle product problem where child care, light chores, safety, reliability, hardware cost, and household data matter more than demos alone. It also connects [[ZhangYi]]'s post-[[ZhangmenOneOnOne]] founder reset to a more patient view of [[EmbodiedAI]], financing bubbles, and sustainable commercialization.

## Key Claims
- [[WeilaiBuyuan]] is using real household deployment, not only lab demos, to shape the [[F2HomeRobot]] feature set and collect [[HouseholdRobotDataFlywheel]] signals.
- [[F2HomeRobot]] is positioned around child care and light housework: reading picture books, music practice, chess, hide-and-seek, toy pickup, trash pickup, and similar household tasks.
- Zhang argues that home robots should be priced against the service they replace or augment, especially high-quality child care, rather than by adding up isolated feature prices.
- [[HomeServiceRobots]] remain constrained by safety and reliability: the episode favors a wheeled form factor and two-claw hand because they are more practical inside most flat apartments than bipedal or dexterous-hand alternatives today.
- [[ConsumerRoboticsFullStack]] is presented as a cost strategy: Weilai Buyuan self-develops joints, motors, actuators, arms, claws, algorithms, and system integration where off-the-shelf industrial parts are too expensive or over-specified.
- Real family usage exposes corner cases that data factories miss, including children inventing new games, pets reacting badly, object instability, and human/animal interaction patterns.
- Zhang sees [[WorldModels]] and [[VisionLanguageActionModels]] as still-evolving routes for robot generalization, so he treats parallel technical paths and household feedback as more practical than betting on one finished model recipe.
- The episode accepts that embodied-intelligence financing has bubble dynamics, but argues that the bubble can still draw talent into a long-term field before the cycle resets.
- The business risk is not whether robots can attract attention, but whether they can stay in homes for months, keep users returning for hours per day, renew or convert, and generate referrals.
- Zhang's experience with [[ZhangmenOneOnOne]] and the Double Reduction shock makes him emphasize longer-horizon judgment, strategic consistency, and commercial durability over fast external validation.
