## Summary
This [[LateTalk]] episode interviews [[YangLingfeng]], co-founder and CEO of [[YangcongXueyuan]], about building a K12 learning company around [[SelfDirectedLearning]] rather than answer delivery or exam-shortcut training. Yang argues that AI can help as a planner, strategy distributor, and contextual support layer, but only if [[LearningExperienceDesign]] returns students to real reasoning instead of turning [[AIAsTutor]] into an answer machine. The source extends the wiki's AI-and-learning branch by adding [[AIShortcutRisk]] and [[EducationTechnologyFairness]] as constraints on education technology.

## Key Claims
- [[YangcongXueyuan]] was founded in 2013 after earlier rural education pilots showed that education gaps were not only hardware gaps; learning interaction, content design, and student confidence also mattered.
- [[YangLingfeng]] breaks [[SelfDirectedLearning]] into willingness, ability, tools, and belief: students need to believe they can independently learn, not only receive better explanations.
- The episode argues that many K12 subjects are learnable for ordinary students, but system-two thinking is effortful and easily avoided when the experience produces repeated failure.
- [[LearningExperienceDesign]] is Yangcong's answer to that difficulty: 5-to-8-minute lessons, animation for abstract concepts, purpose cues, empathy for stuck students, achievement loops, and data-driven iteration are all used to lower the threshold for real thinking.
- Yangcong avoided photo-solution search, two-teacher classes, and large livestream courses because those forms either over-serve students who can pay or move offline classroom logic onto a screen without creating a digital-native learning experience.
- Current large models are useful as contextual support, but Yang says they still struggle to teach system-two K12 knowledge from scratch with enough child-cognitive adaptation.
- AI in education has three broad uses in the source: standardizing teacher workflows, automating learning by-products such as homework correction, and making the core learning experience more flexible and vivid.
- The central risk is [[AIShortcutRisk]]: if AI only follows students' system-one preference for less effort and faster answers, it can manufacture shortcuts that weaken true learning.
- Schools and teachers remain important because many students need an external environment to build self-learning habits, confidence, planning, and responsibility.
- Yangcong's public-interest structure is not an after-profit add-on: rural schools and teachers meeting the company's standard have had free access from the beginning, and the source frames mission, product, and business structure as a Day One alignment problem.
- Yang says the future education goal is not only high scores, but self-reliance: confidence, learning ability, self-discipline, planning, goal-setting, and the ability to learn and apply new things.
- Knowledge remains important in the AI era because it trains ways of thinking; mathematics is singled out as a carrier for logic and understanding complex systems.
