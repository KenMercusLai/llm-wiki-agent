# 118.是，大臣：辛辣讽刺永不过时

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode reads [[YesMinister|《是，大臣》 / Yes Minister]] and [[YesPrimeMinister|《是，首相》 / Yes, Prime Minister]] as durable political satire about elected ambition meeting permanent bureaucratic control. The host explains how [[JamesHacker|James Hacker]], [[HumphreyAppleby|Humphrey Appleby]], [[BernardWoolley|Bernard Woolley]], and [[ArnoldRobinson|Arnold Robinson]] turn reform promises, red boxes, speeches, open-government slogans, media pressure, and honors into a system of comic but precise institutional struggle. The strongest examples are the civil service's containment of open government and the [[BaillieCollege|Baillie College]] plot, where foreign-student fees, honorary doctorates, budget savings, and honours lists become one exchange network.

## Key Claims
- [[YesMinister|《是，大臣》]] remains powerful because its jokes are built from [[InstitutionalMechanismSatire]] rather than only quotable cynicism: ministers, civil servants, prime ministers, media, voters, schools, and honours systems all constrain one another.
- [[CivilServiceContinuity]] is the central structural conflict. [[JamesHacker|Hacker]] arrives as an elected reformer, while [[HumphreyAppleby|Humphrey]] represents the permanent official layer that values stability, secrecy, and departmental interest.
- [[BernardWoolley|Bernard]] is funny because his loyalty is structurally split: he serves the minister, but his career remains inside the civil-service hierarchy.
- The open-government plot shows [[AdministrativeOpacityAsControl]]: transparency language can be praised, drafted, delayed, and proceduralized until it protects secrecy.
- Red boxes, long sentences, standard replies, speeches, committees, and full calendars become [[BureaucraticWorkloadControl]], keeping the minister busy enough that control over information and timing stays with officials.
- The [[BaillieCollege|Baillie College]] story turns education prestige, foreign-student subsidies, honorary doctorates, and honours recommendations into [[HonoursPatronageExchange]].
- [[HumphreyAppleby|Humphrey]] is presented as close to a Machiavellian political technician, but the episode's realism is institutional rather than princely: the civil servant survives by controlling options, information, and procedure.

## Key Quotes
> "赢的那一边" - Bernard's compressed answer to which side civil servants support.

> "不知情的权利" - the comic reversal used to justify opacity against open government.

> "是的，博士" - the final twist of the Baillie College exchange, where title, savings, and honours all settle together.
