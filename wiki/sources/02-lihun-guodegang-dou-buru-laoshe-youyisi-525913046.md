# 02.离婚：郭德纲都不如老舍有意思

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode rereads [[LihunNovel|《离婚》]] as a major [[LaoShe|老舍]] novel whose comedy depends on suffocation, not only joke density. The hosts move through [[LaoLiLihun|老李]], [[ZhangDageLihun|张大哥]], [[XiaoZhaoLihun|小赵]], [[DingErLihun|丁二]], [[LiTaitaiLihun|李太太]], and [[MaShaonainaiLihun|马少奶奶]] to show how Beiping office life, marriage, face, favors, fantasy, and cowardice form a comic but airless social world. The source extends [[LaoSheSatiricalHumanism]] by treating Lao She's vernacular rhythm and crosstalk-adjacent humor as a controlled literary method that makes ordinary life funny and frightening at the same time.

## Key Claims
- The episode pushes against a textbook-only image of [[LaoShe|老舍]] as merely solemn or revolutionary; it presents him as a writer with strong oral rhythm, crosstalk sensibility, and comic timing.
- [[LihunNovel|《离婚》]] is framed as Lao She's own favored novel in the episode and as a work about marriage, office politics, and life paralysis rather than a practical argument for divorce.
- [[LaoLiLihun|老李]] is not simply weak. His half-awake disgust, romantic fantasies, failure to protect his wife, and final departure make him a recognizably ordinary person caught between ideals and action.
- [[ZhangDageLihun|张大哥]] is frightening because he is not a villain. His competence, favors, matchmaking, and decency turn ordinary social order into pressure that leaves little room for another life.
- [[XiaoZhaoLihun|小赵]] represents casual, opportunistic harm inside the office world: he humiliates the vulnerable, trades in sexual access and official connections, and exploits Zhang Dage's family crisis.
- [[DingErLihun|丁二]] reverses the usual hierarchy of usefulness. The man treated as a useless hanger-on becomes the one person who takes decisive action against Xiao Zhao.
- [[LiTaitaiLihun|李太太]] is not only an obstacle to Lao Li's romantic life. Her rough adjustment to Beiping and her care during Lao Li's illness make the marriage plot harder than a simple freedom-versus-arranged-marriage frame.
- [[MaShaonainaiLihun|马少奶奶]] functions as a projected moon or poetic escape for Lao Li, but the plot punctures that projection when Ma Shaoye returns and the imagined free-love alternative collapses into vulgarity.
- The episode treats the finance office as a [[BeipingOfficeWorld]] where punctual work, promotion, superstition, factional influence, favors, and punishment mix into absurd bureaucracy.
- [[ControlledHumorWithPain]] names the episode's key reading of Lao She: humor is present everywhere, but it is restrained enough that laughter deepens discomfort.
- [[CrosstalkLiteraryRhythm]] explains why the source links Lao She's prose to oral performance and xiangsheng history without reducing the novel to spoken comedy.
- [[OrdinaryEscapeCourage]] captures the unresolved ending: leaving a secure promoted post with wife and children is not a clean heroic victory, but it may still be the hardest action available to an ordinary person.

## Key Quotes
> "一切人的大哥" - the episode's compact marker for Zhang Dage's universal social role.

> "含着泪的微笑" - the source's phrase for the older frame Lao She both uses and tries to exceed.

> "你是好人" - Ding Er's judgment of Lao Li before Ding Er becomes the one who acts.
