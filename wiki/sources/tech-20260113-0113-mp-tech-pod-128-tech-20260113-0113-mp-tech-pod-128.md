## Summary
This [[MarketplaceTech]] episode has [[MeganMcCartyCorino|Megan McCarty-Corino]] interview [[TomMinelli]] of [[IDC]] about how AI data-center demand is consuming memory supply, especially [[HighBandwidthMemory]] paired with [[Nvidia]] accelerators. The episode extends [[MemoryChipShortage]] and [[AIHardwareSupplyChainPressure]] by showing how a data-center-led shortage can raise PC, smartphone, and gaming-rig costs, alter consumer product specs, and shift share toward larger vendors. It also adds [[AIPCMemoryDemand]]: the push for NPU-equipped AI PCs increases RAM needs just as memory gets more expensive.

## Key Claims
- [[HighBandwidthMemory]] is described as essential for training and running AI workloads and is often paired with [[Nvidia]] chips.
- [[TomMinelli]] says memory has historically been a boom-and-bust industry, but AI data centers have added a large new demand source.
- [[IDC]] expects the shortage to remain a problem through 2026 and says it could extend into 2027 if AI data-center spending continues.
- The episode says only a few companies can make high-bandwidth memory, while the broader consumer-memory market is also concentrated.
- Minelli says [[MicronTechnology]] announced in December that it would leave the consumer RAM market because data-center customers are larger and margins are better.
- Memory manufacturing is capital intensive, so new entrants are unlikely to relieve the shortage quickly.
- Large PC vendors such as [[HPInc|HP]], [[DellTechnologies|Dell]], [[Lenovo]], and [[Apple]] may be better positioned to secure memory allocations than smaller regional vendors and custom desktop builders.
- Consumers may see higher PC prices, fewer buying options, or tradeoffs where vendors offset RAM costs by using less powerful CPUs or other cheaper components.
- Gaming PCs are especially exposed because buyers want powerful CPUs and large memory configurations while many builders lack large-OEM purchasing leverage.
- AI PCs add another demand layer because they include [[NeuralProcessingUnits]] and need more RAM; the episode cites Microsoft's Copilot Plus PC minimum of 16 GB, with 32 GB framed as better.
- Smartphone price increases may feel less visible in mature markets because of installment plans, while PC buyers often face the full price upfront.

## Key Quotes
> "perfect storm" - Minelli's phrase for AI PCs adding memory demand during a shortage.

> "throughout 2026" - IDC's shortage timeframe in the episode.

> "only three companies" - the source's summary of HBM supplier concentration.
