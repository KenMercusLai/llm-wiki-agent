# 54.玫瑰的名字（下）：真与假，正与邪，诠释与过度诠释

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode completes the discussion of [[UmbertoEco|翁贝托·艾柯 / Umberto Eco]]'s [[TheNameOfTheRose|《玫瑰的名字》 / The Name of the Rose]] by resolving the abbey deaths, the forbidden library, and [[JorgeOfBurgos|豪尔赫 / Jorge of Burgos]]'s control over knowledge. The solution turns the apparent [[BookOfRevelation|《启示录》 / Book of Revelation]] pattern into a lesson in [[InterpretationAndOverinterpretation]]: many signs looked coherent because [[WilliamOfBaskerville|巴斯克维尔的威廉 / William of Baskerville]], the monks, and the reader wanted them to cohere. The episode's strongest synthesis is that Eco uses detective pleasure to expose its own limits: reason matters, but only when joined to [[RationalHumility]], evidence, and resistance to [[ConspiracyTheoryPatternSeeking]].

## Key Claims
- The abbey murders are not a clean Revelation-coded design. The deaths cluster around access to [[AristotlePoeticsBookTwo|亚里士多德《诗学》第二卷 / Aristotle's Poetics Book II]], poisoned pages, sexual jealousy, institutional secrecy, and opportunistic manipulation.
- [[JorgeOfBurgos|Jorge]] is the hidden power center of the monastery because he has long shaped abbots, librarians, access rules, fear, and doctrine from behind the official hierarchy.
- [[AristotlePoeticsBookTwo|亚里士多德《诗学》第二卷]] matters because Jorge fears laughter gaining philosophical legitimacy. A dangerous book becomes dangerous not only as content, but as authorized permission to laugh at solemn power.
- [[WilliamOfBaskerville|William]] still represents observation, experience, and skeptical reason, but this episode stresses his fallibility. His Revelation theory partly works by accident, making the novel an example of [[AntiDetectiveFiction]] as well as detective fiction.
- [[BernardGui|贝尔纳 / Bernard Gui]] shows a different kind of "investigation": he converts fear, torture, confessions, and weak evidence into a [[PoliticalShowTrial]] useful for the [[AvignonPapacy|阿维尼翁教廷 / Avignon Papacy]] delegation.
- [[AdsoOfMelk|阿德索 / Adso of Melk]] and the unnamed village girl expose the social cruelty beneath theological abstractions. Political protection can save Ubertino, but not a poor woman labeled a witch.
- The library fire makes [[KnowledgeMonopoly]] self-defeating: the attempt to preserve dangerous knowledge by hiding it ends in the destruction of the books.
- The closing discussion links Eco's overinterpretation theme to modern conspiracy thinking, where complex hidden patterns feel more seductive than simple, testable explanations.

## Key Quotes
> "一千只蝎子的毒" - Malachi's dying phrase, used in the episode to show how Jorge can nudge William back toward a false Revelation pattern.

> "我从不猜测" - the Holmesian posture the hosts contrast with William's later admission that good reasoning still contains conjecture.

> "真正的真理不会畏惧笑" - the episode's closing formulation of laughter as a test of authority rather than an enemy of truth.
