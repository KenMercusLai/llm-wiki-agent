# 122.匹诺曹：成长的惨痛及其所创作的

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode rereads [[AdventuresOfPinocchio|《木偶奇遇记》 / The Adventures of Pinocchio]] beyond the familiar "lying makes the nose grow" and [[TheWaltDisneyCompany|Disney]] memory. It presents [[CarloCollodi|Carlo Collodi / 卡洛·科洛迪]]'s original as darker, more dreamlike, and more psychologically unsettled than a safe children's moral tale. The episode's central claim is that [[Pinocchio]] becomes human not by simple obedience, but by passing through hunger, fear, guilt, repeated error, parental loss, and finally [[GrowthAsProtectorRole|the shift from protected child to protector]].

## Key Claims
- [[AdventuresOfPinocchio|《木偶奇遇记》]] is not mainly a warning about lying. The nose episode is brief, while the original's larger structure is hunger, temptation, injury, death fear, recurrence, work, and family responsibility.
- [[CarloCollodi|Collodi]]'s source history matters: the episode says the story began as an 1881 serial and initially ended with the puppet hanged by villains before reader pressure drew the author back into the longer version.
- [[Pinocchio]] is already himself before he is carved. The talking piece of wood feels pain, causes trouble, insults adults, and resists being treated as raw material, so the story is about discovering a self as much as manufacturing a child.
- [[HeroJourneyNarrative]] is used to map the fairy tale's movement through call, refusal, mentor, tests, symbolic death, whale/shark-belly darkness, return, and transformation.
- [[Geppetto]]'s sacrifices make fatherhood concrete: he gives food, builds new feet, sells his coat for schoolbooks, searches at sea, and later becomes weak enough that Pinocchio must support him.
- [[BlueFairy|The Blue Fairy]] functions as more than a reward dispenser. The episode reads her changing forms, apparent death, mother role, and testing as part of Pinocchio's guilt, attachment, and moral maturation.
- [[GrowthAsProtectorRole]] is the episode's clearest growth theory. Pinocchio's school success matters less than the shark-belly rescue, daily labor for his father, and giving his savings to the impoverished fairy.
- [[NonhumanBecomingHumanMotif]] connects Pinocchio with [[TheLittleMermaid|《海的女儿》]], robot and cyborg stories, Chinese fox or object-spirit tales, and [[GhostInTheShellStandaloneComplex|攻壳机动队]]-style questions about whether becoming human means imitating, exceeding, or discarding a body.
- The original is read as a [[DreamLogicNarrative]]: episodes connect with the force of dream association, sudden punishment, symbolic animals, fake food, death tableaux, and strange returns rather than clean causal realism.
- The episode's psychological claims about mother figures, unconscious structure, and animal symbolism are kept source-scoped; the source itself notes that some of this interpretation is the host's extrapolation rather than fully documented scholarly argument.

## Key Quotes
> "我的鼻子会变长" - the Pinocchio paradox used to open the episode and mark the character's cultural saturation.

> "当我们成为好人的时候，一切都变了模样，而变得更加美好" - the line the episode uses to argue that "good" means finding oneself, not merely obeying.

## Contradictions
- No direct contradiction found. The source aligns with the Andersen and Alice branches by arguing that children's classics can be frightening, complex, and resistant to safe moral paraphrase.
