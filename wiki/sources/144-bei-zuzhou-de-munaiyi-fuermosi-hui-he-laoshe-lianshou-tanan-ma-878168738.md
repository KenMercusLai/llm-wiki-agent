# 144. 被诅咒的木乃伊：福尔摩斯会和老舍联手探案吗？

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode reads [[ShimadaSoji|岛田庄司]]'s [[TheCursedMummy|《被诅咒的木乃伊》]] less as a peak locked-room puzzle than as a playful [[HolmesPasticheTradition|福尔摩斯同人]] literary game. The novel places [[SherlockHolmes|福尔摩斯]], [[JohnWatson|华生]], and [[NatsumeSoseki|夏目漱石]] inside a London case involving a false Eastern curse, a mummy corpse, an impostor, a sealed room, and two conflicting manuscript accounts. The episode then extends the pattern toward Chinese Holmes afterlives and asks whether [[LaoShe|老舍]] or other Chinese figures in Britain could be imagined into a similar cross-cultural detective encounter.

## Key Claims
- [[TheCursedMummy|《被诅咒的木乃伊》]] is valuable partly because the "Holmes flavor" works: the case, 221B comedy, Watson framing, Lestrade police context, and gothic atmosphere all draw on familiar Holmes machinery.
- The source frames the novel's core pleasure as [[DualManuscriptNarration|双手稿叙事]]: Soseki's account makes Holmes look unstable and absurd, while Watson's version restores the cool classical detective.
- The mummy case is a fair-enough [[HonkakuMystery|本格推理]] mechanism, but the episode says its trick is not the whole point; literary impersonation, narrative mismatch, and cultural jokes matter at least as much.
- The "Eastern curse" is exposed as [[OrientalistMysteryMisdirection|东方主义谜题误导]]: the criminals rely on mixed-up Chinese/Japanese/Oriental props and British prejudice rather than real Asian knowledge.
- The solution turns on mundane replacement mechanics: the real Kingsley was already dead and naturally mummified, an impostor hid in Japanese armor, and a staged fire helped him escape attention.
- The "此内61" clue resolves only when Soseki rotates the fragment and sees it as part of "19世纪", reconnecting the puzzle to the dead man's final letter rather than to Japanese writing.
- The closing discussion extends [[HolmesPasticheTradition|Holmes pastiche]] into a global reader practice, linking European, Japanese, Indian, and Chinese Holmes parodies, and imagining Holmes beside [[LaoShe|老舍]], Li Siguang, Xu Zhimo, Qian Zhongshu, Yang Jiang, Hua Luogeng, and Yan Fu.

## Key Quotes
> "味儿对" - the episode's shorthand for why the book works as Holmes pastiche even if its trick is not the strongest part.

> "此内61" - the false Japanese-looking clue that eventually becomes part of "19世纪" when rotated.

> "福尔摩斯会和老舍联手探案吗" - the closing thought experiment that extends the Soseki-Holmes device toward Chinese literary history.
