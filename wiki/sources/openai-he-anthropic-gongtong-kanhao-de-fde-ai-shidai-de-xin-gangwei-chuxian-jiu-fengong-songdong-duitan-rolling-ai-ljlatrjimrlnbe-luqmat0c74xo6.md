## Summary
This [[ShizilukouCrossing]] episode with [[RollingAI]] partners [[Agan]] and [[LiuKai]] argues that enterprise AI adoption is less like buying software and more like onboarding [[DigitalEmployees]]. The guests explain why [[OpenAI]] and [[Anthropic]] are emphasizing [[ForwardDeployedEngineer]] work: enterprise AI needs business integration, knowledge governance, system integration, human-AI collaboration design, and incentive changes before agents can produce durable operating results. The episode extends the wiki's agent and context themes into [[FrontlineAIEnablement]], [[BusinessLedAITransformation]], and [[ServiceAsSoftware]].

## Key Claims
- [[ForwardDeployedEngineer]] work is presented as an AI-era implementation layer descended from [[Palantir]]: engineers or business builders enter the customer site, understand the real workflow, and help build usable systems rather than only selling a product.
- AI in enterprises should be treated as [[DigitalEmployees]] rather than ordinary tools; FDEs help those digital workers understand business context, access systems, learn from experts, and actually get on the job.
- [[RollingAI]] frames the FDE job as three linked responsibilities: business integration, knowledge governance, and system integration.
- The episode argues that enterprise AI value often appears at the frontline, where store managers, salespeople, property managers, or service workers combine AI analysis with local context and street-level judgment.
- The guests reject a purely top-down best-practice model; their "apprentice" loop asks AI to shadow strong frontline workers, help them first, then learn from their situated experience.
- The source claims that "AI plus human" usually beats a human alone, and a human usually beats AI alone; therefore strong employees should be treated as teachers and assets, not discarded.
- [[BusinessLedAITransformation]] requires more than model access: the guests say technology is at most one third of the problem, and failures often come from unrealistic CEO expectations, IT-led rather than business-led projects, or unchanged incentives.
- The rental-platform case reframes AI automation away from faster replies alone: AI handles repetitive customer issues while humans shift toward emotionally warmer care and higher-value sales.
- Routine information-transfer and execution-heavy white-collar roles are presented as vulnerable, while judgment, tradeoffs, emotional value, curiosity, and problem-solving become more important.
- [[ServiceAsSoftware]] describes Rolling AI's position between consulting and SaaS: software or agents are the delivery form, but the value is service-like business outcome creation.
