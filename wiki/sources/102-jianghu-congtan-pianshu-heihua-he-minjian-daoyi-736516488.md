# 102.江湖丛谈：骗术、黑话和民间道义

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode uses [[LianKuoru|连阔如]]'s [[JiangHuCongTan|《江湖丛谈》]] to read Jianghu as street-level livelihood rather than wuxia romance. It follows [[ChunDianArgot|春点]] argot, fortune-telling routines, fake medicine, mole-removal scams, staged railway-station fraud, street performance, guards, thieves, beggar groups, and quyi circles as parts of [[StreetJianghuSociety]]. Its core synthesis is that Jianghu mixed extraction and mutual aid: black talk, secret rules, and informal authority could enable fraud, but also helped marginal workers coordinate, protect trade knowledge, enforce reputation, and build a fragile sense of dignity.

## Key Claims
- [[JiangHuCongTan|《江湖丛谈》]] presents Jianghu less as martial heroics than as a social field of itinerant trade, performance, scams, lodging, language, and informal enforcement.
- [[LianKuoru|连阔如]] is treated as unusually qualified to write the book because his life crossed apprenticeships, small business, fortune-telling, storytelling, Beijing theater circles, and wartime media pressure.
- [[ChunDianArgot|春点]] was not a handful of passwords; the episode presents it as a large language system used for secrecy, group recognition, trade coordination, and status boundary-making.
- The episode distinguishes revealing Jianghu secrets from betraying Jianghu. It frames Lian's disclosure as public-serving because it exposed predatory tactics without simply converting insider knowledge into official status.
- Street scams depended on [[JianghuScamCraft]]: observation, cold reading, free first hooks, staged collaborators, ambiguous pricing, threat of bad luck, fake expertise, and victim role assignment.
- Fortune-telling and physiognomy scams worked partly by reading emotion, clothing, accent, geography, occupation, and social distress, then converting ordinary inference into supernatural authority.
- Fake medicine and mole-removal scams show how bodily fear, asymmetrical knowledge, and pseudoscientific packaging make victims vulnerable before any formal transaction begins.
- "调门" railway-station scams resemble modern [[SocialEngineeringFraud]] because several participants manufacture a situation in which the victim feels like an insider, rescuer, bargain-hunter, or lucky finder.
- The episode refuses to reduce Jianghu to fraud. Skilled performers, guards, acrobats, storytellers, and street artists could have real craft and still rely on audience psychology, helpers, and reputation.
- [[InformalJianghuOrder]] appears through 老何, 拜相/拜码头, 常春会, 生意下处, trade spacing, forbidden eavesdropping, thief-guard negotiation, and mediation through respected insiders.
- Jianghu morality was practical rather than pure. Rules and generosity often served the condition that everyone needed enough order to keep eating.
- The episode links old argot to modern [[JargonBoundaryMaking]] in internet startup language, academic/theory language, and youth slang, arguing that jargon can protect precision or solidarity but can also become status display and exclusion.

## Key Quotes
> "江湖不是武侠" - the episode's basic corrective.

> "宁舍一锭金，不传一句春" - the secrecy ethic around [[ChunDianArgot|春点]].
