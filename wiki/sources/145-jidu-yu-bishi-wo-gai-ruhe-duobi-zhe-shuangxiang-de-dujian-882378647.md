# 145.嫉妒与鄙视：我该如何躲避这双向的毒箭？

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode uses [[SusanFiske|Susan Fiske / 苏珊·菲斯克]]'s [[JiDuYuBiShi|《嫉妒与鄙视》]] to treat envy and contempt as linked products of [[SocialComparisonPressure|social comparison pressure]] rather than as private moral defects. It argues that upward comparison can produce envy, downward comparison can produce contempt, and both emotions have adaptive signal value while also risking [[DehumanizationByComparison|dehumanization by comparison]], online attack, group hostility, and health damage. The practical answer is not denial of ugly feeling, but [[EmpathyAgainstComparison|empathy against comparison]]: concrete information about others, changed comparison frames, benign envy, praise, cooperation, and team designs that make people feel like a shared unit.

## Key Claims
- [[EnvyContemptComparison|Envy and contempt]] are "fraternal twins" because both arise from comparison, but they point in opposite status directions.
- Envy is framed as wanting what another person has while feeling that they should not have it; contempt is framed as having what another lacks and deciding that the other is not worth concern.
- Modern equality ideals coexist with visible inequality, so exams, class tracks, elite credentials, taste hierarchies, region, work, income, and online display all multiply comparison triggers.
- Social animals use comparison to infer status and cooperation terms; the episode treats envy and contempt as evolved social signals rather than as purely useless emotions.
- Envy can motivate effort, competition, and resistance to unfair advantage, but chronic envy can damage self-respect, close relationships, group solidarity, health, and willingness to accept help.
- Contempt can help enforce norms against real harm, but it can also combine disgust, distance, superiority, racism, dehumanization, and anxiety over status challenge.
- [[SchadenfreudeAsComparisonEmotion|Schadenfreude]] is presented as a mixture of envy and contempt: an envied person falls, and their misfortune becomes emotionally rewarding.
- Group identity amplifies the pattern because nationality, ethnicity, gender, school, fandom, or class can become part of the self; shared resentment can then feel like belonging.
- Networked "toilet culture" and pile-ons are interpreted as comparison stress and lower-position anger moving through [[InternetMoralTrial|internet moral trial]], [[Deindividuation]], and group approval.
- Repair depends on making people concrete: more individuating information can weaken stereotypes, especially when it restores the missing [[WarmthCompetenceStereotype|warmth or competence]] dimension.
- Personal strategies include self-reliance, selective ignoring, self-support, checking whether the goal is truly worth wanting, replacing horizontal comparison with self-development comparison, and accepting [[EnvyContemptComparison|benign envy]] that does not try to deprive others.
- Team and relationship strategies include avoiding unnecessary comparison, reducing status flaunting, explaining differences, recognizing distinct abilities, and building a "unit relation" close to [[CommunityFeeling|community feeling]].

## Key Quotes
> "我想拥有你已经拥有的，而你不应该拥有它" - the episode's compact definition of envy.

> "我拥有你没有的东西，并认为你不值得关心" - the matching definition of contempt.

> "同理心是解药" - the closing answer to the two-way arrow of envy and contempt.
