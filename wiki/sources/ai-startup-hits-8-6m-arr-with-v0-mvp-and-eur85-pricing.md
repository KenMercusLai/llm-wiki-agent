## Summary
This [[TheSaaSPodcast]] episode features [[MariusMiners]] describing how [[PeakAI]] grew quickly in the emerging [[AISearchAnalytics]] category. The conversation traces failed startup ideas, [[Antler]], fast idea cycling, pre-product selling, a V0/vibe-coded prototype, non-binding LOIs, mid-market pricing around EUR85/$89 per month, and growth through LinkedIn, word of mouth, and AI search itself. Miners argues that early B2B validation is strongest when buyers already name the problem as urgent, not when founders successfully pitch an interesting solution.

## Key Claims
- [[PeakAI]] helps companies understand how they appear across AI search and answer tools such as [[ChatGPT]], [[Perplexity]], and [[Gemini]], including brand visibility, citations, and sentiment.
- The company reportedly reached more than 2,000 customers, $8.6 million ARR, and 55 team members within 14 months.
- [[MariusMiners]] and his co-founders discarded legal-tech and regulatory-tech ideas after a few weeks because they did not feel strong market pull.
- The validation method centered on asking target customers for their top problems before pitching a solution, making [[PreProductSelling]] a test of existing urgency rather than persuasion skill.
- Early signals included expert SEO professionals already discussing AI search, warm social leads, free trials, LOIs, and later willingness to pay; Miners still says actual credit-card payment beats non-binding LOIs.
- The first prototype was built quickly with AI, helped secure around eight or nine LOIs and roughly EUR100K from [[Antler]], and was then replaced by a production product built in about six weeks.
- Peak chose mid-market and SMB-friendly pricing instead of only enterprise pricing, arguing that smaller marketing teams need fast time-to-value rather than long pilots and deep custom integrations.
- Growth came through social content, especially LinkedIn, word of mouth, and [[GenerativeEngineOptimization]] as an acquisition channel for Peak itself.

## Key Quotes
> "vibe coded" - Miners's description of the first prototype.

> "pretty ruthless" - Miners's caution about treating LOIs as validation.

> "path of least resistance" - how the team found early users already discussing AI search.

> "100 cold emails is not much" - Miners's reminder that weak outreach volume can misread demand.
