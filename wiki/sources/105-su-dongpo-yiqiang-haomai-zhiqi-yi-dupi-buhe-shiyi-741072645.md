# 105.苏东坡：一腔豪迈之气，一肚皮不合时宜

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode rereads [[SuShi|苏轼 / 苏东坡]] as a flawed, tender, politically stubborn human being rather than only a carefree cultural icon. It follows his life from [[SuXun|苏洵]], [[SuZhe|苏辙]], [[ChengFuren|程夫人]], and [[OuyangXiu|欧阳修]] through [[WangAnshi|王安石]], the [[WangAnshiReforms|王安石变法]], [[SongFactionalPolitics|宋代党争]], and the [[WutaiPoetryCase|乌台诗案]]. Its main contribution is a Su Dongpo cluster around [[EarnedOptimism]], [[ConscienceOverFaction]], [[ScholarOfficialPublicDuty]], [[PoetryAsPoliticalEvidence]], and [[LocalGovernanceAsCare]].

## Key Claims
- The episode argues that [[SuShi|苏轼 / 苏东坡]] should not be flattened into a perfect "坡仙": his naivete, bluntness, temper, appetite, and poor political caution are part of why his goodness feels human.
- [[ChengFuren|程夫人]] is treated as an early moral source for Su Shi's later refusal to ignore suffering; household teaching, the Fan Pang story, and care for living things become biographical ground rather than decorative anecdote.
- [[OuyangXiu|欧阳修]]'s recognition of Su Shi shows how literary talent entered official life, but the episode repeatedly connects writing ability to public duty rather than only fame.
- The [[WangAnshiReforms|王安石变法]] are presented with their fiscal and security motives, while the hosts stress the harshness of implementation and the danger of policy becoming extraction from ordinary people.
- [[SongFactionalPolitics|宋代党争]] is not treated as a clean new-party-versus-old-party morality play. Su Shi opposed harmful new-law practice, then later also objected when the old party retaliated against reformers.
- The [[WutaiPoetryCase|乌台诗案]] shows how poetry, memorials, jokes, and sympathy for imprisoned poor people could be converted into [[PoetryAsPoliticalEvidence|political evidence]].
- [[Huangzhou|黄州]] is framed as Su Shi's decisive maturation point: poverty, surveillance, farming, [[DongpoRou|东坡肉]], [[RedCliffFu|《赤壁赋》]], and [[NianNuJiaoChibiHuaiGu|《念奴娇·赤壁怀古》]] turn loss of status into creative and ethical consolidation.
- The episode reads [[DingFengBoSuShi|《定风波》]] against escapist optimism: "莫听" means not letting wind and rain rule the heart after they have been heard, not refusing reality.
- Su Shi's later local service in [[Hangzhou|杭州]], [[Xuzhou|徐州]], [[Huizhou|惠州]], and [[Danzhou|儋州]] supports the episode's [[ScholarOfficialPublicDuty]] frame: literary greatness is joined to flood control, water works, public health, infant rescue, bridges, and schools.
- [[WangChaoyun|王朝云]]'s "一肚皮不合时宜" and the "此心安处是吾乡" story both make inner peace a matter of conscience and relation, not simply geographical comfort or leisure.

## Key Quotes
> "人无完人" - the episode's entry point for deflating a perfect Su Dongpo image.

> "人生到处知何似，应似飞鸿踏雪泥" - the Fengxiang-era poem used to frame transience and drift.

> "莫听穿林打叶声，何妨吟啸且徐行" - the line the episode rereads as resilience rather than denial.

> "一肚皮不合时宜" - Wang Chaoyun's phrase for Su Shi's temperamental mismatch with political convenience.
