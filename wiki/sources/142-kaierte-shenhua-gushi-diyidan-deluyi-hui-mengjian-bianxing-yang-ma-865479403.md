# 142. 凯尔特神话故事（第一弹）：德鲁伊会梦见变形羊吗？

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode opens a [[CelticMythology]] series by treating "Celtic" as a broad linguistic and cultural field rather than one nation, region, or fixed canon. It connects Irish, Welsh, Scottish, Breton, Manx, and continental Celtic material to Indo-European comparison, Christian preservation, Romantic revival, and modern fantasy influence. Its central stories - [[Danu|达努]], [[TuathaDeDanann|Tuatha De Danann / 达努神族]], [[LeborGabalaErenn|《入侵之书》]], [[TuanMacCairill|图安]], [[FionnMacCumhaill|芬恩]], and [[Ossian|Oisin / Ossian / 鹅相]] - make [[CelticAnimalTransformation]], [[DruidicKnowledge]], and [[CelticOtherworld]] the episode's strongest wiki contributions.

## Key Claims
- "Celtic mythology" is a convenience label for a multi-region field: Ireland, Wales, Scotland, Brittany, Man, England, and Gaul preserve different languages, stories, and later textual layers.
- The episode contrasts [[CelticMythology]] with the darker apocalyptic tone of Norse/Germanic mythology, arguing that Celtic material often keeps death porous through reincarnation, soul continuity, animal transformation, and otherworldly time.
- [[Danu|达努]] and the sacred oak Bile open the source's creation account; [[TheDagda|The Dagda / 达格达]] and other descendants lead into the [[TuathaDeDanann|Tuatha De Danann / 达努神族]] as a divine people tied to Ireland.
- The [[TuathaDeDanann|Tuatha De Danann / 达努神族]] retreat into the sidhe after the Sons of Mil take Ireland, turning defeated gods into underground or fairy-like powers rather than erasing them.
- [[LeborGabalaErenn|《入侵之书》]] shows Christianized medieval preservation: Biblical flood genealogies and older Irish migration memories coexist instead of forming one clean historical account.
- [[TuanMacCairill|图安]] turns reincarnation into cultural memory. His successive forms as stag, boar, eagle, salmon, and human let one witness carry settlement waves, old faith, hospitality ethics, and eventual baptism across eras.
- [[DruidicKnowledge]] is presented as a mixture of wisdom, ritual authority, magic, hospitality law, animal transformation, and a darker sacrificial side.
- The [[FionnMacCumhaill|芬恩]] stories use dogs, enchanted women, transformed kin, and impossible athletic contests to show how the Fenian material can be comic, tender, violent, and heroic at once.
- The episode links [[CelticMythology]] to modern cultural afterlives including Enya, Asterix, Brave, Frozen II, Harry Potter, [[JRRTolkien|J.R.R. Tolkien / 托尔金]], George R. R. Martin, and Arthurian legend without claiming all of them share one direct source line.
