# sp.03 串台｜趁生命气息逗留：泽拉兹尼与他的梦境

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode uses [[RogerZelazny|Roger Zelazny / 罗杰·泽拉兹尼]]'s Chinese short-story collection [[ForABreathITarry|《趁生命气息逗留》 / For a Breath I Tarry]] as a route into his career, translation history, and position in [[ScienceFictionNewWave|New Wave science fiction]]. Translator [[Beimin|北明]] and editor [[Doudou|逗逗]] argue that Zelazny's formal experimentation remains anchored in humanism, resistance, myth, poetry, body, and awe rather than simple nihilism. The episode's close readings connect [[LastDefenderOfCamelot|《卡美洛的最后守护者》]], [[HomeIsTheHangman|《刽子手返乡》]], [[PermafrostZelazny|《永久冻土》]], and [[TwentyFourViewsOfMountFujiByHokusai|《富岳二十四景》]] to [[RobotHumanBoundary]], [[EmbodiedPersonhood]], [[DigitalTranscendenceAsControl]], and [[MythicScienceFictionStyle]].

## Key Claims
- [[ForABreathITarry|《趁生命气息逗留》]] works as a strong Zelazny entry point because the collection spans his early, middle, and later writing rather than presenting only a fixed late style.
- The episode positions Zelazny as a transitional figure in [[ScienceFictionNewWave|New Wave science fiction]]: he experiments with psychology, style, genre boundary, myth, and literary allusion while keeping a traditional science-fiction core of human dignity, struggle, and grandeur.
- [[MythicScienceFictionStyle]] is central to the episode's Zelazny reading. Religious legend, Arthurian myth, poetry, Hokusai prints, and classical intertext do not decorate the science-fiction premise; they change the emotional scale of robots, immortality, revenge, and uploaded life.
- In [[LastDefenderOfCamelot|《卡美洛的最后守护者》]], Lancelot's immortality is punishment as much as miracle, and Merlin's manipulation of the Grail turns heroic faith into a modern conflict over power, loneliness, and manufactured meaning.
- In the title story [[ForABreathITarry|《趁生命气息逗留》]], Frost's attempt to become human depends on learning, poetry, fear, body, and sensation; the episode treats this as a major case for [[EmbodiedPersonhood]].
- [[HomeIsTheHangman|《刽子手返乡》]] differs from simpler robot-rule stories because the robot's breakdown is read as trauma and self-formation rather than only command failure. The episode contrasts this with [[IsaacAsimov|Isaac Asimov]]'s robot-law conflict stories.
- [[PermafrostZelazny|《永久冻土》]] turns revenge and ecological merger into a nonhuman voice: Glenda's union with local mycelium lets the story shift away from ordinary human narration into [[NonhumanNarrativeVoice]].
- [[TwentyFourViewsOfMountFujiByHokusai|《富岳二十四景》]] reads digital uploading less as clean transcendence than as possessive control. The ex-husband's disembodied survival still carries loneliness, surveillance, and coercive intimacy, making the story a case for [[DigitalTranscendenceAsControl]].
- The episode uses [[LordOfLight|《光明王》 / Lord of Light]], [[TheDoorsOfHisFaceTheLampsOfHisMouth|《脸上的门，口中的灯》]], and [[DangerousVisions|Dangerous Visions]] as extension points for readers interested in Zelazny's mythic science fiction and the wider New Wave field.

## Key Quotes
> "确实非常漂亮" - Beimin's source-scoped respect for Li Keqin's earlier translation of the title story.

> "我害怕" - the title story's compressed humanizing moment, as discussed in the episode.

> "不那么无聊" - the episode's shorthand for how New Wave science fiction loosened the field's older constraints.
