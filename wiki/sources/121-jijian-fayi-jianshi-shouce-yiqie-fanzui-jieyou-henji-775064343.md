# 121.极简法医鉴识手册：一切犯罪，皆有痕迹

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode uses [[JijianFayiJianshiShouce|《极简法医鉴识手册》]] to demystify [[ForensicScience|forensic science / 法证鉴识]] for non-specialist readers. It contrasts crime fiction and CSI-style certainty with real investigation, where [[LocardsExchangePrinciple]], [[TraceEvidenceForensics]], [[BloodstainPatternAnalysis]], [[ForensicDeathTimeInference]], [[ForensicEntomology]], and [[ForensicToxicology]] can all help but rarely work as magic single clues. The ending turns toxicology toward public safety, arguing that public poisoning and inhalation toxins are not only animal-harm issues but shared environmental danger.

## Key Claims
- "Forensic" is broader than medical examination. The episode treats法庭科学/法证鉴识 as a court-facing evidence system where medical examiners, crime-scene investigators, laboratory specialists, psychologists, botanists, entomologists, and toxicologists all perform different roles.
- Popular mystery tropes overstate simple signs. Drowning cannot be decided only by water in the lungs; postmortem interval cannot be read precisely from stomach contents; and mental illness claims can be feigned or misunderstood without careful expert testing.
- [[LocardsExchangePrinciple]] supplies the episode's title logic: contact among people, objects, and places usually leaves exchanged material, but finding and interpreting that trace still requires sampling discipline and context.
- [[CSIEffect]] can distort public expectations. Juries and online audiences may demand DNA or laboratory certainty even when local resources, sample quality, or the case itself do not permit it.
- [[BodyFarmForensicResearch]] and insect evidence make death-time inference more empirical, but [[ForensicDeathTimeInference]] remains probabilistic because temperature, water, burial, freezing, insects, food, and environment can all change decomposition.
- [[BloodstainPatternAnalysis]], ballistics, fingerprints, gastric contents, insulin testing, carbon monoxide, cyanide, and volatile solvents are useful only when handled as part of a wider evidentiary chain.
- The source strengthens [[EvidenceOverTestimony]] by arguing that human memory is not a camera, while also stressing that physical evidence has its own limits and failure modes.
- The public-poisoning discussion connects [[ForensicToxicology]] to [[InhalationPoisonPublicSafety]], [[AnimalWelfareAsPublicHealth]], and [[AnimalAbuseViolenceLink]]: a toxin placed in public space can endanger children, adults, pets, and shared urban trust at the same time.
