## Summary
This [[MarketplaceTech]] episode has [[MeganMcCartyCorino|Megan McCarty-Carino]] interview [[AvivOvadia]] of the [[AIAndDemocracyFoundation|AI and Democracy Foundation]] about an AI-intensified [[InformationApocalypse]]. Ovadia argues that generative AI lowers the cost of scams, propaganda, nonconsensual imagery, and persuasive fakes while raising the cost of deciding what is real. The episode's strongest contribution is the trust-collapse frame linking [[AIInformationPollution]], [[AIRealityVerificationTax]], [[AIContentProvenance]], [[RealityApathy]], and democratic institution design.

## Key Claims
- Generative AI does not create the information crisis from scratch; it accelerates older distrust, manipulation, fraud, and propaganda dynamics.
- Ovadia warned for roughly a decade about an information environment where people cannot reliably trust what they see and hear.
- The harm includes fake media, real media being dismissed as fake, financial scams, propaganda, nonconsensual imagery, and emotionally polarizing AI feedback loops.
- Attempts to infer masked people's faces with generative AI can misidentify people and expose them to attack.
- Problematic content has become cheaper to create, while verification has become more expensive for ordinary users, journalists, and institutions.
- Ovadia treats [[ContentCredentials]] and related media-provenance standards as part of the response, but says adoption by major platforms remains limited.
- [[LinkedIn]] is named as one platform that has implemented the standard in some form.
- General skepticism can degrade into [[RealityApathy]], where people stop believing anything enough for institutions or commerce to function.
- The [[AIAndDemocracyFoundation|AI and Democracy Foundation]] focuses on governance and democratic institutions that can remain robust under AI-shaped information pressure.
- Ovadia's optimism is conditional: a better path exists only if society deliberately builds tools and institutions for it.

## Key Quotes
> "information apocalypse" - Ovadia's long-running warning about media trust collapse.

> "reality apathy" - Ovadia's term for disbelief hardening into civic and institutional paralysis.

> "go and do it" - his closing frame for conditional optimism.
