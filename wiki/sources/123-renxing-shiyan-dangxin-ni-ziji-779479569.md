# 123.人性实验：当心你自己！

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode introduces [[SocialPsychology|social psychology / 社会心理学]] as an evidence-seeking way to study "human nature" without relying on folk wisdom or after-the-fact certainty. It moves through experiments on [[HostileMediaEffect|hostile media perception]], [[PrimingAndStereotypeMemory|priming and stereotype-shaped memory]], [[ActorObserverBias|actor-observer attribution]], [[Deindividuation|deindividuation]], the [[GilbertSpinozaHypothesis|Gilbert-Spinoza hypothesis]], [[MoneyIllusion|money illusion]], and [[EmotionalContagion|emotional contagion]]. The central warning is that people are less rational, objective, and self-transparent than they feel, especially inside partisan, anonymous, noisy, or emotionally charged media environments.

## Key Claims
- [[SocialPsychology]] matters because intuition, cultural common sense, and "I knew it all along" explanations can feel obvious after the fact while still being wrong.
- Social reality is partly constructed by perception, group identity, culture, and available concepts; people do not simply read reality straight from the world.
- [[HindsightBias]] makes social-psychology findings look like old common sense, but experiments can expose how easily common sense accepts both a claim and its opposite.
- [[HostileMediaEffect]] shows how partisans can watch the same game or news report and each conclude that the coverage is biased against their side.
- [[PrimingAndStereotypeMemory]] shows that labels, words, and stereotypes can shape not only immediate judgment but also later memory.
- "Be objective" is too weak as a bias-control instruction; [[ConsiderTheOppositeBiasCorrection|consider-the-opposite correction]] works better because it forces the person to test the same evidence from the opposing standpoint.
- [[BetterThanAverageEffect|better-than-average effect]] and related overconfidence make educated or verbally competent people especially likely to think bias is other people's problem.
- [[ActorObserverBias]] makes people explain others through character while explaining themselves through circumstances, which can feed cruelty toward victims, poor people, or disliked opponents.
- [[Deindividuation]] shows how anonymity, group membership, darkness, and responsibility diffusion can weaken self-control and make antisocial behavior more likely.
- The [[GilbertSpinozaHypothesis]] says people often believe first and reject later; distraction, speed, spectacle, and noisy presentation can leave false information with residual influence even after it is marked false.
- [[MoneyIllusion]] and surface-number effects show that people can be moved by nominal amounts, clock settings, chip denominations, or inflation-blind pay changes despite knowing the underlying conversion.
- [[EmotionalContagion]] can make anger, fear, charisma, and political performance enter the body before reflective resistance catches up.

## Key Quotes
> "我早就知道了" - the phrase used to mark hindsight bias and false common-sense confidence.

> "别人有特征，而我有处境" - the episode's compressed formulation of actor-observer bias.

> "当心你自己" - the final practical warning: bias is not only a defect in other people.
