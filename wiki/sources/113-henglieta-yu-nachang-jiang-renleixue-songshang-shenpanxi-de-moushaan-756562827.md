# 113.亨丽埃塔与那场将人类学送上审判席的谋杀案

## Summary
This [[MihuanChishu|蜜獾吃书]] episode uses [[HenriettaSchmerler|Henrietta Schmerler / 亨丽埃塔·施梅勒]]'s 1931 murder during [[WhiteMountainApache|White Mountain Apache]] fieldwork to examine [[AnthropologyFieldworkSafety]], [[FieldworkSexualViolence]], and the discipline's responsibility after harm. The hosts reconstruct Schmerler as a young scholar rather than only a victim, then follow the fieldwork setup, disappearance, investigation, trial, media rumor, and later family archival work. The central tension is [[CulturalRelativismVictimProtectionBoundary]]: rejecting racist explanations of violence cannot require excusing sexual violence, blaming the victim, or protecting disciplinary access at the cost of truth.

## Key Claims
- [[HenriettaSchmerler|Schmerler]] entered [[ColumbiaUniversity|Columbia University]] anthropology in 1930, studied with [[RuthBenedict]], [[FranzBoas]], and the wider Columbia anthropology circle, and went to the [[WhiteMountainApache|White Mountain Apache]] reservation after Boas funded an independent fieldwork plan.
- The episode treats the fieldwork structure itself as risky: she traveled alone, lacked a mature protection protocol, lived in an isolated cabin arranged by [[DonnerApacheAgent|Donner]], and was trying to build access to ceremonies, stories, and informants under pressure.
- The murder investigation moved from rumor and delayed search toward testimony and confession. In the episode's account, [[GurneySeymour|Gurney Seymour]] confessed after police pressure, then recanted in court and claimed Schmerler had initiated or provoked the encounter.
- Trial strategy and press coverage repeatedly shifted attention from violence to Schmerler's character, sexuality, methods, and supposed cultural misunderstanding, making the case a key example of [[VictimBlamingAsInstitutionalDefense]] and [[VictimStigmaInTrueCrime]].
- [[RuthBenedict]] and [[MargaretMead]] are not treated as simple villains, but the source criticizes how Columbia anthropologists and reservation officials moved quickly toward self-protection, reputation management, field-access protection, or political damage control.
- The episode distinguishes anti-racism from exculpation. It rejects blaming violence on "Apache culture" or a racial essence, while also rejecting a cultural-relativist move that would turn women's safety into someone else's local custom.
- [[LinYuping]]'s later fieldwork sexual-assault account is used to show that [[FieldworkSexualViolence]] is not limited to remote, poor, colonized, or "non-modern" settings; power, gender, institutional pressure, and retaliation can appear in contemporary urban research as well.

## Key Quotes
> "唯一担心的是响尾蛇" - Schmerler's reported response to racist warnings before entering the field.

> "尊重异文化不能自动等于放弃保护受害者" - the episode's compressed boundary around cultural relativism and violence.
